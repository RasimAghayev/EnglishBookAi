# 🟥 PART 4 · Chapter 21
# Modals (4) — Advice & Expectation
### *should · ought to · had better · be supposed to* — "You **should** rest." / "You'**d better** hurry!" 💡

> **The one-line idea.** These words sit **between option and obligation**. They don't say "you must" — they say **"it's a good idea"**, **"I expect it"**, or **"do it… or else."**
> *should / ought to* = **recommended / expected (~80%).** *had better* = **strong advice + a warning.** *be supposed to* = **what's expected or arranged (but maybe not real).**
> 🇦🇿 *Bu sözlər **seçim** ilə **məcburiyyət** arasındadır. "Məcbursan" demir — **"yaxşı olar"**, **"gözləyirəm"**, ya da **"et… yoxsa!"** deyir.*
> *should / ought to* = **məsləhət görülür / gözlənilir (~80%).** *had better* = **güclü məsləhət + xəbərdarlıq.** *be supposed to* = **gözlənilən / planlaşdırılan (amma bəlkə də reallıq başqadır).*

---

## 🎯 In this chapter you will learn to…
```
✅ give ADVICE / recommend:           should / ought to ("You should rest.")
✅ give STRONG advice + warning:       had better ("You'd better hurry — or you'll miss it.")
✅ say what's EXPECTED / arranged:     be supposed to ("We're supposed to start at nine.")
✅ express EXPECTATION / probability:  should / ought to ("She should be home by now.")
✅ criticise / regret the PAST:        should have done / ought to have done ("You should have called.")
✅ say "I did it, and it was wrong":   shouldn't have done ("I shouldn't have eaten so much.")
✅ handle questions & negatives:       Should I…? / shouldn't / 'd better not / oughtn't to
✅ tell ADVICE apart from OBLIGATION:  should (a good idea) vs must / have to (necessary — Ch.20)
```
> 🇦🇿 *Bu fəsildə: məsləhət vermək; güclü məsləhət + xəbərdarlıq; gözlənilən/planlaşdırılan şeyləri demək; ehtimal bildirmək; keçmişə görə tənqid/təəssüf; sual və inkar formaları; məsləhəti məcburiyyətdən ayırmaq.*

## 🧭 THE BIG PICTURE — the advice scale

*Should* is **not** a weak *must*. It lives on a different scale — the scale of **pressure**, from a free option up to a non-negotiable rule:

```
  weak ◄──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────► strong
   could            might want to        SHOULD / OUGHT TO        HAD BETTER        must / have to
   (an option)      (a gentle hint)      (recommended ~80%)       (do it — or else)  (no choice)
   "You could       "You might want      "You should see          "You'd better      "You must show
    take a taxi."     to book early."      a doctor."               leave now!"        your ID."
```

> 🧠 **The key mental move of this chapter:** **advice ≠ obligation.**
> *"You **should** see a doctor"* = my recommendation (you're free to ignore it).
> *"You **must** see a doctor"* = it's necessary (Ch.20). Mixing these up is the #1 learner error here.
> 🇦🇿 ***should** = məsləhət (rədd edə bilərsən); **must** = məcburiyyət. Bu ikisini qarışdırmaq buradakı ən böyük səhvdir.*

## 📋 THE FORMS — at a glance

| Form | Affirmative | Negative | Question | Note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **should** | I/you/he **should go** | **shouldn't** (should not) go | **Should** I go? | no -s, no *to*, one form for all persons |
| **ought to** | I/you/he **ought to go** | **oughtn't to** go *(rare)* | **Ought** I **to** go? *(rare)* | the only modal that keeps **to**; mostly affirmative |
| **had better** | I'**d better go** | I'**d better not** go | **Had** I **better** go? *(rare)* | + bare verb (no *to*); near future; **no real past** |
| **be supposed to** | I'**m supposed to go**; he'**s supposed to** | I'**m not supposed to** go | **Am** I **supposed to** go? | ordinary *be* + supposed + **to**; has present & past |

> ⚠️ **Three quick form-traps:** ~~should to go~~ → *should go* · ~~he shoulds~~ → *he should* · ~~had better to go~~ → *had better go*.
> *ought* is the exception — it **does** take *to*: *ought **to** go.* 🇦🇿 *Yalnız **ought** "to" saxlayır; should və had better "to" almır.*

## 🔑 THE CORE — *should / ought to* do TWO jobs

These two are near-twins (*ought to* is a touch more formal / moral). Both do two completely different jobs:

```
   JOB 1 — ADVICE / recommendation:   "You should drink more water."   (it's a good idea)
   JOB 2 — EXPECTATION / probability:  "The train should be here soon."  (I expect it ~80%)
```

| 💡 Advice ("a good idea") | 🔭 Expectation ("I expect / it's likely") |
|---|---|
| You **should** apologise to her. | He left at six, so he **should** be home by now. |
| You **ought to** save some money. | This recipe **ought to** serve four people. |
| You **shouldn't** work so hard. | The results **shouldn't** take more than a week. |
| **Should** I tell him the truth? | **Should** the parcel arrive today, call me. *(formal)* |

> 🗣️ **should vs ought to:** they mean the same, but *should* is far more common in speech; *ought to* feels slightly more **formal / moral** ("You **ought to** be ashamed"). In negatives and questions, native speakers almost always pick *should* — *oughtn't to* and *Ought I to…?* sound old-fashioned. 🇦🇿 *Mənaca eynidir; **should** danışıqda daha çox işlənir, **ought to** bir az daha rəsmi/əxlaqi səslənir.*

> 🧪 **Which job is it?** If you can replace it with *"I recommend…"* → **advice.** If you can replace it with *"I expect / it's probably…"* → **expectation.**

## ⚡ *had better* ('d better) — strong advice with a WARNING

This is **not** a polite suggestion. *Had better* means **"do it — because something bad will happen if you don't."** There's an implied threat or consequence.

```
   should      ──►  it's a good idea (friendly)           "You should back up your files."
   had better  ──►  do it OR ELSE (urgent, warning)        "You'd better back up your files —
                                                            the laptop's dying!"
```

- **Form:** *'d better* + **bare verb** (no *to*): *You'd better **leave** now.*
- **Negative:** *'d better **not*** (never ~~hadn't better~~): *You'd better **not** tell anyone.*
- **Time:** points at **now / the near future** — a specific situation, not timeless advice.
- **No past:** there is no past form. For past regret, switch to *should have* (see ⏪ below).

| ✅ Use *had better* | ❌ Don't use it for… |
|---|---|
| You'**d better** see a dentist about that tooth. *(urgent, specific)* | ~~People had better eat vegetables.~~ → general advice = *should* |
| We'**d better** hurry — the shop closes in five minutes. | ~~In the past I had better study.~~ → past = *should have studied* |
| You'**d better not** be lying to me. *(warning/threat)* | — |

> 🗣️ **Native feel:** *had better* can sound **firm, even menacing**, between adults — a parent or boss to you, or you warning someone. With a friend it adds urgency: *"We'd better go!"* Often shortened to *"You **better** be careful"* in casual speech (the *'d* drops — fine spoken, not in writing). 🇦🇿 *had better = "et — yoxsa pis olar." Güclü, bəzən hədə kimi səslənir; ümumi məsləhət üçün yox, konkret vəziyyət üçün.*

## 🎭 *be supposed to* — expectation, rules… and the GAP with reality

*Be supposed to* tells you **what is expected** — by rules, by plans, or by general reputation. Its secret power is the **gap**: it very often hints that **reality is different.**

| Use | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **rule / duty** | what you're expected/required to do | You'**re supposed to** wear a seatbelt. |
| **arrangement** | what's planned to happen | We'**re supposed to** meet at eight. |
| **reputation / report** | what people say is true | This café **is supposed to** have the best coffee in town. |

```
 The GAP:  "You're supposed to knock first."   → implication: …but you didn't.
           "We were supposed to land at noon."  → implication: …but we didn't / we're late.
           "It's supposed to be sunny today."   → that's the forecast — reality may differ.
```

- **Negative** = *not allowed / not the plan*: *You'**re not supposed to** park here.* (≈ it's against the rules)
- **Past** *was/were supposed to* + verb = a past expectation, **usually unfulfilled**: *"I **was supposed to** finish yesterday"* (but I didn't).

> 🗣️ **Native feel:** *be supposed to* is everywhere in speech for **rules you might break, plans that might fail, and rumours**. It's softer and more "human" than *must* — it acknowledges that people don't always comply. 🇦🇿 *be supposed to = gözlənilən/planlaşdırılan/deyilən. Tez-tez "amma əslində belə olmadı" mənasını da daşıyır (reallıqla fərq).*

## 🔭 *should / ought to* for EXPECTATION (the "logical probability" use)

Besides advice, *should* makes a **confident prediction** — you expect something because it's logical:

| Sentence | What it means |
|---|---|
| She studied hard, so she **should** pass. | I expect her to pass (it's likely, ~80%). |
| It's a short flight; we **should** land by six. | Based on logic, landing by six is expected. |
| This **ought to** be easy for you. | I expect it to be easy. |
| £20 **should** be enough. | I expect that's enough. |

> 🔗 **Link to Ch.19:** this is weaker than *must* (deduction, "surely true") and stronger than *might* (possibility). On the certainty scale: **must (sure) > should/ought to (expected ~80%) > may/might/could (maybe).** Use *should* only for **expected / hoped-for** outcomes, not bad ones — we don't say ~~"You should be ill"~~ as a prediction. 🇦🇿 *should = məntiqi gözlənti (~80%); must-dan zəif, might-dan güclü. Yalnız gözlənilən/arzu olunan nəticələr üçün.*

## ⏪ THE PAST — *should have / ought to have done* (regret & criticism)

This is the most important past form in the chapter — and a top exam point.

```
   should have + V3   ──►  the good idea DIDN'T happen → regret / criticism
   shouldn't have + V3 ──►  it DID happen, but it was a mistake → regret
```

| Form | The reality | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **should have** done | you **didn't** do it | You **should have called** — we were worried. *(but you didn't)* |
| **ought to have** done | it **didn't** happen | They **ought to have warned** us. *(but they didn't)* |
| **shouldn't have** done | you **did** do it | I **shouldn't have said** that. *(but I did — I regret it)* |
| **should have** (expectation) | it was expected, maybe unmet | The bus **should have arrived** by now. *(I expected it; where is it?)* |

> 🇦🇿 ***should have done** = etməli idin, amma etmədin (təəssüf/tənqid). **shouldn't have done** = etdin, amma etməməli idin.*

> 🗣️ **"You shouldn't have!"** said about a gift = a warm *"oh, that's too kind!"*, not a complaint — twin of *"You didn't have to!"* from Ch.20. 🇦🇿 *Hədiyyə üçün deyilərsə — minnətdarlıq bildirir.*

> ⏪ **Past of the others:** *had better* has **no** past → use *should have*. *be supposed to* → *was/were supposed to* (= the plan/expectation, usually unmet): *"You **were supposed to** lock the door."* (but you didn't).

## ❓ QUESTIONS, negatives & short answers

| Function | Example |
|---|---|
| ask for advice | **Should** I take the job? / What **should** I do? / Where **should** we go? |
| ask about expectation | **Should** we be worried? / **Shouldn't** they be here by now? |
| negative advice | You **shouldn't** skip breakfast. |
| short answers | "Should I apologise?" — "Yes, you **should**." / "No, you **shouldn't**." |
| *had better* (rare Q) | "**Hadn't** we **better** leave?" *(formal/old-fashioned; usually just "We'd better leave, hadn't we?")* |
| *supposed to* | "**Are** we **supposed to** sign here?" — "Yes, you **are**." |

> 🗣️ Real speech prefers *"Do you think I should…?"* over a bare *"Should I…?"* when softening: *"Do you think I should call her?"* 🇦🇿 *Məsləhət soruşarkən "Should I…?" və ya daha yumşaq "Do you think I should…?" işlədilir.*

## ⛔ COMMON MISTAKES (fix these now)

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| You should to rest. | You **should rest.** | no *to* after *should* |
| He shoulds apologise. | He **should apologise.** | modals take no -s |
| You had better to go. | You **had better go.** | *had better* + bare verb |
| You hadn't better go. | You'**d better not** go. | negative = *'d better **not*** |
| I should went home. | I **should have gone** / I **should go** home. | past advice = *should have* + V3 |
| She is supposed be here. | She **is supposed to** be here. | *be supposed **to*** keeps *to* |
| You **must** see a doctor. *(meaning advice)* | You **should** see a doctor. | advice ≠ obligation — don't over-use *must* |
| You should have to call. | You **should call** / You **should have called.** | don't stack *should + have to* by accident |

## 🅰️ §A — LEVEL DIFFERENCES (A1 → C2)

How the *same idea* — "it's a good idea / it's expected" — grows with your level:

| Level | What the learner can do | Typical sentence |
|---|---|---|
| **A1** | basic *should* for advice | "You **should** sleep." |
| **A2** | *shouldn't*; simple questions | "You **shouldn't** smoke." / "**Should** I go?" |
| **B1** | *had better*; *be supposed to*; *ought to* | "You'**d better** hurry." / "We'**re supposed to** start now." |
| **B2** | *should have / shouldn't have* (past); *should* = expectation | "You **should have called.**" / "She **should** be home by now." |
| **C1** | the *be supposed to* gap; *was supposed to*; *It's high time* | "We **were supposed to** meet — what happened?" / "It's high time you **decided**." |
| **C2** | register, irony, moral *ought*, softened advice | "One **ought** to know better." / "You **shouldn't** have, really!" |

> 🇦🇿 *A1: sadə should. B1: had better, be supposed to. B2: keçmiş should have + gözlənti mənası. C1: be supposed to fərqi, was supposed to. C2: üslub və incəliklər.*

## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT

1. **Most everyday advice = *should*.** *Ought to* is rarer and a little more moral/formal; *had better* is reserved for genuine urgency or warning. 🇦🇿 *Gündəlik məsləhət üçün adətən **should** işlənir.*
2. **"You'd better…" can sound like a threat.** Tone matters: between friends it's urgency ("we'd better run!"); from a boss or parent it can be a warning ("You'd better not be late again"). 🇦🇿 *"You'd better…" bəzən hədə kimi səslənir — tona diqqət.*
3. **"You shouldn't have!"** about a gift = gratitude, not criticism — said with a smile. 🇦🇿 *Hədiyyə üçün — minnətdarlıq.*
4. **"It's supposed to be good"** quietly signals *"that's the reputation — I haven't confirmed it."* Natives use it to report without vouching. 🇦🇿 *"deyilənə görə yaxşıdır" — özün təsdiq etmədən nəql edirsən.*
5. **Softened advice is more polite.** *"I think you should…"*, *"Maybe you should…"*, *"You might want to…"*, *"Do you think you should…?"* feel gentler than a bare *"You should."* 🇦🇿 *Məsləhəti yumşaltmaq daha nəzakətlidir: "Maybe you should…"*
6. **"should" for expectation is everywhere:** *"It should work now," "That should do it," "This shouldn't take long."* — confident but not 100%. 🇦🇿 *"should" həm də gözlənti üçün geniş işlənir.*

## 🅲 §C — ALTERNATIVE EXPRESSIONS (say it another way)

| Instead of… | You can say… | Shade |
|---|---|---|
| You **should** rest. | You'**d be wise to** rest. / **It would be a good idea to** rest. / **Why don't you** rest? | formal / neutral / casual |
| You **shouldn't** do that. | I **wouldn't** do that. / **It's not a good idea to** do that. | softer |
| You'**d better** leave. | You **really need to** leave. / If I were you, I'**d** leave **now.** | urgent |
| You'**re supposed to** sign. | You'**re meant to** / **required to** sign. | neutral / formal |
| You **should have** told me. | You **could have told me.** / **Why didn't you** tell me? | reproachful |
| He **ought to** know. | He **really should** know. / He'**d** better know. | varying force |

> 🇦🇿 *Eyni məsləhət/gözlənti fikrini fərqli güc və üslubda demək olar.*

# 📖 MINI STORY #1 — "The Interview Advice"
### *Advice, warnings and expectations — a big day, and everyone has an opinion.*
### 🇦🇿 *Məsləhətlər, xəbərdarlıqlar və gözləntilər — böyük gün, və hər kəsin öz fikri var.*

---

The night before his interview, Rashad couldn't sleep. His sister leaned in the doorway.

"You **should** get some rest," she said. "And you **shouldn't** drink any more coffee — it's nearly midnight."

"I know, I know. I'm just nervous."

"Listen," she said gently. "You'**d better** set two alarms — you **mustn't** be late for this one." *(Ch.20 obligation slips in: being on time is non-negotiable.)* "The bus **should** get you there in twenty minutes, but leave early."

In the morning their mother had her own advice. "You'**re supposed to** wear a tie to these things, aren't you? And you **ought to** take a spare copy of your CV. You **should have** printed two last night!"

"I did, Mum," Rashad smiled. "Both of them. I learned my lesson last time — I **shouldn't have** turned up empty-handed."

The interview itself went well. The manager said, "You **should** hear from us by Friday. We'**re supposed to** make all decisions by the end of the week."

On the bus home, Rashad realised he **needn't have** worried so much *(Ch.20 again)* — and that, this time, he **had** listened to good advice. *Maybe I should listen more often*, he thought.

| "You **mustn't** be late." | 🔴 obligation (Ch.20) | forbidden / non-negotiable |

| "You **should have** printed two." | ⏪ past criticism | you didn't (and should have) |

```
4. 🇦🇿→EN: "Bir az dincəlməlisən." / "Müsahibəyə gecikməməlisən." / "Cümə gününə qədər cavab gözlənilir."
```
> 💬 **Answer (task 2):** *should have printed* = he was advised to but (in the past) the good thing **didn't fully happen** — here it's the mother's criticism. *shouldn't have turned up empty-handed* = last time he **did** arrive with nothing, and he regrets it. One is "you didn't do the good thing"; the other is "I did the bad thing."

# 🧨 EXPANSION PACK — Advice & Expectation (E1–E12)
### *The deep layer: the advice scale, the past split, the supposed-to gap, register, reported speech & idioms.*
### 🇦🇿 *Dərin qat: məsləhət şkalası, keçmişin ikiyə bölünməsi, "supposed to" fərqi, üslub, dolayı nitq və idiomlar.*

---

## 🅴1 — The ADVICE STRENGTH SCALE

Advice has degrees too — from a free option up to a near-order, mirroring the obligation scale of Ch.20:

```
 WEAKER ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────► STRONGER
  could            might want to      should / ought to      had better        must / have to
  "an option"      "a gentle hint"    "recommended ~80%"     "do it — or else"  "no choice" (Ch.20)
  💡 suggestion                                              ⚡ warning           🚦 obligation
```

| Strength | Modal | Example |
|---|---|---|
| just an option | could | You **could** take the train. |
| gentle hint | might want to | You **might want to** book early. |
| clear recommendation | should / ought to | You **should** book early. |
| urgent advice + threat | had better | You'**d better** book now — seats are vanishing! |
| no choice (→ Ch.20) | must / have to | You **must** book before Friday. *(it's the rule)* |

> 🔗 Chapter 20 owns the **right** end (true obligation); this chapter owns the **middle-left** (option → strong advice). *had better* is the bridge — advice with the *force* of obligation but none of its authority.

---

## 🅴2 — *should* vs *ought to*: the cases that fool people

They share a meaning, but they don't share habits. Four refinements:

1. **Speech defaults to *should*.** *Ought to* is less frequent and feels a touch more **formal or moral**.
2. **Negatives & questions almost always use *should*.** *Oughtn't to…?* and *Ought I to…?* sound archaic — natives say *shouldn't* / *Should I…?*
3. ***ought* keeps *to*.** It's the **only** modal that does: *ought **to** go* (never ~~ought go~~).
4. ***ought to* carries a moral edge.** *"You **ought to** be ashamed"* / *"You **ought to** know better"* feel ethical, not just practical.

| Situation | Natural choice | 🇦🇿 |
|---|---|---|
| everyday spoken advice | **should** | You **should** rest. |
| a moral judgement | **ought to** | You **ought to** apologise. |
| a question | **Should** I…? | **Should** I tell her? |
| a negative | **shouldn't** | You **shouldn't** worry. |

---

## 🅴3 — The advice REGISTER ladder

| Register | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| formal / written | be advised to · would be well advised to | Passengers **are advised to** arrive early. |
| neutral | should · ought to | You **should** see a specialist. |
| friendly / softened | I think you should · maybe you should · you might want to | **Maybe you should** call first. |
| casual suggestion | Why don't you…? · How about…? | **Why don't you** take a break? |
| urgent / warning | had better · you really need to | You'**d better** leave now. |

> ⚠️ A bare *"You should…"* to a stranger or a superior can sound bossy. Soften it: *"You might want to…"* / *"It might be worth …ing."*
> 🇦🇿 *Tanımadığın və ya yuxarı vəzifəli adama quru "You should…" bir az amiranə səslənir — yumşaltmaq daha nəzakətlidir.*

---

## 🅴4 — ⚠️ *should have done* vs *shouldn't have done* (the C1 splitter)

A timeline makes the difference visible — the advice-world twin of Ch.20's *needn't have / didn't need to*:

```
 should have done:    ❶ the good thing did NOT happen   →  ❷ criticism / regret  ("you didn't — but you should have")
 shouldn't have done: ❶ the bad thing DID happen        →  ❷ regret              ("you did — but you shouldn't have")
```

| Sentence | Did it happen? | Feeling |
|---|---|---|
| You **should have called.** | No — you didn't call | criticism / "why didn't you?" |
| You **shouldn't have called.** | Yes — you did call | regret / "that was a mistake" |
| I **should have studied.** | No — I didn't | self-reproach |
| I **shouldn't have studied** *(that topic)*. | Yes — I did | wasted effort / regret |

> 🧪 **Test:** Can you add "…but you didn't"? → **should have**. Can you add "…but you did"? → **shouldn't have**.
> 🇦🇿 *should have done = etməli idin, amma etmədin. shouldn't have done = etdin, amma etməməli idin.*

---

## 🅴5 — *be supposed to*: the gap between expectation and reality

The semi-modal with a built-in *"…but maybe not"*:

| Use | Example | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| expected by rule | You'**re supposed to** show ID. | …though some don't |
| planned / arranged | We'**re supposed to** start at nine. | …let's see if we do |
| reputation / report | It'**s supposed to** be a great film. | people say so — unconfirmed |
| **past plan NOT met** | I **was supposed to** finish yesterday. | …but I didn't |
| mild prohibition (neg.) | You'**re not supposed to** park here. | it's against the rules |

> 🤝 ***be meant to*** is its near-twin: *"You'**re meant to** tip"* ≈ *"You're supposed to tip"*; *"It'**s meant to** rain"* ≈ "the forecast says so."
> 🇦🇿 *be supposed to / be meant to = gözlənilən, planlaşdırılan, ya da deyilən — çox vaxt "amma əslində belə olmadı" çaları ilə.*

---

## 🅴6 — *should / ought to* for EXPECTATION (the probability link to Ch.19)

The same words that advise also **predict** — on the certainty scale they sit just below *must*:

```
 SURER ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────► LESS SURE
  must (deduction, ~sure)   >   should / ought to (expected, ~80%)   >   may / might / could (~50%)
  "It must be ready."            "It should be ready by now."             "It might be ready."
```

| Sentence | Reading |
|---|---|
| He left early, so he **should** be there by now. | I expect he is (logical, ~80%). |
| This **ought to** work. | I expect it to work. |
| The repair **shouldn't** cost much. | I don't expect it to be expensive. |
| They **should have** landed by now. | I expected them to — have they? |

> ⚠️ Use it only for **expected / hoped-for** outcomes. We don't predict bad things with *should*: not ~~"It should rain and ruin the trip"~~ — there you'd say *"It might…"*.
> 🇦🇿 *should = məntiqi gözlənti (~80%); must-dan zəif, might-dan güclü. Yalnız gözlənilən/arzu olunan nəticələr üçün.*

---

## 🅴7 — SOFTENING & STRENGTHENING advice

| 🔽 Soften (more polite / tentative) | 🔼 Strengthen (more force) |
|---|---|
| I **think** you should… | You **really** should… |
| **Maybe / Perhaps** you should… | You **ought to**, you know. |
| You **might want to** … | You'**d better** … *(warning)* |
| It **might be a good idea to** … | I **strongly advise** you to … |
| **If I were you, I'd** … | **Make sure** you … / **Whatever you do,** … |
| **Have you thought about** …ing? | You **must** … *(if it's truly necessary — Ch.20)* |

> 🗣️ *"If I were you, I'd…"* is the most natural way to give personal advice in conversation — softer than *"You should"* because it frames it as *your own* choice. 🇦🇿 *"If I were you, I'd…" = "Sənin yerinə olsaydım…" — danışıqda ən təbii məsləhət formasıdır.*

---

## 🅴8 — QUESTIONS for advice (+ short answers)

| Question | Register | Short answers |
|---|---|---|
| **Should** I apologise? | neutral, direct | Yes, you should. / No, you shouldn't. / I don't think you should. |
| **What/Where/How should** I…? | asking for guidance | "You should try the blue one." |
| **Do you think** I should…? | softened | "Yes, I think you should." |
| **Shall** I…? | offer / suggestion *(→ Ch.22)* | "Yes, please." / "No, it's fine." |
| **Hadn't** we **better** leave? | formal/old | "Yes, we'd better." |

> 🗣️ For real advice, native speakers often prefer *"Do you think I should…?"* or *"What would you do?"* over a blunt *"Should I…?"* 🇦🇿 *Məsləhət üçün çox vaxt "Do you think I should…?" daha yumşaq səslənir.*

---

## 🅴9 — Advice & expectation in REPORTED SPEECH

| Direct | Reported |
|---|---|
| "You **should** rest." | She said I **should** rest. *(should stays — it's already a past-form modal)* |
| "You **ought to** apologise." | He said I **ought to** apologise. |
| "You'**d better** hurry." | She warned me I'**d better / had better** hurry. |
| "We'**re supposed to** start at nine." | He said we **were supposed to** start at nine. |
| "You **should have** called." | She said I **should have** called. *(no further backshift)* |

> 🔑 *should / ought to / had better* **don't backshift** (they have no further past). Only *be supposed to* changes (*am/is → was*). → cross-ref Reported Speech chapter.

---

## 🅴10 — Emphatic & idiomatic *should / ought*

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **I should think / hope so** | emphatic agreement | "Will he apologise?" — "**I should hope so!**" |
| **You should have seen / heard it!** | "it was remarkable" | **You should have seen** her face! |
| **Why should I?** | indignant refusal | "Help him." — "**Why should I?**" |
| **How should I know?** | "I've no idea" | "Where's the key?" — "**How should I know?**" |
| **Should you need…** | formal *if* | **Should you need** anything, call reception. |
| **I should say / imagine** | tentative opinion (BrE) | It's about ten miles, **I should say.** |
| **It's (high) time** + past | overdue advice | **It's high time** you **learned** to drive. |

> 🇦🇿 *Bu hazır ifadələr danışıqda çox işlədilir — should/ought-un idiomatik üzləri.*

## 🅴11 — ADVICE & EXPECTATION ACROSS ALL TENSES (master table)

| Time | 💡 advice (should / ought to) | 🔭 expectation | 🎭 be supposed to |
|---|---|---|---|
| **present** | You **should** rest. | She **should** be home. | We'**re supposed to** start now. |
| **past** | You **should have** rested. | They **should have** arrived by now. | We **were supposed to** start at nine. |
| **future** | You **should** rest tomorrow. | It **should** be ready by Friday. | We'**re supposed to** start tomorrow. |
| **had better** | You'**d better** rest. *(now / near future)* | — | — |

> \* *had better* has **no past and no future of its own** — it always points at *now / the near future*. For the past, switch to *should have* (§E4).
> 🇦🇿 *had better-in keçmiş və gələcək forması yoxdur — həmişə indi/yaxın gələcəyə baxır. Keçmiş üçün should have işlət.*

## 🅴12 — 🇦🇿 CONTRASTIVE SYNTHESIS

| English | 🇦🇿 | Watch out |
|---|---|---|
| You **should** go. | Getməlisən. | **advice** — same *-məli* as obligation! |
| You **must / have to** go. *(Ch.20)* | Getməlisən / getməyə məcbursan. | **obligation** — AZ doesn't split these |
| You'**d better** go. | Getsən yaxşıdır / Get, yoxsa… | warning + threat |
| You **should have** gone. | Getməli idin (amma getmədin). | past regret/criticism |
| It **should** be ready. | Hazır olmalıdır. | **expectation**, not obligation |
| You'**re supposed to** go. | Getməli idin / guya getməlisən. | expectation / arrangement |

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani overlap trap:** *-malı/-məli* covers **three** different English ideas — *should* (advice), *must / have to* (obligation), and *should be* (expectation, *olmalıdır*). English forces you to pick. Before translating, ask: am I **recommending**, **requiring**, or **predicting**?

| You **should** rest. / You'**d better** rest. | advice → warning (or else) | məsləhət → xəbərdarlıq |
| You **should have** called. / You **shouldn't have** called. | didn't (criticism) → did (regret) | etmədin → etdin |
| It **should** be ready. / It **must** be ready. *(Ch.19!)* | expectation (~80%) → deduction (~sure) | gözlənti → nəticə |
| We'**re supposed to** start now. / We **should** start now. | the plan/rule → my advice | plan → məsləhət |
| You **shouldn't** smoke. / You **mustn't** smoke. *(Ch.20)* | advice → prohibition | məsləhət → qadağa |

> 🔬 Rows 1, 4 and 6 are the **advice-vs-obligation / expectation-vs-deduction** clashes across Ch.19–21 — context decides. → §E12.

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "The Road Trip"
### *Advice, warnings, expectations and a few regrets — a journey to a mountain lake.*
### 🇦🇿 *Məsləhətlər, xəbərdarlıqlar, gözləntilər və bir neçə təəssüf — dağ gölünə səyahət.*

> Read it twice. **First** for the story; **second** circling every *should · shouldn't · ought to · had better · be supposed to · should have.*
> 🇦🇿 *İki dəfə oxu: əvvəl hekayə üçün, sonra hər modal feli dövrəyə alaraq.*

---

The mountain lake was **supposed to** be the most beautiful place in the country, and Elnur and Kamran had planned the trip for weeks.

"We **should** leave at six," Kamran said the night before. "If we do, we **should** be there by lunchtime — it's only three hours."

"You worry too much," laughed Elnur. "We'll be fine."

But in the morning Kamran was anxious again. "You'**d better** fill up the tank before we go — there aren't many petrol stations on that road. And we'**d better not** miss the turning after the bridge; the map's tricky there."

"I'll fill up on the way," Elnur said, waving a hand.

They set off an hour late. By ten the fuel light was blinking. "I **should have** filled up in town," Elnur admitted. "And weren't you **supposed to** bring the paper map?"

"It's still on the kitchen table," Kamran groaned. "We **shouldn't have** trusted the phone — there's no signal up here at all."

They pulled into a tiny petrol station just in time. The old attendant chuckled. "The lake? You **ought to** take the old road. It's longer, but you'll never forget the view. And don't rely on your phone — it'**s supposed to** lose signal past the bridge."

He was right on both counts. The new road was closed for repairs. "We **should have** asked someone sooner," Kamran sighed.

When they finally reached the lake, it stole their breath. It really **was** everything people said.

"They say it'**s supposed to** be even better at sunset," Elnur murmured.

Kamran smiled. "Then we'**d better** stay. And next time, my friend, we **should** definitely bring a map."

## 🏁 End of Chapter 21

You can now **soften the pressure** below obligation. You **advise** and **recommend** with *should / ought
to*; you give **strong advice with a warning** using *had better* (+ bare verb, near future, no real past);
you say what's **expected, arranged or rumoured** with *be supposed to* — alert to its built-in **gap** with
reality. The same *should / ought to* also expresses **logical expectation** (~80%, weaker than *must*'s
deduction, stronger than *might*). For the **past**, you criticise or regret with *should have done* (the good
thing didn't happen) and *shouldn't have done* (the bad thing did) — and you keep **advice** firmly apart from
**obligation** (Ch.20). The four standard sections complete the picture. 💡

```
 ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ 💡 advice:        should / ought to        "You should rest."             │
 │ ⚡ advice+warning: had better (+ bare verb)  "You'd better hurry!"          │
 │ 🎭 expectation:    be supposed to            "We're supposed to start at 9."│
 │ 🔭 probability:    should / ought to (~80%)  "She should be home by now."   │
 │ ⏪ past:           should have done (didn't) vs shouldn't have done (did)   │
 │ ⚠️ advice ≠ obligation · no "should to / shoulds / had better to"          │
 └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
