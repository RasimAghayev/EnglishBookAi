## 🧭 THE BIG PICTURE — how to take this test

```
   Start at A1. Work UP through the sections.
   Score each section /10.
   YOUR LEVEL = the highest section where you score 8 or more.
   The section where you drop below 8 = your NEXT study target.
```

> 🧠 **The key mental move:** don't just total 60 — **read the pattern by section.** Full marks at A1–B1 but 5/10 at B2? You're a solid B1 whose next job is the B2 chapters. The test is a **placement tool**, not just a score.
> 🇦🇿 *Əsas fikir: sadəcə 60-ı toplama — **bölmə üzrə nümunəni oxu.** Bu, yerləşdirmə alətidir, təkcə bal deyil.*

> 📋 **Rules:** no notes, no dictionary. Time yourself (~45 min). Do all 60, then check the key **once**. 🇦🇿 *Qaydalar: qeydsiz, lüğətsiz. Vaxt tut (~45 dəq). Hamısını et, sonra bir dəfə yoxla.*

## 🅱️ §B — INSIGHT (on finishing — and never really finishing)

1. **A test is a snapshot, not a stamp.** Retake it in a month; watch your ceiling rise. 🇦🇿 *Test bir anlıq şəkildir, möhür deyil.*
2. **Mastery is maintenance.** Language fades without use — keep **producing** (Ch.41). 🇦🇿 *Ustalıq qulluq tələb edir — istifadə et.*
3. **The gaps are the gift.** Every question you missed is a precise next step, not a failure. 🇦🇿 *Boşluqlar hədiyyədir — dəqiq növbəti addım.*
4. **You've built a system, not a pile of rules.** The charts (Ch.38) and roadmap (Ch.43) tie it together. 🇦🇿 *Sən qaydalar yığını yox, **sistem** qurmusan.*
5. **Native fluency is a direction, not a finish.** Even natives keep learning their language. Enjoy the road. 🇦🇿 *Doğma səlislik istiqamətdir, son deyil.*
6. **Teach it to keep it.** Explaining a rule to someone else locks it in — become the tutor. 🇦🇿 *Saxlamaq üçün öyrət.*

# 📖 MINI STORY #1 — "The Last Page"

### *A learner reaches the final chapter — and discovers the book was never the destination.*
### 🇦🇿 *Öyrənən son fəslə çatır — və kitabın heç vaxt son məqsəd olmadığını anlayır.*

---

Kənan turned to the last chapter and smiled. Forty-nine chapters. He remembered starting at Chapter 1, barely able to say *"I am a student"* without stumbling.

He took the Final Mastery Test. A1 — easy. A2 — easy. B1 — he breezed through the present perfect and the conditionals that had once terrified him. B2 — the past perfect, the passive, reported speech: all solid. C1 — the inversions and clefts made him think, but he got most of them. C2 — he dropped a few, but understood every question.

**52 out of 60.** His ceiling: a strong **C1**.

He sat back, remembering the journey. The chapter where *used to* and *would* finally clicked. The mini-story about the road trip. The day *"If I had known"* stopped being a puzzle and became a sentence he just *said*.

His tutor called to congratulate him. "So — you've finished the book. What now?"

Kənan laughed. "I thought finishing the book was the goal. But the test showed me something. The book gave me the **system** — the charts, the roadmap, the rules. But the language itself? That I keep building **every day**, by using it."

"Exactly," said the tutor. "The book was never the destination. It was the **map**. Now you walk the road."

Kənan looked at the eight questions he'd missed — all C2 register and idiom. Not failures. **Directions.** His next month was already planned: Chapter 37, Chapter 40, and a conversation partner.

He closed the book, then opened his notebook and wrote his first sentence of the rest of his English life:

*"I used to think fluency was a finish line. Now I know it's a way of travelling."*

And this time, every word was correct — and, better still, it sounded like **him**.

## 🅴1 — How to READ your section scores

```
   Read the PATTERN, not just the total:
   A1 ✅ A2 ✅ B1 ✅ B2 ⚠️(5/10) C1 ❌ C2 ❌   →  you are a solid B1; next target = B2
   A1 ✅ A2 ✅ B1 ✅ B2 ✅ C1 ✅(8/10) C2 ⚠️   →  strong C1; polish register for C2
```

> 🔑 Your **ceiling** = the highest section at **8+/10**. The first section below 8 = your **next study zone** — go to its chapters. 🇦🇿 *Tavan = 8+/10 olan ən yüksək bölmə; ilk 8-dən aşağı bölmə növbəti zonandır.*

## 🅴2 — Building your personal NEXT-STEP plan

| Your ceiling | Study next (chapters) |
|---|---|
| A2 | present perfect (8), conditionals 1–2 (24), modals (20–21) |
| B1 | past perfect (10), conditional 3 (24), passive (26), reported (25) |
| B2 | mixed conditionals (24), inversion & cleft (34), advanced modals (19–21) |

> 🇦🇿 *Tavanına görə növbəti fəsilləri seç — plan dəqiq olsun.*

## 🅴3 — Self-assessing PRODUCTIVE skills (the test can't grade these)

The 60 questions test **recognition/accuracy** — but real level also needs **production**. Grade yourself:

| Skill | Ask yourself | Band signal |
|---|---|---|
| **speaking fluency** | Can I talk 2 min without freezing? | pauses shrink = rising |
| **speaking range** | Did I use 3+ different structures? | conditionals/relatives = B2+ |
| **writing accuracy** | Did I make <2 errors per paragraph? | few errors = B2+ |
| **writing range** | Did I use complex sentences? | subordination = B2+ |

> 🇦🇿 *60 sual tanıma/dəqiqliyi yoxlayır — istehsalı özün qiymətləndir (səlislik, rəngarənglik, dəqiqlik).*

---

## 🅴4 — SPEAKING self-test (record + rubric)

```
   Task: speak 2 minutes on "The most important decision I've made."
   Then score yourself /5 on each:
   □ fluency (few pauses)   □ range (varied tenses/structures)
   □ accuracy (few errors)  □ pronunciation (clear)   □ organisation (logical)
```

> 🔑 Listen back **once**, note **one** fix, and re-record. That single loop is the fastest speaking gain. 🇦🇿 *Bir dəfə dinlə, bir düzəliş qeyd et, yenidən yaz.*

---

## 🅴5 — WRITING self-test (task + rubric)

```
   Task: write a 150-word opinion paragraph on "Should exams be abolished?"
   Then score /5:
   □ task response (answered fully)   □ coherence (linkers, flow)
   □ grammar range (complex sentences)  □ grammar accuracy (few errors)  □ vocabulary (precise)
```

| English demands what Azerbaijani doesn't… | Watch it forever |

| **articles + register via modality** | AZ uses Siz/sən → politeness in verbs |

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "Graduation Day"

### *The same two learners from Chapter 41's exchange — a year on. Where hard work and a map lead.*

A year after their language exchange, Nadir and Sevil sat the Final Mastery Test together — the same test, side by side, just as they'd once traded corrections.

Nadir, who **used to freeze** in conversation, now **breezed** through the speaking-heavy sections. *(past habit → present ease.)* Sevil, who **had once made** the same errors for years, checked her articles and prepositions instinctively. *(past perfect → present accuracy.)*

They compared results. Both had reached a strong **C1**.

"Do you remember," Sevil laughed, "when you couldn't say three sentences without translating in your head?"

"And when you said *'I have seen him yesterday'* every single time?" Nadir grinned.

"We each had half the puzzle," she said. "Now look at us."

Their tutor, who **had been watching** their progress for a year, smiled. *(past perfect continuous.)* "So — you've finished the book, passed the test, reached C1. Are you done?"

They looked at each other. "No," said Nadir. "**If we stopped now, we'd lose it.** So we won't." *(2nd conditional.)*

"We're going to keep the daily habit," Sevil added. "Five translations, one recording, one paragraph. **By next year, we'll have been doing it for two years straight.**" *(future perfect continuous.)*

"And when we hit C2," Nadir said, "**we'll start teaching.** The best way to keep it is to give it away."

Their tutor nodded. "That's the real graduation — not finishing the book, but becoming someone who never stops. **Had you treated the test as the end**, you'd have plateaued. Instead, you treated it as a **checkpoint**." *(inverted 3rd conditional.)*

Outside, Sevil looked at the certificate in her hand, then at the notebook in her bag — the one still half-empty, waiting for tomorrow's sentences.

"The test is over," she said. "The learning isn't."

And somewhere in that sentence — effortless, accurate, entirely her own — was the proof of everything the book had given them.
