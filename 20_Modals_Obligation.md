# 🟥 PART 4 · Chapter 20
# Modals (3) — Obligation & Necessity
### *must · have to · have got to · need(n't) · mustn't · don't have to* — "I **must** go." / "You **don't have to** pay." 🚦

> **The one-line idea.** These words answer the question **"Is it necessary — and who says so?"**
> *Must / have to* = **yes, necessary.** *Mustn't* = **no — forbidden.** *Don't have to / needn't* = **no — but optional.**
> 🇦🇿 *Bu sözlər bir suala cavab verir: "Lazımdır? — və bunu kim deyir?"*
> *Must / have to* = **bəli, lazımdır.** *Mustn't* = **xeyr — qadağandır.** *Don't have to / needn't* = **xeyr — amma istəyə bağlıdır.**

---

## 🎯 In this chapter you will learn to…
```
✅ say something is NECESSARY:        must / have to / have got to / need to
✅ say who imposes it:                must (I/the speaker) vs have to (rules/outside)
✅ say something is FORBIDDEN:         mustn't  ( = don't do it! )
✅ say something is NOT necessary:     don't have to / needn't / don't need to ( = optional )
✅ avoid THE classic trap:             mustn't ≠ don't have to
✅ handle the PAST:                    had to / didn't have to / needn't have vs didn't need to
✅ handle the FUTURE & questions:      will have to / Do I have to? / Must I? / Need I?
✅ use semi-modals:                    be supposed to / required to / obliged to / meant to / be to
```
> 🇦🇿 *Bu fəsildə: nəyinsə lazım, qadağan və ya istəyə bağlı olduğunu deməyi; keçmiş, gələcək və sual formalarını; yarı-modal felləri öyrənəcəksən.*

## 🧭 THE BIG PICTURE — three doors, not two

Most learners think there are **two** answers (yes / no). In English there are **three**:

```
 ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │                    "Is this action necessary?"                                │
 ├───────────────────────┬────────────────────────┬──────────────────────────── ┤
 │  🟢 YES — necessary    │  🔴 NO — forbidden!     │  ⚪ NO — but optional         │
 │  obligation           │  prohibition           │  absence of obligation       │
 ├───────────────────────┼────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────┤
 │  must                 │  mustn't               │  don't have to               │
 │  have to              │  can't                 │  needn't                     │
 │  have got to          │  not allowed to        │  don't need to               │
 │  need to              │                        │  (it's up to you)            │
 ├───────────────────────┼────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────┤
 │  "You must wear a     │  "You mustn't smoke    │  "You don't have to come —   │
 │   seatbelt."          │   here."               │   only if you want."         │
 └───────────────────────┴────────────────────────┴──────────────────────────────┘
```

> 🧠 **The single most important takeaway of this chapter:** the negative of *must* splits in two.
> *mustn't* (🔴 forbidden) and *don't have to* (⚪ optional) are **opposites of each other**, not synonyms.
> 🇦🇿 ***must***-un inkarı **iki yerə bölünür**: *mustn't* (qadağan) və *don't have to* (lazım deyil) — bunlar bir-birinin **əksidir**, eyni şey deyil.

## 📋 THE FORMS — at a glance

### 🟢 Obligation (necessary)
| Form | Affirmative | Negative | Question | Note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **must** | I/you/he **must go** | — *(see mustn't below)* | **Must** I go? | no -s, no *to*, one form for all persons |
| **have to** | I/you **have to go**; he **has to go** | I **don't have to**; he **doesn't have to** | **Do** you **have to** go? | behaves like a normal verb (do/does/did) |
| **have got to** | I'**ve got to go**; he'**s got to go** | (rare) haven't got to | **Have** you **got to** go? | informal, mostly spoken, mostly present |
| **need to** | I **need to go** | I **don't need to** | **Do** you **need to** go? | ordinary verb |
| **need** (modal) | *(rare in +)* | I **needn't go** | **Need** I go? | no -s, no *to*; mostly negative/question, formal |

### 🔴 Prohibition (forbidden) ⚪ vs No obligation (optional)
| Meaning | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 forbidden | **mustn't** (= must not) | You **mustn't** touch that wire. |
| 🔴 forbidden | **can't / not allowed to** | You **can't** park here. |
| ⚪ optional | **don't / doesn't have to** | You **don't have to** wear a tie. |
| ⚪ optional | **needn't / don't need to** | You **needn't** wait for me. |

> ⚠️ ***must*** has **no infinitive, no -ing, no past, no future of its own.** For all of those, borrow from **have to**:
> ~~to must~~ → *to have to* · ~~musted~~ → *had to* · ~~will must~~ → *will have to*. → §C.

## 🔑 THE CORE DISTINCTION — *must* vs *have to* (WHO says so?)

Both mean "it's necessary." The difference is **the source of the pressure**.

```
   must  ──►  the obligation comes from ME / the speaker (internal, personal, felt)
 have to ──►  the obligation comes from OUTSIDE (rules, laws, other people, circumstances)
```

| *must* (I feel it / I impose it) | *have to* (the world imposes it) |
|---|---|
| I **must** call my mother — I miss her. | I **have to** call the office — it's company policy. |
| You **must** see that film, it's brilliant! *(my strong advice)* | You **have to** show ID at the door. *(the rule)* |
| I really **must** stop eating sugar. *(my own decision)* | Diabetics **have to** watch their sugar. *(medical necessity)* |
| We **must** meet up soon! *(a warm personal urge)* | Employees **have to** clock in by 9. *(the regulation)* |

> 🗣️ **Native habit:** in everyday speech, **have to** is far more common for facts and rules.
> *must* (affirmative) sounds stronger, more personal, sometimes more formal or emphatic.
> On signs and in written rules, though, *must* is standard: **"Passengers must keep their tickets."**
> 🇦🇿 *Danışıqda qaydalar üçün adətən **have to** işlədilir; **must** daha şəxsi, daha güclü səslənir. Yazılı qaydalarda isə **must** standartdır.*

> 🧪 **Quick test:** *Could you add "…because that's the rule"?* → use **have to.**
> *Could you add "…because I personally feel I should"?* → use **must.**

---

## 🗨️ *have got to* — the spoken cousin of *have to*

- Means the same as **have to**, but it's **informal** and very common in speech (esp. British English).
- Usually **present**; for past, switch to **had to**.

| have got to | = have to |
|---|---|
| I'**ve got to** run — I'm late! | I **have to** run. |
| She'**s got to** finish by Friday. | She **has to** finish by Friday. |
| **Have** you **got to** leave already? | **Do** you **have to** leave already? |

> 🗣️ In fast speech: *"I've gotta go," "She's gotta finish."* (*gotta* = got to). Fine in conversation, **not** in formal writing.

---

## ⚠️ THE BIG TRAP — *mustn't* vs *don't have to*

This is the **#1 mistake** Azerbaijani (and most) learners make. Burn this in:

```
 ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │  mustn't        =  it is FORBIDDEN.  Do NOT do it.        🔴 STOP    │
 │  don't have to  =  it is NOT NECESSARY.  Do it or not.    ⚪ FREE     │
 └────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

| Sentence | Real meaning | 🇦🇿 |
|---|---|---|
| You **mustn't** tell anyone. | It's a secret — telling is forbidden. | Heç kimə deməməlisən (qadağandır). |
| You **don't have to** tell anyone. | You can keep it private if you like — your choice. | Heç kimə deməyə bilərsən (məcbur deyilsən). |
| Children **mustn't** play near the road. | Forbidden — danger! | Uşaqlar yol kənarında oynamamalıdır (qadağa). |
| You **don't have to** finish the soup. | It's okay to leave it. | Şorbanı bitirməyə məcbur deyilsən. |

> 🧠 **AZ bridge.** Azerbaijani often uses one structure (*-mamalısan*) for both ideas, so learners merge them.
> In English they are **opposite**: *mustn't* removes your freedom; *don't have to* gives it to you. → §A, §D.

## 🧩 *need* — modal **and** ordinary verb

English lets *need* behave **two ways**:

| | Ordinary verb (common) | Modal verb (formal/negative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Form** | need**s**, needed, *to* + V | no -s, no *to*, no *did* |
| **Affirmative** | She **needs to** rest. | *(rare — use "need to")* |
| **Negative** | You **don't need to** worry. | You **needn't** worry. |
| **Question** | **Do** you **need to** go? | **Need** I go? *(formal)* |

> 🟰 *needn't* = *don't need to* = *don't have to* (all ⚪ "not necessary"). The modal *needn't* is a touch more formal/British.
> ⚠️ Don't mix the two systems: ~~She needn't to go~~ / ~~Do you need go?~~ ❌. Pick one lane and stay in it.

---

## ⏪ THE PAST — where *must* disappears

***must* has no past tense.** Past obligation → **had to** (for everyone, all meanings).

| Present | Past |
|---|---|
| I **must** / **have to** leave early. | I **had to** leave early. |
| He **mustn't** smoke here. | He **wasn't allowed to** smoke here. *(mustn't has no clean past)* |
| You **don't have to** pay. | You **didn't have to** pay. |

### 🔬 The famous pair: *needn't have done* vs *didn't need to do*

This subtle contrast separates B2 from C1.

```
 didn't need to do   →  it was NOT necessary.  (we usually imply: so you probably didn't do it)
 needn't have done   →  you DID it, but it was a WASTE — it wasn't necessary after all.
```

| Sentence | What actually happened | 🇦🇿 |
|---|---|---|
| You **didn't need to cook** — we ate out. | It wasn't necessary; (likely) you didn't cook. | Bişirməyə ehtiyac yox idi. |
| You **needn't have cooked** — look, so much food is left! | You **did** cook, but it was unnecessary. | Bişirməyinə ehtiyac yox idi (amma bişirdin — boş yerə). |
| I **didn't need to take** an umbrella. | Not necessary; I probably left it home. | Çətir götürməyə ehtiyac yox idi. |
| I **needn't have taken** an umbrella — it didn't rain. | I **took** one, pointlessly. | Çətir götürmüşdüm — boş yerə (yağmadı). |

> 🧠 *needn't have + V3* = "you did a thing that turned out unnecessary" (often a gentle reproach or relief). → §B, §D.

## ⏩ THE FUTURE & questions

| Idea | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| future obligation | **will have to / 'll have to** | You'**ll have to** wait — the doctor's busy. |
| near/planned future | **am/is/are going to have to** | We'**re going to have to** sell the car. |
| asking about obligation | **Do I have to…? / Have I got to…?** | **Do I have to** sign here? |
| formal/emphatic question | **Must I…? / Need I…?** | **Must I** repeat everything? · **Need I** say more? |

> 🗣️ *"Must I?"* often carries reluctance or irritation: *"Must I really go to the dentist?"* (= I don't want to).

---

## 🧱 SEMI-MODALS of obligation

When you need a tense or form that *must* can't provide — or a particular shade of meaning:

| Semi-modal | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **be supposed to** | expected to (by rule/arrangement), but maybe not done | You'**re supposed to** wear a helmet. *(…though some don't)* |
| **be meant to** | (BrE) ≈ be supposed to | We'**re meant to** be there by eight. |
| **be required to** | formal obligation (rules, law) | Visitors **are required to** sign in. |
| **be obliged to / be forced to** | obligation under pressure | He **was obliged to** resign. |
| **be to** | official instruction / arrangement | All staff **are to** report to reception. |
| **had better** | strong advice with a warning *(→ Ch.21)* | You'**d better** apologise. |

> 🔎 **be supposed to** has a useful gap-between-rule-and-reality flavour: *"You're supposed to queue, but people push in."* → §B, §C.

## ⛔ COMMON MISTAKES (fix these now)

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| You ~~must to~~ go. | You **must** go. | *must* takes a bare infinitive (no *to*). |
| He ~~musts~~ leave. | He **must** leave. | modals never take **-s**. |
| I ~~will must~~ help. | I **will have to** help. | *must* has no future — use *have to*. |
| Yesterday I ~~must~~ work. | Yesterday I **had to** work. | *must* has no past — use *had to*. |
| You don't ~~must~~ shout. | You **mustn't** shout. *(or)* You **don't have to** shout. | "don't must" doesn't exist; pick the meaning. |
| You ~~mustn't~~ pay — it's free, but you can tip. | You **don't have to** pay. | "optional", not "forbidden". |
| She ~~needn't to~~ come. | She **needn't** come. / She **doesn't need to** come. | modal *need* takes no *to*. |
| ~~Do I must~~ sign? | **Do I have to** sign? / **Must I** sign? | don't mix *do* with *must*. |

## 🅰️ §A — LEVEL DIFFERENCES (A1 → C2)

How the *same idea* — "it's necessary" — grows with your level:

| Level | What the learner can do | Typical sentence |
|---|---|---|
| **A1** | basic *must / have to* for rules | "I **must** go." / "I **have to** work." |
| **A2** | negative split begins; *don't have to* | "You **don't have to** come." / "You **mustn't** be late." |
| **B1** | past *had to*; future *will have to*; questions | "We **had to** wait." / "**Do** I **have to** pay?" |
| **B2** | *must* vs *have to* (source); *needn't*; *be supposed to* | "I **have to** by law, but I also feel I **must**." |
| **C1** | *needn't have* vs *didn't need to*; *be to / be obliged to* | "You **needn't have** worried." / "Staff **are to** report by nine." |
| **C2** | nuance, register, irony, emphatic *must* in writing | "One **must** suppose he meant well." / "You really **needn't** have." |

> 🇦🇿 *A1: sadə must/have to. B2: mənbə fərqi və needn't. C1: needn't have vs didn't need to. C2: üslub və incəliklər.*

## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT

1. **Affirmative *must* is fading in speech.** Native speakers mostly say *have to / have got to / gotta* for everyday necessity. *Must* survives in: written rules ("Candidates must…"), strong personal urges ("I must say…", "You must try this!"), and deductions (Ch.19). 🇦🇿 *Danışıqda təsdiq "must" azalır; "have to/gotta" üstünlük təşkil edir.*
2. **"You must come round sometime!"** is a *friendly invitation*, not an order. Tone turns obligation into warmth. 🇦🇿 *Bu, əmr yox, mehriban dəvətdir.*
3. **"I must admit / I must say / I must confess"** — fixed softeners that introduce an honest opinion. 🇦🇿 *Fikir bildirməzdən əvvəl işlədilən hazır ifadələrdir.*
4. **"You didn't have to do that!"** said about a gift = *touched, grateful* ("how kind!"), not a complaint. 🇦🇿 *Hədiyyə üçün deyilərsə — minnətdarlıq bildirir.*
5. **"needn't have"** often expresses **relief or mild regret** about wasted effort: "I needn't have rushed." 🇦🇿 *Boş yerə görülmüş işə görə rahatlıq və ya yüngül təəssüf bildirir.*

## 🅲 §C — ALTERNATIVE EXPRESSIONS (say it another way)

| Instead of… | You can say… | Shade |
|---|---|---|
| You **must** finish today. | You'**ve got to** / It'**s essential that** you finish today. | informal / formal |
| You **have to** wear a badge. | You'**re required to** / It'**s compulsory to** wear a badge. | formal, official |
| You **mustn't** enter. | Entry **is prohibited.** / You'**re not allowed to** enter. | sign / spoken |
| You **don't have to** decide now. | There'**s no need to** / You'**re under no obligation to** decide now. | neutral / formal |
| I **must** apologise. | I **owe you an apology.** / I **feel I should** apologise. | softer |
| We **had to** cancel. | We **were forced to** / We **had no choice but to** cancel. | emphatic |

# 📖 MINI STORY #1 — "The New Job"
### *Obligation, prohibition and freedom — all in one first day at work.*
### 🇦🇿 *İşin ilk günü — məcburiyyət, qadağa və azadlıq, hamısı bir yerdə.*

---

On her first morning, Leyla was nervous. The manager handed her a thick booklet.

"A few rules," he said. "You **have to** clock in by nine — that's company policy. You **must** wear your ID badge at all times; security insists. And you **mustn't** share your password with anyone. Ever."

Leyla nodded, scribbling notes.

"Now, the relaxed part," he smiled. "You **don't have to** wear a suit — smart casual is fine. You **needn't** answer emails after six; we respect your evenings. And lunch? You **don't have to** eat at your desk. In fact, you **mustn't** — we want people to take a real break."

By Friday, Leyla had settled in. She'd **had to** stay late twice, but she **hadn't had to** work the weekend. She realised she **needn't have** been so nervous on Monday; everyone was kind.

As she left, her manager called out: "Next month you'**ll have to** train the new intern — but that's a good sign. It means we trust you."

Leyla grinned. She **didn't have to** be told twice that she'd done well.

## 🅴1 — The OBLIGATION STRENGTH SCALE

Necessity isn't on/off — it has degrees, just like the certainty scale in Ch.19:

```
 STRONGER ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────► WEAKER
  must / have to   >   be required to   >   be supposed to   >   should / ought to   >   could
  "no choice"          "official rule"      "expected (but…)"     "good idea"          "one option"
  🚦 obligation                                                    💡 advice (Ch.21)
```

| Strength | Modal | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 100% — no choice | must / have to | You **must** have a visa. |
| official requirement | be required to | You **are required to** declare cash over €10,000. |
| expected, gap possible | be supposed to | You'**re supposed to** book ahead. *(many don't)* |
| recommended | should / ought to | You **should** book ahead. *(advice — Ch.21)* |

> 🔗 This chapter owns the **left** half (true obligation); **Chapter 21** owns the **right** half (advice). The middle (*be supposed to*) is shared.

---

## 🅴2 — *must* vs *have to*: the cases that fool people

The internal/external rule is the start. Four refinements:

1. **Questions almost always use *have to*.** *"Do I have to?"* is natural; *"Must I?"* sounds formal/reluctant.
2. **Negatives split** (the whole reason for §⚠️): *mustn't* ≠ *don't have to*. *must* simply **has no plain negative meaning "no need"**.
3. **Reported & past → *have to*.** *"I must go"* → *"She said she **had to** go."* (not "musted").
4. **Headlines / official tone → *must*.** *"Applicants must be 18+."* on a form; in chat you'd say *"You've gotta be 18."*

| Situation | Natural choice | 🇦🇿 |
|---|---|---|
| a felt, personal need | **must** | I **must** get fit. |
| a rule someone else made | **have to** | I **have to** wear a hard hat. |
| asking about a rule | **Do I have to?** | Mən bunu etməliyəm? |
| official written rule | **must** | Visitors **must** sign in. |

## 🅴3 — The *have to / have got to / gotta* register ladder

| Register | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| formal / written | must · have to | Candidates **must** arrive early. |
| neutral spoken | have to | I **have to** go now. |
| informal spoken | have got to | I'**ve got to** go now. |
| very casual / fast speech | gotta | I **gotta** go. |

> ⚠️ *gotta* is a **spelling of pronunciation** — fine in texts and dialogue, **never** in essays or formal email.
> 🇦🇿 *gotta yalnız tələffüzü əks etdirir — rəsmi yazıda işlədilmir.*

---

## 🅴4 — ⚠️ *needn't have done* vs *didn't need to do* (the C1 splitter)

A timeline makes the difference visible:

```
 needn't have done:   ❶ you DID the action  →  ❷ later it proved unnecessary  →  😅 wasted effort
 didn't need to do:   ❶ it WASN'T necessary →  ❷ (so you usually didn't do it) →  🆗 no waste
```

| Sentence | Did the action happen? | Feeling |
|---|---|---|
| You **needn't have** paid. | **Yes** — and it was unnecessary | regret / "oh, you shouldn't have" |
| You **didn't need to** pay. | Probably **no** — it wasn't required | neutral fact |
| I **needn't have** hurried. | I **did** hurry — pointlessly | relief / mild annoyance |
| I **didn't need to** hurry. | I **didn't** hurry — no need | neutral |

> 🧪 **Test:** Can you add "…but I did it anyway"? → **needn't have**. Can you add "…so I didn't"? → **didn't need to**.
> 🇦🇿 *needn't have = iş görülüb, amma lazımsız çıxıb. didn't need to = lazım deyildi (deməli, çox vaxt görülməyib).*

---

## 🅴5 — *be supposed to*: the gap between rule and reality

This semi-modal carries a built-in *"…but it doesn't always happen"* flavour.

| Use | Example | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| expected by rule/arrangement | You'**re supposed to** wear a seatbelt. | …though some people don't |
| what's generally believed | It'**s supposed to** be the best café in town. | people say so |
| **past expectation NOT met** | We **were supposed to** meet at six. | …but we didn't |
| reproach | You **weren't supposed to** tell her! | but you did |

> 🧠 *was/were supposed to* + (it didn't happen) is one of the most useful "real-life" structures: plans that fell through.
> 🇦🇿 *was supposed to = nəzərdə tutulmuşdu (amma olmadı).*

---

## 🅴6 — *be to* — the official, the arranged, the fated

*be + to + infinitive* is a compact, rather formal way to give instructions or state arrangements:

| Use | Example | 🇦🇿 |
|---|---|---|
| official instruction / order | All staff **are to** report to HR. | Bütün işçilər HR-a getməlidir. |
| formal arrangement / schedule | The President **is to** visit Baku in May. | Prezident may ayında Bakıya gələcək. |
| prohibition (negative) | You **are not to** leave this room. | Bu otağı tərk etməməlisən. |
| condition of success (*if … is to*) | If we **are to** win, we must train harder. | Qalib gəlmək istəyiriksə, daha çox məşq etməliyik. |
| news / headlines | *Talks **are to** resume next week.* | Danışıqlar gələn həftə bərpa olunacaq. |

> 🔎 *be to* feels like a notice or a news report. In speech you'd usually use *have to / will / is going to*.

---

## 🅴7 — The PROHIBITION family (🔴 ways to say "don't!")

| Form | Strength / register | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **mustn't** | strong, personal/rule | You **mustn't** lie to me. |
| **can't** | spoken, "not allowed" | You **can't** park here. |
| **be not allowed to / be not permitted to** | rule-based | Phones **aren't allowed** in the exam. |
| **be prohibited / forbidden** | formal, written | Smoking **is prohibited.** |
| **be not supposed to** | softer — "shouldn't really" | You'**re not supposed to** feed the animals. |
| **be not to** | formal order | Visitors **are not to** touch the exhibits. |

> ⚠️ All of these are 🔴 (forbidden). Keep them apart from ⚪ *don't have to / needn't* (optional). → §⚠️.

---

## 🅴8 — QUESTIONS of obligation (+ short answers)

| Question | Register | Short answers |
|---|---|---|
| **Do I have to** sign? | neutral, most common | Yes, you do. / No, you don't (have to). |
| **Have I got to** sign? | informal (BrE) | Yes, you have. / No, you haven't. |
| **Must I** sign? | formal / reluctant | Yes, you must. / No, you needn't. |
| **Need I** sign? | formal | Yes, you must / need to. / No, you needn't. |

> 🗣️ Note the **mismatched answers**: a *"Must I…?"* question is often answered with *"No, you needn't / don't have to"* — because *mustn't* would change the meaning to "forbidden". → §⚠️.

## 🅴9 — Obligation in REPORTED SPEECH

| Direct | Reported |
|---|---|
| "You **must** finish today." | She said I **had to / must** finish that day. |
| "I **have to** leave." | He said he **had to** leave. |
| "You **mustn't** tell anyone." | She warned me I **mustn't / wasn't to** tell anyone. |
| "You **needn't** wait." | He said I **needn't / didn't have to** wait. |

> 🔑 *have to* backshifts cleanly to *had to*. *must* (obligation) usually → *had to*; but *mustn't* (prohibition) often **stays** *mustn't*. → cross-ref Reported Speech chapter.

---

## 🅴10 — Emphatic & idiomatic *must / need*

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **I must say / I must admit** | softener before an opinion | I **must say**, that was excellent. |
| **If you must know** | reluctant reveal | **If you must know**, I was late because I overslept. |
| **must-have / must-see / must-read** | (adj.) essential thing | This app is a **must-have**. |
| **a must** | (noun) something essential | Comfortable shoes are **a must**. |
| **Must you…?** | irritation | **Must you** hum like that? |
| **Needs must** | "no choice, in hard times" | I'd rather not, but **needs must**. |
| **if need be** | if necessary | I'll stay late **if need be**. |

> 🇦🇿 *Bu hazır ifadələr danışıqda çox işlədilir — must/need-in idiomatik üzləri.*

## 🅴11 — OBLIGATION ACROSS ALL TENSES (master table)

| Time | 🟢 necessary | 🔴 forbidden | ⚪ optional |
|---|---|---|---|
| **present** | must / have to | mustn't / can't | don't have to / needn't |
| **past** | had to | wasn't allowed to / couldn't | didn't have to / needn't have* |
| **future** | will have to / 'll need to | won't be allowed to / mustn't | won't have to / needn't |
| **present perfect** | have had to | haven't been allowed to | haven't had to |
| **with modals (infinitive)** | to have to | not to be allowed to | not to have to |

> \* *needn't have done* = past **unnecessary action that still happened** (§E4) — a special case, not a plain past of *needn't*.
> 🧠 Notice the empty cell where *must* "should" be in past/future: **there isn't one.** *have to* fills every gap.

## 🅴12 — 🇦🇿 CONTRASTIVE SYNTHESIS

| English | 🇦🇿 | Watch out |
|---|---|---|
| You **must / have to** go. | Getməlisən. | both = "lazımdır" |
| You **mustn't** go. | Getməməlisən (qadağa). | 🔴 forbidden |
| You **don't have to** go. | Getməyə məcbur deyilsən. | ⚪ optional — **not** the same as above! |
| I **had to** go. | Getməli oldum. | past = had to |
| I **must have** gone *(Ch.19!)* | Yəqin getmişəm. | this is **deduction**, not obligation |
| You **needn't have** gone. | Getməyinə ehtiyac yox idi (amma getdin). | wasted action |

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani overlap trap:** *-malı/-məli* covers both *must* and *have to*, and *-mamalı/-məməli* gets used for **both** *mustn't* and *don't have to*. English **forces** you to choose: is it 🔴 forbidden or ⚪ optional? Always ask that question before you translate.

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "The Border Crossing"
### *Rules, bans and free choices — a journey told through obligation.*
### 🇦🇿 *Qaydalar, qadağalar və sərbəst seçimlər — məcburiyyət üzərindən danışılan səfər.*

> Read it twice. **First** for the story; **second** circling every *must · have to · mustn't · don't have to · needn't · had to.*
> 🇦🇿 *İki dəfə oxu: əvvəl hekayə üçün, sonra hər modal feli dövrəyə alaraq.*

---

Murad reached the border at dawn. A long queue had already formed.

A guard handed him a form. "You **have to** fill this in before the window," she said. "Everyone **must** show a passport — no exceptions. And you **mustn't** cross the white line until your number is called."

Murad's stomach tightened. He'd heard the customs officers were strict.

"Do I **have to** declare my laptop?" he asked.

"No, personal items are fine — you **don't have to** declare those. But you **mustn't** bring fresh fruit across; that's banned. And you **needn't** open every bag — only the one we point to."

The wait was long. He **had to** stand for two hours, and twice he **had to** show his ticket. By the time he reached the booth, he was sure something would go wrong.

It didn't. The officer stamped his passport in ten seconds and waved him through.

"You **needn't have** worried," laughed the traveller behind him, an old man with a kind face. "First time? Everyone thinks they'**ll have to** explain their whole life. You **don't have to** say a word if your papers are in order."

Murad smiled, relieved. He **needn't have** packed so carefully, **needn't have** rehearsed his answers, **needn't have** lost a night's sleep.

As he stepped into the new country, the old man called after him: "Next time you'**ll have to** teach *someone else* not to worry!"

## 🏁 End of Chapter 20

You can now handle the **three doors** of necessity. You say something is **necessary** with *must /
have to / have got to / need to*; you mark **who imposes it** (*must* = me / the speaker; *have to* =
the outside world); you **forbid** with *mustn't*; and you grant **freedom** with *don't have to /
needn't*. You borrow from *have to* whenever *must* runs out of forms — **had to** (past), **will have
to** (future) — and you can split the tricky past pair **needn't have done** (you did it, pointlessly)
from **didn't need to do** (it wasn't necessary). Semi-modals (*be supposed to / required to / be to*)
and the four standard sections complete the picture. 🚦

```
 ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ 🟢 necessary:  must / have to / 've got to / need to                   │
 │ 🔴 forbidden:  mustn't  (= DON'T do it)                                │
 │ ⚪ optional:    don't have to / needn't  (= it's up to you)             │
 │ ⚠️ mustn't ≠ don't have to   ·   no "must to / musts / will must"      │
 │ PAST: had to · needn't have done (did it) vs didn't need to (no need)  │
 └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
