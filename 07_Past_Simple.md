## 1. 🟢 Introduction

Welcome to **Part 2 — the Past**. The **Past Simple** is the tense you'll use most when
you **tell stories, give news about the past, and talk about your life**. It describes an
action that **started and finished in the past** — it is **over, done, complete**.

```
   Present Simple:  I work in Baku.          → now / always
   Past Simple:     I worked in Baku (2019). → finished, in the past
```

The form is the **second form of the verb (V2)**:
- **Regular:** add **-ed** → *work → work**ed***, *play → play**ed***.
- **Irregular:** the word changes → *go → **went***, *see → **saw***, *eat → **ate***.

> 💡 **Big idea:** Past Simple = **"finished in the past, often with a time word"**
> (yesterday, last week, in 2010, two days ago). The action has **no connection to now**.

---

## 2. ❓ Why Do We Use This Tense?

Four main reasons:

| # | Use | Example | Azərbaycanca |
|---|-----|---------|--------------|
| 1 | **Finished action at a specific past time** | I **bought** a car last week. | konkret keçmiş hərəkət |
| 2 | **A sequence of past events** (storytelling) | She **woke up**, **dressed**, and **left**. | ardıcıl hadisələr |
| 3 | **Past habits / repeated past actions** | We **played** outside every day as kids. | keçmiş vərdiş |
| 4 | **Past states / facts (no longer true)** | He **lived** in London for years. | keçmiş vəziyyət |

> 📌 If there is a **finished-time word** (yesterday, ago, in 2015, last night) → it's
> almost always the **Past Simple**.

## 4. 📅 Timeline

The action happens at a **finished point (or period) in the past** — a closed dot, with
**no line to now**.

```
   PAST                                  NOW                FUTURE
 ────┼────────X───────────────────────────┼───────────────────┼────►
            (yesterday /                  │
             last week /                   │
             in 2019)                      │
        "I visited Rome in 2019."  → done, closed, no connection to NOW
```

> Compare with the **Present Perfect** (a bridge to now). The Past Simple **cuts the
> bridge** — it lives entirely **in the past**. ✂️🌉

## 5. 🧮 Formula

Model verbs: **work** (regular → V2 = worked) and **go** (irregular → V2 = went).
Helper for negatives/questions: **did** (the past of *do*).

### ➕ Positive
```
   S  +  V2          (same form for ALL subjects — no -s, no changes!)
```
| Subject | V2 | Example |
|---------|----|---------|
| I / You / He / She / It / We / They | **V2** | I **worked**. / She **went**. / They **ate**. |

> 🎉 **Good news:** in the positive, the verb is the **same for everybody** — no third-person -s!

### ➖ Negative
```
   S  +  did not (didn't)  +  V1     ← verb goes BACK to base form!
```
| Subject | did not | V1 | Example |
|---------|---------|----|---------|
| all subjects | **did not (didn't)** | V1 | I **didn't work**. / She **didn't go**. |

> ⚠️ **Golden rule:** after **did/didn't**, the verb returns to **V1**.
> ✅ *She **didn't go*** — ❌ *She didn't **went***.

### ❓ Question
```
   Did  +  S  +  V1  ?
```
- **Did** you **work**? · **Did** she **go**? · **Did** they **eat**?

### ❓➖ Negative Question
```
   Didn't  +  S  +  V1  ?   (= surprise / checking)
```
- **Didn't** you **see** it? *(I thought you did!)* · **Didn't** he **call**?

### ✔️ Short Answers
| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Did you work? | Yes, I **did**. | No, I **didn't**. |
| Did she go? | Yes, she **did**. | No, she **didn't**. |

> ⚠️ Short answers use **did**, never the main verb: ❌ *Yes, I worked.* → ✅ *Yes, I **did**.*

---

## 6. 🟦 The Verb "to be" in the Past — was / were (SPECIAL!)

*to be* does **NOT** use *did*. It changes to **was / were** and makes negatives/questions
**by itself**.

| Subject | Positive | Negative | Question |
|---------|----------|----------|----------|
| I / He / She / It | **was** | wasn't | **Was** I…? |
| You / We / They | **were** | weren't | **Were** you…? |

```
   I was tired.        I wasn't tired.        Was I tired?
   They were late.     They weren't late.     Were they late?
```

> ⚠️ ❌ *Did you was…?* → ✅ *Were you…?* — **never** mix *did* with *was/were*.
> 🇦🇿 *"idim/idin/idi"* → *was/were.*

## 7. 🏗️ Sentence Structure

```
 POSITIVE   ┌─────┬──────────┬─────────┐
            │  S  │   V2     │ rest... │
            │ She │  went    │ home.   │
            └─────┴──────────┴─────────┘

 NEGATIVE   ┌─────┬───────────┬──────┬───────┐
            │  S  │ did not   │  V1  │ rest  │
            │ She │ didn't    │ go   │ home. │
            └─────┴───────────┴──────┴───────┘

 QUESTION   ┌─────┬─────┬──────┬─────────┐
            │ Did │  S  │  V1  │ rest ?  │
            │ Did │ she │ go   │ home?   │
            └─────┴─────┴──────┴─────────┘
```

## 8. 📜 Grammar Rules

1. **Positive:** use **V2** for every subject (no -s, no helper).
2. **Negative & question:** use **did/didn't + V1** — the main verb loses its past form.
   *She went* → *She **didn't go*** → ***Did** she go?*
3. **"to be"** uses **was/were** (no *did*).
4. **Use a finished-time word** (yesterday, ago, last…, in + year) — or the past time is
   clear from context.
5. **Modal-like "could"** is the past of *can*: *I **could** swim at five.*
6. Don't confuse V2 and V3: Past Simple uses **V2** (*I **saw***), Present Perfect uses
   **V3** (*I have **seen***).

---

## 9. ✏️ Spelling Rules (adding -ed to regular verbs)

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs → **+ed** | work → worked, play → played |
| Ends in **-e** → **+d** | live → lived, like → liked |
| Consonant + **-y** → *y → ied* | study → stud**ied**, try → tr**ied** |
| Vowel + **-y** → just **+ed** | play → played, enjoy → enjoyed |
| Short verb (1 vowel + 1 consonant, stressed) → **double** | stop → sto**pp**ed, plan → pla**nn**ed |
| Ends in **-l** (British) → often double | travel → trave**ll**ed |

```
   study → studied   (consonant + y)
   stop  → stopped   (double p)
   live  → lived     (+d only)
   play  → played    (vowel + y)
```

---

## 10. 🔊 Spelling vs. Sound — The 3 Sounds of -ed

The **-ed** ending has **three** pronunciations: /t/, /d/, /ɪd/.

| Sound | When (base verb ends in…) | Examples |
|-------|---------------------------|----------|
| **/t/** | voiceless sound: p, k, f, s, sh, ch | worked /wɜːkt/, stopped /stɒpt/, washed /wɒʃt/ |
| **/d/** | voiced sound or vowel: b, g, l, n, r, vowels | played /pleɪd/, lived /lɪvd/, called /kɔːld/ |
| **/ɪd/** | **t** or **d** (adds a syllable!) | wanted /ˈwɒntɪd/, needed /ˈniːdɪd/, started /ˈstɑːtɪd/ |

```
   worked → /t/   (one syllable)
   played → /d/   (one syllable)
   wanted → /ɪd/  (TWO syllables: wan-tid)
```

> 🗣️ **Only /ɪd/ adds a syllable.** Say: *worked · played · wanted* and feel the difference.


## 12. ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| She **didn't went**. | She **didn't go**. | after *did*, use V1 |
| **Did** you **saw** it? | **Did** you **see** it? | after *did*, use V1 |
| I **goed** to school. | I **went** to school. | *go* is irregular → went |
| He **was went** home. | He **went** home. | don't add *was* to V2 |
| **Did** you **was** there? | **Were** you there? | *be* uses was/were, not *did* |
| They **was** happy. | They **were** happy. | they → *were* |
| I **have seen** him yesterday. | I **saw** him yesterday. | finished time → Past Simple |

> 📌 **Top traps:** (1) keeping the past form after *did* (*didn't went*), and
> (2) making irregular verbs "regular" (*goed, eated, buyed*). Memorize irregulars! 🧠

## 13. ⚖️ Comparison with Similar Tenses

### 🆚 Past Simple vs. Present Perfect (recap from Chapter 05)

| | **Past Simple** | **Present Perfect** |
|--|-----------------|--------------------|
| **Time** | finished, specific | unfinished / connected to now |
| **Words** | yesterday, ago, last, in 2010 | ever, never, just, yet, for, since |
| **Example** | I **lost** my keys **yesterday**. | I**'ve lost** my keys. *(still lost now)* |
| **Focus** | **when** it happened | the **result now** |

```
   "I saw that film last night."    → Past Simple (finished time)
   "I've seen that film."           → Present Perfect (experience, no time)
```

### 🆚 Past Simple vs. Past Continuous (preview — Chapter 08)

| Past Simple | Past Continuous |
|-------------|-----------------|
| completed action | action in progress in the past |
| *I **cooked** dinner.* | *I **was cooking** dinner (when he called).* |

> 🔎 The Past Simple often interrupts the Past Continuous — full detail in **Chapter 08**.

## 14. 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani Translation Tips

1. **Past Simple ≈ Azerbaijani qəti keçmiş "-dı/-di/-du/-dü":**
   - *I **went*** → *Mən get**dim***
   - *She **saw*** → *O gör**dü***
   - This mapping is usually **easy and direct** — good news!

2. **⚠️ The helper "did" has NO Azerbaijani word** (like "do/does"). In questions and
   negatives, English uses *did + V1*, but Azerbaijani just changes the verb ending.
   - *Did you go?* → *Getdin?* (no separate word for "did").

3. **was/were = idim/idin/idi/idik/idiniz/idilər:**
   - *I **was** a student* → *Mən tələbə **idim**.*

4. **Irregular verbs are the real work.** Azerbaijani has no V2/V3 split — you must
   **memorize** English irregular forms (Chapter 45).

> 💡 **Strategy:** when you see *yesterday / last / ago / in [year]* → switch your brain to
> **Past Simple** automatically.

## 26. ✍️ Writing Tips

- The Past Simple is the **main tense of storytelling and reports**.
- Keep it consistent within a narrative; don't drift into the present.
- Use **sequence words** (first, then, after that, finally) to link events.
- Switch to **Past Continuous** for background/interrupted actions (Chapter 08).

---

## 27. 🎧 Listening Tips

- The **-ed** ending is quick — listen for *wanted* (/ɪd/, two syllables) vs *worked* (/t/).
- *didn't* is one fast beat; the main verb after it is **base form**.
- *was/were* are usually **weak**: /wəz/, /wə/ — easy to miss.

## 28. ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: When do I use Past Simple vs. Present Perfect?**
Past Simple = **finished time** (yesterday, ago, in 2010). Present Perfect = **no time /
connected to now**.

**Q2: Why is "She didn't went" wrong?**
*did* already carries the past, so the main verb returns to **V1**: *didn't go.*

**Q3: How do I know a verb's V2?**
Regular → +ed. Irregular → memorize (Chapter 45). When unsure, check the list.

**Q4: Do I use "did" with "was/were"?**
**No.** *to be* makes its own negatives/questions: *wasn't / weren't / Was…? / Were…?*

**Q5: Is "used to" the same as Past Simple?**
*used to* stresses a **past habit/state that stopped** (Chapter 23). *I used to smoke* ≈
*I smoked (regularly) but not now.*

# 📚 Mini Story — *"The Day Everything Changed"* (≈320 words)

> Almost every verb is **Past Simple**. Notice the sequence of events and the **was/were**
> for states. Read aloud.

It **was** a cold morning in November. Kamran **woke up** early, **made** a cup of coffee,
and **sat** by the window. He **felt** strange — something **was** different, but he
**didn't know** what.

At eight o'clock, the phone **rang**. It **was** his old friend, Tural. "You won't believe
it," Tural **said**. "I **found** your grandfather's letters in the old house!"

Kamran's heart **jumped**. His grandfather **died** ten years ago, and the family
**thought** they **lost** everything from him. He **grabbed** his coat, **locked** the
door, and **drove** straight to the old house.

When he **arrived**, Tural **was** waiting outside. They **went** in together. The house
**smelled** of dust and memories. In a wooden box, they **found** a pile of yellow
envelopes. Kamran **opened** the first one carefully. His hands **shook**.

The letters **told** a story nobody **knew**. His grandfather **wrote** about the war, about
love, about a promise he **made** to a woman he never **forgot**. Kamran **read** for hours.
He **laughed**, and then he **cried**.

"He **was** a poet," Kamran **whispered**. "I never **realised**."

That afternoon, they **photographed** every letter. They **didn't want** to lose them
again. Kamran **felt** closer to his grandfather than ever before, even though they **never**
truly **spoke** as adults.

Before he **left**, Kamran **stood** in the empty room and **said** a quiet "thank you."
The morning **started** like any other — but it **became** the day everything **changed**.

He **drove** home slowly. The sky **turned** orange, and for the first time in years, he
**understood** where he **came** from. He **didn't** feel alone anymore.

Some doors, he **thought**, **open** only when you stop looking for them.

### 🔎 Story Notes — Spot the Grammar
- **Regular V2 (-ed):** *made (irreg.), opened, laughed, cried, photographed, started, turned, realised.*
- **Irregular V2:** *woke, sat, felt, rang, was, said, found, died, thought, grabbed (reg.), drove, went, told, wrote, read, stood, became, understood, came.*
- **was/were (states):** *It was cold · Tural was waiting · He was a poet.*
- **Negatives (didn't + V1):** *didn't know · didn't want · didn't feel.*
- **Notice:** the whole story is a **sequence of finished past actions** — that's the heart of the Past Simple.

# 🧠 Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

1. **"DID eats the past."** 🍽️ When *did* appears, it "eats" the past form — the verb
   returns to **V1**: *She went → Did she **go**? / She didn't **go**.* (Just like "DOES
   eats the S" in Chapter 03.)

2. **One form for everyone (positive):** the Past Simple positive is the **same for I, you,
   he, we, they** — no -s to worry about. 🎉

3. **was = I/he/she/it · were = you/we/they.** Mnemonic: *"**WE WERE** there."* (we → were).

4. **-ed sound trick:** only **t/d verbs** get the extra "-id" syllable (*wanted, needed*).
   Everything else is a quick /t/ or /d/.

5. **Irregulars in families:** *think→thought, bring→brought, buy→bought, catch→caught,
   teach→taught* — the **"-ought/-aught" gang.** Learn them together. 🧠

## E1. 🎯 Wh- Questions: Subject vs. Object (the "no DID" case)

Just like the Present Simple (Ch.3 E2), when the **question word is the subject**, we do
**NOT** use *did*. We keep the **V2** form.

```
   OBJECT question (normal):   What  DID  you break?     → did + V1
   SUBJECT question (special): Who   broke the window?   → V2, NO did
```

| Type | Question | Why |
|------|----------|-----|
| Subject Q | **Who called** you? | "Who" = subject → no *did*, keep V2 |
| Subject Q | **What happened**? | "What" = subject → no *did* |
| Subject Q | **Who won** the match? | no *did*, keep V2 |
| Object Q | **Who did** you **call**? | "Who" = object → use *did* |
| Object Q | **What did** he **say**? | "What" = object → use *did* |

> 🧠 **Trick:** If the answer is *"Ali did."* / *"My brother did."* → it's a **subject
> question** (no *did* in the question, verb stays V2). *"Who broke it?" → "Ali did."*

---

## E2. 🌀 Past Form ≠ Past Time! (Unreal / Hypothetical Present)

**Surprise rule:** English uses the **past form** to talk about **unreal or imaginary
present/future** situations — NOT the past. This is huge for B2+.

| Structure | Example | Meaning (it's about NOW, not the past!) |
|-----------|---------|------------------------------------------|
| **I wish + past** | I **wish** I **knew** the answer. | I don't know it now (regret) |
| **If only + past** | **If only** he **were** here. | he isn't here now |
| **Second conditional (if + past → would)** | If I **had** money, I**'d** travel. | I don't have money now |
| **It's time + past** | It's time we **left**. | we should leave now |
| **Would rather + past** | I**'d rather** you **didn't** smoke. | I prefer you don't (now) |
| **as if / as though + past** | He acts **as if** he **owned** the place. | he doesn't own it |

```
   "I wish I had a car."     → past form "had" — but it means NOW (I don't have one).
   "If I were you..."        → "were" for unreal present (not real past).
```

> ⚠️ In careful/formal English, use **were** (not *was*) for all subjects in unreal
> sentences: *If I **were** you · I wish she **were** here.* (*was* is common in casual speech.)
> 🔎 Full treatment in **Chapter 24 — Conditionals**.

## E3. 💪 could vs. was/were able to (past ability)

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **could** | general past ability (could do it anytime) | I **could** swim when I was five. |
| **couldn't** | general past inability (both single & general) | I **couldn't** find my keys. |
| **was/were able to** | a **specific** success on **one** occasion | The exit was blocked, but we **were able to** escape. |
| **managed to** | specific success (with effort) | She **managed to** finish on time. |

```
   ✅ I could play piano as a child.          (general ability — could is fine)
   ❌ Yesterday the test was hard but I could pass it.
   ✅ Yesterday the test was hard but I was able to / managed to pass it.  (one occasion)
```

> 🧠 **Rule:** For **one specific achievement** in the past, prefer **was able to / managed
> to**, not *could*. (But **couldn't** is fine for both.)

## E4. 🔁 Past Habits: Past Simple vs. used to vs. would (preview)

Three ways to express repeated past actions (full lesson in **Chapter 23**):

| Form | Example | Note |
|------|---------|------|
| **Past Simple** (+ frequency) | We **often played** outside. | neutral |
| **used to + V1** | We **used to play** outside. | habit/state that **stopped** |
| **would + V1** | We **would play** outside for hours. | repeated **actions** only (NOT states) |

```
   ✅ I used to live in Baku.   (past state — used to OK)
   ❌ I would live in Baku.      (state → "would" is wrong here)
   ✅ Every summer, we would visit the sea.  (repeated action → would OK)
```

> ⚠️ **would** works for repeated **actions**, but **not** for **states** (*be, have, like,
> know*). For states, use **used to**.

## E5. ⏱️ Past Simple in Time Clauses (sequence)

After *when, after, before, as soon as, until, by the time*, the Past Simple shows the
**order** of past events.

| Example | Order |
|---------|-------|
| **When** the film **ended**, we **left**. | film ended → then left |
| **After** I **ate**, I **washed** the dishes. | ate → then washed |
| **As soon as** she **arrived**, she **called** me. | arrived → immediately called |
| I **waited until** he **came**. | waiting up to his arrival |

> 💡 Compare with **Past Perfect** (Ch.9), which makes the **earlier** action clearer:
> *"When we arrived, the film **had** already **started**."* (started before we arrived.)

12. The door was locked, but we ___ open it with a key. (one occasion)
13. I ___ find my phone anywhere. (inability)
14. It was tough, but she ___ finish the race. (one achievement)

### L) Past habit — used to / would / both?
15. We ___ (live) by the sea. (state → used to)
16. Every winter, we ___ (build) a snowman. (action → both)
17. He ___ (have) long hair. (state → used to)

### M) Time clauses — put both verbs in the Past Simple
18. As soon as the bell ___ (ring), the students ___ (leave).
19. After she ___ (finish), she ___ (go) home.

### N) Tag questions — add the correct tag
20. You saw him, ___?
21. They didn't come, ___?
22. It was expensive, ___?

### ✅ Expansion Answer Key

**I)** 1. broke *(subject — no did)* 2. did … do 3. did … invite 4. happened *(subject)* 5. won *(subject)*

**J)** 6. knew 7. were 8. went 9. didn't tell 10. owned *(all unreal → past form)*

**K)** 11. could 12. were able to/managed to 13. couldn't 14. was able to/managed to

**L)** 15. used to live 16. used to / would build ✅✅ 17. used to have *(state → not "would")*

**M)** 18. rang … left 19. finished … went

**N)** 20. didn't you 21. did they 22. wasn't it

## E10. ⚠️ Extra Common Mistakes (Azerbaijani-Specific)

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| Who did break the window? | Who **broke** the window? | subject question → no *did* |
| I wish I have a car. | I wish I **had** a car. | unreal present → past form |
| If I would be you... | If I **were** you... | second conditional → *were*, no *would* in if-clause |

| There was many people. | There **were** many people. | plural → *were* |

## ⚖️ Special Set — Past Simple vs. Present Perfect (the key skill!)

| Past Simple (finished time) | Present Perfect (no time / connected to now) |
|-----------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| I **saw** that film **last night**. | I**'ve seen** that film. *(experience)* |
| She **lost** her keys **yesterday**. | She**'s lost** her keys. *(still lost now)* |
| We **moved** here **in 2018**. | We**'ve moved** house. *(we live elsewhere now)* |
| **Did** you **eat**? *(specific occasion)* | **Have** you **eaten**? *(at any time up to now)* |
| He **broke** his leg **last winter**. | He**'s broken** his leg. *(it's in a cast now)* |
| They **finished** the report **an hour ago**. | They**'ve finished** the report. *(it's done now)* |

> 🧠 Cover the right column; say each left sentence; then **remove the time word** and turn
> it into Present Perfect. This is the chapter's #1 self-test.

# 📖 Mini Story #2 — *"The Wrong Train"* (≈320 words)

> A short adventure — Past Simple is the natural tense of storytelling. Watch the sequence
> of finished events, the **was/were** for states, and the **didn't + V1** negatives.
> Read aloud.

It **happened** on a rainy Tuesday. Nigar **was** in a hurry. She **ran** to the station,
**jumped** on the first train, and **found** a seat by the window. She **felt** relieved.

But after ten minutes, something **seemed** wrong. The stations **looked** unfamiliar.
She **checked** the sign and **froze** — she **took** the wrong train! It **didn't go** to
the city; it **went** to the mountains.

She **didn't panic**. She **stayed** calm and **decided** to enjoy the mistake. The train
**climbed** higher, and the view **became** beautiful. Green hills **rolled** past, and a
river **shone** below.

An old man **sat** next to her. He **smiled** and **asked** where she **was** going.
"Nowhere, apparently," Nigar **laughed**. She **told** him the whole story. He **chuckled**
and **said**, "The best journeys **begin** by accident."

When the train **stopped** at a small village, the man **stood** up. "Come," he **said**.
"My daughter **owns** a café here. You **look** like you **need** good tea." Nigar
**hesitated**, then **followed** him.

The café **was** warm and full of laughter. The daughter **made** fresh bread and **poured**
strong tea. They **talked** for hours. Nigar **forgot** about her meeting, her stress, her
plans. For once, she **didn't check** her phone.

In the evening, she **caught** the train back. The rain **stopped**, and the sky **turned**
pink. She **realised** that the "wrong" train **gave** her the best day she **had** in
months.

The next morning, she **wrote** in her diary: *"Yesterday I **missed** my stop — and
**found** something better."*

She **never forgot** that village. And whenever life **felt** too planned, she **remembered**
the rainy Tuesday when one small mistake **opened** an unexpected door.

### 🇦🇿 Full Azerbaijani Translation — *"Səhv qatar"*

mindi və pəncərənin yanında bir yer tapdı. Özünü rahat hiss etdi.

Amma on dəqiqədən sonra nəsə səhv görünürdü. Stansiyalar tanış deyildi. O, lövhəni yoxladı
və donub qaldı — səhv qatara minmişdi! Qatar şəhərə getmirdi; dağlara gedirdi.

O, panika etmədi. Sakit qaldı və səhvdən həzz almaq qərarına gəldi. Qatar daha da yuxarı
qalxdı və mənzərə gözəlləşdi. Yaşıl təpələr keçib gedirdi və aşağıda bir çay parıldayırdı.

Qoca bir kişi onun yanında oturdu. Gülümsədi və hara getdiyini soruşdu. "Görünür, heç
yerə," — deyə Nigar güldü. Ona bütün hekayəni danışdı. Kişi qımışdı və dedi: "Ən yaxşı
səfərlər təsadüfən başlayır."

Qatar kiçik bir kənddə dayananda kişi ayağa qalxdı. "Gəl," — dedi. "Qızımın burada kafesi

### 🔎 Story Notes — Spot the Grammar
- **Irregular V2:** *ran, found, felt, took, went, became, shone, sat, told, said, stood, made, forgot, caught, gave, had, wrote, missed (reg.).*
- **Regular V2 (-ed):** *happened, jumped, checked, stayed, decided, climbed, rolled, smiled, asked, laughed, talked, poured, stopped, turned, realised, opened, remembered.*
- **was/were (states):** *She was in a hurry · The café was warm · the sky was pink.*
- **Negatives (didn't + V1):** *didn't go · didn't panic · didn't check.*
- **Notice:** the whole story is a **chain of finished past events** linked by sequence
  (*after ten minutes · when the train stopped · in the evening · the next morning*).
