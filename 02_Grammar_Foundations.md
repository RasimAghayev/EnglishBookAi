# 🧱 Grammar Foundations — The Toolbox

### *Everything you must know BEFORE the tenses*

> fel, tamamlıq, köməkçi fellər (be/do/have)** və ən vacibi — **felin formaları
> (V1/V2/V3/V-ing)**. Bu fəsil bütün kitabın açarıdır. Tələsmə, hər şeyi sadə izah edəcəyik.

---

## 1. 🧩 What Is a Sentence? — Subject + Verb (+ Object)

Every English sentence needs **two things minimum**: a **subject** and a **verb**.

```
        SUBJECT        VERB        OBJECT
          who?       does what?     what?
        ┌───────┐   ┌────────┐   ┌─────────┐
        │  She  │ → │  reads │ → │ a book  │
        └───────┘   └────────┘   └─────────┘
          (S)          (V)          (O)
```

| Part | Question it answers | Example |
|------|--------------------|---------|
| **Subject (S)** | Who/what does the action? | **She** reads a book. |
| **Verb (V)** | What is the action/state? | She **reads** a book. |
| **Object (O)** | Who/what receives the action? | She reads **a book**. |

> ⚠️ **Azerbaijani difference:** In Azerbaijani the verb usually comes **last**
> (*Mən **kitab oxuyuram*** = I a-book read). In English the verb comes **early**,
> right after the subject: ✅ *I **read** a book.*

> 📌 **The verb is the king of the sentence.** Tenses are all about **changing the verb.**

---

## 2. 👤 Subject Pronouns — Who Does the Action

Before verbs, memorize these. They decide the verb's form.

| Person | Singular | Azərbaycanca | Plural | Azərbaycanca |
|--------|----------|--------------|--------|--------------|
| 1st | **I** | mən | **we** | biz |
| 2nd | **you** | sən | **you** | siz |
| 3rd | **he / she / it** | o (kişi/qadın/əşya) | **they** | onlar |

> 📌 **The big one: "he / she / it"** is called the **third person singular**.
> This little group changes verbs the most (it adds **-s**: *he work**s***).
> Remember it as: **"He, She, It — add an S to fit."** 🧠

---

## 3. 🏃 Two Big Types of Verbs

### A) Action verbs (dynamic) — things you DO
*run, eat, write, call, build, study, jump, send…*

### B) State verbs (stative) — things you ARE / FEEL / THINK / OWN
*be, like, love, want, know, understand, need, believe, own, seem…*

```
 ACTION (you can see it)        STATE (you cannot see it)
 ───────────────────────        ─────────────────────────
   run, jump, write               know, love, want
   cook, drive, speak             believe, understand, own
```

> ⚠️ **Why this matters:** State verbs usually **do NOT** take the *-ing* form.
> ✅ *I **know** the answer.*  ❌ *I am knowing the answer.*
> We will return to this many times — for now, just notice the two groups.

---

## 4. 🛠️ Main Verbs vs. Auxiliary (Helping) Verbs

A sentence has one **main verb** (the real meaning). Sometimes it also has a small
**helping verb** (auxiliary) that adds grammar — like time, questions, or negatives.

```
        AUXILIARY        MAIN VERB
        (the helper)     (the meaning)
        ┌────────┐       ┌────────┐
   She  │   is   │   →   │ working│   right now.
        └────────┘       └────────┘
```

The three magic helping verbs of English tenses are:

| Auxiliary | Its main job in tenses | Azərbaycanca |
|-----------|------------------------|--------------|
| **be** (am/is/are/was/were) | builds **continuous** tenses + passive | davam edən hərəkət |
| **have** (have/has/had) | builds **perfect** tenses | bitmiş/nəticəli hərəkət |
| **do** (do/does/did) | builds **questions & negatives** in simple tenses | sual və inkar |

> 📌 **Master these three helpers and you have mastered half of English grammar.**

---

## 5. 🟦 The Verb "to be" — The Most Important Verb

*to be* means **"olmaq / var olmaq"** (am / is / are). It is special: it changes a lot,
and Azerbaijani often **drops** it. In English you **cannot** drop it.

| Subject | Present | Past | Example |
|---------|---------|------|---------|
| I | **am** | **was** | I **am** happy. / I **was** tired. |
| He / She / It | **is** | **was** | She **is** a doctor. |
| You / We / They | **are** | **were** | They **are** here. |

```
   Azerbaijani:   O   xəstə-DİR.        (the "to be" is hidden inside -dir)
   English:       She IS  sick.         (the "to be" must be written!)
```

> ⚠️ **Top Azerbaijani mistake:** ❌ *He sick.* → ✅ *He **is** sick.*
> Never forget am/is/are.

## 6. 🔑 THE HEART OF EVERYTHING — The 5 Verb Forms

This is the **most important table in the whole book.** Every tense is just a
combination of these forms. We label them with simple codes.

| Code | Name | "work" | "go" (irregular) | Used for |
|------|------|--------|------------------|----------|
| **V1** | Base form (infinitive without *to*) | work | go | present simple, future, after *to* |
| **V1-s** | 3rd person -s (he/she/it) | work**s** | go**es** | present simple (he/she/it) |
| **V2** | Past simple form | work**ed** | **went** | past simple |
| **V3** | Past participle | work**ed** | **gone** | all perfect tenses, passive |
| **V-ing** | -ing form (present participle) | work**ing** | go**ing** | all continuous tenses |

```
                      go
                    /  | \  \
                   /   |  \  \
                  /    |   \  \
                V1   V1-s  V2   V3   V-ing
                go   goes  went gone going
```

> 🧠 **Memory hook:** *"One verb, five faces."* — **Bir fel, beş üz.**
> The tense decides **which face** the verb wears.

## 7. ✅ Regular vs. ❌ Irregular Verbs

### Regular verbs — easy! Just add **-ed** for V2 and V3.

| V1 | V2 (-ed) | V3 (-ed) |
|----|----------|----------|
| work | worked | worked |
| play | played | played |
| call | called | called |

### Irregular verbs — you must **memorize** them (V2 and V3 change shape).

| V1 | V2 | V3 | Azərbaycanca |
|----|----|----|--------------|
| go | went | gone | getmək |
| see | saw | seen | görmək |
| eat | ate | eaten | yemək |
| take | took | taken | götürmək |
| write | wrote | written | yazmaq |
| be | was/were | been | olmaq |

> 📌 There are about **180 common irregular verbs.** The **full list is in
> Chapter 23**. Don't memorize them all now — you'll absorb them lesson by lesson.

## 8. ✏️ Spelling Rules (the small but important ones)

### ➕ Adding **-s / -es** (he/she/it, present simple)

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs: add **-s** | work → works, play → plays |
| Ends in *-s, -ss, -sh, -ch, -x, -o*: add **-es** | go → go**es**, watch → watch**es**, kiss → kiss**es** |
| Consonant + **-y**: *y → ies* | study → stud**ies**, fly → fl**ies** |
| Vowel + **-y**: just add **-s** | play → plays, buy → buys |

### ➕ Adding **-ing**

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs: add **-ing** | go → going, read → reading |
| Ends in silent **-e**: drop *e*, add **-ing** | make → mak**ing**, write → writ**ing** |
| Short verb (1 vowel + 1 consonant): **double** the consonant | run → ru**nn**ing, sit → si**tt**ing |
| Ends in **-ie**: *ie → y* + ing | lie → l**y**ing, die → d**y**ing |

### ➕ Adding **-ed** (regular V2/V3)

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs: add **-ed** | work → worked |
| Ends in **-e**: just add **-d** | live → live**d** |
| Consonant + **-y**: *y → ied* | study → stud**ied** |
| Short verb (1 vowel + 1 consonant): **double** the consonant | stop → sto**pp**ed |

> 💡 We will repeat the relevant spelling rules **inside each tense**, so you'll
> see them again in context. No need to memorize everything today.

## 9. ⏳ Time vs. Tense — A Key Idea

Many learners confuse **time** and **tense**. They are not the same!

```
   TIME   = real-world moment   →  PAST  ·  PRESENT  ·  FUTURE   (only 3)
   TENSE  = the verb form we use →  12 tenses in English
```

- **Time** is reality: yesterday, now, tomorrow. There are only **3 times**.
- **Tense** is grammar: the shape of the verb. English has **12 tenses**.

> ⚠️ Surprise! Sometimes a "present tense" talks about the **future**:
> *The train **leaves** at 6 PM tomorrow.* (present simple form, future meaning!)
> So we learn tenses by **situation and meaning**, not just by the clock.

---

## 10. 🧬 The 2 Questions Behind Every Tense: TIME × ASPECT

Every English tense answers **two questions** at the same time:

```
   QUESTION 1: WHEN?            QUESTION 2: WHAT KIND? (aspect)
   ─────────────────           ──────────────────────────────
   ▸ Past                       ▸ Simple      — a fact / complete action
   ▸ Present                    ▸ Continuous  — in progress (-ing)
   ▸ Future                     ▸ Perfect     — connected / completed before
                                ▸ Perfect Continuous — duration up to a point
```

**Time (3) × Aspect (4) = 12 tenses.** That's the whole system!

| Aspect | Helper + form | Meaning | Feeling |
|--------|---------------|---------|---------|
| **Simple** | V1 / V2 / will V1 | fact, habit, complete | a dot • |
| **Continuous** | be + V-ing | in progress, temporary | a wave 〜 |
| **Perfect** | have + V3 | finished, with a result/link | a bridge ⌒ |
| **Perfect Continuous** | have been + V-ing | duration up to now/then | a long line with a result ━●|

## 11. 🗺️ The Master Tense Map (with formulas)

Here is the whole book on one page. Use the verb **"work"** as the model.

| | **PAST** | **PRESENT** | **FUTURE** |
|--|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Simple** | worked | work / works | will work |
| **Continuous** | was/were working | am/is/are working | will be working |
| **Perfect** | had worked | have/has worked | will have worked |
| **Perfect Continuous** | had been working | have/has been working | will have been working |

```
   SIMPLE        →  fact / habit / general truth
   CONTINUOUS    →  happening around that time (-ing)
   PERFECT       →  before that time, with a result (have + V3)
   PERF. CONT.   →  how long, up to that time (have been + -ing)
```

> Bu cədvəli əl ilə köçür — bütün kitab boyu lazım olacaq.

## 12. ➕➖❓ How Positives, Negatives & Questions Work (the pattern)

Across almost all tenses, the **negative** and **question** follow one pattern:

```
   POSITIVE :   S  +  (aux)  +  verb
   NEGATIVE :   S  +  (aux)  +  NOT  +  verb
   QUESTION :  (aux) +  S    +  verb  ?
```

The auxiliary "jumps" to the front to make a question, and "not" attaches to it
for the negative. Only **Present/Past Simple** borrow the helper **do/does/did**
(because they have no other helper).

| Tense type | Example positive | Negative | Question |
|------------|------------------|----------|----------|
| Simple (uses *do*) | She **works**. | She **does not** work. | **Does** she work? |
| Continuous (uses *be*) | She **is working**. | She **is not** working. | **Is** she working? |
| Perfect (uses *have*) | She **has worked**. | She **has not** worked. | **Has** she worked? |

> 🧠 **Trick:** *"Find the helper, then flip or negate it."* — Köməkçi feli tap,
> sonra onu qabağa at (sual) və ya *not* əlavə et (inkar).

## 13. ✂️ Quick Sound Note: Contractions

Native speakers **squeeze** helping verbs and *not*. Learn these now — you'll hear them everywhere.

| Full | Contraction | | Full | Contraction |
|------|-------------|--|------|-------------|
| I am | I'm | | is not | isn't |
| you are | you're | | are not | aren't |
| he is | he's | | do not | don't |
| we have | we've | | does not | doesn't |
| they will | they'll | | did not | didn't |
| I would | I'd | | has not | hasn't |
| | | | will not | **won't** ⚠️ |

> ⚠️ *will not* becomes **won't**, not "willn't". Just memorize this odd one.

Try these before moving on. **Answers are below — don't peek!**

**A) Identify the verb form (V1 / V1-s / V2 / V3 / V-ing):**
1. She **goes** to school.
2. They **wrote** a letter.
3. I am **reading** now.
4. We have **eaten** lunch.
5. He likes to **play**.

**B) Add the correct helping verb (am/is/are/do/does/have/has):**
6. She ___ working right now.
7. ___ you like coffee? (question)
8. They ___ finished the project.
9. He ___ not like tea.
10. I ___ a student.

**C) Fix the mistake (Azerbaijani-style error):**
11. ❌ He sick today.
12. ❌ I am know the answer.
13. ❌ She go to work every day.

### ✅ Answer Key

**A)** 1. V1-s 2. V2 3. V-ing 4. V3 5. V1
**B)** 6. is 7. Do 8. have 9. does 10. am
**C)**
11. ✅ He **is** sick today. *(don't drop "to be")*
12. ✅ I **know** the answer. *(state verb — no -ing)*
13. ✅ She **goes** to work every day. *(he/she/it → add -es)*
