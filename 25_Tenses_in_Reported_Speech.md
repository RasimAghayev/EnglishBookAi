# 1. 📌 Introduction / Giriş

There are **two ways** to tell people what someone said:

```
   DIRECT SPEECH (the exact words, in "quotes"):
        Tom said, "I am tired."

   REPORTED (INDIRECT) SPEECH (retelling, no quotes):
        Tom said (that) he was tired.
        ┌──────┘        └──┘    └─┘
        reporting verb  pronoun  backshifted verb
```

Notice **three things changed** going from direct → reported:
```
   1. "I"  →  "he"        (pronoun changes)
   2. am   →  was         (verb backshifts: present → past)
   3. the quotes disappear, and "that" can join the two parts
```

That middle change — **am → was** — is the heart of this chapter: **the backshift.** 🔁

---

# 2. 🤔 Why do we use it? / Niyə işlədirik?

Because most of the time we **don't** repeat people word-for-word — we **retell**:

```
   He told me, "I will call you tomorrow."   ← his exact words (this morning)
   → He told me he would call me the next day.   ← me, retelling it later
```

When you retell a **past** conversation, the moment of speaking is now **over**, so English pushes
the verbs **back in time** to match. It's a way of saying *"this was said before now."* 🇦🇿
Keçmişdə deyilən sözü nəql edəndə İngiliscə felləri geriyə sürüşdürür — çünki o an artıq keçib.

# 4. ⏳ Timeline / Zaman xətti — why "back"?

```
   DIRECT (the moment of speaking)        REPORTED (you retell it later)
   ●──────────────────────────────────────────────●──────────▶ time
   "I am tired."  ← said HERE              ...he WAS tired ← told HERE (now)

   The words were spoken in the PAST,
   so the verbs step back to match:   present → past,  past → past perfect.
```

> 🇦🇿 Söz keçmişdə deyilib → felləri də keçmişə "çəkirik". Bir addım geriyə. ⬅️

# 5. 🧪 Formula / Düstur — the backshift table

### 🔁 The core backshift (when reporting verb is PAST: said, told, asked…)
```
 ┌──────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────────┐
 │ DIRECT                   │ REPORTED (one step back)      │
 ├──────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────┤
 │ Present Simple  (work)   │ Past Simple      (worked)     │
 │ Present Cont.   (am V-ing)│ Past Cont.      (was V-ing)  │
 │ Present Perfect (have V3)│ Past Perfect     (had V3)     │
 │ Past Simple     (worked) │ Past Perfect     (had worked) │
 │ Past Continuous (was V-ing)│ Past Perfect Cont. (had been)│
 │ am/is/are going to       │ was/were going to             │
 │ will                     │ would                         │
 │ can                      │ could                         │
 │ may                      │ might                         │
 │ must (obligation)        │ had to                        │
 │ shall                    │ should / would                │
 └──────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────────┘
```

### 🚫 What does NOT change
```
   Past Perfect (had done)        → stays "had done"  (can't go further back)
   would / could / might / should → no change
   ought to / used to             → no change
```

### 📝 Basic patterns
```
 ✅ STATEMENT:
    Direct:   She said, "I love this song."
    Reported: She said (that) she loved that song.

 ❓ YES/NO QUESTION  (use if/whether, statement order, no "do/did", no "?")
    Direct:   He asked, "Do you like coffee?"
    Reported: He asked if I liked coffee.

 ❓ WH- QUESTION  (keep wh-word, statement order)
    Direct:   "Where do you live?"
    Reported: She asked where I lived.

 📋 COMMAND / REQUEST  (tell/ask + object + (not) to + V1)
    Direct:   "Close the door."          → He told me to close the door.
    Direct:   "Please don't be late."     → She asked me not to be late.
```

# 6. 🧱 Sentence Structure / Cümlə quruluşu

```
   REPORTED STATEMENT:
   ┌──────────┬──────┬────────────────────────────┐
   │ subject  │ said │ (that) + backshifted clause │
   │ Tom      │ said │ (that) he was tired.        │
   └──────────┴──────┴────────────────────────────┘

   REPORTED QUESTION:
   ┌─────────┬───────┬───────────┬───────────────────────┐
   │ subject │ asked │ if/wh-word│ subject + verb (statement order) │
   │ She     │ asked │ where     │ I lived.               │
   └─────────┴───────┴───────────┴───────────────────────┘

   REPORTED COMMAND:
   ┌─────────┬───────────┬────────┬──────────────┐
   │ subject │ told/asked│ object │ (not) to + V1│
   │ He      │ told      │ me     │ to wait.     │
   └─────────┴───────────┴────────┴──────────────┘
```

🔑 In reported **questions**, there's **no inversion and no "do/did"** — the word order becomes a
normal **statement**. ❌ *She asked where did I live* → ✅ *She asked where I lived.*

# 7. 📐 Grammar Rules / Qrammatika qaydaları

1. **Backshift one step** when the reporting verb is past (said/told/asked). 👉 §5.
2. **Pronouns change** to match the new speaker: *I → he/she, we → they, my → his/her,* etc.
3. **Time & place words shift** (👉 §9): *now → then, today → that day, tomorrow → the next day…*
4. **`say` vs `tell`:** *tell* needs a **person object** (*tell **me***); *say* doesn't.
   ✅ *He said (that)…* / *He told **me** (that)…* · ❌ *He said me…* / ❌ *He told that…*
5. **Questions** → statement word order, **no do/does/did**, **no "?"**, use **if/whether** for
   yes/no.
6. **Commands/requests** → *(not) to + V1* with *tell/ask/order* + object.
7. **"that" is optional** in statements: *He said (that) he was busy.*
8. **No backshift needed** when:
   - the reporting verb is **present** (*He says he is busy*), OR
   - the statement is **still true / a general fact** (optional): *She said the Earth is round.*

```
   QUICK MAP of the trickiest changes:
   am/is/are → was/were      have/has → had       will → would
   do/does → did             can → could          must → had to
   "I"/"you"/"we" → he/she/they (as the situation needs)
```

# 8. ✍️ Time & Pronoun Shifts / Vaxt və əvəzlik dəyişmələri

| Direct | → Reported |
|---|---|
| now | then / at that moment |
| today | that day |
| tonight | that night |
| tomorrow | the next day / the following day |
| yesterday | the day before / the previous day |
| this (week) | that (week) |
| last (week) | the (week) before / the previous (week) |
| next (week) | the following (week) |
| here | there |
| this/these | that/those |
| ago | before |

> 💡 Only shift time/place words if the **time or place has actually changed** by the time you
> report. If you report *"I'll see you tomorrow"* on the **same day**, *tomorrow* can stay. 🇦🇿
> Vaxt/yer dəyişibsə dəyiş; dəyişməyibsə saxla.

# 9. 🔔 Signal Words / Siqnal sözləri (reporting verbs)

Beyond *say / tell*, rich reporting verbs make you sound advanced:

```
   neutral:   say, tell, mention, state, add, explain, reply
   questions: ask, wonder, enquire, want to know
   commands:  tell, order, command, warn, remind, advise
   requests:  ask, beg, request, urge
   promises:  promise, swear, assure, guarantee
   opinions:  claim, argue, admit, deny, suggest, insist, complain
```
> 💡 *"He **admitted** he was wrong"* says more than *"He said he was wrong."* Choose the verb that
> carries the **attitude**. 🇦🇿 Münasibəti çatdıran fel seç: *admit (etiraf etmək), deny (inkar
> etmək), promise (söz vermək).*

# 10. 🚫 Common Mistakes / Ən çox edilən səhvlər

```
 ❌ He said he is tired.        (reporting a PAST statement)
 ✅ He said he was tired.        (backshift: is → was)

 ❌ She asked where do I live.
 ✅ She asked where I lived.      (statement order, no "do")

 ❌ He asked me do I like it.
 ✅ He asked me if I liked it.    (yes/no → "if", no "do")

 ❌ He said me that he was busy.
 ✅ He told me that he was busy.   (tell + person)  /  He said that he was busy.

 ❌ She told to call her.
 ✅ She told me to call her.       (tell needs an object)

 ❌ He said that "he was tired".   (mixing quotes with reported)
 ✅ He said that he was tired.      (no quotes in reported speech)
```

> 🇦🇿 **Ən böyük səhv (Azərbaycan təsiri):** *"O dedi ki, yorğunam"* → düz tərcümə **"He said he
> is tired"** olur, amma İngiliscə backshift lazımdır → **"He said he was tired."** Azərbaycanca
> zaman dəyişmir, İngiliscə dəyişir!

# 11. ⚖️ Comparison / Müqayisə

### `say` vs `tell`
```
   SAY  →  no person needed:   He said (that) he was late.
                               (if you add a person: "say TO me")
   TELL →  needs a person:      He told ME (that) he was late.
   ❌ He said me...   ❌ He told that...
```

### Statement vs Question vs Command (the 3 shapes)
```
 ┌───────────┬─────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┐
 │ TYPE      │ DIRECT                  │ REPORTED                       │
 ├───────────┼─────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────┤
 │ statement │ "I'm cold."             │ She said she was cold.         │
 │ yes/no Q  │ "Are you cold?"         │ She asked if I was cold.       │
 │ wh- Q     │ "Why are you cold?"     │ She asked why I was cold.      │
 │ command   │ "Put on a coat."        │ She told me to put on a coat.  │
 └───────────┴─────────────────────────┴────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Backshift vs No-backshift
```
   PAST reporting verb → backshift:    He said he WAS busy.
   PRESENT reporting verb → no shift:  He says he IS busy.
   Still-true fact → optional:         He said water BOILS / BOILED at 100°C.
```

# 12. 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani Translation Tips / Tərcümə məsləhətləri

This is the chapter where Azerbaijani habits **mislead** you most — so read carefully. ⚠️

**The core difference:** Azerbaijani reported speech usually **keeps the original tense**; English
**shifts it back.**

```
   AZ: O dedi ki, yorğunam.        (literally: "He said that I-am-tired" — present kept)
   EN: ❌ He said that he is tired.
   EN: ✅ He said that he was tired.   (backshift!)

   AZ: Dedi ki, sabah gələcək.      (keeps "gələcək" = will come)
   EN: ✅ He said he would come the next day.   (will → would, tomorrow → the next day)
```

**Three Azerbaijani-interference traps:**
1. **Forgetting the backshift** — because AZ doesn't do it. Train yourself: *said/told →* push the
   verb back.
2. **Question word order** — AZ word order is flexible; English reported questions become **plain
   statements** (no *do/did*, no inversion).
3. **say/tell** — AZ uses one verb (*demək / söyləmək*); English splits into *say* (no object) and
   *tell* (+ object).

> 🇦🇿 Qızıl qayda: **Azərbaycanca "dedi ki" + eyni zaman; İngiliscə "said that" + bir addım
> geriyə (backshift).**

# 13. 🔊 Pronunciation Notes / Tələffüz qeydləri
```
   said      → /sed/   (NOT "sayed" — short "e", like "bed")
   told      → /təʊld/
   asked     → /ɑːskt/  (the "-ed" = /t/, "askt")
   would     → /wʊd/  ;  he'd say  → /hiːd seɪ/

```
> 💡 *said* rhymes with *bed*, **not** *paid*. A very common pronunciation slip. 🇦🇿 *said* →
> "sed" (qısa e), "seyd" deyil.

# 23. 🎤 Speaking Tips / Danışıq məsləhətləri
1. In real speech, people often **don't backshift** if it's still true or just happened:
   *"He said he's on his way."* — natural and fine. But for exams/writing, **backshift**.
2. Use **strong reporting verbs** (*admitted, promised, warned*) to sound fluent.
3. Drop **"that"** in casual speech: *"She said she was tired."*

# 24. 🖊️ Writing Tips / Yazı məsləhətləri
1. In formal writing, **backshift consistently** and keep pronouns clear.
2. Vary reporting verbs — don't repeat *said* ten times. (§9)
3. No quotation marks in reported speech. Quotes = direct speech only.
4. Watch **time words**: *yesterday → the day before*, etc.

# 25. 🎧 Listening Tips / Dinləmə məsləhətləri
1. Listen for the **reporting verb** (*said, told, asked, claimed*) — it frames everything after.
2. *if/whether* signals a **reported yes/no question** is coming.
3. In summaries, speakers compress quotes into reported speech — track **who said what**.

# 26. 📖 Reading Tips / Oxu məsləhətləri
1. In news & essays, reported speech (with *claimed, argued, denied*) shows the writer's
   **distance** from a claim — they're reporting, not endorsing.
2. No quotes = reported; quotes = exact words. The verb tense tells you it's a retelling.
3. Spot the backshift to locate **when** the original statement happened.

# 27. 🗽 Native Speaker Usage / Doğma danışanlar necə işlədir
- Casual speech: natives **skip backshift** for fresh/still-true news (*"She says she's coming"*).
- *"He was like…"* and *"She goes…"* are super-informal ways to introduce (almost) direct speech.
- Strong reporting verbs (*admitted, insisted, reckoned*) are everywhere in storytelling.
- In writing and exams, the **full backshift** is expected and "correct."

# 28. ❓ FAQ / Tez-tez verilən suallar

**Q1. Do I ALWAYS have to backshift?**
For a **past** reporting verb in formal English, yes. In casual speech (and for still-true facts),
it's often skipped: *"He said he's busy."* 👉 §7, §23.

**Q2. `say` or `tell`?**
*tell* + **person** (*told me*); *say* needs **no** person (*said that…*). ❌ *said me* / ❌ *told
that*. 👉 §11.

**Q3. How do I report a question?**
Use **statement word order**, drop **do/does/did**, drop the **"?"**. Yes/no → **if/whether**;
wh- → keep the wh-word. 👉 §5–6.

**Q4. What happens to `will / can / must`?**
*will → would, can → could, may → might, must → had to.* 👉 §5.

**Q5. Does `would/could/should` change?**
No — they're already "back." *would → would, could → could.* 👉 §5.

**Q6. Why is this hard for Azerbaijani speakers?**
Because Azerbaijani **keeps the original tense** (*O dedi ki, yorğunam*), so the English backshift
feels unnatural. You must add it deliberately. 👉 §12.

**Q7. Does "that" have to be there?**
No — it's optional in statements: *He said (that) he was late.* It is **not** used in questions.

# 📚 MINI STORY — *"The Message"*
### (reported speech in action) 📱

> 🎯 Read 🇬🇧 first. **Bold** = reported speech. Notice the backshift, the *say/tell* choice, and
> the reported questions/commands. Then read 🇦🇿 and do the questions.

## 🇬🇧 The Message

When Leyla got home, her brother Murad was pacing the kitchen.

"Mum called," he said. **He told me that she would be late.**

"Did she say why?" I asked.

**He said that her meeting had run over,** and **that she wanted us to start dinner without her.**
**She also asked whether we had fed the cat.**

I hadn't. **I told him I would do it right away.**

Then my phone buzzed. It was Dad. **He texted that he was stuck in traffic** and **asked if we
could save him a plate.** **He promised he would be home by nine.**

Murad sighed. **He said that nobody in this family was ever on time.** I laughed and **reminded
him that he himself had been an hour late to his own birthday party.**

He didn't argue.

At nine, the door opened. Mum and Dad walked in together — they had met at the corner. Mum looked
at the table, where Murad and I were already eating.

"**I told you they'd manage,**" Dad said to her, smiling.

Mum just shook her head. **She said that one day she would come home and find the house on fire,**
**and we would still be arguing about whose turn it was to feed the cat.**

She wasn't entirely wrong.

## 🔑 Story key — reported speech, decoded
| 🇬🇧 From the story | Original (direct) | What changed |
|---|---|---|
| He told me that she **would** be late | "She **will** be late." | will → would; tell + me |
| He said her meeting **had run** over | "Her meeting **ran/has run** over." | past/perfect → past perfect |
| she **wanted** us to start dinner | "I **want** you to start." | want → wanted; you → us |
| She asked **whether we had fed** the cat | "**Have you fed** the cat?" | yes/no Q → whether + statement order |
| I told him I **would** do it | "I **will** do it." | will → would; tell + him |
| He asked **if we could** save him a plate | "**Can you** save…?" | can → could; Q → statement |
| He promised he **would** be home by nine | "I **will** be home." | will → would |
| nobody **was** ever on time | "Nobody **is** ever on time." | is → was |
| I reminded him he **had been** an hour late | "You **were** an hour late." | were → had been |
| I told you they**'d manage** | "They**'ll** manage." | will → would ('d) |

> 🧠 Every line steps **one back**: *will → would, is → was, were → had been.* That's the whole
> chapter, lived out in one family dinner. 📱

# 🧩 EXPANSION PACK — *Reported Speech* (E1–E12)
### Beyond say/tell — the patterns that sound advanced. ☕

> 🎯 §1–§29 gave you the backshift and the three shapes. This pack adds the **reporting-verb
> patterns** (the real B2/C1 skill), the **no-backshift** cases, **indirect questions** for
> politeness, and the AZ traps. Read slowly.

---

## E1 · When you DON'T backshift 🚫🔁
Backshift is **optional or skipped** in these cases:

```
 1. Reporting verb is PRESENT:    He says (that) he is tired.        (no shift)
 2. Still TRUE now:               She said she is from Baku.          (still true → optional)
 3. A general FACT / law:         He said water boils at 100°C.       (timeless → optional)
 4. Just-now / fresh news:        "He said he's on his way."          (natural in speech)
 5. After "would/could/might/should/used to/ought to": already back → no change.
```
> 🧠 **Rule of thumb:** if it's **still true** or you're using a **present** reporting verb, you
> can leave the tense alone. For exams + past conversations, **backshift to be safe.**
> 🇦🇿 Hələ də doğrudursa və ya "says" işlədirsənsə → zaman dəyişməyə bilər.

---

## E2 · Reporting-verb PATTERNS — the advanced core 🎯
Different reporting verbs take **different grammar** after them. This is what separates B1 from C1.

```
 ┌─────────────────────────────┬──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ PATTERN                     │ VERBS & EXAMPLE                              │
 ├─────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────┤
 │ verb + that-clause          │ say, admit, claim, explain, complain, deny*  │
 │                             │ He admitted that he was wrong.               │
 │ verb + obj + that           │ tell, remind, warn, promise                  │
 │                             │ She reminded me that it was late.            │
 │ verb + to-infinitive        │ offer, refuse, agree, promise, threaten, decide│
 │                             │ He offered to help. / She refused to sign.   │
 │ verb + obj + to-infinitive  │ tell, ask, advise, warn, order, remind, beg  │
 │                             │ They advised me to wait.                     │
 │ verb + -ing                 │ suggest, admit, deny, recommend, mention     │
 │                             │ He suggested leaving early.                  │
 │ verb + preposition + -ing   │ apologize for, insist on, accuse sb of,      │
 │                             │ congratulate sb on, blame sb for             │
 │                             │ She apologized for being late.               │
 │                             │ He accused me of lying.                      │
 └─────────────────────────────┴──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
> 💡 *deny* can take **that** OR **-ing**: *He denied that he had taken it / He denied taking it.*
> 🇦🇿 Hər feldən sonra fərqli quruluş gəlir — felləri **strukturu ilə birlikdə** öyrən.

---

## E3 · Modals in reported speech 🔧
```
   will → would        can → could        may → might        must → had to / must*
   shall → should/would  (offers: "Shall I help?" → He offered to help.)
   DON'T change: would, could, might, should, ought to, had better, used to
   needn't → didn't have to / didn't need to
   mustn't → stays mustn't (prohibition)
```
> ⚠️ *must* for **deduction** (logic) usually **stays must**: *"She must be tired" → He said she
> must be tired.* *must* for **obligation** → **had to**: *"You must leave" → He said I had to
> leave.* 🇦🇿 Məcburiyyət → *had to*; məntiqi nəticə → *must* qalır.

---

## E4 · The "pointing words" shift (deixis) 👉
Words that "point" (here/this/come) shift because **you're now somewhere/somewhen else**:

```
   this/these → that/those      here → there
   come → go      bring → take      ("Come here!" → He told me to go there.)
   today → that day      now → then      ago → before
```
```
   Direct:   "Bring this book here now."
   Reported: He told me to take that book there then.
```
> 🧠 Imagine you've **moved away** from the speaker's time and place — every "near me" word becomes
> a "far from me" word. 🇦🇿 "Yaxın" sözləri "uzaq" sözlərinə çevrilir: *this→that, here→there.*

---

## E5 · Reporting offers, refusals, promises, threats (verb + to) 🤝
A neat way to compress speech with **to-infinitive** verbs:

```
   "I'll carry that for you."   → He offered to carry it.
   "No, I won't do it."          → She refused to do it.
   "Yes, all right, I'll come."   → He agreed to come.
   "I promise I'll pay you back." → She promised to pay me back.
   "Do this or you're fired!"     → He threatened to fire me.
   "Let's eat out."               → He suggested eating out. (or: suggested that we eat out)
```
> 💡 These sound far more fluent than *"He said that he would carry it."* Use the **right verb** +
> **to**. 🇦🇿 *offer to (təklif etmək), refuse to (imtina etmək), agree to (razılaşmaq), threaten to
> (hədələmək).*

---

## E6 · Reporting "Let's…" and suggestions 💭
```
   "Let's go to the beach."  → She suggested going to the beach.
                              → She suggested (that) we go to the beach.
   "Why don't we wait?"       → He suggested waiting.
   "Shall we start?"          → She suggested starting.
```
⚠️ **Never** *"suggested me to go"* — *suggest* does **not** take *obj + to*. ✅ *suggested going /
suggested that we go.*
> 🇦🇿 *suggest* + **-ing** və ya **that... should**; ❌ *suggest sb to do* DEYİL.

## E7 · Indirect (embedded) questions — for politeness 🙏
The same "statement word order, no do/did" rule makes questions **polite** — even in the present!

```
   Direct (blunt):    "Where is the station?"
   Indirect (polite): Could you tell me where the station is?
                      Do you know if the bank is open?
```
🔑 After *Could you tell me… / Do you know… / I wonder…*, the question becomes a **statement**: no
inversion, no *do/does/did*. This works in the **present** too — it's the same grammar as reported
questions.
```
 ❌ Do you know where is the station?
 ✅ Do you know where the station is?
 ❌ Could you tell me what time does it open?
 ✅ Could you tell me what time it opens?
```
> 💡 Hugely useful for **travel + IELTS Speaking** politeness. 🇦🇿 Nəzakətli sual: *Could you tell
> me where... is?* — söz sırası **düz cümlə** kimi olur.

## E8 · Reporting answers, greetings & exclamations 🎭
```
   "Yes." → He agreed. / He said yes.
   "No." → She refused. / She said no.
   "Congratulations!" → He congratulated me (on passing).
   "Happy birthday!" → She wished me a happy birthday.
   "Thank you!" → He thanked me.
   "Sorry!" → She apologized.
   "What a beautiful house!" → He exclaimed that it was a beautiful house. / He admired the house.
```
> 💡 For greetings/exclamations, use a **verb that captures the act** (*thank, apologize,

## E9 · Time-word shifts — but only IF the time has changed ⏰
```
   now → then        today → that day        tonight → that night
   tomorrow → the next day / the following day
   yesterday → the day before / the previous day
   last week → the week before        next week → the following week
   ago → before        "this morning" → "that morning"
```
⚠️ **Don't shift if it's still the same time!** Reporting *"I'll call you tonight"* **on the same
night** → *"He said he'd call me tonight"* (tonight stays).
> 🇦🇿 Vaxt **dəyişibsə** dəyiş; eyni gündürsə saxla. Mexaniki dəyişmə!

## E10 · 🗺️ THE MASTER DECISION MAP — reporting any sentence
```
 ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │  START: What KIND of sentence am I reporting?                                │
 └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
        │
        ├── STATEMENT ──► say / tell(+obj) / admit / claim ... (+ that)
        │                 └─ backshift one step (unless still-true/present verb)
        │
        ├── YES/NO QUESTION ──► ask (+obj) + IF/WHETHER + statement order (no do/did, no "?")
        │                       "Do you like it?" → asked if I liked it
        │
        ├── WH- QUESTION ──► ask (+obj) + WH-word + statement order
        │                    "Where do you live?" → asked where I lived
        │
        ├── COMMAND / REQUEST ──► tell / ask / advise / warn / order (+obj) + (NOT) TO + V1
        │                         "Sit." → told me to sit · "Don't go." → told me not to go
        │
        └── OFFER/PROMISE/REFUSAL/SUGGESTION ──► special verb:
                  offer/agree/promise/refuse/threaten + TO + V1
                  suggest/recommend + V-ing  (or that + should)
 ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │  THEN: shift pronouns (I→he/she) + time words (tomorrow→the next day)        │
 │        + pointing words (this→that, here→there). Choose say (no obj)/tell(obj)│
 └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## E11 · 🏋️ Advanced exercises (patterns, modals, indirect Qs)
```

 2. Use a TO-verb: "No, I won't apologize." (refuse) → He ______
 3. Use -ing: "Let's take a break." (suggest) → He ______
 4. Pattern: "I'm sorry I broke it." (apologize for) → She ______
 5. Pattern: "You took my pen!" (accuse ... of) → He ______

 7. Make it a POLITE indirect question: "What time does the shop open?" → Could you tell me ______ ?

```

```

 2. He refused to apologize.                       (refuse + to)
 3. He suggested taking a break.                   (suggest + -ing)
 4. She apologized for breaking it.                (apologize for + -ing)
 5. He accused me of taking his pen.               (accuse sb of + -ing)
 6. The sign said we had to wear a helmet.          (must → had to, obligation)
 7. Could you tell me what time the shop opens?     (statement order, no "does")

```

## E12 · 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani-interference mistakes (deep cut)
```
 ❌ He said he is tired.            (past report)
 ✅ He said he was tired.            → AZ "dedi ki, yorğunam" zaman saxlayır; EN backshift edir.

 ❌ She asked where do I live.
 ✅ She asked where I lived.          → reported Q = düz cümlə (do/did yox).

 ❌ He said me to wait.
 ✅ He told me to wait. / He asked me to wait.   → "say me" YOX; tell/ask + obj.

 ❌ He suggested me to come.
 ✅ He suggested that I come. / He suggested coming.   → suggest + -ing / that.

 ❌ She told that she was busy.
 ✅ She said that she was busy. / She told ME that...   → tell obyekt istəyir.

 ❌ He asked me that I was OK.
 ✅ He asked me if I was OK.          → sual "that" ilə YOX, "if/whether" ilə.
```
> 🧠 The 5 deep AZ traps: **(1)** forgetting backshift; **(2)** keeping question word order/do-did;
> **(3)** *say me* instead of *tell/ask me*; **(4)** *suggest sb to do*; **(5)** using *that*
> instead of *if/whether* for yes/no questions.

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — *"The Missing Painting"*
### (reported speech with strong reporting verbs) 🕵️

> 🎯 Story #1 was a light family scene. This one is a **detective interview** — perfect for
> reported speech, because every witness *claims, denies, admits, insists*. **Bold** = reported
> speech; the key shows the original words. Read 🇬🇧 first, then 🇦🇿, then the questions.

## 🇬🇧 The Missing Painting

The painting had vanished overnight. Detective Aliyeva interviewed everyone in the gallery.

The night guard was first. **He insisted that he had not left his post,** and **he claimed that
the alarm had never gone off.** When Aliyeva asked him **whether he had seen anything unusual,**
**he admitted that he had fallen asleep for a few minutes** — but **he swore that the painting had
still been there at midnight.**

The cleaner was next. **She said that she had finished at ten** and **explained that the side door
had been unlocked when she left.** Aliyeva asked her **why she hadn't reported it.** **She replied
that she had assumed the guard knew.**

The gallery owner was furious. **He demanded to know how this could have happened** and **accused
the guard of being careless.** **He told Aliyeva that the painting was worth a fortune** and
**warned her that the press would destroy his reputation.**

Aliyeva listened to all of them. Then she walked to the side door, knelt down, and smiled.

"Everyone **told me** the door was unlocked," she said. "But nobody **mentioned** the fresh paint
on the handle. The thief **left a fingerprint.**"

The owner went pale. Slowly, he **confessed that he had staged the theft himself** — **he admitted
that he had needed the insurance money.**

As they led him away, he kept repeating that **he hadn't meant for it to go this far.**

Aliyeva just shook her head. Everyone, she thought, **tells a story.** The truth is usually the
detail they forget to mention.

## 🇦🇿 İtkin Düşmüş Rəsm *(translation)*

Rəsm bir gecədə yoxa çıxmışdı. Detektiv Əliyeva qalereyadakı hər kəslə müsahibə apardı.

Gecə növbətçisi birinci idi. Təkid etdi ki, postunu tərk etməyib və iddia etdi ki, siqnalizasiya
heç vaxt işə düşməyib. Əliyeva ondan soruşanda ki, qeyri-adi nəsə görüb, etiraf etdi ki, bir neçə
dəqiqəlik yuxuya gedib — amma and içdi ki, gecə yarısı rəsm hələ də yerində olub.

Təmizlikçi növbəti idi. Dedi ki, saat onda işini bitirib və izah etdi ki, gedəndə yan qapı açıq
olub. Əliyeva ondan soruşdu ki, niyə bunu xəbər verməyib. Cavab verdi ki, növbətçinin bildiyini
güman edib.

Qalereya sahibi qəzəbli idi. Tələb etdi ki, bunun necə baş verdiyini bilsin və növbətçini
diqqətsizlikdə ittiham etdi. Əliyevaya dedi ki, rəsm bir sərvət dəyərindədir və xəbərdar etdi ki,
mətbuat onun nüfuzunu məhv edəcək.

Əliyeva hamısına qulaq asdı. Sonra yan qapıya getdi, dizini yerə qoydu və gülümsədi.

"Hamı mənə dedi ki, qapı açıq idi," dedi. "Amma heç kim dəstəkdəki təzə boyanı qeyd etmədi. Oğru
barmaq izi qoyub."

Sahibin rəngi qaçdı. Yavaş-yavaş etiraf etdi ki, oğurluğu özü səhnələşdirib — etiraf etdi ki,

Onu aparanda, dönə-dönə təkrar edirdi ki, işin bu yerə çatmasını istəməyib.

Əliyeva sadəcə başını buladı. Hər kəs, deyə düşündü, bir hekayə danışır. Həqiqət adətən onların
qeyd etməyi unutduğu detaldır.

## 🔑 Story key — reported speech, decoded
| 🇬🇧 From the story | Original (direct) | Verb & change |
|---|---|---|
| He insisted that he **had not left** his post | "I **didn't leave** my post." | insist + that; didn't → had not |
| he claimed the alarm **had** never **gone off** | "The alarm **never went** off." | claim; went → had gone |
| asked **whether he had seen** anything | "**Have you seen** anything?" | yes/no Q → whether + had seen |
| he admitted he **had fallen** asleep | "I **fell** asleep." | admit; fell → had fallen |
| She said she **had finished** at ten | "I **finished** at ten." | say; finished → had finished |
| asked **why she hadn't reported** it | "**Why didn't you report** it?" | wh- Q → statement order |
| accused the guard **of being** careless | "You **are** careless!" | accuse sb of + -ing |
| He told Aliyeva the painting **was** worth a fortune | "It **is** worth a fortune." | tell + obj; is → was |
| he confessed he **had staged** the theft | "I **staged** the theft." | confess; staged → had staged |
| he hadn't **meant** for it to go this far | "I **didn't mean**…" | didn't → hadn't |

> 🧠 Notice the **strong reporting verbs** doing the heavy lifting: *insisted, claimed, admitted,
> swore, explained, demanded, accused, warned, confessed.* They carry the speaker's **attitude** —
> far richer than repeating "said." That's the secret to advanced reported speech. 🕵️

## 🏁 End of Chapter 25

You can now turn anyone's words into **reported speech**: shift the verb **one step back**
(present → past, *will → would*, *can → could*), change **pronouns** and **time words**, choose
**say vs tell**, and reshape **questions** (statement order + if/whether) and **commands** (*to +
V1*). Most importantly, you learned the trap that catches Azerbaijani speakers: **AZ keeps the
tense; English shifts it back.** 🗣️

```

 │ Question  → statement order, no do/did, if/whether, no "?"           │
```
