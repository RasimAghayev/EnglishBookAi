# 🟪 PART 5 · Chapter 33
# Linking Structures — joining ideas
### *although · despite · because · so · however · moreover · in order to* — "**Although** it rained, we went." / "It rained; **however**, we went." 🪢

> **The one-line idea.** Linkers show the **relationship** between two ideas — **contrast, cause, result, addition** or **purpose**. But before you pick one by meaning, you must ask a grammar question: **what comes after it — a full clause, a noun, or a new sentence?** That decides which linker is legal.
> *Although it rained* (+ clause) · *Despite the rain* (+ noun) · *It rained. However, …* (new sentence).
> 🇦🇿 *Bağlayıcılar iki fikir arasındakı **əlaqəni** göstərir — **ziddiyyət, səbəb, nəticə, əlavə** və ya **məqsəd**. Amma mənadan əvvəl qrammatik sual: **ondan sonra nə gəlir — tam cümlə, isim, yoxsa yeni cümlə?***
> 🇦🇿 *Azərbaycanca çox bağlayıcı **şəkilçi və ya son qoşmadır** (sözdən SONRA): "yağış yağmasına **baxmayaraq**", "soyuq **olduğu üçün**" — İngiliscədə isə əksinə, ÖNdə: "**despite** the rain", "**because of** the cold".*

## 🧭 THE BIG PICTURE — meaning × grammar

Every linker sits at the crossing of **two questions**: *what relationship?* (meaning) and *what follows it?* (grammar). The grammar decides the form:

```
   WHAT FOLLOWS THE LINKER?
   ┌───────────────────────────┬──────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────┐
   │ a CLAUSE (subject+verb)    │ a NOUN / -ing            │ a NEW SENTENCE            │
   │ → CONJUNCTION              │ → PREPOSITION            │ → CONJUNCTIVE ADVERB      │
   │ although it rained         │ despite the rain         │ It rained. However, …     │
   │ because it was late        │ because of the delay     │ …; therefore, …           │
   └───────────────────────────┴──────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────┘
```

> 🧠 **The key mental move:** don't just translate the meaning — check the **shape** of what follows. *Because* and *because of* mean the same, but one needs a **clause** and the other a **noun**. Choosing the wrong grammatical type is the #1 error in this whole area.
> 🇦🇿 *Əsas fikir: yalnız mənanı tərcümə etmə — ondan **sonra gələnin formasına** bax. "because" (cümlə) və "because of" (isim) eyni mənadadır, amma qrammatikası fərqlidir.*

## 📋 THE FORMS — the four grammatical types

| Type | Followed by | Examples | Punctuation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinating conj.** (FANBOYS) | clause = clause | *and, but, or, so, yet, for, nor* | comma **before** it |
| **Subordinating conj.** | + subject + verb (clause) | *because, although, since, while, if, so that, whereas* | comma if clause is **first** |
| **Preposition** | + noun / -ing | *despite, in spite of, because of, due to* | — |
| **Conjunctive adverb** | links **sentences** | *however, therefore, moreover, nevertheless, consequently* | **;** or **.** before; **,** after |

> 🔑 The same meaning often has **one of each type**. Contrast: *but* (coord.), *although* (subord.), *despite* (prep.), *however* (adverb). Learn them in **families** so you can swap by grammar. 🇦🇿 *Eyni məna üçün çox vaxt hər növdən biri var — onları **ailələr** halında öyrən.*

## 🔑 THE CORE — CONTRAST ("but the opposite is true")

The contrast family is where learners slip most, because the four types look interchangeable but aren't:

| Type | Linker | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **subordinating** | although / though / even though **+ clause** | **Although** it was expensive, I bought it. |
| **preposition** | despite / in spite of **+ noun / -ing** | **Despite** the price, I bought it. / **In spite of** being tired… |
| **conjunctive adverb** | however / nevertheless / nonetheless | It was expensive. **However**, I bought it. |
| **coordinating** | but / yet | It was expensive, **but** I bought it. |
| **comparison** | whereas / while **+ clause** | He likes tea, **whereas** she prefers coffee. |

```
   Although / though / even though  +  SUBJECT + VERB     "Although it rained, …"
   Despite / in spite of            +  NOUN / -ing / the fact that   "Despite the rain, …"
```

> ⚠️ **Two golden traps.**
> (1) ~~Despite it was raining~~ → **Despite the rain** / **Despite the fact that it was raining** / **Although it was raining.** (*Despite* can't take a bare clause.)
> (2) ~~Although it rained, **but** we went.~~ → drop one: **Although it rained, we went.** (Never pair *although* with *but*.)
> 🇦🇿 *(1) "Despite" cümlə almır — isim/-ing/the fact that. (2) "Although" və "but" bir yerdə işlənmir — birini sil.*

> 🗣️ ***though*** can end a sentence (informal): *"It's pricey. I'll take it, **though**."* And *even though* is the strongest/most emphatic of the three. 🇦🇿 *"though" cümlə sonunda gələ bilər (qeyri-rəsmi); "even though" ən güclüdür.*

## 🎯 CAUSE & RESULT — "why" and "so what happened"

### Cause / reason (looking back to the cause)

| Type | Linker | Example |
|---|---|---|
| subordinating **+ clause** | because / since / as | We stayed in **because** it was cold. / **Since** it's late, let's go. |
| preposition **+ noun** | because of / due to / owing to | The delay was **due to** fog. / **Because of** the cold, we stayed in. |
| coordinating (formal) | for | He smiled, **for** he had won. |

### Result / effect (looking forward to what happens)

| Type | Linker | Example |
|---|---|---|
| coordinating **+ clause** | so | It was cold, **so** we stayed in. |
| conjunctive adverb | therefore / consequently / as a result / thus | It was cold; **therefore**, we stayed in. |
| **so + adj/adv + that** | so … that | It was **so** cold **that** we stayed in. |
| **such (+ a) + (adj) noun + that** | such … that | It was **such** a cold day **that** we stayed in. |

> 🔑 **so vs such:** *so* + **adjective/adverb** (*so cold, so quickly*); *such* + **(a/an) + (adjective) + noun** (*such a cold day, such nice people*). Both need **that** for the result. 🇦🇿 *so + sifət/zərf; such + (a) + (sifət) + isim; ikisi də nəticə üçün "that" ilə.*

> ⚠️ ~~because of it was cold~~ → **because it was cold** (clause) / **because of the cold** (noun). 🇦🇿 *"because of" cümlə yox, isim alır.*

---

## ➕ ADDITION — "and there's more"

| Type | Linker | Example |
|---|---|---|
| coordinating | and | She sings **and** dances. |
| adverb (informal) | also / too / as well | She sings; she **also** dances. / She dances **too**. |
| conjunctive adverb (formal) | moreover / furthermore / in addition | The plan is costly. **Moreover**, it's risky. |
| adverb (adding a clinching reason) | besides | I won't go; **besides**, I'm tired. |
| preposition **+ noun/-ing** | as well as / in addition to | **As well as** singing, she dances. |
| paired | not only … but also | She **not only** sings **but also** dances. |

> 🗣️ *moreover / furthermore* are **formal/written**; in speech people say *and, also, plus, on top of that*. *Besides* often adds a **decisive** extra point. 🇦🇿 *"moreover/furthermore" rəsmi/yazılı; danışıqda "also, plus". "besides" həlledici əlavə arqument verir.*

---

## 🎪 PURPOSE — "in order to achieve this"

| Type | Linker | Example |
|---|---|---|
| infinitive | to / in order to / so as to **+ verb** | I went out **to** buy milk. / **in order to** catch the train. |
| subordinating **+ clause** | so that (+ can/could/will/would) | I saved money **so that** I **could** travel. |
| precaution **+ clause** | in case | Take an umbrella **in case** it rains. |
| preposition **+ noun/-ing** | for | a tool **for** cutting / We stopped **for** a rest. |

> ⚠️ **Purpose *to*, not *for* + verb:** ~~I went for to buy milk~~ / ~~for buy~~ → *I went **to** buy milk.* Use *for* only with a **noun** (*for milk*) or *-ing* naming a function (*a knife for cutting*). 🇦🇿 *Məqsəd üçün "to + fel"; "for" yalnız isimlə (for milk) və ya funksiya bildirən -ing ilə.*

> 🔑 ***in case* ≠ *if*:** *"Take an umbrella **in case** it rains"* = as a precaution (take it **now**, before). *"Take an umbrella **if** it rains"* = only on condition it's already raining. 🇦🇿 *"in case" = ehtiyat üçün (əvvəlcədən); "if" = yalnız şərt yerinə yetsə.*

---

## ✏️ PUNCTUATION — where the comma and semicolon go

```
   ① Fronted subordinate clause  →  COMMA after it:
        "Although it rained, we went out."   "Because I was tired, I slept."
   ② Main clause first           →  usually NO comma:
        "We went out although it rained."
   ③ Conjunctive adverb between two sentences  →  ; …, or . …,
        "It rained; however, we went."   /   "It rained. However, we went."
   ④ Coordinating conj. joining two clauses  →  COMMA before it:
        "It rained, but we went."
```

> ⛔ **Comma splice trap:** ~~"It rained, however we went."~~ (comma is too weak for *however*) → use a **semicolon**: *"It rained**;** however, we went."* or a full stop. 🇦🇿 *"however"-dən əvvəl vergül azlıq edir — nöqtəli vergül (;) və ya nöqtə lazımdır.*

> 🇦🇿 *① Öndəki budaq cümlədən sonra vergül; ② əsas cümlə əvvəldirsə adətən vergül yox; ③ "however/therefore" iki cümlə arasında — ; və ya . + sonra vergül; ④ FANBOYS iki cümləni birləşdirəndə əvvəlində vergül.*

## 🧱 MORE linkers — sequence, example, condition

| Relationship | Linkers | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **sequence / time** | first, then, next, after that, meanwhile, finally | **First**, boil the water. **Then** add the rice. |
| **giving examples** | for example, for instance, such as, like | Citrus fruits, **such as** lemons, are sour. |
| **condition** *(→ Ch.24)* | if, unless, as long as, provided (that) | You'll pass **as long as** you study. |
| **clarifying** | in other words, that is (i.e.), namely | It's nocturnal — **in other words**, active at night. |
| **concession + emphasis** | admittedly, of course, it's true (that) | **Admittedly**, it was risky. |

> 🇦🇿 *Ardıcıllıq (first/then), nümunə (such as/for example), şərt (if/unless — Ch.24), aydınlaşdırma (in other words) bağlayıcıları da var.*

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Despite it was raining, we went. | **Despite the rain** / **Although it was raining**, we went. | *despite* + noun, not a clause |
| Although he tried, but he failed. | **Although he tried, he failed.** | don't pair *although* + *but* |
| Because of it was late, we left. | **Because it was late** / **Because of the late hour**, we left. | *because of* + noun |
| It was raining, however we stayed. | It was raining**;** however**,** we stayed. | *however* needs ; or . |
| It was so a hot day that… | It was **such a hot day** that… | *such* + a + adj + noun |
| They were such kind that… | They were **so kind** that… | *so* + adjective |

## 🅰️ §A — LEVEL DIFFERENCES (A1 → C2)

How the *same skill* — joining ideas — grows with your level:

| Level | What the learner can do | Typical sentence |
|---|---|---|
| **A1** | *and, but, or, because, so* | "I'm tired, **so** I'll sleep." |
| **A2** | *although, when, if, also* | "**Although** it's cold, I like it." |
| **B1** | *however, because of, so that, such/so…that* | "It rained; **however**, we went." |
| **B2** | *despite/in spite of, whereas, moreover, therefore, in order to* | "**Despite** the cost, …" / "…, **whereas** she…" |
| **C1** | *nevertheless, consequently, owing to, in case, no matter* | "**Owing to** delays, …" / "…, **nevertheless**, …" |
| **C2** | register control; *notwithstanding, albeit, insofar as, hence* | "**Albeit** costly, it worked." / "**Notwithstanding** the risks, …" |

> 🇦🇿 *A1: and/but/because/so. A2: although/if/also. B1: however/because of/so that. B2: despite/whereas/moreover. C1: nevertheless/owing to/in case. C2: albeit/notwithstanding.*

## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT

1. **Speech leans on simple linkers.** *And, but, so, because* carry most conversation; *moreover / nevertheless* sound bookish out loud. 🇦🇿 *Danışıqda and/but/so/because üstünlük təşkil edir; moreover/nevertheless kitabi səslənir.*
2. **"though" at the end is very natural.** *"Nice place. Bit expensive, though."* Softer than a fronted *although*. 🇦🇿 *Cümlə sonunda "though" çox təbiidir.*
3. **"So…" opens spoken turns.** Natives start sentences with *"So,"* to signal a result or resume a topic — fine in speech, avoid in formal writing. 🇦🇿 *"So,…" danışıqda cümlə açır; rəsmi yazıda qaç.*
4. **"however" mid-sentence takes commas.** *"The plan, however, failed."* — a neat way to slip in contrast. 🇦🇿 *"however" cümlə ortasında cüt vergüllə.*
5. **Overusing formal connectors backfires.** Stacking *"Moreover, furthermore, in addition…"* sounds robotic; vary them. 🇦🇿 *Rəsmi bağlayıcıları həddən çox işlətmək süni səslənir.*
6. **"Because" can start a sentence.** The old "never begin with *because*" rule is a myth — just finish the thought: *"Because it was late, we left."* 🇦🇿 *Cümləyə "Because" ilə başlamaq olar — sadəcə fikri tamamla.*

# 📖 MINI STORY #1 — "The Startup Pitch"

### *Contrast, cause, result, addition and purpose — all in one nerve-racking presentation.*
### 🇦🇿 *Ziddiyyət, səbəb, nəticə, əlavə və məqsəd — bir gərgin təqdimatda.*

---

Zaur had prepared for weeks. **Although** he was nervous, he walked in with a smile. *(contrast — clause.)*

"Our app helps people save money," he began. "**Despite** a crowded market, we've grown fast — **because** we solve a real problem." *(contrast + noun; cause + clause.)*

An investor leaned forward. "Growth is good. **However**, how do you make money?" *(contrast — new sentence.)*

"Great question. We charge a small monthly fee, **so** the income is steady. **Moreover**, businesses pay to advertise to our users." *(result; addition, formal.)*

"The costs must be high, though," another said. *(contrast — end position, informal.)*

"They were," Zaur admitted, "**but** they've fallen sharply. **In order to** cut them, we moved everything to the cloud." *(coordinator; purpose.)*

"**Because of** that decision, our servers are cheaper **and** faster." *(cause + noun; addition.)*

The room went quiet. It was **such** a bold plan **that** nobody spoke for a moment. *(result — such…that.)*

"**Since** you clearly know your numbers," the lead investor finally said, "we'd like to talk further. **In case** you're wondering — yes, we're interested." *(cause; purpose/precaution.)*

Zaur exhaled. All the late nights had been worth it — **not only** had he won their attention, **but also** their trust. *(addition — paired.)*

### ✍️ After-reading tasks
```
1. Find all the CONTRAST linkers. Which take a clause, which a noun, and which start a new sentence?
2. "Despite a crowded market" vs "because we solve a problem" — why 'despite' + noun but 'because' + clause?
3. Rewrite "It was such a bold plan that nobody spoke" using 'so … that'.
4. Turn "In order to cut them, we moved to the cloud" into a 'so that' purpose clause.
5. 🇦🇿→EN: "Həyəcanlı olmasına baxmayaraq, gülümsədi." / "Real problemi həll etdiyimiz üçün böyüdük." /
   "Xərcləri azaltmaq üçün buluda keçdik."
```
> 💬 **Answers**
> 1. Contrast linkers: *"Although he was nervous"* (clause), *"Despite a crowded market"* (noun), *"However, …"* (new sentence), *"but they've fallen"* (coordinator), *"…, though"* (end-position adverb).
> 2. *Despite* is a **preposition**, so it must be followed by a **noun** (*a crowded market*). *Because* is a **subordinating conjunction**, so it takes a **full clause** (*we solve a problem*). Same "cause/contrast" idea, different grammar.
> 3. *"The plan was **so** bold **that** nobody spoke."* (so + adjective + that).
> 4. *"We moved to the cloud **so that** we could cut them (the costs)."*
> 5. Despite being nervous (In spite of his nerves), he smiled. / We grew because we solve a real problem. / We moved to the cloud (in order) to cut costs.

# 🧨 EXPANSION PACK — Linking Structures (E1–E12)
### *The deep layer: the meaning×grammar matrix, the four families, punctuation, position, register & formal linkers.*
### 🇦🇿 *Dərin qat: məna×qrammatika matrisi, dörd ailə, durğu işarələri, mövqe, üslub və rəsmi bağlayıcılar.*

---

## 🅴1 — The MEANING × GRAMMAR matrix (the whole system)

Pick a linker by crossing **what it means** with **what follows it**:

| Meaning ↓ / Grammar → | + CLAUSE (conj.) | + NOUN/-ing (prep.) | between SENTENCES (adverb) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **contrast** | although, though, whereas | despite, in spite of | however, nevertheless |
| **cause** | because, since, as | because of, due to | *(—)* |
| **result** | so *(coord.)* | *(—)* | therefore, consequently |
| **addition** | *(and — coord.)* | as well as, in addition to | moreover, furthermore |
| **purpose** | so that, in case | for | *(—)* |

> 🔑 Read the matrix **across** a row to swap grammar while keeping meaning: contrast = *although* (clause) / *despite* (noun) / *however* (sentence). 🇦🇿 *Sətri soldan sağa oxu — məna eyni qalır, qrammatika dəyişir.*

---

## 🅴2 — ⚠️ CONTRAST family (the clause / noun / sentence splitter)

```
   although / though / even though / whereas   +  SUBJECT + VERB      "Although it rained, …"
   despite / in spite of                       +  NOUN / -ing / the fact that   "Despite the rain, …"
   however / nevertheless / nonetheless        →  new SENTENCE (; or .)          "…; however, …"
   but / yet                                   →  coordinator (comma before)     "…, but …"
```

| Want a… | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| clause | **although** | **Although** she was ill, she worked. |
| noun | **despite** | **Despite** her illness, she worked. |
| -ing | **in spite of** | **In spite of** being ill, she worked. |
| bare clause after *despite* | **despite the fact that** | **Despite the fact that** she was ill, … |
| two contrasted facts | **whereas / while** | He saves, **whereas** she spends. |

> ⚠️ *Despite / in spite of* **cannot** take a bare clause — add *the fact that* or switch to *although*. And never combine *although … but …*. 🇦🇿 *"despite" bare cümlə almır — "the fact that" əlavə et və ya "although"-a keç; "although…but…" birlikdə olmaz.*

---

## 🅴3 — CAUSE family (clause vs noun)

| Type | Linker | Example |
|---|---|---|
| clause | **because** | We stayed **because** it was late. |
| clause (info known) | **since / as** | **Since** it's late, let's go. |
| noun | **because of** | **Because of** the delay, … |
| noun (formal) | **due to / owing to** | **Due to** fog, flights were cancelled. |
| noun (formal) | **on account of** | **On account of** the strike, … |
| coordinator (literary) | **for** | He rejoiced, **for** he had won. |

> 🔑 **due to vs because of:** in careful writing, *due to* traditionally follows a **noun + be** (*"The delay was **due to** fog"*), while *because of* modifies a **verb** (*"cancelled **because of** fog"*). In practice they overlap. 🇦🇿 *"due to" ənənəvi olaraq isim+be-dən sonra; "because of" feli təsvir edir — praktikada üst-üstə düşür.*

---

## 🅴4 — RESULT family (the *so / such* splitter)

```
   so   +  ADJECTIVE / ADVERB           +  that      "so cold THAT …"  "so quickly THAT …"
   such +  (a/an) + (adjective) + NOUN  +  that      "such a cold day THAT …"  "such nice people THAT …"
```

| Structure | Example |
|---|---|
| **so + adj + that** | It was **so** hot **that** we melted. |
| **so + adv + that** | She spoke **so** fast **that** I missed it. |
| **so + much/many/little/few + noun + that** | There was **so much** noise **that** I left. |
| **such + a + adj + singular noun + that** | It was **such a** good film **that** I cried. |
| **such + adj + plural/uncount noun + that** | They were **such** kind people **that** … / It was **such** bad weather **that** … |

> ⚠️ ~~so a hot day~~ / ~~such hot that~~ → **such a hot day** / **so hot that**. 🇦🇿 *so + sifət; such + (a) + isim.*

---

## 🅴5 — ADDITION family

| Type | Linker | Example |
|---|---|---|
| coordinator | and | bread **and** butter |
| adverb (informal) | also / too / as well | I **also** agree. / Me **too**. |
| conjunctive adverb (formal) | moreover / furthermore / additionally | It's late. **Moreover**, I'm tired. |
| adverb (clinching point) | besides / anyway | I can't. **Besides**, it's raining. |
| preposition + noun/-ing | as well as / in addition to | **As well as** French, she speaks Italian. |
| paired (+ inversion if fronted) | not only … but also | She's **not only** clever **but also** kind. |

> 🔑 *as well as* + **-ing** when a verb follows: *"**As well as** teach**ing**, she writes."* And fronted *not only* triggers inversion (Ch.34): *"**Not only did** she sing, **but** she also danced."* 🇦🇿 *"as well as" feldən əvvəl -ing alır; "not only" öndə gələndə inversiya (Ch.34).*

---

## 🅴6 — PURPOSE family

| Type | Linker | Example |
|---|---|---|
| infinitive | to / in order to / so as to + verb | I called **to** apologise. / **in order to** explain. |
| negative purpose | in order not to / so as not to | I left early **so as not to** be late. |
| clause | so that (+ can/could/will/would) | I wrote it down **so that** I **wouldn't** forget. |
| precaution | in case (+ present/past) | Save it **in case** you **need** it. |
| preposition + noun | for | We stopped **for** lunch. |

> 🔑 negative purpose isn't ~~to not be late~~ → **so as not to be late** / **in order not to be late**. And *in case* looks **forward as a precaution** (never *in case of* + clause). 🇦🇿 *Mənfi məqsəd: "so as not to / in order not to"; "in case" = əvvəlcədən ehtiyat.*

## 🅴7 — PUNCTUATION deep-dive

```
   FRONTED subordinate clause   →  , after it        "Because it was late, we left."
   MAIN clause first            →  (usually no comma) "We left because it was late."
   FANBOYS coordinator          →  , before it        "It was late, so we left."
   CONJUNCTIVE ADVERB (2 sents) →  ; …,  or  . …,     "It was late; therefore, we left."
   ADVERB inside one clause     →  , … ,  around it    "We, therefore, left."
```

| Problem | Wrong | Right |
|---|---|---|
| comma splice with *however* | It rained, however we stayed. | It rained**;** however**,** we stayed. |
| no comma after fronted clause | Although late we left. | **Although late, we left.** |
| comma before *that* result | It was so hot, that we melted. | It was so hot **that** we melted. |

> 🇦🇿 *Öndəki budaq cümlədən sonra vergül; FANBOYS-dan əvvəl vergül; "however/therefore" iki cümlə arasında ; və ya . + vergül; "so...that"-dən əvvəl vergül YOX.*

## 🅴8 — POSITION & flexibility

| Linker | Positions | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **however** | front / mid / (rarely end) | **However**, … / …, **however**, … / … , **however**. |
| **though** | front (conj.) or **end** (adverb) | **Though** it's late… / It's late, **though**. |
| **therefore** | front / mid | **Therefore**, … / We are, **therefore**, … |
| **although** | only **front of its clause** | **Although** it rained… *(clause can be 1st or 2nd)* |
| **so / and / but** | only **between** clauses | …, **so** … |

> 🗣️ *however* and *therefore* are movable and take commas wherever they sit; *although/because/so* are anchored to clause junctions. 🇦🇿 *"however/therefore" hərəkət edə bilər (vergüllə); "although/because/so" cümlə birləşmə nöqtəsinə bağlıdır.*

---

## 🅴9 — REGISTER ladder (formal ↔ informal)

| Relationship | Formal | Neutral | Informal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **contrast** | nevertheless, notwithstanding | however, although | but, though, still |
| **cause** | owing to, inasmuch as | because, since | cos ('cause), seeing as |
| **result** | consequently, thus, hence | therefore, so | so, that's why |
| **addition** | furthermore, moreover | in addition, also | plus, and, on top of that |
| **purpose** | in order to, with a view to | so that, to | to |

> 🇦🇿 *Rəsmi: nevertheless/owing to/consequently/furthermore. Qeyri-rəsmi: but/cos/so/plus. Üslub kontekstə uyğun olmalıdır.*

## 🅴10 — Advanced & formal linkers (C1–C2)

| Linker | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **albeit** | although (+ phrase, not full clause) | a useful, **albeit** costly, tool |
| **notwithstanding** | despite | **Notwithstanding** the risks, they proceeded. |
| **hence / thus** | therefore (formal) | Costs rose; **hence** the price increase. |
| **whereby** | by which (means) | a system **whereby** users earn points |
| **insofar as** | to the extent that | helpful **insofar as** it saves time |
| **lest** | in order that … not (literary) | She spoke softly, **lest** she wake the baby. |
| **inasmuch as** | because / to the extent | valuable **inasmuch as** it's rare |

> 🇦🇿 *Rəsmi/qabaqcıl: albeit (olsa da), notwithstanding (baxmayaraq), hence (buna görə), whereby (vasitəsilə), lest (…-masın deyə).*

## 🅴11 — Linkers that trigger INVERSION (preview of Ch.34)

Some negative/limiting linkers, when **fronted**, force question-style inversion:

| Fronted linker | Inverted example |
|---|---|
| **not only** … (but also) | **Not only did** he apologise, but he also paid. |
| **no sooner** … than | **No sooner had** we sat down **than** the phone rang. |
| **hardly / scarcely** … when | **Hardly had** I arrived **when** it started. |
| **not until** … | **Not until** later **did** I understand. |
| **only then / only after** | **Only then did** she realise. |

> 🔗 Full treatment of inversion is **Chapter 34**. Note the pattern: negative adverbial + **auxiliary + subject** (like a question). 🇦🇿 *Bəzi mənfi bağlayıcılar öndə gələndə sual kimi inversiya yaradır — tam izah Ch.34-də.*

## 🅴12 — 🇦🇿 CONTRASTIVE SYNTHESIS

| English | 🇦🇿 | Placement |
|---|---|---|
| **Although** it rained, … | Yağış yağ**sa da**, … | AZ: suffix **-sa da** (after verb) |
| **Despite** the rain, … | Yağışa **baxmayaraq**, … | AZ: postposition **baxmayaraq** (after) |
| **because** it was late | gec ol**duğu üçün** | AZ: **-duğu üçün** (after clause) |
| **because of** the delay | gecikmə **üzündən** / **görə** | AZ: postposition (after noun) |
| …, **so** we left. | …, **ona görə də** getdik. | AZ: front of 2nd clause (like EN) |
| …, **however**, … | …, **lakin / bununla belə**, … | AZ: front of 2nd clause (like EN) |
| **in order to** win | qazan**maq üçün** | AZ: **üçün** (after verb-noun) |

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani contrast in one line:** many English linkers are **prepositions/conjunctions that come BEFORE** their element (*despite the rain, because of the delay, in order to win*), whereas their Azerbaijani equivalents are **postpositions or verb-suffixes that come AFTER** (*yağışa **baxmayaraq**, gecikmə **üzündən**, qazanmaq **üçün**; yağ**sa da**, ol**duğu üçün***). So the AZ learner must **flip the position** — put the connector at the **front** of the clause/phrase, not the end — and must not attach it as a suffix. Only sentence-level adverbs (*so, however* ↔ *ona görə, lakin*) sit in the same place in both languages.

| **Meanwhile**, prepare the sauce. | Bu arada sousu hazırla. |
| Citrus fruits, **such as** oranges, are healthy. | Sitrus meyvələri, məsələn portağal, faydalıdır. |
| **For example**, take the case of Japan. | Məsələn, Yaponiya nümunəsini götürək. |
| It's nocturnal — **in other words**, active at night. | O, gecə heyvanıdır — yəni gecələr aktivdir. |
| **Finally**, add salt to taste. | Nəhayət, dada görə duz əlavə et. |

> 🔢 sequence (first/then/meanwhile/finally), examples (such as/for example), clarifying (in other words). → §🧱.

## 📦 BLOCK 11 — ⚡ MINIMAL PAIRS — one change flips the grammar

| Pair | What changes | 🇦🇿 |
|---|---|---|
| **Although** it rained / **Despite** the rain | clause → noun | (eyni məna, fərqli qrammatika) |
| **because** it was cold / **because of** the cold | clause → noun | soyuq olduğu üçün / soyuğa görə |
| It was **so** hot **that** / **such a** hot day **that** | adjective → noun phrase | (so + sifət / such + isim) |
| It rained, **so** we left / I hid **so that** they left | result → purpose | nəticə → məqsəd |
| Take a coat **in case** it's cold / **if** it's cold | precaution → condition | ehtiyat → şərt |
| It rained**,** but we went / It rained**;** however, we went | comma → semicolon | (durğu fərqi) |

> 🔬 Rows 1–2 are the **clause-vs-noun** splitter; rows 3–5 separate **so/such**, **result/purpose** and **in case/if**. → §E2, §E3, §E4, §E6.

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "The Job Offer"

### *Weighing a decision — contrast, cause, result, addition and purpose in one long night.*
### 🇦🇿 *Bir qərarı ölçüb-biçmək — bir uzun gecədə ziddiyyət, səbəb, nəticə, əlavə və məqsəd.*

> Read it twice. **First** for the story; **second** circling every linker — and note its relationship and grammar type.
> 🇦🇿 *İki dəfə oxu: əvvəl hekayə üçün, sonra hər bağlayıcını dövrəyə alaraq — əlaqəsi və qrammatik növü nədir?*

---

Sevda had been offered a job in another city. **Although** the salary was excellent, she wasn't sure. *(contrast — clause.)*

"**Despite** the money, I'd be leaving everyone I know," she told her friend. *(contrast — noun.)*

"True. **However**, opportunities like this are rare," her friend replied. *(contrast — new sentence.)*

"I know. **Because** the company is growing fast, I'd learn a lot. **Moreover**, they'd pay for my training." *(cause — clause; addition — formal.)*

"So what's stopping you?"

"My mother isn't well, **so** I worry about being far away." *(result — coordinator.)* "I'd be moving **in order to** build my career, **but** what about family?" *(purpose; contrast.)*

Her friend thought for a moment. "You could visit often. **Since** it's only a two-hour train, it's not the other side of the world." *(cause — clause.)*

"That's fair. **In case** things don't work out, I could always come back." *(precaution.)*

The more they talked, the clearer it became. The job was **such** a good chance **that** turning it down would haunt her. *(result — such…that.)*

"**As well as** the career boost," Sevda said slowly, "it would push me to grow." *(addition — noun phrase.)* "**Owing to** my fear, I've been saying no to everything. Not this time." *(cause — formal.)*

The next morning, she accepted — nervous, **yet** certain. *(contrast — coordinator.)*

## 🏁 End of Chapter 33

You can now **join ideas with precision.** The trick is to pick a linker by **two things at once** — its **meaning** (contrast, cause, result, addition, purpose) and the **grammar of what follows** (a clause → conjunction; a noun/-ing → preposition; a new sentence → conjunctive adverb). So *although* takes a **clause**, *despite* a **noun**, and *however* stands between **sentences** — all "contrast," but not interchangeable. Keep *because* (clause) apart from *because of* (noun), *so* (result) apart from *so that* (purpose), and *so + adjective* apart from *such + a + noun*. Punctuate carefully: **comma** after a fronted clause, **semicolon** before *however/therefore*, **comma** before a FANBOYS coordinator. 🪢

```
 ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ ↔️ contrast:  although/though + CLAUSE · despite/in spite of + NOUN · however│
 │ ⬅️ cause:     because/since/as + CLAUSE · because of/due to + NOUN           │
 │ ➡️ result:    so + clause · therefore · so + ADJ + that · such + a + N + that│
 │ ➕ addition:  and · also · moreover/furthermore · besides · as well as       │
 │ 🎯 purpose:   to / in order to + VERB · so that + clause · in case + clause   │
 │ ✏️ punctuate: fronted clause →, · however →;…, · FANBOYS →,                  │
 │ ⚠️ ~~Despite it was~~ · ~~Although…but…~~ · ~~because of it was~~ · ~~for to buy~~│
 └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
