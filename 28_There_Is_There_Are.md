# 🟪 PART 5 · Chapter 28
# There is / There are — introducing existence
### *there is · there are · there was · there will be · there has been* — "**There's** a problem." / "**Are there** any questions?" 🧱

> **The one-line idea.** *There is / there are* is how English **announces that something exists** — it puts a brand-new thing "on stage" before we say anything else about it.
> The *there* here is an **empty placeholder** (a "dummy subject"), **not** the place-word *there*. The **real subject comes after the verb**, and it — not *there* — decides *is* vs *are*.
> 🇦🇿 *Bu quruluş İngiliscədə **nəyinsə mövcud olduğunu elan etməyin** yoludur — yeni bir şeyi "səhnəyə" çıxarır.*
> *Buradakı **there** boş bir yerdoldurandır (formal mübtəda), yer bildirən **there** deyil. **Əsl mübtəda feldən sonra gəlir** və *is/are*-ı o müəyyən edir.*
> 🇦🇿 *Azərbaycan dilində bu — sadəcə **var** (there is/are) və **yoxdur** (there isn't/aren't) sözləridir: "Masada kitab **var**."*

---

## 🎯 In this chapter you will learn to…
```
✅ announce that something exists:        There is / There are ("There's a café nearby.")
✅ make is vs are agree correctly:         the noun AFTER the verb decides ("There are two cafés.")
✅ move it through time:                    was/were · has/have been · had been · will be
✅ add modality:                            There must / might / should / used to be
✅ tell "there" apart from "it":            existence (there) vs specific/weather/time (it)
✅ ask & deny existence:                    Is there…? / Are there…? / There isn't / There's no…
✅ use advanced patterns:                   There seems to be… / There's no point (in) …ing
✅ avoid the top traps:                     ~~It is a book on the table~~ → There is a book…
```
> 🇦🇿 *Bu fəsildə: mövcudluğu elan etmək; is/are uzlaşması; bütün zamanlar; modallar; there↔it fərqi; sual və inkar; qabaqcıl quruluşlar; ən çox rast gəlinən səhvlər.*

## 🧭 THE BIG PICTURE — putting a new thing "on stage"

Normal English sentences start with the subject: *"A café is nearby."* But when the thing is **new information**, English prefers to **delay** it and open with an empty *there* — a spotlight that says *"something exists — here it comes":*

```
   ordinary:      [ A café ]  is  nearby.          ← subject known / fronted
   existential:   There  is  [ a café ]  nearby.   ← "there" holds the seat; the REAL subject follows
                    ▲         └─ the real (notional) subject → decides is/are
                    └─ dummy subject: empty, unstressed, means nothing by itself
```

> 🧠 **The key mental move:** *there* is a **grammatical placeholder**, not a place. The verb (*is/are/was/were…*) agrees with the **noun that comes after it**, because *that* is the true subject.
> 🇦🇿 ***there** — məkan deyil, boş qrammatik yerdoldurandır. Fel özündən **sonrakı** isimlə uzlaşır, çünki əsl mübtəda odur.*

> 🔎 **Two different *there*s.** *"**There**'s a book **there**."* — the **first** *there* is existential (unstressed, dummy); the **second** is the **place** adverb (stressed, = "in that spot"). 🇦🇿 *İki fərqli "there": birincisi mövcudluq (vurğusuz), ikincisi yer zərfi (vurğulu).*

## 📋 THE FORMS — at a glance

| Time | Singular / uncountable | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| **present** | **There is** (There's) a car. | **There are** two cars. |
| **past** | **There was** a car. | **There were** two cars. |
| **present perfect** | **There has been** a delay. | **There have been** delays. |
| **past perfect** | **There had been** a delay. | **There had been** delays. |
| **future (will)** | **There will be** (There'll be) a test. | **There will be** tests. |
| **future (going to)** | **There is going to be** a storm. | **There are going to be** storms. |
| **future perfect** | **There will have been** a change. | **There will have been** changes. |
| **+ modal** | **There must / might / should be** a reason. | **There must be** reasons. |

> ⚠️ **The verb is always a form of *be*** (or modal + *be*). *There* never changes; only the *be*-part carries the tense and the number. 🇦🇿 *Fel həmişə **be**-nin bir formasıdır; **there** dəyişmir — zaman və kəmiyyəti yalnız *be* daşıyır.*

> 🔤 **Contractions:** *There's* (there is/has), *There're* *(rare in writing)*, *There'll* (there will), *There'd* (there had/would), *There've* (there have). 🇦🇿 *"There's" = there is / there has.*

---

## 🔑 THE CORE — *is* or *are*? The noun after the verb decides

*There* itself has no number. Look **past** it to the real subject:

```
   There  IS   +  a / an / one / some (uncountable) / a singular noun
   There  ARE  +  two, three… / plural nouns / several / many / a few
```

| Singular / uncountable → **is** | Plural → **are** |
|---|---|
| There **is** a message for you. | There **are** three messages. |
| There **is** some milk in the fridge. | There **are** some eggs too. |
| There **is** a lot of traffic. *(uncountable)* | There **are** a lot of cars. *(countable plural)* |
| There **is** nothing to worry about. | There **are** no problems. |

> 🧩 **The list rule (formal):** with a list, the verb agrees with the **first** noun.
> *"There **is** a pen and two books on the desk."* (first noun = *pen*, singular) → *is*.
> *"There **are** two books and a pen on the desk."* (first noun = *books*, plural) → *are*.
> 🇦🇿 *Siyahıda fel **birinci** isimlə uzlaşır (rəsmi qayda).*

> 🗣️ **Real speech breaks this.** Native speakers very often say *"There's two people waiting"* — *there's* + plural is extremely common in conversation, though it's still marked wrong in careful writing and exams. **Write *there are*; recognise *there's* when you hear it.** 🇦🇿 *Danışıqda "There's + cəm" çox işlənir, amma yazıda/imtahanda **there are** düzgündür.*

## ⏳ THERE across the TENSES

The pattern never changes — only the *be*-form moves through time:

| Sentence | Tense | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **There is** a good film on tonight. | present | exists now |
| **There was** a storm last night. | past simple | existed then |
| **There were** hundreds of people. | past simple (pl) | existed then |
| **There has been** an accident. | present perfect | recent / result now |
| **There have been** several complaints. | present perfect (pl) | up to now |
| **There had been** no warning. | past perfect | before another past point |
| **There will be** a meeting tomorrow. | future (will) | prediction/schedule |
| **There is going to be** trouble. | future (going to) | evidence now → future |
| **There will have been** three attempts by Friday. | future perfect | completed before a future point |

> 🇦🇿 *Quruluş dəyişmir — yalnız *be* zamanla hərəkət edir: was/were (keçmiş), has/have been (indiki bitmiş), had been (keçmiş bitmiş), will be / going to be (gələcək).*

> ⚠️ **Continuous existence** isn't ~~there is being~~. To say something is happening, add a participle **after** the noun: *"There **is** a man **waiting** outside."* / *"There **were** children **playing** in the yard."* 🇦🇿 *Davametmə üçün "there is being" YOX — ismdən sonra feli sifət: "There is a man **waiting**."*

---

## 🎭 THERE + MODALS — existence with a degree of certainty

Put any modal before *be* to colour the existence with possibility, necessity or the past:

| Sentence | Meaning |
|---|---|
| There **must be** a mistake. | I'm sure one exists (deduction — Ch.19). |
| There **might / may / could be** a delay. | possibly one exists. |
| There **can't be** any milk left. | I'm sure none exists. |
| There **should / ought to be** enough chairs. | I expect enough exist (~80%). |
| There **used to be** a cinema here. | one existed in the past (not now). |
| There **has to / needs to be** a solution. | one is necessary. |

> 🔗 This is the **existence** version of the deduction/probability scale from **Ch.19–21**: *there must be* (sure) > *there should be* (expected) > *there might be* (possible). 🇦🇿 *"there + modal + be" = mövcudluğu əminlik dərəcəsi ilə: must (əminəm) > should (gözləyirəm) > might (bəlkə).*

> 🕰️ ***There used to be*** is the natural way to say something existed before but doesn't now: *"There **used to be** a bakery on this corner."* 🇦🇿 *"There used to be…" = "əvvəllər … var idi (indi yoxdur)."*

---

## 🔀 "THERE" vs "IT" — two dummy subjects, two jobs

Both *there* and *it* can be empty subjects — but they do **opposite** jobs:

```
   THERE  →  says something EXISTS (new, indefinite: a/an, some, any, no, numbers)
             "There is a problem."   "There are some emails."
   IT     →  refers to something SPECIFIC/known, OR = weather / time / distance / general fact
             "It is a problem."  (a known one)   "It's cold."  "It's five o'clock."  "It's ten miles."
```

| ✅ Use *there* (existence) | ✅ Use *it* (identify / weather / time) |
|---|---|
| **There's** a car outside. | **It's** my brother's car. |
| **There are** two reasons. | **It's** obvious why. |
| **There's** going to be a storm. | **It's** going to rain. |
| **Is there** a problem? | **Is it** a big problem? |

> ⛔ **The #1 error:** ~~"It is a book on the table."~~ → **"There is a book on the table."** Use *there* to introduce a **new, indefinite** thing; use *it* only for something **already identified** (or weather/time/distance). 🇦🇿 *Ən böyük səhv: yeni/qeyri-müəyyən şeyi təqdim edərkən **there** işlət, **it** yox. "There is a book on the table."*

> 🧪 **Article test:** *a / an / some / any / no / two* after the blank → usually **there**. *the / this / my / that* → usually **it / they**. 🇦🇿 *Boşluqdan sonra a/some/no/rəqəm → there; the/this/my → it/they.*

## ❓ QUESTIONS & NEGATIVES

| Function | Example |
|---|---|
| yes/no question | **Is there** a bank near here? / **Are there** any seats left? |
| past / future Q | **Was there** a problem? / **Will there be** food? / **Has there been** any news? |
| "how much/many" | **How many** people **are there**? / **How much** time **is there**? |
| negative (be + not) | There **isn't / aren't**… / There **wasn't / weren't**… / There **won't be**… |
| negative with *no* | There'**s no** milk. / There **are no** seats. *(no + noun = emphatic)* |
| negative with *any* | There **isn't any** milk. / There **aren't any** seats. |
| short answers | "Is there any tea?" — "Yes, there **is**." / "No, there **isn't**." |

> 🔑 **Two ways to say "not any":** *there isn't **any** + noun* = *there is **no** + noun*. Both are correct; *no* sounds a little more emphatic. Don't double up: ~~there isn't no milk~~. 🇦🇿 *"there isn't any" = "there is no" — ikisi də düzgün; ikiqat inkar YOX.*

> 🗣️ In questions natives still keep agreement: *"**Are there** any…?"* (plural), *"**Is there** a…?"* (singular). 🇦🇿 *Suallarda da uzlaşma qalır: Is there a…? / Are there any…?*

## 🧱 ADVANCED patterns worth knowing

| Pattern | Example | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **there seems / appears to be** | **There seems to be** a mistake. | softens a claim; *seem/appear* agrees |
| **there's no point (in) …ing** | **There's no point (in)** waiting. | pointlessness |
| **there's no use / good …ing** | **There's no use** crying. | = it's useless |
| **there's no need to…** | **There's no need to** apologise. | = you don't have to (Ch.20) |
| **there's bound / sure / likely to be** | **There's bound to be** traffic. | prediction of existence |
| **there being** (non-finite) | I didn't know about **there being** a meeting. | gerund subject — formal (C1–C2) |
| **there remains / exists / arose** *(formal)* | **There remains** one question. | literary/formal variants of *be* |

> 🇦🇿 *Qabaqcıl quruluşlar: "there seems to be" (yumşaldır), "there's no point in …ing" (mənasızdır), "there's bound to be" (mütləq olacaq), "there being" (rəsmi, qeyri-fini).*

## 🅰️ §A — LEVEL DIFFERENCES (A1 → C2)

How the *same idea* — "something exists" — grows with your level:

| Level | What the learner can do | Typical sentence |
|---|---|---|
| **A1** | *there is / there are* (present) | "**There is** a book." / "**There are** two cats." |
| **A2** | past *was/were*; questions & negatives | "**Was there** a party?" / "**There aren't** any eggs." |
| **B1** | future *will be / going to be*; *there's no…* | "**There will be** a test." / "**There's no** time." |
| **B2** | perfect forms; *there + modal* | "**There have been** delays." / "**There must be** a reason." |
| **C1** | *there seems to be*; *there's no point …ing*; *there used to be* | "**There seems to be** a problem." / "**There's no point** arguing." |
| **C2** | *there being*; formal *there remains/exists*; subtle *there/it* | "…despite **there being** no evidence." / "**There remains** much to do." |

> 🇦🇿 *A1: sadə there is/are. A2: keçmiş, sual, inkar. B1: gələcək, there's no. B2: bitmiş zamanlar, modallar. C1: there seems to be, there's no point. C2: there being, rəsmi variantlar.*

## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT

1. **"There's" is the reflex opener.** In speech, natives start with *"There's…"* constantly — even before plurals (*"There's two things…"*). It's the natural "here comes something" signal. 🇦🇿 *Danışıqda "There's…" ən çox işlənən başlanğıcdır — hətta cəmdən əvvəl.*
2. **"There you go / there you are"** are set phrases, not existential — they mean "here it is" or "I told you so." 🇦🇿 *"There you go" = hazır idiomdur, mövcudluq bildirmir.*
3. **"There + be" hides weak news.** *"There's been a mistake"* is gentler and less accusatory than *"You made a mistake"* — it removes the blame. 🇦🇿 *"There's been a mistake" — günahı kimsəyə yönəltmədən deyilir.*
4. **Uncountables take *is*.** *"There **is** furniture / news / information / advice"* — these feel plural to learners but take *is*. 🇦🇿 *Sayılmayan isimlər *is* alır: information, news, advice.*
5. **"There's no ...ing" = it's impossible.** *"There's no denying it," "There's no telling," "There's no knowing."* A fixed, very natural pattern. 🇦🇿 *"There's no denying it" = danmaq mümkün deyil.*
6. **Formal writing keeps agreement strict.** In essays/IELTS, always match: *there are three reasons*, never *there's*. Examiners notice. 🇦🇿 *Rəsmi yazıda uzlaşmaya ciddi əməl et.*

## 🅲 §C — ALTERNATIVE EXPRESSIONS (say it another way)

| Instead of… | You can say… | Shade |
|---|---|---|
| **There is** a solution. | A solution **exists.** / We **have** a solution. | formal / plain |
| **There are** many reasons. | **Many reasons exist.** / We can find **many reasons.** | formal |
| **There's** a book on the table. | A book **is** on the table. / A book **lies** on the table. | fronted / descriptive |
| **There will be** problems. | Problems **will arise.** / We're going to **have** problems. | formal / casual |
| **There's no** point arguing. | **It's pointless** arguing. / Arguing **is useless.** | *it* / plain |
| **There seems to be** an error. | It **appears that** there's an error. / We **seem to have** an error. | tentative |

> 🇦🇿 *Eyni mövcudluq fikrini "exist", "have", "arise" ilə də demək olar — daha rəsmi və ya sadə.*

# 📖 MINI STORY #1 — "The New Flat"

### *Existence, absence and discovery — a first look around an empty apartment.*
### 🇦🇿 *Mövcudluq, yoxluq və kəşf — boş mənzilə ilk baxış.*

---

Leyla pushed open the door of the empty flat and stepped inside.

"Well," said the agent, "**there is** a lot to like here. **There's** plenty of light, and **there are** two good bedrooms."

Leyla walked through slowly. **There was** a faint smell of fresh paint. In the kitchen **there were** brand-new cupboards, but **there wasn't** an oven yet.

"**Is there** any heating?" she asked.

"Of course. **There's** a radiator in every room. And **there used to be** an old boiler, but the owner replaced it — so **there shouldn't be** any problems this winter."

Leyla frowned at the ceiling. "**There seems to be** a small crack up there."

"Ah — **there might be** a little settling, nothing serious. **There have been** no complaints from the flat below."

She looked out of the window. **There was** a park across the road, and **there were** children playing on the grass.

"**There's no** point pretending," Leyla smiled. "I love it. But **there's** one more question — **will there be** parking?"

"**There will be**," the agent promised. "**There's** a space reserved for every flat. **There won't be** any trouble finding room for your car."

That evening Leyla told her sister: "I've found it. **There was** something about that flat… **it** just felt like home." *(note: **it** here = the specific flat, already known — not existential.)*

---

### 🔍 Spot the structure — sort them out

| From the story | Form | Job |
|---|---|---|
| "**There is** a lot to like." | there is (uncountable) | announces existence |
| "**There are** two good bedrooms." | there are (plural) | existence, plural |
| "**There was** a faint smell." | there was (past) | existed then |
| "**There wasn't** an oven yet." | there wasn't (negative past) | absence |
| "**Is there** any heating?" | question | asks about existence |
| "**There used to be** an old boiler." | there + used to be | past existence, not now |
| "**There shouldn't be** any problems." | there + modal | expected absence (~80%) |
| "**There seems to be** a crack." | there seems to be | softened claim |
| "**There have been** no complaints." | present perfect | up to now |
| "**There's no** point pretending." | there's no + …ing | pointlessness |
| "**It** just felt like home." | *it* (not existential!) | refers to the known flat |

> 💬 **Answers**

> 3. (a) *"There is a crack."* (b) *"There might / may be a crack."*
> 4. Question: *"Will there be parking?"* Negative: *"There won't be (any) parking."*
> 5. There are two bedrooms. / There isn't an oven yet. (There's no oven yet.) / There used to be an old boiler. / Will there be a parking space?

# 🧨 EXPANSION PACK — There Is / There Are (E1–E12)

### 🇦🇿 *Dərin qat: formal mübtəda, uzlaşma tələləri, bütün zamanlar, modallar, there↔it, inkar və idiomlar.*

## 🅴1 — Why English needs a *dummy* subject at all

English sentences **must** have a subject in the normal position. When you want to announce a **brand-new** thing, you don't yet want it as the subject — so English drops in an empty *there* to fill the seat, and lets the real subject follow:

```
   ✗ "Is a café nearby."        ← ungrammatical: no subject in the front slot
   ✓ "There is a café nearby."  ← "there" fills the slot; "a café" is the real subject
```

- *there* here is **unstressed** and means nothing — pure grammar.
- It differs from the **place** adverb *there* (*"Put it there,"* stressed = "in that spot").
- The construction is called **existential** *there* (it asserts existence).

> 🇦🇿 *İngilis cümləsində mübtəda **məcburidir**. Yeni bir şeyi elan edərkən boş **there** yeri doldurur, əsl mübtəda isə sonra gəlir. Bu — məkan bildirən "there" deyil.*

---

## 🅴2 — Agreement: the list rule & tricky quantifiers

The verb agrees with the **notional subject** after it. Watch these:

| Structure | Verb | Example |
|---|---|---|
| a list → agree with the **first** noun | first noun | There **is** a cat and two dogs. / There **are** two dogs and a cat. |
| **a lot of / plenty of** + count noun | plural | There **are** a lot of people. |
| **a lot of / plenty of** + uncount | singular | There **is** a lot of traffic. |
| **a number of** + plural | plural | There **are** a number of options. |
| **none of / neither of** | usually singular (formal) | There **is** none left. / **Neither is** correct. |
| **a pair / a couple of** | plural | There **are** a couple of issues. |

> 🗣️ **Speech vs writing:** conversation freely uses *there's* before plurals (*"there's two things"*). Exams and formal writing demand *there are*. Learn both; **produce** *there are*. 🇦🇿 *Danışıq: there's + cəm; yazı/imtahan: there are.*

## 🅴3 — THERE across ALL tenses (master table)

| Tense | Singular / uncountable | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| present simple | There **is** | There **are** |
| past simple | There **was** | There **were** |
| present perfect | There **has been** | There **have been** |
| past perfect | There **had been** | There **had been** |
| future (will) | There **will be** | There **will be** |
| future (going to) | There **is going to be** | There **are going to be** |
| future perfect | There **will have been** | There **will have been** |
| continuous *(via participle)* | There **is** a man **waiting** | There **are** people **waiting** |

> ⚠️ There is **no** *"there is being."* For ongoing action, add a participle **after** the noun: *"There **were** children **playing**."* 🇦🇿 *"there is being" YOX — ismdən sonra feli sifət: "There is a man waiting."*

---

## 🅴4 — ⚠️ *has been* vs *have been* vs *had been* (the timing splitter)

Existence in the perfect world — the difference is **number** and **time-anchor**:

```
 there HAS been  →  singular/uncountable, up to NOW      "There has been a change."
 there HAVE been →  plural, up to NOW                    "There have been changes."
 there HAD been  →  before another PAST point            "There had been no warning (before it hit)."
```

| Sentence | Reading |
|---|---|
| There **has been** an accident. | one accident, relevant now |
| There **have been** several accidents. | more than one, up to now |
| There **had been** complaints before they acted. | complaints existed *before* a past action |

> 🇦🇿 *has been (tək, indiyə qədər) / have been (cəm, indiyə qədər) / had been (başqa keçmiş andan əvvəl).*

---

## 🅴5 — Existence with MODALS (the certainty scale)

The same deduction ladder from Ch.19–21, applied to *existence*:

```
 SURER ──────────────────────────────────────────────► LESS SURE
  there MUST be (I deduce)  >  there SHOULD be (~80%)  >  there MIGHT / MAY / COULD be (maybe)
  "There must be a reason."    "There should be enough." "There might be a delay."
```

| Modal frame | Meaning |
|---|---|
| There **must be** a mistake. | logical certainty |
| There **can't be** any left. | logical impossibility (negative) |
| There **should / ought to be** room. | expectation (~80%) |
| There **might / may / could be** a problem. | possibility |
| There **used to be** a shop here. | past existence, gone now |
| There **has to / needs to be** a solution. | necessity |

> 🇦🇿 *"there + modal + be" mövcudluğu əminlik dərəcəsi ilə verir. "there used to be" = əvvəllər var idi (indi yox).*

## 🅴6 — 🔀 *there* vs *it* — the full contrast

| Job | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| introduce **new/indefinite** existence | **there** | **There's** a dog in the garden. |
| identify a **known/definite** thing | **it / they** | **It's** the neighbour's dog. |
| **weather** | **it** | **It's** raining. |
| **time / date** | **it** | **It's** half past six. |
| **distance** | **it** | **It's** ten miles to town. |
| **general fact / cleft** | **it** | **It's** true. / **It was** John who called. |

> 🧪 **Article test:** *a / an / some / any / no / numbers* after the gap → **there**. *the / this / that / my / possessives* → **it / they**. 🇦🇿 *Boşluqdan sonra a/some/no/rəqəm → there; the/this/my → it/they.*

> ⛔ Never ~~"It is a book on the table"~~ (introducing a new book) → **"There is a book on the table."**

---

## 🅴7 — NEGATION: *no* vs *any* vs *none* vs *nothing*

| Pattern | Example | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **isn't / aren't + any** | There **aren't any** seats. | neutral negative |
| **is / are + no** | There **are no** seats. | more emphatic |
| **is / are + no + one/thing/where** | There'**s no one** here. / There'**s nothing** to do. | negative pronouns |
| **be + none** | There **are none** left. | stands alone |
| ❌ double negative | ~~There isn't no milk.~~ | pick ONE negative |

> 🔑 *there isn't **any*** = *there is **no*** — same meaning, *no* is stronger. Never combine two negatives. 🇦🇿 *"isn't any" = "is no"; ikiqat inkar olmaz.*

---

## 🅴8 — QUESTIONS & tag questions

| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| yes/no | **Is there** a lift? / **Are there** any shops? / **Was there** a queue? |
| future / perfect | **Will there be** wifi? / **Has there been** any progress? |
| quantity | **How many** rooms **are there**? / **How much** milk **is there**? |
| tag question | There's a problem, **isn't there?** / There aren't any left, **are there?** |
| short answers | "Is there time?" — "Yes, there **is** / No, there **isn't**." |

> 🗣️ The tag copies *there* + the *be*-form: *"There's a bank near here, **isn't there?**"* 🇦🇿 *Tag sualı "there" + *be*-ni təkrarlayır: "…, isn't there?"*

## 🅴9 — THERE in reported speech & conditionals

| Direct / real | Reported / unreal |
|---|---|
| "There **is** a problem." | He said there **was** a problem. *(backshift)* |
| "There **will be** delays." | She warned there **would be** delays. |
| "There **has been** a mistake." | He admitted there **had been** a mistake. |
| *real:* If there **is** time, we'll eat. | *unreal:* If there **were** time, we'd eat. |
| — | I wish there **were** more hours in the day. *(Ch.29)* |

> 🔑 In existential *there*, the **verb still back-shifts** normally (*is → was, will → would*). Unreal *there were* pairs with conditionals & wishes (Ch.24, Ch.29). 🇦🇿 *there quruluşunda fel adi qaydada backshift olur; qeyri-real "there were" şərt və arzularla işlənir.*

|---|---|---|
| **There you go / There you are** | "here it is" / "as I said" | **There you go** — one coffee. |
| **There's no denying / telling / knowing** | it's impossible to… | **There's no denying** he's talented. |

## 🅴11 — *There* + non-*be* verbs & the non-finite *there being* (C1–C2)

In **formal/literary** English, a few verbs of existence/appearance can replace *be* after *there*:

| Verb | Example |
|---|---|
| **remain** | There **remains** one question. |
| **exist** | There **exist** several theories. |
| **arise** | There **arose** a dispute. |
| **appear / come** | There **appeared** a figure in the doorway. / There **came** a knock. |
| **seem / appear to be** | There **seems to be** a misunderstanding. |

And the **non-finite** *there being* works as a gerund/subject (very formal):

> *"I was surprised at **there being** no reply."* / *"**There being** no objections, the motion passed."*

> 🇦🇿 *Rəsmi dildə "be" əvəzinə remain/exist/arise gələ bilər; "there being" isə qeyri-fini (gerund) formadır.*

## 🅴12 — 🇦🇿 CONTRASTIVE SYNTHESIS

| English | 🇦🇿 | Watch out |
|---|---|---|
| There **is** a book on the table. | Masada bir kitab **var**. | **var** = there is/are — no dummy subject |
| There **are** two books. | İki kitab **var**. | **var** doesn't change for plural! |
| There **isn't** any milk. | Süd **yoxdur**. | **yoxdur** = there isn't/aren't |
| There **was** a café here. | Burada kafe **var idi**. | **var idi / vardı** = there was |
| **Is there** a question? | Sual **varmı**? | **-mı** or rising tone = the question |
| There **used to be** a shop. | Əvvəllər dükan **var idi**. | AZ uses the same **var idi** |
| There **must be** a reason. | Bir səbəb **olmalıdır**. | modality via **olmalıdır** |

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani contrast in one line:** Azerbaijani uses **var / yox(dur)** — a single word placed at the **end** of the clause, with **no dummy subject** and **no singular/plural agreement**. English demands a front *there*, forces *is/are* to agree with the following noun, and marks tense on *be*. So a learner must add three things Azerbaijani never needs: **the placeholder, the agreement, and the tense.**

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "The Old Photograph"

### *There was, there is, there will be — a grandmother, a grandchild, and a town square across the years.*
### 🇦🇿 *There was, there is, there will be — nənə, nəvə və illər boyu bir şəhər meydanı.*

> Read it twice. **First** for the story; **second** circling every *there is · there are · there was · there were · there used to be · there will be.*
> 🇦🇿 *İki dəfə oxu: əvvəl hekayə üçün, sonra hər "there + be" quruluşunu dövrəyə alaraq.*

---

Nərmin found the old photograph in a drawer and carried it to her grandmother.

"Nənə, where is this?"

Her grandmother smiled. "That's the town square, sixty years ago. **There used to be** a fountain right in the middle — you can just see it there. And **there were** market stalls all around it every Saturday."

"**Is there** still a market now?" Nərmin asked.

"No, sadly. **There isn't** a market any more. **There's** a car park where the stalls were. But **there are** still a few of the old buildings." She pointed. "**There was** a little bakery on that corner. **There has been** a shop of some kind there for a hundred years."

"**Were there** lots of people?"

"Oh, **there were** crowds! On feast days **there was** music, and **there were** children everywhere. **There's** nothing like it today — everyone stays at home now."

Nərmin studied the picture. "**There seems to be** a boy waving at the camera."

"**There must be**," her grandmother laughed. "That's your grandfather! **There couldn't have been** a photo of that square without him in it somewhere."

They sat quietly for a moment. Then Nərmin said, "**There should be** a way to bring the market back."

"Maybe **there will be**, one day," her grandmother said softly. "**There's** always hope. And now **there is** you — perhaps **it** will be you who does it." *(note: **it** = a specific, known idea, not existential.)*

### 🔍 Spot the structure — sort them out

| From the story | Form | Job |
|---|---|---|
| "**There used to be** a fountain." | there + used to be | past existence, gone now |
| "**There were** market stalls." | there were (past, plural) | existed then |
| "**Is there** still a market?" | question | asks about existence |
| "**There isn't** a market any more." | negative present | absence now |
| "**There's** a car park." | there is (present) | exists now |
| "**There has been** a shop… for a hundred years." | present perfect | existence up to now |
| "**Were there** lots of people?" | past question | asks about past existence |
| "**There seems to be** a boy." | there seems to be | softened observation |
| "**There must be**." | there + modal (deduction) | logical certainty |
| "**There couldn't have been** a photo…" | there + modal perfect | past impossibility |
| "**There will be**, one day." | there will be (future) | prediction/hope |
| "**It** will be you…" | *it* (not existential!) | refers to a known idea |

### ✍️ After-reading tasks
```
1. List every "there + be" line and sort them by time: past · present · present perfect · future.
2. Find "There used to be a fountain." What does 'used to be' tell you about the fountain now?
3. Find the one "it" that is NOT existential. Why is it "it" and not "there"?
4. Rewrite "There seems to be a boy waving" as (a) a plain statement and (b) a certainty with 'must'.
5. 🇦🇿→EN: "Ortada fəvvarə var idi." / "İndi bazar yoxdur." / "Çoxlu adam var idimi?" / "Bir gün yenə olacaq."
```
> 💬 **Answers**
> 1. Past: *there used to be, there were, there was, Were there.* Present: *there is/there's, there isn't, Is there, there seems to be, there must be, there should be.* Present perfect: *there has been.* Future: *there will be.*
> 2. *Used to be* tells us the fountain **existed in the past but is gone now** — it's no longer there.
> 3. *"It will be you who does it."* — *it* points to a **specific, known idea** (the act of bringing the market back / the person), not a new indefinite thing, so existential *there* doesn't fit. This is also a **cleft** ("It is X who…").
> 4. (a) *"There is a boy waving."* (b) *"There must be a boy waving."*
> 5. There used to be a fountain in the middle. / There isn't a market now. (There's no market now.) / Were there a lot of people? / One day there will be (one) again.

## 🏁 End of Chapter 28

You can now **introduce existence** the way English really does it. *There* is an **empty placeholder** — the real subject follows the verb and decides **is vs are** (agree with the noun after it; with a list, the **first** noun). You move existence through time with *was/were · has/have been · had been · will be · is going to be*, and colour it with modals — *there **must / might / should / used to** be*. You keep **there** (new, indefinite existence) apart from **it** (a known thing, or weather/time/distance), ask with **Is/Are there…?**, deny with **isn't any / is no**, and reach for advanced frames like *there seems to be* and *there's no point …ing*. 🧱

```
 ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ 🧱 exist:    There is / There are        "There's a café." "There are two."│
 │ ⏳ time:      was/were · has/have been · had been · will be · going to be   │
 │ 🎭 modal:     There must / might / should / used to be…                     │
 │ 🔀 there≠it:  new & indefinite = there · known / weather / time = it        │
 │ ❓ ask/deny:  Is/Are there…? · There isn't any / There is no…               │
 │ ⚠️ agree with the noun AFTER the verb · not "It is a book on the table"     │
 └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
