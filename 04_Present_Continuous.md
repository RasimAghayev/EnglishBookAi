# 🌊 Present Continuous — The Tense of "Right Now"

### *Part 1 · Chapter 04 · Actions in progress*

> **Azərbaycanca qısa giriş:** Present Continuous (İndiki Davamedici Zaman) **məhz indi,
> bu anda baş verən** hərəkətləri bildirir. Azərbaycan dilindəki *"-ır/-ir + indi"*
> mənasına bənzəyir: *Mən indi oxu**yuram*** → *I **am reading** now.* Formul çox sadədir:
> **be (am/is/are) + fel + ing**. Gəl öyrənək! 🌊

---

## 1. 🟢 Introduction

In Chapter 03 you learned the **Present Simple** — the tense of *habits and facts*
(*"I work every day"*). Now meet its partner: the **Present Continuous** — the tense of
**actions happening right now**, at this very moment.

```
   Present Simple:      I work in an office.   ← my job in general (always)
   Present Continuous:  I am working now.      ← this exact moment (in progress)
```

The Present Continuous always has **two parts**: a form of **be** (am/is/are) + a verb
with **-ing**. It paints a "live picture" — something is happening **as we speak**.

> 💡 **Big idea:** Present Simple = the **always pattern** • · Present Continuous = the
> **right-now movie** 🎬.

---

## 2. ❓ Why Do We Use This Tense?

We use the Present Continuous for **5 main reasons**:

| # | Use | Example | Azərbaycanca |
|---|-----|---------|--------------|
| 1 | **Action happening NOW** (this moment) | She **is sleeping**. | indi baş verir |
| 2 | **Temporary action** (around now, this period) | I**'m staying** with my aunt this week. | müvəqqəti |
| 3 | **Changing / developing situations** | Prices **are rising**. | dəyişən vəziyyət |
| 4 | **Future arrangements** (fixed plans) | We**'re meeting** John tomorrow. | planlaşdırılmış gələcək |
| 5 | **Annoying habits** with *always* | He**'s always losing** his keys! | təkrarlanan əsəbi hərəkət |

> 📌 If you can add **"right now"** or **"at the moment"** and it makes sense →
> you probably need the **Present Continuous**.

## 4. 📅 Timeline

The action is **in progress** around **now** — it started before and continues after this moment.

```
   PAST                       NOW                       FUTURE
 ────┼──────────────[============●============]──────────────┼────►
                     ▲           ▲            ▲
                  started     we speak    still going
                  earlier      here

   "I am reading a book."  → I started before now, I'm still reading, I'll continue.
```

> The Present Continuous is a **wave 〜** wrapping around the present moment, not a single dot.

---

## 5. 🧮 Formula

Model verb: **work**. The helper is **be** (am/is/are).

### ➕ Positive
```
   S  +  am / is / are  +  V-ing
```
| Subject | be | V-ing | Example |
|---------|-----|-------|---------|
| I | **am** ('m) | working | I**'m working**. |
| He / She / It | **is** ('s) | working | She**'s working**. |
| You / We / They | **are** ('re) | working | They**'re working**. |

### ➖ Negative
```
   S  +  am / is / are  +  NOT  +  V-ing
```
| Subject | be + not | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| I | am not ('m not) | I**'m not working**. |
| He / She / It | is not (isn't) | She **isn't working**. |
| You / We / They | are not (aren't) | They **aren't working**. |

### ❓ Question
```
   Am / Is / Are  +  S  +  V-ing  ?
```
- **Am** I working? · **Is** she working? · **Are** they working?

### ❓➖ Negative Question
```
   Isn't / Aren't  +  S  +  V-ing  ?   (= surprise / checking)
```
- **Isn't** he working today? *(I thought he was!)*
- **Aren't** you coming with us?

### ✔️ Short Answers
| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Are you working? | Yes, I **am**. | No, I**'m not**. |
| Is she working? | Yes, she **is**. | No, she **isn't**. |
| Are they working? | Yes, they **are**. | No, they **aren't**. |

> ⚠️ Never say *"Yes, I'm."* in a short answer → it must be **"Yes, I am."** (full form).

## 6. 🏗️ Sentence Structure

```
 POSITIVE   ┌─────┬──────────┬───────────┬─────────┐
            │  S  │ am/is/are│  V-ing    │ rest... │
            │ She │   is     │  reading  │ a book. │
            └─────┴──────────┴───────────┴─────────┘

 NEGATIVE   ┌─────┬──────────┬─────┬──────────┬─────────┐
            │  S  │ am/is/are│ not │  V-ing   │ rest... │
            │ She │   is     │ not │ reading  │ a book. │
            └─────┴──────────┴─────┴──────────┴─────────┘

 QUESTION   ┌──────────┬─────┬──────────┬─────────┐
            │ Am/Is/Are│  S  │  V-ing   │ rest ?  │
            │   Is     │ she │ reading  │ a book? │
            └──────────┴─────┴──────────┴─────────┘
```

## 7. 📜 Grammar Rules

1. **You always need TWO parts:** a form of **be** + **V-ing**. Never drop *be*.
   ❌ *She working.* → ✅ *She **is** working.*
2. **Choose the right "be":** I → am · he/she/it → is · you/we/they → are.
3. **Stative verbs usually do NOT take -ing** (know, like, want, believe…). Use Present
   Simple instead. ❌ *I am knowing.* → ✅ *I know.*
4. For **negatives**, put **not** after *be* (not after the main verb).
5. For **questions**, move *be* to the front.
6. **Time markers** (*now, at the moment, today, this week*) signal this tense.

---

## 8. ✏️ Spelling Rules (adding -ing)

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs → **+ing** | read → read**ing**, play → play**ing** |
| Ends in silent **-e** → drop *e*, add **-ing** | make → mak**ing**, write → writ**ing**, come → com**ing** |
| Short verb (1 vowel + 1 consonant, stressed) → **double** the consonant | run → ru**nn**ing, sit → si**tt**ing, stop → sto**pp**ing |
| Ends in **-ie** → *ie* becomes *y* + ing | lie → l**y**ing, die → d**y**ing, tie → t**y**ing |
| Ends in **-l** (British English) → often double | travel → trave**ll**ing, cancel → cance**ll**ing |
| Ends in **-y** or **-w** → just add **-ing** (no change) | play → playing, snow → snowing |

```
   write → writing   (drop e)
   sit   → sitting   (double t)
   lie   → lying     (ie → y)
   fix   → fixing    (no change)
```

> ⚠️ **Don't drop e from verbs ending in -ee, -ye, -oe:** see → see**ing**, dye → dye**ing**.

## 10. ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| She **working** now. | She **is working** now. | never drop *be* |
| I **am know** the answer. | I **know** the answer. | *know* = state verb, no -ing |
| He **is play** football. | He **is playing** football. | need **-ing** |
| They **is** coming. | They **are** coming. | they → *are* |
| I **am wanting** it. | I **want** it. | *want* = state verb |
| She **doesn't** working. | She **isn't** working. | use *be* not, not *doesn't* |
| Yes, I**'m**. *(short answer)* | Yes, I **am**. | full form in short answers |

> 📌 **Top Azerbaijani mistake:** dropping the *be* verb (because Azerbaijani hides it).
> Always say *"She **is** working"*, never *"She working."* 🧠

## 11. ⚖️ Comparison with Similar Tenses

### 🆚 Present Continuous vs. Present Simple (the big one!)

| | **Present Simple** | **Present Continuous** |
|--|--------------------|-----------------------|
| **Meaning** | habit, fact, always | happening now, temporary |
| **Form** | V1 (+s) | am/is/are + V-ing |
| **Time** | every day, usually | now, at the moment |
| **Feeling** | permanent • | temporary 〜 |
| **Example** | I **work** in a bank. | I **am working** today. |
| **State verbs** | ✅ I **know**. | ❌ ~~I am knowing~~ |

```
   PERMANENT (always)           TEMPORARY / NOW
   ──────────────────           ─────────────────
   He lives in Baku.       vs.  He is staying in Baku this week.
   I teach English.        vs.  I am teaching a new class today.
   Water boils at 100°C.   vs.  Look! The water is boiling.
```

> 🧠 **The test:** Can you add **"every day"**? → Present Simple.
> Can you add **"right now"**? → Present Continuous.

### Quick contrast table

| Present Simple | Present Continuous |
|----------------|--------------------|
| She **drinks** coffee. *(habit)* | She **is drinking** coffee. *(now)* |
| It **rains** in winter. *(general)* | It **is raining**. *(now)* |
| I **go** to the gym. *(routine)* | I **am going** to the gym. *(future plan)* |

## 12. 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani Translation Tips

1. **Both Present Simple and Present Continuous often translate to Azerbaijani -ır/-ir.**
   Azerbaijani usually doesn't separate "now" from "always" in the verb — **you must add
   the English -ing form** when the meaning is "right now."
   - *Mən oxuyuram* could be *I read* (habit) **or** *I am reading* (now). **Context decides.**

2. **The helper "be" (am/is/are) has no separate Azerbaijani word** — it's hidden in
   *-am/-san/-dır*. In English you **must** write it: *I **am** reading.*

3. **"now / right now / indi"** is your strongest clue to switch to the continuous form.

4. **State verbs:** Azerbaijani lets you say *"Mən bilirəm"* (= I am knowing?) — but English
   forbids *am knowing*. Translate it as the simple **I know**.

## 13. 🔊 Pronunciation Notes

### The **-ing** ending → /ɪŋ/
- working /ˈwɜːkɪŋ/ · playing /ˈpleɪɪŋ/ · running /ˈrʌnɪŋ/
- The final sound is **/ŋ/** (a nasal "ng"), **not** "n" + "g" separately.

### Casual speech: "-ing" → "-in'"
- In relaxed speech, natives drop the g: *"What are you **doin'**?"* /ˈduːɪn/
- ⚠️ This is **informal** — fine in speech, avoid in formal writing.

### Contractions (very common!)
| Full | Spoken |
|------|--------|
| I am working | **I'm** workin' /aɪm/ |
| She is working | **She's** working /ʃiːz/ |
| They are working | **They're** working /ðeə/ |
| What are you | **Whatcha** / Whaddya |

> 🗣️ **Try it:** *"I'm cooking."* → say it fast: */aɪm ˈkʊkɪn/*.

## 23. 🗣️ Speaking Tips

- ✅ Use contractions: *I'm, she's, they're* — full forms sound stiff in conversation.
- ✅ Use it to **describe what you see**: *"Look, it's raining!"*
- ✅ Use it for **near-future plans**: *"I'm meeting Sam tonight."*
- ⚠️ Don't put state verbs in -ing: say *"I want it"*, not *"I'm wanting it."*

> 🗣️ **Shadow:** *"I'm cooking, she's calling, they're leaving."*

---

## 24. ✍️ Writing Tips

- Great for **describing pictures, graphs in motion, and current trends**.
- In formal emails: *"I am writing to…", "We are currently reviewing…"*
- Don't overuse it for permanent facts — those need the **Present Simple**.
- Keep *be* and *-ing* together; never separate them with the main verb missing.

---

## 25. 🎧 Listening Tips

- Listen for the contracted *be*: *"He's leaving"* can sound like *"Heez leaving."*
- The casual *-in'* ending: *"They're comin'."*
- *"Whatcha doin'?"* = *What are you doing?* — extremely common in fast speech.

---

## 26. 📖 Reading Tips

- In news, the continuous signals **ongoing/breaking events**: *"Crews are fighting the fire."*
- In novels, it sets a **scene in motion**: *"Rain is falling on the empty streets."*
- Captions under photos often use it: *"A man is crossing the bridge."*

---

## 27. 🌟 Native Speaker Usage

- **Drop *be*-less casual questions:** *"You coming?"* (= Are you coming?)
- **Emphatic complaints with *always*:** *"He's always complaining!"* (annoyance, not literal)
- **Softening news:** *"I'm thinking we should leave."* (gentler than "We should leave.")
- **Live narration:** *"OK, so I'm walking into the shop and the alarm's going off…"*

---

## 28. ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What's the difference between "I work" and "I'm working"?**
*I work* = my general job/habit. *I'm working* = right now (or temporarily/today).

**Q2: Can I say "I'm loving it"?**
Grammatically state verbs avoid -ing, but **advertising/casual** English breaks this for
emphasis (*"I'm loving it!"*). In exams, prefer *"I love it."*

**Q3: How can present tense talk about the future?**
*"I'm meeting Sam tomorrow"* = a **fixed arrangement** (planned, with a time). See Ch. 13.

**Q4: Why is "She is knowing" wrong?**
*know* is a **state verb**. States don't "happen in progress." Use *She knows.*

**Q5: Is "What are you doing?" now or future?**
Both are possible! *Now*: this moment. *Future*: *"What are you doing tonight?"* (plans).

# 📚 Mini Story — *"A Busy Morning at the Office"* (≈300 words)

> Notice how nearly every verb is **Present Continuous** — everything is happening *now*.

It's nine o'clock, and the office **is buzzing** with energy. Phones **are ringing**,
keyboards **are clicking**, and the coffee machine **is humming** in the corner.

Nigar, the team leader, **is standing** by the window. She **is talking** to a client on
the phone. "Yes, we **are finishing** the design today," she **is saying** calmly. Behind
her, two designers **are arguing** about a color. They **aren't shouting**, but they
**are getting** a little loud.

In the meeting room, Tural **is presenting** the new app. He **is pointing** at the
screen and explaining each feature. "As you **are seeing** here," he says, "the menu
**is loading** faster now." The clients **are nodding**. One of them **is taking** notes;
another **is recording** the meeting.

Near the door, the new intern, Sevda, **is struggling** with the printer. It **isn't
working** again. "Why **is** this thing always **breaking down**?" she **is whispering**
to herself. A kind colleague **is coming** over to help.

Meanwhile, outside, the rain **is falling** softly, and people **are hurrying** past with
umbrellas. The city **is waking up**, the traffic **is building**, and somewhere a bus
**is honking**.

Back inside, the boss **is reading** an email and **frowning**. "We**'re losing** time,"

next move.

By half past nine, things **are calming** down. The argument **is ending**, the printer
**is finally working**, and Nigar **is smiling**. "We**'re doing** great," she **is
telling** the team. "Keep going."

# 🧠 Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

1. **"BE + ING = happening now."** 🎬 No *be*, no continuous. *be* is the engine; *-ing* is the wheel.

2. **"Two parts, always together":** picture **be** holding hands with **-ing**. They never separate.

3. **Wave 〜 vs. Dot •:** Continuous = a **wave** wrapping around *now*. Simple = a **dot** of habit.

4. **The "Look! / Listen!" trigger:** these words flip a switch → use **-ing**.

5. **State verbs are "frozen"** ❄️ — they can't move, so they can't take *-ing*. *know, like, want* stay still.

## E1. 📅 Present Continuous for the FUTURE (fixed arrangements)

This is one of the most important "extra" uses. We use the Present Continuous for
**future plans that are already arranged** — there is a **time/place/person** decided.

```
   S + am/is/are + V-ing + (future time)
   I'm meeting Sara at 6 tomorrow.   ← arranged: time + person fixed
```

| Example | Why continuous? |
|---------|-----------------|
| We**'re flying** to Baku on Friday. | tickets booked → arranged |
| She**'s starting** her new job next week. | decided & scheduled |
| **Are** you **doing** anything tonight? | asking about plans |
| I**'m not working** tomorrow. | arrangement (day off) |

> 🧠 **Test:** Is it a **plan with a time, like a diary entry**? → Present Continuous.
> *I'm seeing the dentist at 3* = it's written in my calendar.

---

## E2. 🔮 Future Forms Compared — Don't Mix Them Up

A quick preview (full detail in **Chapters 11–13**). Four ways to talk about the future:

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **Present Continuous** | fixed **arrangement** (time decided, often with others) | I**'m meeting** Sam at 6. |
| **going to** | **intention / plan** (decided, maybe no exact time) | I**'m going to** start a diet. |
| **will** | **instant decision / prediction / promise** | I**'ll** help you. / It **will** rain. |
| **Present Simple** | **timetable / schedule** (official) | The train **leaves** at 6. |

```
   ARRANGEMENT  → I'm meeting Sam at six.   (a date in the diary)
   TIMETABLE    → The train leaves at six.  (an official schedule)
   INTENTION    → I'm going to relax.       (my plan)
   DECISION/PREDICT → I'll get it. / It'll rain. (spontaneous / forecast)
```

> ⚠️ **Common error:** using Present Simple for personal plans.
> ❌ *I meet Sam tomorrow.* → ✅ *I**'m meeting** Sam tomorrow.*

---

## E3. 😤 "Always / Constantly / Forever" + Continuous = Emotion

Normally *always* goes with Present Simple. **But** when *always / constantly / forever
/ continually* appear **with the Present Continuous**, they add **emotion** —
usually **annoyance**, sometimes humor or admiration. It means *"too often / again and
again."*

| Sentence | Feeling |
|----------|---------|
| He**'s always losing** his keys. | 😤 annoyance |
| You**'re constantly interrupting** me! | 😤 frustration |
| She**'s forever helping** people. | 😊 admiration |
| My laptop **is always crashing**. | 😩 complaint |

```
   FACT (neutral):     He always loses his keys.   (Present Simple)
   EMOTION (annoyed):  He's always losing his keys! (Present Continuous + always)
```

> 🇦🇿 Azərbaycanca: *"O həmişə açarlarını itirir!"* (şikayət, qıcıq mənasında).

## E4. 📈 Changing & Developing Situations

The Present Continuous is the natural tense for things that are **getting bigger, smaller,
better, worse** — i.e., **trends in motion**. Often used with: *get, become, grow,
rise, fall, increase, improve, change* + comparatives.

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| The days **are getting** longer. | gradual change |
| Prices **are rising** more and more. | upward trend |
| My English **is becoming** stronger. | improvement |
| The world **is changing** fast. | ongoing change |
| It**'s getting** harder and harder. | intensifying |

```
   get / become / grow + comparative   →   "more and more", "better and better"
   The traffic is getting worse and worse.
```

## E5. 🧊➡️🌊 Stative Verbs That CAN Go Continuous

In Chapter 3 (E5) we met verbs with **two meanings**. Here's how they behave in the
**continuous** when the meaning becomes an **action** or a **temporary state**:

| Verb | Stative (Simple) | Continuous (action / temporary) |
|------|------------------|----------------------------------|
| think | *I **think** it's good.* (opinion) | *I**'m thinking** about it.* (mental process) |
| have | *I **have** a car.* (own) | *I**'m having** lunch.* (eating) |
| see | *I **see** your point.* (understand) | *I**'m seeing** the doctor at 4.* (meeting) |
| taste | *It **tastes** sweet.* (quality) | *She**'s tasting** the soup.* (action) |
| feel | *I **feel** it's wrong.* (opinion) | *I**'m feeling** sick.* (temporary health) |
| be | *He **is** kind.* (character) | *He**'s being** rude today.* (acting that way now) |
| look | *You **look** tired.* (appearance) | *I**'m looking** at the map.* (action) |
| weigh | *It **weighs** 2 kg.* (measure) | *The grocer**'s weighing** the fruit.* (action) |
| measure | *The room **measures** 4m.* | *He**'s measuring** the wall.* (action) |
| expect | *I **expect** so.* (think) | *She**'s expecting** a baby.* (special: pregnant) |

> 💡 **"I'm loving it" / "I'm liking this"** — modern, casual, emphatic English
> sometimes puts *love/like/want* in the continuous to stress a **temporary, current
> feeling**. It's now common in speech and ads — but in **formal writing/IELTS, use the
> simple form**: *I love it.*

## E6. 🧍 Verbs of Posture & Temporary Position

Verbs like **stand, sit, lie, kneel, wait, stay, live** are often continuous when the
position/situation is **temporary** or **happening now**:

| Permanent / habitual (Simple) | Temporary / now (Continuous) |
|-------------------------------|------------------------------|
| She **lives** in Baku. | She**'s living** with friends *this month*. |
| He **works** in a bank. | He**'s working** from home *this week*. |
| I **stay** at the Hilton when I travel. | I**'m staying** at the Hilton *right now*. |
| The statue **stands** in the square. | A man **is standing** by the door. |

> 🧠 Ask: **temporary or permanent?** Temporary → continuous.

---

## E7. 🚫 When NOT to Use the Continuous

Avoid the Present Continuous (use **Present Simple** instead) for:

| Situation | ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| General facts | The sun **is rising** in the east *(as a fact)* | The sun **rises** in the east. |
| Permanent states | I **am living** in Baku *(my home, permanent)* | I **live** in Baku. |
| State verbs (opinion/possession) | I **am wanting** tea. | I **want** tea. |
| Repeated habits (neutral) | I **am going** to the gym every day. | I **go** to the gym every day. |
| Scientific/technical truths | Water **is boiling** at 100°C *(general law)* | Water **boils** at 100°C. |

> Note: *"The sun is rising"* IS correct when you mean **right now this morning** —
> but not as a permanent scientific fact. Meaning decides!

## E8. 🎚️ Adverb Placement with the Continuous

Mid-position adverbs (always, still, just, only, constantly, probably) go **between
*be* and *-ing*:**

```
   S  +  be  +  [adverb]  +  V-ing
   She is   always       complaining.
   I  am    still        waiting.
   They are probably     coming.
   He is    just         leaving.
```

| ✅ Correct | ❌ Wrong |
|-----------|---------|
| She is **always** complaining. | She always is complaining. |
| I am **still** working. | I still am working. |
| They are **just** leaving. | They just are leaving. |

# 📖 Mini Story #2 — *"The Evening Market"* (≈330 words)

> A second story by request — everything is happening **right now**. Watch for the
> continuous of action, the *always*-annoyance use, and a future arrangement. Read aloud.

The sun **is going** down, and the evening market **is coming** to life. Lights
**are flickering** on above the stalls, and the smell of grilled corn **is drifting**
through the air.

Aygün **is walking** between the stalls with her little brother, Elvin. He **is holding**
her hand and **is pointing** at everything. "Look, they**'re selling** balloons!" he
**is shouting**. Aygün **is laughing**. "Yes, but we**'re looking** for tomatoes, not
balloons."

At the first stall, an old man **is arranging** his fruit. "Everything **is getting**
cheaper now, before we close," he **is calling** out. Two women **are arguing** over the
last melon, but they **aren't really angry** — they **are smiling**. Nearby, a boy
**is juggling** oranges, and a small crowd **is gathering** to watch.

"Why **is** that man always **shouting**?" Elvin **is whispering**. Aygün **is grinning**.
"He**'s always doing** that. It**'s** how he **sells** his fish." The fish seller
**is waving** his arms and **is promising** the freshest catch in the city.

Aygün **isn't buying** much tonight. She **is saving** her money. But she **is enjoying**
the noise, the colors, and the movement. Everywhere, people **are bargaining**,
**laughing**, and **carrying** heavy bags. A musician in the corner **is playing** a
saz, and a few children **are dancing**.

"We**'re meeting** Mum at the gate at eight," Aygün **is reminding** her brother. "So
we**'re not staying** much longer." Elvin **is begging** for one more look at the
balloons.

The sky **is turning** dark purple now. The market **is glowing**. Somewhere a kettle
**is whistling**, a phone **is ringing**, and the whole street **is buzzing** with life.

Aygün **is breathing** it all in. "I **love** this place," she **is thinking**. Tonight,
the city **is alive**, and she **is right** in the middle of it.

### 🔎 Story Notes — Spot the Grammar
- **Action in progress (now):** *is going, is coming, are flickering, is drifting, are bargaining…*
- **Negatives:** *aren't really angry, isn't buying, 're not staying*
- **Question inside speech:** *"Why is that man always shouting?"*
- **"always" + continuous (annoyance/habit):** *"He's always doing that."*
- **Future arrangement:** *"We're meeting Mum at the gate at eight."*
- **State verbs stay simple:** *I **love** this place* · *how he **sells** his fish* (not *am loving*, *is selling* — wait: "is selling" *is* used earlier as a true ongoing action; "sells" here = his general method). Good contrast to notice!

> haqqında daha 3 cümlə de.*
