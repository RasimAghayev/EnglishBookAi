## 1. ūüüĘ Introduction

In Chapter 03 you learned the **Present Simple** ‚ÄĒ the tense of *habits and facts*
(*"I work every day"*). Now meet its partner: the **Present Continuous** ‚ÄĒ the tense of
**actions happening right now**, at this very moment.

```
   Present Simple:      I work in an office.   ‚Üź my job in general (always)
   Present Continuous:  I am working now.      ‚Üź this exact moment (in progress)
```

The Present Continuous always has **two parts**: a form of **be** (am/is/are) + a verb
with **-ing**. It paints a "live picture" ‚ÄĒ something is happening **as we speak**.

> ūüí° **Big idea:** Present Simple = the **always pattern** ‚ÄĘ ¬∑ Present Continuous = the
> **right-now movie** ūüé¨.

## 5. ūüßģ Formula

Model verb: **work**. The helper is **be** (am/is/are).

### ‚ěē Positive
```
   S  +  am / is / are  +  V-ing
```
| Subject | be | V-ing | Example |
|---------|-----|-------|---------|

## 7. ūüďú Grammar Rules

1. **You always need TWO parts:** a form of **be** + **V-ing**. Never drop *be*.
   ‚ĚĆ *She working.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ *She **is** working.*
2. **Choose the right "be":** I ‚Üí am ¬∑ he/she/it ‚Üí is ¬∑ you/we/they ‚Üí are.
3. **Stative verbs usually do NOT take -ing** (know, like, want, believe‚Ä¶). Use Present
   Simple instead. ‚ĚĆ *I am knowing.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ *I know.*
4. For **negatives**, put **not** after *be* (not after the main verb).
5. For **questions**, move *be* to the front.
6. **Time markers** (*now, at the moment, today, this week*) signal this tense.

## 8. ‚úŹÔłŹ Spelling Rules (adding -ing)

## 11. ‚öĖÔłŹ Comparison with Similar Tenses

### ūüÜö Present Continuous vs. Present Simple (the big one!)

| | **Present Simple** | **Present Continuous** |

| **Meaning** | habit, fact, always | happening now, temporary |

| **Feeling** | permanent ‚ÄĘ | temporary „Äú |
| **Example** | I **work** in a bank. | I **am working** today. |
| **State verbs** | ‚úÖ I **know**. | ‚ĚĆ ~~I am knowing~~ |


## 12. ūüá¶ūüáŅ Azerbaijani Translation Tips

1. **Both Present Simple and Present Continuous often translate to Azerbaijani -ńĪr/-ir.**

## 28. ‚Ěď Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What's the difference between "I work" and "I'm working"?**
*I work* = my general job/habit. *I'm working* = right now (or temporarily/today).

**Q2: Can I say "I'm loving it"?**
Grammatically state verbs avoid -ing, but **advertising/casual** English breaks this for
emphasis (*"I'm loving it!"*). In exams, prefer *"I love it."*

**Q3: How can present tense talk about the future?**
*"I'm meeting Sam tomorrow"* = a **fixed arrangement** (planned, with a time). See Ch. 13.

**Q4: Why is "She is knowing" wrong?**
*know* is a **state verb**. States don't "happen in progress." Use *She knows.*

**Q5: Is "What are you doing?" now or future?**
Both are possible! *Now*: this moment. *Future*: *"What are you doing tonight?"* (plans).

## E1. ūüďÖ Present Continuous for the FUTURE (fixed arrangements)

This is one of the most important "extra" uses. We use the Present Continuous for
**future plans that are already arranged** ‚ÄĒ there is a **time/place/person** decided.

```
   S + am/is/are + V-ing + (future time)
   I'm meeting Sara at 6 tomorrow.   ‚Üź arranged: time + person fixed
```

| Example | Why continuous? |
|---------|-----------------|
| We**'re flying** to Baku on Friday. | tickets booked ‚Üí arranged |
| She**'s starting** her new job next week. | decided & scheduled |
| **Are** you **doing** anything tonight? | asking about plans |
| I**'m not working** tomorrow. | arrangement (day off) |

> ūüß† **Test:** Is it a **plan with a time, like a diary entry**? ‚Üí Present Continuous.
> *I'm seeing the dentist at 3* = it's written in my calendar.

---

## E2. ūüĒģ Future Forms Compared ‚ÄĒ Don't Mix Them Up

A quick preview (full detail in **Chapters 11‚Äď13**). Four ways to talk about the future:

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **Present Continuous** | fixed **arrangement** (time decided, often with others) | I**'m meeting** Sam at 6. |
| **going to** | **intention / plan** (decided, maybe no exact time) | I**'m going to** start a diet. |
| **will** | **instant decision / prediction / promise** | I**'ll** help you. / It **will** rain. |
| **Present Simple** | **timetable / schedule** (official) | The train **leaves** at 6. |

```
   ARRANGEMENT  ‚Üí I'm meeting Sam at six.   (a date in the diary)
   TIMETABLE    ‚Üí The train leaves at six.  (an official schedule)
   INTENTION    ‚Üí I'm going to relax.       (my plan)
   DECISION/PREDICT ‚Üí I'll get it. / It'll rain. (spontaneous / forecast)
```

> ‚ö†ÔłŹ **Common error:** using Present Simple for personal plans.
> ‚ĚĆ *I meet Sam tomorrow.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ *I**'m meeting** Sam tomorrow.*

## E3. ūüė§ "Always / Constantly / Forever" + Continuous = Emotion

Normally *always* goes with Present Simple. **But** when *always / constantly / forever
/ continually* appear **with the Present Continuous**, they add **emotion** ‚ÄĒ
usually **annoyance**, sometimes humor or admiration. It means *"too often / again and
again."*

| Sentence | Feeling |
|----------|---------|
| He**'s always losing** his keys. | ūüė§ annoyance |
| You**'re constantly interrupting** me! | ūüė§ frustration |
| She**'s forever helping** people. | ūüėä admiration |
| My laptop **is always crashing**. | ūüė© complaint |

```
   FACT (neutral):     He always loses his keys.   (Present Simple)
   EMOTION (annoyed):  He's always losing his keys! (Present Continuous + always)
```

## E4. ūüďą Changing & Developing Situations

The Present Continuous is the natural tense for things that are **getting bigger, smaller,
better, worse** ‚ÄĒ i.e., **trends in motion**. Often used with: *get, become, grow,
rise, fall, increase, improve, change* + comparatives.

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| The days **are getting** longer. | gradual change |
| Prices **are rising** more and more. | upward trend |
| My English **is becoming** stronger. | improvement |
| The world **is changing** fast. | ongoing change |
| It**'s getting** harder and harder. | intensifying |

```
   get / become / grow + comparative   ‚Üí   "more and more", "better and better"
   The traffic is getting worse and worse.
```

## E5. ūüßä‚ě°ÔłŹūüĆä Stative Verbs That CAN Go Continuous

In Chapter 3 (E5) we met verbs with **two meanings**. Here's how they behave in the
**continuous** when the meaning becomes an **action** or a **temporary state**:

| Verb | Stative (Simple) | Continuous (action / temporary) |
|------|------------------|----------------------------------|
| think | *I **think** it's good.* (opinion) | *I**'m thinking** about it.* (mental process) |
| have | *I **have** a car.* (own) | *I**'m having** lunch.* (eating) |
| see | *I **see** your point.* (understand) | *I**'m seeing** the doctor at 4.* (meeting) |
| taste | *It **tastes** sweet.* (quality) | *She**'s tasting** the soup.* (action) |
| feel | *I **feel** it's wrong.* (opinion) | *I**'m feeling** sick.* (temporary health) |
| be | *He **is** kind.* (character) | *He**'s being** rude today.* (acting that way now) |
| look | *You **look** tired.* (appearance) | *I**'m looking** at the map.* (action) |
| weigh | *It **weighs** 2 kg.* (measure) | *The grocer**'s weighing** the fruit.* (action) |
| measure | *The room **measures** 4m.* | *He**'s measuring** the wall.* (action) |
| expect | *I **expect** so.* (think) | *She**'s expecting** a baby.* (special: pregnant) |

> ūüí° **"I'm loving it" / "I'm liking this"** ‚ÄĒ modern, casual, emphatic English
> sometimes puts *love/like/want* in the continuous to stress a **temporary, current
> feeling**. It's now common in speech and ads ‚ÄĒ but in **formal writing/IELTS, use the
> simple form**: *I love it.*

## E6. ūüßć Verbs of Posture & Temporary Position

Verbs like **stand, sit, lie, kneel, wait, stay, live** are often continuous when the
position/situation is **temporary** or **happening now**:

| Permanent / habitual (Simple) | Temporary / now (Continuous) |
|-------------------------------|------------------------------|
| She **lives** in Baku. | She**'s living** with friends *this month*. |
| He **works** in a bank. | He**'s working** from home *this week*. |
| I **stay** at the Hilton when I travel. | I**'m staying** at the Hilton *right now*. |
| The statue **stands** in the square. | A man **is standing** by the door. |

> ūüß† Ask: **temporary or permanent?** Temporary ‚Üí continuous.

## E7. ūüöę When NOT to Use the Continuous

Avoid the Present Continuous (use **Present Simple** instead) for:

| Situation | ‚ĚĆ Wrong | ‚úÖ Right |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| General facts | The sun **is rising** in the east *(as a fact)* | The sun **rises** in the east. |
| Permanent states | I **am living** in Baku *(my home, permanent)* | I **live** in Baku. |
| State verbs (opinion/possession) | I **am wanting** tea. | I **want** tea. |
| Repeated habits (neutral) | I **am going** to the gym every day. | I **go** to the gym every day. |
| Scientific/technical truths | Water **is boiling** at 100¬įC *(general law)* | Water **boils** at 100¬įC. |

> Note: *"The sun is rising"* IS correct when you mean **right now this morning** ‚ÄĒ
> but not as a permanent scientific fact. Meaning decides!

## E8. ūüéöÔłŹ Adverb Placement with the Continuous

Mid-position adverbs (always, still, just, only, constantly, probably) go **between
*be* and *-ing*:**

```
   S  +  be  +  [adverb]  +  V-ing
   She is   always       complaining.
   I  am    still        waiting.
   They are probably     coming.
   He is    just         leaving.
```

| ‚úÖ Correct | ‚ĚĆ Wrong |
|-----------|---------|
| She is **always** complaining. | She always is complaining. |
| I am **still** working. | I still am working. |
| They are **just** leaving. | They just are leaving. |
