# ⏳⏪ Past Perfect Continuous — How Long, Up to a Past Point

### *Part 2 · Chapter 10 · had been + V-ing*

> **Azərbaycanca qısa giriş:** Past Perfect Continuous (Davamedici Bitmiş Keçmiş Zaman)
> keçmişdə müəyyən bir ana qədər **nə qədər müddət** davam edən hərəkəti bildirir.
> *Yorğun idi, çünki saatlarla işləyirdi* → *He was tired because he **had been working** for
> hours.* Formul: **had been + fel + ing**. İki şeyi vurğulayır: **müddət** (nə qədər) və
> nəticənin **səbəbi** (niyə). ⏳⏪

---

## 1. 🟢 Introduction

The **Past Perfect Continuous** is the tense of **"how long something had been happening"**
before a past moment. It looks **back** from a point in the past and measures the **duration**
of an action that was **still going** (or had just stopped) at that point.

```
   Past Perfect:            She HAD WRITTEN three letters.   → completed (how many)
   Past Perfect Continuous: She HAD BEEN WRITING for hours.  → duration (how long)
```

The form has **three parts**: **had** + **been** + a verb with **-ing**. It combines the
"step back" of the Past Perfect (Ch. 09) with the "in progress" feel of the continuous (Ch. 04).

> 💡 **Big idea:** Past Perfect Continuous = a **"long action leading up to a past moment"** ⏳
> — emphasis on **duration** and often the **cause** of a past result.

---

## 2. ❓ Why Do We Use This Tense?

Three main reasons:

| # | Use | Example | Azərbaycanca |
|---|-----|---------|--------------|
| 1 | **Duration up to a past point** (how long) | By 6 PM, she **had been working** for nine hours. | keçmiş ana qədər müddət |
| 2 | **Cause of a past result/evidence** | The ground was wet; it **had been raining**. | keçmiş nəticənin səbəbi |
| 3 | **A long action that had just stopped** | He was out of breath; he **had been running**. | təzəcə dayanmış uzun hərəkət |

> 📌 Two big jobs: show **HOW LONG** an action lasted before a past moment, and explain
> **WHY** a past situation was the way it was (the **evidence/cause**).

---

## 3. 🌍 Real-Life Situations

- ⏳ **Saying how long before a past event:** *"When the bell rang, we **had been studying** for two hours."*
- 😩 **Explaining a past state (cause):** *"She was exhausted — she **had been cleaning** all day."*
- 🌧️ **Reading evidence:** *"The streets were wet; it **had been raining**."*
- 😤 **Long, repeated effort:** *"He finally passed — he **had been trying** for years."*
- 🛑 **An action that had just finished:** *"My eyes hurt; I **had been staring** at the screen."*

---

## 4. 📅 Timeline

A **long action** runs up to a **past point**. The arrow shows the duration **before** that
moment; the action is still going (or just stopped) at the point.

```
   PAST                                                      NOW
 ────[========== had been working ==========]●────────────────┼────►
     │                                       │
   started (earlier)                    past point: "By 6 PM"
                                        (we look back from here)

   "By 6 PM, she HAD BEEN WORKING for nine hours."
   the wave 〜 lasts a long time and reaches the past dot ●
```

> ⏳ Compare with Past Continuous (Ch. 08): that was a single **moment** in progress; this one
> measures the **whole stretch of time** leading **up to** a past point.

## 5. 🧮 Formula

Model verb: **work**. Helper: **had been** (same for **all** subjects! 🎉).

### ➕ Positive
```
   S  +  had been  +  V-ing
        (had been → I, you, he, she, it, we, they — NO change!)
```
| Subject | had been | V-ing | Example |
|---------|----------|-------|---------|
| I / You / He / She / It / We / They | **had been** | working | She **had been working**. |

> 🎉 Like the Past Perfect, **had been never changes** — one helper for everyone.

### ➖ Negative
```
   S  +  had  +  NOT  +  been  +  V-ing
        (hadn't been)
```
- I **hadn't been working**. · They **hadn't been waiting** long.

### ❓ Question
```
   Had  +  S  +  been  +  V-ing  ?
```
- **Had** you **been waiting** long? · **Had** she **been crying**?

### ❓➖ Negative Question
```
   Hadn't  +  S  +  been  +  V-ing  ?
```
- **Hadn't** they **been working** on it for months?

### ✔️ Short Answers
| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Had you been waiting long? | Yes, I **had**. | No, I **hadn't**. |
| Had she been crying? | Yes, she **had**. | No, she **hadn't**. |

> ⚠️ **been** never changes, and the main verb is always **V-ing**: *had been **working**,
> had been **raining**, had been **trying**.*

## 6. 🏗️ Sentence Structure

```
 POSITIVE   ┌─────┬──────────┬──────────┬──────────────────┐
            │  S  │ had been │  V-ing   │ rest...          │
            │ She │ had been │ working  │ for nine hours.  │
            └─────┴──────────┴──────────┴──────────────────┘

 NEGATIVE   ┌─────┬─────┬─────┬──────┬──────────┐
            │  S  │ had │ not │ been │  V-ing   │
            │  I  │ had │ not │ been │ sleeping │
            └─────┴─────┴─────┴──────┴──────────┘

 QUESTION   ┌─────┬─────┬──────┬──────────┬─────────┐
            │ Had │  S  │ been │  V-ing   │ rest ?  │
            │ Had │ you │ been │ waiting? │         │
            └─────┴─────┴──────┴──────────┴─────────┘
```

> 💬 **Contractions:** *I had been → I'd been · they had been → they'd been · had not been →
> hadn't been.* Example: *"I'd been waiting for an hour."*

---

## 7. 📜 Grammar Rules

1. **Three parts:** *had* + *been* + **V-ing**. *had been* is the **same** for every subject.
2. Use it for the **duration** of an action **up to a past point** — often with **for / since**.
3. It often explains the **cause** of a past situation (evidence): *"He was tired because he
   **had been working**."*
4. **State verbs** (know, be, like, want, believe) do **NOT** take this form — use the Past
   Perfect: ❌ *had been knowing* → ✅ *had known*.
5. Spelling of **V-ing** follows the usual rules (Ch. 04/08).
6. **Don't overuse it** — for *how many* / a completed result, use the **Past Perfect** (Ch. 09):
   *"She **had written** three letters"* (how many), not *"had been writing three letters."*

> 🧠 **Golden rule:** like the Past Perfect, this tense needs a **past reference point** to look
> back from. It measures the **time before** that point.

---

## 8. ✏️ Spelling Rules (the V-ing)

Same **-ing** rules as Chapters 04, 08:

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs → **+ing** | work → working, rain → raining |
| Silent **-e** → drop *e* + ing | write → writing, drive → driving |
| Short CVC → double consonant | run → running, sit → sitting |
| Ends in **-ie** → *ie → y* + ing | lie → lying, die → dying |

> 📌 *had* and *been* never change spelling. All the work is on the **-ing** verb.

## 9. 🚩 Signal Words

```
 DURATION:        for · since · how long · all day/morning/night · the whole time
 UP-TO-A-POINT:   by · by the time · before · when · until
 CONTEXT:         because (cause/result) · so (result)
```

| Signal | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **for** | She **had been working for** three hours. |
| **since** | He **had been waiting since** noon. |
| **how long** | **How long had** you **been studying**? |
| **all day** | They **had been driving all day**. |
| **before** | I **had been living** there for years **before** I moved. |
| **by the time** | **By the time** help arrived, they **had been waiting** for hours. |
| **because** | She was tired **because** she **had been running**. |

> 💡 **for/since + how long** = the classic trigger. *for* + a length of time (*for two hours*);
> *since* + a starting point (*since 2 o'clock*).

## 10. ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| She **had been knowing** him for years. | She **had known** him for years. | state verb → Past Perfect |
| I **had been work** for hours. | I **had been working** for hours. | need **-ing** |
| They **had being** waiting. | They **had been** waiting. | it's *been*, not *being* |
| He **has been** running before he arrived. | He **had been** running before he arrived. | past context → *had* |
| We **had been driving** 300 km. *(distance/result)* | We **had driven** 300 km. | a result/amount → Past Perfect |
| By 6 PM, she **had been finishing** the report. | By 6 PM, she **had finished** the report. | completed result → Past Perfect |
| I **had been waited** for an hour. | I **had been waiting** for an hour. | active → *waiting*, not *waited* |

> 📌 **Top Azerbaijani traps:** (1) using this form with **state verbs** (*had been knowing* ❌),
> and (2) choosing it for a **completed result/amount** instead of the simple Past Perfect.

## 11. ⚖️ Comparison with Similar Tenses

### 🆚 Past Perfect Continuous vs. Past Perfect (the key comparison!)

| | **Past Perfect Continuous** | **Past Perfect** |
|--|----------------------------|------------------|
| **Focus** | **duration** (how long) / ongoing | **completion** (how many) / result |
| **Form** | had been + V-ing | had + V3 |
| **Example** | She **had been writing** for hours. | She **had written** three letters. |
| **Feel** | the **activity** and its length | the **finished result** |

```
   had been writing  →  〜〜〜〜〜〜●   (the long process, how long)
   had written       →  ✅●           (the finished result, how many)
```

> 🧠 **Choose by the question:** *How long?* → Continuous (*had been writing*). *How many /
> finished?* → simple Past Perfect (*had written*).

### 🆚 Past Perfect Continuous vs. Past Continuous

| | **Past Perfect Continuous** | **Past Continuous** |
|--|----------------------------|---------------------|
| **Reference** | duration **up to** a past point | a single **moment** in the past |
| **Form** | had been + V-ing | was/were + V-ing |
| **Example** | By 6, she **had been working** for 9 hrs. | At 6, she **was working**. |

```
   Past Continuous:          ──[ working ]──●(at 6)     (in progress AT the moment)
   Past Perfect Continuous:  [===== working =====]●(by 6) (the whole stretch BEFORE 6)
```

### 🆚 The "evidence" pair — was raining vs had been raining

| Sentence | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| It **was raining** when I left. | rain in progress at that moment |
| The ground was wet; it **had been raining**. | rain happened **before** (now stopped) — the cause |

> 💡 **had been + V-ing** is the **detective tense**: you see a past **result** (wet ground,
> tired face, red eyes) and explain the **earlier cause**.

## 12. 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani Translation Tips

1. **Past Perfect Continuous ≈ Azerbaijani duration + "-ırdı" / "neçə saatdır ...-irdi":**
   - *She **had been working** for hours* → *O, saatlar**dır işləyirdi*** / *saatlarla işləyirdi.*
   - *I **had been waiting** since noon* → *Günortadan bəri gözləyirdim.*

2. **The "for/since" part** is the heart:
   - *for three hours* → *üç saat**dır*** / *üç saat ərzində*
   - *since 2 o'clock* → *saat 2-dən bəri*

3. **had been** has no separate word in Azerbaijani — the **duration phrase** (*saatlardır,
   ... bəri*) carries the meaning. But in English you **must** say *had been + V-ing*.

4. **Cause/result:** *Yorğun idi, çünki bütün gün işləyirdi* → *He was tired because he **had
   been working** all day.*

> 💡 **Strategy:** if the Azerbaijani sentence answers **"nə qədər müddət?"** (how long) about
> a past-before-past action, English wants **had been + V-ing**. If it answers **"neçə dəfə? /
> bitdimi?"** (how many / finished), use the simple **had + V3**. ⏳

## 23. 🗣️ Speaking Tips

- ✅ Use it to **explain a past state**: *"I was tired because I **had been working**."*
- ✅ Use **for / since** to give the duration, and **how long** to ask about it.
- ✅ Use **contractions** ('d been) — it sounds natural and fast.
- ⚠️ Don't use it with **state verbs** (*know, be, want*) — use the Past Perfect.
- ⚠️ For a **finished amount** (*three letters, 300 km*), use the Past Perfect, not this.

> 🗣️ **Shadow:** *"I'd been waiting for an hour."* · *"She'd been crying."* · *"How long had
> you been studying?"*

---

## 24. ✍️ Writing Tips

- Use it to give the **backstory and cause** of a past situation (why someone was tired, why
  the ground was wet) — richer than a plain Past Simple.
- Combine **result (Past Simple/Past Perfect) + cause (Past Perfect Continuous)**: *"She failed
  the exam; she **had been working** two jobs and **hadn't been sleeping**."*
- Don't overuse it — switch back to Past Perfect for completed facts and amounts.

## 28. ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Past Perfect Continuous or Past Perfect?**
*How long / ongoing / cause?* → Continuous (*had been working*). *How many / finished result?*
→ simple Past Perfect (*had worked*).

**Q2: Past Perfect Continuous or Past Continuous?**
*Was/were + -ing* = a **moment** in progress. *Had been + -ing* = the **whole duration up to**
a past point.

**Q3: Can I use it with state verbs?**
No — *know, be, like, want, believe* use the **Past Perfect**: *I **had known** him for years.*

**Q4: Is *had been* the same for everyone?**
Yes! 🎉 No agreement: I/you/he/she/it/we/they all use **had been**.

**Q5: been or being?**
It's **been**. *had **been** waiting* ✅ / *had **being** waiting* ❌.

**Q6: What does 'd been mean?**
**'d been + -ing** is always **had been** (Past Perfect Continuous). (*'d* alone can be had/would,
but *'d been + -ing* is unambiguous.)

# 📚 Mini Story — *"The Long Wait"* (≈320 words)

> The **Past Perfect Continuous** shows **how long** things had been happening before the main
> moment — and **why** people felt the way they did. Watch the duration and the cause. Read aloud.

By the time the train finally **arrived**, the passengers on Platform 3 **were** furious. They
**had been waiting** for almost three hours. The board **had been showing** "DELAYED" since
early morning, and no one **had been telling** them why.

Leyla **sat** on her suitcase, exhausted. She **had been travelling** since dawn, and her feet
**ached**. Next to her, an old man **had been reading** the same newspaper for an hour; he
**had** clearly **been pretending** to be calm, but his fingers **were** tapping nervously.

A young mother **was** trying to soothe her baby, who **had been crying** on and off the whole
time. "I'm so sorry," she **kept** saying. "He **hadn't been sleeping** well."

Then, finally, the announcement **came**. The train **was** approaching. The crowd **stood** up
as one. Leyla **noticed** that the rails **were** wet — it **had been raining** outside while
they **had been waiting** in the stuffy hall.

As the doors **opened**, a tired conductor **stepped** out. "I apologise for the delay," he
**said**. "There **had been** a fault on the line. Crews **had been working** on it since
five AM."

The passengers **grumbled**, but slowly their anger **faded**. They **had been blaming** the
staff — but the staff **had been struggling** too.

Leyla **found** a seat by the window and finally **relaxed**. She **had been dreading** this
journey for weeks, and now the hardest part **was** over. As the train **pulled** away, she
**looked** at the platform, where a few latecomers **were** still **running**.

She **smiled**. At least she **wasn't** one of them. She **had been waiting** so long that, for
once, she **was** early.

### 🔎 Story Notes — Spot the Grammar

- **Past Perfect Continuous (duration / cause before the main scene):** *had been waiting, had
  been showing, had been telling, had been travelling, had been reading, had been pretending,
  had been crying, hadn't been sleeping, had been raining, had been working, had been blaming,
  had been struggling, had been dreading.*
- **Past Simple (the main scene — the train arriving):** *arrived, were, sat, ached, came,
  stood, noticed, opened, stepped, said, grumbled, faded, found, relaxed, pulled, looked,
  smiled.*
- **The key contrast:** the **arrival scene** is Past **Simple**; everything that explains
  **how long** people had waited and **why** they felt that way is Past Perfect **Continuous**.
  ⏳⏪
- **The "evidence" move:** *"the rails were wet — it **had been raining**"* → a past result +
  its earlier cause. That's the detective use from §11.
- **Pattern to copy:** tell the moment in Past Simple, then explain the **build-up** with *had
  been + V-ing* (duration + cause).

# 🧠 Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

1. **"HAD BEEN + ING = the long action leading up to a past moment."** ⏳ Think of a runner
   crossing a past finish line — you measure how long they'd been running.

2. **how LONG → had been + -ing · how MANY → had + V3.** Duration vs result. Ask the question
   first, then choose.

3. **The detective rule** 🕵️: see a past **clue** (wet ground, red eyes, tired face) → explain
   the **cause** with *had been + -ing*.

4. **"had been never changes."** 🎉 One helper for everyone — no agreement.

5. **It's *been*, not *being*.** *had **been** waiting* ✅. Say "BEEN" with a long *ee* in your
   head.

6. **State verbs are statues** 🗿 (still!) — *know, want, be* stay simple: *had known*, not
   *had been knowing*.

# 🧬 EXPANSION PACK — Past Perfect Continuous (Go Deeper)

> You asked to **EXPAND**. These advanced notes (E1–E12) take you from "I know *had been +
> V-ing*" to "I use it like a native." Each block builds on §5 and §11. E10 is a **synthesis
> of all four past tenses** — your Part 2 master map.

---

## E1. 🗣️ Past Perfect Continuous in Reported Speech (backshift)

When we **report** speech, the **Present Perfect Continuous** and the **Past Continuous** both
shift back to the **Past Perfect Continuous**.

```
   Direct                          →   Reported
   "I have been waiting."          →   He said he HAD BEEN WAITING.
   "I was working all morning."    →   She said she HAD BEEN WORKING all morning.
```

| Direct | Reported (Past Perfect Continuous) |
|--------|-----------------------------------|
| "We **have been driving** for hours." | They said they **had been driving** for hours. |
| "She **was crying**." | He said she **had been crying**. |

> 💡 Like the Past Perfect, this is the **"end of the road"** for backshift. (Full reported
> speech = Ch. 25.) 🇦🇿 *"...işləyirdi" dedi.*

---

## E2. 🕵️ Cause vs Evidence — the two "results" interpretations

This tense almost always points to a **past result**. There are two flavours:

| Type | The action… | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Action just stopped** (result of the action) | had finished just before | He was out of breath; he **had been running**. |
| **Action still going** (interrupted/continuing) | was still in progress at the point | When I found her, she **had been crying** (and still was). |

```
   just stopped:   [==== running ====]●(now panting)
   still going:    [==== crying ==========●========>  (continues past the point)
```

> 📌 Context decides which. Both share the same form; both explain **why** the past scene
> looked the way it did (the **detective** use).

---

## E3. 🔀 Borderline verbs — *live / work / study / teach / feel*

Some verbs work in **both** the simple Past Perfect and the Past Perfect Continuous, with a
**tiny** meaning shift (permanent/result vs temporary/duration):

| Past Perfect (had + V3) | Past Perfect Continuous (had been + V-ing) |
|-------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| She **had lived** there for ten years. *(fact / longer-term)* | She **had been living** there for ten years. *(emphasis on the ongoing stretch)* |
| He **had worked** there since 2010. *(neutral)* | He **had been working** there since 2010. *(activity, maybe temporary)* |
| I **had studied** French for years. *(fact)* | I **had been studying** French for years. *(the effort over time)* |

> 💡 With these verbs + *for/since*, **both are usually fine** and mean almost the same. The
> Continuous just **highlights the duration/activity** more.

---

## E4. 🗿 State verbs stay SIMPLE (no continuous)

True **state verbs** never take the continuous, even with *for/since*. Use the **simple Past
Perfect**:

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---------|---------|
| had been knowing | **had known** |
| had been wanting | **had wanted** |
| had been believing | **had believed** |
| had been owning | **had owned** |
| had been being (tired) | **had been** (tired) |

**State verb families to keep simple:** *know, believe, understand, want, need, like, love,
hate, own, belong, seem, contain.*

> 🧠 **Statue test** 🗿: if the verb is about a **state in the mind** or **possession** (not a
> visible activity), keep it **had + V3**, not *had been + -ing*.

---

## E5. 🔁 Temporary & repeated actions

The Past Perfect Continuous suits **temporary** situations and **repeated** actions up to a
past point:

| Example | Note |
|---------|------|
| He **had been staying** with friends until he found a flat. | temporary (not permanent → not *had stayed*) |
| She **had been calling** me all week. | repeated action over a period |
| They **had been meeting** every Friday before the lockdown. | repeated routine up to a point |

> 📌 For a **permanent**/long-term fact, the simple Past Perfect can sound better: *"He **had
> lived** there all his life."*

---

## E6. ⚖️ had been + V-ing vs had + V3 with *for/since* (the fine line)

Both can take *for/since*, but they answer **different questions**:

| Question | Tense | Example |
|----------|-------|---------|
| **How long was it going?** | had been + V-ing | She **had been writing** for two hours. |
| **How much / how many done?** | had + V3 | She **had written** two chapters. |
| **A state lasting up to then?** | had + V3 (state verbs) | He **had known** her for years. |

```
   Activity + duration  → had been + -ing   (the process)
   Quantity / result    → had + V3           (the outcome)
   State + duration     → had + V3           (states can't be continuous)
```

> 🧠 **Decision tree:** ① Is it a **state verb**? → had + V3. ② Else, are you counting a
> **result/amount**? → had + V3. ③ Else (duration of an activity)? → had been + V-ing.

---

## E7. ⏱️ "How long…?" — asking & answering

The natural question for this tense:

```
   How long  +  had  +  S  +  been  +  V-ing  ?
   How long  +  had  +  you + been  + waiting ?
```

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| **How long had** you **been waiting**? | (I'd been waiting) **for** an hour. |
| **How long had** they **been living** there? | (They'd been living there) **since** 2015. |
| **How long had** it **been raining**? | (It had been raining) **all night**. |

> 💡 Answer with **for** (a length: *for an hour*) or **since** (a start point: *since noon*).

---

## E8. ➖ Negatives — what *hadn't been + V-ing* emphasises

The negative stresses that an activity **was not happening over a period** before a past point:

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| He failed because he **hadn't been studying**. | no studying over the period (the cause) |
| I was awake because I **hadn't been sleeping** well. | poor sleep over recent nights |
| They **hadn't been getting** along for months. | a bad situation over time |

> 📌 Often pairs with a **result**: *"She was weak; she **hadn't been eating** properly."* The
> negative explains the **lack of activity** behind a past state.

---

## E9. 🔗 Word order with *by the time / before / when*

The connector sets the **past point**; the Past Perfect Continuous gives the **build-up**:

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **By the time** + Past Simple, + had been + -ing | **By the time** help came, they **had been waiting** for hours. |
| had been + -ing + **before** + Past Simple | They **had been arguing** for an hour **before** he left. |
| **When** + Past Simple, + had been + -ing | **When** I saw her, she **had been crying**. |

> ⚠️ The clause order can flip, but the **tenses keep the meaning**: the *-ing* action is the
> longer, earlier build-up; the Past Simple is the **point** you measure to.

## E10. 🗺️ PART 2 MASTER MAP — All Four Past Tenses Side by Side

> Now that Part 2 is complete, here is the **whole past system** in one view. Same situation
> (working at an office), four tenses:

| Tense | Form | Focus | Example |
|-------|------|-------|---------|
| **Past Simple** | V2 | a completed past event | She **worked** late yesterday. |
| **Past Continuous** | was/were + V-ing | in progress at a past **moment** | At 8 PM, she **was working**. |
| **Past Perfect** | had + V3 | the **earlier** of two past events / result | By 8 PM, she **had finished**. |
| **Past Perfect Continuous** | had been + V-ing | **duration** up to a past point / cause | By 8 PM, she **had been working** for 9 hrs. |

```
 TIMELINE (looking back from NOW):

   [== had been working ==]●────●──────────────●─────────► NOW
    (duration up to a pt)  had  was working   worked
                           finished (moment)  (event)
   Perfect Continuous   Perfect   Continuous   Simple
```

**Decision flow:**
```
   One simple past event?                → Past Simple        (worked)
   In progress at a past moment?         → Past Continuous    (was working)
   Earlier than another past event?      → Past Perfect       (had finished)
   How LONG up to a past point / cause?  → Past Perfect Cont. (had been working)
```

# 📚 Mini Story #2 — *"The Finish Line"* (≈330 words)

> Another **STORY**. Watch the engine again: the **Past Simple** carries the race-day moment,
> and the **Past Perfect Continuous** shows the **long build-up** — how long Nigar had been
> training and why she felt the way she did. Read aloud.

When Nigar **reached** the starting line, her heart **was** pounding. She **looked** at the
thousands of runners around her and **smiled**. She **had been training** for this marathon for
eleven months.

It **hadn't been** easy. For almost a year, she **had been waking** up at five every morning.
She **had been running** in the rain, in the cold, in the dark. Her family **had been telling**
her to rest, but she **hadn't been listening**. She **had been dreaming** of this day for too
long.

The gun **fired**, and the crowd **surged** forward. By the tenth kilometre, Nigar **felt**
strong. Her legs **had been carrying** her steadily, and her breathing **was** calm. She
**had been pacing** herself carefully, just as her coach **had taught** her.

But at kilometre thirty, everything **changed**. Her legs **began** to ache. The other runners
**had been pushing** hard, and now her body **was** screaming at her to stop. Sweat **had been
running** into her eyes for an hour. She **wanted** to give up.

Then she **remembered**. All those early mornings. All the months she **had been sacrificing**
weekends, parties, sleep. She **hadn't been doing** all that to quit now.

She **kept** going.

When the finish line finally **appeared**, Nigar **could** barely **see** it through her tears.
The crowd **was** roaring. She **crossed** the line and **collapsed** onto the grass, exhausted
but glowing.

A volunteer **handed** her a medal. "You did it," she **said**.

Nigar **laughed** weakly. Her muscles **ached** because she **had been pushing** them to the
limit — but it **was** the best pain she **had** ever **felt**.

She **had been chasing** this finish line for almost a year. And now, at last, she **was**
standing beyond it.

- **Past Simple (the race-day moments):** *reached, looked, smiled, fired, surged, felt, began,
  changed, wanted, remembered, kept, appeared, crossed, collapsed, handed, said, laughed,
  ached, was.*
- **The key contrast:** the **race itself** (start, surge, ache, finish) is Past **Simple**;
  the **eleven months of preparation** behind it is Past Perfect **Continuous** — that's what
  gives the ending its emotional weight. ⏳⏪
- **The "cause" move:** *"Her muscles ached because she **had been pushing** them to the limit"*
  → a past result + its earlier cause (the detective use, §11).
- **State verb note:** *"It **hadn't been** easy"* uses *be* — a **state**, so it stays *had
  been* (no -ing). You can't say *had been being easy*.
- **Pattern to copy:** tell the **big moment** in Past Simple, then reveal the **long effort**
  with *had been + V-ing* (duration + cause + payoff).

> action had lasted (use *for* or *since*). Then retell the ending in **3 sentences**, keeping
> at least **two** Past Perfect Continuous verbs.
