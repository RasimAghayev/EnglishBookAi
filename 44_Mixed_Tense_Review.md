# 1. 📌 How tenses are built — TIME × ASPECT

Every English tense is a combination of **TIME** (when) and **ASPECT** (how the action relates to
that time):

```
                    SIMPLE        CONTINUOUS        PERFECT           PERFECT CONTINUOUS
                  (fact/habit)   (in progress)   (completed/result) (duration + still relevant)
   ┌───────────┬──────────────┬────────────────┬──────────────────┬────────────────────────┐
   │ PRESENT   │ do / does    │ am/is/are +-ing│ have/has + V3    │ have/has been + -ing   │
   │ PAST      │ did (V2)     │ was/were +-ing │ had + V3         │ had been + -ing        │
   │ FUTURE    │ will + V1    │ will be + -ing │ will have + V3   │ will have been + -ing  │
   └───────────┴──────────────┴────────────────┴──────────────────┴────────────────────────┘
   = 3 TIMES × 4 ASPECTS = 12 TENSES
```

> 🧠 **The whole system in one idea:** pick your **TIME** (row) and your **ASPECT** (column) — the
> cell is your tense. Learn the **4 aspects** once, and all 12 tenses fall into place. 🇦🇿 3 zaman ×
> 4 aspekt = 12 zaman.

# 2. 🎚️ The 4 ASPECTS — what each one MEANS (the heart of everything)

If you understand the four aspects, you understand all twelve tenses. Here they are with **one verb
(work)** across all three times:

```
 ┌──────────────────────┬───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ SIMPLE               │ a fact, a habit, a completed whole.                           │
 │                      │ "I work." · "I worked." · "I will work."                      │
 ├──────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
 │ CONTINUOUS           │ in progress, temporary, around a point in time.               │
 │                      │ "I am working." · "I was working." · "I will be working."     │
 ├──────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
 │ PERFECT              │ completed BEFORE a point, with a result/connection.           │
 │                      │ "I have worked." · "I had worked." · "I will have worked."    │
 ├──────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
 │ PERFECT CONTINUOUS   │ duration UP TO a point (how long), often still relevant.      │
 │                      │ "I have been working." · "I had been working." ·              │
 │                      │ "I will have been working."                                   │
 └──────────────────────┴───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

> 💡 **The two big questions** that pick your aspect:
> 1. *Is it in progress / temporary?* → **continuous** (–ing).
> 2. *Is it completed before / connected to a point, or about how long?* → **perfect** (have/had +
> V3) or **perfect continuous** (have been + –ing).
> Neither? → **simple.**

# 5. 🥊 THE CONFUSABLE PAIRS — every tricky showdown in one place

This is the most useful section in the book. Whenever two tenses fight, come here. ⚔️

### ⚔️ Present Simple ↔ Present Continuous
```
   I work in a bank.        → permanent / habit
   I'm working from home.    → temporary / right now
   ⚠️ State verbs (know, want, like) → ALWAYS simple: ❌ I'm knowing → ✅ I know.
```

### ⚔️ Present Perfect ↔ Past Simple   (the classic!)
```
   I have lost my keys.      → result NOW (I still can't get in) · no time said
   I lost my keys yesterday. → finished time (yesterday) → Past Simple
   🔑 A finished time word (yesterday, in 2010, ago) FORCES Past Simple.
   🇦🇿 İkisi də "itirdim" olur — amma vaxt deyilibsə → Past Simple.
```

### ⚔️ Present Perfect ↔ Present Perfect Continuous
```
   I've written three emails.       → result / HOW MANY (finished)
   I've been writing emails.         → activity / HOW LONG (duration)
```

### ⚔️ Past Simple ↔ Past Continuous
```
   When she called, I cooked.        → I cooked AFTER she called (sequence)
   When she called, I was cooking.    → cooking was already in progress (background)
```

### ⚔️ Past Simple ↔ Past Perfect
```
   When I arrived, the train left.    → it left as/after I arrived
   When I arrived, the train had left. → it left BEFORE I arrived (earlier event)
```

### ⚔️ will ↔ going to ↔ Present Continuous (futures)
```
   I'll help you.            → decision NOW / offer / prediction
   I'm going to study tonight. → prior PLAN / intention
   It's going to rain.        → evidence (dark clouds)
   I'm meeting Tom at six.     → fixed ARRANGEMENT (diary)
```

### ⚔️ Future Perfect ↔ Future Perfect Continuous
```
   By June, I'll have finished the book.        → completed (how many / result)
   By June, I'll have been writing for a year.   → duration (how long)
```

### ⚔️ used to ↔ would ↔ Past Simple
```
   I used to live there.     → past habit/state (now finished) — states OK
   We would visit every summer. → repeated past action (NOT states)
   I lived there in 2010.     → one finished fact with a time
```

> 💡 Bookmark this section. 90% of tense mistakes are choosing the wrong member of one of these
> pairs. 🇦🇿 Səhvlərin əksəriyyəti bu cütlüklərdən birində yanlış seçimdir.

# 6. 🔔 SIGNAL WORDS MASTER GRID

Words that **pull** you toward a tense:

```
 ┌──────────────────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ SIGNAL WORD                          │ TENSE IT SUGGESTS                     │
 ├──────────────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────┤
 │ always, usually, every day, often    │ Present Simple                        │
 │ now, right now, at the moment, look!  │ Present Continuous                    │
 │ just, already, yet, ever, never, so far│ Present Perfect                      │
 │ for / since (+ duration, up to now)   │ Present Perfect (Continuous)          │
 │ yesterday, ago, last…, in 2010, then  │ Past Simple                          │
 │ while, as, when (+ background)        │ Past Continuous                      │
 │ by the time, already (in the past), before│ Past Perfect                    │
 │ tomorrow, next…, soon, I think/probably│ Future (will)                       │
 │ plan, intend, going to, evidence      │ going to                             │
 │ by + future time, by then            │ Future Perfect                       │
 │ by … for + duration (future)          │ Future Perfect Continuous            │
 └──────────────────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────────────────┘
```
> ⚠️ Signals are **clues, not laws** — context can override them. But *yesterday → Past Simple* and
> *for/since + now → Present Perfect* are nearly always reliable.

# 7. 🧊 THE STATE-VERB RULE (applies across ALL continuous tenses)

Some verbs describe **states**, not actions, so they **avoid all continuous forms**:

```
   THINKING:  know, believe, understand, remember, mean, doubt, recognise
   FEELING:   like, love, hate, want, need, prefer
   SENSES:    see, hear, smell, taste (when involuntary)
   BEING/HAVING: be, seem, belong, own, have (=possess), contain, cost
```
```
 ❌ I am knowing the answer.   ✅ I know the answer.
 ❌ She is wanting tea.         ✅ She wants tea.
 ⚠️ Some flip meaning: "I'm having lunch" (action ✅) vs "I have a car" (state ❌-ing).
```
> 🧠 If it's a **state** (not a deliberate action), use the **simple** form in every time. 🇦🇿 Hal
> felləri heç bir continuous formada işlənmir.

# 9. 🇦🇿 AZERBAIJANI-INTERFERENCE MASTER LIST

The mistakes that come from translating directly from Azerbaijani — gathered in one place:

```
 1. ❌ I am knowing / wanting.        ✅ I know / want.        (state verbs → simple)
 2. ❌ I lost my keys (result now).   ✅ I have lost my keys.  (result now → Present Perfect)
 3. ❌ I have seen him yesterday.     ✅ I saw him yesterday.   (finished time → Past Simple)
 4. ❌ If it will rain, I'll stay.    ✅ If it rains, I'll stay.(no future in if-clause)
 5. ❌ He said he is tired.           ✅ He said he was tired.  (backshift in reported speech)
 6. ❌ The house built in 1990.       ✅ The house was built.   (passive needs "be")
 7. ❌ I was wrote / had went.        ✅ was written / had gone. (use V3, not V2)
 8. ❌ When I arrived, the train left.✅ ...had left.            (earlier event → Past Perfect)
 9. ❌ I born in Baku.                ✅ I was born in Baku.     (fixed passive)
 10.❌ Since two hours.               ✅ For two hours.          (for + duration; since + start point)
```

> 🧠 Azerbaijani is a **wonderful base** — it has habit (-ır), continuous (-ırdı), perfect
> (-mışdı), conditional (-sa), and passive (-ıl). The traps come from places where English makes a
> distinction Azerbaijani doesn't (perfect vs past, state verbs, no future after "if").

```

# 📚 MINI STORY — *"A Life in Twelve Tenses"*
### (one short life, every tense) ⏳

> 🎯 This story is a **showcase**: it deliberately uses **all twelve tenses** plus special forms.
> **Bold** = the tense moments. After reading, see if you can name each one (the key helps). Then
> read 🇦🇿 and do the questions.

## 🇬🇧 A Life in Twelve Tenses

Maria **lives** in a small town by the sea. *(present simple)* Right now, she **is sitting** on the
balcony, watching the boats. *(present continuous)*

She **has lived** here for forty years. *(present perfect)* In fact, she **has been painting** the
same harbour since she was young. *(present perfect continuous)*

She still remembers the day she **arrived.** *(past simple)* It **was raining**, and she **was
carrying** a single suitcase. *(past continuous)* She **had left** the city after her husband
**had been working** abroad for too long. *(past perfect / past perfect continuous)*

Tomorrow, she **will open** her first exhibition. *(future simple — will)* She **is going to
invite** the whole town. *(going to)* At this time tomorrow, hundreds of people **will be walking**
through the gallery. *(future continuous)*

By the end of the year, she **will have sold** dozens of paintings. *(future perfect)* And by her
next birthday, she **will have been painting** that harbour for fifty years. *(future perfect
continuous)*

"I **used to** think no one would ever notice my work," she says. *(used to)* "If I **had given
up**, none of this **would have happened.**" *(third conditional)*

The boats **are still painted** every spring, just as they always **were.** *(passive / past)*

And the sea? The sea, she **will tell** you, **has been here** far longer than any of us — and
**will still be here** long after we **are gone.**

    = the duration that CAUSED her to leave. Both are "before" the main past story.
 3. "If I had given up, none of this would have happened." It tells us she did NOT give up
    (unreal past) — and that her not giving up made everything possible.
 4. Future Perfect: "she will have sold dozens of paintings." Future Perfect Continuous:
    "she will have been painting … for fifty years."
 5. (Your own three sentences — check each tense is correct!)
```

### The final polish — the skills that span every tense. ☕

> 🎯 §1–§29 gathered the whole system. This pack sharpens the **cross-cutting skills**: minimal-pair

## E1 · The MINIMAL-PAIR gym — tiny change, huge meaning 🥋
The fastest way to *feel* tense differences: change **one word** and watch the meaning move.

```
   I live here.              ↔  I'm living here.            (permanent ↔ temporary)
   I've read it.             ↔  I've been reading it.        (finished ↔ ongoing)
   I lost my keys.           ↔  I've lost my keys.           (past fact ↔ result now)
   When she came, I left.    ↔  When she came, I'd left.     (after ↔ before)
   When she came, I was leaving.                            (in progress at that moment)
   I'll call him.            ↔  I'm going to call him.        (decision now ↔ prior plan)
   By June I'll have written.  ↔  By June I'll have been writing. (done ↔ duration)
```
> 💡 Say each pair aloud and feel the shift. This trains your **ear**, not just your rules. 🇦🇿 Bir
> sözü dəyiş — məna dəyişir. Cütlükləri ucadan oxu.

---

## E2 · Time expressions that work with SEVERAL tenses ⏰
Some time words attach to different tenses and **change meaning**:

```
   this morning:
      "I've drunk three coffees this morning." (it's still morning → present perfect)
      "I drank three coffees this morning."     (morning is over → past simple)

   for:
      "I've lived here for 5 years."   (up to now → present perfect)
      "I lived there for 5 years."      (finished → past simple)
      "I'll have lived here for 5 years." (up to a future point → future perfect)

   recently / lately:
      "I've been tired lately."         (present perfect — up to now)
      "She left recently."               (past simple — also fine)
```
> 🧠 The same word can take **different tenses** — the **time frame** (finished or not?) decides.
> 🇦🇿 Eyni vaxt sözü müxtəlif zamanlarla işlənə bilər — çərçivə bitibmi, yox?

---

## E3 · NO future after time words — the time-clause rule ⛔
After *when, after, before, as soon as, until, by the time, once, the moment* → use **present** (or
present perfect) for a future meaning, **never** *will*:

```
 ❌ When I will arrive, I'll call.       ✅ When I arrive, I'll call.
 ❌ I'll wait until you will finish.       ✅ I'll wait until you finish.
 ❌ After I will eat, I'll rest.           ✅ After I eat / After I've eaten, I'll rest.
 ❌ As soon as it will stop raining...     ✅ As soon as it stops raining...
```
🔑 The **main clause** can have *will*; the **time clause** uses **present**.
> 🇦🇿 *when/after/until/as soon as* + **present** (gələcək məna olsa belə). "will" əsas cümlədə
> qalır.

---

## E4 · Mixing tenses in ONE sentence — valid combinations 🔗
Two clauses can have **different tenses** — as long as the time logic works:

```
 ✅ I think (present) she left (past) yesterday.
 ✅ He said (past) he will come (… → would come in reported speech).
 ✅ I've finished (perfect) the work I started (past) on Monday.
 ✅ By the time you read (present) this, I'll have gone (future perfect).
 ✅ She was tired (past) because she had been working (past perfect cont.).
```
🔑 The rule isn't "all clauses match" — it's **"each clause fits its own time, and the sequence
makes sense."** Watch especially **reported speech** (backshift) and **time clauses** (no future).
> 🇦🇿 Cümlədəki hər hissə öz vaxtına uyğun olmalıdır — hamısı eyni zaman olmaq MƏCBUR deyil.

---

## E5 · Register — how text TYPE shifts tense choice 📑
The same idea is told in different tenses depending on the **kind of text**:

```
   STORYTELLING (narrative):  "She walked in. It was raining. She had forgotten her coat."
                              → past tenses, layered (Ch 27)
   NEWS / RECENT:             "A fire has broken out downtown."
                              → present perfect for fresh news
   SCIENCE / FACTS:           "Water boils at 100°C. The sample is heated."
                              → present simple + passive (timeless/objective)
   ACADEMIC CITATION:         "Smith argues that… / The study found that…"
                              → present for claims, past for completed research
   INSTRUCTIONS / PROCESS:    "First the beans are roasted, then ground."
                              → present simple passive
```
> 💡 Before choosing a tense, ask: *what kind of text is this?* Narration, news, science, and
> instructions each have a **default voice**. 🇦🇿 Mətnin növü zaman seçimini dəyişir.

---

## E6 · The SAME verb, two meanings — state vs action 🔄
Several verbs flip between **state** (no -ing) and **action** (-ing OK) depending on meaning:

```
   have:   I have a car. (own → state, no -ing)   ·  I'm having lunch. (eat → action ✅)
   think:  I think it's true. (opinion → state)    ·  I'm thinking about it. (process ✅)
   see:    I see what you mean. (understand → state)·  I'm seeing the doctor. (meeting ✅)
   taste:  This tastes good. (state)               ·  I'm tasting the soup. (action ✅)
   be:     He is rude. (permanent)                 ·  He's being rude. (acting that way now ✅)
   feel:   I feel it's wrong. (opinion)            ·  I'm feeling sick. (physical, both OK)
```
> 🧠 Ask: is this a **state** (a condition that just *is*) or an **action** (something *being
> done*)? The same verb can be either. 🇦🇿 Eyni fel həm hal, həm hərəkət ola bilər — məna həll edir.

---

## E7 · Present tenses for the FUTURE — consolidated 🔮
Two present forms point to the future (you met these in Part 3):

```
   PRESENT SIMPLE → timetables / schedules (fixed, official):
      "The train leaves at 6." · "The shop opens at nine." · "The exam starts Monday."

   PRESENT CONTINUOUS → personal arrangements (diary plans):
      "I'm meeting Tom tomorrow." · "We're flying to Baku on Friday."
```
```
   timetable/official → present simple ·  personal arrangement → present continuous
   prediction/decision → will         ·  plan/intention/evidence → going to
```
> 🇦🇿 Cədvəl → present simple; şəxsi razılaşma → present continuous; proqnoz → will; niyyət →
> going to.

---

## E8 · The "HOW LONG" family — duration across all times ⏳
"How long" questions almost always use a **perfect continuous** (or perfect with state verbs):

```
   PRESENT:  How long have you been waiting?   → "For an hour." (up to now)
   PAST:     How long had you been waiting (when the bus came)? → (up to a past point)
   FUTURE:   How long will you have been waiting (by 9)?        → (up to a future point)
   STATE VERB: How long have you known her?    → "known", not "been knowing"!
```
🔑 **How long + duration → perfect continuous.** But with **state verbs** (know, have, be) → plain
**perfect** (*have known*, not *have been knowing*).
> 🇦🇿 "Nə qədər vaxtdır?" → perfect continuous; amma hal felləri ilə → sadə perfect.

---

## E9 · Special structures that demand a "past" form for non-past meaning 🎭
Some expressions use a **past tense** to talk about the **present/unreal** — like the 2nd
conditional:

```
   I wish I had a car.            (= I don't have one now — "had" = unreal present)
   It's time we left.             (= we should leave now — "left" = should happen)
   I'd rather you didn't smoke.    (= please don't, now)
   He talks as if he knew everything. (= he doesn't really know)
   Suppose you won the lottery...   (imaginary present)
```
🔑 After *wish, if only, it's time, would rather, as if/as though* (for unreal) → use **past
simple** for present meaning, **past perfect** for past meaning.
> 🧠 Same logic as conditionals (Ch 24): *the further from reality, the further back the tense.*
> 🇦🇿 *wish/it's time/would rather* → indiki üçün **past** forma (qeyri-real).

## E12 · 🔍 SELF-DIAGNOSIS — find & fix your own tense errors
Run your writing through this 7-point check:

```
 ✅ 1. Did I use -ing with a STATE verb? (know/want/like)  → switch to simple.
 ✅ 2. Is there a finished-time word (yesterday/ago) with present perfect? → past simple.
 ✅ 3. "since" with a duration?  → "for" (for 2 hours, since 2 o'clock).
 ✅ 4. "will" after if/when/after/until?  → present.
 ✅ 5. Reporting a past statement?  → did I backshift? (is→was, will→would)
 ✅ 6. Passive: did I use BE + V3 (not V2)? and not passivize an intransitive verb?
 ✅ 7. Two past events — is the earlier one in Past Perfect (had + V3)?
```

> these 7 buckets. 🇦🇿 Yazdığını bir dəfə **yalnız zamanlar üçün** yoxla — səhvlərin çoxu bu 7

## 🟢 ROUND 2 — The confusable pairs
```
  9. I ______ (see) that film three times. (in my life)
 10. I ______ (see) that film on Saturday.
 11. I ______ (write) two emails so far this morning. (how many)
 12. I ______ (write) emails all morning — I'm exhausted. (how long)
 13. I ______ (live) in Paris, but I moved away last year.
 14. When the guests arrived, I ______ (cook) — it was already on the table. (finished before)
```

<details>
<summary>👉 ROUND 2 — Answers & why</summary>

```
  9. have seen           (life experience, no finished time)
 10. saw                 (Saturday → past simple)
 11. have written         (result / how many → present perfect)
 12. have been writing     (duration / how long → present perfect continuous)
 13. lived               (finished period → past simple)
 14. had cooked           (finished BEFORE they arrived → past perfect)
```
> 🧠 Finished time → past simple. Result-now → present perfect. How long → perfect continuous.
> Earlier event → past perfect.
</details>

## 🟡 ROUND 3 — Special forms (conditionals, reported, passive)
```
 15. If I ______ (have) more money, I would travel. (I don't)
 16. If you ______ (heat) ice, it melts.
 17. If they ______ (warn) us, we wouldn't have come.
 18. He said he ______ (be) tired. (reporting "I am tired")
 19. She asked if I ______ (like) coffee. (reporting "Do you like coffee?")
 20. The house ______ (build) in 1990. (passive)
 21. The road ______ (repair) right now. (passive)
```

<details>
<summary>👉 ROUND 3 — Answers & why</summary>

```
 15. had                  (2nd conditional — if + past simple)
 16. heat                 (zero conditional — fact)
 17. had warned            (3rd conditional — if + past perfect)
 18. was                  (backshift: is → was)
 19. liked                (reported question — statement order, backshift)
 20. was built             (past passive)
 21. is being repaired      (present continuous passive)
```

## 🟠 ROUND 4 — Spot the mistake (the master error list)
```
 22. I am knowing the answer.
 23. I have seen him yesterday.
 24. I have lived here since three years.
 25. If it will rain, we will stay home.
 26. He said he is tired.
 27. When I arrived, the train left. (it had gone)
 28. The work is done last week.
 29. I would of helped you.
```

<details>
<summary>👉 ROUND 4 — Answers & why</summary>

```
 22. ❌ am knowing → ✅ I KNOW the answer.            (state verb → simple)
 23. ❌ have seen ... yesterday → ✅ I SAW him yesterday. (finished time → past simple)
 24. ❌ since three years → ✅ FOR three years.         (for + length; since + start point)
 25. ❌ will rain → ✅ If it RAINS...                   (no will in if-clause)
 26. ❌ is → ✅ He said he WAS tired.                   (backshift)
 27. ❌ left → ✅ ...the train HAD left.                (earlier event → past perfect)
 28. ❌ is done → ✅ The work WAS done last week.        (right passive tense)
 29. ❌ would of → ✅ I would HAVE helped you.           (always "would have")
```
</details>

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — *"The Interview"*
### (mixed tenses, told naturally) 💼

> 🎯 Story #1 was a calm *showcase* of all 12 tenses. This one is **plot-driven** — the tenses mix
> the way they really do in a tense, real-life moment. **Bold** = tense moments; the key sorts
> them. Read 🇬🇧 first, then 🇦🇿, then the questions.

## 🇬🇧 The Interview

Nargiz **had been preparing** for this interview for three weeks.

She **had read** everything about the company. She **had practised** her answers in the mirror until
she **knew** them by heart. Still, as she **sat** in the waiting room, her hands **were shaking.**

"They **will ask** about my last job," she thought. "And if they **ask** why I left, I **must** stay
calm."

A door **opened.** "Nargiz? We**'re ready** for you."

The room **was** bright. Three people **were sitting** behind a long table. One of them **was
smiling**; the others **were taking** notes.

The first questions **were** easy. She **talked** about her studies, her skills, the projects she
**had completed.** She **was** doing well — she **could feel** it.

Then the smiling man **leaned** forward. "Tell me," he **said**, "if we **offered** you this job,
what **would** you change in your first month?"

Nargiz **paused.** This **was** the question she **had been waiting** for. She **took** a breath and
**spoke** from the heart.

When she **finished**, the room **was** quiet. For a moment she **was sure** she **had said** too
much.

"Thank you," the man **said**, standing. "We **'ll be** in touch very soon."

That evening, she **was sitting** at home, replaying every word, when her phone **buzzed.** It
**was** an email.

*"We are pleased to offer you the position…"*

She **read** it three times. By this time next month, she **will have started** her new job — and
one day, when someone **asks** her how she **got** it, she **will tell** them: *"I**'d prepared** so
hard that, by the time they asked, I **had** nothing left to fear."*

## 🇦🇿 Müsahibə *(translation)*

Nərgiz üç həftə idi bu müsahibəyə hazırlaşırdı.

Şirkət haqqında hər şeyi oxumuşdu. Cavablarını güzgü qarşısında əzbərləyənə qədər məşq etmişdi. Yenə
də, gözləmə otağında oturarkən, əlləri əsirdi.

"Son işim haqqında soruşacaqlar," deyə düşündü. "Əgər niyə getdiyimi soruşsalar, sakit qalmalıyam."

Bir qapı açıldı. "Nərgiz? Sizi gözləyirik."

Otaq işıqlı idi. Üç nəfər uzun masanın arxasında oturmuşdu. Onlardan biri gülümsəyirdi; digərləri
qeyd aparırdı.

İlk suallar asan idi. Təhsili, bacarıqları, tamamladığı layihələr haqqında danışdı. Yaxşı çıxış
edirdi — bunu hiss edə bilirdi.

Sonra gülümsəyən kişi irəli əyildi. "Deyin görək," dedi, "əgər bu işi sizə təklif etsək, ilk
ayınızda nəyi dəyişərdiniz?"

Nərgiz duruxdu. Bu, gözlədiyi sual idi. Bir nəfəs aldı və ürəkdən danışdı.

Bitirəndə otaq sakit idi. Bir an çox şey dediyinə əmin oldu.

"Təşəkkür edirik," kişi ayağa qalxaraq dedi. "Çox tezliklə sizinlə əlaqə saxlayacağıq."

O axşam evdə oturub hər sözü təkrar-təkrar düşünürdü ki, telefonu vibrasiya etdi. Bir e-poçt idi.

*"Sizə vəzifəni təklif etməkdən məmnunuq…"*

Onu üç dəfə oxudu. Gələn ay bu vaxt yeni işinə başlamış olacaq — və bir gün, kimsə ondan bunu necə
əldə etdiyini soruşanda, deyəcək: *"O qədər çox hazırlaşmışdım ki, soruşanda qorxacaq heç nəyim
qalmamışdı."*

## 🔑 Story key — spot the mix
🟧 **Past Perfect / Perfect Continuous (the build-up before the interview):**
```
   had been preparing (for three weeks) · had read · had practised · had completed · had been waiting
```
🟩 **Past Continuous (the scene around the events):**
```
   were shaking · were sitting · was smiling · were taking notes · was sitting (that evening)
```
🟦 **Past Simple (the main events, in order):**
```
   sat · opened · leaned · said · paused · took · spoke · finished · buzzed · read
```
🟨 **Present / Present Perfect (inside dialogue & reflection):**
```
   "We're ready" · "We are pleased" · she could feel it
```
🟪 **Future + Conditional (her thoughts & the ending):**
```
   "They will ask" · "if they ask... I must stay calm" · "if we offered you... what would you change"
   · "We'll be in touch" · "I will have started" · "when someone asks... I will tell them"
```

> 🧠 Notice nobody *planned* to use nine tenses — they appear **naturally** because the moment
> itself spans time: the **preparation** (past perfect), the **scene** (past continuous), the
> **events** (past simple), the **hopes** (future/conditional), and the **result that still
> matters** (present perfect). That's real English. This is what the whole book has been building
> toward. 💼

    action before a future point.
