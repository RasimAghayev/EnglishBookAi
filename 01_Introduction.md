## 👋 Welcome / Xoş gəldin

Welcome, my friend. **Xoş gəldin, dostum.**

You are about to learn one of the most powerful skills in the English language:
**how to control time with words.**

In English, the difference between *"I work"*, *"I am working"*, *"I have worked"*,
and *"I had been working"* is not just grammar — it is **meaning**. Each form tells
the listener *when* something happens, *how long* it lasts, and *whether* it is
finished. This is what English **tenses** do.

> **Azərbaycanca:** İngilis dilində bir hərəkətin nə vaxt baş verdiyini, nə qədər
> davam etdiyini və bitib-bitmədiyini göstərmək üçün **zamanlar (tenses)** istifadə
> olunur. Bu kitab səni sıfırdan başlayıb usta səviyyəsinə (C2) çatdıracaq.

This book was written **especially for you** — a native Azerbaijani speaker at the
**A2–B1** level who wants clear, visual, practical explanations without confusing
academic language. We will always explain **WHY** before **HOW**.

## 🇦🇿 Special Advice for Azerbaijani Learners

A few honest notes, teacher-to-student, about where Azerbaijani speakers often struggle:

1. **Azerbaijani has fewer "perfect" forms than English.**
   English splits the past into *Past Simple*, *Present Perfect*, and *Past Perfect*.
   Don't translate word-for-word — learn the **situation** instead. We'll show you how.

2. **Word order is different.**
   Azerbaijani verb usually comes **last** (*Mən kitab oxuyuram*). English verb comes
   **early** (*I read a book*). Train your brain to put the verb near the start.

3. **The verb "to be" (am/is/are) is often dropped in Azerbaijani** but is
   **required** in English. *"O xəstədir"* → ✅ *"He **is** sick"*, not ❌ *"He sick."*

4. **Articles (a / an / the)** don't exist in Azerbaijani — they will feel optional,
   but they matter. We'll point them out.

5. **The /θ/ and /ð/ "th" sounds** don't exist in Azerbaijani. We give you exercises
   for them in the pronunciation sections.

> Don't be afraid of mistakes. **Səhvlərdən qorxma.** Mistakes are proof you are learning.
