## ① TENSES (1–10)

| # | ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | I **have seen** him yesterday. | I **saw** him yesterday. | finished time → past simple |
| 2 | I **live** here since 2010. | I'**ve lived** here since 2010. | *since* → present perfect |
| 3 | When I arrived, they **already left**. | …they **had** already **left**. | earlier past → past perfect |
| 4 | I **am knowing** the answer. | I **know** the answer. | stative verb, no continuous |
| 5 | She'**s working** here since May. | She'**s been working** here since May. | *since* + duration → perfect continuous |
| 6 | By 8 I **will finish**. | By 8 I **will have finished**. | *by* + future → future perfect |
| 7 | I **didn't went** home. | I **didn't go** home. | *did* + base verb |
| 8 | He **don't** like it. | He **doesn't** like it. | 3rd person → *doesn't* |
| 9 | I **am agree** with you. | I **agree** with you. | *agree* is a verb, no *be* |
| 10 | While I **cooked**, the phone **was ringing**. | While I **was cooking**, the phone **rang**. | background = continuous, event = simple |

## ② ARTICLES — a / an / the / zero (11–20) 🇦🇿

*(Azerbaijani has no articles — this is the #1 interference family.)*

| # | ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 11 | I am **teacher**. | I am **a teacher**. | job → *a/an* |
| 12 | She is **best** student. | She is **the best** student. | superlative → *the* |
| 13 | I like **the music**. *(in general)* | I like **music**. | general → zero article |
| 14 | He goes to **the university**. *(as a student)* | He goes to **university**. | institution as purpose → zero |
| 15 | I bought **a new furnitures**. | I bought **new furniture**. | uncountable, no plural/*a* |
| 16 | **The life** is hard. *(general)* | **Life** is hard. | abstract general → zero |
| 17 | I saw **a elephant**. | I saw **an elephant**. | vowel sound → *an* |
| 18 | She plays **the football**. | She plays **football**. | sports → no *the* |
| 19 | I have **an information**. | I have **some information**. | uncountable → *some* |
| 20 | **Sun** is hot today. | **The sun** is hot today. | unique thing → *the* |

## 🅰️ §A — LEVEL DIFFERENCES (which family bites at each level)

| Level | Most common family | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **A1–A2** | agreement, articles, *be* patterns | "He don't…", "I am teacher" |
| **B1** | tenses (did/have done), prepositions | "I have seen it yesterday" |
| **B2** | conditionals, word order, quantifiers | "If I will…", "much people" |
| **C1** | subtle articles, confused pairs, register | "the life", "sensible/sensitive" |
| **C2** | rare collocations, nuance | near-native slips only |

> 🇦🇿 *A2: uzlaşma, artikllər. B1: zamanlar, sözönləri. B2: şərtlər, söz sırası. C1: incə artikllər, qarışan cütlüklər.*

---

## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT

1. **Article errors mark a non-native instantly.** *"I am teacher"* / *"the life is hard"* are the fastest giveaways for 🇦🇿 speakers. Drill articles hard. 🇦🇿 *Artikl səhvləri dərhal nəzərə çarpır.*
2. **Wrong prepositions rarely block meaning but sound "off".** *"depend of"* is understood, but jars. Learn verbs **with** their prepositions. 🇦🇿 *Səhv sözönü mənanı pozmur, amma "yad" səslənir.*
3. **"say vs tell" is a signature error.** *Tell* needs a person (*tell me*); *say* doesn't (*say something*). 🇦🇿 *"tell" adam istəyir, "say" istəmir.*
4. **-ed vs -ing adjectives flip the meaning.** *"I'm boring"* means you bore others! Say *"I'm bored."* 🇦🇿 *"boring" = darıxdırıcı; "bored" = darıxmış.*
5. **Double negatives sound uneducated in standard English.** *"I don't know nothing"* → *anything*. (AZ and casual dialects allow it; standard English doesn't.) 🇦🇿 *Standart İngiliscədə ikiqat inkar olmaz.*
6. **Fix families, not sentences.** Correcting one *"much people"* is useless if you don't learn the **countable/uncountable rule** behind it. 🇦🇿 *Cümləni yox, **ailəni** düzəlt.*

---

## 🅲 §C — ALTERNATIVE EXPRESSIONS (self-correction phrases)

| When you catch yourself… | Say instead |
|---|---|
| unsure of an article | pause: "a… the… **a** teacher" — job = *a* |
| unsure of a preposition | recall the chunk: "good **at**, interested **in**, afraid **of**" |
| about to say "since three years" | switch: "**for** three years" |
| about to say "I have seen it yesterday" | drop the time or the perfect: "I **saw** it yesterday" |
| about to say "much people" | "**many** people / **a lot of** people" |

> 🇦🇿 *Özünü düzəltmə: artikl (iş = a), söz-birləşmələri (good at), for/since, many/much.*

## 🅴1 — The "NAME THE FAMILY" method

```
   Error appears  →  ❶ NAME the family (article? preposition? tense?)
                   →  ❷ RECALL the one-line rule
                   →  ❸ SCAN for the same error elsewhere in your writing
                   →  ❹ LOG it on your personal error list
```

> 🔑 A correction you can't **categorise** you'll repeat. Turning *"oh, wrong preposition"* into *"ah, the **adjective + preposition** family — good **at**, afraid **of**"* fixes a dozen future sentences. 🇦🇿 *Kateqoriyalaşdıra bilmədiyin səhvi təkrarlayacaqsan — ailəni adlandır.*

## 🅴2 — ARTICLES decision tree (the biggest 🇦🇿 family)

```
   Is it a specific/known thing (or unique)?  ── yes → THE   (the sun, the book I bought)
        └─ no → Is it singular & countable?    ── yes → A/AN (a teacher, an apple)
                 └─ no (plural / uncountable / general) → ZERO (music, life, teachers, advice)
```

| Situation | Article | Example |
|---|---|---|
| job / one of many | a/an | She's **a** doctor. |
| superlative / unique / known | the | **the** best / **the** sun / **the** book you lent me |
| general (plural/uncountable/abstract) | zero | I like **music** / **Life** is short / **teachers** work hard |
| institutions "for their purpose" | zero | go to **school / university / bed / hospital** |

> 🇦🇿 *Azərbaycanca artikl yoxdur — buna görə hər isimdə soru ver: konkret (the)? tək sayıla bilən (a/an)? ümumi (sıfır)?*

## 🅴3 — PREPOSITION collocations (memorise as chunks)

| Adjective + prep | Verb + prep |
|---|---|
| good **at** · bad **at** | depend **on** · rely **on** |
| interested **in** · involved **in** | listen **to** · belong **to** |
| afraid **of** · proud **of** · fond **of** | look **at** · look **for** · look **after** |
| married **to** · similar **to** | arrive **at/in** · agree **with** |
| worried **about** · keen **on** | apologise **for** · succeed **in** |
| responsible **for** · famous **for** | consist **of** · suffer **from** |

> ⚠️ No preposition after: **discuss, enter, marry, phone, answer, reach** (*discuss it*, *enter the room*, *phone me*). 🇦🇿 *Bunlardan sonra sözönü YOX: discuss/enter/marry/phone/answer/reach.*

---

## 🅴4 — SAY · TELL · SPEAK · TALK

| Verb | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **say** | say (something) / say to sb | She **said** hello. / She **said to** me… |
| **tell** | tell **sb** (something) | She **told me** the news. |
| **speak** | speak (a language) / speak to sb | I **speak** French. / **speak to** the manager |
| **talk** | talk to/with sb / talk about | We **talked about** work. |

> ⚠️ *tell* needs a **person**: ~~he told that~~ → *he told **me** that* / *he **said** that*. Fixed: *tell the truth/a lie/a story/the time/a joke*. 🇦🇿 *"tell" adam istəyir; "say" istəmir.*

---

## 🅴5 — MAKE · DO collocations

| MAKE (create/produce) | DO (perform/activity) |
|---|---|
| make a mistake, a decision, a plan | do homework, a job, the housework |
| make a noise, a mess, progress | do business, an exercise, research |
| make money, a profit, a cake | do someone a favour, your best |
| make an effort, a promise, friends | do the shopping, the washing-up |

> 🔑 Rough rule: **make** = produce something new; **do** = perform an action/task. But learn them as **chunks**. 🇦🇿 *make = yaratmaq; do = yerinə yetirmək — amma birləşmə kimi öyrən.*

## 🅴6 — -ED vs -ING adjectives (the *bored/boring* family)

```
   -ED   →  how you FEEL          "I'm bored / interested / excited / tired / confused."
   -ING  →  what CAUSES it         "The film is boring / interesting / exciting / tiring."
```

| Feeling (-ed) | Cause (-ing) |
|---|---|
| I'm **bored**. | The lesson is **boring**. |
| I'm **interested**. | The topic is **interesting**. |
| I'm **confused**. | The rules are **confusing**. |
| I'm **surprised**. | The result was **surprising**. |

> ⚠️ *"I am boring"* = you bore others! Say *"I am **bored**."* 🇦🇿 *"I'm boring" = başqalarını darıxdırırsan; "I'm bored" = darıxmısan.*

## 🅴7 — COUNTABLE / UNCOUNTABLE master

| | Countable (plural) | Uncountable (singular) |
|---|---|---|
| a lot | many / a lot of | much / a lot of |
| small amount | a few / few | a little / little |
| "not much" | fewer | less |
| questions | How many? | How much? |
| examples | books, people, ideas | information, advice, furniture, news, money, homework, luggage, bread, water |

> ⚠️ Common uncountables that trap learners: **information, advice, furniture, news, luggage, homework, progress, research, money** — no plural, no *a*. 🇦🇿 *Sayılmayanlar: information, advice, furniture, news, luggage, homework — cəm yox, "a" yox.*

## 🅴9 — COMPARATIVES & SUPERLATIVES errors

| ❌ | ✅ | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| more better | better / much better | no double comparative |
| most easiest | easiest | no double superlative |
| more easy | easier | short adj → *-er* |
| more tall than | taller than | short adj → *-er* |
| the more good | the best | irregular: good→better→best |
| expensiver | more expensive | long adj → *more* |

> 🔑 1 syllable → *-er/-est*; 3+ syllables → *more/most*; 2-syllable varies (*happy→happier*, *modern→more modern*). Irregular: good/better/best, bad/worse/worst, far/further. 🇦🇿 *Qısa sifət → -er/-est; uzun → more/most; ikiqat müqayisə YOX.*

## 🅴10 — DOUBLE NEGATIVES & tag errors

| ❌ | ✅ | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| I don't know **nothing**. 🇦🇿 | I don't know **anything**. | one negative only |
| I didn't see **nobody**. | I didn't see **anybody**. | one negative only |
| She can't hardly walk. | She can **hardly** walk. | *hardly* is already negative |
| You're coming, **isn't it**? 🇦🇿 | …, **aren't you**? | tag must match the verb |
| He is late, **no**? 🇦🇿 | He is late, **isn't he**? | proper tag |

> 🇦🇿 *Azərbaycan ikiqat inkarı təbii sayır ("heç nə bilmirəm"), İngiliscə isə YOX — bir inkar + "anything/anybody".*

## 🅴12 — 🇦🇿 CONTRASTIVE SYNTHESIS (why each family is hard)

| Family | 🇦🇿 cause | Fix habit |
|---|---|---|
| **articles** | AZ has **no a/an/the** | ask "specific/countable/general?" every noun |
| **prepositions** | AZ uses **postpositions/suffixes** | learn verb+prep as chunks |
| **he/she** | AZ **"o"** is gender-neutral | check the person's gender |
| **double negative** | AZ **allows** it | one negative + *any-* |
| **"I am agree"** | AZ **"razıyam"** (adjective-like) | *agree* is a verb: "I agree" |
| **tense (did/have done)** | AZ **-dı/-mış** overlap | use finished-time test |

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani contrast in one line:** the biggest error families for AZ speakers come from **three structural gaps** — Azerbaijani has **no articles**, uses **postpositions** where English uses prepositions, and has a **gender-neutral "o"** (no he/she). Add that Azerbaijani **allows double negatives** and treats *"razıyam"* (agree) like an adjective, and you can predict 80% of a learner's mistakes in advance. Knowing the **source** of each error is the fastest cure: you stop translating structure and start **rebuilding** it in English.
