# 🟪 PART 5 · Chapter 32
# Relative Clauses — adding information smoothly
### *who · whom · whose · which · that · where · when* — "the man **who** called" / "the town **where** I grew up" 🔗

> **The one-line idea.** A relative clause **glues extra information onto a noun** using a linking word (*who, which, that, whose, where, when…*). It lets you turn two short sentences into one smooth sentence.
> *"I met a man. He lives next door."* → *"I met the man **who** lives next door."*
> 🇦🇿 *Nisbi budaq cümlə əlavə məlumatı bir isimə **bağlayıcı söz** (*who, which, that…*) ilə "yapışdırır" — iki qısa cümləni bir hamar cümləyə çevirir.*
> 🇦🇿 *Azərbaycanca isə bu, **feli sifətdir** və isimdən **ƏVVƏL** gəlir: "**zəng edən** adam" = "the man **who called**." (Söz sırası tam əksinədir!)*

---

## 🎯 In this chapter you will learn to…
```
✅ join sentences with a relative pronoun:  who / which / that ("the book that I read")
✅ show possession:                          whose ("the girl whose bag was stolen")
✅ add place / time / reason:                where / when / (the reason) why
✅ tell DEFINING from NON-DEFINING:          commas or no commas — it changes the meaning
✅ leave out the pronoun (zero relative):    "the film (that) I saw" — object only, defining only
✅ place prepositions:                        "the man I spoke to" / "the man to whom I spoke"
✅ comment on a whole clause:                 which ("He was late, which annoyed everyone.")
✅ reduce a clause to a participle:           "the man standing there" (= who is standing)
```
> 🇦🇿 *Bu fəsildə: cümlələri birləşdirmək; yiyəlik (whose); yer/zaman/səbəb; təyinedici↔əlavə; əvəzliyin buraxılması; sözönləri; bütöv cümləyə şərh; feli sifətə qısaltma.*

---

## 🧭 THE BIG PICTURE — a clause that describes a noun

A relative clause works like a long adjective: it comes **right after the noun** it describes and starts with a **relative pronoun** that "points back" to that noun:

```
   two sentences:   "This is the house.  +  Jack built it."
   one sentence:     "This is the house  [ that Jack built ]."
                          ▲ noun (head)      ▲ relative clause describes it
                                             └─ "that" points back to "the house"
```

> 🧠 **The key mental move:** the relative pronoun **replaces** the repeated noun and **links** the clause. English puts this clause **after** the noun; Azerbaijani puts a **participle before** the noun. So an Azerbaijani speaker must "flip" the order and add a linking word English requires but Azerbaijani never uses.
> 🇦🇿 *Əsas fikir: nisbi əvəzlik təkrarlanan ismi əvəz edir və budaq cümləni bağlayır. İngiliscə bu cümlə isimdən **sonra** gəlir; Azərbaycanca feli sifət isimdən **əvvəl**. Sıranı "çevirmək" və İngiliscənin tələb etdiyi bağlayıcı sözü əlavə etmək lazımdır.*

## 📋 THE FORMS — at a glance

| Pronoun | Refers to | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **who** | people (subject/object) | the woman **who** lives here / **who** I met |
| **whom** | people (object, formal) | the man **whom** I called / to **whom** I spoke |
| **whose** | possession (people & things) | the boy **whose** dog barks / a house **whose** roof leaks |
| **which** | things / animals | the car **which** broke down |
| **that** | people or things *(defining only)* | the book **that** I read / the man **that** called |
| **where** | place | the office **where** I work |
| **when** | time | the year **when** I was born |
| **(the reason) why** | reason | the reason **why** he left |

> 🔑 *that* is the **all-purpose** defining pronoun (people **or** things), but it **can't** be used in non-defining clauses or right after a preposition (~~to that~~). 🇦🇿 *"that" universal təyinedici əvəzlikdir (həm insan, həm əşya), amma əlavə (vergüllü) cümlələrdə və sözönündən sonra işlənmir.*

---

## 🔑 THE CORE — DEFINING vs NON-DEFINING clauses

This one distinction controls **commas, the word *that*, and omission.** Get it, and half the chapter falls into place:

```
   DEFINING (no commas)      →  ESSENTIAL: tells you WHICH one. Can't remove it.
        "The students WHO studied passed."   (only the ones who studied)
        → can use THAT · can omit an object pronoun

   NON-DEFINING (commas)     →  EXTRA: adds a comment. Could be removed.
        "The students, WHO studied hard, passed."   (all of them; by the way, they studied)
        → NO 'that' · NO omission · commas (or pauses) required
```

| ✅ Defining (identifies) | ✅ Non-defining (comments) |
|---|---|
| People **who exercise** live longer. | My father, **who is 70**, still runs. |
| The book **that won** the prize is here. | This book, **which won a prize**, is here. |
| The man **I met** was kind. | Mr Ali, **whom I met yesterday**, was kind. |
| Is this the key **that opens** the door? | I lost my keys, **which was annoying**. |

> ⚠️ **Non-defining rules are strict:** use **commas**, **never *that*** (~~My car, that is red~~ → *which*), and **never omit** the pronoun. The commas change meaning: *"My sister who lives in Baku"* (I have several sisters) vs *"My sister, who lives in Baku,"* (I have one sister — extra info). 🇦🇿 *Əlavə cümlə: vergül işlət, "that" işlətmə, əvəzliyi buraxma. Vergül mənanı dəyişir.*

---

## ✂️ OMITTING the relative pronoun (the "zero relative")

You can **drop** the relative pronoun — but only in a **defining** clause, and only when it's the **object** of the clause:

```
   OBJECT pronoun  → can omit:   "the film [that] I watched"      (I = a new subject follows → omit OK)
   SUBJECT pronoun → must keep:  "the film that won an award"     (no new subject → 'that' is the subject → keep)
```

| Keep it (subject) | Drop it (object) |
|---|---|
| the man **who** called me | the man **(who/that)** I called |
| the bus **that** goes to town | the bus **(that)** I took |
| people **who** complain | the people **(that)** I met |

> 🧪 **Test:** right after the pronoun, is there **another subject** (I, you, she…)? → the pronoun is the **object** → you can omit it. If a **verb** follows directly → it's the **subject** → keep it. 🇦🇿 *Əvəzlikdən dərhal sonra başqa mübtəda varsa → tamamlıqdır → buraxıla bilər. Fel gəlirsə → mübtədadır → saxlanılır.*

---

## 👤👉 SUBJECT vs OBJECT relative pronouns

The pronoun's job inside the clause decides which form and whether you can drop it:

| Role | People | Things | Can omit? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **subject** | who / that | which / that | ❌ no |
| **object** | who / whom / that / — | which / that / — | ✅ yes (defining) |
| **possessive** | whose | whose | ❌ no |

> 🔑 As a **subject**, the pronoun is followed **directly by a verb** (*"the chef **who cooks** here"*). As an **object**, it's followed by **a subject + verb** (*"the chef **(who) I hired**"*). 🇦🇿 *Mübtəda kimi: birbaşa fel gəlir. Tamamlıq kimi: mübtəda + fel gəlir.*

---

## 🔗 PREPOSITIONS in relative clauses

Everyday English leaves the preposition at the **end** (stranded); formal English moves it to the **front** with *whom/which*:

```
   informal:  "the man  (who/that)  I spoke  TO."      "the house (that) I live IN."
   formal:    "the man  TO WHOM     I spoke."          "the house IN WHICH I live."
```

| Informal (stranded, can omit pronoun) | Formal (fronted, keep whom/which) |
|---|---|
| the person **(who) I work with** | the person **with whom** I work |
| the topic **(that) we talked about** | the topic **about which** we talked |
| the chair **(that) you sat on** | the chair **on which** you sat |

> ⚠️ After a **fronted preposition**, use **whom** (people) or **which** (things) — **never *that*** (~~about that~~) and never drop the pronoun. 🇦🇿 *Öndəki sözönündən sonra whom/which; "that" YOX, buraxılma YOX.*

---

## 📍 RELATIVE ADVERBS — *where · when · why*

These replace a **preposition + which** and describe **place, time or reason**:

| Adverb | = | Example | Also possible |
|---|---|---|---|
| **where** | in/at/to which | the hotel **where** we stayed | the hotel **(that) we stayed at** / **at which** we stayed |
| **when** | on/in/at which | the day **when** we met | the day **(that) we met** / **on which** we met |
| **why** | for which | the reason **why** he left | the reason **(that) he left** / **for which** he left |

> ⚠️ ***where* already contains the preposition** — don't add another and don't add a pronoun: ~~the town where I live in it~~ → *the town **where** I live.* And *when/why* are often just **dropped**: *"the day we met," "the reason he left."* 🇦🇿 *"where" sözönünü artıq daxil edir — ikinci sözönü və ya əvəzlik əlavə etmə. when/why tez-tez buraxılır.*

---

## 🌐 *which* commenting on a WHOLE clause

*Which* (non-defining, always with a comma) can refer back not to one noun but to the **entire idea** before it:

| Sentence | *which* = |
|---|---|
| He passed the exam, **which** delighted his parents. | the fact that he passed |
| She was two hours late, **which** annoyed everyone. | her being late |
| They cancelled the trip, **which** was a shame. | the cancellation |

> ⛔ Use **which**, never *what*: ~~He was late, what annoyed everyone~~ → *…, **which** annoyed everyone.* 🇦🇿 *Bütöv cümləyə şərh üçün **which** (vergüllə); "what" YOX.*

---

## ✨ REDUCED relative clauses (participle clauses)

You can shrink a relative clause to a **participle**, dropping the pronoun and *be*:

```
   ACTIVE (-ing):   "the man WHO IS standing there"   →  "the man STANDING there"
   PASSIVE (V3):    "the letter WHICH WAS written yesterday" → "the letter WRITTEN yesterday"
```

| Full clause | Reduced |
|---|---|
| the people **who are waiting** outside | the people **waiting** outside |
| the car **which was parked** here | the car **parked** here |
| anyone **who wants** to join | anyone **wanting** to join |

> 🇦🇿 **Bridge to Azerbaijani:** this reduced form is the **closest** English gets to the Azerbaijani structure — *"the man **standing** there"* ≈ *"orada **duran** adam"* (both: participle + noun, no pronoun, no finite verb). Reduced relatives are a natural stepping-stone for AZ learners. 🇦🇿 *Bu qısaldılmış forma İngiliscənin Azərbaycan quruluşuna ən yaxın halıdır: "standing man" ≈ "duran adam".*

## ⛔ COMMON MISTAKES (fix these now)

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| the man **which** called | the man **who / that** called | *which* isn't for people |
| the book **who** I read | the book **which / that** I read | *who* isn't for things |
| My mum, **that** is a nurse, … | My mum, **who** is a nurse, … | non-defining → *who*, never *that* |
| the film **that I saw it** | the film **that I saw** | no resumptive *it* |
| the town **where I live in** | the town **where I live** | *where* already = *in which* |
| I have a friend **lives** in Rome. | I have a friend **who lives** in Rome. | can't drop a **subject** pronoun |
| the reason **because** he came | the reason **why** he came | reason → *why*, not *because* |
| He lost, **what** upset him. | He lost, **which** upset him. | whole-clause comment → *which* |

## 🅰️ §A — LEVEL DIFFERENCES (A1 → C2)

How the *same skill* — describing a noun with a clause — grows with your level:

| Level | What the learner can do | Typical sentence |
|---|---|---|
| **A1** | *(pre-taught chunks)* | "the man **who** works here" |
| **A2** | basic defining *who / which / that* | "the book **that** I like" |
| **B1** | *whose*; omitting object pronoun; *where/when* | "the girl **whose** bag…" / "the café **where** we met" |
| **B2** | defining vs non-defining (commas); prepositions at the end | "My boss, **who is kind**, …" / "the man I spoke **to**" |
| **C1** | *whom*; fronted prepositions; sentential *which* | "the client **to whom** I wrote" / "…, **which** helped." |
| **C2** | reduced relatives; nuanced register; stacking clauses | "anyone **wanting** to apply" / "the letter **written** by hand" |

> 🇦🇿 *A1: hazır ifadələr. A2: sadə who/which/that. B1: whose, əvəzliyin buraxılması, where/when. B2: təyinedici↔əlavə, sözönü sonda. C1: whom, öndəki sözönü, sentential which. C2: qısaldılmış cümlələr.*

## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT

1. **Speech loves *that* and the zero relative.** *"The film I saw," "the guy that called"* — natives drop *whom* and often drop the object pronoun entirely. 🇦🇿 *Danışıqda "that" və əvəzliyin buraxılması çox işlənir.*
2. **Commas are audible.** A non-defining clause is spoken with **pauses** and a slight drop in pitch — the spoken version of the commas. 🇦🇿 *Əlavə cümlə danışıqda **fasilə** ilə deyilir — vergülün səsli qarşılığı.*
3. **"whom" sounds formal.** Most people say *"the person who I met"*; *whom* survives mainly after prepositions (*"to whom"*) and in careful writing. 🇦🇿 *"whom" rəsmi səslənir; danışıqda çox vaxt "who".*
4. **Sentential *which* is everywhere in comments.** *"He apologised, which was nice."* A very natural way to react to a whole situation. 🇦🇿 *Bütöv cümləyə "which" şərhi çox təbiidir.*
5. **Stranded prepositions are normal, not "wrong".** *"the house I grew up in"* is perfectly good English; the "never end with a preposition" rule is a myth. 🇦🇿 *Sonda sözönü tamamilə düzgündür — "heç vaxt sözönü ilə bitirmə" qaydası mifdir.*
6. **Reduced relatives make writing crisp.** *"Passengers travelling to Baku…"* is tighter than *"Passengers who are travelling…"* — common in notices and formal prose. 🇦🇿 *Qısaldılmış cümlələr yazını yığcam edir.*

# 📖 MINI STORY #1 — "The Old Bookshop"

### *Defining and non-defining clauses, whose, where and one sentential which — a shop full of stories.*
### 🇦🇿 *Təyinedici və əlavə budaq cümlələr, whose, where və bir sentential which — hekayələrlə dolu bir dükan.*

---

On a narrow street stood a bookshop **that** had been there for eighty years.

The owner, **who** was called Mr Cavad, knew every customer **who** walked in. "This," he liked to say, "is a shop **where** books find their readers."

One rainy afternoon a young woman **whose** coat was soaked hurried inside. She was looking for a novel **(that)** her grandmother had loved — a book **which** was long out of print.

"Ah," said Mr Cavad, **who** rarely forgot a title, "the one **you mean** is upstairs." He led her to a shelf **where** the old editions were kept.

There it was: a faded book **whose** cover still showed a garden. The woman, **who** had almost given up hope, gasped.

"My grandmother, **who** passed away last year, read this to me every summer," she said — **which** brought a tear to Mr Cavad's eye.

"Then it belongs with you," he said, refusing her money — a gesture **that** she never forgot.

Years later, she opened a bookshop of her own, **which** she named after the little shop **where** a stranger had once been so kind.

```
1. Find two non-defining clauses. Why do they take commas? What extra info do they add?
2. "a novel (that) her grandmother had loved" — the 'that' is in brackets. Why can it be omitted here?
3. Find the sentential 'which'. What does it refer to — a noun, or the whole idea before it?
4. Rewrite "a shop where books find their readers" using 'in which' instead of 'where'.

```
> 💬 **Answers**
> 1. E.g. *"The owner, who was called Mr Cavad, …"* and *"My grandmother, who passed away last year, …"* — commas because they add **non-essential extra info** about an already-identified person (the owner / her grandmother); you could remove them and the sentence still makes sense.

> 3. *"…, which brought a tear to Mr Cavad's eye."* — *which* refers to the **whole idea** (her telling him the story / the whole situation), not a single noun.
> 4. *"a shop **in which** books find their readers."*
> 5. The customer who called left. / The book (that) my grandmother loved. / The shop where I grew up.

# 🧨 EXPANSION PACK — Relative Clauses (E1–E12)
### *The deep layer: the linking engine, the comma divide, omission, prepositions, sentential which, reductions & idioms.*
### 🇦🇿 *Dərin qat: bağlayıcı mexanizm, vergül bölgüsü, buraxılma, sözönləri, sentential which, qısaltmalar və idiomlar.*

---

## 🅴1 — The relative-clause engine (replace + link)

A relative pronoun does **two jobs at once**: it **replaces** the repeated noun and **links** the two clauses:

```
   "I met a man."  +  "He fixed my car."
             └──────────────┐
   "I met the man  WHO  fixed my car."
                    ▲ replaces "he" AND links the clause to "the man"
```

- English attaches the clause **after** the head noun (head-initial).
- The pronoun's **form** (who/which/whose) depends on the noun; its **role** (subject/object) depends on the clause.

> 🇦🇿 *Nisbi əvəzlik iki iş görür: təkrarlanan ismi **əvəz edir** və budaq cümləni **bağlayır**. İngiliscə budaq cümlə isimdən **sonra** gəlir (head-initial); Azərbaycanca isə **əvvəl** (head-final).*

---

## 🅴2 — ⚠️ DEFINING vs NON-DEFINING (the comma divide)

The single most tested distinction — and the commas genuinely **change the meaning**:

```
   "My sister WHO lives in Baku is a doctor."       → DEFINING: I have several sisters;
                                                       this identifies which one. (no commas)
   "My sister, WHO lives in Baku, is a doctor."     → NON-DEFINING: I have ONE sister;
                                                       the clause is just extra info. (commas)
```

| Feature | Defining | Non-defining |
|---|---|---|
| commas | ❌ none | ✅ required |
| *that* allowed? | ✅ yes | ❌ no (*who/which* only) |
| omit object pronoun? | ✅ yes | ❌ no |
| function | identifies *which one* | adds a comment |
| removable? | no — changes meaning | yes — sentence still works |

> 🇦🇿 *Vergül mənanı dəyişir: vergülsüz = hansı olduğunu deyir (təyinedici); vergüllü = əlavə şərh. Əlavə cümlədə "that" yox, buraxılma yox.*

---

## 🅴3 — SUBJECT vs OBJECT & the omission test

```
   SUBJECT relative  →  pronoun + VERB directly     "the chef who COOKS here"   → keep pronoun
   OBJECT relative   →  pronoun + SUBJECT + verb     "the chef (who) I HIRED"    → can drop pronoun
```

| Clause | Pronoun's role | Omit? |
|---|---|---|
| the bus **that goes** to town | subject | ❌ keep |
| the bus **(that) I took** | object | ✅ drop |
| the woman **who phoned** | subject | ❌ keep |
| the woman **(who) I phoned** | object | ✅ drop |

> 🧪 If a **new subject** follows the pronoun → object → droppable. If a **verb** follows → subject → keep. Only in **defining** clauses. 🇦🇿 *Əvəzlikdən sonra yeni mübtəda → tamamlıq (buraxıla bilər); fel → mübtəda (saxlanılır). Yalnız təyinedici cümlədə.*

---

## 🅴4 — *that* vs *which* vs *who* — when each wins

| Situation | Preferred | Note |
|---|---|---|
| defining, thing | **that** or **which** | *that* more common in speech |
| defining, person | **who** or **that** | *who* preferred for people |
| non-defining (any) | **who / which** | **never** *that* |
| after a preposition (fronted) | **whom / which** | never *that* |
| after *all, everything, only, superlatives* | **that** | "the only thing **that** matters" |

> 🔑 Use **that** especially after *all / everything / nothing / the only / superlatives*: *"everything **that** glitters," "the best film **that** I've seen."* 🇦🇿 *all/everything/the only/superlativ-dən sonra adətən **that**.*

---

## 🅴5 — *whose* (people **and** things)

*Whose* = "of whom / of which" — it shows **possession**, and works for things too:

| Example | = |
|---|---|
| the man **whose** wallet was stolen | the wallet of the man |
| a company **whose** profits fell | the profits of which fell *(formal)* |
| the tree **whose** branches touch the roof | the branches of which… |

> 🗣️ For things, *whose* is common and natural; the formal alternative *"of which"* sounds heavy: *"a book the cover **of which** is torn"* → usually *"a book **whose** cover is torn."* 🇦🇿 *"whose" həm insan, həm əşya üçün; əşyalar üçün "of which" rəsmi və ağırdır.*

---

## 🅴6 — PREPOSITIONS: stranded vs fronted

```
   STRANDED (informal):  "the person  (who/that)  I spoke  TO."   ← preposition at the end
   FRONTED (formal):     "the person  TO WHOM     I spoke."       ← preposition + whom/which
```

| Informal | Formal |
|---|---|
| the tool **(that) I opened it with** | the tool **with which** I opened it |
| the people **(who) I work for** | the people **for whom** I work |
| the way **(that) it works** | the way **in which** it works |

> ⚠️ Fronted preposition → **whom/which** only (never *that*, never omit). Stranded → *that*/zero fine. 🇦🇿 *Öndəki sözönü → whom/which; sonda → that/sıfır olar.*

---

## 🅴7 — RELATIVE ADVERBS (*where / when / why*)

Each equals a **preposition + which** — so don't double the preposition:

| Adverb | Equivalent | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **where** | at/in/to which | the lab **where** we work / **at which** we work |
| **when** | on/in/at which | the moment **when** it happened / **at which** it happened |
| **why** | for which | the reason **why** / **for which** |

> ⚠️ ~~the house where I live **in**~~ (double) · ~~the day when we met **on**~~ (double). *When/why* (and often *where*) can be **dropped**: *"the day we met," "the reason he left."* 🇦🇿 *"where/when/why" sözönünü artıq daşıyır — ikiqat sözönü YOX; when/why tez-tez buraxılır.*

---

## 🅴8 — Sentential *which* (comment on the whole clause)

*Which*, after a comma, can point to the **entire preceding idea**, not one noun:

| Sentence | *which* = |
|---|---|
| He forgot my birthday, **which** hurt me. | the forgetting |
| The train was cancelled, **which** meant a long wait. | the cancellation |
| She speaks four languages, **which** is impressive. | the whole fact |

> ⛔ Always *which*, never *what*; always with a **comma**. 🇦🇿 *Həmişə "which" (vergüllə), "what" YOX.*

---

## 🅴9 — REDUCED relative clauses (the Azerbaijani bridge)

Drop the pronoun + *be*, leaving a **participle**:

```
   ACTIVE:  "the people WHO ARE waiting"     →  "the people WAITING"       (-ing)
   PASSIVE: "the car WHICH WAS stolen"       →  "the car STOLEN"           (V3)
   with adjective/phrase: "the man WHO IS in the corner" → "the man IN THE CORNER"
```

| Full | Reduced |
|---|---|
| passengers **who are travelling** to Baku | passengers **travelling** to Baku |
| the money **that was found** | the money **found** |
| the only student **who was awake** | the only student **awake** |

> 🇦🇿 **Bridge:** *"the man **standing** there"* mirrors Azerbaijani *"orada **duran** adam"* — participle + noun, no pronoun. English reduced relatives = the closest match to the AZ **-an/-ən** structure. 🇦🇿 *Qısaldılmış cümlə = AZ "-an/-ən" quruluşunun eynisi: "duran adam" = "standing man".*

---

## 🅴10 — *what*, *whoever*, and cleft sentences

| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **what** (= the thing that) | fused relative | **What** I need is time. *(= the thing that I need)* |
| **whoever / whatever / wherever** | any person/thing/place | **Whoever** did this will pay. |
| **cleft: It was … who/that** | emphasis | **It was Sara who** solved it. |
| **cleft: The place where …** | emphasis | **The reason why** I called is simple. |

> ⚠️ *What* is **not** a relative pronoun for a noun: ~~"the book what I read"~~ → *"the book **that** I read."* *What* stands **alone** (= "the thing that"). 🇦🇿 *"what" isimdən sonra işlənmir (~~book what~~); tək başına "o şey ki" mənasında.*

---

## 🅴11 — Punctuation & style

| Rule | Example |
|---|---|
| non-defining → paired commas | Mr Ali, **who called**, is here. |
| don't comma a defining clause | ~~The man, who called, is here.~~ *(if defining)* |
| avoid stacking too many | prefer two sentences to a chain of *which…who…that…* |
| use reductions to tighten | "applicants **meeting** the criteria" > "applicants **who meet**…" |

> 🇦🇿 *Əlavə cümlə cüt vergüllə; təyinedici cümləyə vergül qoyma; həddən çox budaq cümlə yığma; qısaltmalarla yığcamlaşdır.*

---

## 🅴12 — 🇦🇿 CONTRASTIVE SYNTHESIS

| English | 🇦🇿 | The build |
|---|---|---|
| the man **who called** | zəng **edən** adam | subject participle **-ən** |
| the book **(that) I read** | **oxuduğum** kitab | object participle **-duğu** + mənsubiyyət |
| the city **where I was born** | **doğulduğum** şəhər | -duğu form covers place too |
| the woman **whose coat was wet** | paltosu islanmış qadın | possessor marked on the noun |
| the letter **that will arrive** | **gələcək** məktub | future participle **-əcək** |
| the man **standing** there | orada **duran** adam | reduced ≈ AZ participle |

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani contrast in one line:** Azerbaijani has **no relative pronouns and no post-noun clause.** Instead it turns the verb into a **participle placed before the noun**: **-an/-ən** for a subject relative (*gələn* = "who comes"), **-dığı/-diyi** (+ possessive) for an object/oblique relative (*oxuduğum* = "that I read"), **-acağı/-əcəyi** for the future. So the AZ learner must make **three switches**: (1) move the clause from **before** the noun to **after** it; (2) turn a **participle** back into a **full finite clause**; (3) **add** a relative pronoun (*who/which/that/where*) that Azerbaijani simply doesn't have. English **reduced relatives** (*the man standing there*) are the one place the two languages meet.

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "The Witness"

### *Defining clauses that identify, whose for possession, where for place — a witness helps the police.*
### 🇦🇿 *Kimliyi müəyyən edən təyinedici cümlələr, yiyəlik üçün whose, yer üçün where — şahid polisə kömək edir.*

> Read it twice. **First** for the story; **second** circling every relative clause — and note which ones **identify** (defining) and which just **comment** (non-defining).
> 🇦🇿 *İki dəfə oxu: əvvəl hekayə üçün, sonra hər nisbi cümləni dövrəyə alaraq — hansı **kimliyi müəyyən edir**, hansı sadəcə **şərh verir**?*

Detective Rəna showed the witness a set of photographs.

"Take your time. Point to anyone **who looks familiar**." *(defining — identifies which person.)*

The witness studied them. "That's him — the man **who was standing** by the door. He's the one **(that) I saw** take the bag." *(defining; second one has an omitted object pronoun.)*

"Are you sure? This is the same man **whose photo** appeared in the paper last week." *(defining — whose.)*

"Positive. And there was a woman with him, **whose hair** was bright red." *(defining — whose.)*

"Do you remember the place **where it happened**?" Rəna asked. *(relative adverb — where.)*

"The café **on the corner** — the one **that closed** recently." *(reduced phrase + defining.)*

"Good. The café, **which reopened yesterday**, has CCTV." *(non-defining — extra info, commas.)*

The witness nodded. "There's one more thing **(that) I noticed**." *(defining, omitted pronoun.)* "The car **they escaped in** had a dented door." *(defining with stranded preposition.)*

"That's the detail **which will help** us most," said Rəna, **which pleased** the witness enormously. *(defining, then sentential which.)*

An hour later, the police arrested a man **whose car** matched the description exactly — the very break **that** the case had needed.

### ✍️ After-reading tasks
```
1. Find every defining clause that IDENTIFIES a person or thing. Why can't the commas be added?
2. Find the one non-defining clause about the café. What extra info does it add, and why the commas?
3. "the one (that) I saw" and "the car they escaped in" both omit the pronoun. Explain why that's allowed.
4. Find the sentential 'which'. What does it refer to?
5. 🇦🇿→EN: "Qapıda duran adam." / "Saçı qırmızı olan qadın." / "Hadisənin baş verdiyi yer."
```
> 💬 **Answers**
> 1. E.g. *"anyone who looks familiar," "the man who was standing by the door," "the man whose photo appeared," "the place where it happened."* No commas because each clause is **essential** — it tells us **which** person/place is meant; remove it and we don't know who/where.
> 2. *"The café, which reopened yesterday, has CCTV."* — the café is already identified (the corner one); *"which reopened yesterday"* is **extra**, non-essential info, so it takes commas.
> 3. Both pronouns are the **object** of their clause (*"I saw [it]," "they escaped in [it]"*) and the clauses are **defining** — object pronouns in defining clauses can be dropped.
> 4. *"…, which pleased the witness enormously."* — *which* refers to the **whole idea** (Rəna saying it was the most helpful detail), not a single noun.
> 5. The man (who is) standing at the door. / The woman whose hair is red. / The place where it happened.

## 🏁 End of Chapter 32

You can now **weave extra information into a noun.** A relative clause follows the noun and opens with a linking word: **who/whom** (people), **which** (things), **that** (either, defining only), **whose** (possession), and the relative adverbs **where/when/why** (place/time/reason). The great divide is **defining** (no commas — tells you *which one*, allows *that* and omission) versus **non-defining** (commas — an extra comment, no *that*, no omission). You can **drop an object pronoun** in defining clauses (*"the film I saw"*), **strand or front prepositions** (*"the man I spoke to"* / *"to whom I spoke"*), let **which** comment on a whole clause (*"…, which was a shame"*), and **reduce** a clause to a participle (*"the man standing there"*) — the form closest to Azerbaijani's pre-noun participle. 🔗

```
 ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ 👤 people:   who / whom / that        "the man who called"                 │
 │ 📦 things:   which / that              "the car which broke down"           │
 │ 🔑 possess:  whose                     "the girl whose bag was stolen"      │
 │ 📍 adverbs:  where / when / why        "the town where I grew up"           │
 │ ✂️ defining: no commas · that OK · drop object pronoun ("the film I saw")   │
 │ 💬 non-def.: commas · NO that · NO omission ("My dad, who is 70, …")        │
 │ ⚠️ no resumptive it · where = in which · whole clause → , which             │
 └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
