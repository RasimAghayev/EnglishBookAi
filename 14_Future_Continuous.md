## 1. ūüüĘ Introduction

The **Future Continuous** describes an action that **will be in progress** at a specific moment
**in the future**. It's the future version of the Past Continuous (Ch. 08): a "movie playing"
‚ÄĒ but ahead of us, not behind.

```
   Future Simple:      I WILL fly to Baku tomorrow.       ‚Üí the whole action (fact)
   Future Continuous:  This time tomorrow, I'LL BE FLYING. ‚Üí in progress at that moment
```

The form has **three parts**: **will** + **be** + a verb with **-ing**.

This chapter also covers a **second future tool** you already half-know: the **Present
Continuous for arrangements** (*"I'm meeting Ali at 6"*) ‚ÄĒ for **fixed plans** with a time and
place set.

> ūüí° **Big idea:** Future Continuous = **"at that future moment, the action will already be
> happening."** ūüõę Picture a future point and zoom in ‚ÄĒ something is mid-action.

## 5. ūüßģ Formula

Model verb: **work**. Helper: **will be** (same for **all** subjects! ūüéČ). Main verb = **V-ing**.

### ‚ěē Positive
```
   S  +  will be  +  V-ing
        (I'll be, you'll be, he'll be, she'll be, we'll be, they'll be)
```
| Subject | will be | V-ing | Example |
|---------|---------|-------|---------|
| I / You / He / She / It / We / They | **will be** | working | She **will be working**. |

> ūüéČ Like *will*, **will be never changes** ‚ÄĒ one helper for everyone.

### ‚ěĖ Negative
```
   S  +  will not be  +  V-ing     (won't be)
```
- I **won't be working**. ¬∑ They **won't be coming**.

### ‚Ěď Question
```
   Will  +  S  +  be  +  V-ing  ?
```
- **Will** you **be using** the car? ¬∑ **Will** she **be joining** us?

### ‚Ěď‚ěĖ Negative Question
```
   Won't  +  S  +  be  +  V-ing  ?
```
- **Won't** you **be needing** this?

### ‚úĒÔłŹ Short Answers
| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Will you be working? | Yes, I **will**. | No, I **won't**. |

> ‚ö†ÔłŹ Always **will be + V-ing** (three parts). The verb is *-ing*, and *be* never changes:
> *will be **working**, will be **flying**.*

## 7. ūüďú Grammar Rules

1. **Three parts:** *will* + *be* + **V-ing**. *will be* is the **same** for every subject.
2. Use it for an action **in progress at a future moment** ‚ÄĒ often with *at 8 o'clock, this
   time tomorrow, when you arrive*.
3. **State verbs** (know, be, like, want) usually do **NOT** take this form ‚ÄĒ use the **Future
   Simple**: ‚ĚĆ *I'll be knowing* ‚Üí ‚úÖ *I'll know*.
4. In **time clauses** (when, while, as soon as), use the **present**, not *will*: *"When you
   **arrive**, I'**ll be waiting**."*
5. The **Present Continuous** expresses **fixed future arrangements** (time + place set): *"I'**m
   meeting** Ali at 6."* (See ¬ß11.)
6. Spelling of **V-ing** follows the usual rules (Ch. 04/08).

> ūüß† **Golden rule:** Future Continuous needs a **future moment** to zoom into. It's the action
> caught **mid-flow** at that point.

## 8. ‚úŹÔłŹ Spelling Rules (the V-ing)

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs ‚Üí **+ing** | work ‚Üí working, fly ‚Üí flying |

| Ends in **-ie** ‚Üí *ie ‚Üí y* + ing | lie ‚Üí lying, die ‚Üí dying |

> ūüďĆ *will* and *be* never change. All the work is on the **-ing** verb.

## 11. ‚öĖÔłŹ Comparison with Similar Tenses

### ūüÜö Future Continuous vs. Future Simple (will)

| | **Future Continuous** | **Future Simple (will)** |
|--|----------------------|--------------------------|
| **Meaning** | in progress at a future moment | the whole action / a fact / decision |
| **Form** | will be + V-ing | will + V1 |
| **Example** | At 8, I'**ll be eating**. | At 8, I'**ll eat**. *(I'll start at 8)* |
| **Feel** | caught mid-action | the event as a point |

```
   will eat:      ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚óŹ(8: start eating)‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ
   will be eating: ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ[==== eating ====]‚óŹ(8: already mid-meal)‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ
```

### ūüÜö Future Continuous vs. Present Continuous (for arrangements) ‚ÄĒ THE other future tool

The **Present Continuous** is used for **fixed future arrangements** ‚ÄĒ when a **time and place
are set** (often with another person):

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **Present Continuous** | a fixed **arrangement** (planned, agreed) | I'**m meeting** Ali at 6 tomorrow. |
| **Future Continuous** | an action **in progress** at a future time | At 6, I'**ll be meeting** Ali *(mid-meeting)*. |
| **be going to** | an **intention** | I'**m going to** meet Ali soon. |

```
   Present Continuous = a diary entry ūüďí   "I'm seeing the dentist at 3."  (booked!)
   Future Continuous  = a zoomed-in moment "At 3, I'll be seeing the dentist." (mid-visit)
```

> ūüí° For **personal arrangements with a set time**, the **Present Continuous** is the most
> natural: *"We'**re flying** to Baku on Monday"* (tickets booked) sounds better than *"We'll
> fly"* or *"We'll be flying"* for a plan.

### ūüÜö The four "future" forms at a glance

| Form | Best for | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| **will + V1** | decision now, prediction, promise | I'**ll** help you. |
| **be going to** | intention, evidence | I'**m going to** study. |
| **Present Continuous** | fixed arrangement (time + place) | I'**m meeting** Ali at 6. |
| **Future Continuous** | in progress at a future moment | At 6, I'**ll be meeting** Ali. |

> ūüß† **Quick guide:** *decision/predict* ‚Üí will ¬∑ *intention/evidence* ‚Üí going to ¬∑ *fixed plan*
> ‚Üí Present Continuous ¬∑ *mid-action at a future time* ‚Üí Future Continuous.

## 28. ‚Ěď Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Future Continuous or Future Simple?**
*In progress* at a future moment ‚Üí **will be + -ing**. The *whole action / a decision* ‚Üí
**will + V1**.

**Q2: When do I use the Present Continuous for the future?**
For **fixed arrangements** with a set time/place: *"I'm meeting Ali at 6."* It feels more
**arranged** than *going to*.

**Q3: Can I use it with state verbs?**
No ‚ÄĒ *know, want, be, like* use **will**: *"I'll know soon"*, not *"I'll be knowing."*

**Q4: Is "Will you be -ing?" different from "Will you -?"**
Yes ‚ÄĒ *"Will you be using the car?"* is a **neutral question about plans**; *"Will you use the
car?"* can sound like a **request**. The continuous is softer/more neutral.

**Q5: will be or going to be?**
For an action **in progress** at a future time, prefer **will be + -ing**. *"going to be -ing"*
exists but is less common.

# ūüß¨ EXPANSION PACK ‚ÄĒ Future Continuous (Go Deeper)

> You asked to **EXPAND**. These advanced notes (E1‚ÄďE12) take you from "I know will be + -ing"
> to "I use the whole future system smoothly." E10 is a **decision flowchart** for choosing the
> right future form. Each block builds on ¬ß5 and ¬ß11.

## E1. ūüĒĄ "It'll happen anyway" ‚ÄĒ the inevitable/routine future

A special use: the Future Continuous shows an action that will happen **as a matter of course**
‚ÄĒ part of the normal flow, **not** a special decision or plan:

| Future Simple (a decision) | Future Continuous (it'll happen anyway) |
|----------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| I'**ll see** Tom ‚ÄĒ I'll make a point of it. | I'**ll be seeing** Tom on Monday anyway. |
| I'**ll pass** the shop *(deliberate)*. | I'**ll be passing** the shop, so I'll get milk. |

```
   will + V1:        a chosen action      ("OK, I'll do it.")
   will be + -ing:   already in the flow   ("I'll be doing it anyway.")
```

> ūüí° This makes offers feel **light, no trouble**: *"I'**ll be going** past the post office, so
> I'll post it for you."* (= it costs me nothing extra). ūüá¶ūüáŅ *"Onsuz da..."*

---

## E2. ūüôč "Will you be -ing?" vs "Will you -?" ‚ÄĒ inquiry vs request

This is a subtle but very useful difference:

| Question | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **Will you be using** the car tonight? | a **neutral inquiry** about your plans |
| **Will you use** the car tonight? | can sound like a **request/instruction** |
| **Will you be joining** us? | softly asking about your plans |
| **Will you join** us? | a direct invitation/request |

```
   "Will you be -ing?"  = I'm just checking your plans (no pressure) ūüôā
   "Will you -?"        = I might be asking you to do it (a request) ūüôŹ
```

> ūüďĆ To ask about someone's plans **without sounding pushy**, use **Will you be + -ing?** This
> is why waiters and clerks say *"Will you be paying by card?"* ‚ÄĒ it's polite and neutral.

---

## E3. ūüõ°ÔłŹ Softening & distancing ‚ÄĒ avoiding an imposition

Because *"Will you be -ing?"* sounds like you're **not** asking a favour, it's a polite way to
find something out before you ask:

```
   ‚ĚĆ blunt:    "Are you going to drive to town? Give me a lift."
   ‚úÖ smooth:   "Will you be driving to town later? ‚ÄĒ if so, could I get a lift?"
```

> ūüí° The Future Continuous **separates** "checking your plans" from "asking a favour," so you
> don't seem to assume an answer. Very useful in emails and polite requests.

---

## E4. ūüĒģ Predicting the present (or the ongoing "now")

The Future Continuous can describe what is **probably happening right now** or at a known time ‚ÄĒ
a confident guess about an action in progress:

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Don't call now ‚ÄĒ they'**ll be having** dinner. | they're probably eating right now |
| It's 3 AM in Tokyo; she'**ll be sleeping**. | she's almost certainly asleep now |
| He'**ll be wondering** where we are. | he's probably wondering right now |

> ūüß† Here *will* = "**I'm sure / it's logical**," not "future." It's a prediction about a
> **present, ongoing** action. Pairs naturally with *probably / surely / by now*.

---

## E5. ūüďí Present Continuous vs going to vs will ‚ÄĒ the "plan" trio

For **future plans**, three forms compete. The difference is **how fixed/arranged** the plan is:

| Form | Fixedness | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **will** | decided **now**, not arranged | OK, I'**ll** visit her. |
| **be going to** | an **intention** (maybe not arranged) | I'**m going to** visit her sometime. |
| **Present Continuous** | a **fixed arrangement** (time/place set) | I'**m visiting** her at 6 on Friday. |

```
   Loosest ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚Ėļ Tightest
   will (just decided) < going to (intend) < Present Cont. (booked, time+place)
```

> ūüďĆ If there's a **set time and place** (and often another person involved), the **Present
> Continuous** is the most natural: *"We'**re getting** married in June."* (date fixed).

---

## E6. ‚Źį Future Continuous in time clauses (when / while / as)

After *when, while, as, by the time*, use the **present** ‚ÄĒ the Future Continuous goes in the
**main** clause:

| Time clause (present) | Main clause (Future Continuous) |
|-----------------------|----------------------------------|
| **When** you **arrive**, | I'**ll be waiting**. |
| **While** you **are** at work, | I'**ll be cleaning**. |
| **As** the sun **sets**, | we'**ll be driving** home. |
| **By the time** you **wake** up, | I'**ll be flying**. |

```
   ‚õĒ When you will arrive‚Ä¶    ‚úÖ When you arrive, I'll be waiting.
```

> ūüß† Same rule as Ch. 11: **no *will* in the time clause** ‚ÄĒ but the main clause can be Future
> Continuous instead of Future Simple.

## E7. ūüóŅ State verbs stay SIMPLE (use will + V1)

State verbs don't take the continuous ‚ÄĒ even in the future. Use the **Future Simple**:

| ‚ĚĆ Wrong | ‚úÖ Right |
|---------|---------|
| I'll be knowing | I'**ll know** |
| I'll be wanting | I'**ll want** |
| I'll be understanding | I'**ll understand** |
| I'll be believing | I'**ll believe** |
| I'll be being (tired) | I'**ll be** (tired) |

**State verb families:** *know, believe, understand, want, need, like, love, hate, own, seem,
belong.*

> ūüß† **Statue test** ūüóŅ: a mental state or possession (not a visible activity) ‚Üí **will + V1**,
> not *will be + -ing*.

---

## E8. ‚Ź≥ Rarer forms ‚ÄĒ *going to be + -ing* & future-in-the-past continuous

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **going to be + V-ing** | in-progress future (intention flavour) ‚ÄĒ less common | This time tomorrow, I'**m going to be** travelling. |
| **was/were going to be + V-ing** | a future-continuous plan seen from the **past** | I knew that by 9 I **would be waiting** / *was going to be waiting*. |
| **would be + V-ing** | future-in-the-past continuous (Ch. 17) | She promised she **would be waiting** at the gate. |

> ūüí° For most situations, **will be + -ing** is the standard. *going to be + -ing* exists but
> sounds heavier ‚ÄĒ prefer *will be + -ing* unless you want to stress a prior plan.

---

## E9. ūüďä Adding certainty ‚ÄĒ adverbs with the Future Continuous

Soften or strengthen with adverbs (placed **after** *will*, around *be*):

| Certainty | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Sure | At 8, I'**ll definitely be working**. |
| Likely | She'**ll probably be sleeping** by then. |
| Possible | They **might be waiting** outside. *(might be + -ing)* |
| Negative | We **won't be needing** that. |

```
   will + (definitely/probably) + be + V-ing
   might / may / could + be + V-ing   (possible, less certain)
```

> ūüďĆ *might/may/could + be + -ing* = "**possibly** in progress": *"He **might be** still
> **working**."*
