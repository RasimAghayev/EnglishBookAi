# 🛫 Future Continuous — In Progress in the Future (will be + V-ing)

### *Part 3 · Chapter 14 · will be + V-ing (+ Present Continuous for arrangements)*

> **Azərbaycanca qısa giriş:** Future Continuous (Davamedici Gələcək Zaman) gələcəyin müəyyən
> bir anında **davam edəcək** hərəkəti bildirir. *Sabah bu vaxt təyyarədə uçacağam* → *This
> time tomorrow, I'**ll be flying**.* Formul: **will be + fel + ing**. Bu fəsildə həm də gələcək
> **razılaşmalar** üçün Present Continuous-u öyrənəcəyik: *Saat 6-da Əli ilə görüşürəm* → *I'**m
> meeting** Ali at 6.* 🛫

---

## 1. 🟢 Introduction

The **Future Continuous** describes an action that **will be in progress** at a specific moment
**in the future**. It's the future version of the Past Continuous (Ch. 08): a "movie playing"
— but ahead of us, not behind.

```
   Future Simple:      I WILL fly to Baku tomorrow.       → the whole action (fact)
   Future Continuous:  This time tomorrow, I'LL BE FLYING. → in progress at that moment
```

The form has **three parts**: **will** + **be** + a verb with **-ing**.

This chapter also covers a **second future tool** you already half-know: the **Present
Continuous for arrangements** (*"I'm meeting Ali at 6"*) — for **fixed plans** with a time and
place set.

> 💡 **Big idea:** Future Continuous = **"at that future moment, the action will already be
> happening."** 🛫 Picture a future point and zoom in — something is mid-action.

## 2. ❓ Why Do We Use This Tense?

> 📌 Two big jobs: show an action **mid-flow at a future time**, and ask **politely** about
> someone's plans without sounding pushy.

## 4. 📅 Timeline

Zoom in on a **future point** — the action is **already running** there (it started a bit
before and continues a bit after).

```
   PAST            NOW                        FUTURE
 ──────────────────┼───────────[==== will be flying ====]──────►
                                       ▲

```

> 🛫 Compare with **Future Simple**: *"I'll fly tomorrow"* = the whole action as a fact. *"I'll

## 5. 🧮 Formula

Model verb: **work**. Helper: **will be** (same for **all** subjects! 🎉). Main verb = **V-ing**.

### ➕ Positive
```
   S  +  will be  +  V-ing
        (I'll be, you'll be, he'll be, she'll be, we'll be, they'll be)
```
| Subject | will be | V-ing | Example |
|---------|---------|-------|---------|
| I / You / He / She / It / We / They | **will be** | working | She **will be working**. |

> 🎉 Like *will*, **will be never changes** — one helper for everyone.

### ➖ Negative
```
   S  +  will not be  +  V-ing     (won't be)
```
- I **won't be working**. · They **won't be coming**.

### ❓ Question
```
   Will  +  S  +  be  +  V-ing  ?
```
- **Will** you **be using** the car? · **Will** she **be joining** us?

### ❓➖ Negative Question
```
   Won't  +  S  +  be  +  V-ing  ?
```
- **Won't** you **be needing** this?

### ✔️ Short Answers
| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Will you be working? | Yes, I **will**. | No, I **won't**. |

> ⚠️ Always **will be + V-ing** (three parts). The verb is *-ing*, and *be* never changes:
> *will be **working**, will be **flying**.*

## 6. 🏗️ Sentence Structure

```
 POSITIVE   ┌─────┬─────────┬──────────┬──────────────────┐
            │  S  │ will be │  V-ing   │ rest...          │
            │ She │ will be │ working  │ at 8 PM.         │
            └─────┴─────────┴──────────┴──────────────────┘

 NEGATIVE   ┌─────┬──────┬─────┬──────┬──────────┐
            │  S  │ will │ not │ be   │  V-ing   │
            │  I  │ will │ not │ be   │ sleeping │
            └─────┴──────┴─────┴──────┴──────────┘  (= won't be sleeping)

 QUESTION   ┌──────┬─────┬──────┬──────────┬─────────┐
            │ Will │  S  │ be   │  V-ing   │ rest ?  │
            │ Will │ you │ be   │ driving? │         │
            └──────┴─────┴──────┴──────────┴─────────┘
```

> 💬 **Contractions:** *I will be → I'll be · will not be → won't be.* Example: *"I'll be
> waiting."*

---

## 7. 📜 Grammar Rules

1. **Three parts:** *will* + *be* + **V-ing**. *will be* is the **same** for every subject.
2. Use it for an action **in progress at a future moment** — often with *at 8 o'clock, this
   time tomorrow, when you arrive*.
3. **State verbs** (know, be, like, want) usually do **NOT** take this form — use the **Future
   Simple**: ❌ *I'll be knowing* → ✅ *I'll know*.
4. In **time clauses** (when, while, as soon as), use the **present**, not *will*: *"When you
   **arrive**, I'**ll be waiting**."*
5. The **Present Continuous** expresses **fixed future arrangements** (time + place set): *"I'**m
   meeting** Ali at 6."* (See §11.)
6. Spelling of **V-ing** follows the usual rules (Ch. 04/08).

> 🧠 **Golden rule:** Future Continuous needs a **future moment** to zoom into. It's the action
> caught **mid-flow** at that point.

---

## 8. ✏️ Spelling Rules (the V-ing)

Same **-ing** rules as Chapters 04, 08:

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs → **+ing** | work → working, fly → flying |
| Silent **-e** → drop *e* + ing | write → writing, drive → driving |
| Short CVC → double consonant | run → running, sit → sitting |
| Ends in **-ie** → *ie → y* + ing | lie → lying, die → dying |

> 📌 *will* and *be* never change. All the work is on the **-ing** verb.

## 9. 🚩 Signal Words

```
 FUTURE MOMENT:   at 8 o'clock · this time tomorrow/next week · by then · in an hour
 DURATION FRAME:  all day tomorrow · the whole evening · from 2 to 4
 CONNECTORS:      when · while · as · during
```

| Signal | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **at 8 o'clock** | **At 8**, I'**ll be having** dinner. |
| **this time tomorrow** | **This time tomorrow**, we'**ll be flying**. |
| **when you arrive** | **When** you **arrive**, I'**ll be waiting**. |
| **all day** | I'**ll be working all day** tomorrow. |
| **while** | **While** you sleep, I'**ll be driving**. |

> 💡 *this time tomorrow / next week* is the classic Future Continuous trigger — it points to a
> specific future moment to zoom into.

## 10. ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| At 8, I **will having** dinner. | At 8, I **will be having** dinner. | need *be* + V-ing |
| I'**ll be knowing** the result. | I'**ll know** the result. | state verb → Future Simple |
| She **will be work** at 9. | She **will be working** at 9. | need **-ing** |
| When I **will arrive**, I'll be waiting. | When I **arrive**, I'll be waiting. | time clause → present |
| They **will being** sleeping. | They **will be** sleeping. | *be*, not *being* |
| I **am going to be having** dinner *(unnatural)* | I'**ll be having** dinner. | use *will be + -ing* |
| Tomorrow at 6 I **meet** Ali. *(in-progress meaning)* | At 6 I'**ll be meeting** Ali. *(or arrangement:* I'm meeting Ali at 6.*)* | choose the right future form |

> 📌 **Top Azerbaijani traps:** (1) dropping the *be* (*will having* ❌), and (2) using it with
> **state verbs** (*will be knowing* ❌).

## 11. ⚖️ Comparison with Similar Tenses

### 🆚 Future Continuous vs. Future Simple (will)

| | **Future Continuous** | **Future Simple (will)** |
|--|----------------------|--------------------------|
| **Meaning** | in progress at a future moment | the whole action / a fact / decision |
| **Form** | will be + V-ing | will + V1 |
| **Example** | At 8, I'**ll be eating**. | At 8, I'**ll eat**. *(I'll start at 8)* |
| **Feel** | caught mid-action | the event as a point |

```
   will eat:      ────●(8: start eating)────
   will be eating: ──[==== eating ====]●(8: already mid-meal)──
```

### 🆚 Future Continuous vs. Present Continuous (for arrangements) — THE other future tool

The **Present Continuous** is used for **fixed future arrangements** — when a **time and place
are set** (often with another person):

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **Present Continuous** | a fixed **arrangement** (planned, agreed) | I'**m meeting** Ali at 6 tomorrow. |
| **Future Continuous** | an action **in progress** at a future time | At 6, I'**ll be meeting** Ali *(mid-meeting)*. |
| **be going to** | an **intention** | I'**m going to** meet Ali soon. |

```
   Present Continuous = a diary entry 📒   "I'm seeing the dentist at 3."  (booked!)
   Future Continuous  = a zoomed-in moment "At 3, I'll be seeing the dentist." (mid-visit)
```

> 💡 For **personal arrangements with a set time**, the **Present Continuous** is the most
> natural: *"We'**re flying** to Baku on Monday"* (tickets booked) sounds better than *"We'll
> fly"* or *"We'll be flying"* for a plan.

### 🆚 The four "future" forms at a glance

| Form | Best for | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| **will + V1** | decision now, prediction, promise | I'**ll** help you. |
| **be going to** | intention, evidence | I'**m going to** study. |
| **Present Continuous** | fixed arrangement (time + place) | I'**m meeting** Ali at 6. |
| **Future Continuous** | in progress at a future moment | At 6, I'**ll be meeting** Ali. |

> 🧠 **Quick guide:** *decision/predict* → will · *intention/evidence* → going to · *fixed plan*
> → Present Continuous · *mid-action at a future time* → Future Continuous.

## 12. 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani Translation Tips

1. **Future Continuous ≈ Azerbaijani "-acaq/-əcək" + context (davam edən):**
   - *I'**ll be flying*** → *Uçacağam* / *uçuş edəcəyəm* (an + davam edən məna kontekstdən).
   - *This time tomorrow, I'**ll be working*** → *Sabah bu vaxt işləyəcəyəm / işləməkdə olacağam.*

2. Azerbaijani **does not** have a separate continuous future, so *"will be working"* and *"will
   work"* both often become *"işləyəcəm."* The **"in progress at that moment"** sense comes from
   **time words** (*sabah bu vaxt* = this time tomorrow).

3. **Present Continuous for arrangements** ≈ *"-ır/-ir"* (indiki zamanla gələcək plan):
   - *I'**m meeting** Ali at 6* → *Saat 6-da Əli ilə görüşürəm.*

4. **Polite inquiry:** *Will you be using…?* ≈ *"...istifadə edəcəksən?"* — softer than *Are you
   going to use…?*

> 💡 **Strategy:** since Azerbaijani uses one future ending, decide by **English logic**: is the
> action **in progress** at a future moment? → *will be + -ing*. A **fixed arrangement**? →
> Present Continuous. 🛫

## 23. 🗣️ Speaking Tips

- ✅ Use it to picture a **future moment in progress**: *"This time tomorrow, I'll be…"*
- ✅ Use it for **polite questions**: *"Will you be needing anything?"* (softer than "Do you

- ✅ Use the **Present Continuous** for fixed plans: *"I'm seeing the dentist at 3."*
- ⚠️ Don't use it with **state verbs** (*know, want, be*) — use *will*.
- ⚠️ Don't drop the *be*: it's *will **be** working*.

> 🗣️ **Shadow:** *"I'll be waiting."* · *"Will you be joining us?"* · *"This time tomorrow,

## 24. ✍️ Writing Tips

- Use the Future Continuous to set a **future scene** in progress (great for narratives and
  predictions about life ahead).
- Use the **Present Continuous** for **scheduled** items (meetings, flights, appointments).
- Keep **time clauses** in the present: *"When the sun **rises**, we'**ll be hiking**."*
- Don't overuse it — mix with **will** (facts) and **going to** (plans).

## 28. ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Future Continuous or Future Simple?**
*In progress* at a future moment → **will be + -ing**. The *whole action / a decision* →
**will + V1**.

**Q2: When do I use the Present Continuous for the future?**
For **fixed arrangements** with a set time/place: *"I'm meeting Ali at 6."* It feels more
**arranged** than *going to*.

**Q3: Can I use it with state verbs?**
No — *know, want, be, like* use **will**: *"I'll know soon"*, not *"I'll be knowing."*

**Q4: Is "Will you be -ing?" different from "Will you -?"**
Yes — *"Will you be using the car?"* is a **neutral question about plans**; *"Will you use the
car?"* can sound like a **request**. The continuous is softer/more neutral.

**Q5: will be or going to be?**
For an action **in progress** at a future time, prefer **will be + -ing**. *"going to be -ing"*
exists but is less common.

# 📚 Mini Story — *"This Time Tomorrow"* (≈320 words)

> Watch the **Future Continuous** paint future moments in progress, and the **Present Continuous**
> mark a fixed arrangement. Read aloud and feel the future come alive.

It was the night before the big trip. Kamran **couldn't** sleep. He **kept** imagining the next
day.

*This time tomorrow,* he **thought**, *I'**ll be sitting** on a plane. I'**ll be flying** over
the mountains. And by evening, I'**ll be walking** through the streets of Rome.*

His sister **knocked** on the door. "Still awake? You have an early start."

"I know," Kamran **said**. "I'**m catching** the 6 AM flight." *(a fixed arrangement)* "While
you'**re** all still sleeping, I'**ll be standing** in the security line."

She **laughed**. "Lucky you. While you'**re** eating pasta in Rome, I'**ll be sitting** in a
boring lecture."

"Don't worry," he **said**. "I'**ll be sending** you photos the whole time. Every hour, you'**ll
be getting** a new one."

"You'd better," she **warned**. "**Will** you **be visiting** the Colosseum?"

"Of course. This time on Saturday, I'**ll be standing** right in front of it."

She **smiled** and **sat** on the edge of his bed. "You know, in a few years, you **won't be
dreaming** about trips like this. You'**ll be taking** them all the time."

Kamran **grinned**. "I hope so. But right now, I'm just nervous. What if I oversleep? What if
I miss the flight?"

"You won't," she **said**. "I'**ll be waking** you up at four. I **promise**." *(promise → will)*

He **felt** calmer. As she **left**, she **turned** at the door. "Get some sleep. Because this
time tomorrow…"

"…I know," Kamran **laughed**. "I'**ll be flying**."

He **closed** his eyes. Outside, the city **was** quiet. But in his mind, he **was** already
soaring above the clouds — and somewhere ahead, Rome **was** waiting.

### 🔎 Story Notes — Spot the Grammar

- **Future Continuous (in progress at a future moment):** *I'll be sitting, I'll be flying,
  I'll be walking, I'll be standing, I'll be sending, you'll be getting, will you be visiting,
  I'll be standing, you won't be dreaming, you'll be taking, I'll be waking, I'll be flying.*
  → each one zooms into a **future moment** and catches the action **mid-flow**.
- **Present Continuous (fixed arrangement):** *"I'm catching the 6 AM flight"* → a **booked
  plan** with a set time. More natural than *"I'll catch."*
- **will (promise / prediction):** *"I'll be waking you up at four. I promise"* (promise);
  *"You won't"* (prediction).
- **The contrast in action:** the **booked flight** uses Present Continuous; the **moments
  during the trip** use Future Continuous; the **promise** uses will. Three future tools, one
  scene. ✅
- **Time clause:** *"While you're all still sleeping, I'll be standing in line"* → present after
  *while*, Future Continuous in the main clause. ✅
- **Pattern to copy:** picture a future moment (*this time tomorrow*) and describe what you'**ll
  be doing** there.

# 🧠 Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

1. **WILL BE + ING = "zoom into a future moment — it's already playing."** 🛫 Like pausing a
   film on a future frame.

2. **"This time tomorrow…"** ⏰ is the magnet phrase. Whenever you say it, reach for *will be +
   -ing*.

# 🧬 EXPANSION PACK — Future Continuous (Go Deeper)

> You asked to **EXPAND**. These advanced notes (E1–E12) take you from "I know will be + -ing"
> to "I use the whole future system smoothly." E10 is a **decision flowchart** for choosing the
> right future form. Each block builds on §5 and §11.

---

## E1. 🔄 "It'll happen anyway" — the inevitable/routine future

A special use: the Future Continuous shows an action that will happen **as a matter of course**
— part of the normal flow, **not** a special decision or plan:

| Future Simple (a decision) | Future Continuous (it'll happen anyway) |
|----------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| I'**ll see** Tom — I'll make a point of it. | I'**ll be seeing** Tom on Monday anyway. |
| I'**ll pass** the shop *(deliberate)*. | I'**ll be passing** the shop, so I'll get milk. |

```
   will + V1:        a chosen action      ("OK, I'll do it.")
   will be + -ing:   already in the flow   ("I'll be doing it anyway.")
```

> 💡 This makes offers feel **light, no trouble**: *"I'**ll be going** past the post office, so
> I'll post it for you."* (= it costs me nothing extra). 🇦🇿 *"Onsuz da..."*

---

## E2. 🙋 "Will you be -ing?" vs "Will you -?" — inquiry vs request

This is a subtle but very useful difference:

| Question | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **Will you be using** the car tonight? | a **neutral inquiry** about your plans |
| **Will you use** the car tonight? | can sound like a **request/instruction** |
| **Will you be joining** us? | softly asking about your plans |
| **Will you join** us? | a direct invitation/request |

```
   "Will you be -ing?"  = I'm just checking your plans (no pressure) 🙂
   "Will you -?"        = I might be asking you to do it (a request) 🙏
```

> 📌 To ask about someone's plans **without sounding pushy**, use **Will you be + -ing?** This
> is why waiters and clerks say *"Will you be paying by card?"* — it's polite and neutral.

---

## E3. 🛡️ Softening & distancing — avoiding an imposition

Because *"Will you be -ing?"* sounds like you're **not** asking a favour, it's a polite way to
find something out before you ask:

```
   ❌ blunt:    "Are you going to drive to town? Give me a lift."
   ✅ smooth:   "Will you be driving to town later? — if so, could I get a lift?"
```

> 💡 The Future Continuous **separates** "checking your plans" from "asking a favour," so you
> don't seem to assume an answer. Very useful in emails and polite requests.

---

## E4. 🔮 Predicting the present (or the ongoing "now")

The Future Continuous can describe what is **probably happening right now** or at a known time —
a confident guess about an action in progress:

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Don't call now — they'**ll be having** dinner. | they're probably eating right now |
| It's 3 AM in Tokyo; she'**ll be sleeping**. | she's almost certainly asleep now |
| He'**ll be wondering** where we are. | he's probably wondering right now |

> 🧠 Here *will* = "**I'm sure / it's logical**," not "future." It's a prediction about a
> **present, ongoing** action. Pairs naturally with *probably / surely / by now*.

---

## E5. 📒 Present Continuous vs going to vs will — the "plan" trio

For **future plans**, three forms compete. The difference is **how fixed/arranged** the plan is:

| Form | Fixedness | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **will** | decided **now**, not arranged | OK, I'**ll** visit her. |
| **be going to** | an **intention** (maybe not arranged) | I'**m going to** visit her sometime. |
| **Present Continuous** | a **fixed arrangement** (time/place set) | I'**m visiting** her at 6 on Friday. |

```
   Loosest ───────────────────────────────────► Tightest
   will (just decided) < going to (intend) < Present Cont. (booked, time+place)
```

> 📌 If there's a **set time and place** (and often another person involved), the **Present
> Continuous** is the most natural: *"We'**re getting** married in June."* (date fixed).

---

## E6. ⏰ Future Continuous in time clauses (when / while / as)

After *when, while, as, by the time*, use the **present** — the Future Continuous goes in the
**main** clause:

| Time clause (present) | Main clause (Future Continuous) |
|-----------------------|----------------------------------|
| **When** you **arrive**, | I'**ll be waiting**. |
| **While** you **are** at work, | I'**ll be cleaning**. |
| **As** the sun **sets**, | we'**ll be driving** home. |
| **By the time** you **wake** up, | I'**ll be flying**. |

```
   ⛔ When you will arrive…    ✅ When you arrive, I'll be waiting.
```

> 🧠 Same rule as Ch. 11: **no *will* in the time clause** — but the main clause can be Future
> Continuous instead of Future Simple.

---

## E7. 🗿 State verbs stay SIMPLE (use will + V1)

State verbs don't take the continuous — even in the future. Use the **Future Simple**:

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---------|---------|
| I'll be knowing | I'**ll know** |
| I'll be wanting | I'**ll want** |
| I'll be understanding | I'**ll understand** |
| I'll be believing | I'**ll believe** |
| I'll be being (tired) | I'**ll be** (tired) |

**State verb families:** *know, believe, understand, want, need, like, love, hate, own, seem,
belong.*

> 🧠 **Statue test** 🗿: a mental state or possession (not a visible activity) → **will + V1**,
> not *will be + -ing*.

---

## E8. ⏳ Rarer forms — *going to be + -ing* & future-in-the-past continuous

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **going to be + V-ing** | in-progress future (intention flavour) — less common | This time tomorrow, I'**m going to be** travelling. |
| **was/were going to be + V-ing** | a future-continuous plan seen from the **past** | I knew that by 9 I **would be waiting** / *was going to be waiting*. |
| **would be + V-ing** | future-in-the-past continuous (Ch. 17) | She promised she **would be waiting** at the gate. |

> 💡 For most situations, **will be + -ing** is the standard. *going to be + -ing* exists but
> sounds heavier — prefer *will be + -ing* unless you want to stress a prior plan.

---

## E9. 📊 Adding certainty — adverbs with the Future Continuous

Soften or strengthen with adverbs (placed **after** *will*, around *be*):

| Certainty | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Sure | At 8, I'**ll definitely be working**. |
| Likely | She'**ll probably be sleeping** by then. |
| Possible | They **might be waiting** outside. *(might be + -ing)* |
| Negative | We **won't be needing** that. |

```
   will + (definitely/probably) + be + V-ing
   might / may / could + be + V-ing   (possible, less certain)
```

> 📌 *might/may/could + be + -ing* = "**possibly** in progress": *"He **might be** still
> **working**."*

## E10. 🗺️ DECISION FLOWCHART — Which future form?

> Use this to choose between **all** the future forms covered so far:

```
 START: What do you mean about the future?
   │
   ├─ Is the action IN PROGRESS at a future moment? ──────► WILL BE + V-ing
   │     ("At 8, I'll be eating.")
   │
   ├─ Is it a FIXED arrangement (time + place set)? ─────► PRESENT CONTINUOUS
   │     ("I'm meeting Ali at 6.")
   │
   ├─ A timetable / schedule? ───────────────────────────► PRESENT SIMPLE
   │     ("The train leaves at 9.")
   │
   ├─ A PLAN/INTENTION (decided before) or EVIDENCE? ────► BE GOING TO
   │     ("I'm going to study."  /  "It's going to rain.")
   │
   └─ A DECISION NOW, PREDICTION, or PROMISE? ───────────► WILL + V1
         ("I'll get it."  /  "It'll be fine.")
```

| Form | One-word trigger |
|------|------------------|
| **will + V1** | decide-now / predict / promise |
| **be going to** | intention / evidence |
| **Present Continuous** | arranged |
| **Present Simple** | timetable |
| **will be + V-ing** | mid-action |

> 🧠 **Screenshot this.** Almost every future sentence you build maps to one of these five.

# 📚 Mini Story #2 — *"Where Will We Be?"* (≈330 words)

And somewhere in the future, that night **was** already waiting for them.

### 🔎 Story Notes — Spot the Grammar

- **Future Continuous (imagined future moments in progress):** *I'll be studying, I'll be
  living, I'll probably be surviving, I'll be running, I'll be making, you'll still be fixing,
  we won't be sitting, we'll be scattered, we'll be sitting.* → each zooms into a **future
  moment** and pictures the action **mid-flow**.
- **Present Continuous (fixed arrangement):** *"we'll all meet right here"* is set up as a
  **promise/plan**; *"even if I'm working / I'm living abroad"* uses present forms for future
  conditions.
- **will (predictions/promises):** *"we'll come back", "I'll find a way", "things would change…
  they would keep"* (the last two are **future-in-the-past** — *will* seen from the past
  narration, Ch. 17 preview).
- **The contrast in action:** the friends **imagine ongoing future scenes** with *will be +
  -ing*; they **make a firm plan** to meet; they **promise** with *will*. Three future tools,
  one rooftop. ✅
- **probably placement:** *"I'll **probably** be surviving"* → adverb after *will*, before *be*
