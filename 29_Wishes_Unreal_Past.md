# 🟪 PART 5 · Chapter 29
# Wishes & the Unreal Past
### *I wish · if only · would rather · it's (high) time* — "I **wish** I **knew**." / "**If only** I **hadn't** said it!" 🌙

> **The one-line idea.** To talk about what is **not real** — what you want but don't have, what you regret, what you wish would change — English **steps one tense back in time.** A **past** form no longer means past *time*; it means **distance from reality.**
> *I wish I **had** a car* (I don't). *I wish I **had had** more time* (I didn't). *I wish it **would** stop* (it won't).
> 🇦🇿 *Real olmayanı — istədiyin, amma sahib olmadığın, peşman olduğun, dəyişməsini istədiyin şeyi — demək üçün İngiliscə **bir zaman geriyə** addımlayır. **Keçmiş** forma artıq keçmiş *vaxt* deyil, **reallıqdan uzaqlıq** deməkdir.*
> 🇦🇿 *Azərbaycanca bu — **"kaş (ki)"** + arzu/şərt formasıdır: "**Kaş** maşınım **olaydı**." / "**Kaş** oxu**saydım**."*

---

## 🎯 In this chapter you will learn to…
```
✅ wish about the PRESENT:        wish + past simple     ("I wish I knew." — I don't)
✅ wish about the PAST:           wish + past perfect    ("I wish I had known." — I didn't)
✅ wish for CHANGE / complain:    wish + would           ("I wish it would stop raining.")
✅ wish for ABILITY:              wish + could           ("I wish I could help.")
✅ be dramatic / emotional:       if only (+ same forms) ("If only I had listened!")
✅ prefer what OTHERS do:         would rather + past    ("I'd rather you didn't smoke.")
✅ say it's overdue:              it's (high) time + past("It's time we left.")
✅ tell WISH from HOPE:           unreal wish vs real hope("I wish I could" vs "I hope I can")
```
> 🇦🇿 *Bu fəsildə: indi haqqında arzu; keçmiş haqqında arzu; dəyişiklik/şikayət; bacarıq arzusu; emosional "if only"; başqalarının davranışına üstünlük; "artıq vaxtdır"; wish↔hope fərqi.*

---

## 🧭 THE BIG PICTURE — "one step back = one step from reality"

Real English sentences use present tense for the present. But **unreal** English — wishes, regrets, imaginary situations — takes the tense **one step into the past** to signal *"this isn't true."*

```
   REALITY (present)          →   UNREAL WISH (one step back)
   "I don't have a car."      →   "I wish I HAD a car."          (past simple = unreal PRESENT)
   "I didn't study."          →   "I wish I HAD STUDIED."        (past perfect = unreal PAST)
   "It keeps raining."        →   "I wish it WOULD stop."        (would = wanting a CHANGE)

        the further back the tense →  the more "unreal" the meaning
```

> 🧠 **The key mental move:** in a wish, a **past-tense verb points at the present**, and a **past-perfect verb points at the past.** The tense is not about *time* — it's about *truth*. This is the same "unreal past" that powers the 2nd and 3rd conditionals (Ch.24).
> 🇦🇿 *Əsas fikir: arzuda **keçmiş fel indini**, **past perfect keçmişi** göstərir. Zaman deyil, **reallıq** məsələsidir — bu, 2-ci və 3-cü şərt cümlələrindəki "unreal past" ilə eynidir (Ch.24).*

## 📋 THE FORMS — at a glance

| Structure | Refers to | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **wish + past simple** | an unreal **present** | I **wish** I **had** more money. |
| **wish + past perfect** | an unreal **past** (regret) | I **wish** I **had studied** medicine. |
| **wish + would** | a wanted **change** / complaint | I **wish** you **would** listen. |
| **wish + could** | an unreal **ability** | I **wish** I **could** sing. |
| **if only + (same forms)** | emotional wish / regret | **If only** I **had known!** |
| **would rather + past** | preference about **another** person | I'**d rather** you **didn't** go. |
| **it's (high) time + past** | something **overdue** | It's **time** we **left**. |

> ⚠️ **Never** use *will / would have / can* right after *wish* to make a wish: ~~I wish I will~~, ~~I wish I would have~~, ~~I wish I can~~. → *I wish I could*; *I wish I had.* 🇦🇿 *wish-dən sonra bu arzu mənasında **will / would have / can** işlənmir.*

> 🔤 ***were* for everybody.** In careful/standard English, wishes use ***were*** for all persons: *"I wish I **were** taller."* Everyday speech allows *"I wish I **was** taller."* Both are heard; *were* is safer in exams. 🇦🇿 *Rəsmi dildə bütün şəxslər üçün **were**: "I wish I were…"; danışıqda "was" da olur.*

## 🔑 THE CORE — *I wish* + past simple vs + past perfect

The single most important contrast in the chapter is **which reality you're regretting** — the present or the past:

```
   wish + PAST SIMPLE   ──►  a PRESENT situation you'd change   "I wish I lived nearer." (I don't, now)
   wish + PAST PERFECT  ──►  a PAST action you regret           "I wish I'd left earlier." (I didn't, then)
```

| 🌙 Present wish (past simple) | ⏪ Past wish (past perfect) |
|---|---|
| I wish I **knew** the answer. *(I don't know)* | I wish I **had known** sooner. *(I didn't know)* |
| I wish I **had** a bigger flat. *(I don't have)* | I wish I **had bought** that flat. *(I didn't buy)* |
| I wish you **were** here. *(you're not)* | I wish you **had been** there. *(you weren't)* |
| I wish it **wasn't/weren't** so cold. *(it is)* | I wish it **hadn't rained.** *(it did)* |

> 🧪 **Which one?** Can you rephrase it as *"…but the situation is X **now**"*? → **past simple.** Can you rephrase it as *"…but that's what **happened**"*? → **past perfect.**
> 🇦🇿 *Hansı? İndiki vəziyyəti dəyişmək istəyirsənsə → past simple; keçmiş hərəkətə peşmansansa → past perfect.*

> 💔 **Negatives regret the opposite:** *"I wish I **hadn't** said that"* = I **did** say it, and I regret it. *"I wish I **had** said something"* = I stayed silent, and I regret that. 🇦🇿 *İnkar əks reallığı bildirir: "hadn't said" = dedim (peşmanam); "had said" = demədim (peşmanam).*

---

## 🌬️ *I wish + would* — wanting a change (and complaining)

Use *wish + would* when you want a **different behaviour** or a **change** — often with a note of **irritation**. The subject of *wish* and the subject of *would* are usually **different** (you can't easily change yourself with *would*):

| Sentence | Feeling |
|---|---|
| I wish it **would** stop raining. | frustration with a situation |
| I wish you **would** listen to me. | complaint about someone's behaviour |
| I wish he **wouldn't** interrupt. | annoyance at a repeated habit |
| I wish the bus **would** come. | impatience |

> ⚠️ **Don't say *I wish I would*.** To wish about **your own** ability or state, use *could* or the past simple: ~~I wish I would be taller~~ → *I wish I **were** taller*; ~~I wish I would sing~~ → *I wish I **could** sing.*
> 🇦🇿 *"I wish I would" demə. Öz bacarığın/vəziyyətin üçün **could** və ya past simple işlət.*

> 🔎 *wish + would* is for **actions and changes**, not fixed states: we say *"I wish I **had** a car"* (state), not ~~"I wish I would have a car."~~ 🇦🇿 *wish + would — hərəkət/dəyişiklik üçün, sabit vəziyyət üçün yox.*

---

## 💔 *IF ONLY* — the emotional twin of *wish*

*If only* means the same as *I wish*, but it's **stronger and more emotional** — a sigh, a regret, a longing. It uses the **same three structures**:

| Structure | Example |
|---|---|
| + past simple (present wish) | **If only** I **had** more time! |
| + past perfect (past regret) | **If only** I **had listened** to you! |
| + would (wanting change) | **If only** he **would** call! |

> 🗣️ *If only* often stands **alone** as an exclamation, trailing off with feeling: *"If only…"* It's common at the start of a sentence, frequently with **!**. 🇦🇿 *"If only" = "kaş (ki)" — daha güclü, emosionaldır; çox vaxt nida ilə: "If only I had known!"*

---

## 🙅 *WOULD RATHER* + past — preferring what someone **else** does

Chapter 22 gave you *"I'd rather **stay**"* (+ bare verb) for your **own** actions. When the preference is about **another person's** action, *would rather* takes an **unreal past** tense:

```
   I'd rather + BARE VERB          →  my own action     "I'd rather walk."
   I'd rather + (someone) + PAST   →  someone else's     "I'd rather you walked."
```

| Sentence | Meaning |
|---|---|
| I'd rather you **didn't** smoke here. | I prefer that you don't (now/generally). |
| I'd rather you **came** tomorrow. | I prefer a different day. |
| Would you rather I **left**? | Do you prefer that I go? |
| She'd rather we **hadn't told** him. | past preference/regret. |

> 🇦🇿 *Başqasının hərəkəti üçün "would rather + keçmiş zaman": "I'd rather you didn't smoke" = "siqaret çəkməməyini istərdim." Öz hərəkətin üçün isə "I'd rather + məsdərsiz fel" (Ch.22).*

---

## ⏰ *IT'S (HIGH) TIME* + past — it's overdue

*It's time* + a **past-tense** verb means the action **should already be happening** — there's a hint of mild criticism or urgency:

| Form | Example | Nuance |
|---|---|---|
| **it's time + to-infinitive** | It's **time to go.** | neutral, no criticism |
| **it's time + subject + past** | It's **time we went.** | overdue — we should have gone |
| **it's high / about time + past** | It's **high time** you **learned** to drive. | stronger — well overdue |

> ⚠️ ~~It's time we go~~ → **It's time we went.** The past tense here is **unreal** (present meaning), just like in wishes. 🇦🇿 *"It's time we went" (keçmiş forma, indiki məna) — "artıq getməyimizin vaxtıdır." "It's high time" daha güclüdür.*

---

## 🆚 *WISH* vs *HOPE* — unreal vs real

This trips up almost every learner. The rule is about **possibility**:

```
   HOPE  →  the thing is POSSIBLE / likely  →  present or will/future
            "I hope you pass." "I hope it doesn't rain." "I hope you'll come."
   WISH  →  the thing is UNREAL / impossible / contrary to fact  →  past forms
            "I wish I could pass." "I wish it wasn't raining." "I wish you could come."
```

| ✅ Hope (real, possible) | ✅ Wish (unreal, impossible) |
|---|---|
| I **hope** you **feel** better soon. | I **wish** I **felt** better. *(but I don't)* |
| I **hope** it **doesn't** rain tomorrow. | I **wish** it **weren't** raining now. |
| I **hope** she **passes** the exam. | I **wish** I **had passed** the exam. |

> ⛔ **Classic error:** ~~"I hope I had more money"~~ (using *hope* for the unreal). If it can't/won't happen → **wish** + past: *"I **wish** I **had** more money."* 🇦🇿 *hope = mümkün olan üçün (ümid); wish = qeyri-mümkün / reallığa zidd üçün (kaş). Qarışdırma!*

> 🎁 **Set phrase exception:** *wish + (someone) + noun* is a fixed formula, not unreal: *"I **wish** you a happy birthday / good luck / a safe trip."* 🇦🇿 *"I wish you…" + isim = hazır ifadə (arzu etmək), qeyri-real deyil.*

## 🧱 ADVANCED & related patterns

| Pattern | Example | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **wish + could** (ability) | I wish I **could** drive. | unreal ability |
| **wish + could have** (past ability) | I wish I **could have come.** | past unreal ability |
| ***were* subjunctive** | If I **were** you… / I wish I **were** taller. | formal; all persons |
| **suppose / imagine + past** | Suppose you **won** the lottery. | hypothetical (like a wish) |
| **would sooner** (= would rather) | I'd **sooner** you **didn't**. | more formal |
| **It's not as if / as though** | It's not **as if** he **cared**. | unreal comparison |

> 🇦🇿 *Əlaqəli quruluşlar: wish + could (bacarıq); were-subjunctive; suppose/imagine + keçmiş (fərziyyə); would sooner (= would rather).*

## 🅰️ §A — LEVEL DIFFERENCES (A1 → C2)

How the *same idea* — "I want what isn't real" — grows with your level:

| Level | What the learner can do | Typical sentence |
|---|---|---|
| **A1** | *hope* for real things; fixed *wish + noun* | "I **hope** so." / "I **wish** you luck." |
| **A2** | basic *wish + past simple* | "I **wish** I **had** a bike." |
| **B1** | *wish + past perfect*; *if only* | "I **wish** I **had studied**." / "**If only** I knew!" |
| **B2** | *wish + would*; *it's time + past*; *would rather + past* | "I **wish** he **would** stop." / "It's **time** we **left**." |
| **C1** | *were*-subjunctive; *wish + could have*; *wish* vs *hope* control | "I **wish** I **were** braver." / "I **wish** I **could have** helped." |
| **C2** | register & nuance; *would sooner*; *as if + past* | "I'd **sooner** you **hadn't**." / "It's not **as if** he **cared**." |

> 🇦🇿 *A1: hope + hazır wish. A2: wish + past simple. B1: wish + past perfect, if only. B2: wish + would, it's time. C1: were-subjunctive, wish vs hope. C2: üslub, would sooner.*

## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT

1. **"I wish" softens complaints.** *"I wish you'd told me"* is gentler than *"You should have told me"* (Ch.21) — it voices regret without direct blame. 🇦🇿 *"I wish you'd told me" şikayəti yumşaldır.*
2. **"If only" is the language of longing.** Poems, songs and heartfelt moments favour *if only* over *I wish* for its emotional weight. 🇦🇿 *"If only" — həsrət, arzunun emosional dili.*
3. **"I wish!" as a reply = "no chance."** *"Are you rich?" — "I wish!"* means "sadly not." 🇦🇿 *Tək başına "I wish!" = "kaş elə olaydı — amma yox."*
4. **"was" vs "were" is a class/formality signal.** *"I wish I was"* is everyday; *"I wish I were"* sounds educated/careful. Both are understood. 🇦🇿 *"was" gündəlik, "were" rəsmi/savadlı səslənir.*
5. **"wish + would" is nearly always a complaint.** If there's no irritation or desire for change, natives switch to the past simple. 🇦🇿 *"wish + would" demək olar həmişə şikayətdir.*
6. **Hope, not wish, for the doable future.** Before an exam a native says *"I hope I pass,"* never *"I wish I pass."* Save *wish* for the impossible. 🇦🇿 *Mümkün gələcək üçün "hope", qeyri-mümkün üçün "wish".*

| Instead of… | You can say… | Shade |

> 🇦🇿 *Eyni arzu/təəssüf fikrini "regret", "should have", "I'd love to", "fingers crossed" ilə də demək olar.*

# 📖 MINI STORY #1 — "The Letter Never Sent"

### *Present wishes, past regrets and one longing 'if only' — a woman, a drawer, and an old letter.*
### 🇦🇿 *İndiki arzular, keçmiş peşmanlıqlar və bir həsrətli "if only" — bir qadın, bir siyirmə və köhnə bir məktub.*

---

Sevil found the letter she had written twenty years ago but never posted.

"I wish I **had sent** it," she said quietly. *(past regret — she didn't send it.)*

Her daughter looked up. "Who was it for?"

"An old friend. We argued, and I wrote this to apologise… but I was too proud. I wish I **weren't** so stubborn." *(present wish — she still is.)*

"You could find her now, Mum."

"I wish it **were** that simple," Sevil sighed. "I don't even know where she lives. If only I **had kept** in touch!" *(if only — emotional past regret.)*

"Well, I wish you **would** at least try," said her daughter. "I hate seeing you sad." *(wish + would — wanting a change in behaviour.)*

Sevil smiled at that. "You're right. I wish I **had** your courage." *(present wish.)* "It's high **time** I **stopped** making excuses." *(it's high time + past — overdue.)*

"I'd rather you **called** her than sat here wishing," her daughter said gently. *(would rather + past — preference about her mother.)*

"I hope she still remembers me," Sevil said, reaching for her phone. *(hope — a real, possible future — not a wish.)*

And this time, she didn't put it off.

### 🔍 Spot the wish — sort the structures

| From the story | Structure | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| "I wish I **had sent** it." | wish + past perfect | past regret (she didn't) |
| "I wish I **weren't** so stubborn." | wish + past simple | present wish (she is) |
| "I wish it **were** that simple." | wish + past simple (*were*) | present, contrary to fact |
| "If only I **had kept** in touch!" | if only + past perfect | emotional past regret |
| "I wish you **would** at least try." | wish + would | wanting a change |
| "I wish I **had** your courage." | wish + past simple | present wish |
| "It's high **time** I **stopped**…" | it's high time + past | overdue |
| "I'd rather you **called** her…" | would rather + past | preference (other person) |
| "I **hope** she still remembers me." | hope (real/possible) | **not** a wish — it can happen |

### ✍️ After-reading tasks
```
1. Find every wish. Which are present wishes (past simple) and which are past regrets (past perfect)?
2. "I hope she still remembers me" — why is this HOPE, not WISH? What would change if it were a wish?
3. Rewrite "I wish I weren't so stubborn" as (a) a piece of advice (Ch.21) and (b) an 'if only'.
4. Turn "It's high time I stopped making excuses" into a plain 'should' sentence.
5. 🇦🇿→EN: "Kaş məktubu göndərəydim." / "Kaş bu qədər inadkar olmasaydım." / "Ona zəng etməyini istərdim."
```
> 💬 **Answers**
> 1. Present wishes (past simple): *"I wish I weren't so stubborn," "I wish it were that simple," "I wish I had your courage."* Past regrets (past perfect): *"I wish I had sent it," "If only I had kept in touch!"* Wish + would: *"I wish you would try."*
> 2. It's **hope** because the friend remembering her is **possible / real** — it can still happen. A wish needs the unreal: *"I wish she still remembered me"* would imply she **doesn't** (contrary to fact).
> 3. (a) *"You shouldn't be so stubborn."* (b) *"If only I weren't so stubborn!"*
> 4. *"I should stop making excuses."* / *"I really should stop making excuses."*
> 5. I wish I had sent the letter. / I wish I weren't (wasn't) so stubborn. / I'd rather you called her.

# 🧨 EXPANSION PACK — Wishes & the Unreal Past (E1–E12)
### *The deep layer: the unreal-past engine, the timing split, wish vs hope, the subjunctive, if only, would rather & idioms.*
### 🇦🇿 *Dərin qat: "unreal past" mexanizmi, zaman bölgüsü, wish↔hope, subjunktiv, if only, would rather və idiomlar.*

---

## 🅴1 — The "unreal past" engine (why past = distance, not time)

English has no special "wishing" verb form — so it **borrows the past tense** and repurposes it to mean *"not real."* The further back the tense, the further from reality:

```
   present fact:  "I don't know."     →  present unreal:  "I wish I KNEW."        (past simple)
   past fact:     "I didn't know."    →  past unreal:     "I wish I HAD KNOWN."   (past perfect)
                        └─ same mechanism drives 2nd & 3rd conditionals (Ch.24) ─┘
```

- A **past-simple** verb after *wish* = an **unreal present.**
- A **past-perfect** verb after *wish* = an **unreal past.**
- This "backshift for unreality" is the single engine behind **wishes, if only, it's time, would rather,** and **conditionals.**

> 🇦🇿 *İngiliscədə ayrıca "arzu" forması yoxdur — keçmiş zaman **"real deyil"** mənasında işlədilir. Bu mexanizm həm arzuları, həm şərt cümlələrini (Ch.24) idarə edir.*

---

## 🅴2 — ⚠️ wish + past simple vs past perfect (the timing splitter)

The exam favourite. A timeline makes it obvious:

```
 wish + PAST SIMPLE   →  regret a situation that is true NOW      "I wish I lived nearer." (I don't, now)
 wish + PAST PERFECT  →  regret an action completed in the PAST   "I wish I'd moved nearer." (I didn't, then)
```

| Sentence | Reality | Time |
|---|---|---|
| I wish I **had** a dog. | I don't have one | now |
| I wish I **had had** a dog as a child. | I didn't have one | the past |
| I wish she **were** here. | she isn't | now |
| I wish she **had been** here. | she wasn't | the past |

> 🧪 **Test:** "…but I don't / it isn't" → **past simple.** "…but I didn't / it wasn't" → **past perfect.** 🇦🇿 *"amma indi yox" → past simple; "amma o vaxt olmadı" → past perfect.*

---

## 🅴3 — wish + would: the complaint modal

*wish + would* has two special rules that fool learners:

1. **Different subjects.** You wish that **someone/something else** would change — not yourself: *"I wish **you** would…," "I wish **it** would…"* Not usually ~~"I wish I would."~~
2. **Actions/changes, not states.** *would* wants a change in **behaviour** or a **future event**, not a fixed state: *"I wish I **had** a car"* (state ✅), not ~~"I wish I would have a car."~~

| ✅ Works | ❌ Doesn't work |
|---|---|
| I wish you **would** stop shouting. | ~~I wish I would be taller.~~ → *I wish I **were** taller* |
| I wish it **would** warm up. | ~~I wish I would sing well.~~ → *I wish I **could** sing* |
| I wish they **wouldn't** be so loud. | ~~I wish it would be sunny (state).~~ → *I wish it **were** sunny* |

> 🇦🇿 *wish + would: (1) mübtəda **fərqli** olmalıdır; (2) yalnız **hərəkət/dəyişiklik** üçün, sabit vəziyyət üçün yox. Özün üçün → could / past simple.*

---

## 🅴4 — ⚠️ WISH vs HOPE (the possibility test — C1 splitter)

The deciding question: **can it (still) happen?**

```
  CAN happen / likely   →  HOPE + present or will     "I hope you pass." "I hope it'll be sunny."
  CAN'T happen / untrue →  WISH + past forms          "I wish I could pass." "I wish it were sunny."
```

| Situation | HOPE (real) | WISH (unreal) |
|---|---|---|
| an exam tomorrow | I **hope** I **pass**. | *(after failing)* I **wish** I **had passed**. |
| the weather | I **hope** it **doesn't rain**. | I **wish** it **weren't** raining. |
| a visit | I **hope** you **can** come. | I **wish** you **could** come. *(you can't)* |

> ⛔ ~~"I hope I had more money"~~ (unreal with *hope*) ✗ → **"I wish I had more money."** And *hope* takes **present/future**, never a bare unreal past. 🇦🇿 *Sual: hələ baş verə bilər? → hope. Mümkün deyil / reallığa zidd? → wish.*

---

## 🅴5 — The *were*-subjunctive across all persons

In wishes, conditionals and *as if*, standard English uses ***were* for every person** — this is the surviving English subjunctive:

| Person | Subjunctive (formal/standard) | Colloquial |
|---|---|---|
| I | I wish I **were** rich. | I wish I **was** rich. |
| he/she/it | I wish it **were** warmer. | I wish it **was** warmer. |
| set phrase | **If I were you**, I'd rest. *(never "was" here)* | — |

> 🗣️ *"If I **were** you"* is **fixed** — natives keep *were* even in casual speech. Elsewhere, *was* is common but less formal; exams reward *were*. 🇦🇿 *Standart dildə bütün şəxslər üçün **were**; "If I were you" isə həmişə **were**.*

---

## 🅴6 — *IF ONLY* — register & position

Same three structures as *wish*, but stronger feeling. Notes:

| Feature | Detail | Example |
|---|---|---|
| emotional weight | more heartfelt than *I wish* | **If only** I had listened! |
| standalone | can end on a sigh | "…**If only.**" |
| position | usually **fronts** the clause | **If only** she **were** here. |
| + would | for a longed-for change | **If only** the pain **would** stop. |

> 🇦🇿 *"If only" = "kaş (ki)" — daha güclü, emosionaldır; adətən cümlənin əvvəlində, çox vaxt nida ilə.*

---

## 🅴7 — *would rather / would sooner* + past (others' actions)

| Structure | Subject | Example |
|---|---|---|
| would rather + **bare verb** | **same** subject *(Ch.22)* | I'd rather **stay** in. |
| would rather + subj + **past** | **different** subject | I'd rather you **stayed** in. |
| would rather + subj + **past perfect** | different, past regret | I'd rather you **hadn't** told him. |
| **Would you rather** + subj + past…? | question | **Would you rather** I **drove**? |
| would **sooner** (= rather) | more formal | I'd **sooner** you **didn't**. |

> 🇦🇿 *Öz hərəkətin → would rather + məsdərsiz fel; başqasının hərəkəti → would rather + subyekt + keçmiş zaman.*

---

## 🅴8 — *IT'S (HIGH / ABOUT) TIME* + past

| Form | Nuance | Example |
|---|---|---|
| it's time **to** + verb | neutral | It's **time to** go. |
| it's time + subj + **past** | mild criticism (overdue) | It's **time** you **went**. |
| it's **high** time + past | stronger — well overdue | It's **high time** you **found** a job. |
| it's **about** time + past | often reproachful | It's **about time** they **apologised**! |

> ⚠️ The verb is an **unreal past** with **present** meaning: ~~It's time we go~~ → *It's time we **went**.* 🇦🇿 *"it's time" + keçmiş forma (indiki məna) = "artıq … vaxtıdır"; "high/about time" daha güclü/məzəmmətli.*

---

## 🅴9 — Wishes with modals & in reported speech

| Pattern | Example | Note |
|---|---|---|
| wish + **could** | I wish I **could** drive. | unreal present ability |
| wish + **could have** | I wish I **could have** stayed. | unreal past ability |
| wish + **would** | I wish he **would** try. | wanting change |
| reported wish | She said she wished she **knew**. | *wish* stays; inner verb already back-shifted |
| reported (past wish) | He said he wished he **had gone**. | past perfect stays |

> 🔑 After *wish*, use **could/could have** for ability — never *can*; and **would** for change — never *will*. In reporting, the already-unreal tense doesn't shift again. 🇦🇿 *wish-dən sonra bacarıq üçün could/could have; dəyişiklik üçün would; nəqldə artıq geriləmiş zaman bir daha dəyişmir.*

---

## 🅴10 — Idiomatic & emphatic wishes

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **I wish!** | "sadly not / no chance" | "Are you on holiday?" — "**I wish!**" |
| **You wish!** | "in your dreams!" | "She'll go out with me." — "**You wish!**" |
| **Don't I wish!** | emphatic agreement | "Bet you'd love a day off." — "**Don't I wish!**" |
| **wish + (sb) + noun** | fixed good-wishes | I **wish** you **luck** / **a safe trip.** |
| **as if / as though + past** | unreal comparison | He talks **as if** he **owned** the place. |
| **would that…** *(literary)* | archaic *I wish* | **Would that** it **were** so. |

> 🇦🇿 *Bu ifadələr çox işlənir: "I wish!" (kaş elə olaydı — amma yox), "You wish!" (xəyalında!), "as if + keçmiş" (guya).*

---

## 🅴11 — WISHES ACROSS TIME (master table)

| I want to change… | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| a **present** state | wish / if only + **past simple** | I wish I **spoke** French. |
| a **past** action | wish / if only + **past perfect** | I wish I **had learned** French. |
| **someone's behaviour** (future/repeated) | wish / if only + **would** | I wish you **would** practise. |
| my **present ability** | wish + **could** | I wish I **could** speak French. |
| my **past ability/chance** | wish + **could have** | I wish I **could have** studied abroad. |

> 🇦🇿 *İndiki vəziyyət → past simple; keçmiş hərəkət → past perfect; davranış dəyişikliyi → would; bacarıq → could / could have.*

---

## 🅴12 — 🇦🇿 CONTRASTIVE SYNTHESIS

| English | 🇦🇿 | Watch out |
|---|---|---|
| I wish I **had** a car. | Kaş maşınım **olaydı**. | optative **-aydı/-əydi** |
| I wish I **had studied**. | Kaş **oxusaydım**. | conditional-past **-saydı/-səydi** |
| I wish it **would** stop. | Kaş **dayansaydı** / dayanaydı. | AZ uses the same wish-form |
| **If only** I **had known!** | **Kaş** biləydim! | *kaş* = if only / I wish |
| I **hope** you pass. | Keçəcəyinə **ümid edirəm**. | **ümid** = hope (real) |
| It's **high time** you **left**. | Artıq getməyinin **vaxtıdır**. | overdue |
| I'd rather you **didn't**. | Etmə**məyini** istərdim. | preference (other) |

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani contrast in one line:** Azerbaijani marks a wish **openly** with the particle **kaş (ki)** plus a dedicated **optative/conditional** ending (*olaydı, oxusaydım*) — the "unreality" is built into the mood. English has **no such particle or mood**; it hides unreality inside an ordinary-looking **past tense.** So the AZ learner already *feels* wishing (via *kaş*) but must learn to express it by **shifting the tense back** instead of adding a word — and must split *kaş* into **wish** (unreal) vs **ümid/hope** (real).

## 📦 BLOCK 12 — 🎬 REAL-LIFE — regret, longing & complaint (decode each one)

| The situation | What it means | 🇦🇿 |
|---|---|---|
| After a fight: "I **wish** I **hadn't** shouted." | past regret | Kaş qışqırmayaydım. |

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "The Rainy Holiday"

### *Present wishes, past regrets, complaints to the sky and one bit of real hope — a washed-out week by the coast.*
### 🇦🇿 *İndiki arzular, keçmiş peşmanlıqlar, göyə şikayətlər və bir damla real ümid — sahildə yağışlı bir həftə.*

> Read it twice. **First** for the story; **second** circling every *wish · if only · would rather · it's time · hope.*
> 🇦🇿 *İki dəfə oxu: əvvəl hekayə üçün, sonra hər arzu quruluşunu dövrəyə alaraq.*

---

Rain streaked the window of the little seaside cottage. Kamran stared out at the grey water.

"I wish it **would** stop," he muttered. *(complaint — wanting change.)* "Three days now."

"I wish we **had booked** for July instead of April," said Aysel, closing her book. *(past regret — they didn't.)* "If only I **had checked** the forecast!" *(if only — past regret.)*

"Don't. It's not your fault. I just wish there **were** something to do indoors." *(present wish, subjunctive.)*

"There is — I wish you **would** play cards with me instead of sulking," she teased. *(wish + would — a gentle complaint.)*

Kamran laughed despite himself. "You're right. I wish I **weren't** so grumpy on holiday." *(present wish.)*

"I'd rather you **smiled** than glared at the sea," Aysel said. *(would rather + past — about him.)* "And honestly, it's high **time** we **stopped** treating rain like a disaster." *(it's high time + past — overdue.)*

So they made tea, dealt the cards, and by evening the cottage was full of laughter. Kamran even admitted, "I almost wish it **would** rain tomorrow too." *(playful wish + would.)*

"Careful what you wish for," Aysel grinned. "But I **hope** it clears up — I'd love one day on the beach." *(hope — a real, possible future — not a wish.)*

The next morning, unbelievably, the sun came out.

## 🏁 End of Chapter 29

You can now speak about **what isn't real.** English signals the unreal by stepping **one tense back**: *wish + past simple* for a **present** you'd change (*"I wish I knew"*), *wish + past perfect* for a **past** you regret (*"I wish I'd known"*), and *wish + would* to demand a **change** or voice a **complaint** (*"I wish it would stop"*). *If only* is the same, but with more **feeling**; *would rather + past* states a preference about **someone else** (*"I'd rather you didn't"*); and *it's (high) time + past* says something is **overdue** (*"It's time we left"*). Above all, you keep **wish** (unreal, impossible) apart from **hope** (real, possible) — and remember *were* for all persons, and never *will / can / would have* after *wish*. 🌙

```
 ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ 🌙 present wish:  wish + PAST SIMPLE       "I wish I knew."                 │
 │ ⏪ past regret:   wish + PAST PERFECT      "I wish I had known."            │
 │ 🌬️ want a change: wish + WOULD             "I wish you would listen."       │
 │ 💔 emotional:     if only (+ same forms)   "If only I had listened!"        │
 │ 🙅 others' acts:  would rather + PAST      "I'd rather you didn't."         │
 │ ⏰ overdue:       it's (high) time + PAST  "It's time we left."             │
 │ ⚠️ wish (unreal) ≠ hope (real) · use "were" · never wish + will/can/would-have│
 └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
