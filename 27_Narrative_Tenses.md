# 1. 📌 Introduction / Giriş

When you tell a story about the past, you don't say everything in one flat tense. Watch the
difference:

```
   FLAT (one tense — boring, unclear):
   "I walked home. It rained. I forgot my umbrella. I got wet."

   LAYERED (narrative tenses — vivid, clear):
   "I was walking home when it started to rain. I had forgotten my umbrella,
    so by the time I got home, I had been getting wet for twenty minutes."
```

A good story uses **four past tenses together**, each with a different job:

```
 ┌──────────────────────────┬─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ TENSE                    │ JOB IN THE STORY                           │
 ├──────────────────────────┼─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
 │ Past Simple              │ the main EVENTS, in order (the backbone)    │
 │ Past Continuous          │ the BACKGROUND / scene / interrupted action │
 │ Past Perfect             │ an EARLIER past (flashback / before)        │
 │ Past Perfect Continuous  │ a DURATION leading up to a past moment      │
 └──────────────────────────┴─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

This chapter is the **capstone of Part 2** — all four past tenses, finally working as a team. 🧩

---

# 2. 🤔 Why do we use them? / Niyə işlədirik?

Because a story has **layers**, not just a line of events:

```
   • What happened next?        → Past Simple        (the action)
   • What was going on around it?→ Past Continuous    (the scene)
   • What had happened before?   → Past Perfect       (the backstory)
   • How long had it been going? → Past Perfect Cont. (the build-up)
```

Using only Past Simple makes a story **flat and sometimes unclear** (which event came first?).
Layering the tenses makes it **vivid, ordered, and easy to follow** — like adding depth to a flat
drawing. 🇦🇿 Hekayəyə **dərinlik** verir: hadisə, fon, keçmişin keçmişi, müddət.

# 4. ⏳ Timeline / Zaman xətti — the four layers

```
   EARLIER PAST            MAIN STORY TIME              (now)
   ◄─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────►
                                                              
   had done ●·····┐                                            
   (Past Perfect) │  before the story starts                  
                  ▼                                            
   was doing ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~  (background, in progress)         
   (Past Continuous)      │                                    
                          ✕── did ──► did ──► did  (main events, in order)
                          (Past Simple = the backbone)         
                                                              
   had been doing ●———————┘  (duration leading up to a past point)
   (Past Perfect Continuous)
```

> 🧠 **Past Simple** is the *line* of the story. **Past Continuous** is the *scenery* around it.
> **Past Perfect** is a *step back* to before. **Past Perfect Continuous** is the *length* of that
> step back. 🇦🇿 Backbone = -dı; fon = -ırdı; əvvəl = -mışdı; müddət = -mışdı + neçə vaxt.

# 5. 🧪 Formula / Düstur — each tense's role (quick recap)

```
 1️⃣ PAST SIMPLE — main events (V2)
    I woke up, got dressed, and left the house.
    🇦🇿  Oyandım, geyindim və evdən çıxdım.  (-dı/-di)

 2️⃣ PAST CONTINUOUS — background / interrupted action (was/were + V-ing)
    The sun was shining and birds were singing.
    I was walking home when the storm began.
    🇦🇿  Günəş parlayırdı.  Evə gedirdim ki...  (-ırdı/-irdi)

 3️⃣ PAST PERFECT — the earlier past / flashback (had + V3)
    When I arrived, the train had already left.
    🇦🇿  Çatanda qatar artıq getmişdi.  (-mışdı/-mişdi)

 4️⃣ PAST PERFECT CONTINUOUS — duration up to a past point (had been + V-ing)
    I was exhausted because I had been driving for ten hours.
    🇦🇿  Yorğun idim, çünki on saat idi sürürdüm.  (-mışdı + müddət)
```

### 🔗 The narration patterns (how they combine)
```
   while/as + Past Continuous , Past Simple
       While I was cooking, the phone rang.

   when + Past Simple , Past Simple        (one after another)
       When the bell rang, everyone stood up.

   Past Simple + because + Past Perfect (Continuous)
       She was upset because she had failed / had been waiting for hours.

   By the time + Past Simple , Past Perfect
       By the time we arrived, the film had started.
```

# 6. 🧱 Sentence Structure / Cümlə quruluşu — layering a scene

```
   SET THE SCENE          →  Past Continuous   (It was raining. People were rushing.)
   GIVE THE BACKSTORY     →  Past Perfect      (I had left my umbrella at home.)
   TELL THE MAIN EVENTS   →  Past Simple       (I ran, I slipped, I fell.)
   SHOW THE BUILD-UP      →  Past Perfect Cont.(I had been running for ages.)
```

```
   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │  "It WAS getting dark.  (continuous = scene)                          │
   │   I HAD WALKED for hours and HAD BEEN looking for the path.          │
   │                          (perfect / perfect continuous = before)      │
   │   Suddenly, I SAW a light, and my heart JUMPED."                      │
   │                          (simple = main events)                       │
   └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

🔑 The **Past Simple** carries the plot forward. The other three add **depth around it**.

---

# 7. 📐 Grammar Rules / Qrammatika qaydaları

1. **Past Simple = the sequence of main events.** Each new V2 moves the story forward.
2. **Past Continuous = background & interruptions.** Use *while/as* + was/were V-ing for the scene;
   the Past Simple event "interrupts" it.
3. **Past Perfect = the EARLIER of two past events.** Use it to show something happened **before**
   the main story moment (flashback, reason, "already").
4. **You don't need Past Perfect for events already in order.** If you say events in the sequence
   they happened, plain Past Simple is enough:
   ✅ *I got up, had breakfast, and left.* (no perfect needed — already in order)
   ✅ *When I got to the station, the train **had** left.* (perfect: it left FIRST, out of order)
5. **Past Perfect Continuous = how long** an action had been going **before** a past point (often
   explains a past state: tiredness, wet clothes, anger).
6. **Don't slip into the present.** Keep the whole story in the past (a very common error).
7. **while vs when:** *while/as* + continuous (longer background); *when* + simple (point event).

```
   THE GOLDEN QUESTION when two past things happen:
   "Which came FIRST?"
     → the earlier one = Past Perfect (had done)
     → the later one   = Past Simple (did)
   If they're just a sequence (1→2→3), all Past Simple is fine.
```

# 8. ✍️ Form Recap / Forma xülasəsi

| Tense | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Past Simple | V2 | walked / went / saw |
| Past Continuous | was/were + V-ing | was walking / were going |
| Past Perfect | had + V3 | had walked / had gone |

> ⚠️ Use **V3** (not V2) after *had*: ✅ *had **gone*** / ❌ *had **went***. 🇦🇿 *had* + V3
> (getmişdi), V2 yox.

# 9. 🔔 Signal Words / Siqnal sözləri (narration linkers)

```
   SEQUENCE:   first, then, next, after that, later, finally, in the end
   SIMULTANEOUS: while, as, just as, at the same time, meanwhile
   INTERRUPTION: when, suddenly, all of a sudden, just then, the moment
   EARLIER:    before, already, by the time, earlier, previously, until then
   DRAMATIC:   no sooner ... than, hardly ... when, scarcely ... when
   DURATION:   for hours, all day, since morning (with perfect continuous)
```
> 💡 *"By the time…"* almost always pairs with **Past Perfect**: *By the time help arrived, the
> fire had spread.* 🇦🇿 Hekayə bağlayıcıları: *əvvəlcə, sonra, birdən, nəhayət, ...-anda.*

# 10. 🚫 Common Mistakes / Ən çox edilən səhvlər

```
 ❌ I was tired because I worked all day.        (no sense of build-up)
 ✅ I was tired because I had been working all day.

 ❌ When I arrived, the train left.   (sounds like it left AS/after I arrived)
 ✅ When I arrived, the train had (already) left.   (it left BEFORE — Past Perfect)

 ❌ While I cooked dinner, the phone rang.
 ✅ While I was cooking dinner, the phone rang.    (background = continuous)

 ❌ I had finished my work, then I went home, then I had eaten.  (over-using Past Perfect)
 ✅ I finished my work, went home, and ate.         (simple sequence = Past Simple)

 ❌ So I'm walking home and suddenly I see a dog...  (slipping into present)
 ✅ So I was walking home and suddenly I saw a dog... (keep it past!)
```

> 🇦🇿 **İki ən böyük səhv:** (1) əvvəl baş verən hadisə üçün **Past Perfect** işlətməmək; (2)
> hekayəni keçmişdə başlayıb **indiki zamana** sürüşdürmək.

# 12. 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani Translation Tips / Tərcümə məsləhətləri

Each English narrative tense maps to a familiar Azerbaijani form (you met these in Part 2):

```
   ENGLISH                          AZERBAIJANI
   Past Simple (walked)         →   getdim / yazdı        (-dı/-di)
   Past Continuous (was walking)→   gedirdim / yazırdı     (-ırdı/-irdi)
   Past Perfect (had walked)    →   getmişdim / yazmışdı    (-mışdı/-mişdi)
   Past Perfect Cont. (had been →   neçə saat idi gedirdim /
       walking)                     bir müddət idi yazırdı  (müddət + -ırdı)
```

⚠️ **Two traps for Azerbaijani speakers:**
1. **Past Perfect for the earlier event.** Azerbaijani often uses simple *-dı* for both events and
   lets context show the order. English **must** mark the earlier one with **had + V3**.
   - AZ: *Çatanda qatar getmişdi/getdi* → EN: *…the train **had** left.* (Past Perfect needed)
2. **Keep the whole story in the past.** Don't drift into present like AZ storytelling sometimes
   does for vividness.

> 🇦🇿 Qızıl qayda: **Əvvəl baş verən = had + V3 (-mışdı). Hadisələr sırası = Past Simple (-dı).
> Fon = Past Continuous (-ırdı).**

# 23. 🎤 Speaking Tips / Danışıq məsləhətləri
1. **Set the scene first** with Past Continuous (*It was a cold night, the wind was howling…*),
   then drop in the events with Past Simple.
2. Use *"By the time…"* + Past Perfect to sound advanced and clear.
3. Keep the whole story **in the past** — don't slip into the present.
4. Use **'d** contractions (*I'd been waiting*) — they sound natural and fluent.

# 24. 🖊️ Writing Tips / Yazı məsləhətləri
1. Make **Past Simple the backbone**; sprinkle the others for depth — don't overload.
2. Use Past Perfect **only** for genuinely earlier events; don't tag it onto every verb.
3. Vary your **linkers** (while, by the time, no sooner) to control the timeline.
4. Open a story with a **continuous** scene-setter for atmosphere.

# 25. 🎧 Listening Tips / Dinləmə məsləhətləri
1. The **'d** (*I'd, she'd*) before V3 = Past Perfect; before V-ing + "been" = Perfect Continuous.
2. *"By the time… / before… / already…"* signal an earlier event is being placed.
3. Track the **order** of events, not just the order they're spoken — perfect tenses reorder time.

# 26. 📖 Reading Tips / Oxu məsləhətləri
1. In fiction, a shift to **Past Perfect** = a **flashback** to earlier events.
2. **Past Continuous** openings paint the **scene**; the **Past Simple** that follows is the action.
3. Notice how authors mix all four to control **pace** and **suspense**.

# 27. 🗽 Native Speaker Usage / Doğma danışanlar necə işlədir
- Natives layer these tenses **automatically** when telling stories — it's the heartbeat of every
  anecdote, joke, and novel.
- In very casual speech, people sometimes use the **historic present** for drama (*"So he walks
  in…"*) — but standard narration stays in the past.
- *"I'd been…"* and *"By the time…"* are everywhere in natural storytelling.
- Over-using Past Perfect sounds unnatural; natives use it **only** when order matters.

# 28. ❓ FAQ / Tez-tez verilən suallar

**Q1. Do I have to use all four tenses in every story?**
No. **Past Simple** is the core. Add the others **only when** you need background, an earlier
event, or a duration. 👉 §11.

**Q2. When exactly do I need Past Perfect?**
Only for the **earlier** of two past events — when you mention them **out of order** or stress that
one happened **before** another. A plain sequence (1→2→3) just uses Past Simple. 👉 §7.

**Q3. while or when?**
*while/as* + **continuous** (background); *when* + **simple** (point/interruption). 👉 §7, §9.

**Q4. Past Perfect or Past Perfect Continuous?**
*Perfect* = a completed earlier event ("had left"). *Perfect Continuous* = the **duration** before
("had been waiting for an hour"). 👉 §5, §11.

**Q5. Why do my stories sound flat?**
Probably all Past Simple. Add a **continuous** scene-setter and a **perfect** backstory for depth.
👉 §1, §6.

**Q6. How does this map to Azerbaijani?**
*-dı* (simple), *-ırdı* (continuous), *-mışdı* (perfect), *-mışdı + müddət* (perfect continuous).
The trap: AZ often uses *-dı* for the earlier event too; English needs **had + V3**. 👉 §12.

**Q7. Can I tell a past story in the present tense?**
Only as a deliberate informal style (*"So he walks in…"*). For exams/writing, **stay in the past**.

# 📚 MINI STORY — *"The Last Train Home"*
### (all four narrative tenses woven together) 🚂

> 🎯 Read 🇬🇧 first. The colours in the key show **which tense does which job**. Then read 🇦🇿 and
> do the questions.

## 🇬🇧 The Last Train Home

It **was raining** when Kamal **reached** the station, and the platform **was almost empty**.

He **had been travelling** all day. He **had left** his hometown at dawn, **had changed** trains
twice, and by evening he **was exhausted**. All he **wanted** was to get home.

But when he **looked** at the board, his heart **sank**. The last train **had already left**.

A station guard **was locking** the gates. "You **missed** it by ten minutes," he **said**. "It
**left** at nine."

Kamal **sat** down on a wet bench. He **had been looking forward** to this evening for weeks — his
sister **was getting** married the next morning, and now he **was stuck**, two hundred kilometres
away.

While he **was sitting** there, a car **pulled up**. A woman **rolled down** the window. "You look
like someone who **has missed** a train," she **said**, smiling. "I'm driving north. Get in."

Kamal **hesitated**. He **had never accepted** a lift from a stranger before. But something in her
voice **felt** kind, so he **got** in.

They **talked** the whole way. It **turned out** she **had grown up** in his town and **had been
living** abroad for years. By the time they **arrived**, the rain **had stopped**, and the sky
**was turning** pink.

He **reached** the house just as his family **was waking** up.

Years later, whenever he **told** the story, he **would** smile and say that he **had missed** a
train — but **had caught** something far better.

## 🔑 Story key — which tense, which job
🟦 **Past Simple — the main events (backbone):**
```
   reached · looked · sank · said · sat · pulled up · got in · talked · arrived · reached
```
🟩 **Past Continuous — background / scene / in progress:**
```
   was raining · was locking · was sitting · was getting (married) · was turning (pink) ·
   was waking up
```
🟨 **Past Perfect — the earlier past (flashback / before):**
```
   had left · had changed · had already left · had never accepted · had grown up · had missed
```
🟧 **Past Perfect Continuous — duration up to a past point:**
```
   had been travelling (all day) · had been looking forward (for weeks) · had been living (abroad for years)
```

> 🧠 See it work: the **Past Simple** carries the plot (reached → looked → sank → sat). The
> **Past Continuous** sets each scene (it *was raining*, the guard *was locking* up). The **Past
> Perfect** fills in the backstory (the train *had left*, he *had never accepted* a lift). The
> **Past Perfect Continuous** shows the build-up (he *had been travelling* all day). Four tenses,
> one seamless story. 🚂

# 🧩 EXPANSION PACK — *Narrative Tenses* (E1–E12)
### The storyteller's advanced toolkit. ☕

> 🎯 §1–§29 gave you the four narrative tenses. This pack adds the **literary devices** — dramatic
> inversion, future-in-the-past, the narrative present, *would/was to* for fate — plus the AZ
> traps. These are what make a story sound **written by a real author**. Read slowly.

---

## E1 · The narrative (historic) present — for drama 🎬
In very **informal, vivid** storytelling, speakers switch to the **present** to make the past feel
live:

```
   "So I'm walking home, right, and this guy comes up to me and goes, 'Got the time?'..."
```
- ✅ Great for jokes, gossip, "you had to be there" anecdotes — it pulls the listener in.
- ⚠️ **Avoid in exams and formal writing** — there, stay fully in the past.
> 🇦🇿 Çox qeyri-rəsmi danışıqda canlılıq üçün indiki zamana keçirik (*"Gedirəm, birdən..."*), amma
> rəsmi yazıda KEÇMİŞDƏ qal.

---

## E2 · Dramatic INVERSION — the author's flourish 🎩
For suspense, front a negative/limiting adverb and **invert** (auxiliary before subject). Common in
narration:

```
   No sooner HAD I sat down THAN the phone rang.
   Hardly HAD we left WHEN it started to pour.
   Scarcely HAD the words left his mouth WHEN he regretted them.
   Little DID I know (that) it would be the last time I saw her.
   Never HAD she felt so afraid.
```
🔑 Structure: **[Negative adverb] + had + subject + V3 + …** Note the pairings: *No sooner… **than***
(not "when"), *Hardly/Scarcely… **when**.*
> 💡 One inversion per paragraph = elegant; too many = purple prose. 🇦🇿 Gərginlik üçün inversiya:
> köməkçi fel mübtədadan **əvvəl** gəlir.

---

## E3 · `would` & `used to` inside a story — recurring past 🔁
To describe **repeated** actions that formed the *backdrop* of a story (not single events), use
**would / used to** (👉 taught in full in **[Chapter 23 — Used to / Would](23_Used_to_Would.md)**):

```
   "That summer, we WOULD wake at dawn. Grandma USED TO make tea on the old stove,
    and we WOULD sit on the steps and watch the sun come up. Then, one morning, everything changed."
```
- **used to / would** paint the *habitual scene*; the **Past Simple** ("one morning… changed")
  breaks it with the actual event.
> 🧠 Repeated background = *would/used to*. Single event = Past Simple. 🇦🇿 Təkrarlanan fon →
> *would/used to (-ardı/-ərdi)*; tək hadisə → Past Simple.

---

## E4 · Future-in-the-past — `was going to / was about to` ⏭️
To show a **plan or imminent action** in the past (often **interrupted**), use these:

```
   I WAS GOING TO call you, but I lost my phone.        (a past intention, maybe unfulfilled)
   I WAS ABOUT TO leave when the doorbell rang.          (on the very edge of doing it)
   She WAS ON THE POINT OF giving up when help arrived.   (formal, dramatic)
   We WERE DUE TO arrive at six, but the train was late.  (a scheduled plan)
```
🔑 *was/were going to + V1* (intention) · *was/were about to + V1* (imminent). Both are classic
**"…when X happened"** setups in stories.
> 🇦🇿 *...edəcəkdim, amma...* (was going to) / *...etmək üzrə idim ki...* (was about to).

---

## E5 · Past Perfect with after / before / when — optional or needed? ⚖️
With *after, before, by the time*, the **order is already clear**, so Past Perfect is often
**optional**:

```
   After he had finished, he left.   =   After he finished, he left.   ✅ both fine
   Before I had eaten, I felt sick.   →   often just: Before I ate...   ✅
```
But with **when** (and when order would otherwise be ambiguous), Past Perfect **changes the
meaning**:
```
   When I arrived, the train LEFT.        → it left AS/after I arrived (we both move on)
   When I arrived, the train HAD LEFT.     → it left BEFORE I arrived (I missed it)
```
> 🧠 *after/before* make the order obvious → Past Perfect optional. *when* → Past Perfect can be
> **essential** for meaning. 🇦🇿 *after/before* ilə sıra aydındır; *when* ilə *had+V3* mənanı
> dəyişir.

---

## E6 · `would` & `was to` — fate, destiny, future-in-the-past 🔮
A beautiful literary device: looking **forward** from a past point to what **was going to happen
later** (which the character didn't yet know):

```
   He left the village that morning. He WOULD never see it again.
   She was just a clerk then — but she WAS TO BECOME the country's first president.
   Little did they know that this decision WOULD change everything.
```
- **would + V1** = "destined to happen later" (from the story's viewpoint).
- **was/were to + V1** = formal "was destined/scheduled to."
> 💡 This creates **dramatic irony** — the reader senses the future the character can't. 🇦🇿
> *...bir daha görməyəcəkdi* (would never see) / *...olacaqdı* (was to become).

---

## E7 · State verbs stay SIMPLE in narration 🧊
Even when setting a scene, **state verbs** (feel, see, know, realise, understand, want, seem,
believe) stay **Past Simple** — not continuous:

```
 ✅ I KNEW something was wrong.        ❌ I was knowing...
 ✅ She SEEMED nervous.                 ❌ She was seeming...
 ✅ Suddenly I REALISED the truth.      ❌ I was realising...
 ✅ I FELT a chill.                      (✅ "I was feeling ill" = ongoing physical state, ok)
```
> 🧠 Action verbs → continuous for scene (*was walking*). State verbs → **simple** even for the
> scene (*knew, saw, wanted*). 🇦🇿 Hal felləri (*know, see, want*) hekayədə də **continuous olmur**.

---

## E8 · Pace control — continuous (slow) vs simple (fast) ⏱️
Tense choice sets the **rhythm** of a scene:

```
   SLOW / ATMOSPHERIC (continuous):
   "The rain was falling. The candles were flickering. Somewhere, a clock was ticking."

   FAST / ACTION (a chain of Past Simple):
   "He grabbed the bag, kicked the door, jumped the fence, and ran."
```
- Pile up **continuous** verbs to slow time and build mood.
- Pile up **Past Simple** verbs to speed it up for action.
> 💡 Writers switch deliberately: linger in continuous, then snap into simple for the big moment.
> 🇦🇿 Continuous = ləng/atmosfer; Past Simple zənciri = sürətli hərəkət.

---

## E9 · Reported speech INSIDE a story (combine Ch 17) 🗣️
Narration and reported speech meet constantly — the backshift fits naturally into the past story:

```
   "She told me she HAD SEEN him the day before. I asked where he HAD GONE,
    but she just said she DIDN'T KNOW."
```
- *had seen / had gone* (Past Perfect) = events **before** the reported moment — exactly the
  narrative-tense logic.
> 🧠 Inside a past story, reported speech **backshifts** just as in Chapter 17 — and the "earlier"
> verbs become Past Perfect. The two chapters reinforce each other. 🇦🇿 Hekayə içində nəql →
> backshift + had+V3.

## E10 · 🗺️ THE STORYTELLER'S TOOLKIT — build a scene
```
 ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │  WANT TO...                           USE...                                  │
 ├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
 │  move the plot forward (next event)   → Past Simple (V2)                      │
 │  set the scene / atmosphere           → Past Continuous (was V-ing)           │
 │  show two things at once              → Past Continuous + Past Continuous      │
 │  interrupt the background             → was V-ing ... when + Past Simple       │
 │  flash back to an earlier event       → Past Perfect (had V3)                 │
 │  show how long before                 → Past Perfect Continuous (had been V-ing)│
 │  describe a repeated past backdrop    → would / used to (E3)                  │
 │  show an interrupted plan             → was going to / was about to (E4)      │
 │  hint at the future / fate            → would / was to (E6)                   │
 │  add suspense                         → inversion: No sooner had... (E2)       │
 │  slow down / speed up                 → continuous (slow) / simple chain (fast)│
 │  report what someone said             → backshift + Past Perfect (E9)          │
 └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
> ⚠️ Keep **Past Simple** as the spine; everything else decorates it. And **stay in the past**.

## E11 · 🏋️ Advanced exercises (inversion, future-in-past, mixed)
```
 1. Rewrite with inversion: "I had hardly arrived when the show began." → Hardly ______
 2. Rewrite with inversion: "I sat down, and immediately the phone rang." → No sooner ______
 3. Future-in-past: "I intended to call, but I forgot." → I ______ call, but I forgot.
 4. Was about to: "I was on the very edge of leaving when she arrived." → I ______ leave when...
 5. Fate (would): "He left, not knowing he'd never come back." → He left. He ______ never ______ back.
 6. State verb: "Suddenly I ______ (realise) the door was open." (simple or continuous?)
 7. after + perfect (optional): "After she ______ (finish), she went home." (two correct forms?)
 8. Reported in narration: Direct "I have seen him." → She told me she ______ him.
```

<details>
<summary>👉 E11 — Answers & why</summary>

```
 1. Hardly had I arrived when the show began.          (Hardly + had + S + V3 ... when)
 2. No sooner had I sat down than the phone rang.       (No sooner ... THAN)
 3. I was going to call, but I forgot.                  (future-in-the-past intention)
 4. I was about to leave when she arrived.               (imminent action)
 5. He left. He would never come back.                   (would = fate/future-in-past)
 6. realised  (state verb → Past Simple, never "was realising")
 7. "After she had finished, ..." OR "After she finished, ..."  (both correct — "after"
    makes the order clear, so Past Perfect is optional)
 8. ...she had seen him.                                  (backshift: have seen → had seen)
```
</details>

## E12 · 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani-interference mistakes (deep cut)
```
 ❌ When I arrived, the train left.            (if it left BEFORE)
 ✅ When I arrived, the train had left.         → əvvəlki hadisə = had + V3.

 ❌ While I cooked, the phone rang.
 ✅ While I was cooking, the phone rang.         → fon = was/were + V-ing.

 ❌ I was tired because I worked all day.
 ✅ I was tired because I had been working all day. → müddət/səbəb = had been + V-ing.

 ❌ Suddenly I was realising the truth.
 ✅ Suddenly I realised the truth.               → hal felləri continuous OLMUR.

 ❌ So I'm walking home and I see a dog. (in an exam)
 ✅ So I was walking home and I saw a dog.         → hekayəni KEÇMİŞDƏ saxla.

 ❌ He had finished, then he had gone home, then he had eaten.
 ✅ He finished, went home, and ate.               → sadə sıra = Past Simple (had-ı çıxart).
```
> 🧠 The 6 deep AZ traps: **(1)** no Past Perfect for the earlier event; **(2)** simple instead of
> continuous for the scene; **(3)** no perfect continuous for duration; **(4)** state verbs in
> continuous; **(5)** slipping into the present; **(6)** over-using *had* in a plain sequence.

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — *"The House at the End of the Lane"*
### (narrative tenses + the advanced devices) 🌙

> 🎯 Story #1 was warm and lucky. This one is **atmospheric** — it shows the Expansion Pack tools in
> action: **inversion** (*No sooner had…*), **future-in-the-past / fate** (*she would…*), and
> **pace** (slow continuous → fast simple). **Bold** = the narrative-tense moments; the key sorts
> them. Read 🇬🇧 first, then 🇦🇿, then the questions.

## 🇬🇧 The House at the End of the Lane

The rain **was falling** softly as Lale **walked** up the lane. The trees **were dripping**, and
somewhere a dog **was barking**. She **had not been** back in twenty years.

The house **had changed.** The garden, which her mother **had once kept** so carefully, **had grown
wild.** The paint **had peeled.** She **had been driving** since morning, and now, standing at the
gate, she **felt** suddenly very small.

She **had come** to sell it. That, at least, **was** the plan.

She **pushed** the door. It **opened** with a groan. Inside, everything **was waiting** exactly as
it **had been left** — the chairs, the clock, the faded photographs. *Little did she know* what she
**would find** in the attic.

She **climbed** the narrow stairs. Dust **was floating** in the grey light. In the corner **stood**
an old trunk she **had never seen** before.

**No sooner had she opened it than** her breath **caught.** Inside **lay** hundreds of letters —
all addressed to her, all unsent. Her mother **had been writing** to her for years, one letter
every birthday, long after Lale **had stopped** calling.

She **sat** down on the dusty floor and **began** to read. *"My dear Lale,"* the first one **said**,
*"I hope that one day you will come home…"*

Outside, the rain **stopped.** A thin light **broke** through the clouds.

Lale **did not** sell the house that day. She **would** stay, after all — in the place where, she
now understood, she **had been loved** all along.

## 🔑 Story key — which tense / device, which job
🟦 **Past Simple — main events (backbone):**
```
   walked · felt · pushed · opened · climbed · stood/lay (description) · sat · began · stopped · broke
```
🟩 **Past Continuous — scene / atmosphere (slow pace):**
```
   was falling · were dripping · was barking · was waiting · was floating
```
🟨 **Past Perfect — the earlier past (backstory):**
```
   had changed · had once kept · had grown · had peeled · had been left · had never seen ·
   had stopped
```
🟧 **Past Perfect Continuous — duration before a past point:**
```
   had been driving (since morning) · had been writing (for years)
```
🎩 **Advanced devices (Expansion Pack):**
```
   INVERSION:        "No sooner HAD she opened it THAN her breath caught."
   FUTURE-IN-PAST:    "what she WOULD find" · "She WOULD stay, after all"
   FATE / IRONY:      "Little did she know..."
   PASSIVE in narration: "as it had been left" · "she had been loved all along"
```

> 🧠 Feel the craft: the **continuous** verbs slow the opening to a hush (*the rain was falling,
> the trees were dripping*), the **Past Perfect** layers in the sad backstory (*the garden had
> grown wild*), and then **"No sooner had she opened it than…"** snaps the pace for the big moment.
> Finally, *"she would stay"* looks **forward** to a future the character is only just choosing.
> That's narrative tenses doing real emotional work. 🌙
