# ⏳ Present Perfect Continuous — The "How Long" Tense

### *Part 1 · Chapter 06 · have/has been + V-ing*

> **Azərbaycanca qısa giriş:** Present Perfect Continuous (İndiki Bitmiş Davamedici Zaman)
> keçmişdə başlayıb **indiyə qədər davam edən** (və ya yenicə bitmiş) bir **fəaliyyətin
> müddətini** vurğulayır. Sual: **"Nə qədərdir?"** *İki saatdır gözlə**yirəm*** →
> *I **have been waiting** for two hours.* Formul: **have/has + been + fel + ing**.
> Bu, Part 1-i tamamlayan son indiki zamandır! 🏁

---

## 1. 🟢 Introduction

You now know the **Present Perfect** (*"I have done"* — result/experience). Its twin is
the **Present Perfect Continuous** (*"I have been doing"*). Both connect the past to now,
but the continuous one focuses on the **activity itself** and **how long it has lasted**.

```
   Present Perfect:            I've read 50 pages.        → RESULT (how many — done)
   Present Perfect Continuous: I've been reading all day. → ACTIVITY (how long — ongoing)
```

The form has **three parts**: **have/has** + **been** + **V-ing**. The word **been**
never changes — only *have/has* changes for the subject.

> 💡 **Big idea:** This tense answers **"How long have you been doing it?"** and explains
> **"Why are you like this now?"** (tired, dirty, out of breath…).

---

## 2. ❓ Why Do We Use This Tense?

Three main reasons:

| # | Use | Example | Azərbaycanca |
|---|-----|---------|--------------|
| 1 | **Duration up to now** (How long?) | I**'ve been studying** for three hours. | müddət (indiyə qədər) |
| 2 | **Recent activity with present evidence** | You**'re** sweating! **Have** you **been running**? | indiki nəticə/sübut |
| 3 | **Temporary / repeated lately** | She**'s been going** to the gym a lot recently. | son vaxtlar təkrarlanan |

> 📌 Two magic questions point to this tense:
> **"How long…?"** (duration) and **"Why are you (tired/wet/…) now?"** (recent activity).

## 3. 🌍 Real-Life Situations

- ⏱️ **Saying how long:** *"I've been waiting for ages!"*
- 😓 **Explaining your state:** *"Sorry I'm dirty — I've been gardening."*
- 🔁 **Recent habit:** *"He's been working late all week."*
- 🤧 **Noticing evidence:** *"Your eyes are red. Have you been crying?"*
- 📚 **Ongoing effort:** *"I've been learning English since January."*

## 4. 📅 Timeline

The activity **starts in the past** and **continues up to now** (or has just stopped),
with the **duration** in focus.

```
   PAST                                        NOW            FUTURE
 ────┼──[========== continuous activity ==========]●──────────────┼────►
     start                                         │
     (e.g. 2 hours ago)                            │
            └──────── "How long?" = the focus ─────┘

   "I have been waiting for two hours."  → started 2h ago, still waiting (or just stopped)
```

Compare with the **Present Perfect** (focus = the completed result, not the line):
```
 ────┼─────X────────────────────────────────────●──────────────┼────►
     "I have written the report."  (it's finished — result matters, not duration)
```

---

## 5. 🧮 Formula

Model verb: **work**. Three parts: **have/has + been + V-ing**.

### ➕ Positive
```
   S  +  have / has  +  been  +  V-ing
        (has → he/she/it)
```
| Subject | have/has | been | V-ing | Example |
|---------|----------|------|-------|---------|
| I / You / We / They | **have** ('ve) | been | working | I**'ve been working**. |
| He / She / It | **has** ('s) | been | working | She**'s been working**. |

### ➖ Negative
```
   S  +  have / has  +  NOT  +  been  +  V-ing
        (haven't / hasn't)
```
- I **haven't been sleeping** well. · He **hasn't been feeling** good.

### ❓ Question
```
   Have / Has  +  S  +  been  +  V-ing  ?
```
- **Have** you **been waiting** long? · **Has** she **been crying**?

### ❓➖ Negative Question
```
   Haven't / Hasn't  +  S  +  been  +  V-ing  ?
```
- **Haven't** you **been listening**? · **Hasn't** he **been studying**?

### ✔️ Short Answers
| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Have you been waiting? | Yes, I **have**. | No, I **haven't**. |
| Has she been working? | Yes, she **has**. | No, she **hasn't**. |

> ⚠️ Short answers use **have/has** only — never *been* or the main verb.

## 7. 📜 Grammar Rules

1. **Three parts, fixed order:** have/has + **been** + V-ing. *been* never changes.
2. **has** for he/she/it; **have** for the rest.
3. **State verbs are NOT used** in this tense (know, like, be, own…). Use the **Present
   Perfect** instead: ✅ *I've known him for years.* ❌ *I've been knowing him.*
4. Use **for** + period, **since** + start point (same as Present Perfect).
5. Negatives put *not* after *have/has*; questions move *have/has* to the front.
6. With *live, work, study, teach* (long actions), the simple Perfect and the continuous
   are often **both fine** for duration (see §11).

---

## 8. ✏️ Spelling Rules (the V-ing)

Same **-ing** rules as Chapter 04:

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs → **+ing** | wait → waiting, study → studying |
| Silent **-e** → drop *e* + ing | write → writing, make → making |
| Short CVC → double consonant | run → running, sit → sitting, swim → swimming |
| Ends in **-ie** → *ie → y* + ing | lie → lying, die → dying |

> 📌 *been* is always **been** — only the *-ing* word follows spelling rules.

## 10. ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| I **am working** here for two hours. | I**'ve been working** here for two hours. | duration up to now → Perfect Continuous |
| She **has been knowing** him for years. | She**'s known** him for years. | state verb → Present Perfect |
| I've been working **since** two hours. | I've been working **for** two hours. | period → *for* |
| He **has been work** all day. | He **has been working** all day. | need **-ing** |
| They **have being** waiting. | They **have been** waiting. | it's *been*, not *being* |
| I **have been** wait. | I **have been waiting**. | -ing required |

> 📌 **Top Azerbaijani trap:** using the present (*"I am working here for 2 hours"*) for
> something that **started in the past and continues**. English needs **have been + -ing**.

## 11. ⚖️ Comparison with Similar Tenses

### 🆚 Present Perfect Continuous vs. Present Perfect (the key comparison!)

| | **Present Perfect Continuous** | **Present Perfect** |
|--|-------------------------------|--------------------|
| **Form** | have/has been + V-ing | have/has + V3 |
| **Focus** | the **activity** / **how long** | the **result** / **how many / done** |
| **Question** | How **long**…? | How **much/many**…? Is it done? |
| **Example** | I**'ve been painting** (that's why I'm messy). | I**'ve painted** the room (it's finished). |
| **State verbs** | ❌ not used | ✅ used (know, have, be…) |

```
   ACTIVITY/DURATION:   I've been reading this book.    (still reading, or just stopped)
   RESULT/COMPLETED:    I've read this book.            (finished it — done)

   I've been writing emails all morning.   → how long / why I'm tired
   I've written ten emails.                 → how many / result
```

> 🧠 **The test:** **"How long?"** → Continuous. **"How many / Is it finished?"** → Perfect.

### When BOTH are fine (long-duration verbs)
With *live, work, study, teach*, both forms work for duration with little difference:
- *I**'ve lived** here for ten years.* ✅
- *I**'ve been living** here for ten years.* ✅ *(slightly more temporary/emphasis on duration)*

### 🆚 vs. Present Continuous
| Present Continuous | Present Perfect Continuous |
|--------------------|----------------------------|
| now only | from past **until** now |
| *I**'m waiting**.* (right now) | *I**'ve been waiting** for an hour.* (started before, still now) |

## 12. 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani Translation Tips

1. **Present Perfect Continuous often = Azerbaijani "-dır + indiki zaman":**
   - *İki saatdır gözləyirəm* → *I**'ve been waiting** for two hours.*
   - *Səhərdən bəri işləyir* → *He**'s been working** since morning.*

2. **⚠️ The trap:** Azerbaijani uses the **present tense** with a duration word
   (*"...dır ...-ır"*). English **cannot** — it must use **have been + -ing**.
   - ❌ *I work here for two years.* → ✅ *I**'ve been working** here for two years.*

3. **State verbs:** Azerbaijani *"5 ildir onu tanıyıram"* → English uses the **Perfect**,
   not continuous: *I**'ve known** him for 5 years.*

4. **"How long?"** = *"Nə qədərdir?"* — the classic trigger.

## 13. 🔊 Pronunciation Notes

### Contractions & the weak "been"
| Full | Spoken |
|------|--------|
| I have been | **I've been** /aɪv bɪn/ |
| She has been | **She's been** /ʃiːz bɪn/ |

| have not been | **haven't been** /ˈhævnt bɪn/ |

## 28. ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Perfect or Perfect Continuous?**
*How long / activity / why-you're-like-this* → Continuous. *Result / how many / finished*
→ Perfect.

**Q2: Why can't I say "I've been knowing"?**
*know* is a **state verb**. States don't show "activity in progress." Use *I've known.*

**Q3: for or since?**
*for* + length (*for 2 hours*); *since* + starting point (*since 9 AM*).

**Q4: Is the action finished or not?**
Usually **still going** — or **just stopped** with a **visible result** (*"I'm out of
breath — I've been running"*).

**Q5: "I've lived" or "I've been living"?**
With *live/work/study* both are fine for duration. The continuous slightly stresses
**duration/temporariness**.

# 📚 Mini Story — *"All Night Long"* (≈310 words)

> Notice the **Present Perfect Continuous** (how long / why someone is the way they are).
> Watch it pair with the **Present Perfect** (results) and **Present Continuous** (now).

It's six in the morning, and Elnur **has been studying** all night. His eyes are red, his
desk is a mess, and he **hasn't slept** at all. Empty coffee cups stand like little towers
around his laptop.

"You look terrible," his roommate Tural says, walking in. "**Have** you **been working**
the whole night?"

"Yeah," Elnur sighs. "I**'ve been preparing** for the final exam. I**'ve been reading**
the same chapter for three hours, and I still don't understand it."

Tural sits down. "You**'ve been pushing** yourself too hard. **How long** have you **been
revising** like this?"

"For two weeks," Elnur admits. "I**'ve been waking up** early and **going** to bed late.
My brain **has stopped** working."

Tural laughs gently. "No wonder you**'re** exhausted. You**'ve been burning the candle at
both ends**. Come on — let's get some air. You**'ve been sitting** in this room for hours."

They walk outside. The sun **is rising**, and birds **are singing**. Elnur breathes
deeply. "I**'ve been so stressed**," he says. "I**'ve been forgetting** to even eat."

"Well," Tural says, "you**'ve done** the hard part. You**'ve been studying** non-stop, and
that effort **will pay** off. But right now, you need rest."

Back inside, Elnur finally closes his books. He **has been fighting** sleep for hours, and
now he stops fighting. As he lies down, he realises something: he **has actually learned**
a lot. All that work **hasn't been** wasted.

"Maybe," he thinks, smiling, "I**'ve been worrying** for nothing."

Within a minute, he is fast asleep. He **has been waiting** for this rest all night — and
he has finally earned it.

# 🧠 Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

1. **"HAVE BEEN -ING = how long?"** ⏳ The whole tense answers one question: *How long?*

2. **The "evidence" trick** 🕵️: wet hair, red eyes, messy kitchen → *"You've BEEN doing
   something!"* Visible result of a recent activity → Perfect Continuous.

3. **"been never bends":** the word **been** is frozen — it never changes (only have/has does).

4. **DURATION = Continuous, DONE = Perfect.** *been reading* (still/duration) vs *read it* (done).

5. **State verbs are statues** 🗿 — they can't "be -ing." *know/like/be* → use the plain Perfect.

## E1. ✅ vs 🔄 Complete (Perfect) vs Incomplete (Perfect Continuous)

This is the **most important nuance** of the whole chapter. Very often:

- **Present Perfect** → the action is **finished**, focus on the **result**.
- **Present Perfect Continuous** → the action may be **unfinished** (still going), focus on
  the **activity / duration**.

```
   I've read your report.          → I finished it. (complete)
   I've been reading your report.  → I'm in the middle of it. (maybe not finished)

   She's written three emails.     → done: 3 emails exist.
   She's been writing emails.      → busy doing it; number not the point.

   They've painted the fence.      → the fence is fully painted now.
   They've been painting the fence.→ explains the mess / it may be half done.
```

| Question to ask | Answer → tense |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Is it **finished / a result**? | Present Perfect |
| Is it the **ongoing activity / duration / why-you're-like-this**? | Present Perfect Continuous |

> ⚠️ Don't over-apply this. With *live/work/study* + duration, **both** are fine and mean
> almost the same (§E3). The complete/incomplete contrast is strongest with **"product"
> verbs** (read, write, paint, build, eat, clean…).

---

## E2. 🕵️ "Result that REMAINS" vs "Evidence of activity"

Both tenses can describe a **present effect of a past action**, but the *flavour* differs:

| Present Perfect — a permanent/clear result | Present Perfect Continuous — fresh evidence of activity |
|--------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| *I**'ve cut** my finger.* (it's cut now) | *My hands are dirty — I**'ve been gardening**.* |
| *She**'s broken** the vase.* (it's broken) | *Why are you out of breath? You**'ve been running**.* |
| *We**'ve cleaned** the house.* (it's clean) | *Sorry for the mess — I**'ve been cleaning**.* |

> 🧠 Perfect = "**Look at the result.**" · Perfect Continuous = "**That's why I look/feel like
> this now.**"

---

## E3. ⚖️ Borderline Verbs — When BOTH Forms Work

With certain **long-duration verbs**, the two tenses are almost interchangeable for
duration. The continuous slightly stresses **duration / temporariness / effort**.

| Verb | Present Perfect | Present Perfect Continuous |
|------|-----------------|----------------------------|
| live | I**'ve lived** here for 10 years. | I**'ve been living** here for 10 years. |
| work | She**'s worked** there since 2015. | She**'s been working** there since 2015. |
| study | We**'ve studied** English for years. | We**'ve been studying** English for years. |
| teach | He**'s taught** maths for a decade. | He**'s been teaching** maths for a decade. |
| feel | I**'ve felt** ill all week. | I**'ve been feeling** ill all week. |
| wait | They**'ve waited** an hour. | They**'ve been waiting** an hour. |

> 💡 Slight tendency: **Perfect** = more neutral/permanent; **Perfect Continuous** = more
> temporary or emphasising the ongoing effort.

---

## E4. ❌ State Verbs Stay in the Perfect (never continuous)

Even when you mean **duration**, **state verbs** must use the **Present Perfect**:

```
   ✅ I've known her for years.        ❌ I've been knowing her...
   ✅ We've had this car since 2018.    ❌ We've been having this car...
   ✅ He's been ill all week.           ❌ He's been being ill...
   ✅ I've believed that for a long time.❌ I've been believing...
   ✅ They've owned the shop for ages.  ❌ They've been owning...
```

| Always Perfect (state) | Use Continuous (action) |
|------------------------|-------------------------|
| know, believe, understand | learn, study, read |
| have (own), own, belong | (have a shower / lunch = action → continuous OK) |
| like, love, hate, want | enjoy *(borderline)* |
| be, seem, exist | feel *(borderline — both OK)* |

> 🗿 **Statue rule:** state verbs are statues — they can't be "-ing." (From the memory tricks.)

---

## E5. 🔁 Repeated Actions Over a Period

The Present Perfect Continuous also covers **repeated actions** happening **again and again
up to now** (not one single non-stop action):

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| I**'ve been calling** her all day. | many calls, repeated, up to now |
| He**'s been going** to the gym a lot lately. | repeated visits recently |
| We**'ve been eating out** too much this month. | repeated, temporary trend |
| She**'s been taking** taxis to work. | repeated lately (not her permanent habit) |

> 🧠 Compare with **Present Simple** (permanent habit): *She **takes** the bus* (always)
> vs *She**'s been taking** taxis* (lately, temporarily).

---

## E6. 🌧️ Impersonal "It's been + -ing" (weather, time)

A common pattern with weather and elapsed time:

```
   It's been raining all day.
   It's been snowing since this morning.
   It's been ages since we last met.   (note: "since" + last event)
```

> 💡 *"It's been ages/years/a long time since…"* is a frequent, natural phrase. After
> *since* here, use **Past Simple** for the last event: *since we **last met**.*

---

## E7. 📐 "How long…?" Answer Frames

| Question | Natural answers |
|----------|-----------------|
| How long have you been waiting? | **For** an hour. / **Since** 9. / **All** morning. / For **the last/past** hour. |
| How long has it been raining? | Since dawn. / For hours. / All day. |

```
   for + period      → for two hours, for ages
   since + point     → since Monday, since I arrived
   for the last/past → for the past three days
   all + period      → all day, all week
```

## ⚖️ Special Set — Perfect Continuous vs. Perfect (the key skill!)

| Present Perfect Continuous (activity / how long) | Present Perfect (result / how many) |
|--------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| I**'ve been reading** that book. *(still on it)* | I**'ve read** that book. *(finished)* |
| She**'s been writing** emails all morning. | She**'s written** ten emails. |
| We**'ve been painting** the room. *(messy hands)* | We**'ve painted** the room. *(it's done)* |
| He**'s been running**. *(out of breath now)* | He**'s run** 10 km. *(achievement)* |
| They**'ve been cooking** all day. | They**'ve cooked** a huge feast. |
| I**'ve been learning** Spanish. *(ongoing)* | I**'ve learned** 200 words. *(count/result)* |

> 🧠 Cover the right column; say each left sentence; then turn it into a **result** (how
> many / finished) and watch it become Present Perfect.

# 📖 Mini Story #2 — *"The Reunion"* (≈320 words)

> A reunion is full of *"What have you been doing?"* and *"I've been…"* — perfect for the
> Present Perfect Continuous. Watch it pair with the **Present Perfect** (results) and a
> **Past Simple** memory. Read aloud.

After ten years, Leyla and Samir meet again at a small café. They **haven't seen** each
other since school. They **have been writing** messages for weeks, planning this day.

"You look great!" Leyla says. "What **have** you **been doing** all these years?"

Samir smiles. "A lot! I**'ve been living** in Germany. I**'ve been working** as an
engineer, and lately I**'ve been studying** for a master's degree. And you?"

"Honestly, I**'ve been chasing** a dream," Leyla laughs. "I**'ve been running** my own
bakery. I**'ve been getting up** at four every morning. I**'m** exhausted, but I**'ve never
been** happier."

"That's amazing! **Have** you **been baking** all this time?"

"Yes — and I**'ve learned** so much. I**'ve already opened** a second shop." She pauses.
"It **hasn't been** easy, though. I**'ve been struggling** with the costs."

Samir nods. "I understand. I**'ve been saving** for years, and it still feels like never
enough."

They both remember the day they **finished** school. *(Specific past time → Past Simple.)*
"Do you remember," Samir says, "how we **promised** to stay in touch?"

"And we didn't!" Leyla laughs. "But here we are."

"You**'ve been doing** incredible things," Samir says warmly. "I**'ve been telling**
everyone about my talented friend."

Outside, the rain **has been falling** softly, and the café **has been filling** up with
people. Leyla and Samir keep talking — about everything they**'ve been through**,
everything they**'ve been hoping** for.

"We**'ve been apart** for too long," Leyla says. "Let's not lose touch again."

"Agreed," Samir says. "I**'ve been waiting** ten years for this coffee. I**'m** not going
to waste it."

They laugh, and for the first time in a decade, it feels like no time **has passed** at all.

### 🔎 Story Notes — Spot the Grammar
- **Present Perfect Continuous (ongoing activities / how long):** *have been writing, 've been living, 've been working, 've been studying, 've been running, 've been getting up, 've been baking, 've been saving, 've been waiting…*
- **Present Perfect (completed results / experience):** *haven't seen, 've learned, 've already opened, hasn't been easy, 've been through, has passed.*
- **Past Simple (specific shared memory):** *the day they **finished** school · we **promised** · we **didn't**.*
- **The contrast that matters:** *I've been baking all this time* (activity/duration) vs *I've opened a second shop* (result/achievement). Continuous = the journey; Perfect = the milestone.
