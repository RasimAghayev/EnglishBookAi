# 🌉 Present Perfect — The Bridge Between Past and Now

### *Part 1 · Chapter 05 · have/has + V3*

> **Azərbaycanca qısa giriş:** Present Perfect (İndiki Bitmiş Zaman) **keçmişdə baş verən,
> amma indiyə bağlı olan** hərəkətləri bildirir. Azərbaycan dilindəki *"-mış/-miş"* və
> *"-ıb/-ib"* formalarına bənzəyir: *Mən Parisdə ol**muşam*** → *I **have been** to Paris.*
> Formul: **have/has + felin 3-cü forması (V3)**. Bu zaman azərbaycanlılar üçün ən çətinidir —
> ona görə də addım-addım, çox nümunə ilə öyrənəcəyik. 🌉

---

## 1. 🟢 Introduction

So far you know two **present** tenses (Simple, Continuous) and you've met the **past**
idea. The **Present Perfect** is special: it is a **bridge** 🌉. It talks about the
**past**, but it keeps **one foot in the present**. The result, the experience, or the
time period is **still connected to now**.

```
   Past Simple:      I lost my keys (yesterday).   → finished, gone, in the past
   Present Perfect:  I have lost my keys.           → past action, present result
                                                       (I still can't get in NOW)
```

The form is always **have/has + V3** (the third form of the verb — the *past
participle*): *have **eaten**, has **gone**, have **seen**, has **finished**.*

> 💡 **Big idea:** Present Perfect = **"past action + present importance."** *When* it
> happened is **not** the point. *The result now* is the point.

---

## 2. ❓ Why Do We Use This Tense?

Five main reasons:

| # | Use | Example | Azərbaycanca |
|---|-----|---------|--------------|
| 1 | **Life experience** (ever/never — time not stated) | I **have visited** Italy. | təcrübə |
| 2 | **Recent action with a result now** (just) | She **has broken** her leg. | indiki nəticə |
| 3 | **Unfinished time period** (today, this week) | I **have read** two books this week. | bitməmiş dövr |
| 4 | **Duration up to now** (for/since) | We **have lived** here for ten years. | indiyə qədər müddət |
| 5 | **News / recent completion** (just/already/yet) | The bus **has just left**. | yeni xəbər |

> 📌 The key question is: **"Is the past action still connected to NOW?"**
> If **yes** → Present Perfect. If it's **finished and dead in the past** → Past Simple.

---

## 3. 🌍 Real-Life Situations

- 🧳 **Talking about experiences:** *"Have you ever been abroad?"*
- 🤕 **Explaining a present state:** *"I can't drive — I've hurt my hand."*
- 📰 **Giving news:** *"They've just announced the results."*
- ⏳ **Saying how long:** *"I've known him since 2010."*
- ✅ **Reporting completion:** *"I've finished the report."* (it's done, ready now)
- 🔁 **Counting up to now:** *"She's called three times today."*

---

## 4. 📅 Timeline

The action happens **somewhere before now** (the exact time doesn't matter), and it
**connects to the present**.

```
   PAST                                  NOW                 FUTURE
 ────┼────────X?────────X?────────────────●─────────────────────┼────►
            (some       (some            │
             time)       time)           │
              └────── result / relevance reaches NOW ──────────┘

   "I have lost my keys."  → action: past (unknown when) · result: I'm locked out NOW
```

Compare with Past Simple (a closed dot in the past, **no line to now**):
```
   PAST                                  NOW
 ────┼────────X─────────────────────────┼────►
         "I lost my keys yesterday."  (finished, specific time, no link to now)
```

## 5. 🧮 Formula

Model verbs: **work** (regular → V3 = worked) and **see** (irregular → V3 = seen).
Helper: **have / has**.

### ➕ Positive
```
   S  +  have / has  +  V3
        (has → he/she/it)
```
| Subject | have/has | V3 | Example |
|---------|----------|----|---------|
| I / You / We / They | **have** ('ve) | V3 | I**'ve worked**. / They**'ve seen** it. |
| He / She / It | **has** ('s) | V3 | She**'s worked**. / He**'s seen** it. |

### ➖ Negative
```
   S  +  have / has  +  NOT  +  V3
        (haven't / hasn't)
```
- I **haven't** finished. · She **hasn't** arrived. · They **haven't** called.

### ❓ Question
```
   Have / Has  +  S  +  V3  ?
```
- **Have** you **finished**? · **Has** she **arrived**? · **Have** they **eaten**?

### ❓➖ Negative Question
```
   Haven't / Hasn't  +  S  +  V3  ?   (= surprise / checking)
```
- **Haven't** you **finished** yet? · **Hasn't** he **called** you?

### ✔️ Short Answers
| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Have you finished? | Yes, I **have**. | No, I **haven't**. |
| Has she arrived? | Yes, she **has**. | No, she **hasn't**. |

> ⚠️ The short answer uses **have/has**, never the main verb:
> ❌ *Yes, I finished.* → ✅ *Yes, I **have**.*

```
            └──────────┴─────┴──────┴─────────┘
```

## 7. 📜 Grammar Rules

1. **Use V3 (past participle), not V2.** ❌ *I have went.* → ✅ *I have **gone**.*
   (For regular verbs V2 = V3 = -ed; for irregular verbs they differ — see Ch. 23.)
2. **has** for he/she/it; **have** for everyone else.
3. **Never use a finished-time word** (yesterday, last year, in 2010, two days ago)
   with the Present Perfect. Those need **Past Simple**.
   ❌ *I have seen him yesterday.* → ✅ *I **saw** him yesterday.*
4. **You CAN use unfinished-time words** (today, this week, this year, ever, never,
   so far, recently, lately).
5. **for** + a length of time · **since** + a starting point (more in §11).
6. Adverbs *just, already, ever, never* go **between have/has and V3**;
   *yet* goes at the **end**.

---

## 8. ✏️ Spelling & Form Rules (the V3)

Regular verbs: **V3 = V2 = base + -ed** (same -ed spelling rules as Past Simple).

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs → **+ed** | work → worked, play → played |
| Ends in **-e** → **+d** | live → lived, close → closed |
| Consonant + **-y** → *ied* | study → studied, try → tried |
| Short CVC → double consonant | stop → stopped, plan → planned |

Irregular verbs: **you must memorize V3** (often different from V2):

| V1 | V2 (past) | V3 (participle) ✅ use this | Azərbaycanca |
|----|-----------|------------------------------|--------------|
| go | went | **gone / been** | getmək |
| see | saw | **seen** | görmək |
| do | did | **done** | etmək |
| eat | ate | **eaten** | yemək |
| write | wrote | **written** | yazmaq |
| take | took | **taken** | götürmək |
| be | was/were | **been** | olmaq |
| have | had | **had** | sahib olmaq |

> 📌 The **full irregular list (V1/V2/V3 + IPA)** is in **Chapter 23**. Learn them in
> small groups — you'll meet them again and again.

## 9. 🚩 Signal Words

```
 EXPERIENCE:   ever · never · before
 RECENT:       just · already · yet · recently · lately · so far
 UP TO NOW:    today · this week/month/year · this morning (if still morning)
 DURATION:     for · since · how long · all day · all my life
 FREQUENCY:    once / twice / three times · several times
```

| Signal | Position | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **just** | between have/has + V3 | I**'ve just** eaten. |
| **already** | between have/has + V3 | She**'s already** left. |
| **yet** | end (negatives & questions) | Have you finished **yet**? / Not **yet**. |
| **ever** | in questions, before V3 | Have you **ever** flown? |
| **never** | before V3 | I**'ve never** smoked. |
| **for / since** | before the time phrase | for **5 years** / since **2019** |

## 10. ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| I have seen him **yesterday**. | I **saw** him yesterday. | finished time → Past Simple |
| I have **went** there. | I have **gone/been** there. | use V3, not V2 |
| She **have** finished. | She **has** finished. | he/she/it → *has* |
| I have lived here **since** five years. | I have lived here **for** five years. | duration → *for* |
| Have you finished **already**? *(neutral Q)* | Have you finished **yet**? | questions/negatives → *yet* |
| I **am** finished my work. | I **have** finished my work. | perfect needs *have*, not *am* |
| He has **gone** to Paris (and he's back). | He has **been** to Paris. | been = went & returned |

> 📌 **Biggest Azerbaijani trap:** mixing Present Perfect with finished-time words.
> Remember: **no "yesterday / last week / in 2010" with Present Perfect.** 🧠

## 11. ⚖️ Comparison with Similar Tenses

### 🆚 Present Perfect vs. Past Simple (the most important comparison!)

| | **Present Perfect** | **Past Simple** |
|--|--------------------|-----------------|
| **Time** | unfinished / connected to now | finished, specific past time |
| **Time words** | ever, never, just, yet, for, since, today | yesterday, ago, last week, in 2010 |
| **Focus** | the **result / experience now** | **when** it happened |
| **Form** | have/has + V3 | V2 |
| **Example** | I**'ve lost** my keys. *(still lost now)* | I **lost** my keys yesterday. *(then)* |

```
   "I have visited Japan."          → experience (sometime in my life, relevant now)
   "I visited Japan in 2019."       → finished event at a known past time
   "Have you seen this film?"       → ever, in your life?
   "Did you see the film last night?" → that specific finished occasion
```

> 🧠 **The golden test:** Is there a **finished time word** (yesterday, ago, in 2020)?
> → use **Past Simple**. Otherwise, if it connects to now → **Present Perfect**.

### `for` vs `since`
```
   for  + a PERIOD (how long):   for two hours · for a week · for ages · for ten years
   since+ a STARTING POINT:      since Monday · since 2019 · since I was a child · since 9 AM
```
- I**'ve known** her **for** ten years. · I**'ve known** her **since** 2014.

### `been` vs `gone`
| been | gone |
|------|------|
| went **and came back** (experience) | went **and is still there** |
| *He **has been** to Paris.* (he's back now) | *He **has gone** to Paris.* (he's there now) |

> 🔎 Present Perfect vs **Present Perfect Continuous** is covered in **Chapter 06**.

## 12. 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani Translation Tips

1. **Present Perfect ≈ Azerbaijani "-mış/-miş" and "-ıb/-ib":**
   - *I **have eaten*** → *Mən yemiş**əm*** / *Mən ye**mişəm***
   - *She **has gone*** → *O get**ib*** / *O get**mişdir***

2. **⚠️ The biggest difficulty:** Azerbaijani often uses the **same past form** where
   English **splits** into Past Simple vs Present Perfect. So you can't translate
   word-for-word — you must check: **is there a finished-time word?**
   - *Dünən onu gördüm* → *I **saw** him yesterday* (Past Simple — "yesterday"!)
   - *Onu görmüşəm* (təcrübə) → *I **have seen** him* (experience, no time)

3. **"for/since" vs Azerbaijani:** Azerbaijani uses *"-dır"* / *"...-dən bəri"*:
   - *Beş ildir burada yaşayıram* → *I**'ve lived** here **for** five years.*
   - *2019-cu ildən bəri* → *since 2019.*

4. **"already / just / yet":**
   - artıq = already · indicə = just · hələ = yet (negativ/sual).

> 💡 **Strategy:** when you want to say something about the past, **first ask: "Am I
> saying WHEN, or am I saying the RESULT/EXPERIENCE?"** That decides the tense.

## 13. 🔊 Pronunciation Notes

### Contractions (essential — natives always use them)
| Full | Spoken |
|------|--------|
| I have | **I've** /aɪv/ |
| She has | **She's** /ʃiːz/ |
| We have | **We've** /wiːv/ |
| They have | **They've** /ðeɪv/ |
| have not | **haven't** /ˈhævənt/ |
| has not | **hasn't** /ˈhæzənt/ |

> ⚠️ *She's* can mean *she **is*** OR *she **has***! Context (the next word) tells you:
> *She's **happy*** (is) vs *She's **gone*** (has + V3).

### The weak "have"
- In questions, *have* becomes weak: *"Have you eaten?"* → /əv jə ˈiːtən/ ("'v ya eaten").
- *"I would have done"* → *"I'd've done"* (advanced, but you'll hear it).

> 🗣️ **Try it:** *"I've finished."* /aɪv ˈfɪnɪʃt/ · *"She's gone."* /ʃiːz ɡɒn/.

## 23. 🗣️ Speaking Tips

- ✅ Use contractions: *I've, she's, they've, haven't, hasn't.*
- ✅ For experiences, lead with *"Have you ever…?"* and answer with *"I've never…"*.

## 24. ✍️ Writing Tips

- Use it to introduce **recent developments** before giving details in Past Simple.
- For experiences and achievements (CVs, intros): *"I have worked with…", "I have led…"*

## 25. 🎧 Listening Tips

- The weak *have/'ve* is easy to miss: *"I've seen it"* sounds like *"I seen it"* fast
  — but the *'ve* is there.

- *"Haven't you…?"* signals a negative question (surprise).

## 27. 🌟 Native Speaker Usage

- **Giving fresh news:** *"Guess what — I've got a new job!"* (BrE *have got* = have).
- **Reacting:** *"Have you heard? They've broken up!"*
- **Casual experience:** *"I've never seen anything like it."*
- ⚠️ In **American English**, Past Simple often replaces Present Perfect with *just/

## 28. ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: When do I use Present Perfect instead of Past Simple?**
When the time is **unfinished or unstated** and the action **connects to now**
(experience, result, recent news). If there's a **finished time word**, use Past Simple.

**Q2: Why can't I say "I have seen him yesterday"?**
*yesterday* is a **finished time** → it forces **Past Simple**: *I saw him yesterday.*

**Q3: for or since?**
*for* + length (*for 3 years*); *since* + start point (*since 2020*).

**Q4: been or gone?**
*been* = went and **came back**. *gone* = went and is **still there**.

**Q5: Is "I've got" the Present Perfect?**
In form yes, but *have got* usually just means **have/own** (BrE): *"I've got a car"* =
*"I have a car."* It's a special idiom.

# 📚 Mini Story — *"The Letter"* (≈320 words)

> Notice the **Present Perfect** (results & experience) — and how it switches to **Past
> Simple** when a specific past time appears. Read aloud.

Murad **has waited** for this letter for weeks. This morning, it **has finally arrived**.
He **hasn't opened** it yet. His hands **are** shaking.

"**Have** you **read** it?" his sister Nigar asks.
"No, I **haven't**," he says. "I**'ve been** too nervous."

Murad **has applied** to a university in another city. He **has worked** hard for this
chance. He **has studied** every night, he **has given up** parties, and he **has
saved** every manat. Now everything depends on a single page.

He **has dreamed** about this moment a thousand times. He still remembers the day he
**decided** to apply. *(It was two years ago — a specific past time, so: Past Simple.)*
Back then, his teacher **said**, "You can do it." Those words **have stayed** with him
ever since.

Nigar **has noticed** how much her brother **has changed**. "You**'ve grown up** so much,"
she says softly. "Whatever the letter says, I**'m** proud of you."

Murad takes a deep breath. He **has never felt** so scared. Finally, he opens the
envelope. His eyes move across the words. For a second, he **doesn't** speak.

Then he smiles — a huge, shaking smile.

"I**'ve done** it," he whispers. "They**'ve accepted** me!"

Nigar screams with joy. She **has waited** for this almost as much as he has. "We **have
to** call Mum!" she shouts. "She**'s prayed** for this every single day!"

Outside, the morning **has turned** bright and warm. Murad looks at the letter again.
All the long nights, all the sacrifices — they **have finally paid** off.

"This **is** just the beginning," he thinks. He **has opened** one door. And now, for the
first time in months, he **has stopped** worrying — and **started** to hope.

# 🧠 Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

1. **"HAVE + V3 = a bridge to NOW."** 🌉 The action is in the past, the bridge reaches the present.

2. **"No time, no Past Simple":** if there's **no finished-time word**, you're probably in
   Present Perfect territory. Add *yesterday* and the bridge **breaks** → Past Simple.

3. **been = boomerang** 🪃 (went and came **back**); **gone = goodbye** 👋 (went and **stayed**).

4. **for = a Forrest (a stretch)**, **since = a Start point.** *for* a length, *since* a moment.

5. **The "ever/never sandwich":** *ever/never* sit **between** have/has and V3:
   *have **ever** seen · have **never** been.*

# 📦 EXPANSION PACK — Going Deeper into the Present Perfect

> **Azərbaycanca:** Bu hissədə Present Perfect-in daha incə istifadələri var: superlativ
> cümlələr ("ən yaxşı... gördüyüm"), *yet/already/still* dəqiq işlənməsi, gələcək üçün
> *when/after*-dan sonra Present Perfect, *have got*, və inkar + *for/since*. Diqqətlə oxu.

## E1. 🏆 Superlatives & "first time" patterns → Present Perfect

After a **superlative** (the best, the worst, the most…) or words like *the first / second
/ only time*, English uses the **Present Perfect**. This is a fixed, high-value pattern.

```
   It's the [superlative / first time] (that) S + have/has + V3 (...ever...)
```

| Pattern | Example | Azərbaycanca |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| the best … ever | This is **the best meal I've ever had**. | indiyə qədər yediyim ən yaxşı yemək |
| the worst … | That was **the worst film I've ever seen**. | gördüyüm ən pis film |
| the first time | It's **the first time I've flown** alone. | tək uçduğum ilk dəfədir |
| the only … | She's **the only person who has helped** me. | mənə kömək edən yeganə adam |
| the second time | This is **the second time he's been** late. | gecikdiyi ikinci dəfədir |

> 🧠 **Trick:** *"best/worst/first + ... + I've ever ..."* — lock this whole frame into memory.

---

## E2. ✅ *yet · already · still* — Precise Usage

These three confuse learners. Here's the clean rule:

| Word | Meaning | Used in | Position | Example |
|------|---------|---------|----------|---------|
| **already** | sooner than expected | ➕ positives (& surprised Qs) | mid (have **already** V3) | I've **already** done it. |
| **yet** | up to now (expected, not happened) | ➖ negatives & ❓ questions | end | Have you done it **yet**? / Not **yet**. |
| **still** | continuing / surprise it hasn't happened | ➖ negatives | before *haven't* | I **still** haven't done it. |

```
   POSITIVE:   I've already finished.       (done — earlier than expected)
   QUESTION:   Have you finished yet?        (is it done by now?)
   NEGATIVE:   I haven't finished yet.        (not done up to now)
   EMPHATIC:   I still haven't finished!      (annoyed it's not done)
```

> ⚠️ *still* goes **before** the negative auxiliary: *I **still haven't**…* (not *I haven't still*).

---

## E3. ⏳ Negative + *for / since* — "How long since…"

A very common real-life use: saying **how long since** something last happened, using a
**negative** Present Perfect.

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| I **haven't seen** her **for** months. | last time I saw her = months ago |
| We **haven't spoken since** the wedding. | no contact from the wedding until now |
| He **hasn't eaten** all day. | from morning until now, no food |
| It **hasn't rained for** weeks. | dry for weeks, up to now |

**Three ways to say the same idea:**
```
   I haven't seen Ali for two years.
 = The last time I saw Ali was two years ago.
 = It's (been) two years since I last saw Ali.
```

> 🇦🇿 *İki ildir Əlini görmürəm* = *I haven't seen Ali for two years.*

## E4. 🌉 Present Perfect for the FUTURE (after time words)

Like the Present Simple (Ch.3 E6), the **Present Perfect** can appear after *when / after
/ once / as soon as / until / by the time* to mean **"the action will be complete before
the next one."** We do **not** use *will* in that clause.

| ✅ Correct | ❌ Wrong |
|-----------|---------|
| I'll call you **when I've finished**. | …when I **will have** finished / when I will finish |
| **Once you've read** it, send it back. | Once you **will read** it… |
| Let's eat **after he's arrived**. | …after he **will arrive**. |
| **As soon as I've saved** enough, I'll travel. | As soon as I **will save**… |

```
   FUTURE main clause (will)   +   PRESENT PERFECT time clause
   I'll relax                  +   once I've handed in the report.
```

> 💡 This means: *finish action 1 → then action 2.* It previews the **Future Perfect** (Ch.15).

---

## E5. 🔁 Present Perfect vs. Present Perfect Continuous (preview)

You meet the **continuous** version fully in **Chapter 06**, but here's the core idea so
you don't confuse them:

| | **Present Perfect** | **Present Perfect Continuous** |
|--|--------------------|-------------------------------|
| Form | have/has + V3 | have/has **been** + V-ing |
| Focus | **result / how many** (completed) | **duration / how long** (activity) |
| Example | I**'ve written** three emails. | I**'ve been writing** emails all morning. |
| Feeling | finished, countable | ongoing, possibly unfinished |

```
   RESULT:   I've painted the room.        (it's done — look at it!)
   ACTIVITY: I've been painting all day.   (that's why I'm tired/messy)
```

> 🧠 **"How many?" → Perfect. "How long?" → Perfect Continuous.**
> ⚠️ State verbs (*know, have, be*) use the **Perfect**, not the continuous, even for
> duration: ✅ *I've known her for years* — ❌ *I've been knowing her.*

---

## E6. 🎒 *have got* (= have) and *have got to* (= must)

A common idiom, especially **British English**. Same form as Present Perfect, but the
meaning is **present**.

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **have/has got** | possess / own (= have) | I**'ve got** two sisters. (= I have two sisters.) |
| **have/has got to** | obligation (= must / have to) | I**'ve got to** go now. (= I have to go.) |

```
   I've got a car.        = I have a car.      (NOT an action in the past!)
   She's got to leave.    = She has to leave.
```

> ⚠️ Don't confuse *"I've got a car"* (own, now) with true Present Perfect
> *"I've bought a car"* (action with present result).

## E7. 🎚️ Adverb Placement — Full Guide

```
   S + have/has + [mid adverb] + V3 + ... + [end adverb]
```

| Adverb | Position | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| just, already, never, ever, always, recently | **mid** (after have/has, before V3) | She**'s just left**. / I**'ve never been**. |
| yet | **end** (neg/question) | Have you eaten **yet**? |
| still | **before** the negative aux | I **still haven't** eaten. |
| lately, recently, so far, today | **end** (also possible) | I haven't slept well **lately**. |
| for/since + time | **end** | We**'ve waited for an hour**. |

| ✅ Correct | ❌ Wrong |
|-----------|---------|
| I've **already** seen it. | I've seen it already... *(weak; mid is better)* |
| She's **never** lied. | She's lied never. |
| Have you finished **yet**? | Have you yet finished? |

## E8. 📅 "Unfinished time" — why *today / this week* allow Present Perfect

A time period that **includes now** is "unfinished," so it allows the Present Perfect.
The **same words** can take **Past Simple** once the period is **over**.

| Still ongoing → Present Perfect | Finished → Past Simple |
|---------------------------------|------------------------|
| I**'ve drunk** three coffees **today**. *(day not over)* | I **drank** three coffees **yesterday**. |
| She**'s called** twice **this morning**. *(still morning)* | She **called** twice **this morning**. *(said in the afternoon)* |
| We**'ve had** lots of rain **this year**. | We **had** lots of rain **last year**. |

> 🧠 Ask: **is the time period still open?** Open → Perfect. Closed → Past Simple.

## E9. 📝 Advanced Exercises (Expansion)

### I) Complete the superlative/"first time" pattern
1. This is the best book I ___ ever ___ (read).
2. It's the first time she ___ (be) on a plane.
3. That's the worst meal we ___ ever ___ (have).
4. He's the only friend who ___ (stand) by me.

### J) yet / already / still
5. I've ___ finished — that was quick!
6. Have they arrived ___?
7. It's noon and he ___ hasn't woken up!
8. — Is dinner ready? — Not ___.

### K) Negative + for/since (rewrite the idea)
9. The last time I saw him was in 2020. → I ___ (not / see) him ___ 2020.
10. It's been three hours since I ate. → I ___ (not / eat) ___ three hours.

### L) Present Perfect for the future (after when/after/once)
11. I'll text you when I ___ (arrive).
12. Once you ___ (finish), let me know.
13. We'll leave after the rain ___ (stop).

### M) have got / have got to (rewrite with have / must)
14. I've got a headache. → ___
15. She's got to study tonight. → ___

### N) Perfect or Perfect Continuous? (preview)
16. I ___ (read) this book three times. (how many)
17. I ___ (read) all afternoon. (how long / activity)
18. We ___ (know) each other for ten years. (state verb!)

### ✅ Expansion Answer Key

**I)** 1. have … read 2. has been 3. have … had 4. has stood

**J)** 5. already 6. yet 7. still 8. yet

**K)** 9. haven't seen … since 10. haven't eaten … for

**L)** 11. have arrived ('ve arrived) 12. have finished ('ve finished) 13. has stopped

**M)** 14. I **have** a headache. 15. She **has to / must** study tonight.

**N)** 16. have read 17. have been reading 18. have known *(state → Perfect, not continuous)*

## E10. ⚠️ Extra Common Mistakes (Azerbaijani-Specific)

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| It's the first time I am here. | It's the first time I **have been** here. | "first time" → Present Perfect |
| I haven't seen him since two years. | I haven't seen him **for** two years. | duration → *for* |
| I've still not finished / I haven't still finished. | I **still haven't** finished. | *still* before the negative aux |
| I'll call you when I will finish. | I'll call you when I **have finished**. | no *will* after *when* |
| I've got a car yesterday. | I **got** a car yesterday. / I**'ve got** a car. | *have got* = own (now), not past action |
| I've been knowing her for years. | I**'ve known** her for years. | state verb → Perfect, not continuous |

## ⚖️ Special Set — Present Perfect vs. Past Simple (the key skill!)

| Present Perfect (no time / connected to now) | Past Simple (finished time) |
|----------------------------------------------|------------------------------|
| I**'ve been** to Italy. *(experience)* | I **went** to Italy **in 2019**. |

# 📖 Mini Story #2 — *"The Empty Frame"* (≈330 words)

> A detective scene — perfect for the Present Perfect, because every **clue** is the
> **present result** of a **past action**. Watch the switch to **Past Simple** when a
> specific time is mentioned. Read aloud.

Detective Aliyeva steps into the gallery. The alarm **has stopped** ringing, but the
damage **is** done. On the wall, where a famous painting once hung, there **is** now an
empty frame.

"Someone **has stolen** the *Blue River*," the manager says, pale and shaking. "I**'ve
called** the police, I**'ve checked** the cameras, but the thief **has disappeared**."

Aliyeva looks around slowly. "Tell me what **has happened**," she says calmly.

"The guard **has gone** home sick. The back door **has been** unlocked since this
morning. And look — someone **has cut** the wires." The manager points. "They**'ve
thought** of everything."

The detective kneels by the window. "Interesting. The glass **hasn't broken**, so they
**haven't entered** from outside. This **is** an inside job." She examines the floor.
"They**'ve left** muddy footprints. And they**'ve dropped** something."

She lifts a small silver button. "**Has** any staff member **lost** a button recently?"

The manager hesitates. "Actually… the new assistant **arrived** late this morning. He
**said** his coat **was** torn." *(specific past — "this morning", "said", "was" → Past
Simple.)*

Aliyeva smiles. "So he **has been** here since eight, he **has had** access to the keys,
and now he **has vanished**. I think we**'ve found** our thief."

Within an hour, the police **have located** the assistant at the train station. He
**hasn't boarded** the train yet. In his bag, they find the *Blue River*, rolled up and
hidden.

"You**'ve made** one mistake," Aliyeva tells him. "You**'ve left** a button behind."

The painting **has returned** safely to its frame. The gallery **has reopened**. And
Detective Aliyeva? She **has solved** another case — and she **hasn't even finished** her
morning coffee.

Əliyeva yavaşca ətrafa baxır. "Mənə nə baş verdiyini danış," — deyə sakitcə deyir.

Detektiv pəncərənin yanında diz çökür. "Maraqlıdır. Şüşə sınmayıb, deməli, çöldən girməyiblər.
Bu, içəridən təşkil olunan işdir." O, döşəməni araşdırır. "Palçıqlı ayaq izləri qoyublar. Və

O, kiçik gümüşü bir düymə götürür. "İşçilərdən kimsə bu yaxınlarda düymə itiribmi?"

*Mavi Çay*ı bükülmüş və gizlədilmiş halda tapırlar.

"Bir səhv etmisən," — deyə Əliyeva ona deyir. "Bir düymə qoyub getmisən."

Rəsm təhlükəsiz şəkildə çərçivəsinə qayıdıb. Qalereya yenidən açılıb. Bəs Detektiv Əliyeva?
O, daha bir işi həll edib — və hələ səhər qəhvəsini də bitirməyib.

### 🔎 Story Notes — Spot the Tense Switch
- **Present Perfect (clue = present result of past action):** *has stopped, has stolen, have called, has disappeared, has cut, have left, have dropped, hasn't broken, has solved…*
- **Past Simple (specific past time → "this morning"):** *arrived, said, was (torn)* — the witness reports a **finished** moment.
- **The lesson in action:** detectives describe **results you can see now** (Present Perfect), but **witness statements about a known time** flip to **Past Simple**. That switch is exactly what Chapter 05 teaches.
