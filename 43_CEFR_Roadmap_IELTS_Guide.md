## 🧭 THE BIG PICTURE — level = grammatical range × accuracy

```
   A1 ── A2 ── B1 ── B2 ── C1 ── C2
   │      │     │     │     │     │
   simple ──────────────────────► complex, flexible, accurate
   "I live here"   "I've lived here"   "Had I known, I'd be here now"

   YOUR LEVEL = how WIDE a range you control  ×  how ACCURATELY you use it
```

> 🧠 **The key mental move:** climbing levels isn't learning "harder words" — it's controlling a **wider range of structures** (more tenses, conditionals, clauses) **accurately and flexibly.** The roadmap tells you the **next structure** to add; this book's chapters teach it.
> 🇦🇿 *Əsas fikir: səviyyə qalxmaq "çətin sözlər" yox — **daha geniş quruluş spektrini** dəqiq və çevik idarə etməkdir. Xəritə növbəti quruluşu deyir; fəsillər onu öyrədir.*

## 🎯 IELTS GRAMMAR GUIDE — "Grammatical Range & Accuracy" (GRA)

One of the **four scored criteria** in IELTS Writing & Speaking (25% of your score). It measures **two things at once**:

```
   RANGE     = how many different structures you use (tenses, conditionals, clauses, passives…)
   ACCURACY  = how few errors you make (and how much they disrupt meaning)
```

### Band descriptors (GRA)
| Band | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| **9** | full range, full flexibility; errors as rare as a native's |
| **8** | wide range; **majority of sentences error-free**; occasional slips |
| **7** | **variety of complex structures**; frequent error-free sentences; good control |
| **6** | mix of simple & complex; **some errors**, but meaning stays clear |
| **5** | **limited range**; frequent errors that can strain the reader; faulty punctuation |
| **4** | mostly simple sentences; errors are frequent and cause difficulty |

### What raises your band (the levers)
| Lever | Do this | Chapter |
|---|---|---|
| **complex sentences** | subordinate clauses (although, which, if…) | 32, 33 |
| **range of tenses** | mix perfect, continuous, conditionals | 36, 38 |
| **conditionals** | use 1st, 2nd, 3rd (and mixed at C1) | 24 |
| **relative clauses** | defining + non-defining | 32 |
| **passive** | where natural (esp. Task 1 reports) | 26 |
| **accuracy first** | eliminate basic errors (articles, agreement, prepositions) | 39 |
| **punctuation** | commas, no comma splices | 33 |

> 🔑 **The Band-7 secret:** it's not enough to be **accurate** — you need **range** too. A perfectly correct essay of only simple sentences caps around Band 6. Add complex structures **naturally** (not crammed) to break into 7+. But **range without accuracy** also fails — balance both. 🇦🇿 *Band-7 sirri: təkcə dəqiqlik yox, **rəngarənglik** də lazımdır — amma təbii şəkildə. İkisini tarazla.*

> ⚠️ **For 🇦🇿 speakers, accuracy is often the ceiling.** The interference errors (articles, since/for, prepositions, he/she) from **Ch.39** are what most often keep AZ candidates at Band 6 instead of 7. Fix those families first. 🇦🇿 *🇦🇿 danışanlar üçün adətən **dəqiqlik** tavandır — Ch.39 interferensiya səhvləri çox vaxt 6-da saxlayır. Əvvəlcə onları düzəlt.*

## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE / EXAMINER INSIGHT

1. **Examiners reward range you use *naturally*.** A crammed *"Never had I…"* that doesn't fit loses more than it gains. Deploy complexity where it belongs. 🇦🇿 *Ekspertlər **təbii** işlədilən rəngarəngliyi qiymətləndirir.*
2. **Basic errors cap your band hard.** One *"I am agree"* or *"since three years"* signals a lower level instantly — accuracy in the basics matters more than fancy structures. 🇦🇿 *Sadə səhvlər balı bərk aşağı salır.*
3. **Task 1 loves the passive & comparatives; Task 2 loves conditionals & complex sentences.** Match your range to the task. 🇦🇿 *Task 1 — passiv/müqayisə; Task 2 — şərtlər/mürəkkəb cümlələr.*
4. **Speaking rewards fluency + range together.** Self-correct lightly; keep talking; slip in a conditional or a relative clause naturally. 🇦🇿 *Danışıqda səlislik + rəngarənglik birlikdə.*
5. **Punctuation is graded.** Comma splices and missing commas after fronted clauses cost accuracy marks in Writing. 🇦🇿 *Durğu işarələri də qiymətləndirilir.*
6. **You can't fake C1.** Levels are cumulative — you must **own** B2 before C1 sticks. Follow the roadmap in order. 🇦🇿 *C1-i saxtalaşdıra bilməzsən — səviyyələr kumulyativdir.*

# 📖 MINI STORY #1 — "The Plateau"

### *A learner stuck at "intermediate forever" finds the map — and a way forward.*
### 🇦🇿 *"Əbədi orta səviyyədə" ilişib qalan öyrənən xəritəni tapır — və irəli yol.*

---

For two years, Aysel had felt **stuck**. "My English isn't getting better," she told her tutor. "I've been B1 forever."

"Let's find out exactly where you are," the tutor said, and handed her the **Can-Do checklist**.

Aysel ticked confidently down the A2 and B1 rows: past simple ✅, present perfect ✅, first and second conditionals ✅, *should* and *must* ✅.

Then she reached B2. "Past perfect… I sort of know it. Third conditional… I always mix it up. Passive in every tense… no. Reported speech… not really."

"There's your answer," the tutor smiled. "You're not stuck — you've **completed B1**. You just haven't started **B2**. You've been re-reading things you already know."

"So what do I do?"

"You stop reviewing A2 and B1. You go straight to the **B2 chapters**: past perfect (Ch.10), the third conditional (Ch.24), the passive (Ch.26), reported speech (Ch.25). One at a time."

Aysel frowned. "But I want a higher IELTS band."

"Then this **is** your path. Look —" the tutor pointed at the map. "Band 6 is B2. Band 7 is C1. You're aiming for 7, but you're studying like an A2 student. You need to **climb**, not circle."

For three months, Aysel followed the roadmap **in order**. She stopped feeling "stuck" the moment she had a **direction**. Past perfect clicked. The third conditional stopped scaring her. She started using the passive in her essays — naturally.

At her next mock IELTS, her Grammar score jumped from 6 to 7.

"You were never stuck," the tutor said. "You just didn't have a **map**."

Aysel laughed. "Now I know exactly which box to tick next."

## 🅴1 — What "level" really means (three dimensions)

```
   LEVEL = RANGE  ×  ACCURACY  ×  FLUENCY
           (how many        (how few         (how fast, without
            structures)      errors)          freezing)
```

> 🔑 You can be **accurate but narrow** (correct simple sentences → Band 6), or **wide but inaccurate** (ambitious but error-filled → also capped). Real level needs **all three**. 🇦🇿 *Səviyyə = rəngarənglik × dəqiqlik × səlislik — üçü də lazımdır.*

## 🅴3 — IELTS "Grammatical Range & Accuracy" (deep)

| Band | Range | Accuracy |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | mostly simple sentences | frequent errors; strain the reader |
| 6 | some complex sentences | errors present, meaning clear |
| 7 | **variety** of complex forms | **frequent error-free** sentences |
| 8 | **wide** range | **majority error-free** |
| 9 | full, flexible | native-like |

> 🔑 The single biggest lever from 6→7 is producing **error-free complex sentences** — not just attempting them, but getting them **right**. 🇦🇿 *6→7 üçün ən böyük amil: **səhvsiz mürəkkəb cümlələr**.*

## 🅴7 — The band-6 → band-7 JUMP (accuracy × range)

```
   Band 6:  "Although technology is useful, but it can be bad."   ✗ (although…but; simple ideas)
   Band 7:  "Although technology offers clear benefits, it may also erode our concentration."
            (correct 'although', complex, hedged 'may', precise vocabulary)
```

| Fix | From | To |
|---|---|---|
| double linker | although…but | although…, … |
| flat vocab | bad | detrimental / harmful |
| over-claim | it destroys | it may erode |
| simple only | + a relative/conditional | complex structures |

> 🇦🇿 *6→7: ikiqat bağlayıcını sil, lüğəti dəqiqləşdir, ehtiyatlı ol, mürəkkəb quruluş əlavə et.*

| Your likely ceiling | Because | Fastest fix |
|---|---|---|

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani contrast in one line:** for most AZ learners, the map to a higher band is **not** more vocabulary — it's **(1) killing the predictable interference errors** (articles, since/for, prepositions, he/she, double negatives — all in Ch.39) to lift **accuracy**, and **(2) deliberately adding complex structures** (conditionals, relative clauses, inversion — Ch.24, 32, 34) to lift **range**. Do both, follow the roadmap in order, and the CEFR ladder — and the IELTS band — moves up together.

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "Band 7 by June"

### *A candidate reverse-engineers the IELTS band descriptors into a concrete plan.*
### 🇦🇿 *Bir namizəd IELTS bal təsvirlərini konkret plana çevirir.*

> Read it twice. **First** for the story; **second** noting how she turned a band descriptor into action.
> 🇦🇿 *İki dəfə oxu: əvvəl hekayə üçün, sonra bal təsvirini necə fəaliyyətə çevirdiyini qeyd edərək.*

---

Nigar needed **Band 7** for her visa. She'd scored **6.0** three times. "I keep getting 6," she said. "What am I doing wrong?"

Her tutor pulled out the **Grammatical Range & Accuracy** descriptors. "Read Band 6 and Band 7. What's the difference?"

Nigar read aloud. "Band 6: *'a mix of simple and complex sentences… some errors.'* Band 7: *'a variety of complex structures… frequent error-free sentences.'*"

"So?"

"So I need… **more complex structures**, and **fewer errors**."

"Exactly. Let's check which you're missing." They looked at her last essay. It was full of correct **simple** sentences — but almost no conditionals, no relative clauses, no passives. **Range** was her gap.

"And your errors?" The tutor circled three: *"good in writing," "since two years," "childrens."* "Articles, prepositions, plurals — the classic 🇦🇿 slips (Ch.39). **Accuracy** is your other gap."

They made a plan:
- **Range:** every Task 2 essay must include **one conditional, one relative clause, and one passive** — used *naturally*.
- **Accuracy:** kill the three interference families — a checklist before submitting.

For six weeks, Nigar wrote one essay every two days, then **self-corrected** with Ch.39 and **added complexity** with Ch.24, 32, 26. Her sentences grew from *"Technology is bad"* to *"Although technology offers clear benefits, it may, if left unchecked, erode the skills it was meant to enhance."*

At her next test: **Grammar 7.0. Overall 7.**

"You didn't get luckier," her tutor said. "You **reverse-engineered the descriptor**. You saw exactly what Band 7 wanted — range plus accuracy — and you built it."

Nigar smiled. "Band 6 was a map after all. It told me precisely what I was missing."
