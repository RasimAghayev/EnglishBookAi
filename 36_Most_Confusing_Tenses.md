## 🧭 THE BIG PICTURE — why tenses get confused

Every tense choice answers **three questions** at once. Confusion happens when two tenses share two answers and differ on only one:

```
   ① TIME:    past   ·  present  ·  future        (when?)
   ② ASPECT:  simple ·  continuous ·  perfect ·  perfect continuous   (how viewed?)
   ③ DISTANCE (reality): real  ·  unreal/hypothetical                 (Ch.24, 29)
```

> 🧠 **The key mental move:** when you're stuck between two tenses, don't re-learn both — **ask which single dimension they differ on** (finished vs relevant? completed vs in-progress? real vs unreal?). The test for each duel below isolates exactly that dimension.
> 🇦🇿 *Əsas fikir: iki zaman arasında qalanda, onların **yalnız bir ölçüdə** fərqləndiyini soruş (bitib vs aktual? tamamlanıb vs davam edir? real vs qeyri-real?).*

# 🟢 PRESENT DUELS

## ⚔️ Duel 1 — Present Simple ↔ Present Continuous
```
   SIMPLE      →  permanent / habit / general truth        "I work in Baku."  "Water boils at 100°."
   CONTINUOUS  →  now / temporary / around now             "I'm working from home this week."
```
| Present Simple | Present Continuous |
|---|---|
| She **works** at a bank. *(job)* | She'**s working** late tonight. *(temporary)* |
| I **drink** coffee. *(habit)* | I'**m drinking** tea for a change. *(now)* |
> 🧪 **Test:** *always/usually*? → **simple.** *now/at the moment/these days*? → **continuous.** 🇦🇿 *daimi/vərdiş → simple; indi/müvəqqəti → continuous.*

## ⚔️ Duel 2 — Stative ↔ Dynamic verbs
```
   STATIVE (states: know, like, want, believe, own, seem)  →  usually SIMPLE, not continuous
   DYNAMIC (actions)  →  can be continuous
```
| ✅ | ❌ / different meaning |
|---|---|
| I **know** the answer. | ~~I am knowing~~ |
| I **love** this song. | ~~I am loving it~~ *(informal ad-speak only)* |
| I **think** it's true. *(opinion)* | I'**m thinking** about it. *(mental activity — different meaning)* |
> 🧪 **Test:** is it a **state** (thought, emotion, possession, sense)? → **simple.** Some verbs switch (*have, think, see*) with a change of meaning. 🇦🇿 *Vəziyyət felləri (know/like/want) davametmə almır.*

## ⚔️ Duel 3 — Present Simple ↔ Present Perfect (states + for/since)
```
   PRESENT SIMPLE   →  a fact right now                    "I live in Baku."
   PRESENT PERFECT  →  from the past up to now (for/since)  "I've lived in Baku for ten years."
```
> 🧪 **Test:** *for/since* + "still true"? → **present perfect** (not present simple): ~~I live here since 2010~~ → *I've lived here since 2010.* 🇦🇿 *"for/since" + indiyə qədər → present perfect (present simple yox).*

# 🔵 PAST DUELS

## ⚔️ Duel 4 — Past Simple ↔ Present Perfect
```
   PAST SIMPLE      →  FINISHED time, "when" is known/implied     "I saw it yesterday."
   PRESENT PERFECT  →  connection to NOW, time unspecified        "I've seen it (already)."
```
| Past Simple | Present Perfect |
|---|---|
| I **visited** Paris in 2019. | I'**ve visited** Paris (at some point). |
| **Did** you **eat**? *(AmE also for recent)* | **Have** you **eaten** yet? |
> 🧪 **Test:** is there a **finished-time word** (*yesterday, in 2019, last week, ago*)? → **past simple.** Time **unstated** / relevant now? → **present perfect.** 🇦🇿 *Bitmiş vaxt sözü → past simple; vaxt deyilmirsə/indiyə aiddirsə → present perfect.*

## ⚔️ Duel 5 — Past Simple ↔ Past Continuous
```
   PAST CONTINUOUS  →  action IN PROGRESS (background / interrupted)   "I was cooking when…"
   PAST SIMPLE      →  the completed action / the interruption          "…the phone rang."
```
| Past Continuous (background) | Past Simple (event) |
|---|---|
| While I **was reading**, | the lights **went** out. |
| They **were sleeping** when | the alarm **rang**. |
> 🧪 **Test:** longer/background action? → **was/were + -ing.** Short/completed event? → **past simple.** 🇦🇿 *Uzun/fon hərəkət → past continuous; qısa/bitmiş → past simple.*

## ⚔️ Duel 6 — Past Simple ↔ Past Perfect
```
   PAST PERFECT  →  the EARLIER of two past events        "had done"
   PAST SIMPLE   →  the LATER event                        "did"
```
| Past Perfect (first) | Past Simple (second) |
|---|---|
| When I **arrived**, they **had** already **left**. | *(they left → then I arrived)* |
| She **had studied** French before she **moved** to Paris. | *(studied → then moved)* |
> 🧪 **Test:** two past events, need to show which came **first**? → **past perfect** for the earlier one. 🇦🇿 *İki keçmiş hadisə — daha əvvəlki üçün past perfect.*

## ⚔️ Duel 7 — used to ↔ would ↔ Past Simple (past habits)
```
   USED TO      →  past habits AND past states (no longer true)   "I used to have a dog."
   WOULD        →  past repeated ACTIONS only (not states)        "We would swim every day."
   PAST SIMPLE  →  a single finished action                        "I swam yesterday."
```
> 🧪 **Test:** past **state** (*have/be/know/like*)? → **used to** only (not *would*). Repeated **action**? → *used to* **or** *would*. One-off? → **past simple.** 🇦🇿 *Keçmiş vəziyyət → yalnız used to; təkrar hərəkət → used to/would; bir dəfəlik → past simple.*

# 🟣 PERFECT DUELS

## ⚔️ Duel 8 — Present Perfect ↔ Present Perfect Continuous
```
   PRESENT PERFECT (have done)          →  RESULT / completion / "how many"   "I've written three emails."
   PRES. PERF. CONTINUOUS (have been doing) →  DURATION / activity / "how long"  "I've been writing all morning."
```
| have done (result) | have been doing (activity) |
|---|---|
| I'**ve painted** the fence. *(it's done)* | I'**ve been painting** — that's why I'm messy. |
| She'**s read** three books. | She'**s been reading** all day. |
> 🧪 **Test:** *how many / finished*? → **have done.** *how long / still going / explains a state*? → **have been doing.** 🇦🇿 *nəticə/neçə → have done; müddət/necə uzun → have been doing.*

## ⚔️ Duel 9 — Past Perfect ↔ Past Perfect Continuous
```
   PAST PERFECT (had done)              →  completed before a past point    "By 6, he had left."
   PAST PERF. CONTINUOUS (had been doing) →  ongoing up to that past point   "He was tired; he'd been working."
```
> 🧪 **Test:** completed-before? → **had done.** Duration/activity leading up to a past moment? → **had been doing.** 🇦🇿 *Əvvəl tamamlanıb → had done; keçmiş ana qədər davam edib → had been doing.*

## ⚔️ Duel 10 — Present Perfect ↔ Past Perfect
```
   PRESENT PERFECT  →  up to NOW                       "I have finished." (now)
   PAST PERFECT     →  up to a PAST point               "I had finished (before you called)."
```
> 🧪 **Test:** connected to **now** → *have done*; connected to an **earlier past moment** → *had done*. 🇦🇿 *İndiyə qədər → have done; keçmiş bir ana qədər → had done.*

# 🟠 FUTURE DUELS

## ⚔️ Duel 11 — will ↔ going to
```
   WILL       →  decided NOW (spontaneous) / prediction / promise / offer   "I'll get it!"
   GOING TO   →  plan already MADE / prediction from evidence               "I'm going to start a course."
```
> 🧪 **Test:** deciding **as you speak**? → **will.** Already **planned** or **evidence right now**? → **going to** (*"Look — it's going to rain"*). 🇦🇿 *Anlıq qərar → will; hazır plan/dəlil → going to.*

## ⚔️ Duel 12 — going to ↔ Present Continuous (future)
```
   GOING TO            →  intention (maybe no fixed detail)     "I'm going to visit Rome."
   PRESENT CONTINUOUS  →  fixed ARRANGEMENT (time/place set)     "I'm flying to Rome on Friday."
```
> 🧪 **Test:** a **fixed arrangement** with a time/place (often with others)? → **present continuous.** Just an **intention**? → **going to.** 🇦🇿 *Sabit razılaşma → present continuous; niyyət → going to.*

## ⚔️ Duel 13 — will ↔ Present Continuous (future)
```
   WILL                →  prediction / spontaneous decision      "I think I'll stay in."
   PRESENT CONTINUOUS  →  arrangement (it's fixed)               "I'm staying in tonight (planned)."
```
> 🧪 **Test:** already **arranged**? → **present continuous.** Predicting/deciding now? → **will.** 🇦🇿 *Razılaşdırılıb → present continuous; proqnoz/qərar → will.*

## ⚔️ Duel 14 — Future Continuous ↔ Future Perfect
```
   FUTURE CONTINUOUS (will be doing)   →  IN PROGRESS at a future time    "At 8, I'll be eating."
   FUTURE PERFECT (will have done)     →  COMPLETED by a future time      "By 8, I'll have eaten."
```
> 🧪 **Test:** happening **during** a future moment? → **will be doing.** **Finished before** a future moment? → **will have done.** 🇦🇿 *Gələcək anda davam → will be doing; ona qədər tamamlanıb → will have done.*

# 🔴 CONDITIONAL & HABIT DUELS

## ⚔️ Duel 15 — Zero ↔ First Conditional
```
   ZERO   →  always true (fact/law):        If you heat ice, it MELTS.   (if + present, present)
   FIRST  →  a real, specific future:        If it RAINS, we'll STAY in. (if + present, will)
```
> 🧪 **Test:** a **general truth**? → zero (present, present). A **specific likely future**? → first (present, *will*). 🇦🇿 *Ümumi həqiqət → zero; konkret real gələcək → first.*

## ⚔️ Duel 16 — First ↔ Second Conditional
```
   FIRST   →  real / likely:      If I HAVE time, I'll CALL.        (present → will)
   SECOND  →  unreal / imaginary: If I HAD time, I'd CALL.          (past → would)
```
> 🧪 **Test:** do you **expect** it to happen? → **first.** **Hypothetical/unlikely/untrue now**? → **second.** 🇦🇿 *Gözlənilir → first; xəyali/qeyri-real → second.*

## ⚔️ Duel 17 — Second ↔ Third Conditional
```
   SECOND  →  unreal PRESENT/future:  If I KNEW, I'd TELL you.              (past → would)
   THIRD   →  unreal PAST:            If I HAD KNOWN, I'd HAVE TOLD you.    (past perfect → would have)
```
> 🧪 **Test:** imagining **now/future**? → second. Regretting/imagining the **past** (too late)? → third. 🇦🇿 *İndi/gələcək xəyalı → second; keçmiş (artıq gec) → third.*

## ⚔️ Duel 18 — used to ↔ be used to ↔ get used to
```
   USED TO + base verb     →  past habit (no longer)         "I used to smoke."
   BE USED TO + noun/-ing   →  accustomed (familiar now)      "I'm used to the noise / to working late."
   GET USED TO + noun/-ing  →  becoming accustomed (process)  "I'm getting used to it."
```
> 🧪 **Test:** *used **to** + base verb* = past habit; *be/get used **to** + -ing/noun* = (becoming) familiar. Mind the *-ing*! 🇦🇿 *used to + məsdər (keçmiş vərdiş) ≠ be/get used to + isim/-ing (öyrəşmək).*

## ⚔️ Duel 19 — wish + past ↔ wish + past perfect
```
   WISH + PAST SIMPLE   →  unreal PRESENT       "I wish I knew."       (I don't know now)
   WISH + PAST PERFECT  →  regret about PAST     "I wish I had known."  (I didn't know then)
```
> 🧪 **Test:** wishing about **now**? → *wish + past.* Regretting the **past**? → *wish + past perfect.* (Ch.29) 🇦🇿 *İndi haqqında → wish + past; keçmişə peşman → wish + past perfect.*

## ⛔ COMMON MISTAKES (the deadliest confusions)

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Duel |
|---|---|---|
| I **live** here since 2010. | I'**ve lived** here since 2010. | 3 |
| I **have seen** him yesterday. | I **saw** him yesterday. | 4 |
| When I arrived, they **left** (already). | When I arrived, they **had** already **left**. | 6 |
| I **used to** having a dog. | I **used to have** a dog. / I'm **used to having** one. | 18 |
| I **would** have a bike as a kid. | I **used to have** a bike. | 7 |
| If I **would have** money, I'd travel. | If I **had** money, I'd travel. | 16/17 |
| By 8 I **will eat**. | By 8 I **will have eaten**. | 14 |
| I'**m going to** meet him at 6 (fixed plan). | I'**m meeting** him at 6. | 12 |

## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT

1. **Natives feel "finished vs now", not rules.** *"I lost my keys"* (then) vs *"I've lost my keys"* (and can't find them now) — the choice is about **relevance**. 🇦🇿 *Seçim "bitib" vs "indi aktual" hissindədir.*
2. **"going to" often shortens to "gonna" in speech.** *"I'm gonna call him."* Fine spoken, not written. 🇦🇿 *Danışıqda "going to" → "gonna".*
3. **Perfect continuous explains the present.** *"I've been running"* (that's why I'm out of breath) — the activity explains a **current state**. 🇦🇿 *Perfect continuous indiki vəziyyəti izah edir.*
4. **AmE blurs did/have done.** Americans say *"Did you eat yet?"* where Brits say *"Have you eaten yet?"* Both fine in their variety. 🇦🇿 *AmE did/have done fərqini yumşaldır.*
5. **Conditionals aren't about time, they're about reality.** *"If I had…"* isn't past — it's **unreal now**. 🇦🇿 *Şərt cümlələri zaman yox, reallıq haqqındadır.*
6. **"used to" vs "be used to" catches everyone.** The first is a past habit; the second (+ -ing) is being accustomed. The little *to* fools learners for years. 🇦🇿 *"used to" (keçmiş vərdiş) ≠ "be used to + -ing" (öyrəşmiş).*

## 🅲 §C — ALTERNATIVE EXPRESSIONS (when a tense feels risky)

| Instead of risking… | Say it another way |
|---|---|
| present perfect vs past | add a clear time word: *"in 2019," "so far," "already"* |
| 2nd vs 3rd conditional | spell it out: *"if that were true now"* / *"if that had been true then"* |
| used to vs would | use *"in those days," "back then," "as a child"* to anchor the habit |
| future forms | use *"I plan to," "I intend to," "It's likely that"* |
| perfect continuous | use *"for the last hour," "all day"* to signal duration |

> 🇦🇿 *Zaman riskli görünəndə: aydın zaman sözü əlavə et və ya "I plan to / it's likely that" kimi ifadələrlə de.*

# 📖 MINI STORY #1 — "The Returning Traveller"

### *One short scene that quietly fires almost every duel — a homecoming after years abroad.*
### 🇦🇿 *Demək olar hər cütlüyü işə salan qısa səhnə — illər sonra vətənə dönüş.*

---

When Rustam **landed** in Baku, he realised the city **had changed** completely. *(past simple + past perfect — Duel 6.)*

"I **haven't been** here for twelve years," he told the taxi driver. *(present perfect + for — Duel 3/4.)* "I **left** in 2012." *(past simple, finished time — Duel 4.)*

"A lot **has happened** since then," the driver smiled. "Right now they'**re building** a new metro line." *(present perfect + present continuous — Duels 4 & 1.)*

Rustam **used to know** every street. *(past habit, state — Duel 7.)* As a boy, he **would ride** his bike along the boulevard for hours. *(past repeated action — Duel 7.)*

"**Are** you **staying** long?" the driver asked. *(present continuous, arrangement — Duel 13.)*

"I'**m going to** look for a flat," Rustam said. "And I think I'**ll** stay for good." *(going to = intention; will = decision — Duels 11–12.)*

"You'**ll get used to** the changes," the driver laughed. "By this time next year, you'**ll have forgotten** it was ever different." *(get used to — Duel 18; future perfect — Duel 14.)*

That night, unpacking, Rustam **had been travelling** for twenty hours and was exhausted. *(past perfect continuous — Duel 9.)* He looked out at the glittering skyline.

"If I **had come** back sooner," he thought, "I **would have seen** it grow." *(third conditional — Duel 17.)* Then he smiled. "But if I **stay** now, I'**ll watch** what comes next." *(first conditional — Duel 16.)*

*I wish I **had never left**,* he almost thought — then corrected himself. *No. I wish I **were** braver, that's all.* *(wish + past perfect / wish + past — Duel 19.)*

### 🔍 Spot the duel — which is which?

| From the story | Tenses | Duel |
|---|---|---|
| "**had changed**" (after "landed") | past perfect | 6 |

### ✍️ After-reading tasks
```
1. Find "had changed" and "left". Which happened first, and which tense shows that?
2. "used to know" vs "would ride" — why can't we say "would know"?
3. Change "If I had come back sooner, I would have seen it grow" into a SECOND conditional. How does the meaning shift?
4. "I haven't been here for twelve years." Why present perfect, not past simple?
5. 🇦🇿→EN: "Şəhər tamam dəyişmişdi." / "12 ildir burada olmamışam." / "Uşaqkən velosiped sürərdim."
```
> 💬 **Answers**
> 1. *"had changed"* happened **first** (before he landed); the **past perfect** ("had changed") marks the earlier event, the **past simple** ("landed") the later one — Duel 6.
> 2. *would* works only for repeated **actions** (*ride*), not for **states**; *know* is a state, so it needs *used to* — Duel 7.
> 3. Second conditional: *"If I **came** back sooner, I **would see** it grow."* — this now imagines the **present/future** (it's still possible) rather than regretting the **past** (too late) — Duels 16–17.
> 4. Because the twelve years run **up to now** (with *for*), and he's talking about the period **including the present** — that's the **present perfect**, not the finished-time past simple — Duels 3–4.
> 5. The city had changed completely. / I haven't been here for twelve years. / As a boy, I used to ride (would ride) a bike.

# 🧨 EXPANSION PACK — Most Confusing Tenses (E1–E12)
### *The deep layer: the diagnostic method, the trickiest duels dissected, mixed conditionals & self-diagnosis.*
### 🇦🇿 *Dərin qat: diaqnostik metod, ən çətin cütlüklərin təhlili, qarışıq şərtlər və özünü yoxlama.*

---

## 🅴1 — The 3-question DIAGNOSTIC (run it on any tense choice)

```
   ① TIME?     past / present / future        → picks the time zone
   ② ASPECT?   simple / continuous / perfect / perfect continuous
                  · finished or relevant?      (simple vs perfect)
                  · complete or in progress?   (simple vs continuous)
   ③ REALITY?  real / unreal (hypothetical)?   → conditionals & wishes
```

> 🔑 Almost every duel is decided by **one** of these three. Name the dimension, and the answer appears. 🇦🇿 *Hər cütlük bu üç sualdan **biri** ilə həll olunur — ölçünü adlandır, cavab özü gəlir.*

---

## 🅴2 — ⚠️ *did* vs *have done* (the #1 confusion)

| Choose *did* (past simple) when… | Choose *have done* (present perfect) when… |
|---|---|
| a finished-time word is present: *yesterday, last week, in 2019, ago, then* | time is unstated or *ever/never/just/already/yet/so far/recently* |
| you say **when** | you stress **result / experience / relevance now** |
| the period is over | the period includes now (*today, this week*) |

```
   "I SAW it yesterday."     (finished time → past simple)
   "I'VE SEEN it (already)."  (unstated / relevant now → present perfect)
```

> 🌍 AmE often uses the **past simple** with *just/already/yet* (*"I just saw it"*); BrE prefers the **present perfect**. Both fine in their variety. 🇦🇿 *Bitmiş vaxt sözü → did; vaxt deyilmir/aktualdır → have done. AmE just/already ilə did də işlədir.*

---

## 🅴3 — *had done*: when the PAST PERFECT is really needed

Use it to show one past event was **earlier** than another. But it's **optional** when *before/after* already show the order:

| Needed (order unclear) | Optional (order already clear) |
|---|---|
| When I arrived, they **had left**. *(had = earlier)* | After they **left** (had left), I arrived. |
| She realised she **had forgotten** her keys. | Before he **called** (had called), I slept. |

> 🔑 If *before/after/first/then* already sequence the events, the past simple is fine; the past perfect just makes the "earlier" explicit. 🇦🇿 *"before/after" sıranı göstərirsə, past perfect məcburi deyil.*

---

## 🅴4 — *have done* vs *have been doing* (result vs activity)

| have done (result / completion) | have been doing (activity / duration) |
|---|---|
| **how many / finished:** "I've read 3 books." | **how long / still going:** "I've been reading all day." |
| the outcome matters | the activity / its effect matters |
| "I've cleaned the kitchen." *(it's clean)* | "I've been cleaning." *(that's why I'm tired)* |

> ⚠️ **Stative verbs resist the continuous:** *"I've **known** her for years,"* not ~~I've been knowing~~. 🇦🇿 *Vəziyyət felləri (know/be/have) davametmə almır: "I've known her for years".*

---

## 🅴5 — The THREE FUTURES decision tree (will / going to / present continuous)

```
   Is it already ARRANGED (time + place, often with others)?
        └─ YES → PRESENT CONTINUOUS   "I'm meeting her at 6."
        └─ NO  → Is there a plan/intention or present evidence?
                    └─ YES → GOING TO         "I'm going to study law." / "It's going to rain."
                    └─ NO  → deciding now / predicting → WILL   "I'll get it." / "It'll be fine."
```

> 🇦🇿 *Sabit razılaşma → present continuous; niyyət/dəlil → going to; anlıq qərar/proqnoz → will.*

---

## 🅴6 — Future Continuous vs Future Perfect (*at 8* vs *by 8*)

```
   "At 8 o'clock, I'll BE EATING."     → in progress AT that time (future continuous)
   "By 8 o'clock, I'll HAVE EATEN."    → completed BEFORE that time (future perfect)
```

| Signal | Tense |
|---|---|
| **at / this time tomorrow / while** | future continuous (*will be doing*) |
| **by / by the time / before** | future perfect (*will have done*) |

> 🇦🇿 *"at 8 / bu vaxt" → will be doing; "by 8 / -a qədər" → will have done.*

---

## 🅴7 — The FOUR-WAY habit/familiarity sort

| Structure | Form | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **used to** | + base verb | past habit/state (no longer) — "I used to smoke." |
| **would** | + base verb | past repeated **action** only — "We'd swim daily." |
| **be used to** | + noun/-ing | accustomed now — "I'm used to the cold." |
| **get used to** | + noun/-ing | becoming accustomed — "I'm getting used to it." |

> 🧪 *used **to** + base verb* (past) vs *be/get used **to** + -ing/noun* (familiar). The tiny *to* changes everything. 🇦🇿 *used to + məsdər (keçmiş) ≠ be/get used to + -ing/isim (öyrəşmək).*

---

## 🅴8 — The CONDITIONAL ladder (reality × time)

| Type | If-clause | Result | Reality / time |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Zero** | present | present | always true (fact) |
| **First** | present | will + verb | real, likely future |
| **Second** | past | would + verb | unreal present/future |
| **Third** | past perfect | would have + V3 | unreal past (too late) |

```
   If you heat ice, it MELTS.               (0 — law)
   If it RAINS, we'll STAY in.              (1 — likely)
   If I HAD money, I'd TRAVEL.              (2 — imaginary now)
   If I HAD HAD money, I'd HAVE TRAVELLED.  (3 — regret about the past)
```

> 🇦🇿 *0: həqiqət; 1: real gələcək; 2: indi/gələcək xəyalı; 3: keçmişə peşman. Tam izah Ch.24.*

---

## 🅴9 — STATIVE verbs (and the ones that switch)

| Always stative (simple) | Switches meaning in continuous |
|---|---|
| know, believe, understand, mean | **think:** opinion (*I think so*) ↔ activity (*I'm thinking*) |
| like, love, hate, want, need, prefer | **have:** possess (*I have a car*) ↔ experience (*I'm having lunch*) |
| own, belong, seem, cost, contain | **see:** perceive (*I see it*) ↔ meet (*I'm seeing her*) |
| — | **taste/smell:** quality (*it tastes good*) ↔ action (*I'm tasting it*) |

> 🇦🇿 *know/like/want davametmə almır; think/have/see/taste isə mənaya görə dəyişir.*

---

## 🅴10 — MIXED conditionals (crossing time zones)

| Type | If-clause | Result | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| past → present | past perfect | would + verb | If I **had studied** medicine, I'**d be** a doctor now. |
| present → past | past (state) | would have + V3 | If I **were** more careful, I **wouldn't have crashed**. |

> 🔑 Mix when the **condition** and the **result** sit in **different times**: a past cause with a present result, or a present trait with a past result. 🇦🇿 *Şərt və nəticə fərqli zamanda olanda qarışıq şərt: keçmiş səbəb → indiki nəticə, və ya indiki xüsusiyyət → keçmiş nəticə.*

## 🅴12 — 🇦🇿 CONTRASTIVE SYNTHESIS

| English distinction | 🇦🇿 | The gap |
|---|---|---|
| I **saw** / I **have seen** | gördüm / görmüşəm | AZ **-dı** vs **-mış/-ıb**, but overlap is common |
| I **am reading** / I **read** | oxuyuram / oxuyuram | AZ often **one form** for both |
| I **have been reading** | oxuyuram / oxumaqdayam | AZ rarely marks "duration aspect" separately |
| If I **had** money | pulum **olsaydı** | AZ conditional **-saydı/-səydi** |
| If I **had had** money | pulum **olsaydı** | AZ often the **same** form → 2nd/3rd merge |
| I **used to** go | ged**ərdim** | AZ **-ardı/-ərdi** (= would + used to) |

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani contrast in one line:** Azerbaijani makes **fewer aspect distinctions** than English. One Azerbaijani present (*oxuyuram*) covers English *read / am reading / have been reading*; the conditional/optative **-saydı** often covers both the **2nd** and **3rd** conditionals; and **-ardı/-ərdi** merges *would* and *used to*. So the AZ learner's job in this chapter is the **reverse of translation**: English forces you to **split one Azerbaijani form into several English tenses**, choosing by *finished-vs-relevant*, *complete-vs-in-progress*, and *real-vs-unreal*. That's exactly what the 19 tests are for.

| "We **hadn't seen** each other since school." | past perfect (before that meeting) (Duel 6/10) |
| "We **'ve been meaning** to catch up for ages." | present perfect continuous (ongoing intention) (Duel 8) |
| "Next week we'**re having** dinner together." | present continuous = arrangement (Duel 12) |
| "If I'**d known** he was in town, I'**d have called** sooner." | third conditional (Duel 17) |

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "Results Day"

### *Anxiety, memory and hope — a scene that fires the present, past, perfect, future and conditional duels together.*
### 🇦🇿 *Həyəcan, xatirə və ümid — indiki, keçmiş, perfect, gələcək və şərt cütlüklərini birlikdə işə salan səhnə.*

> Read it twice. **First** for the story; **second** naming the duel behind each bold verb.
> 🇦🇿 *İki dəfə oxu: əvvəl hekayə üçün, sonra hər qara felin arxasındakı cütlüyü adlandıraraq.*

---

Nurlan **was pacing** the kitchen when his phone **buzzed**. *(past continuous ↔ past simple — Duel 5.)* Results day.

"I **haven't opened** it yet," he told his sister. *(present perfect + yet — Duel 4.)* "I **'ve been staring** at the envelope for ten minutes." *(present perfect continuous — Duel 8.)*

"You **studied** so hard," she said gently. "Last year you **used to** panic before every test." *(past simple; past habit — Duels 4, 7.)*

"I know. If I **hadn't revised** this time, I **wouldn't have** a chance." *(third conditional — Duel 17.)*

He remembered how, by midnight most nights, he **had already covered** three chapters. *(past perfect — Duel 6.)* He **had been working** harder than ever before. *(past perfect continuous — Duel 9.)*

"Just open it," his sister urged. "Whatever it says, I'**m proud** of you."

"If I **pass**, I'**ll apply** to university," he said. "And if I **failed**… well, I'd retake it." *(first ↔ second conditional — Duels 16–17.)*

He tore it open. His eyes **scanned** the page.

"By this time next year," his sister grinned, "you'**ll be sitting** in a lecture hall." *(future continuous — Duel 14.)*

Nurlan looked up, stunned. "I **got** in. I actually **got** in." *(past simple — result now, Duel 4.)*

"See? I **told** you. I **'ve always known** you'd do it." *(present perfect — Duel 4/10.)*

That night he could barely sleep. *I wish Dad **were** here to see this,* he thought — then softly, *I wish he **had lived** to see it.* *(wish + past / wish + past perfect — Duel 19.)*

### ✍️ After-reading tasks
```
1. Find "was pacing" and "buzzed". Which is the background and which is the event? Which duel?
2. "I've been staring for ten minutes" vs "I've stared at it." Explain the difference (Duel 8).
3. Change "If I pass, I'll apply" into a SECOND conditional and explain the shift in likelihood.
4. Why "I wish Dad were here" but "I wish he had lived"? Which refers to now, which to the past?
5. 🇦🇿→EN: "Zərfə on dəqiqədir baxıram." / "Gecəyarıya qədər üç fəsli bitirmişdim." / "Qəbul olundum!"
```
> 💬 **Answers**
> 1. *"was pacing"* = the **background** (ongoing) action; *"buzzed"* = the **event** that interrupts it. Past continuous ↔ past simple — **Duel 5**.
> 2. *"I've been staring for ten minutes"* stresses the **duration/activity** (and why he's anxious); *"I've stared at it"* would stress a completed act/result. Result vs activity — **Duel 8**.
> 3. Second: *"If I **passed**, I'**d apply**."* This frames it as **less likely / more hypothetical** — as if he doubts he will — whereas the first conditional (*"If I pass…"*) treats passing as a **real, open** possibility.
> 4. *"I wish Dad **were** here"* = an **unreal present** (he isn't here now); *"I wish he **had lived**"* = a **past regret** (he died) — **Duel 19**.
> 5. I've been staring at the envelope for ten minutes. / By midnight, I had (already) finished three chapters. / I got in! (I've got in!)

## 🏁 End of Chapter 36

You now have a **decision toolkit** for the tenses learners mix up most. The trick was never to re-learn each tense — it was to ask **which single dimension** two look-alikes differ on: *finished vs relevant now* (did / have done), *completed vs in progress* (did / was doing), *which came first* (did / had done), *result vs duration* (have done / have been doing), *decided now vs planned* (will / going to), *in progress vs completed by then* (will be doing / will have done), *real vs unreal* (1st / 2nd / 3rd conditional), and *past habit vs familiarity* (used to / be used to). Keep the 19 one-line tests close, and most "tense mistakes" simply disappear. 🔀

```
 ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ 🟢 present:  simple = habit/permanent · continuous = now/temporary         │
 │ 🔵 past:     did = finished time · have done = relevant now · had done = earlier│
 │ 🟣 perfect:  have DONE = result · have BEEN doing = duration/activity       │
 │ 🟠 future:   will = decide now · going to = plan · pres.cont. = arrangement │
 │ 🟠 future²:  will be doing = in progress · will have done = done by then    │
 │ 🔴 cond.:    if + present→will (real) · if + past→would (unreal now) · 3rd = past│
 │ 🔴 habit:    used to + verb (past) ≠ be/get used to + -ing (familiar)       │
 └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
