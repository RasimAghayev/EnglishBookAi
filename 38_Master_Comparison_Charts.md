## 🧭 THE BIG PICTURE — 3 times × 4 aspects = 12 tenses

Every English tense is one **time** crossed with one **aspect**. That's the whole system:

```
              SIMPLE            CONTINUOUS          PERFECT             PERFECT CONTINUOUS
              (fact/complete)   (in progress)       (linked/finished)   (duration + link)
   PRESENT    work / works      am/is/are working   have/has worked     have/has been working
   PAST       worked            was/were working    had worked          had been working
   FUTURE     will work         will be working     will have worked    will have been working
```

> 🧠 **The key mental move:** don't memorise 12 unrelated boxes — memorise **3 times** and **4 aspects**, and every tense is just a crossing. Pick the **time** (when?), then the **aspect** (complete? in progress? linked? duration?). 🇦🇿 *12 ayrı qutu yox — **3 zaman** və **4 aspekt** öyrən; hər zaman onların kəsişməsidir.*

## 📊 CHART 1 — THE 12 TENSES side by side

| Tense | Form | Main use | Signal words | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Present Simple** | V1 / V-s | habits, facts, timetables | always, usually, every day, on Mondays | She **works** in Baku. |
| **Present Continuous** | am/is/are + V-ing | now, temporary, fixed future | now, at the moment, currently, tonight | She **is working** now. |
| **Present Perfect** | have/has + V3 | past act, present relevance; experience | just, already, yet, ever, never, since, for | She **has worked** here for years. |
| **Present Perfect Cont.** | have/has been + V-ing | duration up to now (often visible result) | for, since, all day, how long, lately | She **has been working** all day. |
| **Past Simple** | V2 | finished action, finished time | yesterday, ago, last, in 2020, then | She **worked** late yesterday. |
| **Past Continuous** | was/were + V-ing | in progress in the past; background | while, as, when, at 8 p.m. | She **was working** when I called. |
| **Past Perfect** | had + V3 | the earlier of two past events | by the time, before, after, already | She **had worked** there before. |
| **Past Perfect Cont.** | had been + V-ing | duration up to a past point | for/since (before a past moment) | She **had been working** for hours. |
| **Future Simple** | will + V1 | prediction, decision, promise, offer | tomorrow, soon, next, I think, probably | She **will work** tomorrow. |
| **Future Continuous** | will be + V-ing | in progress at a future time | at this time tomorrow, while, when | She **will be working** at nine. |
| **Future Perfect** | will have + V3 | completed by a future time | by, by the time, before, by then | She **will have worked** by five. |
| **Future Perfect Cont.** | will have been + V-ing | duration by a future time | by … for … | By June she **will have been working** here for a year. |

## ⏰ CHART 2 — THE TIMELINE (where each tense sits)

```
        PAST ◄───────────────────────── NOW ─────────────────────────► FUTURE

  ┌──────────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┐
  │                                   │                                   │
  Past Perfect        Past Simple    NOW      Present Simple     Future Simple
  "had worked"        "worked"        │        "works" (habit)   "will work"
  (before another ────►│              │        Present Cont.  ──►Future Cont.
   past point)         │              │        "is working"      "will be working"
                       │              │        (now)             (at a future time)
  Past Cont. ──────────┤              │                          │
  "was working"        │        Present Perfect                  Future Perfect
  (in progress then)   │        "has worked" ──── links past to NOW │  "will have worked"
                       │        Present Perfect Cont.               │  (done BY a future time)
  Past Perfect Cont.   │        "has been working" ── duration to NOW
  "had been working" ──┘
```

> 🔑 The **perfect** tenses are **arrows** connecting two points (an action reaching *up to* a later moment); the **continuous** tenses are **waves** (something in progress *around* a moment). 🇦🇿 *Perfect = iki nöqtəni birləşdirən ox; continuous = bir anın ətrafında davam edən dalğa.*

## 🔧 CHART 3 — ONE VERB through all 12 tenses (*to work*)

| Tense | ➕ Affirmative | ➖ Negative | ❓ Question |
|---|---|---|---|
| Present Simple | I work / she works | doesn't work | Do you work? |
| Present Continuous | I am working | isn't working | Are you working? |
| Present Perfect | I have worked | haven't worked | Have you worked? |
| Present Perfect Cont. | I have been working | haven't been working | Have you been working? |
| Past Simple | I worked | didn't work | Did you work? |
| Past Continuous | I was working | wasn't working | Were you working? |
| Past Perfect | I had worked | hadn't worked | Had you worked? |
| Past Perfect Cont. | I had been working | hadn't been working | Had you been working? |
| Future Simple | I will work | won't work | Will you work? |
| Future Continuous | I will be working | won't be working | Will you be working? |
| Future Perfect | I will have worked | won't have worked | Will you have worked? |
| Future Perfect Cont. | I will have been working | won't have been working | Will you have been working? |

## 🚦 CHART 4 — MODALS at a glance

| Function | Modal(s) | Example | Chapter |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ability** | can / could / be able to | I **can** swim. / I **could** swim at five. | 18 |
| **permission** | can / could / may | **May** I come in? | 18 |
| **possibility** | may / might / could | It **might** rain. | 19 |
| **deduction (sure)** | must / can't | She **must** be tired. / It **can't** be true. | 19 |
| **obligation** | must / have to / have got to | You **must** stop. / I **have to** go. | 20 |
| **no obligation** | needn't / don't have to | You **needn't** worry. | 20 |
| **prohibition** | mustn't / can't | You **mustn't** smoke. | 20 |
| **advice** | should / ought to / had better | You **should** rest. | 21 |
| **expectation** | should / ought to | They **should** be here by now. | 21 |

> 🔑 The certainty scale: **must** (sure) > **should** (~80%) > **may/might/could** (maybe). Modals take a **bare infinitive** and don't change for person. 🇦🇿 *Əminlik şkalası: must > should > may/might. Modallar məsdərsiz fel alır, şəxsə görə dəyişmir.*

## 🔀 CHART 5 — THE CONDITIONALS

| Type | If-clause | Main clause | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Zero** | if + present | present | always true (fact/law) | If you heat ice, it **melts**. |
| **First** | if + present | will + verb | real, likely future | If it **rains**, we'**ll stay** in. |
| **Second** | if + past | would + verb | unreal present/future | If I **had** time, I'**d help**. |
| **Third** | if + past perfect | would have + V3 | unreal past (regret) | If I'**d known**, I'**d have come**. |
| **Mixed** | if + past perfect | would + verb (now) | past cause → present result | If I'**d studied**, I'**d be** a doctor now. |

> 🔑 The ladder of **reality**: real (0/1) → unreal now (2) → unreal past (3). Never *"if I **would**…"* in the if-clause. 🇦🇿 *Reallıq nərdivanı: real (0/1) → indi qeyri-real (2) → keçmiş qeyri-real (3). If-cümləsində "would" YOX. (Tam: Ch.24.)*

## 🎯 THE ULTIMATE CHEAT SHEET — "which tense do I need?"

```
   ① WHEN is it?  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
        past ─────────┐         present ───────┐        future ────┤
                      ▼                          ▼                   ▼
   ② Is it linked to another point / relevant later?
        past→earlier past?  → PAST PERFECT (had done)
        past→now?           → PRESENT PERFECT (have done)
        future→by a time?   → FUTURE PERFECT (will have done)
        no link, just then? → SIMPLE (did / do / will do)
   ③ In progress around that time?
        yes → add CONTINUOUS (was doing / is doing / will be doing)
   ④ Real or unreal?
        unreal → CONDITIONAL / would / wish + past (Ch.24, 29)
```

| Question | If YES → |
|---|---|
| habit / general truth? | Present Simple |
| happening right now? | Present Continuous |
| finished, with a time word (*yesterday*)? | Past Simple |
| finished, relevant now / *just/already*? | Present Perfect |
| two past events — the earlier one? | Past Perfect |
| completed *by* a future time? | Future Perfect |
| imaginary / hypothetical? | Conditional (would) |

> 🇦🇿 *Sxem: ① nə vaxt? ② başqa nöqtəyə bağlıdır? (perfect) ③ davam edir? (continuous) ④ real, yoxsa qeyri-real? (şərt/would).*

## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT

1. **Natives use maybe 6 tenses 90% of the time.** Present simple/continuous, past simple, present perfect, will, and *going to* carry most speech. The rest add precision. 🇦🇿 *Danışığın 90%-i təxminən 6 zamanla gedir.*
2. **Perfect continuous is common but light.** *"I've been meaning to call," "How long have you been waiting?"* — frequent, but easy once you hear the rhythm. 🇦🇿 *Perfect continuous tez-tez, amma yüngül işlənir.*
3. **Charts are scaffolding.** You learn from the grid, but fluency means the choice becomes **automatic** — you *feel* "finished vs relevant." 🇦🇿 *Cədvəllər dayaqdır; səlislik seçimin **avtomatik** olmasıdır.*
4. **Signal words are training wheels.** Useful early, but real English hides or omits them; meaning and context decide. 🇦🇿 *Siqnal sözlər başlanğıc üçün; real dildə çox vaxt gizlənir.*
5. **The timeline beats translation.** Thinking on the **timeline** (does it reach *now*? is it *before* another point?) is faster than translating from Azerbaijani. 🇦🇿 *Tərcümədən yox, **zaman xəttindən** düşün — daha sürətlidir.*
6. **Modals + conditionals are where levels split.** B2→C1 is mostly mastering modality and unreal conditionals — the charts here are your revision core. 🇦🇿 *B2→C1 keçidi əsasən modallar və qeyri-real şərtlərdir.*

### *A single short biography that uses every one of the 12 tenses — spot them all.*
### 🇦🇿 *12 zamanın hər birini işlədən qısa tərcümeyi-hal — hamısını tap.*

---

Sona **is** a doctor. *(present simple — a fact.)* Right now she **is working** a night shift. *(present continuous — now.)*

She **has worked** at this hospital for twelve years. *(present perfect — up to now.)* Tonight she **has been working** since six, and she's exhausted. *(present perfect continuous — duration.)*

She still remembers her first shift. That night she **treated** forty patients. *(past simple — finished.)* While she **was stitching** a wound, the power failed. *(past continuous — background.)* By the time the lights came back, she **had calmed** everyone down, *(past perfect — earlier event)* because she **had been training** for exactly such emergencies. *(past perfect continuous — duration before a past point.)*

Her career isn't over. Next year she **will lead** the emergency department. *(future simple — prediction.)* This time next month, she **will be interviewing** new doctors. *(future continuous — in progress at a future time.)* By the end of the decade, she **will have saved** thousands of lives. *(future perfect — completed by a future time.)* And by her retirement, she **will have been practising** medicine for forty years. *(future perfect continuous — duration by a future point.)*

"If I **had chosen** any other path," she often says, "I **wouldn't be** this happy." *(mixed conditional — Ch.24.)*

## 🅴1 — The 3 × 4 GRID (memorise this shape)

```
                 SIMPLE          CONTINUOUS        PERFECT           PERFECT CONT.
   PRESENT       do / does       am/is/are + -ing  have/has + V3     have/has been + -ing
   PAST          did             was/were + -ing   had + V3          had been + -ing
   FUTURE        will + V1        will be + -ing    will have + V3    will have been + -ing
```

> 🔑 Read **down** for the aspect pattern (it repeats across all times); read **across** for the time. Learn the shape once and every tense is predictable. 🇦🇿 *Aşağı oxu — aspekt nümunəsi təkrarlanır; yana oxu — zaman.*

---

## 🅴2 — What each ASPECT actually adds

| Aspect | Adds the idea of… | Marker |
|---|---|---|
| **simple** | a complete fact / whole event | base verb |
| **continuous** | in progress, temporary, around a moment | be + **-ing** |
| **perfect** | linked to a later point; completed before it | have + **V3** |
| **perfect continuous** | duration + link (how long, up to a point) | have been + **-ing** |

> 🇦🇿 *simple = bütöv fakt; continuous = davam edən (-ing); perfect = başqa nöqtəyə bağlı (V3); perfect continuous = müddət + bağlılıq.*

---

## 🅴3 — NEGATIVES & QUESTIONS (formation rule)

| Aspect | Operator | Negative | Question |
|---|---|---|---|
| simple (pres/past) | do/does/did | don't/doesn't/didn't + V1 | Do/Does/Did + subj + V1? |
| continuous | be | isn't/wasn't + -ing | Is/Was + subj + -ing? |
| perfect | have | haven't/hadn't + V3 | Have/Had + subj + V3? |
| future/modal | will/modal | won't/can't… + V1 | Will/Can… + subj + V1? |

> 🔑 Only **simple present/past** borrow *do/does/did*; every other tense already has an auxiliary to invert. 🇦🇿 *Yalnız sadə zamanlar do/does/did götürür; qalanların öz köməkçisi var.*

## 🅴4 — PASSIVE across the tenses (Ch.26 recap)

| Tense | Active | Passive (be + V3) |
|---|---|---|
| present simple | they make it | it **is made** |

## 🅴11 — DECISION TREES (which past / which future)

```
   WHICH PAST?
   Finished time word (yesterday)? ── yes → PAST SIMPLE
        └─ no → relevant now?        ── yes → PRESENT PERFECT
                 └─ before another past? ── yes → PAST PERFECT
                 └─ in progress then?    ── yes → PAST CONTINUOUS

   WHICH FUTURE?
   Fixed arrangement? ── yes → PRESENT CONTINUOUS
        └─ plan/evidence? ── yes → GOING TO
             └─ deciding now / predicting? ── yes → WILL
                  └─ done BY a time? ── yes → FUTURE PERFECT
```

## 🅴12 — 🇦🇿 CONTRASTIVE SYNTHESIS (AZ form → English tenses)

| 🇦🇿 form | Example | Maps to English… |
|---|---|---|
| **-ir/-ır** (present) | gedir | present simple · present continuous · (present perfect cont.) |
| **-dı/-di** (past) | getdi | past simple · (present perfect) |
| **-mış/-ıb** (perfect) | gedib | present perfect · past perfect |
| **-mışdı** (pluperfect) | getmişdi | past perfect |
| **-əcək** (future) | gedəcək | future (will) · going to |
| **-ardı/-ərdi** | gedərdi | used to · would (habit) · would (conditional) |
| **-sa/-sə … -ardı** | getsəydi … | 2nd / 3rd conditional |

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani contrast in one line:** Azerbaijani uses **fewer, broader** verb forms; English **splits** each into several precise tenses. One Azerbaijani *gedir* can be English *goes / is going / has been going* depending on **aspect**; one *getdi* can be *went / has gone* depending on **relevance**; one *gedərdi* can be *used to go / would go / would go (if…)* depending on **reality**. This whole chapter's charts exist to help the AZ learner **choose the right English split** from one Azerbaijani form — by asking the cheat-sheet questions: *when? linked? in progress? real?*

### *Past, present and future in one scene — a class buries a capsule and imagines opening it.*

The children **were digging** a hole in the school garden when the teacher **arrived**. *(past continuous ↔ past simple.)*

Years later, when the capsule **was finally opened**, that note **made** a grown woman cry. *(past simple passive; past simple.)* She **had kept** her promise. *(past perfect.)*

> 💬 **Answers**
> 1. *will open* (future simple) → *will be reading* (future continuous, in progress at a future time) → *will have become* (future perfect, completed by then) → *will have been living* (future perfect continuous, duration by then). > 2. *"had brought"* = a **completed** action before the moment (result: the item was there); *"had been saving"* = the **duration/activity** leading up to it (for weeks). Past perfect vs past perfect continuous.
> 3. *"It **will be opened** (by someone)."*
> 4. *"If I forget…, I'll be sad"* = **first** conditional (real, possible). *"If you had the chance…, what would it be?"* = **second** conditional (unreal/hypothetical).
> 5. The children were digging a hole. — Past Continuous. / We have dreamed of it for twenty years. — Present Perfect. / In 2050 we will be grown up. — Future Simple.
