# ⏱️ Present Simple — The Tense of Facts & Habits

### *Part 1 · Chapter 03 · Your first complete tense*

> **Azərbaycanca qısa giriş:** Present Simple (Sadə İndiki Zaman) **hər gün təkrarlanan
> hərəkətləri, vərdişləri, faktları və ümumi həqiqətləri** bildirir. Azərbaycan dilində
> "-ır/-ir/-ur/-ür" şəkilçisinə bənzəyir: *Mən hər gün işlə**yirəm*** → *I **work** every day.*
> Bu, İngilis dilinin ən çox işlənən zamanıdır. Gəl başlayaq! 🚀

---

## 1. 🟢 Introduction

The **Present Simple** is the **first and most important** tense in English. We use
it to talk about things that are **generally true** or that **happen again and again**.

Look at these three sentences:

```
   I  work  in an office.        ← my general job (true in general)
   The sun  rises  in the east.  ← a fact of nature (always true)
   She  drinks  coffee every morning. ← a habit (repeats daily)
```

None of these is happening "right now, this second." They are **true in general**.
That is the soul of the Present Simple.

> 💡 **Big idea:** Present Simple is NOT really about "now." It is about
> **"always / usually / in general."**

---

## 2. ❓ Why Do We Use This Tense?

We use the Present Simple for **5 main reasons**:

| # | Use | Example | Azərbaycanca |
|---|-----|---------|--------------|
| 1 | **Habits & routines** (repeated actions) | I **brush** my teeth every day. | vərdişlər |
| 2 | **Facts & general truths** | Water **boils** at 100°C. | faktlar |
| 3 | **Permanent situations** | She **lives** in Baku. | daimi vəziyyət |
| 4 | **Timetables & schedules** (future meaning) | The train **leaves** at 9 AM. | cədvəl |
| 5 | **Feelings & opinions** (state verbs) | I **like** tea. / I **think** so. | hiss/fikir |

> 📌 If you can add the word **"usually"**, **"always"**, or **"every day"** to a
> sentence and it still makes sense → you probably need the **Present Simple**.

---

## 3. 🌍 Real-Life Situations

When would you actually *use* this tense in real life?

- 🗣️ **Introducing yourself:** *"I'm Ali. I **work** as a teacher and I **live** in Ganja."*
- ☕ **Talking about routine:** *"I **wake up** at 7, **drink** coffee, and **start** work at 9."*
- 🌦️ **Stating facts:** *"It **rains** a lot in winter here."*
- 🍔 **Ordering/preferences:** *"I **don't eat** meat."*
- 🚆 **Schedules:** *"The bus **comes** every 20 minutes."*
- 💻 **Explaining how things work:** *"This function **returns** a list."*

---

## 4. 📅 Timeline

The Present Simple repeats across time — it is not tied to one moment.

```
   PAST                       NOW                      FUTURE
 ────┼─────X─────X─────X───────┼───────X─────X─────X────┼────►
            ▲     ▲     ▲      │       ▲     ▲     ▲
            └─ repeated actions, again and again, in general ─┘

   "I drink coffee every morning."
   X = each morning's coffee (it happened, happens, and will happen)
```

> The action is like the **dots of a habit** spread across all time — past, present,
> and future together. Present Simple = the "**always pattern**." 🔁

## 5. 🧮 Formula

Use the verb **"work"** as our model. Watch the helper **do/does** for negatives & questions.

### ➕ Positive
```
   S  +  V1 (+ -s for he/she/it)
```
| Subject | Verb | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| I / You / We / They | **V1** | I **work**. / They **play**. |
| He / She / It | **V1 + s** | She **works**. / He **plays**. |

### ➖ Negative
```
   S  +  do / does  +  NOT  +  V1
        (does → he/she/it)        ← verb goes BACK to V1, no -s!
```
| Subject | Aux + not | Verb | Example |
|---------|-----------|------|---------|
| I / You / We / They | **do not (don't)** | V1 | I **don't work**. |
| He / She / It | **does not (doesn't)** | V1 | She **doesn't work**. |

> ⚠️ **Golden rule:** after **does/doesn't**, the verb loses its **-s**.
> ✅ *She **doesn't work*** — ❌ *She doesn't work**s***.

### ❓ Question
```
   Do / Does  +  S  +  V1  ?
```
| Aux | Subject | Verb | Example |
|-----|---------|------|---------|
| **Do** | I/you/we/they | V1 | **Do** you **work**? |
| **Does** | he/she/it | V1 | **Does** she **work**? |

### ❓➖ Negative Question
```
   Don't / Doesn't  +  S  +  V1  ?   (= surprise / checking)
```
- **Don't** you **like** coffee? *(I thought you did!)*
- **Doesn't** he **work** here? *(I expected him to.)*

### ✔️ Short Answers
```
   Yes, S + do/does.        No, S + don't/doesn't.
```
| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Do you work? | Yes, I **do**. | No, I **don't**. |
| Does she work? | Yes, she **does**. | No, she **doesn't**. |

> ⚠️ Never answer with the main verb: ❌ *Yes, I work.* (in short answer) → ✅ *Yes, I **do**.*

## 6. 🏗️ Sentence Structure

```
 POSITIVE   ┌─────┬────────────┬──────────────┐
            │  S  │   V1(+s)   │  rest...     │
            │ She │   reads    │  books.      │
            └─────┴────────────┴──────────────┘

 NEGATIVE   ┌─────┬──────────┬─────┬──────────┐
            │  S  │ do/does  │ not │   V1     │
            │ She │  does    │ not │  read    │
            └─────┴──────────┴─────┴──────────┘

 QUESTION   ┌──────────┬─────┬──────┬─────────┐
            │ Do/Does  │  S  │  V1  │  rest ? │
            │  Does    │ she │ read │ books?  │
            └──────────┴─────┴──────┴─────────┘
```

## 7. 📜 Grammar Rules

1. **Add -s/-es only for he / she / it.** (I/you/we/they use plain V1.)
2. **In negatives & questions, the -s moves to "does."** The main verb returns to V1.
   *She play**s*** → *She **does** play* → *She **doesn't** play.*
3. **"do/does" is the helper** for simple tenses (because there's no other helper).
4. **State verbs** (like, know, want, need, love…) normally use Present Simple, not -ing.
5. **Adverbs of frequency** (always, usually, often, sometimes, never) go **before the
   main verb** but **after "to be."**
   *I **always** drink tea.* / *She **is** always late.*

---

## 8. ✏️ Spelling Rules (adding -s / -es)

| Rule | Verbs | Result |
|------|-------|--------|
| Most verbs → **+s** | work, play, read | works, plays, reads |
| Ends in *-s, -ss, -sh, -ch, -x, -o* → **+es** | go, watch, fix, miss | go**es**, watch**es**, fix**es**, miss**es** |
| Consonant + **-y** → *y becomes ies* | study, fly, try | stud**ies**, fl**ies**, tr**ies** |
| Vowel + **-y** → just **+s** | play, buy, say | plays, buys, says |
| Irregular: **have → has** | have | **has** |

```
   study → studies   (consonant + y)
   play  → plays      (vowel + y)
   go    → goes       (+es)
   have  → has        (irregular!)
```

## 9. 🚩 Signal Words

These words are **clues** that you need the Present Simple:

```
 FREQUENCY:  always · usually · often · sometimes · rarely · seldom · never
 TIME:       every day/week/month/year · on Mondays · at weekends
 GENERAL:    in general · normally · generally · as a rule
```

| Signal word | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| **every day** | I check my email **every day**. |
| **usually** | She **usually** walks to work. |
| **always / never** | He **never** eats breakfast. |
| **on Sundays** | We **on Sundays** relax → We relax **on Sundays**. |

> 💡 See "every day," "usually," or "always"? → reach for the **Present Simple**.

## 10. ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| He **work** here. | He **works** here. | he/she/it needs **-s** |
| She **doesn't works**. | She **doesn't work**. | after *does*, drop the -s |
| **Do** she like it? | **Does** she like it? | he/she/it → **does** |
| I **am work** every day. | I **work** every day. | don't add "am" to Present Simple |
| He **don't** eat meat. | He **doesn't** eat meat. | he → *doesn't*, not *don't* |
| She **is having** a car. | She **has** a car. | *have* (own) = state verb, no -ing |
| Yes, I **like**. *(short answer)* | Yes, I **do**. | short answers use do/does |

> 📌 **The #1 Azerbaijani-learner mistake:** forgetting the **-s** on he/she/it.
> Train your ear: *"He, she, it → -s!"* 🧠

## 11. ⚖️ Comparison with Similar Tenses

### Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

| | **Present Simple** | **Present Continuous** |
|--|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Meaning | habit / general / always | happening right now / temporary |
| Form | V1(+s) | am/is/are + V-ing |
| Example | I **work** in a bank. | I **am working** right now. |
| Signal | every day, usually | now, at the moment |

```
   I work here.          → my permanent job (general truth)
   I am working now.     → this exact moment (in progress)
```

### Present Simple vs. Past Simple

| | **Present Simple** | **Past Simple** |
|--|--------------------|-----------------|
| Time | now / always | finished, before now |
| Example | She **lives** in Baku. | She **lived** in Baku (last year). |

> 🔎 Full deep-dive comparison is in **Chapter 04 (Present Continuous)** and
> **Chapter 22 (Mixed Review)**.

## 12. 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani Translation Tips

1. **Present Simple ≈ Azerbaijani indiki zaman (-ır/-ir/-ur/-ür):**
   - *I **work*** → *Mən işlə**yirəm***
   - *She **reads*** → *O oxu**yur***

2. **⚠️ Don't translate the helper "do/does."** It has no Azerbaijani word!
   - *Do you like tea?* → *Çay sevirsən?* (no separate word for "do")
   - "do/does" is just **grammar glue** for questions/negatives.

3. **The -s on he/she/it has no Azerbaijani equivalent** — Azerbaijani doesn't change
   the verb for "o." You must **add it consciously** in English.

4. **"to be" (am/is/are) is often hidden in Azerbaijani -dir/-dır:**
   - *O müəllim**dir*** → *He **is** a teacher.* (the "is" is required in English)

> 💡 **Practice strategy:** translate each English sentence into Azerbaijani in your
> head, then back to English — and **check the -s and the helper** each time.

## 13. 🔊 Pronunciation Notes

### The three sounds of final **-s/-es**: /s/, /z/, /ɪz/

| Sound | When | Examples |
|-------|------|----------|
| **/s/** | after voiceless sounds (p, t, k, f) | works /wɜːks/, sleeps /sliːps/, eats /iːts/ |
| **/z/** | after voiced sounds & vowels | plays /pleɪz/, reads /riːdz/, goes /ɡəʊz/ |
| **/ɪz/** | after s, z, sh, ch, x, ge | watches /ˈwɒtʃɪz/, washes /ˈwɒʃɪz/, uses /ˈjuːzɪz/ |

```
   works  → /wɜːk-s/    (hissing s)
   plays  → /pleɪ-z/    (buzzing z)
   watches→ /wɒtʃ-ɪz/   (extra syllable "iz")
```

### Contractions you'll hear
- **don't** /dəʊnt/ · **doesn't** /ˈdʌz.ənt/
- Native speakers say *"He **doesn't** know"* fast: */hi ˈdʌz.ənt nəʊ/*

> 🗣️ **Try it:** say *works · plays · watches* out loud and feel the difference: s / z / iz.

## 23. 🗣️ Speaking Tips

- ✅ **Always pronounce the -s** on he/she/it. Many learners drop it — train it.
- ✅ Use contractions: say **don't / doesn't**, not "do not / does not" in speech.
- ✅ Link frequency adverbs naturally: *"I **usually** get up early."*
- ✅ Answer with **do/does**: *"Yes, I do."*, not *"Yes, I like."*

> 🗣️ **Shadow this:** *"I **get up**, I **have** coffee, I **go** to work."* — feel the rhythm.

---

## 24. ✍️ Writing Tips

- Use Present Simple for **facts, definitions, and routines** in essays.
- In reports/data description (IELTS Task 1), the **trend facts** use Present Simple:
  *"Sales **remain** stable."*
- Be consistent: don't switch randomly between *works* and *is working*.
- Keep the **-s** under control — proofread every he/she/it verb.

---

## 25. 🎧 Listening Tips

- Listen for the **weak, fast helper**: "d'you" = *do you* ( *"D'you like it?"* ).
- The **-s** is quick and easy to miss — train your ear with: *works / plays / watches*.
- **doesn't** often sounds like */dʌznt/* — almost one beat.

---

## 26. 📖 Reading Tips

- News headlines and science texts are **full of Present Simple** (general truths).
- In stories, Present Simple can create a "live" feeling (the **historic present**):
  *"He **walks** in, **sees** the letter, and **stops**."*
- Recipes & instructions often use it: *"You **add** the salt, then **stir**."*

---

## 27. 🌟 Native Speaker Usage

- Natives drop the helper in casual questions: *"You like it?"* (= Do you like it?)
- They use it for **commentary**: *"He **shoots**, he **scores**!"* (sports)
- For **dramatic storytelling**: *"So I **walk** in and she **goes**, 'Where were you?'"*
- For **directions/instructions**: *"You **turn** left, then you **see** the bank."*

---

## 28. ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: When do I add -s?**
Only for **he / she / it** (third person singular). Not for I/you/we/they.

**Q2: Why does "She doesn't works" sound wrong?**
Because **does** already carries the -s. The main verb returns to **V1**: *doesn't work*.

**Q3: Is Present Simple about "now"?**
Not really. It's about **general / always**. For "right now," use Present Continuous.

**Q4: Can Present Simple talk about the future?**
Yes — for **timetables**: *"The train **leaves** at 6."* (See Chapter 13.)

**Q5: Why can't I say "I am knowing"?**
*know* is a **state verb**. State verbs avoid -ing. Use *I **know***.

# 📚 Mini Story — *"Aysel's Ordinary Day"* (≈300 words)

> Read it aloud. Notice how almost every verb is **Present Simple**.

Aysel **lives** in a small flat in Baku. Every morning her alarm **rings** at six
o'clock. She **gets up**, **opens** the window, and **breathes** the cool air. She
**doesn't like** loud mornings, so she **moves** quietly.

First, she **makes** a cup of tea and **drinks** it slowly by the window. Then she
**takes** a quick shower and **gets** dressed. Aysel **works** as a nurse at the city
hospital, so she always **wears** a clean uniform.

At seven, she **leaves** home and **takes** the bus. The bus **comes** every fifteen
minutes, and it rarely **runs** late. On the way, she **reads** messages from her
friends and **smiles**.

The hospital **opens** at eight, but Aysel **arrives** early. She **likes** to start
calmly. Her colleagues **say** she **is** the friendliest nurse on the floor. During
the day she **helps** patients, **checks** their temperature, and **gives** them their
medicine. She **doesn't sit** down much — nurses **stay** busy.

At lunch, she **eats** a sandwich and **talks** with the other nurses. They often
**laugh** together. Aysel **believes** that a kind word **helps** people heal faster.

After work, she **goes** home, **cooks** dinner, and **calls** her mother. They
**talk** every evening. On weekends, Aysel **visits** her family in Ganja, or she
**meets** friends in a café. She **doesn't watch** much television; she **prefers**
books.

Before bed, she **writes** three good things from her day in a small notebook. Then
she **turns off** the light and **falls** asleep. Tomorrow, the alarm **rings** again
at six — and Aysel **starts** her day, just like always.

## E1. ❓ Wh- Questions (who, what, where, when, why, how)

To ask for **information** (not just yes/no), put a **question word** in front of *do/does*.

```
   WH-WORD  +  do/does  +  S  +  V1  ?
```

| Wh-word | Example | Azərbaycanca |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| **What** | **What** do you **do**? | Nə işlə məşğulsan? |
| **Where** | **Where** does she **live**? | O harada yaşayır? |
| **When** | **When** does the shop **open**? | Mağaza nə vaxt açılır? |
| **Why** | **Why** do they **complain**? | Niyə şikayət edirlər? |
| **How** | **How** does it **work**? | Bu necə işləyir? |
| **How often** | **How often** do you **travel**? | Nə qədər tez-tez səyahət edirsən? |
| **What time** | **What time** does it **start**? | Saat neçədə başlayır? |

> ⚠️ The verb stays **V1** after do/does, even with a Wh-word:
> ✅ *Where does he **work**?* — ❌ *Where does he works?*

## E2. 🎯 Subject Questions — The "No DOES" Special Case

This is a detail that confuses **most** learners. Read slowly.

When the **question word IS the subject** (you're asking *who/what does the action*),
**do NOT use do/does.** Just keep the normal positive word order and add **-s**.

```
   OBJECT question (normal):   Who  DOES she love?     → asking about the object
   SUBJECT question (special): Who  loveS her?         → asking who does the loving
                                    ▲ no "does", verb takes -s
```

| Type | Question | Why |
|------|----------|-----|
| Subject Q | **Who lives** here? | "Who" = subject → no *does*, add -s |
| Subject Q | **What happens** next? | "What" = subject → no *does* |
| Subject Q | **Who wants** tea? | no *does*, verb +s |
| Object Q | **Who does** she **call**? | "Who" = object → use *does* |
| Object Q | **What does** this button **do**? | "What" = object → use *does* |

> 🧠 **Trick:** If you can answer with *"He does."* / *"She does."* → it's a **subject
> question** (no helper, verb keeps -s). *"Who cooks?" → "Mum does."*

## E3. ⏰ Adverbs of Frequency — Full Placement Guide

These tell us **how often**. Their **position** follows clear rules.

### The frequency line (0% → 100%)
```
 never  rarely  seldom  occasionally  sometimes  often  usually  always
   0% ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── 100%
```

### 📌 Placement Rule

| Verb situation | Position | Example |
|----------------|----------|---------|
| With a **normal verb** | **before** the verb | She **always arrives** early. |
| With **"to be"** (am/is/are) | **after** the verb | She **is always** early. |
| With an **auxiliary** | **after** the auxiliary | She **doesn't usually** drink coffee. |

```
   I   ALWAYS   drink   tea.      (before main verb)
   I   am   ALWAYS   tired.       (after "to be")
   I   don't   USUALLY   eat out. (after auxiliary)
```

### ⚠️ Tricky ones — *usually / sometimes / often* can also start a sentence:
- ✅ ***Usually**, I get up at seven.*  ✅ *I **usually** get up at seven.*
- But **always / never / rarely / seldom** do **NOT** start a sentence:
  ❌ *Always I drink tea.* → ✅ *I **always** drink tea.*

### Frequency **expressions** (these go at the END):
```
 once a day · twice a week · three times a month · every other day · every weekend
```
- *I go to the gym **three times a week**.*
- *She calls her mum **every evening**.*

## E5. 🔀 Verbs That Are BOTH Stative AND Dynamic

Some verbs change **meaning** depending on whether they're stative (state) or dynamic
(action). This is **advanced but very useful**.

| Verb | Stative (Present Simple) | Dynamic (can take -ing) |
|------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **have** | own/possess → *I **have** a car.* | activity → *I **am having** lunch.* |
| **think** | opinion → *I **think** it's good.* | mental process → *I**'m thinking** about it.* |
| **see** | perceive/understand → *I **see** what you mean.* | meet → *I**'m seeing** the doctor today.* |
| **taste** | quality → *The soup **tastes** salty.* | action → *The chef **is tasting** the soup.* |
| **smell** | quality → *It **smells** nice.* | action → *She**'s smelling** the flowers.* |
| **feel** | opinion → *I **feel** it's wrong.* | health → *I**'m feeling** better.* (both OK) |
| **look** | appearance → *You **look** tired.* | direction → *I**'m looking** at the screen.* |
| **be** | state → *He **is** kind.* | behaviour → *He**'s being** rude today.* (acting) |

```
   I HAVE a car.        = I own it.          (state → Present Simple)
   I'M HAVING a shower. = I'm doing it now.  (action → Continuous)
```

> 💡 So *"I'm having lunch"* is correct — because here **have = eat (an action)**,
> not **have = own**.

## E6. 🌉 Present Simple for the FUTURE (after time/if words)

Surprise rule: after these words, we use **Present Simple** even though we mean the
**future**. We do **NOT** use *will* in this part.

```
   when · after · before · until · as soon as · if · unless · once · while
```

| ✅ Correct | ❌ Wrong |
|-----------|---------|
| I'll call you **when I arrive**. | …when I **will arrive**. |
| **If it rains**, we'll stay home. | If it **will rain**… |
| Wait here **until she comes**. | …until she **will come**. |
| **As soon as he finishes**, we'll eat. | As soon as he **will finish**… |

```
   MAIN clause (future: will)   +   TIME/IF clause (Present Simple)
   I will leave                 +   when the rain stops.
```

> 📌 This connects to **Chapter 18 (Conditionals)**. For now, remember:
> **"After *when/if*, use Present Simple for the future."** 🇦🇿 *when/if-dən sonra
> gələcək üçün indiki zamandan istifadə et.*

## E7. 🤝 Performative Verbs — Saying = Doing

With some verbs, **saying the sentence IS the action**. We use Present Simple, not -ing.

| Verb | Example | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| promise | I **promise** to help. | the promise happens as you say it |
| apologize | I **apologize** for the delay. | the apology is made now |
| declare | I **declare** the meeting open. | official act |
| insist | I **insist** you stay. | strong request |
| agree | I **agree** with you. | agreement made now |
| name / pronounce | I **name** this ship *Hope*. | ceremonial act |

> 🗣️ Note: *I'm promising…* sounds wrong. ✅ *I **promise**.*

## E8. 🎬 The Narrative / Historic Present (in depth)

Native speakers switch to Present Simple to make **past** stories feel **alive and exciting**:

- **Telling a story/joke:** *"So I **walk** into the room, and he just **stares** at me…"*
- **Sports commentary:** *"Messi **passes**, Suárez **shoots** — he **scores**!"*
- **Summarizing plots:** *"In the film, the hero **loses** everything and then **rises** again."*
- **History writing (dramatic):** *"In 1453, Constantinople **falls**."*
- **Step-by-step demos:** *"First I **click** here, then it **opens** the menu."*

> 💡 You don't have to use this, but **recognizing** it stops you from being confused
> when natives "tell the past in the present."

## E9. 🧭 Present Simple for Instructions, Directions & Demonstrations

| Context | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Directions** | You **go** straight, then you **turn** left. The bank **is** on the right. |
| **Recipes** | First you **chop** the onions, then you **add** the oil. |
| **Manuals/how-to** | You **press** the button and the light **comes** on. |
| **Demonstrations** | Now I **mix** the colors and you **see** green. |

> Note: imperatives (*Go straight! Turn left!*) and Present Simple (*You go straight…*)
> both appear in directions. Both are fine.

## E10. 📋 State (Stative) Verbs — Grouped Reference List

Keep these in Present Simple (avoid -ing in their *stative* meaning):

```
 💭 THINKING/OPINION:  know · believe · think · understand · realize · doubt · suppose · recognize · remember · forget · mean
 ❤️ FEELINGS/WANTS:    like · love · hate · prefer · want · need · wish · mind · care
 👁️ SENSES:            see · hear · smell · taste · feel · seem · appear · look (= appear)
 🏠 POSSESSION:        have (= own) · own · belong · possess · owe
 ⚖️ BEING/MEASURE:     be · exist · consist · contain · include · cost · weigh · measure · depend · matter · involve · fit
```

> 🧠 **Memory grouping:** *"Mind, Emotion, Senses, Possession, Being"* = **M.E.S.P.B.**
> — the five families of state verbs.

## E11. 📝 Advanced Exercises (Expansion)

### I) Subject vs. Object questions — add *do/does* only if needed
1. Who ___ (live) next door?
2. What ___ this word ___ (mean)?
3. Who ___ she ___ (call) every night?
4. What ___ (happen) at the end of the story?
5. Who ___ (want) more tea?

### J) Frequency adverb placement — put the word in the right spot
6. (always) She / drinks / coffee / in the morning.
7. (never) He / is / late.
8. (usually) We / don't / eat / out.
9. (sometimes) I / go / to bed / early.
10. (rarely) They / watch / TV.

### K) Present Simple for the future — choose the correct verb form
11. I'll text you when I ___ (get) home.
12. If it ___ (snow) tomorrow, we'll stay in.
13. Wait until the light ___ (turn) green.
14. As soon as the file ___ (download), open it.
15. She'll be happy if he ___ (call).

### L) Stative or dynamic? — choose Present Simple or Present Continuous
16. This soup ___ (taste) delicious. (quality)
17. The chef ___ (taste) the soup right now. (action)
18. I ___ (have) two brothers. (own)
19. We ___ (have) dinner at the moment. (action)
20. I ___ (think) you're right. (opinion)

---

### ✅ Expansion Answer Key

**I)** 1. lives *(subject Q — no does)* 2. does … mean 3. does … call 4. happens *(subject Q)* 5. wants *(subject Q)*

**J)**
6. She **always drinks** coffee in the morning.
7. He **is never** late. *(after "to be")*
8. We **don't usually** eat out. *(after auxiliary)*
9. I **sometimes go** to bed early. *(or: Sometimes I go…)*
10. They **rarely watch** TV.

**K)** 11. get 12. snows 13. turns 14. downloads 15. calls *(all Present Simple after when/if/until/as soon as)*

**L)** 16. tastes 17. is tasting 18. have 19. are having 20. think

## E12. ⚠️ Extra Common Mistakes (Azerbaijani-Specific)

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| Where he works? | Where **does** he work? | info questions need do/does |
| Who does live here? | Who **lives** here? | subject question → no *does*, add -s |
| When you finish, I **will** help → When you **will finish**… | When you **finish**, I'll help. | Present Simple after *when* |
| She is go to school. | She **goes** to school. | no "is" + V1 in Present Simple |
| I am agreeing with you. | I **agree** with you. | *agree* = performative/state |
| Always I am happy. | I **am always** happy. | *always* after "to be", not at start |
| He is having a brother. | He **has** a brother. | *have* (own) = stative |

# 📖 Mini Story #2 — *"The Little Café on the Corner"* (≈320 words)

> A second story by request. Almost every verb is **Present Simple**. Watch for habits,
> facts, negatives, and questions. Read it **aloud**.

Kamal **owns** a little café on the corner of a quiet street. The café **opens** at
seven and **closes** at nine. Kamal **loves** mornings, so he **arrives** first, before
the sun **climbs** over the rooftops.

Every day he **follows** the same routine. He **unlocks** the door, **turns on** the
lights, and **grinds** fresh coffee. The smell **fills** the room. "Good coffee
**takes** time," he always **says**. He **never** rushes.

His first customer **is** old Mr. Rauf. Mr. Rauf **doesn't drink** coffee; he **prefers**
tea with mint. He **sits** by the window, **reads** the newspaper, and **watches** the
street. "**Does** the world **change** much overnight?" he often **jokes**. Kamal
**smiles** and **brings** him his tea.

At eight, the students **arrive**. They **don't have** much money, so Kamal **gives**
them small discounts. "Knowledge **costs** enough," he **believes**. The students
**laugh**, **study**, and **stay** for hours. One of them, Leyla, **wants** to open her
own café one day. She **asks** Kamal a hundred questions. "How **do** you **choose** the
beans? Why **does** the milk **foam** better when it's cold?" Kamal **answers** every
one. He **enjoys** teaching.

The café **doesn't make** Kamal rich. It **doesn't need** to. It **gives** him
something better: a street that **feels** like home. The neighbors **know** his name.
The dog from the bakery **visits** every afternoon and **waits** for a biscuit.

In the evening, when the last cup **goes** quiet, Kamal **wipes** the tables and
**counts** the day. He **doesn't count** only the money. He **counts** the faces, the
small talks, the quiet thank-yous. Then he **switches off** the light, **locks** the
door, and **walks** home slowly.

Tomorrow, the café **opens** again at seven. And Kamal? He **wakes up** happy, because
he **knows** exactly where he **belongs**.

### 🔎 Story Notes — Spot the Grammar
- **Habits/routine:** *opens, closes, arrives, follows, unlocks…* (repeated daily actions)
- **State verbs:** *owns, loves, prefers, believes, knows, wants, belongs* (no -ing!)
- **Negatives:** *doesn't drink, don't have, doesn't make, doesn't need, doesn't count*
- **Questions inside speech:** *Does the world change…? How do you choose…? Why does the milk foam…?*
- **3rd-person -s everywhere:** *opens, fills, sits, reads, gives, feels, visits, waits…*
