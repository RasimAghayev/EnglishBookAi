# 🎯 Future with *Going To* — Plans & Predictions (be going to + V1)

### *Part 3 · Chapter 12 · am / is / are + going to + V1*

> **Azərbaycanca qısa giriş:** *be going to* (gələcək forması) iki şey üçün işlənir: **(1) əvvəlcədən
> qərarlaşdırılmış planlar/niyyətlər** — *"Bu axşam dərs oxuyacağam"* (planım var), və **(2) indiki
> sübuta əsaslanan proqnozlar** — *"Buludlara bax — yağış yağacaq."* Bu, *will*-dən fərqlidir: *will*
> çox vaxt **anında qərar** və **fikir/inam**la bağlıdır; *going to* isə **artıq qərar verilmiş plan**
> və **gözlə görünən dəlil** ilə. Azərbaycancada hər ikisi çox vaxt **-acaq/-əcək** kimi tərcümə olunur,
> amma məna fərqlidir. 🎯

---

## 1. 🧭 Introduction / Giriş

**Future with *going to*** is one of the two main ways to talk about the future (the other is *will*,
Chapter 11). It is built from the verb **be** + **going to** + the base verb (V1):

```
   I am going to call her.       → my plan, decided already
   It is going to rain.          → my prediction, I can see the clouds
```

🇦🇿 *be going to* iki sütun üzərində dayanır: **PLAN** (niyyət) və **DƏLİL** (proqnoz). Bu fəsildə hər
ikisini, formanı, tələffüzü (*gonna*), və *will* / Present Continuous ilə fərqini öyrənəcəyik.

---

## 2. ❓ Why do we use it? / Niyə işlədirik?

We use **be going to** when:

| Use | Meaning | Example | 🇦🇿 |
|-----|---------|---------|-----|
| **Prior plan / intention** | I decided this **before** speaking | *I'm going to start a diet.* | Pəhriz saxlamaq fikrindəyəm. |
| **Prediction from evidence** | I can **see/feel** it coming now | *She's going to have a baby.* | Onun körpəsi olacaq. |

> 🔑 The big idea: *going to* points to something **already in motion** — either a decision already
> made, or evidence already visible. 🇦🇿 Əsas fikir: *going to* **artıq başlamış** bir şeyə işarə edir
> — ya qərar verilmiş, ya da gözlə görünən dəlil.

## 3. 🌍 Real-life situations / Həyati vəziyyətlər

```
   • Announcing a plan:        "We're going to get married in spring."
   • Talking about goals:      "I'm going to learn to drive this year."
   • Warning from evidence:    "Careful — you're going to drop that!"
   • Weather/health signs:     "I don't feel well. I think I'm going to be sick."
   • Refusing/insisting (plan):"I'm not going to apologize."
```

## 5. 🏗️ Formula / Formul

```
 (+)  Positive:        S + am/is/are + going to + V1
                       I'm going to travel.  ·  She's going to call.

 (−)  Negative:        S + am/is/are + NOT + going to + V1
                       I'm not going to travel.  ·  He isn't going to call.

 (?)  Question:        Am/Is/Are + S + going to + V1 ?
                       Are you going to travel?  ·  Is she going to call?

 (?−) Negative Q:      Aren't/Isn't + S + going to + V1 ?
                       Aren't you going to help?  ·  Isn't it going to rain?

 (✓)  Short answers:   Yes, I am.  /  No, I'm not.
                       Yes, she is. /  No, she isn't.
                       Yes, they are. / No, they aren't.
```

### The "be" must match the subject
```
   I            →  am  going to …
   he/she/it    →  is  going to …
   you/we/they  →  are going to …
```

## 6. 🧱 Sentence Structure / Cümlə quruluşu

```
   ┌─────────┬───────────┬──────────┬───────┬───────────────┐
   │ Subject │ be (am/is/│ going to │  V1   │   rest        │
   │         │   are)    │          │(base) │               │
   ├─────────┼───────────┼──────────┼───────┼───────────────┤
   │  She     │   is      │ going to │ buy   │ a new phone.  │
   │  They    │   are     │ going to │ move  │ to Baku.      │
   │  I        │   am      │ going to │ rest  │ tonight.      │
   └─────────┴───────────┴──────────┴───────┴───────────────┘
```

> ⚠️ After *going to* comes the **bare V1** — never *to* again, never *-ing*, never V2.
> ✅ *going to **go*** · ❌ *going to to go* · ❌ *going to going* · ❌ *going to went*

---

## 7. 📐 Grammar Rules / Qrammatika qaydaları

1. **be + going to + V1.** The only part that changes for the subject is *am/is/are*.
2. **Contractions are normal:** *I'm, you're, he's, she's, it's, we're, they're* + going to.
3. **Negatives:** put *not* after *be* → *isn't/aren't going to* (or *'m not going to*).
4. **Questions:** invert *be* and the subject → *Are you going to…?*
5. **"going to go" / "going to come"** are correct but often shortened in speech to just the
   present continuous: *"I'm going to the party"* (movement) — see §11.
6. **Object questions** use a Wh-word + be: *What are you going to do?*

---

## 8. ✍️ Pronunciation & "gonna" / Tələffüz və *gonna*

In **informal speech**, *going to* + verb is pronounced **"gonna"** /ˈɡʌnə/.

```
   I'm going to call → "I'm gonna call"      ✅ (before a VERB)
   It's going to rain → "It's gonna rain"     ✅
```

> ⚠️ **Big trap:** *gonna* only replaces *going to* when a **verb** follows. When *going to* means
> **movement to a place** (going + to + noun), you **cannot** say *gonna*:
> ✅ *I'm going to the shop.*  ❌ *I'm gonna the shop.*
> 🇦🇿 *gonna* yalnız feldən əvvəl işlənir; "yerə getmək" mənasında YOX.

> 🗒️ Write *going to* in exams/formal writing. *gonna* is **spoken/informal only**.

## 10. ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Ümumi səhvlər

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why / 🇦🇿 |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| I going to call. | I **am** going to call. | *be* is required — köməkçi *be* lazımdır |
| She is going to **calls**. | She is going to **call**. | V1 after *going to* |
| He is going to **buying**. | He is going to **buy**. | V1, not -ing |
| We going to **to** travel. | We are going to **travel**. | one *to* only |
| Is going to rain. | **It** is going to rain. | English needs a subject (*it*) |
| I'm gonna the gym. | I'm **going to** the gym. | *gonna* only before a verb |
| Tomorrow I will buy it (it was already planned). | Tomorrow I'm **going to** buy it. | prior plan → *going to* |

## 11. 🔀 Comparison with similar forms / Müqayisə

### *going to* vs *will* (Chapter 11)
```
   PLAN already made    → going to   "I'm going to visit Gran on Sunday." (planned)
   DECISION now         → will        "I'll visit Gran — good idea!" (decided this second)

   PREDICTION + evidence→ going to   "Look at the score — they're going to lose."
   PREDICTION + opinion → will        "I think they'll lose." (just my belief)

   Offer / promise      → will        "I'll help you." / "I'll pay you back."
```

### *going to* vs Present Continuous (Chapter 13)
```
   going to            = intention / plan        "I'm going to meet Ali." (I intend to)
   Present Continuous  = fixed arrangement       "I'm meeting Ali at 6." (time/place set)
   → Often interchangeable for plans; Present Continuous sounds more "in the diary".
```

> 🇦🇿 **Qısa qayda:** plan varsa → *going to*; dəqiq saat/yer təyin olunubsa → Present Continuous;
> indicə qərar verirsənsə və ya söz/təklif verirsənsə → *will*.

## 28. ❓ FAQ

**Q: Is *going to* the same as *will*?**
A: No. *going to* = prior plan or evidence-based prediction; *will* = on-the-spot decision, offer,
promise, or opinion-based prediction.

**Q: Can I say "I'm going to go"?**
A: Yes, it's correct. In speech many people just say "I'm going" (Present Continuous) for movement.

**Q: Is "gonna" wrong?**
A: It's not wrong — it's **informal spoken** English. Don't write it in formal texts.

# 🪜 A. LEVEL DIFFERENCES (A1 → C2) / Səviyyələr üzrə fərqlər

| Level | What to know | Typical mistakes (acceptable) | Native expectation | IELTS |
|-------|--------------|-------------------------------|--------------------|-------|
| **A1** | *be going to* for simple plans: *I'm going to eat.* | drops *be* ("I going to") | understood, basic | ~3–4 |
| **A2** | negatives & questions; weather predictions | wrong *be* form | mostly clear | ~4–4.5 |
| **B1** | *going to* vs *will*; *Look!*-predictions | over-using *will* for plans | natural in common cases | ~5 |
| **B2** | *was going to* (unfulfilled past plan); evidence-predictions | misses subtle *will/going to* nuance | comfortable | ~6 |
| **C1** | register (*gonna* vs *going to*), *was/had been going to* | none significant | precise & idiomatic | ~7 |
| **C2** | stylistic choice vs *be about/due/set to*; rhetorical predictions | — | indistinguishable from native | ~8+ |

> 🇦🇿 **Qəbul edilə bilən səhvlər:** A1–A2-də *be*-ni unutmaq və ya səhv formada işlətmək normaldır;
> B1-dən sonra *will/going to* fərqi gözlənilir.

# 🗣️ B. NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT / Doğma danışanın baxışı
```
 • HOW NATIVES REALLY SAY IT:  "gonna" almost always in speech → "I'm gonna grab lunch."
 • TEXTBOOK SIMPLIFICATION:    books say "going to" — natives reduce it to "gonna" instantly.
 • SPOKEN SHORTCUTS:           "gon'" in fast/casual: "I'm gon' tell you somethin'."
 • CONTRACTIONS:               I'm / you're / he's / we're / they're + gonna
 • DROPPING "going to go":     "I'm going to the gym" (movement) — no second verb needed.
 • INFORMAL:  "We're gonna hang out."   FORMAL:  "We are going to meet."
 • BUSINESS:  "We're going to roll this out in Q3."
 • ACADEMIC:  rare in essays — prefer "This paper will argue / is likely to".
```

# ♻️ C. ALTERNATIVE EXPRESSIONS / Alternativ ifadələr

Same meaning — *"I plan to start tomorrow"* — expressed many ways:

| Alternative | Register | Nuance / when natives use it |
|-------------|----------|------------------------------|
| I'm **going to** start. | neutral | default plan/intention |
| I'm **gonna** start. | informal/spoken | casual conversation |
| I **will** start. | neutral | decision/promise (often on the spot) |
| I'm **starting** tomorrow. | neutral | fixed arrangement (Present Continuous) |
| I **plan to / intend to** start. | formal | deliberate, written |
| I'm **about to** start. | neutral | imminent — within minutes |
| I'm **due to** start. | formal | scheduled/expected by arrangement |
| I'm **set to** start. | news/formal | reporting an arranged future |
| I'm **planning on** starting. | informal | softened intention |

```
 AMERICAN vs BRITISH:  both use "going to / gonna" identically.
                       BrE also: "I'm meant to start" (≈ supposed to).
 IELTS upgrade:        "The government is set to introduce…" / "is likely to…"
```

# 🧬 EXPANSION PACK — *going to* (Go Deeper) · E1–E12

> You asked to **EXPAND**. These advanced notes take you from "I know the form" to "I use it like a
> native." Each block is short, with ✅/❌ and 🇦🇿 notes. Read slowly. ☕

## E1 · ⏪ *was / were going to* — the plan that didn't happen
The **past** of *going to* describes a plan that was **intended but (often) unfulfilled**.
```
   I was going to call you, but I lost my phone.     → intended, didn't happen
   We were going to travel, then the flight was cancelled.
```
> 🇦🇿 *was/were going to* = "edəcəkdim / edəcəkdik" (amma alınmadı). Çox vaxt "amma…" ilə gəlir.
> (Bunun tam fəsli: **Chapter 17 — Future in the Past**.)

## E2 · 🪜 *had been going to* — a plan dropped even earlier (C1/C2)
For a plan that existed **before another past point** and changed:
```
   I had been going to resign, but then they offered me a raise.
```
> 🇦🇿 Daha əvvəl mövcud olan, sonra dəyişən plan. Yüksək səviyyə (C1–C2).

## E3 · 🔍 Evidence-predictions, levelled up
Strengthen an evidence prediction with these lead-ins:
```
   Judging by the sky, it's going to pour.
   Given these results, profits are going to fall.
   The way things are going, we're going to miss the deadline.
   At this rate, they're going to run out of money.
```
> 🧠 All of these = "the evidence is in front of us **now** → logical future" → *going to* (not *will*).

## E4 · 🚫 "I'm not going to…" — refusal & firmness
The negative of *going to* often carries **determination/refusal**, not just a neutral non-plan:
```
   I'm not going to argue with you.        → I refuse to.
   She's not going to apologize.           → she's determined not to.
```
> 🇦🇿 "Mübahisə etməyəcəyəm" — sadəcə plan deyil, **qətiyyət/imtina** mənası verir.

## E5 · 🔁 *going to* in time clauses & conditionals
The **no-*will*/no-*going-to* after *if/when/as soon as*** rule still applies — keep the **present**
in that clause; the **result** clause can use *going to*:
```
   ✅ When you see her, you're going to be amazed.   (when + present → result going to)
   ✅ If you keep eating that, you're going to feel sick.
   ❌ When you are going to see her…   ❌ If you will keep eating…
```
> Exception (intention): *"If you're going to be late, text me."* = "if you intend/it's heading that way."

## E6 · 🗣️ *going to go / going to come* — natives drop the second verb
Grammatically fine, but in speech people usually shorten **movement** with the Present Continuous:
```
   "I'm going to go to the gym."   →  "I'm going to the gym."   (more natural)
   "Are you going to come?"        →  "Are you coming?"
```
> 🇦🇿 Hərəkət (gəlmək/getmək) mənasında ikinci feli atıb Present Continuous işlədirlər.

## E7 · ⏱️ *going to* vs *be about to* vs *on the point of*
Degrees of **imminence** (how soon):
```
   going to        → future, near or far     "I'm going to redecorate (someday)."
   be about to     → seconds/minutes away    "The film is about to start." 🔜
   on the point of → very imminent (formal)   "She was on the point of leaving."
```

## E8 · 🎙️ Spoken reductions — the register ladder
```
   going to   ▁▁▁▁  careful / formal / writing
   gonna      ▃▃▃▃  standard informal speech (very common)
   gon'       ▅▅▅▅  casual / some dialects: "I'm gon' tell you…"
   I'mma      ▇▇▇▇  very informal AmE slang (= I'm going to): "Imma head out."
```
> ⚠️ Only *going to* in exams/essays. The rest are **listening-comprehension** targets.

## E9 · ❓ Questions — information & subject
```
   INFO Q:    What are you going to do?  ·  Where are they going to stay?
   SUBJECT Q: Who's going to pay?  ·  What's going to happen next?
   (subject question keeps normal order — no extra inversion beyond "be")
```

## E10 · 🆚 The full future-form decision flow
```
   ┌─ Is it a fact/timetable? ───────────────► Present Simple  (The train leaves at 9.)
   │
   ├─ A fixed arrangement (time+place set)? ─► Present Continuous (I'm meeting Ali at 6.)
   │
   ├─ A plan/intention you already had? ─────► going to        (I'm going to study.)
   │
   ├─ Evidence you can see right now? ───────► going to        (It's going to rain.)
   │
   ├─ Deciding this second / offer / promise?► will            (I'll get it!)
   │
   └─ Just your opinion/belief? ─────────────► will            (I think they'll win.)
```

## E11 · 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani-specific traps (deeper)
| ❌ | ✅ | Why / 🇦🇿 |
|----|----|-----------|
| I will study tonight (already planned). | I'm **going to** study tonight. | əvvəlcədən plan → going to, will yox |
| It will rain — look at the clouds! | It's **going to** rain — look! | dəlil → going to |
| I'm going to **go** to school (movement). | I'm **going** to school. | hərəkətdə ikinci feli at |
| He is going to **be** a teacher → drops "be" | He is going to **be** a teacher. | isimdən əvvəl "be" lazımdır |
| I'mma / gonna in an essay. | I am **going to** … | yazıda yalnız tam forma |

## E12 · 🧩 Synthesis — your one-line chooser
```
   PLAN already in your head?         → going to
   PROOF in front of your eyes?       → going to
   Calendar slot booked?              → present continuous
   Public timetable?                  → present simple
   Deciding/offering right now?       → will
```


## ⚖️ *going to* vs *will* — minimal pairs (same situation, different trigger)


have to."

Mum laughs. "I **was going to** pack the kitchen last night, but I fell asleep. So guess what we**'re**
all **gonna** do this morning?"

By noon, the van is full — almost too full. As Dad closes the doors, a box shifts. "Careful!" shouts
Kanan. "That**'s going to** fall!" — and it does, spilling spoons across the drive. Everyone laughs.

"**Are** you **going to** miss this place?" Mum asks Aysel quietly.

Aysel thinks. "A little. But I**'m going to** make new friends. And Grandma says she**'s going to**
visit every month."

The engine starts. "Right," says Dad. "By tonight we**'re going to** be in our new home. Tomorrow
we**'re going to** unpack. And next week —" he winks "— we**'re going to** throw the biggest
house-warming party Baku has ever seen."

As they drive away, dark clouds gather behind them. "Of course," sighs Mum, looking up. "It**'s going
to** rain on moving day." But nobody minds. They**'re** all **going to** remember this day for years.

### 🇦🇿 Tərcümə
Sabah Məmmədovlar ailəsi Bakıya köçəcək. Qutular qapının yanında yığılıb, mikroavtobus sifariş
olunub, hər kəsin bir planı var. "Yeni otağımı mavi rəngləyəcəyəm," deyir balaca Aysel. "Və yazı
masamı pəncərənin yanına qoyacağam." Qardaşı Kanan qutu qülləsinə baxıb qaşqabağını tökür. "O
mikroavtobus çox kiçik olacaq," deyə xəbərdarlıq edir. "Bax — bunların yarısını qoyub gedəcəyik."
"Heç nə qoyub getməyəcəyik," deyir ata qətiyyətlə. "Lazım olsa, iki dəfə gedib-gələcəyik." Ana
gülür: "Dünən axşam mətbəxi yığacaqdım, amma yuxuya getdim. İndi təxmin edin, bu səhər hamımız nə
edəcəyik?" Günortaya yaxın mikroavtobus dolur — az qala həddən artıq. Ata qapıları bağlayanda bir
qutu sürüşür. "Diqqət!" deyə Kanan qışqırır. "O düşəcək!" — və düşür, qaşıqlar həyətə dağılır. Hamı

### 🔎 Uses spotted / Tapılan istifadələr
```
   PLAN/INTENTION:  "I'm going to paint…", "we're going to unpack", "throw a party"
   PREDICTION:      "That van is going to be too small", "That's going to fall!",
                    "It's going to rain on moving day"
   REFUSAL/FIRM:    "We're not going to leave anything."
   was going to:    "I was going to pack the kitchen… but I fell asleep." (unfulfilled)
   gonna (speech):  "what we're all gonna do this morning"
   QUESTION:        "Are you going to miss this place?"
```
> 🧠 Notice how the **same form** carries a plan in one line and a from-the-evidence prediction in
> the next — context tells you which. 🇦🇿 Eyni forma bir cümlədə plan, digərində dəlilə əsaslanan
> proqnoz bildirir — fərqi **kontekst** verir.

You can now use **be going to + V1** for the two things it does best: **plans/intentions** already
decided, and **predictions from present evidence**. You learned the full form (positive, negative,
question, short answers), the **"gonna"** reduction (verbs only!), the crucial contrast with
**will** (on-the-spot decisions/offers/opinions) and **Present Continuous** (fixed arrangements),
plus the new standard sections — **level differences, native insight, alternative expressions,**
and **deep-analysis tasks**. 🎯

```
 └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
