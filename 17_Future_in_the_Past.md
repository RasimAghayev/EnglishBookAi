# 🕰️ Future in the Past — *would · was going to · was about to*

### *Part 3 · Chapter 17 · the future seen from a point in the past*

> **Azərbaycanca qısa giriş:** Bəzən keçmişdə dayanıb **o vaxtdan sonra baş verəcək** (və ya baş
> verməyəcək) bir şeydən danışırıq — buna **"keçmişdəki gələcək"** (*future in the past*) deyilir.
> Açar fikir sadədir: **gələcəyin hər formasını götür və keçmişə çək.** *will → would*, *am/is/are
> going to → was/were going to*, *am/is/are V-ing → was/were V-ing*, *will have done → would have
> done*. Çox vaxt hekayədə və dolayı nitqdə (*"O dedi ki, gələcək…"*) işlənir. Diqqət: bu, **şərt
> cümləsi deyil** — sadəcə keçmişdən baxılan gələcəkdir. 🕰️

---

## 1. 🧭 Introduction / Giriş

**Future in the past** = how we talk, *from a past moment*, about something that was **still in the
future** at that moment.

```
   PAST moment ───●───────────────► (something later)
                  │   "At 8 a.m. I didn't know it yet, but I WOULD MISS the train."
                  │   "We WERE GOING TO leave at nine, but the car broke down."
```

🇦🇿 Yəni: keçmişdə bir nöqtədə dayanırsan, və oradan **sonra olacaq** bir şeyi təsvir edirsən.
İngiliscə bunun üçün gələcək formalarının **keçmiş variantını** işlədir.

> 🔑 **The master rule / Əsas qayda:** take any future form and "shift" it one step into the past.
```
   will           → would
   be going to    → was / were going to
   am/is/are V-ing→ was / were V-ing      (arrangement seen from the past)
   V1 (timetable) → V2 (timetable)        "The train left at 9." (scheduled)
   will be V-ing  → would be V-ing
   will have V3   → would have V3
   is about to    → was about to
   is to + V1     → was to + V1
```

---

## 2. ❓ Why do we use it? / Niyə işlədirik?

| Reason | Example | 🇦🇿 |
|--------|---------|-----|
| **Reported speech** (backshift of *will*) | She said she **would** help. | O dedi ki, kömək edəcək. |
| **Unfulfilled plan** (it didn't happen) | We **were going to** marry, but we split up. | Evlənəcəkdik, amma ayrıldıq. |
| **Narration / fate** (what happened next) | He left home in 1990. He **would never return**. | 1990-da evdən çıxdı. Bir daha qayıtmayacaqdı. |
| **An action interrupted** | I **was about to** call when you knocked. | Sən qapını döyəndə zəng etmək üzrəydim. |
| **Plans/arrangements in a story** | I **was meeting** him the next day, so I went to bed early. | Ertəsi gün onunla görüşürdüm, ona görə tez yatdım. |

> 🔑 Future-in-the-past is the natural language of **stories, memories and reported speech** —
> anywhere the "now" of the sentence sits in the past. 🇦🇿 Hekayə, xatirə və dolayı nitqin dilidir.

> 💡 **Quick Reminder —** the *will → would* change is exactly the **backshift** of reported speech; it is explained in full in **[Chapter 25 — Reported Speech](25_Tenses_in_Reported_Speech.md)**. 🇦🇿 *"will → would" dəyişməsi dolayı nitqdəki **backshift**dir — tam izahı Ch.25-dədir.*

## 3. 🌍 Real-life situations / Həyati vəziyyətlər

```
   • Telling a story:        "It was my last day — tomorrow I would fly home."
   • Reporting a promise:    "He told me he would pay me back."
   • A plan that failed:     "We were going to surprise her, but she found out."
   • A near-miss:            "I was just about to leave when the phone rang."
   • Destiny / history:      "That decision would change everything."
   • Arranged next step:     "She was seeing the lawyer the following morning."
```

## 4. ⏳ Timeline / Zaman xətti

```
            PAST viewpoint              "later" (still past, but AFTER the viewpoint)
   ───────────●────────────────────────────●─────────────────► now
              │                              │
        "On Monday I knew…"           "…that on Friday I WOULD leave."

   was going to / was about to:
   ───────────●═════ (intended) ┄┄✗ (didn't happen)──────────► now
              │
        "We WERE GOING TO leave at nine"  → "…but we didn't."
```

🇦🇿 İki nöqtə var: (1) keçmişdəki baxış nöqtəsi, (2) ondan sonra gələn hadisə. İkincisi birinciyə
görə "gələcək"dir, amma yenə də keçmişdədir.

## 5. 🏗️ Formula / Formul

```
 WOULD (past of will)
   (+)  S + would + V1            I knew he would come.
   (−)  S + wouldn't + V1         I knew he wouldn't come.
   (?)  …whether S would + V1     I asked whether he would come.

 WAS / WERE GOING TO (past of be going to)
   (+)  S + was/were + going to + V1     We were going to call you.
   (−)  S + wasn't/weren't + going to + V1  We weren't going to stay.
   (?)  Was/Were + S + going to + V1?    Was she going to help?

 WAS / WERE + V-ing (arrangement in the past)
   (+)  S + was/were + V-ing + (next time)   I was meeting him the next day.

 PAST SIMPLE (timetable in the past)
   (+)  S + V2 + (scheduled time)            The train left at nine. (per schedule)

 WAS / WERE ABOUT TO (on the point of)
   (+)  S + was/were (just) about to + V1    I was just about to leave.

 WOULD BE + V-ing  (future continuous in the past)
   (+)  S + would be + V-ing                 This time tomorrow he would be flying.

 WOULD HAVE + V3   (future perfect in the past)
   (+)  S + would have + V3                  By Friday she would have finished.

 WAS / WERE TO + V1  (formal / destined)
   (+)  S + was/were to + V1                 He was to become a great leader.
   (unfulfilled)  S + was/were to have + V3  The talks were to have begun on Monday.
```

## 7. 📐 Grammar Rules / Qaydalar

1. **Shift the future back one step.** Every future form has a past-viewpoint twin (see §1 box).
2. ***would* = backshifted *will*.** In reported speech, *will → would*: *"I will help" → He said he
   **would** help.*
3. ***was/were going to* often signals an *unfulfilled* plan** — what was intended but didn't happen:
   *"I was going to phone, but I forgot."* 🇦🇿 Çox vaxt "niyyət vardı, amma olmadı".
4. **This is NOT the second conditional.** *would* here is a real past-future, not an imaginary
   situation. ✅ *He promised he would come.* (real) vs ❌ confusing with *"If he came, he would…"*.
5. **Past Continuous = arrangement seen from the past:** *"I was leaving the next morning."*
6. **Past Simple = timetable seen from the past:** *"The plane took off at six."* (scheduled).
7. ***was about to / was on the point of*** = the event was **imminent** at that past moment (often
   interrupted). 🇦🇿 "az qalmışdı / üzrəydi".
8. ***was/were to + V1*** = formal arrangement or **destiny/what later happened**: *"She was to win
   three medals."* The form *was to have + V3* marks a plan that **failed**.

---

## 8. 🎯 The reporting connection / Dolayı nitqlə əlaqə

Future-in-the-past is mostly what happens to **future forms inside reported speech** (full treatment
in Ch. 25):
```
   DIRECT (present viewpoint)        →   REPORTED (past viewpoint)
   "I will call you."                →   He said he WOULD call me.
   "I'm going to resign."            →   She said she WAS GOING TO resign.
   "We're meeting at six."           →   They said they WERE MEETING at six.
   "The train leaves at nine."       →   He said the train LEFT at nine.
   "I'll have finished by then."     →   She said she WOULD HAVE FINISHED by then.
```
> 🇦🇿 Dolayı nitqdə "indiki baxış" keçmişə sürüşür, ona görə *will → would* olur.

## 10. ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Ümumi səhvlər

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why / 🇦🇿 |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| He said he **will** call. | He said he **would** call. | reported → *will* backshifts to *would* |
| We **are going to** leave, but the car broke down. | We **were going to** leave… | keçmiş niyyət → *was/were going to* |
| I **would** call you yesterday (= intended). | I **was going to** call you yesterday. | niyyət üçün *was going to* |
| This time tomorrow he **would flying**. | …he **would be flying**. | future-continuous-in-past → *would be V-ing* |
| I **was about to leaving**. | I **was about to leave**. | *about to* + **V1** |
| She **was to having** arrived Monday. | She **was to have arrived** Monday. | unfulfilled → *was to have + V3* |
| He knew that he **will** be late. | He knew that he **would** be late. | past viewpoint → *would* |

## 11. 🔀 Comparison — future-in-the-past forms / Formaların müqayisəsi

```
   would + V1            general later action / reported will   "He would arrive at nine."
   was going to + V1     plan/intention (often unfulfilled)     "We were going to call."
   was about to + V1     imminent (about to happen)             "I was about to call."
   was + V-ing           fixed arrangement seen from the past   "I was meeting him next day."
   V2 (past simple)      timetable seen from the past           "The train left at nine."
   would be + V-ing      action in progress later               "He'd be flying by noon."
   would have + V3       completed by a later point             "She'd have finished by then."
   was to + V1           formal arrangement / destiny           "He was to become king."
```

### The close pair: *would* vs *was going to*
```
   "He said he WOULD help."        → a reported promise (neutral, will-future in the past)
   "He WAS GOING TO help (but…)."  → an intention, often that DIDN'T happen
   → For broken plans, natives strongly prefer "was going to".
```

> 🇦🇿 **Qısa qayda:** vəd/proqnoz (dolayı) → *would*; niyyət (xüsusən pozulmuş) → *was going to*;
> az qalmış hadisə → *was about to*; razılaşma → *was V-ing*; cədvəl → *V2*.

## 13. 🔊 Pronunciation notes / Tələffüz qeydləri
```
   would → very often contracted:  I'd, he'd, we'd, they'd  /aɪd, hiːd, wiːd, ðeɪd/
   "would have" in speech → "would've" /ˈwʊdəv/  — NEVER "would of" (spelling error!)
   "was going to" → fast speech "was gonna" /wəz ˈɡʌnə/  (informal)
   "would be" → "I'd be" /aɪd biː/ ; "would have finished" → "I'd've finished" (very reduced)
```
> ⚠️ **"would of" yoxdur!** Düzgün yazılış: *would have / would've*. 🇦🇿 Səsə görə qarışdırma.

- Formal: *The committee was to reconvene the following week.*

## 28. ❓ FAQ
**Q: Is *would* here the same as in the second conditional?** A: No. Here it's a **real** future seen
from the past ("He promised he would come"), not an imaginary situation.
**Q: *would* or *was going to* for a plan that failed?** A: *was going to* — it strongly implies the
plan didn't happen.
**Q: Can I say "would of"?** A: Never. It's **would have / would've** (it just *sounds* like "of").
**Q: Why "The train left at nine" for a future-in-the-past?** A: Same logic as the present timetable
("leaves") — just shifted to the past viewpoint.

# 📖 Mini Story — *"The Letter That Came Too Late"* (≈250 words)

It was the spring of 1991, and young Murad had no idea how that ordinary Tuesday **would change**
everything.

He **was going to** leave for the capital the next morning. His bag was packed; the bus **left** at
six. He **was meeting** a recruiter at noon — a job that, his uncle said, **would set him up for
life**.

That evening, he sat by the window. *Tomorrow*, he thought, *I'll be starting a new life. By winter
I **would have saved** enough to bring my mother to the city.*

He **was just about to** turn off the lamp when someone knocked. It was the postman, breathless. "A
letter," he said. "It **was supposed to** arrive last week."

Murad opened it. The recruiter had written that the position **would no longer be available** — the
company **was to close** its city office. The plan he had built his whole future on **was going to**
collapse before it began.

He sat in silence. The bus **would still leave** at six; he simply **wouldn't be on it**.

Years later, Murad would tell this story with a smile. Missing that bus, he'd say, **was to become**
the luckiest accident of his life. He stayed, opened a small workshop, and met the woman he **would
later marry**.

"Little did I know," he always ended, "that the letter that came too late **would give** me
everything."

# 🗣️ B. NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT
```
 • CONTRACTIONS:   "I'd help", "he'd be flying", "we'd have finished" — natives contract would heavily.
 • "WAS GONNA":    in speech, "was going to" → "was gonna" → "I was gonna call but…".
 • "WOULD'VE":     spoken "would have" = "would've" /ˈwʊdəv/.  Writing "would of" is a mistake.
 • STORY 'WOULD':  novelists use bare "would" to foreshadow: "He boarded the train. He would never
                   see the city again." — dramatic, inevitable.
 • BROKEN PLANS:   the most common everyday use is "I was going to … but …" (excuse/explanation).
 • NEWS REPORTING: "officials said prices would rise", "the company would cut 500 jobs" (backshift).
 • "WAS TO":       biographies/history: "She was to win the Nobel Prize." (= and she did).
```

# ♻️ C. ALTERNATIVE EXPRESSIONS

Expressing *"I intended to call you (but didn't)"* in several ways:

| Alternative | Register | Nuance |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| I **was going to** call you. | neutral | most common; implies it didn't happen |
| I **was about to** call you. | neutral | I was on the very point of it |
| I **had intended to** call you. | formal | emphasises prior intention |
| I **meant to** call you. | informal | apologetic ("sorry, I meant to") |
| I **was supposed to** call you. | neutral | there was an obligation/arrangement |
| I **would have** called (if…) | neutral | conditional — needs a condition |
| I **was on the point of** calling. | formal | very imminent |

```
 IELTS upgrade:  "I had every intention of contacting you, but circumstances intervened."
 AmE/BrE:        identical; AmE more freely uses "was gonna" in casual speech.
 ⚠️ Note:        "was supposed to" adds OBLIGATION; "was going to" is just intention.
```

# 🕵️ D. DEEP ANALYSIS TASKS

### Grammar Detective — find & fix, then explain *why*
```
 1. "She told me she will arrive on Monday."
 2. "We are going to buy the house, but the bank refused the loan." (telling a past story)
 3. "I was about to calling him."
 4. "By the end of the year he would finished the novel."
 5. "He would of won if he'd trained harder."
```

### Compare the meanings
```
 a) "He said he would help."     vs  "He was going to help (but…)."
 b) "I was about to leave."      vs  "I was leaving." (arranged)
 c) "She was to become queen."   vs  "She would become queen."
 d) "We were going to win."      vs  "We would have won (if…)."
```

### Rewrite naturally (reported → direct, or direct → reported)
```
 e) Direct: "I'll text you when I land."   → Reported: She said …
 f) Reported: He said the meeting would start at ten. → Direct: "…"
```

## ✅ Deep-Analysis Answer Key
```
 Detective:
 1. "She told me she WOULD arrive on Monday." (reported → will backshifts to would)
 2. "We WERE GOING TO buy the house, but the bank refused." (past viewpoint → were going to)
 3. "I was about to CALL him." (about to + V1, not V-ing)
 4. "…he would HAVE FINISHED the novel." (future-perfect-in-past → would have + V3)
 5. "He WOULD HAVE won if he'd trained harder." ("would have", never "would of")

 Compare:
 a) "would help" = reported promise (neutral, it may well happen);  "was going to help (but…)" =
    intention that typically DID NOT happen.
 b) "was about to leave" = imminent, on the very point of going;  "was leaving" = a fixed arrangement
    seen from the past (planned departure).
 c) "was to become queen" = destiny/narrative (formal, and it happened);  "would become queen" =
    also later-fact, slightly less formal/more storytelling.
 d) "were going to win" = were on course / intended to;  "would have won (if…)" = conditional, an
    unreal result depending on a condition.

 Rewrite:
 e) She said she would text me when she landed.
 f) "The meeting will start at ten."
```

# 🧩 EXPANSION PACK — Future in the Past (deep dive)

> A deeper layer: the *broken-plan* pattern, *was about to … when*, the three faces of *was to*, the
> dangerous **habitual *would*** confusion, the **real vs unreal *would*** split, backshift exceptions,
> *was supposed to* vs *was going to*, dramatic narrative *would*, and questions/negatives. 🕰️

## E1 · The shift-back table — with mixed practice / Geri sürüşmə cədvəli
```
   PRESENT/FUTURE viewpoint            PAST viewpoint (future-in-the-past)
   "I will leave."                  →  I knew I would leave.
   "I'm going to leave."            →  I knew I was going to leave.
   "I'm leaving at six."            →  I knew I was leaving at six.
   "The bus leaves at six."         →  I knew the bus left at six.
   "I'll be living abroad."         →  I knew I would be living abroad.
   "I'll have left by then."        →  I knew I would have left by then.
   "I'm about to leave."            →  I knew I was about to leave.
   "I'm to leave at dawn." (formal) →  I knew I was to leave at dawn.
```
> 🇦🇿 Hər sətirdə baxış nöqtəsi keçmişə ("I knew…") köçür və forma bir addım geri sürüşür.

---

## E2 · The broken-plan pattern / Pozulmuş plan modeli
The single most frequent everyday use. Structure: **was/were going to + V1 … but/when + result.**
```
   "I was going to call you, BUT I lost your number."        (plan → didn't happen)
   "We were going to leave, WHEN the storm hit."             (plan interrupted)
   "She was going to apologise, but he hung up first."
   "They were going to get married — then they broke up."
```
> 🔑 *was going to* almost always implies **the plan failed or changed.** If it succeeded, you'd
> usually just say what happened (*"I called you"*). 🇦🇿 *was going to* = niyyət vardı, amma çox vaxt
> baş tutmadı.

---

## E3 · *was about to … when* — the interrupted-imminent pattern / Az qalmışdı ki…
```
   was (just) about to + V1   +   when + Past Simple
   "I was just about to leave WHEN the phone rang."
   "She was on the point of signing WHEN she noticed the error."
   "We were on the verge of giving up WHEN help arrived."
```
```
   STRENGTH LADDER (how imminent?):
   was going to ───► was about to ───► was just about to ───► was on the (very) point of
   (intended)        (soon)            (any second)            (formal, one breath away)
```
> 🇦🇿 *was about to* = "indicə olacaqdı"; *just about to* = "lap indicə"; *on the point of* = rəsmi,
> "bir addımlığında". Çox vaxt *when + keçmiş* ilə kəsilir.

---

## E4 · The three faces of *was to* / *was to*-nun üç mənası
```
 (1) DESTINY / NARRATIVE FACT (formal, biography/history):
     "He was to become the country's first president." (= and he did)
     "It was a decision she was to regret for years."

 (2) OFFICIAL ARRANGEMENT (a fixed plan, often in the past):
     "The delegation was to arrive on Tuesday."
     "The ceremony was to take place at noon."

 (3) INSTRUCTION / OBLIGATION (orders given in the past):
     "We were told we were to report at 8 a.m."  (= had to)

 UNFULFILLED (the plan failed) → was/were to HAVE + V3:
     "The talks were to have begun on Monday (but they were postponed)."
     "She was to have sung, but she fell ill."
```
> 🇦🇿 *was to* = (1) qismət/sonradan olan fakt, (2) rəsmi razılaşma, (3) əmr. *was to have + V3* =
> "olmalıydı, amma olmadı".

---

## E5 · ⚠️ *would* = future-in-past **vs** *would* = past habit / İki ayrı "would"
A crucial confusion. The **same word** *would* has two unrelated jobs:
```
   FUTURE-IN-THE-PAST:   "He left in 1990. He would return ten years later."
                         (= a later event, seen from the past — THIS chapter)

   PAST HABIT (= used to):"Every summer we would go to the lake."
                         (= repeated past routine — Chapter 23 territory)
```
How to tell them apart:
```
   • Habitual would needs a REPEATED/frequency cue (every day, often, as a child, on Fridays)
     and describes a ROUTINE. It can be replaced by "used to".
   • Future-in-past would describes ONE later event and CANNOT become "used to".
     ✅ "He would later become king."  ❌ "He used to later become king."
```
> 🇦🇿 Eyni söz *would*, iki iş: (1) keçmişdən gələcək (bu fəsil), (2) keçmiş adət (*used to* ilə əvəz
> olunur — Ch.23). Təkrarlanma sözü varsa → adət; tək hadisədirsə → keçmişdəki gələcək.

---

## E6 · *would be V-ing* & *would have V3* — the continuous/perfect twins / Davam & bitmiş
```
   would be + V-ing  (future-continuous-in-the-past = action IN PROGRESS later)
     "By noon the next day, he would be flying over the Atlantic."
     "She told me that this time tomorrow she would be sitting an exam."

   would have + V3   (future-perfect-in-the-past = COMPLETED by a later point)
     "He calculated that by Friday he would have saved enough."
     "They assured us the repairs would have finished by the weekend."
```
> 🔍 These are simply *will be V-ing* / *will have V3* with the viewpoint moved to the past.
> 🇦🇿 Sadəcə *will be V-ing* / *will have V3*-ün keçmiş baxışlı variantları.

---

## E7 · Real *would* vs unreal (conditional) *would* / Real və qeyri-real "would"
```
   REAL future-in-the-past (THIS chapter) — it actually refers to a real later time:
     "He promised he would come."   (a real promise about a real future)

   UNREAL / hypothetical would (2nd & 3rd conditionals — Ch.24):
     "If he had time, he would come."        (imaginary present/future)
     "If he had asked, I would have helped."  (imaginary past)
```
> 🔑 Test: is there an **if / imaginary** frame? → conditional. Is it a **real plan/promise reported
> from the past**? → future-in-the-past. 🇦🇿 *if* / xəyali çərçivə varsa → şərt; real vəd/plandırsa →
> keçmişdəki gələcək.

---

## E8 · Backshift exceptions — when *will* STAYS *will* / *will*-in qaldığı hallar
In reported speech, *will* usually → *would*, **but not always** (full detail in Ch. 25):
```
   • Still-future / still-true fact:
     "The sun rises in the east." → He said the sun RISES in the east. (general truth, no shift)
   • Reporting verb in the PRESENT:
     "I'll be late." → She says she WILL be late. (present reporting → no backshift)
   • The future is STILL ahead at the time of reporting (optional shift):
     "I'll see you tomorrow." (reported same day) → He said he WILL/WOULD see me tomorrow.
```
> 🇦🇿 Əgər hadisə hələ də gələcəkdədirsə və ya ümumi həqiqətdirsə, *will* qala bilər. Adətən, keçmiş
> baxışda *would* olur.

---

## E9 · *was supposed to* vs *was going to* vs *was meant to* / İncə fərqlər
| Form | Meaning | 🇦🇿 |
|------|---------|-----|
| was **going to** | personal **intention** (often unfulfilled) | niyyət vardı |
| was **supposed to** | **obligation/expectation** set by someone/rules | gözlənilirdi / borcluydu |
| was **meant to** | **intended/expected** (≈ supposed to, slightly softer) | nəzərdə tutulmuşdu |
| had **intended to** | formal prior intention | əvvəlcədən niyyət etmişdi |
```
   "I was going to clean it."      → my own plan
   "I was supposed to clean it."   → it was my duty / I was expected to (and didn't)
   "The film was meant to be good." → people expected it to be
```
> 🇦🇿 *supposed to* = borc/gözlənti; *going to* = şəxsi niyyət; *meant to* = nəzərdə tutulan.

---

## E10 · Dramatic / literary narrative *would* / Ədəbi, dramatik "would"
Writers use bare *would* to **foreshadow** — to hint at fate before it arrives:
```
   "He boarded the train that morning. He would never see the city again."
   "Little did she know, the letter would change her life."
   "It was a small choice. It would haunt him for decades."
```
> ✍️ Effect: inevitability + suspense. Often paired with *"little did (s)he know…"* (inversion).
> 🇦🇿 Yazıçılar *would* ilə taleyi əvvəlcədən işarə edir — qaçılmazlıq və gərginlik yaradır.

---

## E11 · Questions & negatives / Sual və inkar
```
   QUESTIONS (mostly in reported/embedded form):
     "Was she going to help?"        "Were they about to leave?"
     "I asked whether he would come." "Did you know it would rain?"

   NEGATIVES:
     "He said he wouldn't be late."         (would not)
     "We weren't going to stay anyway."     (were not going to)
     "I wasn't about to give up."           (= I had no intention of — emphatic!)
```
> 🌟 *"I wasn't about to + V1"* is idiomatic = **"there was no way I would"** (firm refusal).
> 🇦🇿 *"I wasn't about to…"* = "heç cür … edən deyildim" (qətiyyətli inkar).

---

## E12 · AZ contrastive synthesis / Sintez
| 🇦🇿 | ❌ word-for-word | ✅ natural English | Key |
|-----|------------------|--------------------|-----|
| O dedi ki, gələcək. | He said he will come. | He said he **would** come. | backshift |
| Zəng edəcəkdim, amma unutdum. | I would call you, but I forgot. | I **was going to** call, but I forgot. | broken plan |
| Çıxmaq üzrəydim ki, zəng gəldi. | I was about to leaving… | I **was about to leave** when it rang. | about to + V1 |
| Hər yay gölə gedərdik. | every summer we would-future go | Every summer we **would/used to** go… | habitual would (Ch.23) |
| O, sonra prezident olacaqdı. | he will later become… | He **would** later become president. | narrative would |
| Bitirməliydi, amma bitmədi. | it was to finish (but no) | It **was to have** finished. | unfulfilled was to |

```
   ONE-LINE SYNTHESIS:
   reported promise → would   ·   broken plan → was going to   ·   imminent (interrupted) → was about to
   destiny/formal → was to   ·   failed formal plan → was to have V3
   one later event → narrative would   ·   repeated routine → habitual would/used to (different!)
```

## 10) Minimal pairs — same words, different meaning / cüt nümunələr
```
 a) "He would help."           (reported promise — neutral)
    "He was going to help."     (intended — but probably didn't)
    "He was about to help."     (was on the very point of it)

 b) "Every summer we would swim here."   (PAST HABIT — = used to)
    "Years later he would swim the Channel." (FUTURE-IN-PAST — one later event)

 c) "He would come if he could."         (UNREAL — conditional, Ch.24)
    "He said he would come."             (REAL — future-in-the-past)

 d) "I was going to cook."               (my intention)
    "I was supposed to cook."            (my duty / it was expected)
```

# 📖 Mini Story #2 — *"The Penalty"* (≈250 words)

Ninety minutes were up. The score was level, and Rashad, the substitute keeper, had no idea this
moment **would define** his whole career.

He **wasn't even supposed to** be playing. The first-choice keeper **was going to** start, but he'd
pulled a muscle in the warm-up. So Rashad **was** suddenly **standing** between the posts in a
penalty shoot-out he **was to remember** for the rest of his life.

The coach had told him only one thing: "Whatever happens, you **would be** ready." Rashad had nodded,
though inside he **was about to** be sick with nerves.

Four penalties each. By the fifth, the teams **were** still **level**. One save now and his side
**would have won** the cup.

The striker placed the ball. Rashad **was going to** dive left — that was the plan, the way they'd
studied the videos. But something stopped him. *He'll go right*, he thought. *This time he**'ll be
aiming** for the right corner.*

The whistle blew. The striker ran up. Rashad **was just about to** commit to the left — then threw
himself the other way.

The ball smacked into his gloves.

The stadium erupted. Teammates piled on top of him. Years later, pundits would say that single
guess **was to become** the turning point of his life: the boy who **wasn't going to** play at all
**would go on** to keep goal for the national team.

"I had no idea," he always laughed, "that one dive **would change** everything."

## 🏁 End of Chapter 17

You can now describe the **future as it looked from a moment in the past** — the language of
**stories, memories and reported speech**. The master move is simple: **shift every future form back
one step** — *will → would*, *be going to → was/were going to*, *V-ing → was/were V-ing*, *will be
V-ing → would be V-ing*, *will have V3 → would have V3* — plus **was about to** (imminent) and **was
to** (destiny/formal). You also kept the four standard sections (level differences, native insight,
alternatives, deep-analysis tasks). 🕰️

```
 ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ FUTURE IN THE PAST = the future seen from a past viewpoint.           │
 │ will→would · going to→was going to · V-ing→was V-ing · V1→V2          │
 │ will be V-ing→would be V-ing · will have V3→would have V3             │
 │ was about to (imminent) · was to (destiny/formal) · was to have (failed)│
 │ ⚠️ "would HAVE", never "would of".   It is NOT the 2nd conditional.   │
 └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
