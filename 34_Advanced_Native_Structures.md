# 🟪 PART 5 · Chapter 34
# Advanced Native Structures
### *be about/due/likely/bound to · no sooner…than · inversion · cleft sentences* — "The train **is due to** arrive." / "**No sooner had** I sat down **than**…" ✨

> **The one-line idea.** These are the structures that make English sound **fluent and native** rather than merely correct: the **be + to-infinitive** family (*about to, due to, likely to, bound to*), **inversion** after negative words (*Never had I…*), and **cleft sentences** that spotlight one part (*It was Sara who…*).
> They add **precision, emphasis and polish** — the difference between B1 and C1.
> 🇦🇿 *Bunlar İngiliscəni sadəcə düzgün deyil, **axıcı və doğma** səsləndirən quruluşlardır: **be + to-məsdər** ailəsi, mənfi sözlərdən sonra **inversiya**, və bir hissəni vurğulayan **cleft** cümlələr.*
> 🇦🇿 *Onlar dəqiqlik, vurğu və cila qatır — B1 ilə C1 arasındakı fərq.*

## 🧭 THE BIG PICTURE — three routes to "sounding native"

Beyond correct grammar, advanced English uses three moves to add nuance and emphasis:

```
   ① BE + TO-INFINITIVE  →  precise future & probability
        "The plane is DUE TO land." "It's LIKELY TO rain." "He's BOUND TO win."

   ② INVERSION           →  formal/dramatic emphasis (auxiliary jumps before the subject)
        "NEVER HAD I seen such a mess."  (= I had never seen…)

   ③ CLEFT SENTENCES     →  spotlight one element
        "It was SARA who solved it."  "What I need is TIME."
```

> 🧠 **The key mental move:** these are **choices for effect**, not new tenses. You already know the ideas — now you're learning to **package** them for precision (*be to*), **drama/formality** (inversion), or **focus** (clefts). Use them deliberately; overuse sounds artificial.
> 🇦🇿 *Əsas fikir: bunlar yeni zamanlar deyil, **effekt üçün seçimlərdir**. Fikirləri artıq bilirsən — indi onları dəqiqlik, dram/rəsmiyyət və ya diqqət üçün "qablaşdırırsan".*

## 📋 THE FORMS — the *be + to-infinitive* family

| Structure | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **be about to** | on the point of (very near future) | We'**re about to** leave. |
| **be (just) about to** | imminent | The show'**s just about to** start. |
| **be due to** | scheduled / expected at a time | The train **is due to** arrive at six. |
| **be to** | formal arrangement / instruction / fate | The minister **is to** visit Baku. / You **are to** wait here. |
| **be likely / unlikely to** | (im)probability | Prices **are likely to** rise. |
| **be bound to** | certainty / inevitability | She'**s bound to** pass. |
| **be sure / certain to** | confident prediction | They'**re sure to** win. |
| **be set to** | poised / ready to | The team **is set to** break the record. |
| **be meant / supposed to** | expectation *(Ch.21)* | You'**re meant to** knock first. |
| **be on the verge / point of + -ing** | about to (dramatic) | He was **on the verge of** giving up. |

> ⚠️ All take **to + base verb**, except *be on the verge/point of* which takes **-ing**: *"on the point of **leaving**."* 🇦🇿 *Hamısı "to + fel" alır; yalnız "on the verge/point of" -ing alır.*

## 🔑 THE CORE — precise futures & probabilities

```
   IMMINENT   be about to      "I'm about to call you."      (any second now)
   SCHEDULED  be due to        "It's due to open at nine."   (per timetable)
   FORMAL     be to            "Talks are to begin Monday."  (official / news)
   PROBABLE   be likely to     "It's likely to snow."        (~probable)
   CERTAIN    be bound to      "You're bound to love it."    (surely)
```

| Nuance | Example | 🇦🇿 |
|---|---|---|
| about to (just now) | The kettle'**s about to** boil. | Çaydan qaynamaq üzrədir. |
| due to (timetabled) | Flight BA12 **is due to** depart at 14:00. | Uçuş saat 14:00-da yola düşməlidir. |
| be to (arrangement/order) | You **are to** report to the manager. | Menecerə hesabat verməlisən. |
| likely to (probable) | Sales **are likely to** fall. | Satışlar düşə bilər (ehtimal). |
| bound to (inevitable) | With that talent, she'**s bound to** succeed. | O istedadla mütləq uğur qazanacaq. |

> 🔗 *be meant to / be supposed to* belong here too but were covered as **expectation** in **Chapter 21**. *be to* for the future is common in **news and formal writing**: *"The President is to address the nation."* 🇦🇿 *"be to" xəbərlərdə və rəsmi yazıda gələcək üçün geniş işlənir.*

> 🕳️ **be to for unfulfilled past / destiny:** *"He was **to become** the greatest player of his age"* (fate, looking back); *"She was **to have arrived** yesterday"* (but didn't). Advanced/literary. 🇦🇿 *"was to" — keçmişdən baxanda tale və ya yerinə yetməmiş plan.*

## 🔄 INVERSION after negative & limiting adverbials

When a **negative or limiting adverbial** is moved to the **front** for emphasis, the clause **inverts** like a question — **auxiliary before subject**:

```
   NORMAL:    "I had never seen such chaos."
   INVERTED:  "NEVER HAD I seen such chaos."     ← negative adverb fronted → had + I
```

| Fronted adverbial | Inverted example |
|---|---|
| **Never (before)** | **Never had I** felt so proud. |
| **Rarely / Seldom** | **Seldom do we** see such talent. |
| **Hardly ever** | **Hardly ever does he** complain. |
| **Not only** … (but also) | **Not only did she** sing, but she also danced. |
| **Not until** … | **Not until** midnight **did they** finish. |
| **Only then / only after / only when** | **Only then did I** understand. |
| **Little** (= not at all) | **Little did he** know what awaited. |
| **Under no circumstances** | **Under no circumstances should you** open it. |
| **No sooner** … / **Hardly** … | **No sooner had** we left **than** it rained. |

> 🔑 The rule: front a **negative/limiting** adverbial → then **auxiliary + subject** (add *do/does/did* if there's no other auxiliary): *"Rarely **do I** eat out."* 🇦🇿 *Mənfi/məhdudlaşdırıcı zərf öndə → **köməkçi fel + mübtəda** (operator yoxdursa, do/does/did əlavə et).*

> ⚠️ Only for **emphasis/formality** — it's not required. Overusing inversion sounds theatrical. 🇦🇿 *Yalnız vurğu/rəsmiyyət üçün — məcburi deyil; həddən çox işlətmək teatral səslənir.*

## ⏱️ *no sooner … than* · *hardly/scarcely … when* — "immediately after"

These express **one event happening right after another**, usually with **past perfect + inversion**:

```
   No sooner  HAD I  arrived  THAN  the phone rang.
   Hardly     HAD I  arrived  WHEN  the phone rang.
   Scarcely   HAD I  arrived  WHEN  the phone rang.
```

| Structure | Example |
|---|---|
| **No sooner had** … **than** … | **No sooner had** she sat down **than** the doorbell rang. |
| **Hardly had** … **when** … | **Hardly had** we started **when** it began to rain. |
| **Scarcely had** … **when** … | **Scarcely had** he spoken **when** the crowd cheered. |

> ⚠️ **Pairing traps:** *no sooner* pairs with ***than*** (not ~~when~~); *hardly/scarcely* pair with ***when*** (not ~~than~~). The first verb is usually **past perfect**, the second **past simple**. 🇦🇿 *"no sooner" → **than**; "hardly/scarcely" → **when**. Birinci fel past perfect, ikincisi past simple.*

> 🗣️ Without inversion (informal): *"I had **no sooner** arrived **than** the phone rang"* / *"As soon as I arrived, the phone rang."* 🇦🇿 *İnversiyasız (qeyri-rəsmi): "As soon as…" ilə demək olar.*

## 💎 CLEFT sentences — spotlighting one element

A cleft splits one sentence into two parts to **emphasise** a chosen element:

```
   plain:     "Sara solved the puzzle."
   IT-CLEFT:  "It was SARA who solved the puzzle."      (emphasis on Sara)
              "It was THE PUZZLE that Sara solved."     (emphasis on the puzzle)
   WH-CLEFT:  "What Sara solved was THE PUZZLE."         (emphasis at the end)
              "What I need is TIME."                     ( = the thing I need)
```

| Type | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **It-cleft** | It + be + X + who/that … | **It was** in Baku **that** we met. |
| **Wh-cleft** (pseudo) | What … + be + X | **What** matters **is** honesty. |
| **All-cleft** | All (that) … + be + X | **All I want is** a quiet life. |
| **The thing / reason … is** | The reason (why) … is | **The reason** I called **is** to apologise. |

> 🔑 Clefts move the **focus** to where you want it. *It-clefts* front the emphasised word; *Wh-clefts* usually save it for the **end** (the natural stress position). 🇦🇿 *Cleft cümlələr diqqəti istədiyin sözə yönəldir: it-cleft öndə vurğulayır, wh-cleft sonda.*

> 🗣️ Very common in speech for correcting or insisting: *"**It wasn't** me **who** broke it!"* / *"**What** actually happened **was** this…"* 🇦🇿 *Danışıqda düzəliş/təkid üçün çox işlənir.*

## ✊ EMPHATIC *do* & FRONTING

| Device | Example | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **emphatic do/does/did** | I **do** like it! / She **does** try hard. / We **did** win. | insists / contradicts |
| **do** in imperatives | **Do** sit down. / **Do** be quiet. | polite emphasis |
| **fronting an adverbial** | **Down came** the rain. / **Here comes** the bus. | vivid / literary |
| **so/such + inversion** | **So beautiful was** the view **that**… / **Such was** his anger **that**… | dramatic result |
| **negative fronting** | **Not a word did** she say. | strong emphasis |

> 🔑 Emphatic *do* adds stress to an ordinary verb: *"I told you!"* → *"I **did** tell you!"* With *here/there* + verb of motion, the subject follows the verb — **but** a pronoun subject stays before: *"Here **comes the bus**"* vs *"Here **it comes**."* 🇦🇿 *Vurğulu "do" adi felə təkid qatır; "Here comes the bus" (isim) amma "Here it comes" (əvəzlik).*

## 🔁 POLISHING *would rather · had better · it's (high) time* (recap + native use)

You met these in **Ch.21** (advice) and **Ch.29** (unreal). Native, advanced touches:

| Structure | Native use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **would rather** + past (other) | preference about someone else | I'd rather you **didn't** mention it. |
| **would (just) as soon** | = would rather (idiomatic) | I'd **just as soon** stay home. |
| **had better** + bare verb | urgent advice/warning | You'**d better** not be late. |
| **it's (high/about) time** + past | overdue | It's **high time** we **left**. |
| **would sooner** … **than** | strong preference | I'd **sooner** walk **than** wait. |

> 🇦🇿 *"would rather + keçmiş" (başqasının hərəkəti), "would just as soon" (= would rather), "it's high time + keçmiş" (artıq vaxtdır) — Ch.21 və Ch.29-un doğma incəlikləri.*

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Why |
|---|---|---|

## 🅰️ §A — LEVEL DIFFERENCES (A1 → C2)

| Level | What the learner can do | Typical sentence |
|---|---|---|
| **A1–A2** | *(not yet — basic futures only)* | "It's going to rain." |
| **B1** | *be about to*; basic *it-cleft* | "I'm **about to** leave." / "It was **John** who called." |
| **B2** | *be due/likely/bound to*; *what*-cleft | "It's **likely to** rain." / "**What** I need **is** rest." |
| **C1** | inversion after negatives; *no sooner…than* | "**Never had I**…" / "**No sooner had** we…" |
| **C2** | full range: *be to* (fate), *such/so* inversion, emphatic fronting | "He **was to become** a legend." / "**Such was** the noise **that**…" |

> 🇦🇿 *B1: be about to, sadə it-cleft. B2: be due/likely/bound to, what-cleft. C1: mənfi inversiya, no sooner…than. C2: be to (tale), so/such inversiya, fronting.*

## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT

1. **"about to" is everyday; inversion is not.** Natives say *"I'm about to…"* constantly, but reserve *"Never had I…"* for writing, speeches or drama. 🇦🇿 *"about to" gündəlik; inversiya yazı/nitq üçün.*
2. **Clefts are conversational tools.** *"It was you who said it!"* / *"What I meant was…"* — natives use clefts to correct, insist and clarify. 🇦🇿 *Cleft-lər söhbətdə düzəliş/təkid alətidir.*
3. **Emphatic "do" answers doubt.** *"You never call." — "I DO call!"* It stresses truth against contradiction. 🇦🇿 *Vurğulu "do" şübhəyə/inkara qarşı həqiqəti vurğulayır.*
4. **"due to" = scheduled, not just "because".** *"The train's due to leave"* (timetable) differs from *"delayed due to snow"* (cause, Ch.33). Context decides. 🇦🇿 *"be due to + fel" = cədvəl; "due to + isim" = səbəb (Ch.33).*
5. **"bound to" is confident, "likely to" is cautious.** *"He's bound to win"* (I'm sure) vs *"likely to win"* (probably). 🇦🇿 *"bound to" = əminəm; "likely to" = ehtimal.*
6. **Inversion signals register instantly.** One *"Rarely does one see…"* lifts a whole paragraph into formal English. 🇦🇿 *Bir inversiya bütün mətni rəsmi registrə qaldırır.*

## 🅲 §C — ALTERNATIVE EXPRESSIONS (say it another way)

| Instead of… | You can say… | Register |
|---|---|---|
| It's **about to** rain. | It's **on the point of** raining. / Any minute now it'll rain. | formal / casual |
| He's **bound to** win. | He's **sure/certain to** win. / He'll **definitely** win. | neutral |
| **Never had I** seen it. | I'd **never** seen it (before). | plain |
| **No sooner had** we left **than**… | **As soon as** we left, … / The moment we left, … | neutral |
| **It was Sara who** called. | **Sara** was the one who called. / **Sara** called (stressed). | plain |
| **What I need is** rest. | I **just need** rest. / **All I need is** rest. | casual |

> 🇦🇿 *Eyni vurğunu daha sadə formalarla da vermək olar — inversiya/cleft rəsmiyyət və dram qatır.*

## 🅳 §D — DEEP-ANALYSIS TASKS

```
1. BE + TO family: choose about to / due to / likely to / bound to:
     a) "The meeting ___ start in two minutes." (imminent)
     b) "The 9:15 ___ arrive on platform 3." (timetable)
     c) "With no revision, he's ___ fail." (certain)
     d) "Given the clouds, it's ___ rain." (probable)

2. INVERT for emphasis (front the negative adverbial):
     a) "I had never tasted such coffee." → Never ___
     b) "She not only sang but also played." → Not only ___
     c) "We understood only then." → Only then ___

3. NO SOONER / HARDLY: combine with inversion + correct pair-word:
     a) "We sat down. The phone rang." (no sooner) → ___
     b) "I closed my eyes. Someone knocked." (hardly) → ___

4. CLEFT it: emphasise the underlined part:
     a) "SARA broke the vase." → It ___
     b) "We met IN PARIS." → It ___

5. CLEFT wh-: rewrite for end-focus:
     a) "I need time." → What ___
     b) "Her honesty impresses me." → What ___

6. 🇦🇿→EN: "Qatar saat altıda gəlməlidir." (be due to) / "Mütləq udacaq." (be bound to) /
     "Onu məhz Sara həll etdi." (it-cleft) — translate and identify the structure.
```

# 📖 MINI STORY #1 — "The Award Night"

### *be due to, be bound to, inversion and clefts — one dramatic evening at the ceremony.*
### 🇦🇿 *be due to, be bound to, inversiya və cleft-lər — mərasimdə bir dramatik axşam.*

---

The ceremony **was due to** begin at eight. *(scheduled.)* Backstage, Leyla was **about to** panic. *(imminent.)*

"Relax," said her director. "With a film this good, you'**re bound to** win." *(certainty.)*

"**Never have I** been so nervous," she admitted. *(inversion — emphasis.)*

**No sooner had** the lights dimmed **than** the host walked out. *(no sooner…than.)* **Hardly had** he finished the introduction **when** the first award was announced.

It wasn't hers. **Not only did** she smile, **but** she also clapped for the winner. *(inversion.)*

Then came the big category. "And the award goes to…" The host paused. "**It was** Leyla **who** had waited years for this moment," she would say later — and now her name filled the hall. *(it-cleft.)*

**Little did she know** she'd forget every word of her speech. *(inversion — "not at all did she know".)*

At the microphone, she steadied herself. "**What I want to say is** simply — thank you." *(wh-cleft.)* "This film **is** the reason I never gave up. And it **was** my team **who** made it possible." *(it-cleft.)*

The applause was **such that** she could barely hear herself. *(such + that — dramatic result.)*

Later, her director grinned. "I told you you'd win."

"You **did**," she laughed. *(emphatic do.)* "You really **did**."

### ✍️ After-reading tasks
```
1. Find every inversion. Rewrite each in normal (uninverted) word order.
2. "No sooner had the lights dimmed than…" — why 'than' and not 'when'? Which verb is past perfect?
3. Turn "It was my team who made it possible" into a plain sentence, then into a wh-cleft.
4. Rewrite "you're bound to win" with 'likely' — how does the certainty change?
5. 🇦🇿→EN: "Mərasim səkkizdə başlamalı idi." / "Mütləq qalib gələcəksən." / "Onu məhz mənim komandam mümkün etdi."
```
> 💬 **Answers**
> 1. *"Never have I been so nervous"* → "I have never been so nervous." · *"No sooner had the lights dimmed than…"* → "The lights had no sooner dimmed than…" / "As soon as the lights dimmed, …" · *"Not only did she smile, but…"* → "She not only smiled, but…" · *"Little did she know"* → "She didn't know at all." (Plus *"Hardly had he finished when…"* → "He had hardly finished when…")
> 2. *No sooner* always pairs with **than**; the first verb ("had dimmed") is **past perfect**, the second ("walked") past simple.
> 3. Plain: *"My team made it possible."* Wh-cleft: *"What made it possible was my team."*
> 4. *"You're likely to win"* = probably (cautious); *"bound to win"* = surely/inevitably (confident). *Likely* leaves room for doubt.
> 5. The ceremony was due to begin at eight. / You're bound to win. / It was my team who made it possible.

# 🧨 EXPANSION PACK — Advanced Native Structures (E1–E12)
### *The deep layer: the be+to family, inversion in full, conditional inversion, clefts, emphatic do & register.*

## 🅴1 — The *be + to-infinitive* family map

```
   NOW ────────────────────────────────────────────────────► FUTURE
   about to        due to          be to           likely/bound to
   (any second)    (timetabled)    (formal/fated)  (probability/certainty)
   "about to go"   "due to arrive" "is to visit"   "likely/bound to rain"
```

| Structure | Certainty/Timing | Register |
|---|---|---|
| be **about to** | imminent | neutral |
| be **due to** | scheduled | neutral/formal |
| be **to** | official / arranged / destined | formal / news |
| be **likely to** | probable | neutral |
| be **bound / sure / certain to** | (near) certain | neutral |
| be **set to** | poised | journalistic |
| be **on the verge/point of** + -ing | about to (dramatic) | formal/literary |

> 🇦🇿 *Bu ailə gələcəyi və ehtimalını dəqiqləşdirir: about to (indicə), due to (cədvəl), be to (rəsmi/tale), likely/bound to (ehtimal/qətiyyət).*

---

## 🅴2 — *be to* in depth (formal, orders, plans, fate)

| Use | Example |
|---|---|
| official arrangement / news | The President **is to** visit Baku next week. |
| instruction / order | You **are to** report at 9 a.m. sharp. |
| prohibition (negative) | You **are not to** leave the building. |
| plan in headlines | *Talks **to** resume Monday* |
| destiny (past, looking back) | He **was to become** a legend. |
| unfulfilled plan | She **was to have arrived** yesterday *(but didn't)*. |

> 🔑 *be to* is heavy with **authority or destiny** — common in news, rules and formal writing; rarer in casual speech. 🇦🇿 *"be to" səlahiyyət və ya tale çaları daşıyır — xəbər, qayda və rəsmi yazıda çox işlənir.*

## 🅴3 — INVERSION after negative/limiting adverbials (the full set)

Front the adverbial → **auxiliary + subject** (insert *do/does/did* if needed):

| Fronted | Inverted |
|---|---|
| Never (before) | **Never had** I seen… |
| Rarely / Seldom | **Rarely does** one meet… |
| Hardly ever | **Hardly ever do** they argue. |
| Little | **Little did** we realise… |
| Not only … | **Not only was** he late, but… |
| Not until … | **Not until** dawn **did** we sleep. |
| Only then / after / by … | **Only by** working hard **did** she succeed. |
| No sooner … / Hardly … | **No sooner had** … |
| Under no circumstances | **Under no circumstances must** you… |
| At no time / In no way / On no account | **At no time did** he lie. |
| Nowhere | **Nowhere will** you find better. |

> ⚠️ Inversion needs a **fronted negative/limiting** trigger. A positive adverb doesn't invert: ~~Often do I go~~ → *Often I go / I often go.* 🇦🇿 *İnversiya öndəki **mənfi/məhdudlaşdırıcı** söz tələb edir; müsbət zərf inversiya yaratmır.*

---

## 🅴4 — *no sooner … than* / *hardly · scarcely … when*

```
   No sooner   HAD + subject + V3   THAN   past simple.
   Hardly      HAD + subject + V3   WHEN   past simple.
   Scarcely    HAD + subject + V3   WHEN   past simple.
```

| ✅ Correct | ❌ Wrong |
|---|---|
| No sooner had I left **than** it rained. | ~~No sooner had I left **when** it rained.~~ |
| Hardly had we arrived **when** it started. | ~~Hardly had we arrived **than** it started.~~ |

> 🔑 Memory hook: *no soon**er** … th**an*** (both have the comparative feel); *hardly/scarcely … **when*** (time word). First clause **past perfect**, second **past simple**. 🇦🇿 *"no sooner…than" (müqayisə), "hardly/scarcely…when" (zaman); birinci past perfect, ikinci past simple.*

## 🅴5 — CONDITIONAL inversion (drop the *if*) — cross-ref Ch.24

Formal conditionals can **drop *if*** and invert instead:

| With *if* | Inverted (no *if*) |
|---|---|
| If I **were** you… | **Were I** you… |
| If he **had known**… | **Had he known**… |
| If you **should** need help… | **Should you** need help… |
| If it **were not** for… | **Were it not** for… |
| If I **had not** left… | **Had I not** left… |

> 🔑 Only **were / had / should** invert this way. It sounds **formal/literary**: *"**Should you** require assistance, call reception."* Full conditionals → **Ch.24**. 🇦🇿 *Yalnız were/had/should belə inversiya edir — rəsmi/ədəbi səslənir. Tam şərt cümlələri Ch.24-də.*

## 🅴6 — IT-CLEFTS (what you can spotlight)

*It + be + X + who/that/when/where …* — X can be almost any element **except the main verb**:

| Emphasis on | Example |
|---|---|
| subject (person) | **It was Sara who** called. |
| subject (thing) | **It was the noise that** woke me. |
| object | **It was this book that** I meant. |
| time | **It was in 2020 that** we met. |
| place | **It was here that** it happened. |
| reason (with *because*) | **It was because** he lied **that** I left. |

> ⚠️ You can't cleft the verb itself: ~~It was called that Sara…~~ For verbs, use **emphatic do** (*"She DID call"*) or a wh-cleft (*"What she did was call"*). 🇦🇿 *Feli birbaşa cleft etmək olmaz — vurğulu "do" və ya wh-cleft işlət.*

---

## 🅴7 — WH-CLEFTS / pseudo-clefts (end-focus)

| Opener | Example |
|---|---|
| **What** … is/was | **What** I need **is** more time. |
| **What** … do is (for actions) | **What** she did **was** (to) resign. |
| **All (that)** … is | **All** I want **is** peace. |
| **The thing (that)** … is | **The thing** I love **is** the view. |
| **The reason (why)** … is | **The reason** I called **is** to say sorry. |
| **The place/day where/when** … | **The day** everything changed **was** … |

> 🔑 Wh-clefts push the key information to the **end** — the natural stress spot in English. Great for building suspense or emphasis. 🇦🇿 *Wh-cleft əsas məlumatı **sona** atır — İngiliscədə təbii vurğu yeri.*

---

## 🅴8 — Emphatic *do* & fronting

| Device | Example | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| emphatic **do/does/did** | I **do** apologise. / He **does** know. | insist / contradict |
| **do** + imperative | **Do** come in. / **Do** be careful. | warm emphasis |
| adverbial fronting + inversion | **Down came** the rain. / **Away they went.** | vivid narrative |
| *here/there* + motion verb | **Here comes** the bus. *(but: Here **it** comes.)* | presentational |
| **So + adj + be + subj + that** | **So great was** the noise **that**… | dramatic |
| **Such + be + subj + that** | **Such was** her joy **that**… | dramatic |

> ⚠️ With a **pronoun** subject, don't invert after *here/there*: *"Here **it comes**,"* not ~~Here comes it~~. 🇦🇿 *Əvəzlik mübtəda ilə "here/there"-dən sonra inversiya yox: "Here it comes".*

## 🅴9 — Near-future & "on the point of" idioms

| Expression | Example |
|---|---|
| be **about to** | I was **about to** call you. |
| be **on the verge of** + -ing | She was **on the verge of** tears. |
| be **on the point of** + -ing | He was **on the point of** leaving. |
| be **just about to** | We're **just about to** eat. |
| be **set to** | The film is **set to** break records. |
| **be on the brink of** + -ing/noun | a country **on the brink of** war |

> 🇦🇿 *Yaxın gələcək idiomları: about to, on the verge/point/brink of (+ -ing/isim) — dramatik "az qalmışdı ki".*

## 🅴10 — Fixed inverted & emphatic expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Little did I know** | I had no idea | **Little did I know** it was a trap. |
| **Not a (single)** … | none at all | **Not a word did** he say. |
| **Nowhere** … | in no place | **Nowhere** have I felt so welcome. |
| **Only time will tell** | we'll see | — |
| **Never in my life** | strongest "never" | **Never in my life have I** been so shocked. |
| **Rarely, if ever** | almost never | She **rarely, if ever,** complains. |

> 🇦🇿 *Hazır inversiya ifadələri: "Little did I know" (heç ağlıma gəlməzdi), "Never in my life" (ömrümdə heç vaxt), "Not a word" (bir kəlmə də).*

## 🅴11 — REGISTER: when to actually use these

| Structure | Best in… | Avoid in… |
|---|---|---|
| **be about to / likely to** | everywhere | — |
| **be to** | news, rules, formal writing | casual chat |
| **inversion** | speeches, essays, literature, drama | everyday small talk (sounds pompous) |
| **it-cleft** | speech & writing (correcting/insisting) | — |
| **wh-cleft** | speech & writing (organising info) | — |
| **conditional inversion** | formal writing, notices | casual chat |

> 🗣️ **Golden rule:** these are **seasoning, not the main dish.** One well-placed inversion impresses; three in a paragraph sound theatrical. 🇦🇿 *Qızıl qayda: bunlar **ədviyyatdır, əsas yemək deyil** — yerində bir inversiya təsir edir, üçü teatral səslənir.*

## 🅴12 — 🇦🇿 CONTRASTIVE SYNTHESIS

| English | 🇦🇿 | The contrast |
|---|---|---|
| The train **is due to** arrive. | Qatar gəlməlidir / gəlişi nəzərdə tutulub. | AZ: -məli / "nəzərdə tutulub" |
| He's **bound to** win. | O, mütləq udacaq. | AZ: **mütləq/hökmən** zərfi |
| **Never had I** seen it. | Bunu heç vaxt görməmişdim. | AZ: **no inversion** — sadə söz sırası |
| **It was Sara who** called. | Zəng edən **məhz Sara** idi. | AZ: **məhz** + söz sırası |
| **What I need is** time. | Ehtiyacım olan şey **vaxtdır**. | AZ: "-an şey … -dır" (sonda) |
| I **do** agree. | Mən **həqiqətən də** razıyam. | AZ: **həqiqətən də / axı** hissəcikləri |

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani contrast in one line:** Azerbaijani has **no auxiliary-inversion and no cleft frame**; it creates the same emphasis with **word order** (moving the focused word) and **emphatic particles** — **məhz** ("precisely"), **elə** ("exactly"), **axı / ki** (insistence), **həqiqətən də** ("really do"). So where English **rearranges the grammar** (fronting + inverting, or *It was … who …*), Azerbaijani **keeps the word order and adds a little word.** The AZ learner must resist translating *məhz* as an adverb and instead reach for a **cleft** (*It was … that*), and must learn that fronting a negative in English **forces** the auxiliary to jump — a move Azerbaijani never makes.

## 📦 BLOCK 11 — ⚡ MINIMAL PAIRS — one change shifts the effect

| Pair | What changes | 🇦🇿 |
|---|---|---|
| It's **likely to** rain / It's **bound to** rain | probable → (near) certain | ehtimal → mütləq |
| **about to** leave / **due to** leave | imminent → scheduled | indicə → cədvəl üzrə |
| I **have never** seen / **Never have I** seen | plain → emphatic (inverted) | sadə → vurğulu |
| **It was Sara who** called / **What Sara did was** call | it-cleft → wh-cleft (verb) | (fərqli fokus) |
| He **called** / He **did** call | plain → emphatic | zəng etdi / (axı) zəng etdi |
| **No sooner had** … **than** / **As soon as** … | formal inverted → neutral | rəsmi → neytral |

> 🔬 Rows 1–2 fine-tune the **be+to** meaning; rows 3, 6 contrast **inverted vs plain** register. → §🔑, §🔄.

## 📦 BLOCK 12 — 🎬 REAL-LIFE — news, speeches & formal writing (decode each one)

| The situation | Structure | 🇦🇿 |
|---|---|---|
| News: "The PM **is to** address parliament." | be to (formal) | Baş nazir parlamentdə çıxış edəcək. |
| Timetable: "Flight 88 **is due to** board at gate 5." | be due to | 88 saylı uçuş 5 nömrəli çıxışdan minəcək. |
| Speech: "**Never have we** faced a greater challenge." | inversion | Heç vaxt bu qədər böyük çətinliklə üzləşməmişik. |
| Story: "**No sooner had** the door closed **than** she wept." | no sooner…than | Qapı bağlanan kimi ağladı. |
| Argument: "**It is precisely this** point **that** they ignore." | it-cleft | Onların gözardı etdiyi məhz bu məqamdır. |
| Essay: "**What is needed is** decisive action." | wh-cleft | Lazım olan qətiyyətli hərəkətdir. |
| Reassurance: "I **do** understand your concern." | emphatic do | Narahatlığınızı həqiqətən başa düşürəm. |

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "The Rescue"

### *be about to, inversion, no sooner…than and clefts — a dramatic night on the mountain.*
### 🇦🇿 *be about to, inversiya, no sooner…than və cleft-lər — dağda dramatik bir gecə.*

> Read it twice. **First** for the story; **second** circling every advanced structure — and note its type.
> 🇦🇿 *İki dəfə oxu: əvvəl hekayə üçün, sonra hər qabaqcıl quruluşu dövrəyə alaraq — növü nədir?*

---

The storm **was due to** hit at midnight, but it came early. *(be due to.)*

Aydın and his team **were about to** turn back **when** the radio crackled. *(be about to.)* A climber was stranded above them.

"**Never had I** heard such fear in a voice," Aydın said later. *(inversion.)* "**No sooner had** we started the climb **than** the snow thickened." *(no sooner…than.)*

**Hardly had** they reached the ridge **when** the wind nearly threw them off. *(hardly…when.)* **Little did they know** the climber had broken his leg. *(inversion — "not at all".)*

"**It was** the youngest member of the team **who** found him," Aydın recalled. *(it-cleft.)* "**What saved him was** the emergency shelter we carried." *(wh-cleft.)*

The descent was brutal. **So exhausted were** they **that** every step was a battle. *(so + adj + inversion + that.)* **Only when** they saw the base camp lights **did** they believe they'd make it. *(only when + inversion.)*

At the bottom, the rescued climber gripped Aydın's hand. "You saved my life."

"We **did** what anyone would," Aydín said quietly. *(emphatic do.)* But everyone knew: **it was** courage, not luck, **that** had brought them all home. *(it-cleft.)*

**Rarely does** a night test people so hard — and rarely do strangers become family so fast. *(inversion.)*

## 🏁 End of Chapter 34

You can now reach for the structures that lift English from correct to **native.** The **be + to-infinitive** family pins down the future and its odds — *about to* (any second), *due to* (scheduled), *be to* (formal/fated), *likely to* (probable), *bound to* (certain). **Inversion** fronts a negative or limiting adverbial and flips in the auxiliary for emphasis — *Never had I…, Not only did…, Only then did…* — with the fixed pairs **no sooner … than** and **hardly/scarcely … when** (past perfect + past simple). **Cleft sentences** spotlight one element — *It was X who…* and *What … is …* — and **emphatic *do*** and **fronting** add punch. Use them for effect, not decoration. ✨

```
 ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ ⏳ be + to:   about to · due to · be to · likely to · bound to (+ base verb)│
 │ 🔄 inversion: fronted negative → AUX + subject  "Never had I…"              │
 │ ⏱️ pairs:     no sooner … THAN · hardly/scarcely … WHEN (past perfect)      │
 │ 💎 it-cleft:  It + be + X + who/that …   "It was Sara who called."          │
 │ 💠 wh-cleft:  What … + be + X            "What I need is time."             │
 │ ✊ emphasis:  do/does/did + verb · fronting ("Down came the rain")          │
 │ ⚠️ be due TO arrive · than≠when · no extra 'it' in wh-clefts               │
 └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
