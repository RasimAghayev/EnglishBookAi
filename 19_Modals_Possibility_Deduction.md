# 🟥 PART 4 · Chapter 19
# Modals (2) — Possibility & Deduction
### *may · might · could · must · can't* — "It **might** rain." / "He **must** be tired." 🔎

> **Azərbaycanca qısa giriş:** Bu fəsildə eyni kiçik sözlər (*may, might, could, must, can't*) **yeni
> iş** görür: nə qədər **əmin** olduğumuzu bildirir. İki ideya: **(1) İMKAN/EHTİMAL** — *"ola bilər"*
> (*It may/might/could rain* = yağış yağa bilər). **(2) NƏTİCƏ ÇIXARMA (deduction)** — məntiqlə
> **əminliklə təxmin** etmək: *"He must be tired"* (yəqin yorğundur — *olmalıdır*) və *"He can't be
> serious"* (ciddi ola bilməz). ⚠️ Diqqət: burada *must* **məcburiyyət deyil**, "yəqin ki"dir; *can't*
> isə **qadağa deyil**, "mümkün deyil"dir. Keçmiş üçün: *must have + V3*, *can't have + V3*, *might
> have + V3*. 🔎

---

## 0. 🔑 Quick recall — modal grammar / Modal qrammatikası (xatırlatma)

Same golden rules as Ch. 18: **no -s · + bare infinitive (V1) · no do/does/did · never two modals.**
The new twist here is the **past form: modal + have + V3** (*must have gone, might have left*).
🇦🇿 Yeni element: keçmiş üçün **modal + have + V3**.

---

## 1. 🧭 Introduction / Giriş

These modals show **how sure we are** — they place a guess somewhere on a **certainty scale**:

```
   100% YES  ──────────────────────────────────────────────  100% NO
   must        could / may / might         might not / may not        can't / couldn't
   (sure it is)  (it's possible)            (possibly not)             (sure it isn't)
   "He must     "He could/may/might         "He might not              "He can't
    be home."    be home."                   be home."                  be home."
```
🇦🇿 Bu modallar **əminlik dərəcəsini** göstərir — təxmini "100% bəli"dən "100% xeyr"ə qədər bir
**şkalaya** yerləşdirir.

> 🔑 **Two jobs:** **possibility** (*may/might/could* = it's possible) and **deduction** (*must* = I'm
> sure yes; *can't* = I'm sure no). 🇦🇿 İki iş: imkan və məntiqi nəticə.

---

## 2. ❓ Why do we use them? / Niyə işlədirik?

| Meaning | Modal | Example | 🇦🇿 |
|---------|-------|---------|-----|
| Sure (positive deduction) | **must** | She **must** be exhausted. | Yəqin çox yorğundur. |
| Possible | **may/might/could** | It **might** snow tonight. | Bu gecə qar yağa bilər. |
| Possibly not | **may/might not** | He **may not** know yet. | Hələ bilməyə bilər. |
| Sure (negative deduction) | **can't** | That **can't** be true. | Bu doğru ola bilməz. |
| Past — sure (positive) | **must have + V3** | They **must have** left. | Yəqin getmişlər. |
| Past — possible | **might/may/could have + V3** | She **may have** forgotten. | Unutmuş ola bilər. |
| Past — sure (negative) | **can't/couldn't have + V3** | He **can't have** seen us. | Bizi görmüş ola bilməz. |

## 3. 🌍 Real-life situations / Həyati vəziyyətlər
```
   • Guessing from evidence:   "The lights are off — they must be out."
   • Weather/plans:            "It might rain, so take an umbrella."
   • Reacting to news:         "You passed? That can't be true — amazing!"
   • Explaining the past:      "He's late; he must have missed the bus."
   • Hedging uncertainty:      "She may not have got my message."
   • Detective reasoning:      "The window's broken — someone must have climbed in."
```

## 4. 📏 The Certainty Scale / Əminlik şkalası
```
        SURE YES        PROBABLE      50/50 POSSIBLE     PROBABLY NOT     SURE NO
   ┌──────────┬──────────────┬───────────────────┬──────────────┬──────────────┐
   │  must     │ should* /     │ may / might /     │ might not /   │ can't /       │
   │           │ ought to      │ could             │ may not       │ couldn't      │
   │ "must be" │ "should be"   │ "might be"        │ "may not be"  │ "can't be"    │
   └──────────┴──────────────┴───────────────────┴──────────────┴──────────────┘
   (*should = "expected/probable" — covered with advice in Ch. 21, but it lives on this scale too.)
```
> ⚠️ **Negative of *must* (deduction) is NOT "mustn't"!** The opposite of *"must be"* (sure yes) is
> *"can't be"* (sure no). *mustn't* = prohibition (Ch. 20). 🇦🇿 *must*-ın əksi *can't*-dır, *mustn't*
> deyil!

---

## 5. 🏗️ Formula / Formul
```
 PRESENT / FUTURE (about now or later)
   must / may / might / could / can't + V1        He must be home.   It might rain.
   continuous:  modal + be + V-ing                He must be sleeping.  She might be working.

 PAST (about earlier)
   must / may / might / could / can't + have + V3  They must have left.  She may have forgotten.
   continuous:  modal + have been + V-ing          He must have been waiting for hours.

 NEGATIVES
   may not / might not + V1/have V3                He might not know.  She may not have heard.
   can't / couldn't + V1/have V3                   That can't be true.  He can't have seen us.
```
> ⚠️ Spoken: *must've, might've, could've, can't have* /ˈmʌstəv, ˈmaɪtəv, ˈkʊdəv, ˈkɑːntəv/.
> NEVER write "must of". 🇦🇿 *have*-i "of" kimi yazma!

## 6. 🧱 Sentence Structure / Cümlə quruluşu
```
   ┌─────────┬────────┬──────────┬───────────────┐
   │ Subject │ modal  │ (have)+V │ rest           │
   │  She     │ must   │ be       │ at work.       │  ← present deduction
   │  They    │ might  │ be coming│ later.         │  ← present continuous possibility
   │  He      │ must   │ have left│ already.       │  ← past deduction
   │  You     │ can't  │ have seen│ them — they're away.│ ← past impossibility
   └─────────┴────────┴──────────┴───────────────┘
```

## 7. 📐 Grammar Rules / Qaydalar

1. ***must* = positive certainty** ("I'm sure it's true"), based on evidence/logic — **not** obligation
   here. *"She must be tired."* = I deduce she is.
2. ***can't* (or *couldn't*) = negative certainty** ("I'm sure it's NOT true"), i.e. logical
   impossibility. *"That can't be right."*
3. ***may / might / could* = possibility** (≈ "maybe"). They are largely interchangeable; *might* is
   a touch less certain; *could* suggests one option among several.
4. **Negative possibility = *may not / might not*** ("possibly not"). ⚠️ *couldn't* is **not** used
   for "possibly not" — it means impossibility.
5. **Past = modal + have + V3.** *must have* (sure it happened), *can't/couldn't have* (sure it
   didn't), *may/might/could have* (possibly happened).
6. **Continuous:** *must be V-ing* (now), *must have been V-ing* (earlier) for actions in progress.
7. **The opposite of *must* (deduction) is *can't*, never *mustn't*.** (*mustn't* = "don't!" — Ch. 20.)
8. ***could* vs *couldn't*:** *could* = possibility (yes maybe); *couldn't* = impossibility (sure no).
   They are **not** simple opposites here!

---

## 8. ⚔️ *must* vs *can't* (deduction) — the certainty pair / Nəticə cütlüyü
```
   EVIDENCE → CONCLUSION
   "His car's outside."          → "He must be home."        (sure yes)
   "He never drinks coffee."      → "That can't be his cup."  (sure no)
   "There's no answer."           → "She must be out / She can't be in."

   PAST:
   "The grass is wet."            → "It must have rained."
   "He was in Baku all week."     → "He can't have been at the party."
```
> 🇦🇿 Sübutdan nəticəyə: əminliklə "bəli" → *must*; əminliklə "xeyr" → *can't*.

## 10. ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Ümumi səhvlər

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why / 🇦🇿 |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| She **mustn't** be tired (= I'm sure she isn't). | She **can't** be tired. | *must*-un əksi → *can't* |
| He **must to** be home. | He **must** be home. | modal + V1 (no "to") |
| It **must of** rained. | It **must have** rained. | *have*, not "of" |
| They **couldn't** be at home (= possibly not). | They **may/might not** be at home. | imkanın inkarı → *may/might not* |
| She **must has** left. | She **must have** left. | *have* (not *has*) after a modal |
| He **may be left** already. | He **may have left** already. | keçmiş → *may have + V3* |
| That **can't to** be true. | That **can't** be true. | modal + V1 |

## 11. 🔀 Comparison — possibility vs deduction / İmkan və nəticə
```
   POSSIBILITY (open):  may / might / could   "He might be at work."  (one of several options)
   DEDUCTION (certain): must / can't           "He must be at work."   (I'm sure, from evidence)

   STRENGTH:  might (weak) ≤ may ≤ could ≤ should (probable) < must (certain)
              ... and on the NO side: can't / couldn't (certain not)
```

### *must* — two completely different jobs!
```
   DEDUCTION (this chapter): "You must be Kamran's sister." (I deduce — you look alike)
   OBLIGATION (Ch. 20):      "You must wear a seatbelt."     (it's a rule)
   → Context tells them apart. NEGATIVE separates them clearly:
     deduction "can't be" ↔ obligation "mustn't (do)".
```
> 🇦🇿 *must* iki ayrı iş görür: (1) nəticə ("yəqin ki…" — bu fəsil), (2) məcburiyyət ("…məlisən" —
> Ch.20). İnkarı fərqi göstərir: nəticə → *can't*, məcburiyyət → *mustn't*.

- It must be a caching issue. 🇦🇿 Yəqin keş problemidir.

**✈️ Travel**

- This result may suggest a different conclusion. 🇦🇿 Bu nəticə fərqli bir qənaət təklif edə bilər.

- IELTS: "The data might indicate a seasonal trend."

## 22–26. 🎤 Skills tips
```
 SPEAKING:  Hedge naturally — "It might be…", "He must be…", "That can't be…". Sounds fluent & native.
 WRITING:   Academic writing loves cautious "may/might" ("This may suggest…") to avoid over-claiming.
 LISTENING: Catch reduced "must've / might've / could've" — they signal past deductions/possibility.
 READING:   Detective & news texts use deduction modals to weigh evidence ("the suspect must have…").

## 28. ❓ FAQ
**Q: *may*, *might* or *could* — which?** A: All mean "possible". *might* slightly less sure; *could*
= one option among several. Mostly interchangeable.
**Q: Is "mustn't be" the opposite of "must be"?** A: No! The opposite is **"can't be"**. *mustn't* =
prohibition (Ch.20).
**Q: How do I talk about a past guess?** A: *modal + have + V3*: *must have / might have / can't have.*
**Q: "could" or "couldn't" for "possibly not"?** A: **may/might not.** *couldn't* = impossibility.

# 📖 Mini Story — *"The Empty House"* (≈250 words)

Detective Nuri stood in the doorway. "The kettle's still warm," she said. "They **must have** left
only minutes ago."

Her partner frowned. "They **might** still be inside."

"No. The car's gone — they **can't** be here." She pointed to the table. "Two cups. Someone **must
have** been with them."

"A friend?"

"**Could** be. Or it **might have** been the person they were afraid of." She lifted a cup. "This one's
untouched. The visitor **can't have** stayed long."

In the hall, a coat still hung on the hook. "That's odd," Nuri murmured. "If they ran, why leave a
coat? They **may not have** had time to grab it — or they **may have** left in a hurry, frightened."

"So what happened?"

"Look at the mud. Someone **must have** come in through the garden. The bolt's broken — they
**couldn't have** used the front door." She crouched. "These prints are big. It **can't have** been
the woman; she's tiny. It **must have** been a man — and a heavy one."

Her partner exhaled. "You **could** be wrong."

"I **could**," Nuri admitted. "But the evidence says otherwise. They **must** be scared. They
**can't** have gone far without the car. And whoever was here…" she paused at the window, "…**may
still be** watching."

Outside, a branch cracked. Both detectives froze.

"You see?" Nuri whispered. "I told you he **might** still be close."

# ✂️ Chunking · 🎧 Shadowing · 🧠 Memory Tricks
```
 CHUNK:   [must + be/V1] (sure) · [might/may/could + be/V1] (maybe) · [can't + be] (impossible)
          PAST: [must have + V3] · [might have + V3] · [can't have + V3]
 SHADOW:  copy reactions: "You must be joking!", "It can't be!", "They must've left."
 TRICK:   📏 SCALE: must → may/might/could → may/might not → can't  (yes → maybe → no)
          🔁 Opposite of "must be" is "CAN'T be" (not "mustn't").
          ❌ "must of"  →  ✅ "must have / must've"
```

# 🗣️ B. NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT
```
 • REACTIONS:    "You must be joking!", "It can't be!", "You must be Kamran's brother."
 • REDUCED PAST: "must've / might've / could've / can't've" — heard constantly; write "have".
 • "MAY WELL":   "He may well be right" = quite probably (a strong, natural maybe).
 • "MUST" ≠ obligation here: tone & context (and the negative "can't be") make it deduction.
 • "MAY NOT" vs "CAN'T": "may not" = possibly not; "can't" = impossible. Natives never swap these.
 • HEDGING:      polite/cautious English leans on "might/may" to avoid sounding too certain.
 • "BOUND TO":   "They're bound to be late" = almost certainly (an even stronger 'must').
```

## ✅ Deep-Analysis Answer Key
```

 2. "It must HAVE been the wind." (have, not "of")
 3. "He MAY/MIGHT NOT be tired…" ("possibly not" → may/might not, not couldn't)
 4. "They must HAVE left already." (modal + have + V3, no "to")
 5. "He must HAVE forgotten…" (have, not "has")

 Compare:
 a) "must be" = I'm sure (deduction);  "might be" = it's possible (one option).
 b) "can't be serious" = surely you're joking (deduction);  "mustn't be serious" is wrong here —

 c) "must have left" = sure she left;  "may have left" = possibly left.
 d) "can't have seen us" = it's impossible he saw us;  "might not have seen us" = perhaps he didn't.

# 🧩 EXPANSION PACK — Possibility & Deduction (deep dive)

> A deeper layer: the percentage scale, *must have* vs *should have*, continuous deductions, the two
> meanings of *could have*, *may well* vs *may as well*, the negative axis, semi-modal alternatives
> (*be bound to*), future possibility, deduction questions, certainty adverbs, and AZ traps. 🔎

## E1 · The certainty scale with % / Faiz şkalası
```
   ~100% │ will · won't                    "It will be cold." (prediction-certainty)
    ~95% │ must · can't · be bound to       "He must be home." "It can't be true."
    ~80% │ should · ought to · be likely to "They should be here by now." (expected)
    ~50% │ may · might · could              "It may/might/could rain."
    ~20% │ may not · might not · be unlikely "She might not come."
    ~5%  │ can't · couldn't                  "That can't be right."
```
> 🔑 *must* and *can't* are the **certain** ends of *deduction*; *should* sits at "expected/probable".
> 🇦🇿 *must/can't* = əmin; *should* = gözlənilən; *may/might/could* = bəlkə.

---

## E2 · *must have* vs *should have* (past) / Keçmişdə nəticə və gözlənti
```
   must have + V3   = I'm SURE it happened (deduction):  "The road's wet — it must have rained."
   should have + V3 = it was EXPECTED / probably happened: "They left at six; they should have
                      arrived by now." (and maybe they have — it's the expectation)
   (should have ALSO = criticism of the past — "You should have told me" — see Ch.21)
```
> 🇦🇿 *must have* = əminəm (oldu); *should have* = gözlənilirdi (yəqin oldu) — ya da tənqid (Ch.21).

**Present→past map (memorise):**
```
   must be    → must have been          can't be   → can't have been
   may be     → may have been           might be   → might have been
   could be   → could have been         should be  → should have been
```

---

## E3 · Continuous deductions / Davamedici nəticələr
For an action **in progress**, add *be V-ing* (now) or *have been V-ing* (earlier):
```
   NOW:    "The lights are on — she must be working."
           "He's quiet; he might be sleeping."   "They can't be watching this — it's awful."
   EARLIER:"His eyes are red — he must have been crying."
           "She might have been waiting for ages."   "You can't have been listening!"
```
> 🇦🇿 Davam edən hərəkət üçün: indi → *must be V-ing*; əvvəl → *must have been V-ing*.

---

## E4 · ⚠️ The two meanings of *could have + V3* / *could have*-ın iki mənası
```
   1. PAST POSSIBILITY (maybe it happened — THIS chapter):
      "She's not here. She could have missed the bus." (= perhaps she did)

   2. UNREALISED ABILITY/OPPORTUNITY (it did NOT happen — Ch.18):
      "I could have helped, but I didn't." (= the chance existed; I didn't take it)
```
How to tell them apart: **possibility** answers *"what happened?"*; **unrealised** is followed by
(or implies) *"…but didn't / but it didn't."*
> 🇦🇿 *could have* = (1) bəlkə oldu (imkan), (2) ola bilərdi, amma olmadı (Ch.18). Kontekst ayırır.

---

## E5 · *may well* vs *may as well* / İki fərqli ifadə
```
   may/might well + V1   = quite probably (STRENGTHENS the maybe):
      "He may well be the best candidate." (= very possibly is)
   may/might as well + V1 = suggestion (there's no reason not to — nothing better to do):
      "There's no bus for an hour — we might as well walk."
```
> ⚠️ Totally different! *may well* = strong probability; *may as well* = "we might as well…" suggestion.
> 🇦🇿 *may well* = çox güman; *may as well* = "elə … etsək yaxşıdır" (başqa çarə yoxdur).

---

## E6 · The negative axis — *can't / couldn't have* / Mənfi ox
For **negative deduction about the past**, both *can't have* and *couldn't have* work:
```
   "He can't have stolen it — he was with me."
   "She couldn't have known; nobody told her."
```
⚠️ **There is no "mustn't have" for deduction.** The sure-NO past is *can't/couldn't have + V3*:
```
   ❌ "He mustn't have seen us."  (not standard British deduction)
   ✅ "He can't have seen us." / "He couldn't have seen us."
   (Note: AmE colloquially does say "must not have" for "probably didn't" — but for SURE-not, use can't.)
```
> 🇦🇿 Keçmişdə əmin "xeyr" → *can't/couldn't have*. *mustn't have* (deduksiya üçün) işlənmir.

---

## E7 · The shape of the scale — why no "mustn't" / Şkalanın forması
```
   POSITIVE deduction:  must (be / have V3)        ← "sure YES"
   NEGATIVE deduction:  can't / couldn't (be / have V3)  ← "sure NO"
   There is a GAP: the negative of "must" is NOT "mustn't" (that's obligation/prohibition).

   POSSIBILITY:   may / might / could (be / have V3)      (~maybe yes)
   NEG POSSIBILITY: may not / might not (be / have V3)     (~maybe no)
```
> 🇦🇿 Yadda saxla: *must*-ın deduksiya inkarı **yoxdur** — onun yerinə *can't* gəlir.

---

## E8 · Semi-modal alternatives on the scale / Yarı-modal alternativlər
| Expression | Strength | Example | 🇦🇿 |
|------------|----------|---------|-----|
| **be bound to** | ~certain | "They're bound to be late." | mütləq |
| **be sure to** | ~certain | "She's sure to pass." | mütləq |
| **be likely to** | probable | "It's likely to rain." | ehtimallı |
| **be unlikely to** | improbable | "He's unlikely to agree." | ehtimalsız |
| **there's a (good) chance (that)** | possible | "There's a chance it'll work." | şans var |
| **be bound to have + V3** | ~certain past | "They're bound to have heard." | mütləq eşidiblər |
> 🇦🇿 Bunlar şkalanı modallarsız ifadə etməyin yollarıdır — yazıda və danışıqda çox işlənir.

---

## E9 · Future possibility specifically / Gələcək imkan
For things that **might happen later**, use *may / might / could + V1* (or *may/might well*):
```
   "Prices may rise next year."   "It might/could snow tomorrow."
   "We may well win the contract." (strong)   "They might not finish on time."
   ALT: "It's likely to rain." / "There's a chance it'll clear up."
```
> ⚠️ Don't use *can* for a specific future possibility: ❌ "It can rain tomorrow." ✅ "It may/might rain."
> (*can* = general possibility, Ch.18 E7.) 🇦🇿 Konkret gələcək imkan üçün *may/might/could*, *can* yox.

---

## E10 · Deduction questions / Nəticə sualları
```
   "Could it be a mistake?"            "Might they have left already?"
   "Who can that be at this hour?"     "Where can he have got to?" (idiomatic = where on earth is he)
   "How could she possibly know?"      "What can it mean?"
   NOTE: in questions, COULD/MIGHT are preferred over MUST: ✅ "Could he be lost?" (not "Must he be…")
```
> 🇦🇿 Suallarda *could/might* üstünlük təşkil edir; *must* sualda az işlənir.

---

## E11 · Certainty adverbs + modals / Əminlik zərfləri
Adverbs fine-tune the modal. Watch the word order:
```
   definitely / certainly + will:  "They'll definitely be there." / "She certainly won't agree."
   must + surely/obviously:         "He must surely know by now."
   can't + possibly:                "It can't possibly be true." (emphatic impossibility)
   may/might + well:                "You may well be right."
   probably / possibly / perhaps:   "They're probably asleep." (adverb does the modal's job)
```
> 🇦🇿 Zərflər modalı dəqiqləşdirir: *can't possibly* (qətiyyən ola bilməz), *may well* (çox güman),
> *must surely* (yəqin ki mütləq).

---

## E12 · AZ contrastive synthesis / Sintez
| 🇦🇿 | ❌ word-for-word | ✅ natural English | Key |
|-----|------------------|--------------------|-----|
| O, yorğun ola bilməz (əminəm). | She mustn't be tired. | She **can't** be tired. | sure-no → can't |
| Yəqin yağış yağıb. | It must of rained. | It **must have** rained. | have, not "of" |
| Gəlməyə bilər (bəlkə yox). | She couldn't come. | She **may/might not** come. | maybe-no → may/might not |
| Görmüş ola bilməz (keçmiş). | He mustn't have seen us. | He **can't have** seen us. | past sure-no → can't have |
| Çox güman ki, haqlısan. | You may well right. | You **may well be** right. | may well + be |
| Sabah qar yağa bilər. | It can snow tomorrow. | It **may/might** snow tomorrow. | future poss. → may/might |

```
   ONE-LINE SYNTHESIS:
   sure yes → must (be/have V3)   ·   sure no → can't (be/have V3)
   maybe → may/might/could   ·   maybe-not → may/might not
   expected → should (have)   ·   near-certain → be bound to / will
   in progress → must be V-ing / must have been V-ing   ·   strengthen → may well
```

## 📦 BLOCK 11 — ⚡ MINIMAL PAIRS — one word changes the certainty

| Pair | What changes | 🇦🇿 |
|---|---|---|
| He **must be** ill. / He **might be** ill. | sure (95%) → maybe (40%) | əminəm → bəlkə |
| She **can't have** seen it. / She **may not have** seen it. | sure-not → possibly-not | mümkün deyil → bəlkə görməyib |
| It **must have** broken. / It **should have** broken by now. | sure it did → expected to | yəqin sındı → sınmalı idi |
| They **must be** tired. / They **must have been** tired. | now → then | indi → o vaxt |
| You **may well** be right. / You **may as well** go. | strong maybe → "why not" suggestion | çox güman haqlısan → getsən yaxşıdır |
| It **can't be** John. / It **mustn't be** John. ❌ | correct → ✗ wrong for deduction | düzgün → səhv |

> 🔬 Slow down on the last row: ***mustn't*** ≠ "sure-not". The deduction negative is **can't**. → §E6, §E7.

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "The Missing Ring"
### *A whole story told in deductions. Every guess is a modal.*
### 🇦🇿 *Bütöv bir hekayə yalnız nəticə çıxarmaqla danışılır. Hər təxmin bir modaldır.*

> Read it twice. **First** for the story; **second** circling every *must / can't / might / may / could (have)*.
> 🇦🇿 *İki dəfə oxu: birinci dəfə hekayə üçün, ikinci dəfə hər modal feli dövrəyə alaraq.*

---

Grandmother's ring was gone.

It had been on the kitchen windowsill at breakfast — Nora was **sure** of that. Now, at noon, the little velvet box was open and empty.

"It **can't have** just vanished," she said aloud. "Someone **must have** taken it."

She stood very still and started to think, the way her grandmother had taught her.

*The window was open all morning, so a thief **could have** climbed in.* But the flowerbed below was soft and untouched. *No — nobody **can have** come that way; there'd be footprints.*

*The cat? It **might have** knocked the box off… but then the ring **would** be on the floor, and it **wasn't**.* She checked anyway. Nothing.

That left the family. Her brother Tim had left for school at eight. *He **can't have** taken it — he was gone before she even opened the box.* Her father had been in the garden since nine. *He **may have** come inside for water… but he **wouldn't** touch the ring.*

Then she remembered. Little Aysu, four years old, had been **very** quiet all morning. Too quiet.

"Aysu **must be** up to something," Nora thought. "She's always quiet when she's hiding."

She found her sister under the stairs, wearing every necklace in the house — and on her thumb, far too big, **Grandmother's ring**.

"It's my treasure," Aysu whispered. "A dragon **might come**, so I'm guarding it."

Nora laughed. "Well — the dragon **can't have** it. But you, little guard, **may keep** watching the treasures… on the shelf where they belong."

---

### 🔍 Spot the deduction — what was Nora thinking?

| From the story | Certainty | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| "It **can't have** just vanished." | sure-NOT (past) | impossible that it disappeared by itself |
| "Someone **must have** taken it." | sure-YES (past) | the only logical conclusion |
| "A thief **could have** climbed in." | possibility (past) | one open hypothesis |
| "Nobody **can have** come that way." | sure-NOT (past) | ruled out by the evidence (no footprints) |
| "The cat **might have** knocked it off." | possibility (past) | another hypothesis, then rejected |
| "He **can't have** taken it." | sure-NOT | alibi: he'd already left |
| "He **may have** come inside." | possibility | can't rule it out |
| "Aysu **must be** up to something." | sure-YES (present) | deduction from her behaviour |
| "A dragon **might come**." | future possibility | a child's reasoning! |

> 🧠 Notice the **detective rhythm**: open a *might/could*, test it, then either confirm with *must* or kill it with *can't*. That is exactly the certainty scale in action. → §E1, §E7.
