# 1. 📌 What are signal words? / Siqnal sözləri nədir?

Signal words are little **clues** in a sentence that point to the right tense:

```
   I clean my room every day.       → "every day" 🔔 → Present Simple (habit)
   I'm cleaning my room now.         → "now" 🔔 → Present Continuous
   I cleaned my room yesterday.       → "yesterday" 🔔 → Past Simple
   I've just cleaned my room.          → "just" 🔔 → Present Perfect
   I'll clean my room tomorrow.        → "tomorrow" 🔔 → Future
```

Each tense has its **favourite words.** Spot the word → you often know the tense. This is one of the
**fastest** ways to choose correctly (and to ace fill-in exercises). 🇦🇿 Siqnal sözünü gör → zamanı
təxmin et.

---

# 2. ⚠️ The golden caveat: CLUES, not laws

Signal words are **strong hints**, but **context can override** them:

```
   "now" usually → Present Continuous... BUT "Now I understand!" → Present Simple (state verb)
   "for" → Present Perfect... BUT "I lived there for 5 years" (finished) → Past Simple
   "already" → Present Perfect... BUT in the past: "By 9, she had already left."
```

> 🧠 **Rule:** Let the signal word **suggest** the tense, then **check the meaning** (finished?
> ongoing? state verb?). The signal is the **first guess**, not the final word. 🇦🇿 Siqnal = ilk
> təxmin; məna = son qərar.

# 3. 🗂️ THE MASTER LIST — signal words BY TENSE

### 🟩 PRESENT SIMPLE (habits, facts)
```
   always · usually · often · sometimes · rarely · never · seldom
   every day / week / month / year · on Mondays · at weekends
   normally · generally · as a rule · once a week · twice a month
   (facts have no time word: "Water boils at 100°C.")
```

### 🟩 PRESENT CONTINUOUS (now, temporary)
```
   now · right now · at the moment · at present · currently
   Look! · Listen! · today · this week · these days · still
   (temporary: "for the time being", "nowadays")
```

### 🟩 PRESENT PERFECT (result now, life experience)
```
   just · already · yet · ever · never · so far · recently · lately
   up to now · till now · this week/month (unfinished) · how long...?
   for + duration · since + start point · once / twice / many times (in my life)
```

### 🟩 PRESENT PERFECT CONTINUOUS (duration up to now)
```
   for + duration · since + start point · how long...?
   all day / all morning · lately · recently · these past few weeks
```

### 🟨 PAST SIMPLE (finished past)
```
   yesterday · last night/week/month/year · ... ago (two days ago)
   in 2010 · in the past · then · when (+ a past time) · once upon a time
   the other day · just now (= a moment ago) · at that time
```

### 🟨 PAST CONTINUOUS (in progress in the past)
```
   while · as · when (+ background) · all day yesterday · at 8 o'clock last night
   at that moment · the whole morning
```

### 🟨 PAST PERFECT (before another past)
```
   already (in the past) · by the time · before · after · when (one before the other)
   until then · previously · by + past time · earlier · once (= as soon as)
```

### 🟨 PAST PERFECT CONTINUOUS (duration before a past point)
```
   for + duration (before a past point) · since · how long (in the past)
   all day (up to a past moment) · before (+ a past event)
```

### 🟧 FUTURE SIMPLE — will (predictions, decisions)
```
   tomorrow · next week/month/year · soon · later · tonight · in a minute
   I think · I believe · probably · perhaps · maybe · I'm sure · I expect
   in the future · one day · someday
```

### 🟧 FUTURE — going to (plans, evidence)
```
   (a prior plan / intention) · this weekend · tonight · tomorrow
   Look! / evidence: "those clouds → it's going to rain"
```

### 🟧 FUTURE CONTINUOUS (in progress in the future)
```
   at + future time ("at 8 tomorrow") · this time tomorrow / next week
   all day tomorrow · when you arrive (I'll be working)
```

### 🟧 FUTURE PERFECT (done before a future point)
```
   by + future time ("by 2030", "by then", "by the time you arrive")
   before · until · in two years' time
```

### 🟧 FUTURE PERFECT CONTINUOUS (duration before a future point)
```
   by + future time + for + duration ("by June ... for five years")
   how long ... by ...?
```

# 4. 🥊 THE BIG ONE — `for` vs `since`

The most useful (and most confused) signal pair:

```
   for  + a LENGTH of time (how long):
        for two hours · for five years · for a week · for ages · for a long time

   since + a STARTING point (when it began):
        since 2015 · since Monday · since 9 o'clock · since I was a child · since this morning
```
```
 ✅ I've lived here for ten years.    (length)
 ✅ I've lived here since 2014.        (starting point)
 ❌ since ten years → ✅ for ten years
 ❌ for 2014 → ✅ since 2014
```
> 🧠 **for = a stretch (──) · since = a starting dot (●──→).** Both usually signal **Present
> Perfect / Present Perfect Continuous.** 🇦🇿 *for* = müddət (iki saat); *since* = başlanğıc (2015-dən).

---

# 5. ⚖️ OVERLAP WORDS — one word, several tenses

Some signals attach to **different tenses** — the **time frame** decides:

```
 ┌──────────────┬─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ WORD         │ DEPENDS ON...                                                │
 ├──────────────┼─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
 │ this morning │ still morning → Present Perfect · morning over → Past Simple  │
 │ for          │ up to now → Present Perfect · finished period → Past Simple   │
 │ already      │ now → Present Perfect · in the past → Past Perfect            │
 │ just         │ "a moment ago" → Present Perfect (BrE) / Past Simple (AmE)     │
 │ when         │ + background → Past Continuous · + sequence → Past Simple      │
 │ recently     │ Present Perfect (up to now) OR Past Simple                     │
 │ now          │ action → Present Continuous · state verb → Present Simple       │
 │ today        │ Present Simple/Continuous/Perfect — depends on meaning          │
 └──────────────┴─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
> 💡 When a signal can go two ways, ask the **two questions**: *Is the time finished?* and *Is it a
> state verb?* 🇦🇿 Bəzi sözlər çoxmənalıdır — çərçivəni yoxla.

---

# 6. 🚨 DANGEROUS SIGNALS — the traps

```
 ⚠️ "yesterday / last week / ago / in 2010" + Present Perfect = ❌
     ✅ Finished time word FORCES Past Simple:  I saw him yesterday. (NOT "have seen")

 ⚠️ "since" + a DURATION = ❌
     ✅ since + a point (since Monday); for + a length (for 3 days)

 ⚠️ "when / after / before / as soon as / until" + WILL = ❌
     ✅ Use present in the time clause: "When I arrive, I'll call." (NOT "will arrive")

 ⚠️ "if" + WILL (in the condition) = ❌
     ✅ "If it rains..." (NOT "if it will rain")

 ⚠️ "for the last/first time" → often Present Perfect: "It's the first time I've been here."
```

> 🧠 The deadliest trap of all: **a finished-time word kills the Present Perfect** → use Past
> Simple. 🇦🇿 Bitmiş vaxt sözü (yesterday/ago) → Present Perfect YOX, Past Simple.

---

# 7. 🔀 SIGNALS for conditionals, reported speech & time clauses

```
   CONDITIONALS:  if · unless · as long as · provided (that) · in case · even if · what if
   TIME CLAUSES:  when · while · as soon as · before · after · until · by the time · once
                  → these take PRESENT (not will) for future meaning
   REPORTED:      that · if/whether · (reporting verbs: said, told, asked, claimed...)
   used to / would: when I was young · in those days · back then · every summer (past habit)
```
> ⚠️ Remember: **no future after** *if / when / after / until / as soon as* — use the present. 👉
> Ch 16, Ch 20.

# 🧩 EXPANSION PACK — *Signal Words* (E1–E12)
### Placement, the nuance words, and the tricky prepositions. ☕

> 🎯 §1–§10 listed the signals by tense. This pack handles **where** they go in the sentence, the
> four nuance words (*already / yet / still / just*), the *by* vs *until* trap, and the AZ
> interference. These details separate "correct" from "natural." Read slowly.

---

## E1 · WHERE signal words go (position) 📍
Different signals sit in different places:

```
   FRONT or END (time expressions):
      "Yesterday, I saw him." / "I saw him yesterday."     (both fine)
      "Tomorrow we'll leave." / "We'll leave tomorrow."

   MID-position (frequency adverbs — before the main verb, after "be"/auxiliary):
      "I always drink coffee."   (before main verb)
      "She is always late."       (after "be")
      "I have never been there."   (after auxiliary)

   END (usually): "yet", "anymore", duration with "for/since"
      "Have you finished yet?"  ·  "I've lived here for years."
```
> 🧠 **Time phrases** → front/end. **Frequency adverbs** (always/never/often) → middle. 🇦🇿 Vaxt
> ifadələri → əvvəl/son; tezlik zərfləri → ortada.

---

## E2 · The FREQUENCY scale + placement 📊
```
   100% ▮▮▮▮▮  always
    90% ▮▮▮▮▯  usually / normally / generally
    70% ▮▮▮▯▯  often / frequently
    50% ▮▮▯▯▯  sometimes
    30% ▮▯▯▯▯  occasionally
    10% ▯▯▯▯▯  rarely / seldom / hardly ever
     0% ░░░░░  never
```
🔑 **Placement rule:** frequency adverb goes **before the main verb**, but **after** *am/is/are/was*
and after the first auxiliary:
```
 ✅ I usually walk to work.        ✅ He is usually busy.
 ✅ I have often wondered.          ❌ I walk usually... (awkward)
 ⚠️ "sometimes / usually / often" can also start a sentence: "Sometimes I walk."
    But "always/never" cannot start a normal statement: ❌ "Never I go." → ✅ "I never go."
```
> 🇦🇿 Tezlik zərfi əsas feldən **əvvəl**, "be"-dən **sonra** gəlir.

---

## E3 · The NUANCE FOUR — *already · yet · still · just* 🎯
These four are the heart of Present Perfect (and easy to misplace):

```
 ┌─────────┬────────────────────────────┬──────────────────────┬─────────────────────┐
 │ WORD    │ MEANING                    │ USED IN              │ POSITION            │
 ├─────────┼────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────┼─────────────────────┤
 │ already │ sooner than expected (✓)   │ + statements/questions│ mid (have ALREADY V3)│
 │ yet     │ up to now (?/✗)            │ questions & negatives │ END of sentence      │
 │ still   │ continuing (longer than    │ +/− (esp. surprise   │ mid (still haven't)  │
 │         │ expected)                  │ it hasn't ended)     │                      │
 │ just    │ a very short time ago      │ + statements          │ mid (have JUST V3)   │
 └─────────┴────────────────────────────┴──────────────────────┴─────────────────────┘
```
```
   I've already eaten.            (sooner than you'd think)
   Have you eaten yet?            (question → "yet" at the end)
   I haven't eaten yet.            (negative → "yet" at the end)
   I still haven't eaten.          ("still" stresses it's overdue)
   I've just eaten.                (a moment ago)
```
> 🧠 **already = positive surprise (early) · yet = the "?/✗" word, at the end · still = it's
> dragging on · just = a second ago.** 🇦🇿 *already* (artıq) · *yet* (hələ — sual/inkar) · *still*
> (hələ də) · *just* (indicə).

---

## E4 · `yet` vs `still` vs `already` — the showdown ⚔️
```
   already → it HAPPENED (earlier than expected):  "She's already left."
   yet     → it HASN'T happened (but expected):    "She hasn't left yet." / "Has she left yet?"
   still   → it's CONTINUING (overdue/surprising):  "She still hasn't left." / "She's still here."
```
🔑 *still* + negative goes **before** the auxiliary (*still hasn't*); *yet* goes at the **end**
(*hasn't … yet*). Both can describe the same situation with a different feeling:
```
   "He hasn't replied yet."   (neutral — waiting)
   "He still hasn't replied."  (impatient — it's taking too long!)
```
> 🇦🇿 *yet* = neytral gözləmə (sonda); *still* = səbirsizlik, "hələ də" (əvvəldə).

---

## E5 · Time PREPOSITIONS that act as signals — at / on / in / by 🕐
```
   at + clock time / moment:   at 6 o'clock · at night · at the moment · at that time
   on + day / date:            on Monday · on July 5th · on my birthday
   in + month/year/part of day: in May · in 2030 · in the morning · in two hours
   by + deadline:              by Friday · by 2030 · by then   → often Future Perfect
   from ... to / till:         from 9 to 5 · till midnight
```
> 💡 **"by" + a future time** is a classic **Future Perfect** signal: *"I'll have finished **by**
> Friday."* 🇦🇿 *at* (saat), *on* (gün), *in* (ay/il), *by* (son müddət → Future Perfect).

---

## E6 · `by` vs `until` — the deadline trap ⚠️
A famous confusion — both mean "up to a time," but differently:

```
   by    = NOT LATER THAN (a deadline for a single completed action):
           "Finish it BY 5." (at any point before 5, but done by then)
           → pairs with Future Perfect: "I'll have finished by 5."

   until = UP TO AND THROUGHOUT a time (a continuous action that stops):
           "I'll work UNTIL 5." (the working continues right up to 5)
           → pairs with a continuous/simple action: "Wait until I come back."
```
```
 ✅ Submit the form by Monday.        (deadline)
 ✅ The shop is open until 9.           (continuous, then stops)
 ❌ I'll have finished until Friday.    → ✅ ...BY Friday.
```
> 🧠 **by = a point deadline · until = a duration that stops at that point.** 🇦🇿 *by* = son tarix
> (...-ə qədər, bir nöqtə); *until* = o ana qədər davam (...-ə qədər, müddət).

---

## E7 · Signals that DON'T fix the tense (handle with care) ⚠️
Some "signals" are **too flexible** to trust alone:

```
   today / this week / this month → could be Present Simple, Continuous, OR Perfect:
      "I work hard today." · "I'm working today." · "I've worked a lot today."
   recently / lately → Present Perfect OR Past Simple:
      "I've been busy lately." · "She called recently."
   now → usually Present Continuous, BUT "Now I understand." (state → Present Simple)
```
> 💡 For these, the **signal alone isn't enough** — read the whole sentence's meaning. 🇦🇿 Bəzi
> sözlər çox çevikdir — təkbaşına zamanı təyin etmir.

---

## E8 · When TWO signals fight — which wins? 🥊
Sometimes a sentence has clues pulling different ways. **The most specific / finished-time word
usually wins:**
```
   "I have lived here since 2010, but last year I almost moved."
      → "since 2010" (Present Perfect) governs the first clause;
        "last year" (Past Simple) governs the second. Each clause keeps its own signal!

   ❌ "I have seen him yesterday."  → "yesterday" (finished) BEATS "have" → I SAW him yesterday.
```
🔑 A **finished-time word** (yesterday, ago, last week) **overrides** a perfect-tense signal — it
forces **Past Simple**.
> 🇦🇿 İki siqnal toqquşanda **bitmiş vaxt** sözü qalib gəlir → Past Simple.

---

## E9 · Register — formal vs informal time expressions 📑
```
   INFORMAL:   the other day · ages ago · these days · loads of times · right now · in a bit
   NEUTRAL:    yesterday · recently · currently · soon · often
   FORMAL:     previously · formerly · at present · in due course · hitherto · of late
```
> 💡 *"hitherto"* (= until now), *"of late"* (= recently), *"in due course"* (= soon) appear in
> formal/academic writing. Recognise them; use the neutral ones yourself. 🇦🇿 Rəsmi mətnlərdə
> *previously, at present, of late* işlənir.

## E12 · 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani-interference mistakes (deep cut)
```
 ❌ I have seen him yesterday.
 ✅ I saw him yesterday.            → "dünən" (bitmiş) → Past Simple.

 ❌ I've lived here since five years.
 ✅ I've lived here for five years.  → for = müddət; since = başlanğıc.

 ❌ I have finished it until Friday.
 ✅ I will have finished it by Friday. → son tarix → "by" + Future Perfect.

 ❌ Have you done it already? (meaning: yet)
 ✅ Have you done it yet?            → sual/inkar → "yet".

 ❌ When I will arrive, I'll call.
 ✅ When I arrive, I'll call.        → time clause → present.

 ❌ Never I eat meat.
 ✅ I never eat meat.                → tezlik zərfi feldən əvvəl (cümlə başında "never" YOX).
```
> 🧠 The 5 deep AZ traps: **(1)** finished-time + present perfect; **(2)** *since* + duration;
> **(3)** *until* instead of *by* for deadlines; **(4)** *already* instead of *yet* in
> questions/negatives; **(5)** adverb placement (no fronting *never/always*).

## 🔴 ROUND 5 — Spot the signal-word mistake (❌ → ✅)
```
 27. I have seen him yesterday.
 28. I've worked here since ten years.
 29. When I will get home, I'll call you.
 30. Have you done it already? (you mean: is it done now?)
 31. I'll have finished until Monday.
 32. Never I drink coffee at night.
 33. Look! The sun shines.
```

<details>
<summary>👉 ROUND 5 — Answers & why</summary>

```
 27. ❌ have seen ... yesterday → ✅ I SAW him yesterday.   (finished time → Past Simple)
 28. ❌ since ten years → ✅ FOR ten years.                 (for = length)
 29. ❌ will get → ✅ When I GET home...                     (time clause → present)
 30. ❌ already → ✅ Have you done it YET?                    (question → "yet")
 31. ❌ until Monday → ✅ ...BY Monday.                       (deadline → "by")
 32. ❌ Never I drink → ✅ I NEVER drink coffee at night.     (adverb after subject)
 33. ❌ shines → ✅ Look! The sun IS SHINING.                 (Look! → present continuous)
```
</details>

# 📖 MINI STORY — *"The Baker's Last Morning"*
### (a signal-word showcase) 🥖

> 🎯 This story is **deliberately full of signal words** — each one **bold**, each one pulling a
> tense. As you read, notice how the signal and the tense always match. Read 🇬🇧 first, then 🇦🇿,
> then the questions.

## 🇬🇧 The Baker's Last Morning

**Every morning** for forty years, Rashid **has woken** at four o'clock. He **always** lights the
old oven first. **These days**, his hands shake a little, but he **never** complains.

**Right now**, he **is shaping** the last loaves of his life. **Tomorrow**, the bakery will belong
to someone else.

He **has owned** this shop **since** 1985. He **has baked** bread here **for** thirty-nine years.
He **has already** trained a young woman, Aysel, to take over, and she learns fast.

"Have you mixed the dough **yet**?" he asks.

"I mixed it an **hour ago**," she says. "It's **just** about ready."

He smiles. **Yesterday**, the whole town **came** to say goodbye. **By the time** the shop closed,
he **had shaken** a hundred hands. **While** he **was locking** the door, an old woman **had
slipped** a note under it: *"You fed this town for forty years."*

**Now**, the sun **is rising**. **By eight o'clock**, the shelves **will be** full, **as** they
**always have been.**

"**By next year**," Aysel says quietly, "I **will have been baking** here for a whole year. I hope
I do it as well as you."

"You **already** do," he says.

He takes one last loaf from the oven. **In a moment**, he **will hang** up his apron **for the last
time.** **Soon**, he **will walk** home through the morning light.

But the bread, he knows, **will still rise** here **long after** he **has gone** — **every
morning**, just as it **always has.**
