## 🧭 THE BIG PICTURE — time words as tense-triggers

A time expression is not just decoration — it **points to a tense**. Match them and your grammar self-corrects:

```
   TIME WORD                     →  TENSE IT TRIGGERS
   since / for / already / yet    →  present perfect   "I've lived here since 2019."
   just / recently / so far       →  present perfect   "I've just arrived."
   ago                            →  past simple       "I arrived two hours ago."
   by the time … (past)           →  past perfect      "By the time I came, it had ended."
   by the time … (future)         →  future perfect    "By then, we'll have finished."
   still                          →  any (ongoing)     "She's still working."
```

> 🧠 **The key mental move:** when you see or want a time word, **ask which tense it demands** — then build the verb to match. *Since* and *for* pull the **present perfect**; *ago* pulls the **past simple**; *by the time* pulls a **perfect**. Half of tense errors are really **time-word** errors.
> 🇦🇿 *Əsas fikir: zaman sözünü görəndə **hansı zamanı tələb etdiyini soruş** — sonra feli ona uyğun qur. Zaman səhvlərinin yarısı əslində **zaman-sözü** səhvidir.*

## 🔑 THE CORE — *already · yet · still · just* (the perfect quartet)

These four cluster around the **present perfect** and the idea of "up to now." Each has a distinct job:

```
   ALREADY  →  it happened, sooner than expected (positive)   "I've ALREADY eaten."
   YET      →  up to now — in questions & negatives            "Have you eaten YET?" / "Not YET."
   STILL    →  it continues, longer than expected              "I'm STILL eating." / "I STILL don't get it."
   JUST     →  a very short time ago                           "I've JUST eaten."
```

| Word | ✓ / ? / ✗ | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **already** | ✓ positive | She'**s already** left. |
| **yet** | ? question / ✗ negative | Has she left **yet**? / She hasn't left **yet**. |
| **still** | ongoing (any) | She'**s still** here. / I **still** can't believe it. |
| **just** | ✓ recent | She'**s just** left. |

> ⚠️ **still vs yet in negatives** — a subtle, native distinction:
> *"He **hasn't** called **yet**"* = neutral, up to now. *"He **still hasn't** called"* = impatient/surprised — I expected it by now. 🇦🇿 *"hasn't … yet" = neytral; "still hasn't" = səbirsizlik/təəccüb ("artıq zəng etməliydi").*

> 🗣️ *already* in a **question** shows surprise: *"Have you finished **already**?!"* (so fast?). 🇦🇿 *Sualda "already" təəccüb bildirir: "artıq bitirdin?!"*

## ⏳ *FOR · SINCE · AGO* — duration, starting point, counting back

The classic trio. They all measure time, but from different angles:

```
   FOR   + a PERIOD           →  how long        "for three years," "for a while"
   SINCE + a STARTING POINT   →  from when       "since 2019," "since Monday," "since I was a child"
   AGO   + a PERIOD + past    →  counting back    "three years ago" (measured from now)
```

| Word | Followed by | Tense | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **for** | period | perfect / continuous / any | I've known her **for** ten years. |
| **since** | point in time | present perfect | I've known her **since** 2014. |
| **ago** | period (+ past) | past simple | I met her ten years **ago**. |

> 🧪 **for vs since:** if you can answer *"how long?"* → **for** (+ period). If you can answer *"from when?"* → **since** (+ point). 🇦🇿 *"nə qədər?" → for (müddət); "nə vaxtdan?" → since (başlanğıc nöqtəsi).*

> ⚠️ **ago vs since:** *ago* goes with the **past simple** and counts back from now (*"two days **ago**"*); *since* goes with the **present perfect** (*"**since** Tuesday"*). Never ~~since two days ago~~ / ~~for since 2019~~. 🇦🇿 *"ago" past simple ilə (indidən geri sayır); "since" present perfect ilə.*

## ⌛ *BY · UNTIL · BY THE TIME* — deadline, limit, and the clause

The trap trio for Azerbaijani speakers (because AZ *-a qədər/-dək* covers all three):

```
   BY + point       →  NOT LATER THAN (a deadline; a single completed action)   "Finish BY 5 p.m."
   UNTIL / TILL     →  UP TO (a continuous state that stops at that point)       "Wait UNTIL 5 p.m."
   BY THE TIME + clause  →  triggers a PERFECT tense                              "By the time I came, it HAD ended."
```

| Word | Idea | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **by** | complete no later than | Submit it **by** Friday. |
| **until / till** | continue up to | The shop's open **until** nine. |
| **not … until** | not before | We don't open **until** nine. |
| **by the time** (+ clause, past) | → past perfect | **By the time** we arrived, they **had left**. |
| **by the time** (+ clause, future) | → future perfect | **By the time** you read this, I'**ll have gone**. |

> ⚠️ **by ≠ until.** *"Finish it **by** 5"* = complete it at some point **before** 5 (one action). *"Stay **until** 5"* = continue **up to** 5 (a lasting state). Don't swap them: ~~"Wait by 5"~~ / ~~"Finish until 5"~~. 🇦🇿 *AZ "-a qədər" hər ikisini bildirir — İngiliscə: **by** (son tarix, tamamlanma) vs **until** (davam edən vəziyyət).*

## 🔀 *DURING · WHILE · FOR* — noun vs clause vs duration

All three cover "over a period," but the grammar after them differs:

```
   DURING  + NOUN / noun phrase        "during the film," "during the summer"
   WHILE   + CLAUSE (subject + verb)    "while I was watching the film"
   FOR     + DURATION (how long)        "for two hours"
```

| Word | Followed by | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **during** | a noun / period | I fell asleep **during** the film. |
| **while** | a clause | I fell asleep **while** I was watching the film. |
| **for** | a length of time | I slept **for** two hours. |

> 🧪 **Test:** noun after the gap → **during**; a full clause (subject + verb) → **while**; a number/length → **for**. 🇦🇿 *İsim → during; tam cümlə → while; müddət → for.*

> ⚠️ ~~during I was sleeping~~ → **while** I was sleeping. ~~for the holidays (meaning "throughout")~~ → **during** the holidays. 🇦🇿 *"during" cümlə almır; "while" isim almaz.*

## 📎 MORE time expressions worth mastering

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **so far / up to now** | until this moment (perfect) | We've sold 200 tickets **so far**. |
| **recently / lately** | in the recent past (perfect/past) | I've been busy **lately**. |
| **as yet** | up to now (formal, negative) | No reply **as yet**. |
| **no longer / not … any more** | a state that has ended | He **no longer** works here. / He doesn't work here **any more**. |
| **in** + period | after that time (future) | I'll be back **in** ten minutes. |
| **within** + period | before the end of | Reply **within** 24 hours. |
| **on time** vs **in time** | punctual vs with time to spare | The train was **on time**. / We got there **in time** to eat. |
| **at the end** vs **in the end** | at the final point vs finally | **at the end** of the film / **In the end**, we stayed. |

> 🔑 *in + period* looks forward (*"in an hour" = after an hour*); *within + period* sets a maximum (*"within an hour" = before an hour passes*). 🇦🇿 *"in an hour" = bir saatdan sonra; "within an hour" = bir saat ərzində (bitənə qədər).*

## 🧱 TIME CONJUNCTIONS (joining clauses)

| Conjunction | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **when** | at that time | **When** I arrived, they were eating. |
| **while / as** | during / at the same time | **While** she cooked, I cleaned. |
| **as soon as / the moment** | immediately after | **As soon as** it rings, answer it. |
| **once** | after (and because) | **Once** you finish, you can go. |
| **by the time** | + perfect | **By the time** he calls, I'll have left. |
| **until** | up to the point | Wait **until** I get back. |
| **since** | from the point | I've been here **since** you left. |

> ⚠️ After time conjunctions about the **future**, use the **present**, not *will* (Ch.13): *"**When** it **rains** (not ~~will rain~~), we'll stay in."* / *"**As soon as** she **arrives**, call me."* 🇦🇿 *Gələcək zaman-bağlayıcılarından sonra **indiki zaman** işlədilir, "will" yox.*

| I've known him for 2019. | I've known him **since** 2019. | *since* + starting point |

## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT

1. **"just" is everywhere in speech.** *"I've just seen him," "I just wanted to ask…"* — natives soften and time-stamp with *just* constantly. 🇦🇿 *"just" danışıqda çox işlənir — yumşaldır və zaman qeyd edir.*
2. **"still" carries feeling.** *"You're still here?"* / *"I still love it"* adds surprise, persistence or emotion. 🇦🇿 *"still" hiss qatır — təəccüb, davamlılıq.*
3. **"already" can mean "so soon?!"** *"You've finished already?"* signals surprise, not just completion. 🇦🇿 *"already" sualda "artıq/bu qədər tez?!" təəccübü.*
4. **"yet" is patient; "still…not" is impatient.** Native speakers feel the difference: *"hasn't arrived yet"* (calm) vs *"still hasn't arrived"* (annoyed). 🇦🇿 *"yet" sakit; "still…not" səbirsiz.*
5. **AmE "already/yet" with past simple.** Americans often say *"Did you eat yet?"* / *"I already ate,"* where BrE prefers the present perfect. 🇦🇿 *Amerikan ingiliscəsi "already/yet"-i past simple ilə də işlədir.*
6. **"in the end" ≠ "at the end".** *"In the end, we agreed"* (finally) vs *"at the end of the meeting"* (the final part). A very common slip. 🇦🇿 *"in the end" = nəhayət; "at the end (of)" = sonunda (bir şeyin).*

# 📖 MINI STORY #1 — "The Waiting Room"

### *for, since, already, yet, still and by the time — one long afternoon at the clinic.*
### 🇦🇿 *for, since, already, yet, still və by the time — klinikada bir uzun günorta.*

---

Murad had been sitting in the waiting room **for** two hours. *(duration.)* He'd arrived at one, and it was **already** three. *(sooner-than-expected.)*

"Have they called your number **yet**?" asked the woman beside him. *(up to now — question.)*

"No, not **yet**," he sighed. "I've been here **since** lunchtime, and they **still** haven't reached me." *(from a point; impatient.)*

"I've **just** got here myself," she said. *(a moment ago.)* "I've had this cough **for** a week."

**By the time** a nurse finally appeared, Murad had almost fallen asleep. *(+ past perfect.)*

"Mr Murad? Sorry for the wait. The doctor's been busy **since** this morning." *(from a point.)*

"It's fine," he lied. He'd been rehearsing his symptoms **for** an hour and had **already** forgotten half of them. *(duration; sooner than expected.)*

**During** the examination, the doctor was kind and quick. *(+ noun.)* **While** she typed her notes, Murad relaxed at last. *(+ clause.)*

"You'll feel better **within** a few days," she promised. "Take these **until** the box is empty, and don't stop **until** you've finished them all." *(before the end of; up to.)*

**By the time** he left, it was nearly five. *(+ past.)* He'd been in the building **for** four hours — but at least he **no longer** felt ignored. *(a state that ended.)*

"**In the end**," he told his wife that evening, "it wasn't so bad." *(finally.)*

## 🅴1 — Time words as TENSE-TRIGGERS (the whole system)

```
   PRESENT PERFECT pulls:   for · since · already · yet · just · so far · up to now · recently · lately
   PAST SIMPLE pulls:       ago · yesterday · last (week) · in 2010 · when I was …
   PAST PERFECT pulls:      by the time (past) · before · after · already (in the past)
   FUTURE / present:        by · in · within · when/as soon as (+ present, not will)
```

| See this word | Reach for | Example |
|---|---|---|
| since / for | present perfect | I've waited **for** an hour. |
| ago | past simple | I arrived an hour **ago**. |
| by the time (past) | past perfect | **By the time** I came, it **had** ended. |
| when (future) | present (not *will*) | **When** it **rains**, we'll stay. |

> 🇦🇿 *Zaman sözü feli müəyyən edir: since/for → present perfect; ago → past simple; by the time → past perfect; "when" (gələcək) → indiki zaman.*

---

## 🅴2 — ⚠️ *for · since · ago* (the duration trio)

```
   FOR   + PERIOD              →  present perfect (duration up to now)   "for ten years"
   SINCE + POINT               →  present perfect (from that point)      "since 2014"
   AGO   + PERIOD              →  past simple (measured back from now)   "ten years ago"
```

| Question it answers | Word | Example |
|---|---|---|
| How long? | **for** | I've studied English **for** six years. |
| From when? | **since** | I've studied English **since** 2019. |
| When (counting back)? | **ago** | I started six years **ago**. |

> ⚠️ Never mix: ~~since six years~~ → *for* six years; ~~for 2019~~ → *since* 2019; ~~since ten years ago~~ (redundant) → *ten years ago* **or** *since ten years ago* (only if you really mean "starting from that past point"). 🇦🇿 *Qarışdırma: "since" nöqtə, "for" müddət, "ago" geri sayır.*

> 🔗 *for* also works with other tenses (*"I'll be away **for** a week," "We talked **for** hours"*); *since* almost always signals the **present/past perfect**. 🇦🇿 *"for" başqa zamanlarla da; "since" demək olar həmişə perfect ilə.*

## 🅴3 — *already · yet · still · just* (position & polarity)

| Word | Sentence type | Position | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **already** | ✓ affirmative (surprise in ?) | mid | I've **already** paid. |
| **yet** | ? question / ✗ negative | end | Have you paid **yet**? / Not **yet**. |
| **still** | any (ongoing) | before verb / after *be* | I'm **still** paying it off. |
| **just** | ✓ recent | mid | I've **just** paid. |

> 🌍 **AmE vs BrE:** Americans freely use *already/just/yet* with the **past simple** (*"I already paid," "Did you eat yet?"*); British English prefers the **present perfect** (*"I've already paid," "Have you eaten yet?"*). Both are correct in their variety. 🇦🇿 *AmE bunları past simple ilə də işlədir; BrE present perfect üstün tutur.*

## 🅴4 — *still · yet · no longer / any more* (ongoing / not-yet / ended)

```
   STILL           →  the situation CONTINUES (often longer than expected)   "still working"
   NOT … YET       →  it HASN'T happened, but is expected                    "hasn't arrived yet"
   NO LONGER /     →  the situation has ENDED                                "no longer works here"
   NOT … ANY MORE
```

| State | Word | Example |
|---|---|---|
| continuing | **still** | He's **still** asleep. |
| not happened (expected) | **not … yet** | He hasn't woken **yet**. |
| ended | **no longer / not … any more** | He's **no longer** asleep. / He isn't asleep **any more**. |

> 🔑 *no longer* is more formal and goes **mid-sentence**; *not … any more / any longer* is neutral and goes at the **end**. 🇦🇿 *"no longer" rəsmi, cümlə ortasında; "not … any more" neytral, sonda.*

---

## 🅴5 — ⚠️ *by · until · by the time* (the *-a qədər* splitter)

```
   BY + point         →  deadline: complete NOT LATER THAN (single action)   "by 5 p.m."
   UNTIL/TILL         →  up-to: a state CONTINUES to that point               "until 5 p.m."
   BY THE TIME + clause →  triggers a PERFECT tense                            "by the time … had/will have …"
```

| Test | Word |
|---|---|
| "at some point before X, finish" | **by** |
| "continuously up to X" | **until** |
| "a whole clause about a point" | **by the time** (+ perfect) |

> ⚠️ For Azerbaijani speakers this is the biggest trap: **-a qədər / -dək = both *by* and *until*.** Ask: *deadline for a single action* → **by**; *a lasting state up to a point* → **until**. 🇦🇿 *"-a qədər" həm by, həm until — tək hərəkətin son tarixi → by; davam edən vəziyyət → until.*

---

## 🅴6 — *during · while · for · throughout* (noun / clause / duration)

| Word | Followed by | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **during** | noun / period | I dozed **during** the lecture. |
| **while / as** | clause (subject + verb) | I dozed **while** the lecturer talked. |
| **for** | length of time | I dozed **for** ten minutes. |
| **throughout** | whole period (emphatic) | He stayed calm **throughout** the crisis. |
| **over** | across a period | We chatted **over** dinner. |

> ⚠️ ~~during I slept~~ → **while** I slept; ~~while the film~~ → **during** the film. 🇦🇿 *"during" + isim; "while" + cümlə; "for" + müddət.*

---

## 🅴7 — "up to now" markers: *so far · recently · lately · as yet*

| Marker | Register / use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **so far / up to now / thus far** | progress to this point | **So far**, everything's fine. |
| **recently** | in the near past (perfect/past) | I've **recently** moved. / I moved **recently**. |
| **lately** | recent period (usually perfect) | I've been tired **lately**. |
| **as yet** | up to now (formal, negative) | No news **as yet**. |
| **to date** | up to now (formal) | It's our best result **to date**. |

> 🇦🇿 *"so far/up to now" indiyə qədər; "recently/lately" son vaxtlar; "as yet/to date" rəsmi.*

---

## 🅴8 — *in · within · after* (looking forward)

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **in** + period | after that time | I'll call you **in** an hour. *(= after an hour)* |
| **within** + period | before the end of | Reply **within** an hour. *(= before an hour passes)* |
| **after** + period/point | following | **After** an hour, we left. |
| **inside** (of) + period *(informal)* | within | done **inside** a week |

> ⚠️ *"in an hour"* (future) ≠ *"within an hour."* *In* points to the end of the period; *within* sets a maximum. ~~I'll do it within an hour from now~~ is fine, but *"see you in an hour"* means **after** 60 minutes. 🇦🇿 *"in an hour" = bir saatdan sonra; "within an hour" = bir saat ərzində (bitənə qədər).*

## 🅴9 — Confusable time phrases

| Pair | Difference | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **on time** / **in time** | punctual / with time to spare | The train was **on time**. / We arrived **in time** for lunch. |
| **at the end** / **in the end** | the final point / finally | **at the end** of the road / **In the end**, we agreed. |
| **at first** / **first** | initially (contrast later) / firstly | **At first** it seemed hard. / **First**, boil the water. |
| **at last** / **lastly** | finally (relief) / the last point | **At last**, you're here! / **Lastly**, add salt. |
| **in the beginning** / **at the beginning** | at the start (period) / at the start point | **at the beginning** of the film |

> 🇦🇿 *"on time" = vaxtında; "in time" = vaxtı çatan. "in the end" = nəhayət; "at the end (of)" = sonunda. "at last" = nəhayət (rahatlıq).*

## 🅴10 — TIME CONJUNCTIONS & the "present-for-future" rule

After *when, as soon as, until, before, after, by the time, once, the moment* referring to the **future**, use a **present tense** — never *will* (see Ch.13):

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---|---|
| When it **will** rain… | **When** it **rains**, we'll stay in. |
| As soon as she **will** arrive… | **As soon as** she **arrives**, call me. |
| Before you **will** leave… | **Before** you **leave**, lock up. |
| By the time you **will** read this… | **By the time** you **read** this, I'll have gone. |

> 🔑 The **main** clause keeps *will/future*; the **time** clause takes the **present** (or present perfect for "completed first"): *"**Once** you'**ve finished**, you can go."* 🇦🇿 *Zaman budaq cümləsində gələcək üçün indiki zaman; əsas cümlədə "will".*

---

## 🅴11 — Adverb position quick reference

| Position | Adverbs | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **mid** (after aux/*be*, before main verb) | already, just, still, always, recently | I've **just** eaten. / She's **still** here. |
| **end** | yet, so far, lately, today, ago-phrases, for/since-phrases | Have you eaten **yet**? / I've been busy **lately**. |
| **front** (emphasis / linking) | recently, by then, so far, in the end | **So far**, so good. / **Recently**, prices rose. |

> 🇦🇿 *Orta mövqe: already/just/still; sonda: yet/so far/lately; öndə (vurğu): so far/recently/in the end.*

## 🅴12 — 🇦🇿 CONTRASTIVE SYNTHESIS

| English | 🇦🇿 | The contrast |
|---|---|---|
| **for** three years | üç il (müddətində) / üç ildir | AZ often "-dır" (= it's been) |
| **since** 2019 | 2019-cu **ildən (bəri)** | AZ "-dən bəri" |
| three years **ago** | üç il **əvvəl** | AZ "əvvəl" (postposition) |
| **by** Friday | cüməyə **qədər** | AZ "-a qədər" (= also *until*!) |
| **until** Friday | cüməyə **qədər** | same AZ word → ambiguity |
| **still** here | **hələ** buradadır | AZ "hələ" (= also *yet*) |
| not … **yet** | **hələ** … deyil | same AZ word "hələ" |
| **during** the film | film **ərzində** | AZ postposition "ərzində" |

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani contrast in one line:** Azerbaijani **merges** several English distinctions into single words. **-a qədər/-dək** does the work of both **by** and **until**; **hələ** covers both **still** and **yet**; and durations are often built with the suffix **-dır** (*"üç ildir"* = "it's been three years" ≈ *for* / *since*). So the AZ learner must **split** one word into two English ones — asking, for *-a qədər*, "deadline (**by**) or continuous limit (**until**)?"; for *hələ*, "continuing (**still**) or not-yet (**yet**)?"; and for a duration, "a period (**for**) or a starting point (**since**)?" Meanwhile English time words also **fix the tense**, which Azerbaijani leaves freer.

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "The Reunion"

### *for, since, ago, still, already and no longer — old friends meet after many years.*
### 🇦🇿 *for, since, ago, still, already və no longer — köhnə dostlar illər sonra görüşür.*

> Read it twice. **First** for the story; **second** circling every time expression — and note the tense it triggers.
> 🇦🇿 *İki dəfə oxu: əvvəl hekayə üçün, sonra hər zaman ifadəsini dövrəyə alaraq — hansı zamanı tələb edir?*

---

Aysel recognised him the moment he walked in. They hadn't seen each other **since** university — nearly twenty years **ago**. *(from a point; counting back.)*

"You haven't changed a bit!" she laughed.

"I have, believe me," Kamran grinned. "But you look exactly the same. How long have you lived in Baku now?"

"**For** about ten years," she said. "And you? Are you **still** in Ganja?" *(duration; ongoing.)*

"No, not **any more**. We moved to Sheki three years **ago**." *(ended; counting back.)* "I've **already** opened a small hotel there." *(sooner than expected.)*

"That's wonderful! I didn't know you were **no longer** teaching." *(a state that ended.)*

"I gave it up years ago. What about you — have you finished that novel **yet**?" *(up to now.)*

Aysel laughed. "I **still** haven't! I've been writing it **for** five years and I'm only halfway." *(impatience; duration.)*

"**By the time** you finish, we'll both be old," Kamran teased. *(+ future.)*

They talked **for** hours, remembering names they hadn't spoken **since** their student days. *(duration; from a point.)*

"We shouldn't leave it so long next time," Aysel said as they parted. "**Until** now, I hadn't realised how much I'd missed everyone." *(up to a point.)*

"Agreed. I've **just** decided," said Kamran, "we'll meet every year from now on."

And this time, they kept the promise.

> 💬 **Answers**
> 1. *for ten years / for five years / for hours* → **present perfect** (or past for "talked for hours"), duration. *since university / since their student days* → **present perfect**, starting point. *twenty years ago / three years ago* → **past simple**, counting back from now.
> 2. *"still in Ganja?"* asks whether the situation **continues**; *"not any more"* says it has **ended** — he's no longer there.
> 3. *"still haven't finished"* adds **impatience/frustration** ("after all this time!"), which neutral *"haven't finished yet"* lacks.
> 4. *"By the time you finish, we'll both be old"* — *by the time* + present (*finish*) triggers the idea that, at that future point, the state (*being old*) will already apply; a fuller form: *"…, we'll **have grown** old."*
> 5. We haven't met (seen each other) since university. / I've lived in Baku for ten years. / We moved (here) three years ago.

## 🏁 End of Chapter 35

You can now **pin actions in time with precision.** Time words are **tense-triggers**: *since / for / already / yet / just* pull the **present perfect**; *ago* pulls the **past simple**; *by the time* pulls a **perfect**. Keep the trios apart — **for** (a period) vs **since** (a starting point) vs **ago** (counting back); **by** (a deadline) vs **until** (up to a point) vs **by the time** (+ clause); **during** (+ noun) vs **while** (+ clause) vs **for** (+ duration). Master the quartet **already / yet / still / just** (and the feeling in *still … not*), the *in vs within* and *on time vs in time* pairs, and remember to use the **present** (not *will*) after future time conjunctions. ⏱️

```
 ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ ✅ already:  done, sooner than expected   "I've already eaten."            │
 │ ❓ yet:      up to now (? / ✗), end        "Have you eaten yet?"            │
 │ 🔁 still:    continuing / impatient        "She's still here." "still hasn't"│
 │ ⏳ for/since/ago:  period · point · count-back  ("for 3 yrs / since 2019 / 3 yrs ago")│
 │ ⌛ by/until:  deadline vs up-to  ·  by the time + PERFECT                    │
 │ 🔀 during/while/for:  + noun · + clause · + duration                        │
 │ ⚠️ for≠since · by≠until · during≠while · present (not will) after 'when'    │
 └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
