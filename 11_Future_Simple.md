# 🔮 Future Simple — Talking About the Future (will / be going to)

### *Part 3 · Chapter 11 · will + V1 · be going to + V1*

> **Azərbaycanca qısa giriş:** Future Simple (Sadə Gələcək Zaman) gələcəkdə baş verəcək
> hərəkəti bildirir. İki əsas yolu var: **will + fel** (*-acaq/-əcək* — proqnoz, ani qərar,
> söz vermə) və **be going to + fel** (əvvəlcədən düşünülmüş **plan/niyyət**). *Sabah
> gələcəyəm* → *I **will come** tomorrow.* / *Universitetə girmək fikrindəyəm* → *I'm **going to**
> apply to university.* 🔮

---

## 1. 🟢 Introduction

The **Future Simple** is how we talk about **things that haven't happened yet**. English has
**two** main everyday ways to do this:

```
   will + V1:        I WILL help you.        → decision now, prediction, promise
   be going to + V1: I AM GOING TO study.    → plan/intention already decided, evidence
```

Both point to the **future**, but they have **different feelings** (we compare them carefully
in §11 — this is the heart of the chapter).

> 💡 **Big idea:** **will** = a decision made **at the moment of speaking** or a simple
> prediction. **be going to** = a plan you **already** had, or a prediction based on **evidence
> you can see now**. 🔮

---

## 2. ❓ Why Do We Use This Tense?

| # | Use | will / going to | Example | Azərbaycanca |
|---|-----|-----------------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | **Instant decision** (decided now) | **will** | The phone's ringing — I'**ll** get it. | ani qərar |
| 2 | **Prediction / opinion** | **will** | I think it **will** rain. | proqnoz / fikir |
| 3 | **Promise / offer / threat** | **will** | I **will** always love you. | söz vermə / təklif |
| 4 | **Plan / intention** (decided before) | **going to** | We'**re going to** move house. | plan / niyyət |
| 5 | **Prediction from evidence** | **going to** | Look at those clouds — it'**s going to** rain. | dəlilə əsasən proqnoz |

> 📌 The big two: **will** for decisions/predictions/promises, **going to** for plans you
> already made and predictions you can **see coming**.

## 3. 🌍 Real-Life Situations

- ☎️ **Reacting instantly:** *"Someone's at the door — I'**ll** open it."*
- 🤝 **Making a promise:** *"Don't worry, I **won't** tell anyone."*
- 🌦️ **Predicting:** *"It **will** probably be sunny tomorrow."*
- 🗓️ **Sharing a plan:** *"I'**m going to** start a new job next month."*
- 👀 **Reading the signs:** *"She'**s going to** have a baby!"* (you can see it)
- 🍽️ **Offering:** *"You look tired — I'**ll** make you a coffee."*

---

## 4. 📅 Timeline

The action is **after now**. Both forms place it in the future; the difference is **when the
decision was made**.

```
   PAST            NOW                        FUTURE
 ──────────────────┼──────────────────────────●────────►
                   │                          │
            (decide & speak)            will happen

   will:        decision made HERE ────────► action    ("I'll help.")
   going to:    decision made BEFORE now ───► action    ("I'm going to help.")
```

> 🔮 **will** is born **at the moment of speaking**; **going to** was **already alive** in your
> mind before you spoke.

---

## 5. 🧮 Formula

### ✦ Part A — WILL
Model verb: **go**. Helper: **will** (same for **all** subjects! 🎉). Main verb = **V1** (base).

#### ➕ Positive
```
   S  +  will  +  V1
        (I'll, you'll, he'll, she'll, it'll, we'll, they'll)
```
- I **will go**. / I'**ll** go. · She **will help**. / She'**ll** help.

#### ➖ Negative
```
   S  +  will not  +  V1     (won't)
```
- I **won't go**. · They **won't help**.

#### ❓ Question
```
   Will  +  S  +  V1  ?
```
- **Will** you **come**? · **Will** she **call**?

#### ✔️ Short Answers
| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Will you help? | Yes, I **will**. | No, I **won't**. |

---

### ✦ Part B — BE GOING TO
Helper: **am / is / are** + **going to** + **V1**.

#### ➕ Positive
```
   S  +  am/is/are  +  going to  +  V1
        (I'm, he's/she's/it's, you're/we're/they're)
```
- I **am going to** study. / I'**m going to** study. · She **is going to** travel.

#### ➖ Negative
```
   S  +  am/is/are  +  NOT  +  going to  +  V1
        (isn't / aren't going to)
```
- I'**m not going to** wait. · They **aren't going to** come.

#### ❓ Question
```
   Am/Is/Are  +  S  +  going to  +  V1  ?
```
- **Are** you **going to** eat? · **Is** he **going to** call?

#### ✔️ Short Answers
| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Are you going to come? | Yes, I **am**. | No, I'**m not**. |
| Is she going to help? | Yes, she **is**. | No, she **isn't**. |

> ⚠️ After **will** and after **going to**, always use the **base verb (V1)**: *will **go**,
> going to **go*** — never *will goes / going to goes*.

---

## 6. 🏗️ Sentence Structure

```
 WILL                                    BE GOING TO
 ┌─────┬──────┬─────┐                    ┌─────┬─────────┬──────────┬─────┐
 │  S  │ will │ V1  │                    │  S  │ am/is/  │ going to │ V1  │
 │ She │ will │help │                    │ She │   is    │ going to │help │
 └─────┴──────┴─────┘                    └─────┴─────────┴──────────┴─────┘

 NEGATIVE: She won't help.               NEGATIVE: She isn't going to help.
 QUESTION: Will she help?                QUESTION: Is she going to help?
```

> 💬 **Contractions:** *I will → I'll · will not → won't · I am going to → I'm gonna* (informal
> speech only — write *going to*).

---

## 7. 📜 Grammar Rules

1. **will + V1** is the **same for all subjects** (no *-s*): *He **will** come* (not *wills*).
2. **be going to** changes the *be* part: **am / is / are**, matching the subject.
3. Both are followed by the **base verb (V1)**.
4. **will** → decisions made **now**, predictions, promises, offers, refusals (*won't*).
5. **be going to** → **prior plans/intentions** and predictions based on **present evidence**.
6. In **time clauses** (after *when, before, after, as soon as, until, if*), use the **present
   simple**, NOT *will*: *"I'll call you **when I arrive**."* (not *when I will arrive*).

> 🧠 **Golden rule:** decided **before** speaking → *going to*. decided **as you speak** →
> *will*. Both use **V1**.

---

## 8. ✏️ Spelling / Form Notes

There are **no special endings** to add — the main verb stays in its **base form**:

| ❌ | ✅ |
|----|----|
| She will **goes**. | She will **go**. |
| He is going to **plays**. | He is going to **play**. |
| They will **to come**. | They will **come**. *(no "to" after will)* |
| I will **bought**. | I will **buy**. *(base form, not past)* |

> 📌 The only "form" choices: **will/won't** (no change by subject) and **am/is/are** (change by
> subject). The verb itself never changes.

---

## 9. 🚩 Signal Words

```
 FUTURE TIME:    tomorrow · next week/month/year · soon · later · tonight · in 2030
 PREDICTION:     I think · probably · maybe · I'm sure · I expect · perhaps
 PLAN/INTENTION: I'm planning to · I intend to · I've decided to
 IN ... TIME:    in an hour · in two days · in a few minutes
```

| Signal | Likely form | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **tomorrow / next week** | either | I'**ll** call you tomorrow. / I'**m going to** call tomorrow. |
| **I think / probably** | **will** | I think it'**ll** be fine. |
| **Look! / those clouds** | **going to** | Look — it'**s going to** fall! |
| **I'm planning to** | **going to** | I'**m going to** start a course. |
| **as soon as / when** | present + will | I'**ll** text you **when I get** there. |

> 💡 *I think / probably / I'm sure* lean toward **will**; visible **evidence** leans toward
> **going to**.

## 10. ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| She **will goes** tomorrow. | She **will go** tomorrow. | will + **V1** |
| He **will to call** you. | He **will call** you. | no *to* after will |
| I **am going to study** is decided… *(spontaneous)* | (offer) I'**ll study** with you. | instant decision → will |
| I'll call you when I **will arrive**. | I'll call you when I **arrive**. | time clause → present simple |
| We **going to** travel. | We'**re going to** travel. | need *am/is/are* |
| It **will to rain**. | It **will rain**. | base verb |
| I **will can** help. | I **will be able to** help. | no two modals → *be able to* |
| She **wills** come. | She **will** come. | will never adds *-s* |

> 📌 **Top Azerbaijani traps:** (1) adding *-s* or *to* after *will*, (2) using *will* in time
> clauses (*when I will…* ❌), and (3) forgetting *am/is/are* in *going to*.

## 11. ⚖️ Comparison with Similar Tenses

### 🆚 will vs. be going to (THE key comparison!)

| | **will** | **be going to** |
|--|---------|-----------------|
| **Decision** | made **now** (spontaneous) | made **before** (planned) |
| **Prediction** | based on **opinion/belief** | based on **present evidence** |
| **Feel** | offer, promise, instant reaction | intention, plan, "I can see it coming" |
| **Example** | *(phone rings)* I'**ll** get it. | I'**m going to** call him later (planned). |
| **Example** | I think it'**ll** rain. | *(dark clouds)* It'**s going to** rain. |

```
   will:       💡 decide → speak → act        ("OK, I'll do it!")
   going to:   🧠 already decided → speak → act ("I'm going to do it.")

   PREDICTION:
   will:       I think... (in my head)
   going to:   I can SEE it (evidence now)  👀
```

> 🧠 **Two test questions:**
> ① Did you decide **just now**? → **will**. Did you decide **before**? → **going to**.
> ② Is your prediction a **belief** or **visible evidence**? belief → **will**; evidence → **going to**.

### 🆚 will / going to vs. Present Continuous (for arrangements) — preview

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **Present Continuous** | a fixed **arrangement** (time/place set) | I'**m meeting** Ali at 6. |
| **be going to** | an **intention** (maybe not arranged yet) | I'**m going to** meet Ali soon. |
| **will** | a decision **now** / prediction | I'**ll** meet him if he calls. |

> ⏭️ The Present Continuous for future **arrangements** is coming up (Ch. 13). Preview:
> *"I'm flying to Baku on Monday"* (ticket booked) feels more **fixed** than *"I'm going to
> fly to Baku."*

### 🔑 will + time clauses

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| I'll call when I **will arrive**. | I'll call when I **arrive**. |
| We'll start after they **will come**. | We'll start after they **come**. |

> 📌 After *when, after, before, as soon as, until, if* → **present simple**, even though the
> meaning is future.

## 12. 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani Translation Tips

1. **Both *will* and *going to* often map to Azerbaijani "-acaq/-əcək":**
   - *I **will go*** → *Mən ged**əcəyəm***
   - *I'**m going to** go* → *Mən ged**əcəyəm*** (niyyət)
   - ⚠️ This is **why the difference is hard** — Azerbaijani uses **one** form for both. You must
     decide by **context**: planned before, or decided now?

2. **be going to (plan/intention)** ≈ *"...maq fikrindəyəm / niyyətindəyəm"* / *"-acaq"*:
   - *I'**m going to** study medicine* → *Tibb oxu**maq fikrindəyəm**.*

3. **won't (refusal)** ≈ *"-mayacaq / istəmir"*:
   - *The car **won't** start* → *Maşın işə düş**mür / düşməyəcək**.*

4. **Time clauses:** Azerbaijani often uses future in both clauses, but English uses **present**
   in the *when*-clause: *Çatanda zəng edəcəyəm* → *I'll call **when I arrive*** (not *will
   arrive*).

> 💡 **Strategy:** since Azerbaijani has one future form, train the **two questions** from §11
> (decided when? belief or evidence?) to choose **will** vs **going to**. 🔮

## 23. 🗣️ Speaking Tips

- ✅ Use **will / I'll** for **on-the-spot** offers and decisions: *"I'll get that for you."*
- ✅ Use **going to** for **plans you already have**: *"I'm going to learn to drive."*
- ✅ Use **contractions** (*I'll, won't, I'm gonna*) — it sounds natural.
- ⚠️ Don't use *will* after *when/if/as soon as* — use the **present simple**.
- ⚠️ Don't stack modals: *"I **will be able to** help"*, not *"I will can help."*

> 🗣️ **Shadow:** *"I'll call you later."* · *"We're going to move."* · *"It'll be fine."* ·
> *"I won't forget."*

---

## 24. ✍️ Writing Tips

- In essays, **will** states predictions and outlines: *"This report **will** show…"*
- Use **going to** for stated plans/intentions and evidence-based forecasts.
- Keep **time clauses** in the present: *"When the policy **changes**, prices **will** rise."*
- Avoid overusing *gonna* in writing — write **going to**.

## 25. 🎧 Listening Tips

## 26. 📖 Reading Tips

- News/forecasts use **will/going to** for predictions: *"Temperatures **will** rise."*
- Plans and roadmaps use **going to**: *"The government **is going to** invest…"*
- *shall* in contracts/laws = obligation: *"The tenant **shall** pay rent monthly."*

## 27. 🌟 Native Speaker Usage

- **Instant helpfulness:** *"I'll get it!"* (the phone, the door, the bill).
- **Reassurance:** *"It'll be okay."* / *"You'll be fine."*
- **Casual plans:** *"We're gonna grab a bite — wanna come?"*

> 💡 Natives constantly **contract** (*I'll, you'll, won't*) and use **gonna** in speech. Full

## 28. ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: will or going to — quick rule?**
Decided **now** / prediction-belief → **will**. Decided **before** / evidence you can see →
**going to**.

**Q2: Are they ever interchangeable?**
Often, yes — for general future facts: *"I'll see you tomorrow"* ≈ *"I'm going to see you
tomorrow."* The nuance is decision timing.

**Q3: Why can't I say "when I will arrive"?**
After *when/if/as soon as/before/after*, English uses the **present simple** for future meaning:
*"when I **arrive**."*

**Q4: What about "shall"?**
Formal/British, mainly with *I/we* for offers & suggestions: *"**Shall** we go?"* In law =
obligation.

**Q5: Is "gonna" correct?**
In **speech**, yes (casual). In **writing**, use **going to**.

**Q6: will + can?**
No — you can't stack two modals. Use *"will be able to"*: *"I'll **be able to** come."*

# 📚 Mini Story — *"The Big Decision"* (≈320 words)

> Watch **will** (decisions made *now*, promises) and **be going to** (plans already made,
> evidence) work side by side. Read aloud and notice **why** each form is used.

Aysel **sat** at the kitchen table with the letter in her hand. She **had** read it three
times. The university in London **had accepted** her.

"I'**m going to** take it," she **said** quietly. She **had** already **decided** in her heart.
"I'**m going to** study there."

Her mother **looked** up, worried. "It'**s** so far. Who **will** cook for you? Who **will** look
after you?"

Aysel **smiled**. "I'**ll** be fine, Mum. I **promise** I'**ll** call every day. And I'**ll**
come home for every holiday."

"But the money…" her father **began**.

"I'**ve** thought about that," Aysel **said**. "I'**m going to** get a part-time job. I'**m
going to** save every month. And I'**m going to** apply for a scholarship."

Her father **was** quiet for a moment. Then he **nodded**. "If you work hard, you'**ll**
succeed. I'**m** sure of it."

Outside, dark clouds **were** gathering. "Look," her mother **said**, glancing at the window.
"It'**s going to** rain."

"Then I'**ll** close the windows," Aysel **said**, jumping up. As she **shut** them, she
**felt** a strange mix of fear and excitement.

"Promise me one thing," her mother **said**. "Promise you **won't** forget us."

Aysel **hugged** her. "I **will** never forget you. **Never**. And one day, when I **finish**
my studies, I'**ll** come back and make you proud."

That night, Aysel **couldn't** sleep. She **kept** imagining the future. *In a few months,* she
**thought**, *I'**ll** be in a new city. I'**m going to** meet new people. Everything **is going
to** change.*

She **wasn't** scared anymore. She **was** ready.

Tomorrow, she **would** reply to the letter. But tonight, for the first time in weeks, she
**smiled** herself to sleep.

### 🔎 Story Notes — Spot the Grammar

- **be going to (plans already decided / evidence):** *I'm going to take it, I'm going to study,
  I'm going to get a job, I'm going to save, I'm going to apply, it's going to rain, I'm going
  to meet new people, everything is going to change.* → decided **before** speaking, or **visible
  evidence** (the clouds).
- **will / 'll (decisions now, promises, predictions):** *Who will cook?, I'll be fine, I'll
  call, I'll come home, you'll succeed, I'll close the windows, you won't forget, I will never
  forget, I'll come back.* → promises and **on-the-spot** decisions ("Then I'll close the
  windows").
- **The contrast in action:** her **firm plan** uses *going to* (*"I'm going to study there"*);
  her **instant reaction** to the clouds uses *will* (*"Then I'll close the windows"*). Same
  scene, two forms — exactly §11.
- **Time clause:** *"when I **finish** my studies, I'**ll** come back"* → present simple after
  *when*, will in the main clause. ✅
- **Bonus:** *"she **would** reply"* is **future-in-the-past** (Ch. 17 preview) — *will* seen
  from a past viewpoint.

> (instant decisions/promises), and explain **why** each form fits.

# 🧠 Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

1. **WILL = "decided at the WINDOW of speaking."** 💡 The decision pops up **now** (offers,
   promises, predictions).

2. **GOING TO = "the plan was already GOING in your head."** 🧠 It existed **before** you spoke.

3. **Evidence = going to.** 👀 If you can **see** it coming (clouds, a stumble), use *going to*:
   *"It's going to fall!"*

4. **After WHEN/IF, drop the WILL.** ⛔ *"When I **arrive**…"* (present), not *"when I will
   arrive."*

5. **No double modals.** *will + can* ❌ → *will be able to* ✅.

6. **won't ≠ want.** 🔊 Round your lips for **won't** /wəʊnt/.

# 🧬 EXPANSION PACK — Future Simple (Go Deeper)

> You asked to **EXPAND**. These advanced notes (E1–E12) take you from "I know will/going to"
> to "I use the whole future system." Each block builds on §5 and §11.

---

## E1. 🔀 The First Conditional (if + present, will + V1)

For **real, possible future** conditions, use **if + present simple** in one clause and **will
+ V1** in the other:

```
   IF + present simple , ... WILL + V1
   If it RAINS, we WILL stay home.   (a real possibility)
```

| If-clause (present) | Result (will + V1) |
|---------------------|--------------------|
| If you **study**, | you **will pass**. |
| If she **calls**, | I **will answer**. |
| If we **hurry**, | we **won't miss** it. |

> ⚠️ Never put *will* in the **if-clause**: ❌ *If it **will rain**…* → ✅ *If it **rains**…*
> 🇦🇿 *"Yağış yağsa, evdə qalacağıq."* (Full first conditional = Ch. 24.)

---

## E2. ⏰ Time Clauses — present simple for the future

After **when, as soon as, before, after, until, by the time, once, the moment**, use the
**present simple** (or present perfect), even for future meaning — *will* goes in the **other**
clause only:

| Time clause (present) | Main clause (will) |
|-----------------------|--------------------|
| **When** I **arrive**, | I'**ll** call you. |
| **As soon as** it **stops**, | we'**ll** go out. |
| **Before** you **leave**, | I'**ll** give you the keys. |
| **After** the film **ends**, | we'**ll** eat. |
| **Until** he **apologises**, | I **won't** speak to him. |
| **By the time** you **read** this, | I'**ll have** left. *(future perfect, Ch. 15)* |

```
   ⛔ when I WILL arrive        ✅ when I arrive
   ⛔ as soon as it WILL stop   ✅ as soon as it stops
```

> 🧠 **Rule:** *will* lives in the **result**, never in the **time/if clause**. One *will* per
> sentence, on the main action.

---

## E3. 🎩 *shall* — offers, suggestions, and formal obligation

**shall** is mostly British/formal. Three main uses:

| Use | Subject | Example |
|-----|---------|---------|
| **Offer** | I | **Shall** I open the window? |
| **Suggestion** | we | **Shall** we begin? / **Shall** we go? |
| **Formal obligation** (law/rules) | any | The tenant **shall** pay rent on time. |

```
   Shall I…?  = Do you want me to…?   (offering to help)
   Shall we…? = Let's…?               (suggesting together)
```

> 💡 In everyday modern English (especially American), *shall* for the future is rare — *will*
> covers it. But *"Shall I…?"* and *"Shall we…?"* are still very natural and polite.

---

## E4. 🔁 *will* for willingness, habits & refusal

Beyond predictions, **will / won't** show **willingness** and **typical behaviour**:

| Use | Example | Note |
|-----|---------|------|
| **Willingness** | I'**ll** help you. / She **will** listen if you ask. | readiness to act |
| **Refusal (won't)** | He **won't** apologise. | a person refusing |
| **Machine refusal** | The car **won't** start. | a thing not working |
| **General truth/habit** | Oil **will** float on water. | predictable behaviour |
| **Annoying habit** *(stressed will)* | He **will** leave the door open! | irritation |

> 📌 **won't** is the everyday way to say "**refuses to**": *"The door won't open"* = it refuses
> to open. 🇦🇿 *"Qapı açılmır."*

---

## E5. 🗺️ The "Many Futures" Map — 5 ways to talk about the future

English has **several** future forms. Here's the overview (some are later chapters):

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **will + V1** | decision now · prediction · promise | I'**ll** call you. |
| **be going to + V1** | plan/intention · evidence | I'**m going to** travel. |
| **Present Continuous** | fixed **arrangement** (Ch. 13) | I'**m meeting** Ali at 6. |
| **Present Simple** | timetable/schedule (E9) | The train **leaves** at 9. |
| **be about to / due to** | very near future (E6) | We'**re about to** start. |

```
   Certainty / fixedness ladder (loosest → tightest):
   will (predict)  <  going to (intend)  <  Present Cont. (arrange)  <  Present Simple (timetable)
```

> ⏭️ Chapters 12–14 cover the continuous & perfect futures. This map shows where **will/going
> to** sit in the bigger picture.

## E6. ⏳ The Near Future — *be about to / on the verge of / due to*

For something happening **very soon**:

| Structure | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **be about to + V1** | on the point of (any moment) | The film **is about to** start. |
| **be on the verge of + V-ing** | very close to | They'**re on the verge of** a deal. |
| **be due to + V1** | scheduled/expected to | The train **is due to** arrive at 6. |

> 💡 *"I was about to call you!"* (past) is a very common, natural phrase = I almost did it.

## E7. 📊 Degrees of Certainty — how sure is the future?

Soften or strengthen a prediction with adverbs and modals:

```
 100% ── will definitely ── will probably ── will ── may/might/could ── probably won't ── definitely won't ── 0%
```

| Certainty | Expression | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Sure | **will definitely** | It'**ll definitely** work. |
| Likely | **will probably** | She'**ll probably** come. |
| Possible | **may / might / could** | It **might** rain. |
| Unlikely | **probably won't** | They **probably won't** agree. |
| Impossible | **definitely won't** | That **definitely won't** happen. |

> 📌 Adverb position: *will* + **adverb** + V1 → *"It will **probably** rain."* (probably between
> will and the verb). *might/may/could* replace *will* and mean "maybe."

---

## E8. 👀 will vs going to for predictions — the evidence test

Both predict, but the **basis** differs:

| Basis | Form | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Opinion / belief / I think** | **will** | I think it'**ll** be a good film. |
| **Present evidence (you can see it)** | **going to** | The actor just collapsed — this **is going to** be bad. |

```
   "It will rain."        → my belief / the forecast (in my head)
   "It's going to rain."  → I can SEE the black clouds NOW  👀
```

> 🧠 **Quick test:** Can you point to **evidence right now**? → *going to*. Is it just your
> **opinion**? → *will*.

---

## E9. 🚆 Present Simple for the Future — timetables & schedules

For **fixed timetables** (transport, cinema, official schedules), English uses the **present
simple** with future meaning:

| Example | Why |
|---------|-----|
| The train **leaves** at 9:00. | timetable |
| The film **starts** at 8. | schedule |
| The shop **opens** tomorrow. | fixed opening |
| Our flight **departs** at noon. | timetable |

```
   Timetable future = Present Simple:  "The bus comes at 7."  (not "will come")
```

> 💡 This feels **most fixed** of all — it's an official schedule, not a personal plan. Compare:
> *"The train **leaves** at 6"* (timetable) vs *"I'**m going to** leave at 6"* (my plan).

## E10. 🙏 Politeness & softening with *will / won't*

| Function | Example | Note |
|----------|---------|------|
| **Polite request** | **Will** you pass the salt? | a request |
| **Polite offer** | I'**ll** get that for you. | helpfulness |
| **Soft "just"** | I'**ll just** check, one second. | minimising |
| **Reassurance** | It'**ll** be fine. / You'**ll** be okay. | comfort |
| **Firm refusal** | I **won't** do it. | strong "no" |

> 💡 *"That'll be £10, please"* and *"That'll be the postman"* use *will* for "**that is
> probably**" — a soft, confident guess about the present.

- **Evidence prediction:** *"It's going to rain in a moment"* — the woman reads the sky (her
  evidence), so *going to*, not *will*. ✅
- **Time clause:** *"When that moment **comes**, you **will** remember"* → present simple after
  *when*, will in the main clause. ✅
- **might:** *"what **might** happen"* = a softer, possible future (degree of certainty, E7).

> why each form fits. Then make your **own** three predictions about next year using *will*.
