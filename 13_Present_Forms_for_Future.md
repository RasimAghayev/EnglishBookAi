# 🗓️ Present Forms for the Future — Arrangements & Timetables

### *Part 3 · Chapter 13 · Present Continuous (arrangements) + Present Simple (schedules)*

> **Azərbaycanca qısa giriş:** İngiliscə gələcəyi bildirmək üçün bəzən **indiki zamanlardan** istifadə
> olunur — bu, azərbaycanlılar üçün qəribə görünə bilər. İki hal var: **(1) Present Continuous** —
> *təsbit olunmuş, şəxsi razılaşmalar* (vaxt/yer/adam bəlli): *"I'm meeting Ali at 6"* (saat 6-da Əli
> ilə görüşürəm). **(2) Present Simple** — *cədvəllər, qrafiklər, proqramlar* (rəsmi, dəyişməz):
> *"The train leaves at 9"* (qatar 9-da yola düşür). Hər ikisi gələcəyə aiddir, amma fərqli "dad"
> daşıyır. Bu fəsil onları *going to* və *will* ilə də müqayisə edir. 🗓️

---

## 1. 🧭 Introduction / Giriş

English can use **present tense forms to talk about the future**. Two of them:

```
   PRESENT CONTINUOUS  →  fixed arrangements (diary)   "I'm seeing the dentist tomorrow."
   PRESENT SIMPLE      →  timetables / schedules        "The plane takes off at 7:40."
```

🇦🇿 Diqqət: forma indiki zamandır, amma **məna gələcəkdir**. Fərqi bir **gələcək vaxt sözü** və ya
kontekst göstərir (*tomorrow, at 6, on Monday*).

---

## 2. ❓ Why do we use them? / Niyə işlədirik?

| Form | Future meaning | Example | 🇦🇿 |
|------|----------------|---------|-----|
| **Present Continuous** | a **personal arrangement** already set (time/place/people) | *We're having dinner with Sara on Friday.* | Cümə Sara ilə şam yeyirik. |
| **Present Simple** | a **fixed timetable/schedule** set by a calendar/authority | *The shop opens at 9 tomorrow.* | Mağaza sabah 9-da açılır. |

> 🔑 **Arrangement vs timetable.** An *arrangement* is between people ("we agreed a time"); a
> *timetable* is impersonal and official ("the bus company decided"). 🇦🇿 Razılaşma = insanlar arası;
> cədvəl = rəsmi/dəyişməz.

---

## 3. 🌍 Real-life situations / Həyati vəziyyətlər

```
   • Reading your diary:      "I'm flying to Baku on Monday."
   • Plans with friends:      "We're meeting at the café at 7."
   • Appointments:            "She's seeing the doctor tomorrow."
   • Transport schedules:     "The 8:15 train leaves from platform 2."
   • Programmes/events:        "The conference starts on June 3rd."
   • Opening/closing times:    "The museum closes at 6 on Sundays."
```

## 4. ⏳ Timeline / Zaman xətti

```
   PRESENT CONTINUOUS (arranged future)
        NOW ─────●────────────────────►  ⏰ Friday: dinner with Sara
                 │  (we agreed it earlier — it's "in the diary")

   PRESENT SIMPLE (timetable future)
        🕘 09:00  09:00  09:00 …  fixed, repeating, official → "The shop opens at 9."
        (set by a schedule, not by you)
```

---

## 5. 🏗️ Formula / Formul

```
 PRESENT CONTINUOUS for the future
   (+)  S + am/is/are + V-ing + (future time)      I'm leaving on Sunday.
   (−)  S + am/is/are + not + V-ing + (future time) I'm not leaving on Sunday.
   (?)  Am/Is/Are + S + V-ing + (future time)?      Are you leaving on Sunday?

 PRESENT SIMPLE for the future
   (+)  S + V1(-s) + (future time)                  The film starts at 8.
   (−)  S + don't/doesn't + V1 + (future time)      The film doesn't start until 9.
   (?)  Do/Does + S + V1 + (future time)?           Does the film start at 8?
```

> ⚠️ The **future time marker** (or clear context) is what makes the present mean the future.
> *"I'm meeting Ali."* (could be now) → *"I'm meeting Ali **at 6 tomorrow**."* (clearly future).

---

## 6. 🧱 Sentence Structure / Cümlə quruluşu

```
   PRESENT CONTINUOUS (arrangement)
   ┌─────────┬──────────┬───────┬──────────────────┐
   │ Subject │ am/is/are│ V-ing │ future time       │
   │  We      │   are    │ flying│ to Baku on Monday.│
   └─────────┴──────────┴───────┴──────────────────┘

   PRESENT SIMPLE (timetable)
   ┌──────────┬────────┬──────────────────┐
   │ Subject   │ V1(-s) │ time / schedule   │
   │ The train │ leaves │ at 9:05.          │
   └──────────┴────────┴──────────────────┘
```

---

## 7. 📐 Grammar Rules / Qaydalar

1. **Present Continuous = arrangement.** Use when a time/place is **agreed** with people.
2. **Present Simple = timetable.** Use for **scheduled** events fixed by a calendar/clock/authority.
3. A **future time expression** (or clear context) is usually needed so the present isn't read as "now".
4. **Present Simple (not *will*) after time/conditional words:** *when, after, before, as soon as,
   until, if* → use the present. ✅ *I'll call you when I **arrive**.* ❌ *…when I will arrive.*
5. Stative verbs (know, want, be) don't take the continuous, so for arrangements with *be* we use
   *going to* / *will* instead: ✅ *I'm going to be there at 6.*

---

## 8. 🎯 The "future marker" rule / "Gələcək siqnalı" qaydası
```
   Present Continuous alone can mean NOW or FUTURE.
   A future time word forces the FUTURE reading:

      "I'm working."                 → now (probably)
      "I'm working this Saturday."   → future arrangement ✅
```
> 🇦🇿 Gələcək məna üçün **vaxt sözü** əlavə et: *tomorrow, tonight, on Monday, at 6, next week*.

---

## 9. 🔔 Signal Words / Siqnal sözləri
```
   ARRANGEMENTS (Pres. Continuous):  tonight · tomorrow · this weekend · on Friday ·
                                     at 6 · next week · soon · later
   TIMETABLES (Pres. Simple):         starts/leaves/opens/closes/arrives/begins/ends + time ·
                                     at 9 · on the 3rd · every day · the 8:15
   TIME CLAUSES (Pres. Simple):       when · after · before · as soon as · until · once · if
```

## 10. ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Ümumi səhvlər

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why / 🇦🇿 |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| I will meet Ali at 6 (already arranged). | I**'m meeting** Ali at 6. | razılaşma → Present Continuous |
| The train is leaving at 9 every day. | The train **leaves** at 9 every day. | cədvəl → Present Simple |
| I'll call you when I **will arrive**. | …when I **arrive**. | "when" sonra indiki zaman |
| The film **is starting** at 8 (scheduled). | The film **starts** at 8. | proqram → Present Simple |
| I'm seeing Ali. (meaning future, no time) | I'm seeing Ali **tomorrow**. | gələcək üçün vaxt sözü lazımdır |
| We are going to the concert **start** at 9. | The concert **starts** at 9. | iki ayrı cümlə qarışıb |

## 11. 🔀 Comparison — the four future forms / Dörd gələcək forması

```
   FIXED ARRANGEMENT (diary)   → Present Continuous   "I'm meeting Ali at 6."
   TIMETABLE / SCHEDULE        → Present Simple        "The train leaves at 9."
   PLAN / INTENTION            → going to (Ch.12)      "I'm going to meet Ali."
   EVIDENCE PREDICTION         → going to (Ch.12)      "It's going to rain."
   DECISION NOW / OFFER / OPINION → will (Ch.11)        "I'll meet him — good idea!"
```

> 🇦🇿 **Qısa qayda:** dəqiq vaxt/yer razılaşıbsa → Present Continuous; rəsmi cədvəl → Present Simple;
> niyyət → *going to*; indicə qərar/təklif → *will*.

### going to vs Present Continuous (the close pair)
```
   "I'm going to meet Ali."   → intention (maybe time not fixed yet)
   "I'm meeting Ali at 6."     → arrangement (time fixed, in the diary)
   → Often interchangeable; Present Continuous sounds more "settled".
```

## 28. ❓ FAQ
**Q: Can I use *will* for arrangements?** A: Better not. If it's agreed, natives say *I'm meeting…*.
**Q: Present Continuous or *going to*?** A: Both work for plans; Present Continuous = time fixed.
**Q: Why Present Simple after *when*?** A: English avoids *will* in time/conditional clauses.

# 📖 Mini Story — *"The Itinerary"* (≈250 words)

Leyla is reading her travel plan aloud to her mum. "So — we **fly** out on Saturday at 6:40. The
plane **lands** in Istanbul at nine, and the connecting flight **leaves** at eleven."

"And then?" Mum asks.

"On Sunday I**'m meeting** my old classmate for breakfast. After that, we**'re visiting** the Blue
Mosque — Kanan **'s joining** us there." Leyla checks her phone. "The museum **opens** at nine and
**closes** at six, so we have all day."

"When **do** you **come** back?"

"Our return flight **departs** Wednesday night. But listen — as soon as we **land**, I**'m taking**
you straight to that fish restaurant you love."

Mum smiles. "It **closes** early on weekdays, you know."

"Then we**'re going** the moment we land," Leyla laughs.

That evening, Dad looks at the printed schedule on the fridge. "The taxi **comes** at 4:30 Saturday
morning," he reads. "Everyone**'s getting up** at four. No arguments."

Kanan groans. "Four?! The flight **doesn't leave** until 6:40!"

"Check-in **closes** ninety minutes before," Dad replies calmly. "The plane **doesn't wait** for
anyone. We**'re leaving** at 4:30 — and that **'s** final."

# 🗣️ B. NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT
```
 • DIARY FUTURE:  natives default to Present Continuous for social plans —
                  "I'm seeing them Friday" sounds more natural than "I will see them Friday".
 • TIMETABLE FEEL: Present Simple sounds official/impersonal — perfect for transport & programmes.
 • SPOKEN:        "What are you up to this weekend?" → "I'm…(-ing)".
 • ANNOUNCEMENTS: stations/airports/TV use Present Simple ("the next train departs…").
 • TIME CLAUSES:  natives never say "when I will…"; it's always "when I + present".
 • FORMAL:        "The board convenes…", "Registration commences…".
```

# ♻️ C. ALTERNATIVE EXPRESSIONS

Expressing *"the meeting is at 3 on Friday"* in several ways:

| Alternative | Register | Nuance |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| We**'re meeting** at 3 on Friday. | neutral | arrangement (Present Continuous) |
| The meeting **is** at 3 on Friday. | neutral | stating the schedule (*be* + time) |
| The meeting**'s going to be** at 3. | neutral | plan/intention |
| The meeting **starts** at 3. | neutral | timetable (Present Simple) |
| The meeting **is due to** start at 3. | formal | scheduled/expected |
| The meeting **is scheduled for** 3. | formal | official |
| We**'ve arranged to** meet at 3. | neutral | emphasises the arrangement |

```

 AmE/BrE:        identical usage; AmE "schedule" /ˈskedʒuːl/, BrE /ˈʃedjuːl/.
```

# 🕵️ D. DEEP ANALYSIS TASKS

### Grammar Detective — find & fix, then explain *why*
```
 1. "The plane is leaving at 7 every day."
 2. "I will meet my tutor at 4 — we agreed yesterday."
 3. "Call me when you will be free."
 4. "I'm seeing a film." (meaning: this weekend)
 5. "The shop is opening at 9 tomorrow." (its normal opening time)
```

### Compare the meanings
```
 a) "I'm going to visit Gran."   vs  "I'm visiting Gran on Sunday."
 b) "The train leaves at 9."     vs  "The train is leaving." (right now)
 c) "We'll eat at 8."            vs  "We're eating at 8."
```

## ✅ Deep-Analysis Answer Key
```
 Detective:
 1. "The plane LEAVES at 7 every day." (repeating timetable → Present Simple)
 2. "I'm MEETING my tutor at 4." (arranged → Present Continuous, not will)
 3. "Call me when you ARE free." (no "will" after "when")
 4. "I'm seeing a film THIS WEEKEND." (add a future time marker)
 5. "The shop OPENS at 9 tomorrow." (fixed opening time → Present Simple)

 Compare:
 a) "going to visit" = intention (time maybe loose);  "I'm visiting … on Sunday" = fixed arrangement.
 b) "leaves at 9" = timetable (future);  "is leaving" (no time) = happening now.
 c) "We'll eat at 8" = decision/prediction;  "We're eating at 8" = arrangement already set.
```

# 🧩 EXPANSION PACK — Present Forms for the Future (deep dive)

> A second, deeper layer: the *fixedness* cline, the close pairs, the full time-clause family,
> completion with the present perfect, official/imminent futures (*be to / be about to / be due to*),
> headline & media future, and the AZ traps. 🗓️

## E1 · The "fixedness" cline / Nə qədər təsbit olunub?
```
   LESS FIXED ───────────────────────────────────────────► MORE FIXED
   will              going to            Present Continuous      Present Simple
   (decide now)      (intention)         (arrangement, diary)    (timetable, official)
   "I'll call him."  "I'm going to call." "I'm calling him at 6." "Calls start at 6." 

   Same event, four colours of certainty/source.
```
> 🇦🇿 Eyni hadisə, dörd "rəng": *will* = indicə qərar; *going to* = niyyət; Present Continuous =
> razılaşma (gündəlikdə yazılıb); Present Simple = rəsmi cədvəl (kalendar/saat müəyyən edib).

---

## E2 · Present Continuous vs *going to* — the close pair, deeper / Yaxın cütlük
```
   ✅ INTERCHANGEABLE when a plan exists:
      "I'm meeting Ali tomorrow."  ≈  "I'm going to meet Ali tomorrow."

   ✋ NOT interchangeable:
   1. Pure prediction (no plan) → only going to / will, NEVER Present Continuous
      ✅ "It's going to rain."     ❌ "It's raining tomorrow." (≠ prediction)
   2. Arrangement needing other people/time fixed → Present Continuous is more natural
      "I'm seeing the dentist at 4." (booked)  >  "I'm going to see the dentist." (just intention)
   3. Unarranged intention → going to
      "I'm going to learn Spanish (someday)."  ❌ "I'm learning Spanish someday."
```
> 🔑 Rule of thumb: **plan + fixed details → Present Continuous; intention/prediction → *going to*.**
> 🇦🇿 Detallar (vaxt/yer/adam) təsbit olunubsa → Present Continuous; sadəcə niyyət/proqnoz → *going to*.

---

## E3 · Present Simple future beyond transport / Cədvəldən əlavə
The "timetable" present also covers **fixed programmes set by an authority or calendar**:
```
   • Courses/terms:   "The course runs from May to July."
   • Events:          "Ramadan begins next week."  "The festival ends on Sunday."
   • Itineraries:     "On day three we visit Sheki and drive to Ganja."
   • Opening hours:   "We close at six tonight."
   • Deadlines:       "The offer expires at midnight."
   • TV/cinema:       "The match is on at eight."  "The film comes out in July."
```
> ⚠️ It must be **scheduled by something external** (a calendar, an organisation), not just *your* plan.
> Your personal plan → Present Continuous. 🇦🇿 Xarici cədvəl → Present Simple; **sənin** planın → Continuous.

---

## E4 · The time/conditional clause family (full list) / Zaman-şərt bağlayıcıları
After these words, English uses a **present tense** for future meaning — **never *will*:**
```
   TIME WORDS:   when · after · before · as soon as · once · until / till ·
                 by the time · the moment (that) · the minute (that) · the second (that) ·
                 immediately · directly · whenever · every time
   CONDITION:    if · unless · provided (that) · providing · as long as · so long as ·
                 in case · on condition that · supposing
```
```
   ✅ "I'll text you the moment I land."          ❌ "…the moment I will land."
   ✅ "Wait here until the bell rings."           ❌ "…until the bell will ring."
   ✅ "Unless it rains, we're playing outside."   ❌ "Unless it will rain…"
   ✅ "Take an umbrella in case it rains."        ❌ "…in case it will rain."
```
> 🇦🇿 **"WHEN-dən sonra WILL yoxdur"** — bütün bu sözlərdən sonra indiki zaman işlədilir, baxmayaraq ki
> məna gələcəkdir. Bu, azərbaycanlıların ən tez-tez etdiyi səhvlərdən biridir.

---

## E5 · Completion in the clause — Present Perfect after time words / Tamamlanma
When you want to stress that one action **finishes before** the other, use the **present perfect**
(not *will have*) in the time clause:
```
   "I'll call you when I've finished."        (= after finishing)
   "Once we've eaten, we'll head out."
   "You can go after you've washed up."
   "As soon as the download has completed, restart the app."
```
> 🔍 Nuance: *"when I finish"* = at the moment of finishing; *"when I've finished"* = once it's fully done.
> 🇦🇿 *have + V3* burada "tam bitəndən sonra" mənasını verir — *will have* DEYİL.

---

## E6 · Verbs that resist the -ing future / Davametmə qəbul etməyən fellər
Stative verbs (be, know, like, want, need, understand, belong…) don't normally take the future
Present Continuous. Switch to **going to / will / be**:
```
   ❌ "I'm being there at six."        ✅ "I'll be there at six." / "I'm going to be there."
   ❌ "I'm knowing the result soon."   ✅ "I'll know the result soon."
   ❌ "We're wanting the report Friday." ✅ "We'll want / We need the report by Friday."
   ✅ EXCEPTION (dynamic 'be'): "I'm being careful." (behaviour) — but rare for future arrangements.
```
> 🇦🇿 Hal bildirən fellər (*be, know, want…*) gələcək üçün Present Continuous-da işlənmir → *will / going to*.

---

## E7 · Imminent & emphatic present continuous / Yaxın və qətiyyətli gələcək
The Present Continuous (no explicit time) often means **the very near, already-decided future** —
useful for emphatic statements, refusals and ultimatums:
```
   "I'm leaving." (right now / any second)        — imminent
   "We're not staying for this." (decided, firm)  — emphatic refusal
   "You're coming with me, end of story."          — insistence
   "Right, I'm off." (informal: I'm leaving now)
```
> 🇦🇿 Vaxt sözü olmasa belə, Present Continuous "lap yaxın, artıq qərar verilmiş" gələcəyi bildirə bilər —
> qətiyyətli, emosional çalar daşıyır.

---

## E8 · Future questions — which form? / Gələcək suallar
```
   ARRANGEMENT (plans):   "What are you doing tonight?"  "Who are you meeting?"  "Are you coming?"
   TIMETABLE (schedule):  "What time does it start?"  "When does the train leave?"  "Does the shop open Sunday?"
   ⚠️ DON'T confuse with habit:
      "What do you do?"      = your job (habit)         ≠  "What are you doing?" = now / plan
      "What are you doing Saturday?" = this Saturday (plan)
```
> 🇦🇿 Plan sualı → *are you doing*; cədvəl sualı → *does it start*; peşə/adət sualı → *do you do* (fərqi qarışdırma!).

---

## E9 · Official / scheduled futures — *be to · be due to · be set to* / Rəsmi gələcək
For formal announcements, news, and official plans English uses special "scheduled" patterns:
```
   be to + V1        "The President is to visit Baku in May."        (official plan / instruction)
   be due to + V1    "The flight is due to land at nine."            (scheduled / expected)
   be set to + V1     "Prices are set to rise next month."            (about to, news style)
   be scheduled to   "The launch is scheduled to take place Friday."  (formal)
   be expected to    "Talks are expected to resume soon."             (forecast)
```
> 🎩 *be to + infinitive* also gives **orders/arrangements**: *"You are to report at 8 a.m."* (= must).
> 🇦🇿 Bunlar xəbər/rəsmi dildə cədvəlli gələcəyi bildirir; *be to* həm də əmr/göstəriş verə bilər.

---

## E10 · Imminent future — *be about to · be on the point of* / Az qalıb
```
   be about to + V1            "Hurry — the film is about to start."   (in seconds/minutes)
   be (just) about to + V1     "I was just about to call you!"          (past version)
   be on the point of + V-ing  "She's on the point of resigning."       (formal, very near)
   be on the verge of + V-ing  "They're on the verge of a deal."        (formal)
```
> 🇦🇿 *be about to* = "az qalıb, indicə olacaq". *on the point of / on the verge of* = daha rəsmi, "bir addımlıqda".

---

## E11 · Headline & media future / Başlıq dili
News headlines drop articles/auxiliaries and use the **Present Simple or *to*-infinitive** for the future:
```
   "PM MEETS UNIONS TOMORROW."          (= will meet)
   "PRICES TO RISE IN JULY."            (= are going to / are set to rise)
   "CITY HOSTS SUMMIT NEXT WEEK."       (= is hosting)
```
> 🇦🇿 Qəzet başlıqlarında gələcək çox vaxt **Present Simple** və ya **to + fel** ilə verilir (qısalıq üçün).

---

## E12 · AZ contrastive traps & synthesis / Sintez
| 🇦🇿 thought | ❌ word-for-word | ✅ natural English | Why |
|------------|------------------|--------------------|-----|
| Sabah Əli ilə görüşəcəyəm (razılaşdıq). | I will meet Ali tomorrow. | I**'m meeting** Ali tomorrow. | arranged → Pres. Cont. |
| Qatar 9-da yola düşür. | The train is leaving at 9. | The train **leaves** at 9. | timetable → Pres. Simple |
| Çatan kimi zəng edəcəm. | …when I will arrive. | …when I **arrive**. | no *will* after *when* |
| İş bitən kimi gedirik. | when work will finish | when work **finishes / has finished** | present / present perfect |
| Saat 6-da orada olacam. | I'm being there at 6. | I**'ll be** there at 6. | stative *be* → *will* |
| Film başlamaq üzrədir. | The film is going to start now-now. | The film **is about to** start. | imminent → *be about to* |

```
   ONE-LINE SYNTHESIS:
   diary + details fixed → I'm V-ing   ·   calendar/clock → it V-s
   after when/if/until → present (have+V3 = completion)   ·   stative → will/going to
   official news → is to / is due to   ·   seconds away → is about to
```

## 8) Minimal pairs — same words, different form, different meaning / Cüt nümunələr
```
 a) "I'm meeting Ali at 6."        (arrangement: agreed)
    "I meet Ali at 6 every day."    (habit / routine)
    "I'm going to meet Ali."        (intention)
    "I'll meet Ali — good idea!"    (decision made now)

 b) "The train leaves at 9."       (timetable → future)
    "The train is leaving."         (no time → happening now)

 c) "We're eating at eight."       (arrangement)
    "We eat at eight."              (habit)

 d) "It's going to rain."          (prediction from evidence)
    "It rains a lot here."          (general truth / habit)
    ❌ "It's raining tomorrow."     (Present Continuous ≠ prediction)
```

# 📖 Mini Story #2 — *"Opening Night"* (≈250 words)

The theatre is buzzing. Nigar, the stage manager, taps her clipboard. "Listen up — the doors
**open** at half past seven, and the curtain **rises** at eight sharp. We don't wait for latecomers."

"What **are** we **doing** before that?" asks Tural, still in costume.

"The cast **is meeting** on stage at six for notes. After that, I**'m running** a quick light check."
She glances at her schedule. "The director **is to give** a short speech once the audience **has
settled** — so be ready in the wings."

At 7:45 the lobby fills. "The bar **closes** the moment the bell **rings**," a steward announces,
"so order now."

Backstage, Tural is pale. "I'm not sure I**'m going** out there."

"You **are**," Nigar says firmly. "We **open** in ten minutes."

At 7:58 the warning bell sounds. "Right — places, everyone. The show **is about to start**." The
house lights dim. Through a gap in the curtain, Tural sees the seats fill row by row.

"As soon as the music **stops**," Nigar whispers, "you **walk** on. Don't rush it."

The overture **ends**. A hush falls. Tural breathes, steps into the light — and the audience leans
in. By the interval, he**'s grinning**. "When **does** the second act **begin**?" he asks, suddenly
eager.

Nigar smiles. "The minute you **catch** your breath. We **resume** in fifteen."

## 🏁 End of Chapter 13

You can now use the **present tenses to talk about the future**: the **Present Continuous** for
**fixed personal arrangements** (*"I'm meeting Ali at 6"*) and the **Present Simple** for
**timetables and schedules** (*"The train leaves at 9"*) — plus the vital rule that **time/conditional
clauses take the present, not *will*** (*"when I arrive"*). You compared all **four future forms**,
and worked through the new standard sections (level differences, native insight, alternatives,
deep-analysis tasks). 🗓️

```
 ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ Present Continuous + future time = ARRANGEMENT (diary)            │
 │ Present Simple + schedule        = TIMETABLE (official)           │
 │ after when/if/as soon as/until → PRESENT (never "will")           │
 │ + going to (intention/evidence) + will (decision/offer/opinion)   │
 └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
