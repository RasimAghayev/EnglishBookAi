# 🟥 PART 4 · Chapter 22
# Modals (5) — Will, Would & Shall
### *will · would · shall* — "I'**ll** help you." / "**Would** you mind?" / "**Shall** we?" 🤝

> **The one-line idea.** This trio is about **the will behind an action** — choosing, offering, promising, predicting, refusing.
> *will* = **I choose / I predict** (now & future). *would* = will's **past · unreal · polite** shadow. *shall* = the **"Shall I / we…?"** offer-and-suggestion modal (plus formal/legal future).
> 🇦🇿 *Bu üçlük **hərəkətin arxasındakı iradə** ilə bağlıdır — seçmək, təklif etmək, söz vermək, proqnoz vermək, imtina etmək.*
> *will* = **seçirəm / proqnoz verirəm** (indi və gələcək). *would* = will-in **keçmiş · qeyri-real · nəzakətli** kölgəsi. *shall* = **"Shall I / we…?"** təklif modalı (+ rəsmi/hüquqi gələcək).

## 🎯 In this chapter you will learn to…
```
✅ offer, promise & decide on the spot:   will  ("I'll help you." / "I'll get it!")

✅ describe refusal & stubborn things:     won't ("The car won't start." / "She won't listen.")

✅ recognise the formal / legal use:        shall  ("Tenants shall pay rent monthly.")
```
> 🇦🇿 *Bu fəsildə: təklif, söz, anlıq qərar; proqnoz; imtina (won't); nəzakətli xahiş/dəvət; fərziyyə; keçmiş vərdişlər; üstünlük (would rather/like); Shall I/we…?; rəsmi shall.*

## 🧭 THE BIG PICTURE — one family, three jobs

*Will*, *would* and *shall* are **relatives**. *Would* is what *will* becomes when you push it into the **past**, make it **unreal**, or make it **polite**. *Shall* is the old first-person partner of *will*, now mostly alive in **"Shall I…? / Shall we…?"**

```
        ┌──────────────────────── WILL ─────────────────────────┐
        │  VOLITION  "I'll help you."  (offer / willingness)      │  ← now & future
        │  DECISION  "I'll get it!"     (decided this second)      │
        │  PROMISE   "I'll be there."                              │
        │  PREDICTION "It'll rain." "You'll love it." (belief)    │
        │  REFUSAL   "It won't start." (a thing refuses to work)  │
        └───────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────┘
                                     │  back-shift · make unreal · soften
                                     ▼
        ┌──────────────────────── WOULD ────────────────────────┐
        │  POLITE   "Would you mind?" "Would you like tea?"       │  ← past · unreal · polite
        │  UNREAL   "I'd help if I could."            (→ Ch.24)   │
        │  PAST HABIT "Every summer we'd visit gran." (→ Ch.23)   │
        │  PREFERENCE "I'd rather stay." "I'd love to."           │
        │  PAST REFUSAL "The car wouldn't start."                 │
        └───────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

        ┌──────────────────────── SHALL ────────────────────────┐
        │  OFFER / SUGGEST "Shall I open it?" "Shall we go?"      │  ← 1st-person Q + formal
        │  FORMAL / LEGAL  "The tenant shall pay monthly."         │
        └───────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

> 🧠 **The key mental move:** *would* is not a separate word to memorise — it is **will, one step removed** (into the past, into the imaginary, into politeness). Once you see that, half of *would* explains itself.
> 🇦🇿 ***would** ayrıca öyrəniləcək söz deyil — o, **will-in bir addım uzaqlaşmış halıdır** (keçmişə, xəyala, nəzakətə doğru).*

## 📋 THE FORMS — at a glance

| Form | Affirmative | Negative | Question | Note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **will** | I/you/he **will go** · I'**ll** go | **won't** (will not) go | **Will** you go? | one form for all persons; contracts to **'ll** |
| **would** | I/you/he **would go** · I'**d** go | **wouldn't** (would not) go | **Would** you go? | contracts to **'d**; + bare verb |
| **shall** | I/we **shall go** *(formal)* | **shan't** (shall not) *(BrE, rare)* | **Shall** I/we go? | mainly **I / we**; question = offer/suggestion |

> ⚠️ **Form-traps:** all three take a **bare verb** — ~~will to go~~ → *will go* · ~~would to help~~ → *would help* · ~~he wills~~ / ~~she woulds~~ → no -s.
> 🇦🇿 *Üçü də **məsdərsiz fel** (bare verb) alır: "to" yox, -s yox. I'**d** = I would (burada) — I had ilə qarışdırma; növbəti felə bax: I'd **go** (would), I'd **gone** (had).*

> 🔎 **'d = would or had?** Look at the verb after it. *I'd **go*** (bare verb) = **would.** *I'd **gone*** (past participle) = **had.** 🇦🇿 *Sonrakı felə bax: bare verb → would; V3 → had.*

## 🔑 THE CORE — what *will* really does

*Will* has **two hearts**: the **human** heart (you *choose* something — volition) and the **logical** heart (you *predict* something). Most uses are one of these.

```
   HEART 1 — VOLITION (a choice made by a person now):
        offer:    "I'll carry that for you."
        decision: "Right — I'll take the blue one."   (decided as you speak)
        promise:  "I'll call you tonight, I promise."
        request:  "Will you pass the salt?"

   HEART 2 — PREDICTION (your belief about the future):
        "It'll be cold tomorrow."     "They'll win, I'm sure."     "You'll love this film."
```

| 🤝 Volition (a person chooses) | 🔮 Prediction (you believe / expect) |
|---|---|
| "The phone's ringing." — "I'**ll** get it." | I think it **will** rain later. |
| "I'**ll** help you move on Saturday." | She'**ll** be twenty next month. |
| "**Will** you marry me?" | This **won't** hurt — I promise. |
| "I **won't** tell anyone." *(refusal/promise)* | House prices **will** keep rising. |

> 🧪 **Volition or prediction?** If a **person decides/offers/promises** → volition. If you're **forecasting/guessing the future** → prediction. Many sentences blur the two — that's normal; *will* genuinely carries both.
> 🇦🇿 *Şəxs seçir/təklif edir/söz verir → iradə (volition). Gələcəyi proqnozlaşdırırsan → öncəgörmə. İkisi tez-tez qarışır — bu normaldır.*

> 🗣️ **Instant decision vs plan:** use **will** for a decision made *at the moment of speaking* ("I'**ll** have the soup."); use **going to / present continuous** for a decision *already made* ("I'**m going to** start a course." → Ch.12–13). 🇦🇿 *Anlıq qərar = will; əvvəlcədən qərar = going to.*

## ⚡ *won't* — the modal of REFUSAL (people **and** things)

*Won't* doesn't only mean "will not (in the future)." It often means **refuses to** — and, strikingly, English lets **objects refuse** too.

```
   PERSON refuses:   "She won't listen to anyone."        (= refuses to listen)
   THING "refuses":  "The car won't start."  "The lid won't come off."  "My laptop won't turn on."
   PAST version:     "He wouldn't apologise."  "The door wouldn't open."   (→ wouldn't)
```

| ✅ Refusal *won't / wouldn't* | Reading |
|---|---|
| The engine **won't** start this morning. | it "refuses" — a present problem |
| He **won't** take no for an answer. | he refuses to accept it |
| I asked her, but she **wouldn't** help. | past refusal (a real situation) |
| The window **wouldn't** shut last night. | the thing "refused" in the past |

> 🗣️ **Native feel:** *"It won't work"* about a gadget is everyday English — speakers treat stubborn objects as if they had a will. 🇦🇿 *İngiliscə əşyanın da "imtina etdiyini" deyə bilir: "maşın işə düşmür" = "the car **won't** start."*

## 🎭 *would* — will's past, unreal & polite shadow

This is the busiest modal in the chapter. Every use is *will* "stepped back" one notch.

| Use | Sense | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **polite request** | softer than *will you* | **Would** you open the window, please? |
| **polite offer / invitation** | gentle | **Would** you like some tea? / **Would** you care to join us? |
| **hypothetical result** | unreal *will* (→ Ch.24) | I **would** travel more if I had the money. |
| **past habit** | repeated past action (→ Ch.23) | As children, we **would** play in that field for hours. |
| **future-in-the-past** | reported *will* (→ Ch.17) | He said he **would** call. |
| **past refusal** | *won't* in the past | The printer **wouldn't** print all day. |
| **preference / desire** | with *like/love/rather/prefer* | I'**d** love to come. / I'**d** rather walk. |

> ⚠️ **Past habit *would* vs *used to* (Ch.23):** *would* works only for **repeated actions** (*"We'd swim every day"*), **not** for **states** — you can't say ~~"I would have a dog"~~; that needs *used to* (*"I **used to** have a dog"*). 🇦🇿 *Keçmiş vərdiş üçün **would** yalnız təkrarlanan hərəkətlərə uyğundur; vəziyyət üçün **used to** lazımdır.*

> 🇦🇿 **Contrast goldmine:** Azerbaijani's **-ardı/-ərdi** ending covers *both* English ideas at once — *gedərdi* = "he **would** go" (past habit) **and** "he **would** go" (if…, hypothetical). English splits the load between *would*, *used to* and conditionals; one Azerbaijani form does several jobs.

## 🍵 *would* for PREFERENCES — *would like / would rather / would prefer*

A high-frequency, exam-favourite cluster. All express **what you want**, politely.

| Pattern | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **would like** | + *to* + verb / + noun | I'**d like to** book a table. / I'**d like** a coffee. |
| **would love** | + *to* + verb | I'**d love to** see you again. |
| **would prefer** | + *to* + verb / + noun | I'**d prefer to** stay in. / I'**d prefer** tea. |
| **would rather** | + **bare** verb (no *to*!) | I'**d rather** stay in. / I'**d rather not** talk about it. |
| **would rather** (two subjects) | + **past** verb | I'**d rather** you **didn't** smoke here. |

```
   I'd like / love / prefer  +  TO go        ←  needs "to"
   I'd rather                +  go           ←  NO "to" (bare verb)
   I'd rather  you           +  went         ←  another person → PAST tense
```

> ⚠️ **The classic slip:** ~~I'd rather to go~~ → **I'd rather go.** And *"would like"* ≠ *"like"*: *"I **like** coffee"* (general) vs *"I'**d like** a coffee"* (I want one **now**). 🇦🇿 *would rather + məsdərsiz fel; would like + to. "I'd like" = indi istəyirəm; "I like" = ümumiyyətlə xoşlayıram.*

## 🤝 *shall* — offers, suggestions & the formal future

In modern English, *shall* survives mainly in **questions with *I* and *we***, where it offers help or proposes an action. Statement *shall* (for the future) sounds **formal or old-fashioned** — *will* has taken over.

| Use | Example | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **offer** (Shall I…?) | **Shall I** carry that for you? | = "Do you want me to…?" |
| **suggestion** (Shall we…?) | **Shall we** dance? / **Shall we** order? | = "Let's…, what do you think?" |
| **ask for instructions** | What **shall** I do? / Where **shall** we meet? | seeking a decision |
| **formal / legal future** | The Tenant **shall** pay rent on the 1st. | = obligation in contracts |
| **determination** (formal/old) | We **shall** overcome. / I **shall** return. | emphatic, literary |

```
   Shall I…?  →  an OFFER       "Shall I open the window?"  (= Want me to?)
   Shall we…? →  a SUGGESTION   "Shall we leave now?"        (= Let's…?)
```

> 🗣️ **Native feel:** *"Shall we?"* on its own is a charming, slightly formal invitation to begin something ("Shall we?" — and you both stand up). Outside of *Shall I/we…?* and legal text, most people just use *will*. AmE uses *shall* even less than BrE. 🇦🇿 ***Shall I…?*** = təklif, ***Shall we…?*** = "gəlin… edək?". Qalan hallarda adətən **will** işlənir.*

> 🔁 **Tag for *Let's*:** the question tag for *Let's…* is ***shall we?*** — *"Let's go, **shall we**?"*

## ⏪ THE PAST & beyond — quick map

*Will / would / shall* don't form a neat past tense like ordinary verbs. Here's how time works across them:

| Idea | Present / future | Past |
|---|---|---|
| prediction | It **will** rain. | *(reported)* I knew it **would** rain. |
| willingness / offer | I **will** help. | *(reported)* I said I **would** help. |
| refusal | The car **won't** start. | The car **wouldn't** start. |
| habit | *(now: present simple)* | We **would** / **used to** visit gran. |
| past-from-the-past | — | I **would have helped**, but I didn't know. *(→ Ch.24)* |

> 🇦🇿 *Bu modallar adi keçmiş zaman düzəltmir: gələcək proqnoz → keçmişdə **would** (nəql); imtina → **wouldn't**; vərdiş → **would/used to**.*

## ❓ QUESTIONS, negatives & short answers

| Function | Example |
|---|---|
| request / invitation | **Will** you help me? / **Would** you like to dance? |
| offer / suggestion | **Shall** I get the door? / **Shall** we begin? |
| negatives | I **won't** be late. / I **wouldn't** do that. / We **shan't** be long. *(BrE)* |
| short answers | "Will you come?" — "Yes, I **will**." / "No, I **won't**." |
| | "Would you like one?" — "Yes, I **would**, thanks." / "No, I **wouldn't**." |
| polite softening | **Would** you mind **opening** the window? *(+ -ing!)* |

> ⚠️ **"Would you mind…?"** is followed by **-ing**, not *to*: *Would you mind **waiting**?* And the polite answer is back-to-front — *"No, not at all"* means *"I'm happy to."* 🇦🇿 *"Would you mind + V-ing"; "No" cavabı razılıq bildirir.*

## 🧱 SEMI-MODALS & near-synonyms

| Form | Replaces / shade | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **be willing to** | spells out volition | I'**m willing to** wait. |
| **be going to** | planned future (vs instant *will*) | I'**m going to** call her tonight. |
| **would you mind + -ing** | very polite request | **Would you mind** moving up? |
| **Could / Can you…?** | request (Ch.18) | **Could** you help me? |
| **Let's… / Why don't we…?** | = *Shall we…?* | **Why don't we** start? |
| **Would you care to…?** | formal invitation | **Would you care to** join us? |
| **'ll** for habit | characteristic behaviour | He'**ll** sit there for hours. *(typical of him)* |

> 🇦🇿 *Eyni fikri başqa cür demək olar: be willing to (iradə), be going to (planlı gələcək), Could you…? (xahiş), Why don't we…? (= Shall we…?).*

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| I will to help you. | I **will help** you. | no *to* after a modal |
| She woulds like tea. | She **would like** tea. | modals take no -s |
| I'd rather to stay. | I'**d rather stay.** | *would rather* + bare verb |
| Would you mind to open it? | **Would you mind opening** it? | *mind* + **-ing** |
| I would travelled if I had money. | I **would travel** if I had money. | *would* + bare verb (Ch.24) |

## 🅰️ §A — LEVEL DIFFERENCES (A1 → C2)

How the *same idea* — choosing, offering, predicting — grows with your level:

| Level | What the learner can do | Typical sentence |
|---|---|---|
| **A1** | basic *will* for offers/future; *would like* | "I'**ll** help you." / "I'**d like** a tea." |
| **A2** | *won't*; *will* predictions; *Shall I/we…?* | "It **won't** rain." / "**Shall we** go?" |
| **B1** | polite *would* requests/offers; *would rather* | "**Would** you open the door?" / "I'**d rather** walk." |
| **B2** | *would* for past habits; hypothetical *would*; refusal *wouldn't* | "We'**d** swim every day." / "I'**d** help if I could." |
| **C1** | *would have* (unreal past); *would* for softened opinion | "I **would have** come, but…" / "I **would imagine** so." |
| **C2** | register & nuance; legal *shall*; characteristic *will/would* | "Tenants **shall**…" / "He **would** say that, wouldn't he?" |

> 🇦🇿 *A1: sadə will, would like. A2: won't, Shall we…? B1: nəzakətli would, would rather. B2: keçmiş vərdiş, fərziyyə. C1: would have, yumşaq fikir. C2: üslub, hüquqi shall.*

## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT

1. **Spoken English lives in contractions.** *I'll, you'll, he'd, we'd, won't* dominate speech; the full *will / would* sound emphatic or formal. *"I WILL finish it"* (stressed) = insistence. 🇦🇿 *Danışıqda 'll / 'd / won't üstünlük təşkil edir; tam forma vurğulu səslənir.*
2. **"I'll get it" is reflexive English.** Phone rings, doorbell goes, something drops — natives say *"I'll get it"* automatically. It's the instant-decision *will* in the wild. 🇦🇿 *Anlıq qərar will-i — "mən baxaram / mən götürərəm".*
3. **"Would you…?" out-polites "Will you…?"** Both are requests, but *would* is gentler and safer with strangers. 🇦🇿 *"Would you…?" "Will you…?"-dən daha nəzakətlidir.*
4. **"He would say that"** (stressed *would*) = "that's so typical of him." A characteristic-behaviour *would*, often a little critical. 🇦🇿 *"He **would** say that" = "ondan gözləniləndir" — tənqidi çalar.*
5. **"Shall" is shrinking.** Beyond *Shall I/we…?* and contracts, most natives reach for *will*. In AmE, even *Shall we…?* can sound formal. 🇦🇿 *shall daralır; Shall I/we…?-dən başqa hallarda will.*
6. **"Won't" anthropomorphises objects.** *"It just won't work"* treats a device as stubborn — completely normal, even affectionate, English. 🇦🇿 *"won't" əşyaya iradə verir — "işləmir, inad edir".*

## 🅲 §C — ALTERNATIVE EXPRESSIONS (say it another way)

| Instead of… | You can say… | Shade |
|---|---|---|
| **Will** you help me? | **Could** you help me? / **Would** you mind helping? / Any chance you could help? | more polite / casual |
| I'**ll** do it. | I'**m willing to** do it. / **Let me** do it. / I'**m happy to** do it. | volunteering |
| **Shall** we go? | **Let's** go. / **Why don't we** go? / How about going? | suggestion |
| I'**d like** to… | I **want** to… / I'**d love** to… / I **wouldn't mind** + -ing | varying force |
| It **will** rain. | It'**s going to** rain. / It'**s likely to** rain. | prediction |
| He **won't** listen. | He **refuses to** listen. / He's **not willing to** listen. | refusal |

> 🇦🇿 *Eyni fikri fərqli güc və üslubda demək olar — xahiş, könüllülük, təklif, proqnoz, imtina.*

# 📖 MINI STORY #1 — "The Moving Day"

### *Will for offers and promises, would for politeness and the past, shall for the plan — all in one busy morning.*
### 🇦🇿 *Təkliflər və sözlər üçün will, nəzakət və keçmiş üçün would, plan üçün shall — bir işıqlı səhərdə.*

---

Boxes filled the hallway. Leyla looked at them and sighed.

"Don't worry," said her brother Tural. "I'**ll** help you carry everything down. And Aysel said she'**d** bring the van at ten." *(she'd = she would — a reported promise.)*

"You're a star. **Shall we** start with the heavy ones?" Leyla asked.

"Good idea. **Would** you mind **taking** that lamp first? I'**ll** manage the bookcase."

They worked for an hour. The old wardrobe, though, simply **wouldn't** budge. *(it "refused" — a stubborn object.)* "It **won't** fit through the door," Tural groaned. "We'**ll** have to tip it sideways."

While they rested, Leyla smiled. "You know, when we were little, we'**d** play hide-and-seek in this flat for hours." *(we'd play = past habit.)* "I'**ll** miss it."

"You'**ll** love the new place," Tural promised. "It'**s going to** be wonderful." *(planned/expected future, Ch.12.)*

At ten, the van arrived. "**Would** you like a hand with the boxes?" the driver offered. "Thanks — that'**d** be great," said Leyla. "**Shall I** show you which ones go first?"

By noon the flat was empty. "We **shall** meet again, old flat," Leyla said dramatically, and locked the door. *(formal, theatrical shall.)*

# 🧨 EXPANSION PACK — Will, Would & Shall (E1–E12)
### *The deep layer: the two hearts of will, the unreal past, would vs used to, the politeness ladder, reported speech & idioms.*
### 🇦🇿 *Dərin qat: will-in iki ürəyi, qeyri-real keçmiş, would vs used to, nəzakət pilləkəni, dolayı nitq və idiomlar.*

---

## 🅴1 — The TWO HEARTS of *will* (volition ↔ prediction)

Every use of *will* leans toward one of two centres — a **human choice** or a **logical forecast**:

```
  VOLITION (a person chooses now) ◄─────────────────────────► PREDICTION (you forecast the future)
   offer        decision       promise       request          belief / expectation
  "I'll help."  "I'll take it." "I'll be there." "Will you…?"   "It'll rain." "You'll love it."
   🤝 the will of a PERSON                                       🔮 the will of EVENTS
```

| Leans to VOLITION | Leans to PREDICTION |
|---|---|
| I'**ll** carry that. *(offer)* | It'**ll** be sunny tomorrow. |
| Fine — I'**ll** do it. *(decision)* | They'**ll** win easily. |
| I **won't** let you down. *(promise)* | This **won't** take long. |

> 🧪 **Which heart?** A **person** deciding/offering/promising → volition. A **forecast/guess** about how things will go → prediction. 🇦🇿 *Şəxs seçir → iradə; gələcəyi proqnozlaşdırırsan → öncəgörmə.*

---

## 🅴2 — *will* vs *going to* vs present continuous (the decision question)

All three point to the future, but they package the **decision** differently:

| Form | The decision | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **will** | made **now**, this second | "We're out of milk." — "I'**ll** get some." |
| **be going to** | already **decided** (intention) | I'**m going to** start a diet on Monday. |
| **present continuous** | **arranged** (fixed plan) | I'**m meeting** the dentist at three. |

```
   spontaneous "I'll do it!"   ──►  will          (decided as you speak)
   intention "I'm going to…"   ──►  going to      (decided before, plan in mind)
   arrangement "I'm …ing"      ──►  present cont. (fixed, often with others/time)
```

> 🔗 Full treatment in **Ch.12–13**. The exam favourite: spontaneous offer/decision = *will*; pre-made plan = *going to*. 🇦🇿 *Anlıq qərar = will; əvvəlcədən niyyət = going to; razılaşdırılmış plan = present continuous.*

---

## 🅴3 — *won't / wouldn't* = REFUSAL (the stubborn-object trick)

*Won't* / *wouldn't* often mean **refuse to** — and English lets **machines and objects** "refuse" too:

| Present (*won't*) | Past (*wouldn't*) |
|---|---|
| She **won't** listen. *(refuses)* | She **wouldn't** listen. |
| The car **won't** start. | The car **wouldn't** start. |
| The lid **won't** come off. | The lid **wouldn't** come off. |
| He **won't** take no for an answer. | He **wouldn't** take no for an answer. |

> 🗣️ This is everyday English: a stubborn device *"just won't work."* Compare the neutral *"doesn't"*: *"it doesn't work"* = a fact; *"it won't work"* = it's **resisting** you. 🇦🇿 *İngiliscə əşyanın "imtina etdiyini" deyir: "the car **won't** start" = "maşın işə düşmür".*

---

## 🅴4 — ⚠️ *would have done* — the unreal past (the C1 splitter)

*Would have* + V3 describes a result that **didn't** happen because a condition wasn't met — the imaginary twin of *will*:

```
 would have done:  ❶ a condition was NOT met  →  ❷ so the result did NOT happen
                   "I would have helped"  (…but you didn't ask / I didn't know)
```

| Sentence | What really happened |
|---|---|
| I **would have come**, but I was ill. | I did **not** come. |
| She **would have passed** if she'd studied. | She did **not** pass (she didn't study). |
| We **wouldn't have got** lost with a map. | We **did** get lost (no map). |

> 🔗 This is the **result half of the third conditional** (Ch.24). Don't confuse *would have* (unreal past) with *should have* (Ch.21 — past advice/regret). 🇦🇿 *would have done = şərt yerinə yetmədi, ona görə baş vermədi. should have done (Ch.21) ilə qarışdırma — o, məsləhət/təəssüfdür.*

---

## 🅴5 — *would* vs *used to* for PAST HABITS

Both describe the repeated past — but they don't fully overlap:

| | repeated **actions** | past **states** |
|---|---|---|
| **would** | ✅ "We **would** swim every day." | ❌ ~~"I would have a dog."~~ |
| **used to** | ✅ "We **used to** swim every day." | ✅ "I **used to** have a dog." |

```
   ACTION (repeated)  →  would  OR  used to   ✅
   STATE (have / be / know / like)  →  used to ONLY   ✅   (would ❌)
```

> 🔑 Rule of thumb: if the verb is a **state** (*have, be, know, like, live*), only *used to* works. *Would* also usually needs a **time frame** to feel natural ("Every winter we'd…"). Full chapter: **Ch.23.** 🇦🇿 *Təkrarlanan hərəkət → would və ya used to; vəziyyət → yalnız used to. would adətən zaman çərçivəsi istəyir.*

---

## 🅴6 — *would like / love / rather / prefer* — the preference cluster

| Pattern | Structure | Example | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|
| **would like** | + *to* + V / + noun | I'**d like to** rest. / I'**d like** a coffee. | ≠ *like* (general) |
| **would love** | + *to* + V | I'**d love to** join you. | enthusiastic |
| **would prefer** | + *to* + V / + noun (+ *rather than*) | I'**d prefer to** walk **rather than** drive. | needs *to* |
| **would rather** | + **bare** V | I'**d rather** walk. / I'**d rather not** say. | **no** *to*! |
| **would rather** (2 subjects) | + **past** tense | I'**d rather** you **came** tomorrow. | unreal past |

> ⚠️ Two classics: ~~I'd rather to go~~ → *I'd rather go*; and *"I **like** coffee"* (always) vs *"I'**d like** a coffee"* (now, please). 🇦🇿 *would rather + məsdərsiz fel; would like + to. "I'd like" = indi istəyirəm.*

---

## 🅴7 — The REQUEST / POLITENESS ladder

Same favour, climbing from blunt to very polite:

| Politeness | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| direct order | imperative | **Open** the window. |
| plain request | **Will** you…? / **Can** you…? | **Will** you open the window? |
| polite | **Would** you…? / **Could** you…? | **Would** you open the window? |
| very polite | **Would** you **mind** + -ing? | **Would** you **mind** opening the window? |
| extra-tentative | I **wonder if** you **could**… / **Do** you **think** you could…? | I **wonder if** you could open the window? |

> 🗣️ *Would you mind…?* is followed by **-ing**, and the agreeable answer is the "backwards" one: *"No, not at all"* = "happy to." Overlaps with *can/could* requests (Ch.18). 🇦🇿 *"Would you mind + V-ing"; razılıq cavabı "No, not at all".*

---

## 🅴8 — *shall* — offers, suggestions, the formal future & the *Let's* tag

| Use | Example | Note |
|---|---|---|
| offer (1st person Q) | **Shall I** carry that? | = "Do you want me to?" |
| suggestion (1st-pl Q) | **Shall we** go? | = "Let's…, OK?" |
| ask for a decision | What **shall** I wear? | seeking guidance |
| tag for *Let's* | Let's start, **shall we?** | the fixed tag |
| formal / legal future | The Buyer **shall** pay on delivery. | = obligation in contracts |
| determination (literary) | We **shall** never surrender. | emphatic, formal/old |

> 🗣️ Outside *Shall I/we…?*, the *Let's… shall we?* tag, and legal/formal writing, modern speakers use **will**. AmE uses *shall* even less. 🇦🇿 *Müasir dildə shall əsasən Shall I/we…? və "Let's…, shall we?" tag-ında qalıb; qalan hallarda will.*

---

## 🅴9 — *will / would* in REPORTED SPEECH

*Will* becomes *would* when the reporting verb is past — and *would* (already a back-shifted form) **stays put**:

| Direct | Reported |
|---|---|
| "I **will** help." | He said he **would** help. |
| "It **won't** rain." | She thought it **wouldn't** rain. |
| "I'**d** love to come." | He said he'**d** love to come. *(would stays)* |
| "**Shall** I open it?" | She asked **if she should** open it. *(Shall I → should)* |
| "**Will** you marry me?" | He asked **if she would** marry him. |

> 🔑 *will → would*; *would → would* (no further shift). Offers with *Shall I…?* usually report as ***should***. Full rules in the **Reported Speech** chapter; future-in-the-past *would* is **Ch.17**. 🇦🇿 *will → would; would dəyişmir; "Shall I…?" → should kimi nəql olunur.*

---

## 🅴10 — Emphatic & idiomatic *will / would*

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| stressed **WILL** | insistence / annoyance | He **WILL** keep interrupting! |
| **He would say that** | "so typical of him" | "He blamed the staff." — "He **would**, wouldn't he?" |
| **I would think / imagine / say** | tentative opinion | It's about ten miles, I **would say**. |
| **Boys will be boys** | "that's their nature" | *(fixed saying)* |
| **Accidents will happen** | inevitability | Don't cry — **accidents will happen**. |
| **That'll be the day!** | "I doubt it!" | "He'll apologise." — "**That'll be the day!**" |
| **as luck would have it** | "as it turned out" | **As luck would have it**, the train was late. |

> 🇦🇿 *Bu hazır ifadələr danışıqda çox işlədilir — will/would-un idiomatik üzləri.*

## 🅴11 — WILL / WOULD / SHALL ACROSS TIME (master table)

| Idea | now / future | past | unreal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **prediction** | It **will** rain. | I said it **would** rain. | — |
| **future-perfect** | By June it **will have** finished. | — | By then it **would have** finished *(if…)* |
| **volition / offer** | I **will** help. | I said I **would** help. | I **would** help *(if I could)* |
| **refusal** | It **won't** start. | It **wouldn't** start. | — |
| **habit** | *(present simple)* | We **would / used to** visit. | — |
| **offer / suggestion** | **Shall** I/we…? | — *(reported: should)* | — |

> 🇦🇿 *Bu modallar adi keçmiş düzəltmir: will → would (nəql/qeyri-real); won't → wouldn't; vərdiş → would/used to; Shall I/we…?-nin keçmişi yoxdur.*

---

## 🅴12 — 🇦🇿 CONTRASTIVE SYNTHESIS

| English | 🇦🇿 | Watch out |
|---|---|---|
| It **will** rain. *(prediction)* | Yağış yağacaq. | future **-əcək/-acaq** |
| I'**ll** help you. *(offer/volition)* | Sənə kömək edərəm. | volition often → **-ərəm/-aram** or future |
| The car **won't** start. *(refusal)* | Maşın işə düşmür. | AZ uses **present negative** for "refusing" things |
| **Would** you like tea? | Çay istərdinizmi? | **istərdim** = would like |
| I'**d rather** stay. | Qalmağı üstün tuturam. / Qalsam yaxşıdır. | preference |
| We'**d** visit gran every summer. *(past habit)* | Hər yay nənəgilə **gedərdik**. | **-ardı/-ərdi** = would (habit) |
| I'**d** help if I could. *(unreal)* | Bacarsaydım kömək **edərdim**. | **-ardı/-ərdi** = would (hypothetical) |
| **Shall** we go? | Ged**ək**? | suggestion suffix **-ək/-aq** |

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani overlap trap:** the single ending **-ardı/-ərdi** (*gedərdi*) does the work of **two** English *would*s — the **past-habit** *would* (*we'd visit*) **and** the **hypothetical** *would* (*I'd help if…*). Meanwhile English **future** (*will*) and **future intention** (*going to*) both collapse into Azerbaijani **-əcək**. Before translating, ask: am I **forecasting**, **offering**, **recalling a habit**, or **imagining**?

## 📦 BLOCK 11 — ⚡ MINIMAL PAIRS — one choice changes the meaning

| Pair | What changes | 🇦🇿 |
|---|---|---|
| "I'**ll** get some milk." / "I'**m going to** get milk." | decided now → already planned | indi qərar → əvvəlcədən plan |
| "**Will** you help?" / "**Would** you help?" | plain request → more polite | sadə xahiş → daha nəzakətli |
| "We'**d** swim daily." / "We **used to** have a pool." | habit (action, would ✅) → state (used to only) | hərəkət → vəziyyət |
| "I **like** coffee." / "I'**d like** a coffee." | general taste → I want one now | ümumi zövq → indi istəyirəm |
| "The car **doesn't** start." / "The car **won't** start." | a fact → it's "refusing" | fakt → "imtina edir" |
| "**Shall** we go?" / "**Will** we go?" | suggestion (Let's?) → asking a fact | təklif → fakt sorğusu |

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "The Dinner Party"
### *Offers, promises, predictions, a stubborn oven and a few fond memories — one evening of cooking.*
### 🇦🇿 *Təkliflər, sözlər, proqnozlar, inadkar soba və xoş xatirələr — yemək hazırlanan bir axşam.*

> Read it twice. **First** for the story; **second** circling every *will · won't · would · wouldn't · shall.*
> 🇦🇿 *İki dəfə oxu: əvvəl hekayə üçün, sonra hər modal feli dövrəyə alaraq.*

---

Nigar had invited friends for dinner, and her flatmate Sevda found her staring at the cooker.

"The oven **won't** heat up," Nigar said, jabbing at the dial. *(the appliance "refuses".)* "It just **won't** work tonight, of all nights."

"Relax — I'**ll** take a look," said Sevda. "And if it really **won't** start, we'**ll** use the small one. Everything **will** be fine." *(offer; refusal; plan; prediction.)*

"**Shall I** start on the salad while you fix it?" Nigar asked. *(offer / suggestion.)*

"Good idea. **Would** you **chop** the onions, and I'**ll** sort out the oven?" *(polite request + offer.)*

As they worked, Sevda smiled. "You know, my grandmother **would** cook for twenty people without breaking a sweat. Every Sunday she'**d** fill the whole house with the smell of bread." *(past habits — repeated actions.)*

"She sounds wonderful. I'**d love** to have learned from her," said Nigar. *(preference.)*

The doorbell rang early. "That'**ll** be Elvin — he's always first," laughed Nigar. *(prediction.)* "Elvin, **would** you like something to drink?"

"I'**d rather** wait for the others, thanks," he said. *(preference, bare verb.)*

But the oven still **wouldn't** cooperate. *(past refusal of the object.)* "We **should have** called a repairman last week," Sevda sighed *(Ch.21 — past regret slips in).* "Never mind — the small oven **will** do the job. Dinner **will** just be a little late."

When everyone was seated, Nigar raised her glass. "Thank you all for coming. I promise the food **will** be worth the wait — and next time, I'**ll** check the oven first!" *(promise + resolution.)*

"**Shall we** eat?" said Sevda. And they did.

### ✍️ After-reading tasks
```
1. List every offer in the story. Which use will, which use shall, and which use would?
2. "The oven won't heat up" and "the oven wouldn't cooperate" — what is each saying about the oven,
   and what is the time difference?
3. "She'd cook for twenty people / she'd fill the house with bread." Are these past habits or
   polite requests? How can you tell? Could you use 'used to' instead?
4. Rewrite "Would you chop the onions?" as (a) a plain request and (b) a very direct order.
5. 🇦🇿→EN: "Mən baxaram." / "Salatla başlayımmı?" / "Soğanı doğrayarsınızmı?" / "Hər bazar nənəm çörək bişirərdi."
```
> 💬 **Answers**
> 1. Offers: *"I'll take a look"* (**will** — willingness), *"Shall I start on the salad?"* / *"Shall we eat?"* (**shall** — offer/suggestion), *"Would you like something to drink?"* (**would** — polite offer). Will = I volunteer; Shall I/we…? = Do you want me to / Let's; Would you like…? = gentle invitation.
> 2. *won't heat up* = present — the oven is "refusing" right now. *wouldn't cooperate* = past — it "refused" earlier in the evening. Same idea (a stubborn object), different time: won't = now, wouldn't = then.
> 3. **Past habits** — repeated actions ("every Sunday"). You can tell because there's a time frame and the actions repeated. Yes, *used to* works too: *"she used to cook…"* / *"she used to fill the house…"* (both are actions, so either form fits).
> 4. (a) *"Could you chop the onions?"* / *"Can you chop the onions, please?"* (b) *"Chop the onions."*
> 5. I'll take a look. / Shall I start with the salad? / Would you chop the onions? / Every Sunday my grandmother would (used to) bake bread.

## 🏁 End of Chapter 22

You've now closed the **modal family**. *Will* carries both a **human heart** (offers, decisions, promises, requests — volition) and a **logical heart** (predictions) — and, as *won't*, even lets stubborn **objects refuse**. *Would* is simply *will* **stepped back** — into **politeness** (*Would you…? / Would you like…?*), into the **unreal** (*I'd help if I could* → Ch.24), into the **past** (habits with *would*, refusals with *wouldn't*, reported *will* → Ch.17), and into **preference** (*would like / love / rather / prefer*). *Shall* lives on mainly in **Shall I…? / Shall we…?** (offers and suggestions) plus the **formal/legal** future. Keep the bare verb after all three, mind *would rather* (no *to*) and *would you mind* (+ -ing), and you have the whole family. 🤝

```
 ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ 🤝 will:    offer / decide / promise / predict  "I'll help." "It'll rain." │
 │ 🚫 won't:   refusal (people & things)            "The car won't start."     │
 │ 🙏 would:   polite request / offer               "Would you mind opening…?" │
 │ 🔁 would:   past habit · unreal · reported will  "We'd visit gran."         │
 │ 🍵 would:   preference                            "I'd rather stay."         │

```
