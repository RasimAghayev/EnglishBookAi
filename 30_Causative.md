# 🟪 PART 5 · Chapter 30
# The Causative — getting things done (by others)
### *have / get something done · make · let · help · get someone to* — "I **had** my car **repaired**." / "She **made** me **wait**." 🛠️

> **The one-line idea.** The causative is how English says *"I didn't do it myself — I **arranged** for it, or I **caused** someone to do it."*
> Two branches: **things get done TO them** → *have / get + something + done* ("I had my hair cut"). **People are caused to act** → *make / let / have / get / help + someone + (to) do* ("She made me wait").
> 🇦🇿 *Kauzativ — İngiliscədə "özüm etmədim; **etdirdim**, ya da kiməsə **etdirtdim**" deməyin yoludur.*
> *İki qol: **əşya üzərində iş görülür** → *have/get + əşya + V3* ("saçımı **kəsdirdim**"). **İnsan hərəkətə vadar edilir** → *make/let/have/get/help + kəs + (to) do* ("məni **gözlətdi**").*
> 🇦🇿 *Azərbaycanca bu, felin içindəki **-dır/-dir/-t** şəkilçisidir: kəsmək → kəs**dir**mək, gözləmək → gözlə**t**mək.*

## 🧭 THE BIG PICTURE — "someone else does it"

Normally the subject does the action: *"I repaired the car."* The causative changes that — the subject **organises** the action or **causes** another person to act. English splits into **two patterns** depending on whether a **thing** or a **person** is in the middle:

```
   BRANCH 1 — a THING has something done to it:
        have / get  +  [THING]  +  DONE (past participle)
        "I  had      my car      repaired."     ← a mechanic did it; I arranged it

   BRANCH 2 — a PERSON is caused / allowed to act:
        make / let / have  +  [PERSON]  +  DO (bare infinitive)
        get / help          +  [PERSON]  +  TO DO (to-infinitive)
        "She  made        me         wait."     ← I performed the action (waiting)
        "I    got          him        to help."
```

> 🧠 **The key mental move:** ask *"who actually performs the action?"* If **a service acts on a thing** → *have/get + thing + **past participle***. If **you push a person to act** → *make/let/get… + person + **verb***. Mixing the two is the #1 error.
> 🇦🇿 *Əsas fikir: "hərəkəti əslində kim edir?" Əşya üzərində xidmət → have/get + əşya + **V3**. İnsanı hərəkətə vadar edirsən → make/let/get… + kəs + **fel**.*

> ⚠️ **Word order matters.** *"I **had my car repaired**"* (causative — a garage did it) ≠ *"I **had repaired** my car"* (past perfect — I did it earlier). The **object comes before the past participle** in the causative. 🇦🇿 *"had my car repaired" (kauzativ) ≠ "had repaired my car" (past perfect) — əşya V3-dən ƏVVƏL gəlir.*

## 📋 THE FORMS — at a glance (the master table)

| Verb | Pattern | Example | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **have** (thing) | have + **thing** + **V3** | I **had** the roof **fixed**. | arranged service |
| **get** (thing) | get + **thing** + **V3** | I **got** my phone **repaired**. | informal twin of *have* |
| **have** (person) | have + **person** + **bare V** | I **had** the porter **carry** it. | instruct a named person |
| **get** (person) | get + **person** + **to + V** | I **got** her **to sign** it. | persuade / cause |
| **make** (person) | make + **person** + **bare V** | They **made** me **apologise**. | force / oblige |
| **let** (person) | let + **person** + **bare V** | They **let** me **go**. | allow / permit |
| **help** (person) | help + **person** + **(to) V** | She **helped** me **(to) carry** it. | either form works |

> 🔑 **The form rule in one line:** *make · let · have* (+ person) take a **bare infinitive**; *get* (+ person) takes a **to-infinitive**; *help* takes **either**. When a **thing** is affected (*have/get*), use the **past participle (V3)**.
> 🇦🇿 *make/let/have + kəs → məsdərsiz fel; get + kəs → to-məsdər; help → hər ikisi; əşya (have/get) → V3.*

## 🔑 THE CORE — *have / get something done* (arrange a service)

The everyday causative: you **pay for / arrange** a service; someone else does the work. Structure = **have** (or **get**) **+ object + past participle**:

```
   subject  +  have/get  +  [ OBJECT ]  +  [ V3 ]  ( + by someone )
      "I         had         my hair       cut."
      "We        're getting  the kitchen   painted   (by a decorator)."
```

| 💇 Everyday services (present) | 🔧 One-off jobs (past / perfect / future) |
|---|---|
| I **have** my hair **cut** every month. | I **had** my eyes **tested** last week. |
| She **gets** her nails **done** on Fridays. | We'**ve just had** the car **serviced**. |
| They **have** the windows **cleaned** monthly. | I'**m going to have** my suit **dry-cleaned**. |
| Where do you **get** your car **washed**? | You **should have** your brakes **checked**. |

> 🗣️ **have vs get:** identical meaning; ***get*** is more **informal / spoken** and can suggest a bit more effort ("I finally **got** it fixed"). *Have* is neutral. 🇦🇿 *have və get eyni mənadadır; **get** daha danışıq dilinə xasdır.*

> 🧪 **Spot the difference:** *"I **cut** my hair"* = I did it myself. *"I **had** my hair **cut**"* = a hairdresser did it. Use the causative to make clear **someone else** performed the service. 🇦🇿 *"I cut my hair" = özüm kəsdim; "I had my hair cut" = bərbər kəsdi.*

## 😱 *have / get something done* — the "misfortune" use

The **same** structure has a second life: describing something (usually **bad**) that **happened to** you — you didn't arrange it at all:

| Sentence | Reading |
|---|---|
| He **had** his wallet **stolen** on the train. | someone stole it — a misfortune |
| She **had** her phone **snatched** in the street. | it happened to her |
| We **had** our flight **cancelled**. | the airline cancelled it (out of our control) |
| I **got** my car **towed** away. | the council towed it — bad luck |

> 🔍 **Arranged or unfortunate?** Context and common sense decide. *"I had my hair cut"* = arranged (a service). *"I had my hair pulled"* = something bad done to me. Same grammar, opposite feeling. 🇦🇿 *Eyni quruluş: ya sifariş etdiyin xidmət, ya da başına gələn bədbəxt hadisə — kontekst həll edir.*

---

## 👉 *make* & *let* (+ bare infinitive) — force & permission

These two put a **person** in the middle and take a **bare infinitive** (no *to*):

| Verb | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **make** sb do | force / oblige | The teacher **made** us **rewrite** it. |
| **make** sb do | cause a reaction | That film **made** me **cry**. |
| **let** sb do | allow / permit | My parents **let** me **stay** out late. |
| **let** sb do | (fixed) *let go, let know* | **Let** me **know** if you need help. |

> ⚠️ **No *to* after *make* / *let*:** ~~made me to wait~~ → *made me **wait***; ~~let me to go~~ → *let me **go***. 🇦🇿 *make/let-dən sonra "to" yoxdur: made me wait, let me go.*

> 🚫 **let has no passive.** For the passive of permission, use ***be allowed to***: ~~We were let to leave~~ → *We **were allowed to** leave.* 🇦🇿 *let-in passivi yoxdur — "be allowed to" işlədilir.*

---

## 🤝 *get* & *help* (+ to-infinitive) & *have somebody do*

| Verb | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **get** sb **to** do | persuade / cause | I finally **got** him **to apologise**. |
| **help** sb **(to)** do | assist | She **helped** me **(to) fix** the sink. |
| **have** sb do | instruct (named person, neutral) | The manager **had** the staff **work** overtime. |

> 🔑 *get* is the odd one out among "person" verbs: it needs **to** (*get her **to** sign*), while *make/let/have* use the bare verb. *help* accepts **both** (*help me **carry*** / *help me **to carry***) — the bare form is more common today. 🇦🇿 *get + kəs → "to" ilə; make/let/have → məsdərsiz; help → hər ikisi (adətən məsdərsiz).*

> 🧊 **have sb *doing* (ongoing) & won't have sb *doing* (tolerate):** *"He **had** us **laughing** all night"* (caused an ongoing action); *"I **won't have** you **speaking** to me like that!"* (= I won't tolerate it). Advanced, idiomatic. 🇦🇿 *"have + kəs + V-ing" (davamlı hərəkət yaratmaq); "won't have + kəs + V-ing" (dözməmək).*

---

## 🔄 PASSIVE quirks — *make* grows a *to*, *let* disappears

The passive forms have two famous surprises:

```
   ACTIVE:  They  made    us    wait.      →  PASSIVE:  We were MADE TO wait.     (to appears!)
   ACTIVE:  They  let     us    leave.     →  PASSIVE:  We were ALLOWED TO leave. (let → allowed to)
   ACTIVE:  They  helped  us    (to) move. →  PASSIVE:  We were HELPED TO move.
```

| Active | Passive |
|---|---|
| The boss **made** them **stay**. | They **were made to stay**. |
| Security **let** us **in**. | We **were allowed to** come in. |
| They **had** the documents **signed**. | The documents **were had signed** ✗ → *The documents **got** signed / **were** signed.* |

> 🔑 In the passive, ***make* takes *to*** (*were made **to** wait*), and ***let*** is replaced by ***be allowed to***. 🇦🇿 *Passivdə **make → "to"** alır (were made to wait); **let → "be allowed to"** olur.*

## ❓ QUESTIONS, negatives & tenses

| Function | Example |
|---|---|
| service question | **Where do you have** your car **serviced**? / **How often do you get** your hair **cut**? |
| person question | **What made** you **change** your mind? / **Did** they **let** you **in**? |
| negative | I **didn't have** it **repaired**. / They **wouldn't let** me **speak**. |
| across tenses | I **'m having** it **fixed** *(now)* · **had** it **fixed** *(past)* · **'ll have** it **fixed** *(future)* · **should have** it **fixed** *(modal)* |

> 🗣️ Service questions almost always use *do you have / do you get…* + object + V3: *"Where **do you get** your suits **made**?"* 🇦🇿 *Xidmət sualları: "Where do you have/get + əşya + V3?"*

## 🧱 SEMI-CAUSATIVES & near-synonyms

| Verb | Force | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **force** sb **to** | strong compulsion | They **forced** him **to resign**. |
| **cause** sb/sth **to** | formal "make happen" | The storm **caused** the roof **to collapse**. |
| **allow / permit** sb **to** | formal "let" | Visitors **are permitted to** take photos. |
| **enable** sb **to** | make possible | The grant **enabled** us **to travel**. |
| **oblige / require** sb **to** | formal duty | Staff **are required to** wear ID. |
| **persuade / convince** sb **to** | talk into | I **persuaded** her **to stay**. |

> 🔑 Most formal synonyms of *make/get* take a **to-infinitive** (*force/cause/enable/require to*); only *make* and *let* (and *have*) keep the **bare** verb. 🇦🇿 *Rəsmi sinonimlər (force/cause/enable/require) "to" ilə; yalnız make/let/have məsdərsiz saxlayır.*

## 🅰️ §A — LEVEL DIFFERENCES (A1 → C2)

How the *same idea* — "someone else does it" — grows with your level:

| Level | What the learner can do | Typical sentence |
|---|---|---|
| **A1** | *make / let* + person (basic) | "It **makes** me **happy**." / "**Let** me **see**." |
| **A2** | *make/let* + do; *help* + do | "They **made** us **wait**." / "She **helped** me **carry** it." |
| **B1** | *have/get something done* (services) | "I **had** my hair **cut**." / "I **got** the car **washed**." |
| **B2** | *get sb to do*; misfortune *have sth done*; tenses | "I **got** him **to help**." / "He **had** his bike **stolen**." |
| **C1** | passive quirks; *have sb do*; formal synonyms | "We **were made to** wait." / "The boss **had** us **work** late." |
| **C2** | *have sb doing*; *won't have sb doing*; register | "He **had** us **laughing**." / "I **won't have** you **shouting**." |

> 🇦🇿 *A1: make/let. A2: make/let/help + do. B1: have/get sth done. B2: get sb to do, bədbəxt hadisə. C1: passiv incəliklər, have sb do. C2: have sb doing, üslub.*

## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT

1. **"I had my hair cut" is the default.** Natives rarely say *"I cut my hair"* unless they literally did it themselves. The causative signals "a professional did it." 🇦🇿 *"I had my hair cut" — bərbərin işini bildirir; "I cut my hair" = özüm kəsdim.*
2. **"get" feels more colloquial and effortful.** *"I finally **got** it sorted"* carries a whiff of hassle overcome; *"have"* is neutral. 🇦🇿 *"get" daha danışıq dilinə xas və "nəhayət düzəltdim" çaları verir.*
3. **"made me + emotion" is everywhere.** *"It **made** me **laugh / cry / think**."* A hugely common, natural pattern for reactions. 🇦🇿 *"It made me laugh/cry" — reaksiyalar üçün çox təbii.*
4. **"Let me…" is a soft offer/opener.** *"Let me help," "Let me know," "Let me see."* Not really "permission" — it's polite framing. 🇦🇿 *"Let me…" çox vaxt nəzakətli təklifdir, icazə deyil.*
5. **The "misfortune" causative removes agency.** *"I had my bag stolen"* focuses on the victim, not the thief — softer than *"Someone stole my bag."* 🇦🇿 *Bədbəxt-hadisə kauzativi diqqəti qurbana yönəldir.*
6. **"won't have it" = won't tolerate.** *"I **won't have** any of that!"* is a firm, slightly old-fashioned refusal. 🇦🇿 *"I won't have it" = "buna dözmərəm / icazə vermərəm".*

## 🅲 §C — ALTERNATIVE EXPRESSIONS (say it another way)

| Instead of… | You can say… | Shade |
|---|---|---|
| I **had** the car **repaired**. | I **got** the car repaired. / I **took** the car **to be** repaired. / Someone repaired it **for** me. | informal / descriptive |
| They **made** us **wait**. | They **forced** us to wait. / We **were obliged** to wait. | stronger / formal |
| They **let** us **go**. | They **allowed / permitted** us to go. | formal |
| I **got** him **to help**. | I **persuaded / convinced** him to help. / I **talked** him **into** helping. | talk-into |

| He **had** his phone **stolen**. | Someone **stole** his phone. / His phone **was stolen**. | plain passive |

> 🇦🇿 *Eyni fikri "get", "force/oblige", "allow/permit", "persuade/talk into", "assist" ilə də demək olar — güc və üslub dəyişir.*

# 📖 MINI STORY #1 — "The Big Move"

### *Services arranged, people persuaded, and one small misfortune — the week before moving house.*
### 🇦🇿 *Sifariş edilən xidmətlər, razı salınan insanlar və bir kiçik bədbəxtlik — köçməzdən əvvəlki həftə.*

---

The week before the move, Rəşad had a long list.

"First," he told his wife, "we need to **have** the old sofa **collected** by the charity." *(service arranged.)*

"And I'll **get** the carpets **cleaned** before we hand back the keys," said Nərgiz. *(service arranged.)*

"Good. I've already **had** the internet **transferred** to the new address." *(service arranged, done.)*

There was a lot to organise. Rəşad **made** the children **pack** their own boxes *(force/oblige)*, though he **let** them **keep** their toys out until the last day *(permission)*.

"Can you **get** your brother **to help** on Saturday?" Nərgiz asked. *(persuade — get + to.)*

"I'll try. Last time it took three phone calls to **get** him **to turn** up." Rəşad laughed. "But he did **help** us **(to) load** the van, so I shouldn't complain." *(assist — help + (to).)*

On moving day, the hired driver **had** them **carry** the heavy items first *(instruct — have + bare)*, and by noon the flat was empty.

Then came the only mishap: while they stopped for lunch, Rəşad **had** his phone **stolen** from the café table. *(misfortune — not arranged!)*

"Typical!" he groaned. "I'll have to **get** a new one **set up** tonight." *(service.)*

Still, the new flat was wonderful. That evening, sitting among the boxes, Nərgiz smiled. "It was worth it. Now — **let** me **make** us some tea." *(let + make: a soft offer.)*

```
3. Why "get your brother to help" (with 'to') but "made the children pack" (no 'to')? State the rule.
4. Rewrite "The driver had them carry the heavy items" in the passive.
5. 🇦🇿→EN: "Divanı apartdırdıq." / "Uşaqlara qutuları özlərinin yığmasını etdirdik." / "Telefonunu oğurlatdı."
```
> 💬 **Answers**
> 1. Services: *have the sofa collected, get the carpets cleaned, had the internet transferred, get a new one set up.* **Misfortune:** *"had his phone stolen"* — nobody arranged it; it happened to him.
> 2. *made … pack* = the parents **obliged/forced** the children (no choice). *let … keep* = the parents **allowed/permitted** it (a kindness). One is compulsion, the other is permission.
> 3. Rule: *get* + person takes a **to-infinitive** (*get him **to** help*); *make* (and *let/have*) + person takes a **bare infinitive** (*made them **pack***).
> 4. *"They **were made to carry** the heavy items first (by the driver)."* (passive *make* takes *to*).
> 5. We had the sofa collected. / We made the children pack their own boxes. / He had his phone stolen.

# 🧨 EXPANSION PACK — The Causative (E1–E12)
### *The deep layer: the two branches, the word-order splitter, the verb-form matrix, passive quirks, tenses & idioms.*
### 🇦🇿 *Dərin qat: iki qol, söz sırası fərqi, fel-forma matrisi, passiv incəliklər, zamanlar və idiomlar.*

---

## 🅴1 — The two branches decoded (who performs the action?)

Everything in this chapter hangs on one question: **is a thing being worked on, or is a person being pushed to act?**

```
   THING branch:   subject arranges a service   →  have/get + [THING] + V3 (past participle)
                   "I had the car   washed."      the car doesn't act; a service acts ON it

   PERSON branch:  subject causes a person to act →  make/let/have + [PERSON] + bare V
                                                     get/help       + [PERSON] + to V
                   "I made him    wash the car."   the person performs the washing
```

> 🔑 **The give-away:** if you can add *"by someone"* (a service), it's the **thing** branch → **V3**. If the middle noun is the **doer**, it's the **person** branch → **verb**. 🇦🇿 *"by someone" əlavə oluna bilirsə → əşya qolu (V3). Ortadakı isim hərəkəti edirsə → insan qolu (fel).*

---

## 🅴2 — ⚠️ CAUSATIVE vs PERFECT: the word-order splitter

The single most dangerous confusion in this chapter — same words, opposite meaning, decided by **word order**:

```
   "I had  MY CAR  REPAIRED."   →  causative: object (my car) BEFORE participle → a garage did it
   "I had  REPAIRED  MY CAR."   →  past perfect: participle BEFORE object → I did it, earlier
```

| Sentence | Meaning | Structure |
|---|---|---|
| I **had my hair cut**. | a barber cut it | causative (have + obj + V3) |
| I **had cut** my hair *(before he arrived)*. | I cut it myself, earlier | past perfect (had + V3 + obj) |
| She **had the letter written**. | someone wrote it for her | causative |
| She **had written** the letter. | she wrote it herself | past perfect |

> 🧪 **Test:** object **before** the participle → **causative** (someone else). Participle **before** object → **perfect** (you did it). 🇦🇿 *Əşya V3-dən ƏVVƏL → kauzativ; V3 əşyadan ƏVVƏL → past perfect.*

---

## 🅴3 — *have* vs *get* — same job, different feel

| Feature | **have** sth done | **get** sth done |
|---|---|---|
| register | neutral / slightly formal | informal / spoken |
| nuance | a plain arrangement | often effort or hassle overcome |
| imperative advice | "**Have** it checked." | "**Get** it checked!" *(more urgent/casual)* |
| "manage to finish" | ✗ | ✓ "I must **get** this **done** today." |

> 🗣️ Only *get* has the extra "**manage to complete**" meaning: *"Did you **get** the report **finished**?"* And *"get things done"* = be productive. 🇦🇿 *Yalnız "get"-in "işi bitirməyə nail olmaq" mənası da var: "get things done".*

---

## 🅴4 — The VERB-FORM MATRIX (memorise this)

| Verb (+ person) | Infinitive form | Example | Passive |
|---|---|---|---|
| **make** | **bare** | made me **wait** | were made **to** wait |
| **let** | **bare** | let me **go** | *(none)* → were **allowed to** go |
| **have** | **bare** | had him **fix** it | *(rare)* |
| **get** | **to** | got him **to fix** it | got it **fixed** *(thing)* |
| **help** | **bare or to** | helped me **(to) move** | were helped **to** move |

```
   BARE club:  make · let · have (+ person)      →  "wait / go / fix"
   TO club:    get · force · cause · persuade    →  "to wait / to go / to fix"
   BOTH:       help                              →  "(to) move"
```

> 🇦🇿 *Məsdərsiz klub: make/let/have. "to"-klub: get/force/cause/persuade. Hər ikisi: help.*

---

## 🅴5 — The MISFORTUNE causative (arranged vs bad luck)

*have/get + thing + V3* has two readings — you decide by context:

| Sentence | Arranged service? | Misfortune? |
|---|---|---|
| I **had my hair cut**. | ✅ yes | — |
| He **had his wallet stolen**. | — | ✅ yes |
| We **had the house painted**. | ✅ yes | — |
| She **had her car broken into**. | — | ✅ yes |
| They **had their flight cancelled**. | — | ✅ (out of their control) |

> 🔍 The misfortune reading focuses on the **victim/experiencer**, not the doer: *"I had my bag stolen"* (poor me) vs *"Someone stole my bag"* (neutral). 🇦🇿 *Bədbəxt-hadisə oxunuşu diqqəti qurbana yönəldir; kontekst arranged/misfortune-u ayırır.*

---

## 🅴6 — PASSIVE quirks in depth

```
   make →  ACTIVE "They made us wait."     PASSIVE "We were MADE TO wait."   (bare → to!)
   let  →  ACTIVE "They let us go."        PASSIVE "We were ALLOWED TO go."  (let vanishes)
   help →  ACTIVE "They helped us move."   PASSIVE "We were HELPED TO move." (usually keeps to)
   get  →  ACTIVE "I got it fixed."        (already passive-like: thing + V3)
```

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| passive *make* takes ***to*** | the bare infinitive only survives in the **active** |
| *let* has **no** passive → *be allowed to* | *let* resists the passive entirely |
| *have + person* rarely passivises | use a plain passive instead |

> 🇦🇿 *Passivdə: make → "to" alır; let → "be allowed to" olur; help → adətən "to" saxlayır; have + person nadir hallarda passivə düşür.*

---

## 🅴7 — *make* for reactions & states (not just force)

*make somebody + bare verb / adjective* often means **cause a reaction or state**, with no force at all:

| Sentence | Sense |
|---|---|
| That film **made** me **cry**. | caused an emotion |
| You **make** me **laugh**. | cause a reaction |
| The news **made** everyone **nervous**. | *make* + adjective = cause a state |
| Coffee **makes** me **jittery**. | cause an effect |

> 🔑 *make* + **adjective** (*made me happy/angry/nervous*) is just as common as *make* + verb. 🇦🇿 *"make + sifət" (made me happy) də çox işlənir — vəziyyət yaradır.*

---

## 🅴8 — The three faces of *have + person*

| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **have** sb **do** (bare) | instruct / arrange (neutral) | I'll **have** my assistant **call** you. |
| **have** sb **doing** (-ing) | cause an ongoing action | The comedian **had** the whole room **laughing**. |
| **won't have** sb **doing** | refuse to tolerate | I **won't have** you **speaking** to me like that! |

> 🇦🇿 *have + kəs + məsdərsiz (tapşırıq); have + kəs + V-ing (davamlı hərəkət yaratmaq); won't have + kəs + V-ing (dözməmək).*

---

## 🅴9 — The CAUSATIVE across all tenses (master table)

| Tense | have + thing + V3 | make + person + bare V |
|---|---|---|
| present simple | I **have** my hair **cut** monthly. | She **makes** me **laugh**. |
| present continuous | I'**m having** the car **serviced**. | He'**s making** us **wait**. |
| past simple | I **had** the roof **fixed**. | They **made** us **stay**. |
| present perfect | I'**ve had** it **repaired**. | It'**s made** me **think**. |
| future (will/going to) | I'**ll have** it **checked**. / I'**m going to get** it **done**. | The rule **will make** them **comply**. |
| modal | You **should have** it **looked** at. | That **would make** anyone **angry**. |

> 🇦🇿 *Kauzativ bütün zamanlarda işləyir — yalnız have/get (və ya make) hissəsi zamana görə dəyişir; struktur sabit qalır.*

---

## 🅴10 — Formal synonyms — the "to-club"

When you need a more formal or precise causative, these all take a **to-infinitive**:

| Verb | Force level | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **force** sb to | strong | They **forced** him **to resign**. |
| **oblige / compel** sb to | strong, formal | We were **obliged to** comply. |
| **require** sb to | rule/duty | Staff **are required to** sign in. |
| **cause** sth/sb to | neutral, formal | The heat **caused** the metal **to expand**. |
| **enable** sb to | make possible | The app **enables** you **to book** online. |
| **allow / permit** sb to | = let | Guests **are permitted to** use the pool. |
| **persuade / get** sb to | talk into | I **persuaded** her **to come**. |

> 🇦🇿 *Rəsmi kauzativ sinonimlərin hamısı "to"-məsdər alır: force/oblige/require/cause/enable/allow/permit/persuade to.*

---

## 🅴11 — Idioms & fixed causative phrases

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **let go (of)** | release | **Let go of** the rope! |
| **let (sb) know** | inform | **Let me know** when you arrive. |
| **let alone** | still less | I can't walk, **let alone** run. |
| **make do (with)** | manage with less | We'll **make do with** what we have. |
| **make believe** | pretend | The kids **make believe** they're pirates. |
| **have done with** | finish/end | Let's **have done with** this argument. |
| **get (sth) done** | complete | Let's **get** it **done**. |

> 🇦🇿 *Bu hazır ifadələr çox işlənir: let go (buraxmaq), let me know (xəbər ver), make do (idarə etmək), make believe (uydurmaq).*

## 🅴12 — 🇦🇿 CONTRASTIVE SYNTHESIS

| English | 🇦🇿 | The build |
|---|---|---|
| I **had** my hair **cut**. | Saçımı **kəsdirdim**. | kəs + **dir** (causative suffix) |
| I **had** the car **repaired**. | Maşını **təmir etdirdim**. | etmək → et**dir**mək |
| She **made** me **wait**. | Məni **gözlətdi**. | gözlə + **t** |
| The film **made** me **laugh**. | Film məni **güldürdü**. | gül + **dür** |
| I **got** him **to write** it. | Onu **yazdırdım** / yazmağa razı saldım. | yaz + **dır** |
| The teacher **made** us **read**. | Müəllim bizə **oxutdu**. | oxu + **t** |

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani contrast in one line:** Azerbaijani builds causation **inside the verb** with a suffix — **-dır/-dir/-dur/-dür, -t, -ır, -art** (*kəs→kəsdir, gözlə→gözlət, gül→güldür*), and can even **stack** it for a double causative (*yazdırt-* = "have someone make someone write"). English has **no causative suffix**; it must choose an **outside** structure instead — *have/get + thing + V3* for services or *make/let/get + person + (to) verb* for people. So an AZ learner already thinks causatively by instinct, but must learn to **unpack** that one suffix into English's two separate patterns — and never attach *-dir* logic to an English verb (~~"I laughed him"~~ → *I made him laugh*).

## 📦 BLOCK 11 — ⚡ MINIMAL PAIRS — one change flips the meaning

| Pair | What changes | 🇦🇿 |
|---|---|---|
| I **had my car repaired**. / I **had repaired** my car. | causative (garage) → past perfect (I did it) | etdirdim → (özüm) etmişdim |
| I **cut** my hair. / I **had** my hair **cut**. | I did it → a barber did it | özüm kəsdim → kəsdirdim |
| They **made** me **wait**. / They **let** me **wait**. | forced → allowed | məcbur etdilər → icazə verdilər |
| I **got** him **to help**. / I **made** him **help**. | persuaded (to) → forced (bare) | razı saldım → məcbur etdim |
| He **had** his wallet **stolen**. / He **stole** a wallet. | victim (misfortune) → doer | oğurlatdı → oğurladı |
| We **were made to** stay. / We **were allowed to** stay. | forced → permitted | qalmağa məcbur → qalmağa icazə |

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "The Wedding Day"

### *Services booked, people persuaded, one broken glass — the morning of a big wedding.*
### 🇦🇿 *Sifariş edilən xidmətlər, razı salınan insanlar, bir sınmış qədəh — böyük toyun səhəri.*

> Read it twice. **First** for the story; **second** circling every *have/get + done · make · let · get… to · help.*
> 🇦🇿 *İki dəfə oxu: əvvəl hekayə üçün, sonra hər kauzativ quruluşu dövrəyə alaraq.*

---

The bride, Günel, woke early. There was so much to organise.

She had already **had** the dress **altered** and **got** the flowers **delivered** the day before. *(services arranged.)* Now she needed to **have** her hair and make-up **done**. *(service.)*

"Mum, can you **get** the caterers **to arrive** by eleven?" she asked. *(persuade — get + to.)*

"Already sorted," her mother smiled. "And I've **had** the tables **set up** in the garden." *(service.)*

Her little brother was racing around. "Someone **make** him **sit** still!" Günel laughed. *(force — make + bare.)* But she **let** him **carry** the rings — it made him feel important. *(permission — let + bare.)*

The photographer arrived and **had** everyone **gather** by the roses for a group shot. *(instruct — have + bare.)* He was good at his job: he **made** even the shyest cousins **smile**. *(cause a reaction.)*

Just before the ceremony, a waiter tripped, and Günel **had** a glass **broken** at her feet. *(misfortune — not arranged!)* "It's good luck!" someone shouted, and everyone laughed.

The groom, Tural, had his own worries. His best man had promised to **get** the car **decorated**, but at the last minute they had to **get** a neighbour **to lend** them some ribbon. *(service; then persuade.)*

By evening, the band **had** the whole garden **dancing**. *(have + person + -ing — an ongoing action.)* Günel's father, watching, wiped his eyes.

"That music **makes** me **feel** young again," he said, and **let** himself **be pulled** onto the dance floor.

### ✍️ After-reading tasks
```
1. Find every "have/get + something + done." Which one is a MISFORTUNE rather than a booked service?
2. "make him sit still" vs "let him carry the rings" — explain the difference in Günel's attitude.
3. Why "get the caterers to arrive" (with 'to') but "had everyone gather" (no 'to')? State the rule.
4. "The band had the whole garden dancing." Why '-ing' and not a bare verb here? What does it add?
5. 🇦🇿→EN: "Gəlinliyi düzəltdirdi." / "Onu sakit oturtmaq lazımdır." / "Qonşunu lent verməyə razı saldılar."
```
> 💬 **Answers**
> 1. Services: *had the dress altered, got the flowers delivered, had the tables set up, get the car decorated.* **Misfortune:** *"had a glass broken at her feet"* — the waiter tripped; nobody arranged it.
> 2. *make him sit* = she wants him **forced/obliged** to sit (compulsion). *let him carry* = she **allows/permits** it as a kindness (it makes him feel important). Force vs permission.
> 3. Rule: *get* + person takes a **to-infinitive** (*get them **to** arrive*); *have* + person takes a **bare** infinitive (*had everyone **gather***). *make/let/have* = bare; *get* = to.
> 4. *had … dancing* uses **-ing** to show an **ongoing action** the band caused — the dancing continued over time. A bare verb (*had them dance*) would sound like a one-off instruction; *-ing* paints a lasting scene.
> 5. She had the wedding dress altered. / Someone needs to make him sit still. (He needs to be made to sit still.) / They got the neighbour to lend (them) some ribbon.

## 🏁 End of Chapter 30

You can now say that **someone else did it.** For a **thing**, you arrange a service with *have / get + object + past participle* (*"I had my car repaired"*) — the very same frame that also reports a **misfortune** (*"He had his wallet stolen"*). For a **person**, you *make* or *let* them act (**bare** infinitive — *"made me wait," "let me go"*), *get* them to act (**to**-infinitive — *"got him to help"*), *have* a named person act (**bare** — *"had the porter carry it"*), or *help* them (**either** form). Keep the **object before the participle**, remember that *make* grows a **to** in the passive (*"were made to wait"*) while *let* becomes *be allowed to*, and you've mastered the causative. 🛠️

```
 ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ 🔧 thing:   have / get + OBJECT + V3        "I had my car repaired."        │
 │ 😱 mishap:  have + OBJECT + V3               "He had his wallet stolen."     │
 │ 👉 force:   make + person + BARE V           "They made us wait."            │
 │ 🔓 allow:   let + person + BARE V            "She let me go."                │
 │ 🤝 persuade:get + person + TO V              "I got him to help."            │
 │ 🙌 assist:  help + person + (TO) V           "She helped me (to) move."      │
 │ ⚠️ passive: "were MADE TO wait" · let → "were ALLOWED TO" · object before V3│

```
