# 🎬 Past Continuous — The Interrupted Past

### *Part 2 · Chapter 08 · was/were + V-ing*

> **Azərbaycanca qısa giriş:** Past Continuous (Davamedici Keçmiş Zaman) keçmişin bir
> anında **davam edən** hərəkəti bildirir. Azərbaycan dilindəki *"-ırdı/-irdi/-urdu/-ürdü"*
> formasına bənzəyir: *Saat 8-də televizora baxırdım* → *At 8, I **was watching** TV.*
> Formul: **was/were + fel + ing**. Çox vaxt Past Simple onu "kəsir": *Yeməк bişirirdim,
> o zaman o zəng etdi.* 🎬

---

## 1. 🟢 Introduction

The **Past Continuous** is the tense of **"what was happening"** at a moment in the past.
It paints the **background** — the action **already in progress** — while something else
(usually a **Past Simple** action) happens.

```
   Past Simple:      I cooked dinner.            → completed action
   Past Continuous:  I was cooking when he came. → in progress (background) + interruption
```

The form has **two parts**: **was/were** + a verb with **-ing**. It's the **past version**
of the Present Continuous (Chapter 04).

> 💡 **Big idea:** Past Continuous = a **"past movie still playing"** 🎬 at a certain
> moment — usually interrupted or set as a background.

---

## 2. ❓ Why Do We Use This Tense?

Five main reasons:

| # | Use | Example | Azərbaycanca |
|---|-----|---------|--------------|
| 1 | **Action in progress at a past time** | At 9 PM, I **was studying**. | keçmişdə davam edən hərəkət |
| 2 | **Interrupted action** (background + Past Simple) | I **was sleeping** when the alarm **rang**. | kəsilən hərəkət |
| 3 | **Two parallel actions** | While she **was cooking**, I **was cleaning**. | paralel hərəkətlər |
| 4 | **Background/scene in a story** | The sun **was shining**; birds **were singing**. | hekayə fonu |
| 5 | **Annoying repeated past actions** (always) | He **was always losing** his keys. | təkrarlanan əsəbi hərəkət |

> 📌 Two big clues: a **specific past time** (*at 8 o'clock*) or the word **when/while**
> connecting a background action to an interruption.

---

## 3. 🌍 Real-Life Situations

- 🕗 **Saying what you were doing:** *"I was working when you texted."*
- 🎬 **Setting a scene:** *"It was raining, and the streets were empty."*
- 🚗 **Describing an accident:** *"I was driving slowly when a dog ran out."*
- 😤 **Complaining about the past:** *"He was always interrupting me."*
- 🙋 **Being polite:** *"I was wondering if you could help."*

---

## 4. 📅 Timeline

The action is **in progress** around a **past point**; a **Past Simple** action often
interrupts it.

```
   PAST                                                   NOW
 ────┼──[========= was cooking (in progress) =========]────┼────►
                         ▲
                         X  ← he called (Past Simple — short interruption)

   "I was cooking when he called."
   long action (background) = was cooking | short action (interruption) = called
```

> 🎬 The **long, ongoing** action wraps **around** the **short, completed** one. The wave
> 〜 (continuous) is cut by a dot • (simple).

---

## 5. 🧮 Formula

Model verb: **work**. Helper: **was / were** (the past of *be*).

### ➕ Positive
```
   S  +  was / were  +  V-ing
        (was → I/he/she/it · were → you/we/they)
```
| Subject | was/were | V-ing | Example |
|---------|----------|-------|---------|
| I / He / She / It | **was** | working | I **was working**. |
| You / We / They | **were** | working | They **were working**. |

### ➖ Negative
```
   S  +  was / were  +  NOT  +  V-ing
        (wasn't / weren't)
```
- I **wasn't working**. · They **weren't working**.

### ❓ Question
```
   Was / Were  +  S  +  V-ing  ?
```
- **Was** I working? · **Were** you working? · **Was** she working?

### ❓➖ Negative Question
```
   Wasn't / Weren't  +  S  +  V-ing  ?
```
- **Wasn't** he listening? · **Weren't** you sleeping?

### ✔️ Short Answers
| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Were you working? | Yes, I **was**. | No, I **wasn't**. |
| Was she working? | Yes, she **was**. | No, she **wasn't**. |

> ⚠️ Never say *"Yes, I were."* — it's *"Yes, I **was**."* (I → was).

## 7. 📜 Grammar Rules

1. **Two parts:** was/were + **V-ing**. Choose **was** (I/he/she/it) or **were** (you/we/they).
2. The **interruption** uses the **Past Simple**: *I **was reading** when she **called**.*
3. **State verbs** usually do NOT take -ing — use the Past Simple: ❌ *I was knowing* → ✅ *I knew*.
4. For **two parallel** actions, both can be Past Continuous: *While I **was cooking**, he
   **was watching** TV.*
5. **when** is usually followed by **Past Simple**; **while** by **Past Continuous** (§11).
6. Time markers: *at 8 o'clock, at that moment, all day yesterday, when, while, as*.

---

## 8. ✏️ Spelling Rules (the V-ing)

Same **-ing** rules as Chapters 04 & 06:

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs → **+ing** | read → reading, play → playing |
| Silent **-e** → drop *e* + ing | write → writing, make → making |
| Short CVC → double consonant | run → running, sit → sitting |
| Ends in **-ie** → *ie → y* + ing | lie → lying, die → dying |

> 📌 The only changing word is *was/were*; the main verb just follows the -ing rules.

## 9. 🚩 Signal Words

```
 SPECIFIC PAST TIME:  at 8 o'clock · at that moment · at midnight · this time yesterday
 DURATION:            all day/morning/night yesterday · the whole evening
 CONNECTORS:          when · while · as · just as · meanwhile
```

| Signal | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **at 8 o'clock** | **At 8**, I **was having** dinner. |
| **when** | She **called when** I **was driving**. |
| **while** | **While** he **was sleeping**, the phone **rang**. |
| **all day** | I **was working all day** yesterday. |
| **as** | **As** I **was leaving**, it started to rain. |

> 💡 *while* + Past Continuous and *when* + Past Simple are the classic pair.

## 10. ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| I **was knowing** the answer. | I **knew** the answer. | state verb → Past Simple |
| She **were** reading. | She **was** reading. | she → *was* |
| When he **was calling**, I **was cooking**. *(meaning: he interrupted)* | When he **called**, I **was cooking**. | interruption → Past Simple |
| I **was play** football. | I **was playing** football. | need **-ing** |
| They **was** sleeping. | They **were** sleeping. | they → *were* |
| Yes, I **were**. | Yes, I **was**. | I → was |
| While I **cooked**, he **was watching** TV. | While I **was cooking**, he **was watching** TV. | parallel → both continuous |

> 📌 **Top Azerbaijani traps:** (1) was/were agreement, and (2) using continuous for the
> **short interrupting** action (it should be Past Simple).

## 11. ⚖️ Comparison with Similar Tenses

### 🆚 Past Continuous vs. Past Simple (the key comparison!)

| | **Past Continuous** | **Past Simple** |
|--|--------------------|-----------------|
| **Meaning** | in progress (background) | completed action (interruption/sequence) |
| **Form** | was/were + V-ing | V2 |
| **Role** | the **longer** action | the **shorter**, completed action |
| **Example** | I **was cooking**… | …when he **called**. |

```
   I was cooking  (long, background — Past Continuous)
   when he called (short, interruption — Past Simple)

   ──[==== was cooking ====]──X(called)──
```

### 🔑 when vs. while

| Word | Usually followed by | Example |
|------|---------------------|---------|
| **when** | **Past Simple** (the short action) | **When** the phone **rang**, I was cooking. |
| **while** | **Past Continuous** (the long action) | **While** I **was cooking**, the phone rang. |

```
   when  + short action (Past Simple):     When she arrived, we were eating.
   while + long action (Past Continuous):  While we were eating, she arrived.
```

### Sequence vs. interruption
| Past Simple + Past Simple (sequence) | Past Continuous + Past Simple (interruption) |
|--------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| When he **arrived**, we **left**. *(first he arrived, then we left)* | When he **arrived**, we **were leaving**. *(we were already in the middle of leaving)* |

> 🧠 Two Past Simples = **one after another**. Continuous + Simple = **one inside the other**.

## 12. 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani Translation Tips

1. **Past Continuous ≈ Azerbaijani "-ırdı/-irdi/-urdu/-ürdü":**
   - *I **was reading*** → *Mən oxu**yurdum***
   - *They **were playing*** → *Onlar oyna**yırdılar***

2. **The interruption** (Past Simple) ≈ *"-dı/-di"*:
   - *Yeməк bişirir**dim**, o zaman o zəng et**di*** → *I **was cooking** when he **called**.*

3. **was/were** has no separate word — it's built into the *-ırdı* ending. But in English
   you **must** choose was/were correctly.

4. **State verbs:** Azerbaijani *"bilirdim"* → English uses the **Past Simple** *I knew*,
   not *I was knowing*.

> 💡 **Strategy:** ask "**Was this action already in progress** (background), or did it
> **happen and finish** (interruption/sequence)?" That decides continuous vs simple.

## 23. 🗣️ Speaking Tips

- ✅ Use it to **set the scene** before your main story (Past Simple).
- ✅ Combine with *when* (short action) and *while* (long action).
- ✅ Be polite/tentative: *"I was wondering if…"*, *"I was hoping you could…"*.
- ⚠️ Keep state verbs in the Past Simple (*knew, wanted, liked*).

> 🗣️ **Shadow:** *"I was cooking when you called."* · *"What were you doing?"* · *"It was raining."*

---

## 24. ✍️ Writing Tips

- Open a narrative with the Past Continuous to **build atmosphere**: *"The wind was blowing…"*
- Then switch to **Past Simple** for the events that move the story.
- Use *while/as* to show **simultaneous** actions.
- Don't overuse it — the **main events** should be Past Simple.

---

## 25. 🎧 Listening Tips

- *was/were* are **weak** (/wəz/, /wə/) — listen for the *-ing* that follows.
- *"What were you doing?"* → fast: *"Wha'were you doin'?"*
- The **interruption** verb is Past Simple — a quick V2.

## 28. ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Past Continuous or Past Simple?**
In progress / background → Continuous. Completed / interruption / sequence → Simple.

**Q2: when or while?**
*when* + the short Past Simple action; *while* + the long Past Continuous action. (Both can
flex, but this is the safe default.)

**Q3: Can I use it with state verbs?**
No — *know, like, want, be* use the **Past Simple**: *I knew, I wanted.*

**Q4: "When he arrived, we left" vs "…we were leaving"?**
First version = sequence (he arrived, then we left). Second = we were **already in the
middle of** leaving when he arrived.

**Q5: Why "I was wondering…" for a present request?**
The Past Continuous sounds **softer and more polite** than "I wonder…". It's a fixed
politeness pattern.

# 📚 Mini Story — *"The Storm"* (≈310 words)

> The Past Continuous **sets the scene**; the Past Simple brings the **events**. Watch how
> they work together. Read aloud.

It **was** a wild night. The wind **was howling**, the rain **was pouring**, and the trees
**were bending** in the storm. Inside the small house, Aysel **was sitting** by the fire,
**reading** a book. Her cat **was sleeping** on her lap.

Everything **was** calm — until the power **went** out. Suddenly, the house **was** dark.
Aysel **wasn't** scared, but she **wasn't** comfortable either. She **was looking** for a
candle when she **heard** a sound at the door.

*Knock. Knock.*

Her heart **jumped**. Who **was knocking** at midnight in a storm? She **was holding** her
breath as she **walked** slowly to the door. Outside, the rain **was hammering** down.

She **opened** the door carefully. A man **was standing** there, soaked to the bone. He
**was shivering**, and water **was dripping** from his coat. "I'm so sorry," he **said**.
"My car **broke** down. I **was driving** to the next town when the engine **died**."

Aysel **hesitated** for a second, then **smiled**. "Come in. You'll catch a cold."

While the storm **was raging** outside, they **sat** by the fire. She **made** hot tea, and
he **told** her about his journey. They **were laughing** within minutes. The cat, who
**was watching** suspiciously, finally **relaxed**.

By morning, the storm **had passed**. The sun **was rising**, and the world **looked**
washed and new. The man **fixed** his car and **thanked** her warmly.

"You **saved** me," he **said**.

"No," Aysel **laughed**. "The storm **did**. It **was** the storm that **brought** you here."

As he **drove** away, she **stood** at the door, smiling. Sometimes, she **thought**, the
worst nights **bring** the kindest mornings.

### 🔎 Story Notes — Spot the Grammar
- **Past Continuous (scene/background):** *was howling, was pouring, were bending, was sitting, was sleeping, was looking, was holding, was standing, was shivering, was dripping, was raging, was watching, was rising.*
- **Past Simple (events that move the story):** *went, heard, jumped, walked, opened, said, broke, died, hesitated, smiled, sat, made, told, relaxed, fixed, thanked, saved, drove, stood.*
- **was/were + state:** *It was a wild night · she wasn't scared.*
- **The pattern:** long background action (Continuous) + short event (Simple), often joined
  by *when / while*. That's the engine of Chapter 08.

# 🧬 EXPANSION PACK — Past Continuous (Go Deeper)

> You asked to **EXPAND**. These advanced notes (E1–E12) take you from "I know the form"
> to "I use it like a native." Read slowly; each block builds on §5 and §11.

---

## E1. 🔭 Future-in-the-Past — *was/were going to* & arrangements

The Past Continuous can describe a **plan made in the past** — a future seen from a past
viewpoint. It's the **past version** of "I'm meeting Ali tomorrow" (Ch. 04 future arrangements).

```
   NOW:   "I am leaving tomorrow."        (arrangement, present view)
   PAST:  "I was leaving the next day."   (arrangement, past view)
```

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **was/were + V-ing** | a fixed past arrangement | He **was flying** to Baku that evening. |
| **was/were going to + V1** | a past intention (often unfulfilled) | I **was going to call** you, but I forgot. |
| **was/were about to + V1** | on the very edge of doing it | We **were about to leave** when it rang. |

> 💡 *was going to* often signals a plan that **didn't happen**: *"I **was going to** study,
> but I fell asleep."* (= intention → no result). 🇦🇿 *"...edəcəkdim, amma..."*

---

## E2. 🔑 *when* vs *while* vs *as* — the full picture

All three connect two past actions, but they lean differently:

| Connector | Leans toward | Typical partner | Example |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|---------|
| **when** | a **point** / short event | Past **Simple** | **When** the bomb went off, people were running. |
| **while** | a **duration** / long action | Past **Continuous** | **While** I was cooking, he set the table. |
| **as** | two things **growing together** | either | **As** the sun was rising, the birds began to sing. |

```
   when   →  •  a dot in time   →  "When she arrived…"
   while  →  〜  a stretch of time →  "While she was waiting…"
   as     →  ↗↗ parallel change   →  "As prices were rising, demand was falling."
```

> ⚠️ *when* can sit with **either** tense — and the tense changes the meaning (see E3).
> *while/as* almost always hug the **Continuous**.

---

## E3. ⏱️ The "when" trap — *before*, *during*, or *after*?

The verb tenses after **when** decide the **time order**. This is the single most useful
distinction in the whole chapter:

| Sentence | What happened |
|----------|---------------|
| When she **called**, I **was leaving**. | I was **already in the middle of** leaving (it started first). |
| When she **called**, I **left**. | She called → **then** I left (sequence, one after another). |
| When she **called**, I **had left**. | I left **before** she called (Past Perfect — Ch. 09). |

```
 was leaving   ──[==== leaving ====]── she called happens INSIDE
 left          ── she called ──► then I left   (after)
 had left      ── I left ──► later she called   (before)
```

> 🧠 **Memory hook:** *was -ing* = the action **surrounds** the *when*-event. Past Simple =
> the action **follows** it.

---

## E4. 😤 *always / constantly / forever / continually* + Continuous = emotion

Like the Present Continuous (Ch. 04 E3), the **past** version + *always* expresses a
**repeated, often annoying** past habit — with feeling, not neutral fact.

| Neutral fact (Past Simple) | Emotional (Past Continuous + always) |
|----------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| He **was** late twice. | He **was always being** late! 😤 |
| She **borrowed** my pen. | She **was forever borrowing** my things. |
| They **argued**. | They **were constantly arguing**. |

> 📌 The adverb goes **between** was/were and the -ing verb:
> *He was **always** complaining.* 🇦🇿 *"həmişə... -ırdı"* (şikayət tonu ilə).

---

## E5. 🙏 Politeness & distance — *was wondering / was hoping / was thinking*

Putting a request into the Past Continuous makes it **softer, more tentative, more polite**.
The "distance" of the past tense = social distance = good manners.

| Direct (can sound blunt) | Soft (Past Continuous) |
|--------------------------|------------------------|
| I **wonder** if you can help. | I **was wondering** if you could help. |
| I **hope** you'll join us. | I **was hoping** you'd join us. |
| I **think** we should talk. | I **was thinking** we should talk. |

```
   Politeness ladder (most → least direct):
   Help me.  <  Can you help?  <  Could you help?  <  I was wondering if you could help.
```

> 💡 These are **fixed politeness patterns** — the action isn't really "in progress."
> Native speakers use them constantly in emails and requests.

---

## E6. 🎬 Layering a scene — stacking background actions

In storytelling, several Past Continuous verbs **stack** to build a rich background, then
**one** Past Simple verb **drops the event** in.

```
 BACKGROUND (Continuous, stacked):
   The rain was falling.  The wind was howling.  People were hurrying home.
 EVENT (Simple, single):
   Suddenly, a car screeched to a stop.
```

> 🧠 Think of it like film: the camera **pans the scene** (Continuous), then the **action
> starts** (Simple). Good writing uses *many* Continuous + *few, sharp* Simples.

---

## E7. 🗿➡️🎬 State verbs that "wake up" — temporary & changed meaning

State verbs are usually statues (Past Simple). But some go Continuous when the meaning shifts
to a **temporary action or behaviour** (same logic as Ch. 04/06).

| State (Past Simple) | Action (Past Continuous) | Shift |
|---------------------|--------------------------|-------|
| I **had** a car. (possession) | I **was having** lunch. (eating) | have = activity |
| She **thought** it was true. (opinion) | She **was thinking** about it. (mental process) | think = considering |
| He **was** rude. (permanent trait) | He **was being** rude. (temporary behaviour) | be = acting |
| I **saw** the point. (understood) | I **was seeing** a doctor. (visiting) | see = meeting |

> ⚠️ But pure states stay simple: ❌ *I was knowing / was liking / was wanting* →
> ✅ *I knew / liked / wanted.*

---

## E8. 🔁 Past Continuous vs *used to* vs *would* — in-progress ≠ habit

Three ways to talk about the past — don't mix them up:

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **was/were + V-ing** | in progress at a **specific moment** | At 8, I **was running**. |
| **used to + V1** | a **past habit/state** that stopped | I **used to run** every day. |
| **would + V1** | a **repeated past action** (storytelling) | Every summer, we **would swim** in the lake. |

```
   was running →  one moment, mid-action     ──[ running ]──•(8 o'clock)
   used to run →  a habit, now finished       •─•─•─•─ (then) ──── (not now)
   would run   →  repeated past routine        •─•─•─• (typical pattern)
```

> 📌 **Don't** say *"I was running every day"* for a habit — use *used to / would*. The
> Continuous is for a **single moment in progress**, not a routine.

---

## E9. 🎯 Adverb placement inside *was/were + V-ing*

Mid-position adverbs (*still, just, always, constantly, already, only*) sit **between**
was/were and the -ing verb:

```
   S + was/were + [adverb] + V-ing
       │            │
       └─ was       └─ still / just / always / constantly
```

| Adverb | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **still** | At noon, he **was still sleeping**. |
| **just** | I **was just leaving** when you arrived. |
| **always** | She **was always smiling**. |
| **only** | We **were only joking**. |

> 💡 *still* + Past Continuous = the action **continued longer than expected**:
> *"It was 11 AM and he was **still** working."*

---

## E10. 🪞 Parallel actions — *while / as / meanwhile* (two long actions)

When **two actions run at the same time**, both can be Past Continuous, joined by
*while / as / meanwhile*.

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| While + Cont., Cont. | **While** I **was cooking**, he **was washing** the car. |
| As + Cont., Cont. | **As** the band **was playing**, people **were dancing**. |
| Cont. + . Meanwhile, Cont. | I **was studying**. **Meanwhile**, my brother **was gaming**. |

```
   ──[==== I was cooking ========]──
   ──[==== he was washing ======]──   ← same time, two parallel waves
```

> ⚠️ Common error: *"While I **cooked**, he was watching TV."* → make the long action
> Continuous too: *"While I **was cooking**, he was watching TV."*

## E12. 🧨 Extra Common Mistakes (Azerbaijani-specific)

| While I **cooked**, he **was watching** TV. | While I **was cooking**, he was watching TV. | parallel → both Continuous |

| He **was been** rude. | He **was being** rude. | be → *being*, not *been* |
| She **was always be** late. | She **was always being** late. | always + Cont. → *being* |

> 📌 **Three golden rules to remember:** (1) habit ≠ Continuous (use *used to/would*),
> (2) the **interrupting** action is Past **Simple**, (3) state verbs stay **Simple**.

# 📚 Mini Story #2 — *"The Last Train"* (≈330 words)

> Another **STORY** for you. Watch the same engine again: the **Past Continuous** paints the
> busy station (background), and the **Past Simple** drops in the events that move the plot.
> Read it aloud — feel the two tenses dance.

The station **was buzzing**. People **were rushing** in every direction, suitcases **were
rolling** across the floor, and a tired voice on the speaker **was announcing** delays. Murad
**was standing** by Platform 4, **checking** his watch every few seconds. His train **was
leaving** at 9:15, and it **was already** 9:10.

While he **was waiting**, he **noticed** a little girl. She **was sitting** alone on a bench,
and she **was crying** quietly. Everyone else **was hurrying** past, but Murad **stopped**.

"Are you okay?" he **asked**.

The girl **looked** up. "I **was holding** my mum's hand," she **said**, "but the crowd
**pushed** us apart. Now I can't find her."

Murad's train **was boarding** — he could hear the final call. For one second, he
**hesitated**. Then he **knelt** down. "Don't worry. We'll find her together."

While they **were searching**, the loudspeaker **crackled**: "Train 9:15 to Ganja, doors
closing." Murad **didn't move**. He **was scanning** the crowd, holding the girl's hand.

Then, near the exit, a woman **was running** toward them, her face pale with panic. "Leyla!"
she **shouted**. The girl **let go** of Murad's hand and **ran** straight into her mother's
arms. They **were both crying** now — but happy tears this time.

The mother **turned** to Murad. "Thank you. You missed your train for us."

He **smiled** and **shrugged**. "Trains come again. Mums don't."

As he **was walking** to the ticket office to rebook, he **felt** lighter than he had all
week. Outside, the evening sun **was setting** over the rails, and for the first time that
day, Murad **wasn't** in a hurry.

Yenidən bilet almaq üçün kassaya gedərkən, özünü bütün həftə ərzində olduğundan yüngül hiss
etdi. Çöldə axşam günəşi relslərin üstündə batırdı və o gün ilk dəfə Murad tələsmirdi.

### 🔎 Story Notes — Spot the Grammar

- **Past Continuous (scene/background):** *was buzzing, were rushing, were rolling, was
  announcing, was standing, checking, was leaving, was waiting, was sitting, was crying,
  were hurrying, was boarding, were searching, was scanning, was running, were both crying,
  was walking, was setting, wasn't (in a hurry).*
- **Past Simple (events that move the story):** *stopped, asked, looked, said, pushed,
  hesitated, knelt, crackled, didn't move, shouted, let go, ran, turned, smiled, shrugged,
  felt.*
- **The "interruption" beat:** *While they **were searching**, the loudspeaker **crackled**.*
  → long background (Continuous) cut by a short event (Simple).
- **Politeness/feeling note:** the closing line uses simple present for a timeless truth
  (*"Trains come again. Mums don't."*) — a nice contrast to all the past tenses.
- **Pattern to copy:** open with **stacked Continuous** to build the busy station, then let
  **sharp Past Simple** verbs carry the action. That's professional storytelling.
