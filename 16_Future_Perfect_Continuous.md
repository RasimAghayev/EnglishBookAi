---

## 1. 🟢 Introduction

The **Future Perfect Continuous** is the tense of **"how long an action will have been going"**
by a certain point in the future. You stand at a **future moment** and look **back** at an
action that **has been continuing** up to it — emphasising the **duration**.

```
   Future Perfect:            By June, I'LL HAVE WRITTEN 3 books. → completed (how many)
   Future Perfect Continuous: By June, I'LL HAVE BEEN WRITING for 5 yrs. → duration (how long)
```

The form has **four parts**: **will** + **have** + **been** + a verb with **-ing**. It's the
future twin of the Past Perfect Continuous (Ch. 10).

> 💡 **Big idea:** Future Perfect Continuous = a **"long action measured up to a future
> deadline."** ⏳ Look ahead to a point and ask: **how long** will it have been happening?

## 2. ❓ Why Do We Use This Tense?

| # | Use | Example | Azərbaycanca |
|---|-----|---------|--------------|
| 1 | **Duration up to a future point** (how long) | By 6, I'**ll have been working** for 8 hrs. | gələcək ana qədər müddət |
| 2 | **Cause of a future result/state** | By the finish, he'**ll be tired — he'll have been running** for hours. | gələcək nəticənin səbəbi |
| 3 | **Emphasising long, ongoing effort** | By 2030, she'**ll have been teaching** for 20 years. | uzun davam edən səy |

> 📌 Two big jobs: show **HOW LONG** an action will have lasted by a future moment, and explain
> the **cause** of a future situation.

- 😩 **Explaining future tiredness:** *"By the time he arrives, he'**ll have been driving** all day."*

## 4. 📅 Timeline

A **long action** runs **up to** a **future point**. You look back from that point and measure
the **whole stretch** of time before it.

```
   PAST            NOW                        FUTURE
 ──────────────────┼─────────[========= working =========]●──────►
                             │                            │
                          (start)                    by 6 PM
                                                  (look back: how long?)

   "By 6 PM, I will have been working for 8 hours."
   the wave 〜 lasts a long time and reaches the future dot ●
```

> ⏳ Compare with **Future Perfect**: that's the action **completed** (●done) before the point.
> This one is the action **still going / its duration** measured up to the point.

## 5. 🧮 Formula

Model verb: **work**. Helper: **will have been** (same for **all** subjects! 🎉).

### ➕ Positive
```
   S  +  will have been  +  V-ing
        (I'll have been, you'll have been, he'll have been, we'll have been...)
```
| Subject | will have been | V-ing | Example |
|---------|----------------|-------|---------|
| I / You / He / She / It / We / They | **will have been** | working | She **will have been working**. |

> 🎉 Like all *will* forms, **will have been never changes** — one helper for everyone.

### ➖ Negative
```
   S  +  will not have been  +  V-ing     (won't have been)
```
- I **won't have been working**. · They **won't have been waiting** long.

### ❓ Question
```
   Will  +  S  +  have been  +  V-ing  ?
```
- **Will** you **have been working** long? · **How long will** she **have been waiting**?

### ✔️ Short Answers
| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Will you have been working long? | Yes, I **will**. | No, I **won't**. |

> ⚠️ Four parts, fixed order: **will + have + been + V-ing**. *been* never changes; the verb is
> always **-ing**.

## 6. 🏗️ Sentence Structure

```
 POSITIVE   ┌─────┬────────────────┬──────────┬──────────────────┐
            │  S  │ will have been │  V-ing   │ rest...          │
            │ She │ will have been │ working  │ for 8 hours.     │
            └─────┴────────────────┴──────────┴──────────────────┘

 NEGATIVE   ┌─────┬──────┬─────┬────────────┬──────────┐
            │  S  │ will │ not │ have been  │  V-ing   │
            │  I  │ will │ not │ have been  │ sleeping │
            └─────┴──────┴─────┴────────────┴──────────┘  (= won't have been sleeping)

 QUESTION   ┌──────┬─────┬────────────┬──────────┬─────────┐
            │ Will │  S  │ have been  │  V-ing   │ rest ?  │
            │ Will │ you │ have been  │ waiting? │         │
            └──────┴─────┴────────────┴──────────┴─────────┘
```

> 💬 **Contractions:** *I will have been → I'll have been · won't have been.* In fast speech:
> *"I'll've been waiting"* /aɪləv bɪn/.

---

## 7. 📜 Grammar Rules

1. **Four parts:** *will* + *have* + *been* + **V-ing**. *will have been* is the **same** for
   every subject.
2. Use it for the **duration** of an action **up to a future point** — often with **for / by /
   by the time**.
3. It often explains the **cause** of a future situation: *"He'll be exhausted — he'**ll have
   been travelling** all day."*
4. **State verbs** (know, be, like, want) do **NOT** take this form — use the **Future Perfect**:
   ❌ *will have been knowing* → ✅ *will have known*.
5. In **time clauses** (*by the time, when*), use the **present**: *"By the time you **arrive**,
   I'**ll have been waiting** for an hour."*
6. **Don't overuse it** — for *how many / a result*, use the **Future Perfect** (Ch. 15):
   *"I'**ll have written** 3 books"* (how many), not *"will have been writing 3 books."*

> 🧠 **Golden rule:** like the Future Perfect, it needs a **future point** to look back from —
> but it measures the **time before** that point, not the completion.

---

## 8. ✏️ Spelling Rules (the V-ing)

Same **-ing** rules as Chapters 04, 08, 10:

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs → **+ing** | work → working, rain → raining |
| Silent **-e** → drop *e* + ing | write → writing, drive → driving |
| Short CVC → double consonant | run → running, sit → sitting |
| Ends in **-ie** → *ie → y* + ing | lie → lying, die → dying |

> 📌 *will, have, been* never change. All the work is on the **-ing** verb.

## 9. 🚩 Signal Words

```
 DURATION:        for · how long · all day/night · the whole time
 UP-TO-A-POINT:   by · by then · by the time · before · when
 ANNIVERSARY:     by [date] ... for [length]   ("By June, for 10 years")
 CONTEXT:         because (cause) · so (result)
```

| Signal | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **for** | By 6, she'**ll have been studying for** three hours. |
| **by the time** | **By the time** you call, I'**ll have been cooking** for an hour. |
| **how long** | **How long will** you **have been waiting**? |
| **all day** | By tonight, he'**ll have been driving all day**. |
| **by [date] … for …** | **By 2030**, I'**ll have been teaching for** twenty years. |

> 💡 **by + a future point + for + a length** is the classic frame: *"By June, I'll have been
> working here **for** five years."*

## 10. ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| By June, I **will have been knowing** her for 10 yrs. | By June, I **will have known** her for 10 yrs. | state verb → Future Perfect |
| I **will have been work** for hours. | I **will have been working** for hours. | need **-ing** |
| They **will have being** waiting. | They **will have been** waiting. | *been*, not *being* |
| By 5, I **will have been written** 3 emails. | By 5, I **will have written** 3 emails. | result/amount → Future Perfect |
| She **will has been** working. | She **will have been** working. | *have*, never *has* |
| By the time you **will arrive**, I'll have been waiting. | By the time you **arrive**, I'll have been waiting. | time clause → present |
| I'**ll have been finished** by 6. | I'**ll have finished** by 6. *(completed → FP)* / I'**ll have been working**. | don't confuse with passive/result |

> 📌 **Top Azerbaijani traps:** (1) using this with **state verbs** (*will have been knowing* ❌),
> and (2) choosing it for a **result/amount** instead of the simple Future Perfect.

## 11. ⚖️ Comparison with Similar Tenses

### 🆚 Future Perfect Continuous vs. Future Perfect (the key comparison!)

| | **Future Perfect Continuous** | **Future Perfect** |
|--|------------------------------|--------------------| 
| **Focus** | **duration** (how long) / ongoing | **completion** (how many) / result |
| **Form** | will have been + V-ing | will have + V3 |
| **Example** | By 6, I'**ll have been writing** for hours. | By 6, I'**ll have written** 3 reports. |
| **Feel** | the **process** and its length | the **finished result** |

```
   will have been writing  →  〜〜〜〜〜●(by 6: writing for hours)
   will have written       →  ✅✅✅●(by 6: 3 reports done)
```

> 🧠 **Choose by the question:** *How long?* → Continuous (*will have been working*). *How many
> / finished?* → simple Future Perfect (*will have worked / written*).

### 🆚 Future Perfect Continuous vs. Future Continuous

| | **Future Perfect Continuous** | **Future Continuous** |
|--|------------------------------|------------------------|
| **Reference** | duration **up to** a future point | in progress **at** a future moment |
| **Form** | will have been + V-ing | will be + V-ing |
| **Example** | By 6, I'**ll have been working** for 8 hrs. | At 6, I'**ll be working**. |

```
   Future Continuous:          ──[ working ]●(at 6, mid-action)
   Future Perfect Continuous:  [===== working =====]●(by 6, the whole 8-hr stretch)
```

### 🆚 The "evidence/cause" pair (future version)

| Sentence | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| At 6, it'**ll be raining**. | rain in progress at 6 |
| By 6, the streets will be wet — it'**ll have been raining** all day. | the long cause of a future result |

> 💡 Just like its past twin (Ch. 10), this is the **"detective tense"** — explaining a **future
> result** (tired, wet, soaked) by the long action that caused it.

## 12. 🇦🇿 Azerbaijani Translation Tips

1. **Future Perfect Continuous ≈ Azerbaijani "-mış olacaq" + müddət vurğusu:**
   - *By June, I **will have been working** here for 5 years* → *İyuna qədər burada beş il
     işlə**miş olacağam**.*
   - *I **will have been waiting** for an hour* → *Bir saat gözlə**miş olacağam**.*

2. **The "for / by" frame** is the heart:
   - *by June* → *iyuna qədər*
   - *for five years* → *beş il (müddətində)*

3. Azerbaijani **does not** have a separate continuous-perfect future, so *"will have been
   working"* and *"will have worked"* often both become *"işləmiş olacağam."* The **duration**
   sense comes from the **"for + length"** phrase and context.

4. **Cause/result:** *Yorğun olacaq, çünki bütün gün sürmüş olacaq* → *He'll be tired because
   he'**ll have been driving** all day.*

> 💡 **Strategy:** if the sentence answers **"gələcəkdə o ana qədər nə qədər müddət?"** (how
> long up to a future point), English wants **will have been + V-ing**. If it answers **"neçə
> dəfə? / bitdi?"** (how many / finished), use **will have + V3**. ⏳

## 28. ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Future Perfect Continuous or Future Perfect?**
*How long / ongoing / cause?* → Continuous (*will have been working*). *How many / finished?* →
simple Future Perfect (*will have worked*).

**Q2: Future Perfect Continuous or Future Continuous?**
*Will be + -ing* = in progress **at** a future moment. *Will have been + -ing* = the **whole
duration up to** a future point.

**Q3: Can I use it with state verbs?**
No — *know, be, like, want* use the **Future Perfect**: *"I'll have known her for years."*

**Q4: Is *will have been* the same for everyone?**
Yes! 🎉 No agreement: I/you/he/she/it/we/they all use **will have been**.

**Q5: been or being?**
It's **been**. *will have **been** waiting* ✅ / *will have **being** waiting* ❌.

**Q6: Is this tense really used?**
It's the least common of the 12, but it's the natural choice for **"how long, by a future
point."** Worth recognising and using when the meaning fits.

# 📚 Mini Story — *"Forty Years"* (≈320 words)

> The **Future Perfect Continuous** measures **how long** an action will have been going by a
> future point — and explains the feeling behind it. Watch the *by + for* frame. Read aloud.

Next Friday would be a special day. By then, Mr. Hasanov **would have been teaching** at the
same school for forty years.

He **sat** in his quiet classroom, thinking. *By Friday,* he **thought**, *I **will have been
standing** in front of a blackboard for four decades. I **will have been correcting** homework
every evening. I **will have been waking** up at six every morning — for forty years.*

He **smiled**. He **had loved** every minute.

His former student, now a teacher herself, **knocked** and **came** in. "Sir, the whole school
is planning a surprise. By Friday, we'**ll have been organising** it for two weeks!"

"You shouldn't have," he **said**, touched.

"Of course we should. Do you realise? By the end of this year, you'**ll have taught** over three
thousand students." *(how many → Future Perfect)*

"Has it really been that long?" he **asked** softly.

"It has. And by Friday," she **continued**, "you'**ll have been inspiring** people for two
generations. My mother was your student. So was I. Next year, my daughter starts here too."

Mr. Hasanov **felt** his eyes grow wet. "Forty years," he **whispered**. "By the time I retire,
I'**ll have been doing** the only job I ever wanted."

"And you'll be tired," she **laughed** gently. "By the party, you'**ll have been smiling** so
much your face will hurt!"

He **laughed** too.

On Friday, the hall **was** packed. Hundreds of former students **stood** and **clapped**. As he
**looked** out at them, Mr. Hasanov **realised** something. By that evening, he **will have been
living** his dream — not for forty years, but for a lifetime.

And he **knew**, watching their faces, that long after he **was** gone, his lessons **would** go
on.

### 🔎 Story Notes — Spot the Grammar

- **Future Perfect Continuous (duration up to Friday):** *would have been teaching, will have
  been standing, will have been correcting, will have been waking, will have been organising,
  will have been inspiring, will have been doing, will have been smiling, will have been
  living.* → each measured against a **future point** (*by Friday, by the time I retire*) and
  stressing **how long**.
- **Future Perfect — completed/how many (the contrast):** *"you'**ll have taught** over three
  thousand students"* → a **result/amount**, so simple Future Perfect (*will have + V3*), not
  the continuous. This is the §11 distinction live. ✅
- **Future-in-the-past:** *"would have been teaching … for forty years"* and *"his lessons
  **would** go on"* → *will* shifted to *would* in past narration (E8 of Ch. 17).
- **Time clause (present):** *"By the time I **retire**, I'll have been doing…"* → present after
  *by the time*. ✅
- **Pattern to copy:** pick a **future date** + a **duration**, and say what you **will have
  been doing** by then (and contrast with **how many** using the Future Perfect).

> + V3* (how many), and explain why each form fits. Then say: *"By [future date], I will have
> been … for …"*

# 🧠 Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

1. **WILL HAVE BEEN + ING = "how long, by a future point."** ⏳ Stand at a future deadline and
   measure the time **behind** you.

2. **how LONG → been + -ing · how MANY → will have + V3.** Duration vs result — ask first.

3. **The "by + for" frame.** 🖼️ *"By June, … for five years."* The two phrases lock the tense in.

4. **The detective tense (future)** 🕵️: a future **result** (tired, wet) → explain with *will
   have been + -ing*.

5. **Four words, fixed order:** WILL → HAVE → BEEN → -ING. *been*, not *being*.

6. **State verbs are statues** 🗿 (one last time!) — *know, want, be* stay *will have + V3*:
   *"I'll have known her for years."*

## E1. 🗣️ In Reported Speech — *would have been + V-ing*

Shifted to a past viewpoint, *will have been + V-ing* becomes **would have been + V-ing**:

```
   Direct:    "By June, I'll have been working here for five years."
   Reported:  She said that by June she WOULD HAVE BEEN WORKING there for five years.
```

| Direct | Reported |
|--------|----------|
| "I'll have been waiting for an hour." | He said he **would have been waiting** for an hour. |
| "We'll have been driving all day." | They said they **would have been driving** all day. |

> 💡 *would have been + V-ing* is the **past-shifted twin**. (Full reported speech = Ch. 25.)

---

## E2. 🕵️ Cause vs result — the future "detective" use

Like its past twin (Ch. 10), this tense usually explains the **cause** behind a **future state**:

| Future result | Cause (will have been + V-ing) |
|---------------|--------------------------------|
| He'll be exhausted. | He'**ll have been driving** all day. |
| The streets will be wet. | It'**ll have been raining**. |
| Her eyes will be sore. | She'**ll have been staring** at screens. |
| The plants will be huge. | They'**ll have been growing** all summer. |

> 🧠 **Pattern:** *[future state] + because + [will have been + -ing].* You **predict** a future
> situation and explain it by the **long action** leading up to it.

---

## E3. 🔀 Borderline verbs — *live / work / study / teach*

With *live, work, study, teach* + *for/since*, **both** the Future Perfect and Future Perfect
Continuous are usually fine, with a tiny emphasis shift:

| Future Perfect (neutral/fact) | Future Perfect Continuous (emphasis on duration) |
|-------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| By June, I'**ll have worked** here for 5 yrs. | By June, I'**ll have been working** here for 5 yrs. |
| By 2030, she'**ll have lived** abroad for a decade. | By 2030, she'**ll have been living** abroad for a decade. |

> 💡 The Continuous **highlights the ongoing activity**; the simple Future Perfect states it as
> a **fact**. With these verbs, choose either — they're close in meaning.

---

## E4. 🗿 State verbs stay SIMPLE (Future Perfect)

True state verbs never take the continuous — use **will have + V3**:

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---------|---------|
| will have been knowing | **will have known** |
| will have been wanting | **will have wanted** |
| will have been being | **will have been** |
| will have been owning | **will have owned** |

**State verb families:** *know, believe, understand, want, like, love, own, belong, be, seem.*

> 🧠 **Statue test** 🗿 (final time!): a mental state or possession → **will have + V3**, even
> with *for/since*.

---

## E5. 🖼️ The "by + for" frame & answering "how long?"

The tense lives inside a two-part frame:

```
   BY + [future point]  ......  FOR + [length of time]
   "By June,            I'll have been working here    for five years."
```

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| **How long will** you **have been working** by June? | (I'll have been working) **for** five years. |
| **How long will** they **have been waiting** by 8? | (for) an hour. |

> 💡 Answer with **for + a length** (*for five years*). The **by**-point sets the deadline; the
> **for**-phrase gives the duration. Both together = this tense.

---

## E6. ⚖️ One more pass — FPC vs FP (how long vs how many/result)

| Decide by asking… | Tense | Example |
|-------------------|-------|---------|
| **How long?** (ongoing) | will have been + V-ing | By 5, I'**ll have been writing** for 6 hrs. |
| **How many? / Result?** | will have + V3 | By 5, I'**ll have written** 4 reports. |
| **A state + duration?** | will have + V3 | By 5, I'**ll have known** her for years. |

```
   Decision tree:
   ① State verb?           → will have + V3
   ② Counting a result?    → will have + V3
   ③ Else (duration)?      → will have been + V-ing
```

---

## E7. 🔙 Future-in-the-past continuous perfect — *would have been + V-ing*

Beyond reported speech, this appears when you describe a **future-from-the-past** that involves
duration:

| Example | Viewpoint |
|---------|-----------|
| By the time he arrived, I knew I **would have been waiting** for an hour. | a future-duration seen from the past |
| She realised that by Friday she **would have been teaching** for forty years. | (the story "Forty Years" used this!) |

> 💡 *will have been + -ing* (from now) ↔ *would have been + -ing* (from a past point). Same
> meaning, shifted backward in time.

---

## E8. 📉 Rarity & register — when do people actually use it?

The Future Perfect Continuous is the **least common** of the 12 tenses. In real life:

- ✅ It's natural for **anniversaries of activity** and **"how long, by then"**: *"Next month,
  I'll have been driving for 20 years."*
- ✅ Common in **predictions/forecasts** about long trends.
- ⚠️ In casual speech, people often **simplify** to the Future Perfect or even present: *"Next
  month, I'll have driven for 20 years"* / *"...I've been driving 20 years."*
- 📌 **You should recognise it confidently and produce it when the meaning truly fits** (duration
  up to a future point) — but don't force it where a simpler tense sounds natural.

> 💡 Mastery = knowing it **exists**, **understanding** it, and **using it precisely** — not
> overusing it.

---

## E9. ➖ Negatives — emphasising a future lack of activity

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| By Friday, they **won't have been working** long enough to qualify. | not enough duration by then |
| Don't worry — you **won't have been waiting** long. | a short wait, reassurance |
| By then, I **won't have been sleeping** properly for a week. | a continued lack (cause of tiredness) |

> 📌 Form: **won't have been + V-ing**. Often softens ("you won't have been waiting long") or
> explains a future problem.

## E10. 🗺️🏆 THE GRAND 12-TENSE MASTER MAP

> The capstone. **One verb (*work*), all 12 tenses**, in one place. This is the map of
> everything you've learned in Parts 1–3.

### The 3 × 4 grid

| | **SIMPLE** | **CONTINUOUS** (be + -ing) | **PERFECT** (have + V3) | **PERFECT CONTINUOUS** (have been + -ing) |
|--|-----------|----------------------------|-------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **PRESENT** | I **work** | I **am working** | I **have worked** | I **have been working** |
| **PAST** | I **worked** | I **was working** | I **had worked** | I **had been working** |
| **FUTURE** | I **will work** | I **will be working** | I **will have worked** | I **will have been working** |

### What each column "feels" like

```
 SIMPLE             →  a fact / habit / completed event        (work · worked · will work)
 CONTINUOUS         →  in progress at a moment 〜               (am/was/will be working)
 PERFECT            →  completed before a reference point ●     (have/had/will have worked)
 PERFECT CONTINUOUS →  duration up to a reference point 〜●     (have/had/will have been working)
```

### The whole timeline in one picture

```
        PAST ◄──────────────── NOW ──────────────► FUTURE

 SIMPLE:        worked •          work (habit)        will work •
 CONTINUOUS:    was working 〜     am working 〜        will be working 〜
 PERFECT:       had worked ●→past  have worked ●→now    will have worked ●→future
 PERFECT CONT:  had been 〜●→past   have been 〜●→now     will have been 〜●→future

   ● = completed before a point  ·  〜 = ongoing/duration
```

### The master decision flow

```
 1) WHEN is the time frame?        → PAST · PRESENT · FUTURE   (choose the row)
 2) WHICH aspect fits the meaning? → choose the column:
      • a fact / habit / single event ............ SIMPLE
      • in progress at a moment .................. CONTINUOUS
      • completed before a point / result ........ PERFECT
      • how long, duration up to a point ......... PERFECT CONTINUOUS
 ⇒ Row × Column = your tense.
```

> 🧠 **The big secret:** you don't memorise 12 separate tenses — you choose **1 of 3 times** ×
> **1 of 4 aspects**. The **aspect** (column) is always the same idea, just placed in past,
> present, or future. **Screenshot this map — it's the heart of the whole book.** 🏆

## E12. 🧨 Extra Common Mistakes (Azerbaijani-specific)

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| By June, I **will have been knowing** her for 10 yrs. | By June, I **will have known** her. | state verb → Future Perfect |
| By 5, I **will have been written** 3 emails. | By 5, I **will have written** 3 emails. | amount → Future Perfect |
| At 8, she **will have been working**. *(meaning a moment)* | At 8, she **will be working**. | a moment → Future Continuous |

```
 32. By 10, she won't have been jogging for an hour.        (will → won't)
 33. How long will he have been working on the project?     (will + he ... + V-ing?)
 34. The garden will look amazing because Tom will have been working on it all spring.
 35. (a) — for DURATION up to a future point, English prefers the CONTINUOUS form.
        "will have stood" (b) sounds like a finished count, not an ongoing stretch.
```
> 🧠 **Capstone:** FPC = **a line up to a future dot** ⟶•. It answers **how long** and explains
> **why** a future situation is the way it is. *Will have been + V-ing.*
</details>

## 🇬🇧 The Long Voyage

Mission Control was quiet. On the big screen, a small white dot crawled toward a red planet.

"Status report," said Director Adams.

Young Leyla looked up from her desk. "Sir, the ship left Earth this morning. By the time it
reaches Mars, **the crew will have been travelling for seven months**. Seven months in a metal
box."

Adams nodded slowly. "And the engines?"

"By landing day, **the main engine will have been burning fuel for over two hundred days**. We've
never tested anything for that long. By next Friday alone, **it will have been running
continuously for a week**."

A red light blinked. Leyla checked the screen and smiled. "Don't worry, sir. By the time we get
the first photo back, **the cameras will have been recording for three weeks**, but the batteries
will still be at ninety percent."

Adams walked to the window. Outside, the night sky was full of stars. "You know," he said
quietly, "when that ship lands, **I will have been working on this mission for eleven years**.
Eleven years of late nights. My daughter was six when we started."

"And she'll be seventeen by then, sir," Leyla said. "**She'll have been growing up while you've
been building a road to another world.**"

Adams laughed for the first time that day. "By the time those astronauts step onto the red sand,
**half the people in this room will have been dreaming about it since they were children**."

Leyla looked back at the little white dot, still crawling, still patient. *In two hundred days,*
she thought, *the whole world will be watching. And we'll be tired — because by then,
**we'll have been waiting for this moment our entire lives**.*

The dot moved one pixel closer to red.

Missiya İdarəetmə Mərkəzi sakit idi. Böyük ekranda kiçik ağ nöqtə qırmızı planetə doğru sürünürdü.

Leyla yenidən hələ də sürünən, hələ də səbirli kiçik ağ nöqtəyə baxdı. *İki yüz gündən sonra,*

həyatımız boyu bu anı gözləmiş olacağıq.***

Nöqtə qırmızıya bir piksel daha yaxınlaşdı.

## 🔑 Story key — every FPC sentence, decoded

| 🇬🇧 From the story | Why FPC? | 🇦🇿 |
|---|---|---|
| the crew **will have been travelling** for seven months | duration up to landing | yeddi aydır səyahət etmiş olacaq |
| the engine **will have been burning** fuel for 200+ days | how long, up to a future point | yanacaq yandırmış olacaq |
| it **will have been running** for a week | duration up to "next Friday" | bir həftədir işləmiş olacaq |
| the cameras **will have been recording** for three weeks | how long the action continues | çəkiliş etmiş olacaq |
| I **will have been working** on this mission for eleven years | career duration → future point | on bir ildir işləmiş olacam |
| She**'ll have been growing up** while you've been building… | parallel long process | böyüyüb boya-başa çatmış olacaq |
| half the people **will have been dreaming** about it since childhood | long ongoing wish | arzulamış olacaq |
| we**'ll have been waiting** our entire lives | duration that explains future tiredness | gözləmiş olacağıq |

> 🧠 Notice: **every** bold line measures **HOW LONG** an action keeps going **up to a future
> moment** (landing day, next Friday, the photo). That "moving toward a future dot" feeling is the
> whole heart of this tense. ⟶•
