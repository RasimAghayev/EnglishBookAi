## 🅱️ §B — NATIVE-SPEAKER INSIGHT (on testing)

1. **Speed matters at C1+.** Natives (and top exam takers) choose the right form **instantly** — practise timed to build automaticity. 🇦🇿 *C1+-da sürət vacibdir — vaxtlı məşq et.*
2. **Distractors mimic real errors.** Multiple-choice traps are built from **actual learner mistakes** (Ch.39) — recognising the trap is half the skill. 🇦🇿 *Yanlış variantlar real səhvlərdən qurulur.*
3. **Context beats rules.** Advanced items rarely have a "signal word" — you decide from **meaning**, exactly as natives do. 🇦🇿 *Qabaqcıl suallarda siqnal söz olmur — mənadan qərar ver.*
4. **Your errors cluster.** Almost everyone loses points in the **same 2–3 areas** — find and drill them. 🇦🇿 *Səhvlər eyni 2–3 sahədə toplanır.*
5. **Re-test, don't re-read.** Re-doing a quiz after a week reveals what truly stuck; re-reading only feels productive. 🇦🇿 *Təkrar oxu yox, təkrar test et.*
6. **Exam calm is trainable.** The more mock tests you do, the less the real one rattles you. 🇦🇿 *İmtahan sakitliyi məşqlə gəlir.*

# 📖 MINI STORY #1 — "The Diagnostic"

### *A teacher shows a student how to turn a quiz score into a study plan.*
### 🇦🇿 *Müəllim şagirdə test balını necə tədris planına çevirməyi göstərir.*

---

Farid slid his quiz across the desk. "72 out of 100," he sighed. "I failed."

"You didn't fail," his teacher said. "You just **got 72 pieces of information** about your English. Let's read them."

She spread out his answers. "Look — where did you lose points?"

Farid scanned. "Mostly… the conditionals. And the inversion ones. And a few articles."

"There it is," she smiled. "Not *'I'm bad at English.'* You're **strong** at tenses and modals — you got nearly all of those. You have **three specific gaps**: conditionals, inversion, and articles."

"So what do I do?"

"You don't re-read the whole book. You **revisit three chapters** — 24, 34, and the article rules in 39. Then you **re-test** those areas next week."

Farid frowned at question 71. "I put *'If I studied medicine, I'd be a doctor now.'* The answer says *'had studied.'* Why?"

"Because the **cause is in the past** but the **result is now** — a mixed conditional. See? One wrong answer just **taught you a rule** you'll never forget."

A week later, Farid re-took the conditionals set. **9 out of 10.**

"Now do it again in a month," his teacher said. "If it sticks then, it's really yours."

Farid grinned. "So a low score isn't a verdict…"

"It's a **map**," she said. "It shows you exactly where to dig."

# 🧨 EXPANSION PACK — Test-Taking Strategy (E1–E12)
### *The deep layer: diagnose don't score, trap types, timing, elimination, CEFR/IELTS mapping & re-testing.*
### 🇦🇿 *Dərin qat: diaqnoz, tələ növləri, vaxt, eliminasiya, CEFR/IELTS uyğunluğu və təkrar test.*

---

## 🅴1 — DIAGNOSE, don't score (the loop)

```
   do quiz → check → for each MISS: name the AREA → target the CHAPTER → relearn RULE → re-test → space it
```

> 🔑 The number tells you *where you are*; the **pattern of misses** tells you *where to go*. Always convert a score into a **2–3 chapter study list**. 🇦🇿 *Rəqəm harada olduğunu, səhvlərin nümunəsi hara getməli olduğunu göstərir.*

---

## 🅴2 — TRAP types in multiple choice (distractors)

| Distractor built from… | Example trap | Real rule |
|---|---|---|
| finished-time + perfect | "I have seen it yesterday" | past simple with *yesterday* |
| *since* + present | "I live here since 2010" | present perfect |
| *will* in if-clause | "If I will have time…" | present in if-clause |
| calque collocation | "make a photo" | *take* a photo |
| double negative | "I don't know nothing" | *anything* |
| wrong tag polarity | "You're in, isn't it?" | *aren't you* |

> 🔑 Multiple-choice **wrong options are real learner errors** (Ch.39). Spotting *why* a distractor is tempting is half the answer. 🇦🇿 *Yanlış variantlar real səhvlərdir — niyə cəlbedici olduğunu gör.*

---

## 🅴3 — TIMING strategy

| Exam | Pace | Tactic |
|---|---|---|
| grammar section | ~30–45 sec/question | flag hard ones, move on, return |
| reading gap-fill | read the whole sentence first | context sets the tense |
| under pressure | never leave a blank | eliminate, then best guess |

> 🔑 Don't sink 3 minutes into one item — **flag and return**. A blank scores zero; an educated guess might score. 🇦🇿 *Bir suala çox vaxt sərf etmə — işarələ, keç, sonra qayıt.*

---

## 🅴4 — READING the question stem

| Clue in the stem | Points to |
|---|---|
| *since / for / already / yet* | present perfect |
| *yesterday / ago / last* | past simple |
| *by the time / before* + past | past perfect |
| *if* + present | first conditional (will) |
| *if* + past | second conditional (would) |
| *(the) …that/who follows a noun* | relative clause |

> 🔑 Half of grammar questions are decided by a **signal word** hiding in the stem — hunt for it first (Ch.35, 46). 🇦🇿 *Sualın yarısı gizli siqnal sözlə həll olunur — əvvəlcə onu tap.*

---

## 🅴5 — ELIMINATION technique

```
   4 options → cross out ones that break a RULE you know → choose between what's left
   "She ___ here since May":  ✗ lives (since+present)  ✗ lived (since)  ✓ has lived  ✗ is living
```

> 🔑 You don't always need to *know* the answer — you can **eliminate** the impossible until one remains. 🇦🇿 *Cavabı bilməsən də, mümkünsüzləri sil — biri qalır.*

---

## 🅴8 — IELTS band mapping (Grammatical Range & Accuracy)

| Band | Grammar profile |
|---|---|
| **9** | wide range, full flexibility, errors rare as a native's |
| **7–8** | variety of complex structures; mostly error-free sentences |
| **6** | mix of simple & complex; some errors, meaning clear |
| **5** | limited range; frequent errors that can strain the reader |

> 🔑 Band 7+ needs **range** (not just accuracy): conditionals, relatives, passives, perfect aspects — used **naturally**, not crammed. 🇦🇿 *Band 7+ üçün struktur **rəngarəngliyi** lazımdır — təbii şəkildə.*

## 🅴12 — 🇦🇿 CONTRASTIVE SYNTHESIS (which traps catch AZ speakers)

| Trap | Why it catches AZ speakers | Guard |
|---|---|---|
| article gaps | no articles in AZ | check every noun |
| *since*/*for* + present | AZ "-dır" merges them | since/for → perfect |
| prepositions | AZ postpositions | learn chunks |
| double negative | AZ allows it | one negative + *any-* |
| he/she | AZ "o" is neutral | check gender |
| calque collocations | word-for-word habit | learn native chunks |

> 🧠 **The Azerbaijani contrast in one line:** on a test, an AZ speaker's misses **cluster predictably** around the structural gaps between the languages — **articles, since/for, prepositions, double negatives, he/she, and calques**. Because these are **predictable**, they're **fixable**: keep an error log, notice the family, and pre-empt the trap. The distractors on any good English test are practically designed to catch exactly these — so knowing your interference points turns traps into easy marks.

# 📖 MINI STORY #2 — "Exam Morning"

### *Two students, two strategies — one panics, one diagnoses. The score follows the mindset.*
### 🇦🇿 *İki tələbə, iki strategiya — biri həyəcanlanır, biri diaqnoz qoyur. Bal düşüncəni izləyir.*

> Read it twice. **First** for the story; **second** noting the smart test habits.
> 🇦🇿 *İki dəfə oxu: əvvəl hekayə üçün, sonra ağıllı test vərdişlərini qeyd edərək.*

---

The mock exam started at nine. Two friends, Rəna and Tural, sat side by side.

Tural **froze** on question one and stared at it for four minutes. *(no timing strategy.)* Rəna, seeing a hard item, **flagged it and moved on**. *(smart pacing.)*

At a tricky conditional — *"If I ___ known…"* — Tural panicked and guessed *"would have."* Rəna **eliminated** the impossible: *"can't be 'would have' in the if-part… it must be 'Had' — the inverted third conditional."* *(elimination.)*

By the halfway mark, Tural had answered eight questions. Rəna had answered twenty, **flagging** six to revisit. *(pace + flags.)*

On a gap-fill — *"I ___ here since 2015"* — Rəna spotted the signal instantly: *"'since' → present perfect → 'have lived'."* *(reading the stem.)* Tural wrote *"live"* without noticing the trap. *(the since+present trap.)*

With five minutes left, Rəna **went back to her flagged six**, used the extra thinking time, and **checked her agreement and tenses**. Tural was still stuck on a single article question.

The results came back: Rəna 88, Tural 61.

"You're just smarter," Tural sighed.

"No," said Rəna. "You **knew** most of these. You lost points to **panic and pace**, not grammar. Look — your misses are all *articles* and *since/for*. That's **two chapters**, not a personality."

She showed him her method: **flag hard items, eliminate, hunt the signal word, save time to check, then log every miss by family.**

The next mock, Tural tried it. He scored 79 — and, for the first time, he didn't feel like he'd failed. He felt like he had a **map**.
